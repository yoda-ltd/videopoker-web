# Double Jackpot Poker vs. Quick Quads: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Double Jackpot Poker and Quick Quads are two variants that appeal to players who love Four of a Kind hands. Double Jackpot offers premiums for specific ranks (Face Cards and Aces), while Quick Quads introduces a unique mechanic that allows you to create a "Four of a Kind" using your fifth card as a mathematical addition.

Which one should you choose for the best house edge? Let's break it down.

## Quick Quads: The Innovative Strategy Game

Quick Quads is a unique game where a 3-of-a-Kind can become a 4-of-a-Kind if the remaining two cards add up to the rank of the trio. For example, if you have three 8s, a 5, and a 3, you have "Quick Quads" because 5 + 3 = 8.

*   **Quick Quad Payout:** Same as a standard Four of a Kind (usually 25-for-1).
*   **The Catch:** You must bet **6 coins** per hand to activate the Quick Quads feature.
*   **Theoretical RTP (Full Pay):** **99.62%**
*   **House Edge:** 0.38%

Quick Quads is a high-action game that turns many "losing" hands into big winners, but it requires a specialized strategy to know when to chase the "addition" hands.

## Double Jackpot Poker: The Face Card Bonus Game

Double Jackpot is a traditional "Bonus" style game that pays extra for high-ranking Four of a Kind hands.

*   **Four Aces:** 160-for-1.
*   **Four J, Q, K:** 80-for-1.
*   **Theoretical RTP (9/6):** **99.63%**
*   **House Edge:** 0.37%

## Comparing the House Edge

### 1. Mathematical Return (RTP)
In a head-to-head comparison of Full Pay machines, **the games are virtually identical.**
*   Double Jackpot: 99.63% RTP
*   Quick Quads: 99.62% RTP

A difference of 0.01% is statistically irrelevant for the average player. Both games offer some of the best returns in the casino.

### 2. Strategy Complexity
*   **Double Jackpot** uses standard video poker strategy. If you know how to play Jacks or Better, you are 90% of the way there.
*   **Quick Quads** has a very steep learning curve. You have to be able to do mental math quickly to see if your cards add up to a "Quick Quad." You will also make holds that seem bizarre to a standard player, such as keeping a 3-card 8-8-8 along with a 5, hoping to draw a 3.

### 3. Volatility (The "Swing")
*   **Double Jackpot** is high volatility because it pays 1-for-1 on Two Pair. Your bankroll depends on hitting the Face Card or Ace bonuses.
*   **Quick Quads** is actually lower volatility than many bonus games. Because you are hitting Four of a Kind more frequently (roughly twice as often as in standard games), your bankroll stays more stable. However, the 6-coin bet means you are wagering more per hand.

## Verdict: Which Should You Play?

*   **Play Double Jackpot Poker if** you want a traditional experience with high-paying "jackpot" moments on face cards. It is a simpler, more relaxed game.
*   **Play Quick Quads if** you are a math-oriented player who wants a challenge. The thrill of "creating" a Four of a Kind out of a random 5 and 3 is a unique experience that many players find addictive.

**Final Tip:** Because Quick Quads is a "specialty" game, it is often only available on multi-game machines like Game King. Double Jackpot is more common as a standalone machine. **Always check the pay table!** A 9/6 Double Jackpot machine is better than a "short-pay" Quick Quads machine (which can drop to 97%). Always play the machine with the highest number at the bottom of the table.
