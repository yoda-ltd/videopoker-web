# All American vs. Tens or Better: House Edge and Strategy Synergy

In the landscape of "best video poker strategy comparison," **All American vs. Tens or Better** is a battle of draw-driven volatility vs. entry-level stability. One game rewards you for hitting long-shot draws like Straights and Flushes with an 8-for-1 payout; the other rewards you for the simplest hand possible—a pair of Tens. This creates a massive **Strategy Bifurcation** and alters the fundamental **Payout Weighting** of each game.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Draw" vs. "Pair" Hunt

The most significant **Strategy Bifurcation** occurs in the way you prioritize Straights and Flushes vs. low-end pairs.

*   **All American Strategy:** Because the Straight and Flush both pay 8-for-1, you will often choose to hold a 4-card Flush over a High Pair (Jacks-Aces). This is a classic example of **Payout Weighting**—the 8-for-1 payout is so valuable that it overrides the "safe" pair hold.
*   **Tens or Better Strategy:** In Tens or Better, a pair of Tens is a winner. This means you will hold a pair of Tens, Jacks, Queens, Kings, or Aces over almost any 4-card draw. The goal is to survive and play as many hands as possible to hit the Royal Flush.

## Strategy Synergy: Where the Games Align

Despite the different payout structures, there is some **Strategy Synergy** in the "Base Game" logic:

1.  **Royal Flush Priority:** In both games, a 4-card Royal Flush is the top-tier hold.
2.  **Aces are Key:** In both games, 4 Aces pay a bonus (usually 35-for-1 or 40-for-1). You always hold a pair of Aces.
3.  **High Card Stability:** Both games pay 1-for-1 for a pair of high cards (Tens or Better in one, Jacks or Better in the other), providing a stable floor for your bankroll.

## Variance Inversion: Frequent Draw Wins vs. Constant Pair Wins

The most jarring difference for players is the **Variance Inversion**.

*   **All American:** The volatility is high (~30.0). Because you are chasing 8-for-1 Straights and Flushes, you will experience longer losing streaks between "big" wins. However, the frequent 8-for-1 payouts can quickly recover your bankroll.
*   **Tens or Better:** The volatility is low (~19.0). Because you win on a pair of Tens, you will hit a winning hand on about 40% of your deals. This is the lowest volatility game in the video poker world, making it ideal for clearing casino bonuses.

If you are a professional player looking for a potential edge and don't mind the high volatility of chasing 8-for-1 draws, **All American** offers one of the best returns in the casino.

However, if you are a casual player or a "bonus hunter" who wants to minimize risk and play for a long time on a small bankroll, **Tens or Better** is the superior choice.
