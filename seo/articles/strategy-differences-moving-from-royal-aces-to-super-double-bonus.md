# Strategy Differences: Moving from Royal Aces to Super Double Bonus

The transition from Royal Aces to Super Double Bonus (SDB) represents a shift from "Niche Targeting" to "Broad-Spectrum Bonus Play." This **best video poker strategy comparison** examine the **Strategy Bifurcation** and **Variance Scaling** that occurs when you move from a game obsessed with a single card (the Ace) to one that rewards a variety of four-of-a-kind (Quad) combinations.

## The Mathematical Foundation: Single Bonus vs. Nested Bonus

**Royal Aces** is a "Single-Target" game. It is a Jacks or Better variant that puts a massive premium on 4 Aces. To fund this "Royal" payout, the game uses **Payout Compression** on all other Quads and potentially on mid-tier hands. The **video poker house edge analysis** is dominated by the frequency of hitting 4 Aces.

**Super Double Bonus** is a "Nested-Target" game. It pays different bonuses for different sets of Quads:
*   4 Aces (Highest Bonus)
*   4 Kings/Queens/Jacks (Mid-tier Bonus)
*   4 Threes/Fours/Fives (Entry-level Bonus)

This "Balanced" approach means that while 4 Aces might pay less than in Royal Aces, the overall "Bonus" frequency is much higher.

## Variance Scaling and the Bankroll Buffer

1.  **Royal Aces:** The variance is high and "Unbalanced." The **Variance Scaling** is steep because the game doesn't provide a "Safety Net" for non-Ace hands. The **EV Gap** between a "Winning" session (with Aces) and a "Losing" one (without) is massive.
2.  **Super Double Bonus:** The variance is high (around 45) but "Balanced." The **Variance Scaling** is smoother because you have multiple "Target" hands. hitting 4 Kings in SDB provides a significant "Boost" that keeps you in the game, whereas in Royal Aces, 4 Kings might just pay the "Standard" Quad amount.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Ace" Reset

The move requires a total "Hold" adjustment:
*   In **Royal Aces**, you are an "Ace Specialist." You will hold a single Ace over almost any other high card combination. You will break apart a "Winning" Two-Pair to keep a lone Ace if the pay table is aggressive enough.
*   In **Super Double Bonus**, you are a "Quad Generalist." You still value the Ace, but you don't "Chase" it to the exclusion of all else. You will hold "Jacks or Better" high pairs with more confidence, knowing they carry their own "Bonus" potential.

If you are ready to move from "Ace-Hunting" to a more "Strategic" Quad-focused game, Super Double Bonus is the ultimate destination. It offers a more "Sustainable" high-variance experience for the serious advantage player.
