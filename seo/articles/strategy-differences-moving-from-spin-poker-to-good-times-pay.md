# Strategy Differences: Moving from Spin Poker to Good Times Pay

Transitioning from Spin Poker to Good Times Pay is a unique step in the video poker world. Both games are niche variants that offer a "multi-hand" or "bonus" experience. This guide provides the best video poker strategy comparison to help you understand how to play video poker like a pro during this transition.

## The Spin Poker Baseline

In Spin Poker, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is built on the fact that any pair of Jacks or better is a winning hand. This creates a very stable, low-volatility experience. When moving to Good Times Pay, that "safety net" is removed. You now need at least a pair of Queens or Kings to get your money back.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from Spin Poker to Good Times Pay, you'll find that **80% of your strategy remains the same**. The **20% that changes** is entirely focused on the "Pair threshold." This 20% shift causes the most **Bankroll Drain** if not corrected.

### The 20% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Pair of Jacks Devaluation:** In Spin Poker, a pair of Jacks is a winning hand. In Good Times Pay, it's a "trash" hand that you should rarely hold unless you're drawing for something better (like a three-card Royal Flush).
2.  **Good Times Pay Focus:** Good Times Pay is named "Good Times Pay" because it rewards you specifically for getting multiple winning hands in a row. This makes four-of-a-kind and specific straights much more valuable than in Spin Poker.
3.  **Two Pair Strategy:** While Two Pair pays 2-for-1 in both games, the higher payout for specific "Good Times" hands in Good Times Pay means you might break a Full House to draw for a fifth card more often than you would in Spin Poker.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

Good Times Pay introduces **Volatility Scaling**. Without the Jacks-or-better push, your bankroll will experience more frequent dips. **EV Erosion** occurs when a Spin-Poker player instinctively holds a pair of Jacks in Good Times Pay. This is a "dead hand" and holding it is a direct loss of expected value.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Queens or Kings or Better are your new minimum "win."
*   **Modify:** Discard any pair of Jacks unless they are part of a better draw.
*   **Target:** Aggressively pursue the "Good Times" bonuses; they are the heart of the Good Times Pay pay table.

By recognizing that the "safety net" has moved, you can successfully transition to Good Times Pay without suffering from significant EV Erosion.
