# Transitioning from Slots to Five Deck Poker: An Advantage Play Guide

If you're a slot machine enthusiast, "Five Deck Poker" probably sounds like another "five-reel" slot game. But in the world of professional video poker, Five Deck Poker is a game of skill where **Advantage Play (AP)** principles can give you a massive edge over the house.

## The Hybrid: Slots vs. Five Deck Poker

On a typical slot machine, symbols appear randomly on reels. In Five Deck Poker, each card in your hand is drawn from a separate 52-card deck. This changes the entire mathematical landscape of the game.
While a standard "Jacks or Better" paytable might pay 125-for-1 for a Royal Flush, Five Deck Poker can offer an **800-for-1 or even 4,000-coin payout** for a "Five-of-a-Kind" or a "Natural" Royal Flush, while also providing unique payouts for "Five of a Kind."

## Why Five Deck Poker is Better Than Slots: The ROI Factor

A typical slot machine has a hidden Return to Player (RTP) between 88% and 94%. Five Deck Poker, when played with perfect strategy on a "Full Pay" table, can offer an RTP of **99.5% or even over 100%** if a progressive jackpot is included. 
- **The AP Advantage:** Moving from a 92% slot to a 99.5% Five Deck Poker game is an immediate 7.5% increase in your **ROI (Return on Investment)**. For a professional AP, this is the difference between losing money and playing a "break-even" or even profitable game.

## Strategy: The "Separate Deck" Approach

Because each card comes from a different deck, your strategy must change from traditional video poker. 
1. **The "Five-of-a-Kind" Hold:** In Five Deck Poker, holding a single card can lead to a "Five of a Kind" (five identical cards). This is impossible in standard 52-card poker. The "Expected Value (EV)" of the hands you can complete with a single card is so high that it pulls the strategy toward the top of the deck.
2. **The "Natural" Royal Flush:** While the five decks allow for duplicates, a Royal Flush without duplicates is the "Natural" version, which pays the most. This creates a unique strategic tension: when do you hold for a five-of-a-kind vs. a natural Royal?
3. **High Variance:** Because so much of the RTP is concentrated in the Five-of-a-Kind and Royal Flush, you will experience "dry spells" where you don't hit the big hand. An AP ensures they have a massive **Bankroll** to weather the "Standard Deviation" of losses.

## Advantage Play Tip: The "Duplicate" Trap

In some Five Deck Poker variants, "Five-of-a-Kind" pays more than a Royal Flush. This is a common trap for former slot players who expect the Royal to always be the top prize. You must adjust your strategy to ignore traditional poker hierarchies and focus on the paytable's unique EV. This is a mathematical calculation that an AP memorizes before ever sitting down at the machine.

## Conclusion: From Passive to Pro

Five Deck Poker is the ultimate game for slot players who want to start taking gambling seriously. It offers the high-payout excitement of a slot machine multiple-reel but with the mathematical transparency and skill-based upside of video poker. Focus on the five decks, master the EV, and remember: every "spin" in Five Deck Poker is a calculated investment in your long-term success.
