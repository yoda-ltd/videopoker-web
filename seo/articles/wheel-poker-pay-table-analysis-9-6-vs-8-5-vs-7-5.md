# Wheel Poker Pay Table Analysis: 9/6 vs. 8/5 vs. 7/5

When choosing a machine, the pay table is your roadmap to profitability. In **Wheel Poker**, however, the roadmap has a major detour: the Wheel Spin feature. This **wheel poker strategy guide** breaks down how to evaluate pay tables when a high-value bonus mechanic is in play, and why a "9/6" machine in Wheel Poker isn't the same as a "9/6" machine in standard Jacks or Better.

## Understanding the "Bonus Tax"

Wheel Poker typically requires a 10-coin bet per line (5 for the base game, 5 for the feature). The feature triggers a wheel spin on every Natural Three-of-a-Kind. Because the wheel has a high **Bonus Event EV** (with a top prize of 2,000 coins), casinos often lower the base game pay table to maintain their house edge.

### The Standard Benchmarks:
*   **9/6:** Full House pays 9, Flush pays 6. (Target RTP: ~99.5% + Wheel value)
*   **8/5:** Full House pays 8, Flush pays 5. (Target RTP: ~97.3% + Wheel value)
*   **7/5:** Full House pays 7, Flush pays 5. (Target RTP: ~96.2% + Wheel value)

## Analysis of the Wheel Poker Spin Mechanic

The wheel spin isn't just a "extra." It is a fundamental part of the RTP. 
*   **Feature Hit Frequency:** You will hit a Three-of-a-Kind approximately once every 8 to 9 hands in Jacks or Better. 
*   **Weighted Spin Value:** The wheel slices are not equal. While you see a 2,000-coin slice, the statistical average (the "expected value") of a spin is roughly 470 coins.

### Does a 9/6 Wheel Poker machine exist?
True 9/6 Wheel Poker is rare. If you find one, the total RTP (Base + Wheel) can actually exceed 100% with perfect play, making it a "positive expectation" (+EV) game. More commonly, you will find **8/5** or **7/5** versions.

## 9/6 vs. 8/5 vs. 7/5: The Real Cost

| Pay Table | Base RTP | Wheel Contribution | Total Theoretical RTP |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **9/6** | 99.54% | ~0.80% | **100.34%** |
| **8/5** | 97.30% | ~0.80% | **98.10%** |
| **7/5** | 96.15% | ~0.80% | **96.95%** |

*Note: The "Wheel Contribution" is calculated by dividing the Bonus Event EV by the total cost of the hands required to trigger it.*

## Strategy Impact: The Three-of-a-Kind Priority

In a standard **wheel poker strategy guide**, the biggest shift in play occurs when comparing a 9/6 to a 7/5 table. 
1.  **Chasing the Spin:** Because the wheel triggers on a Three-of-a-Kind, the value of holding a pair (to draw into a set) is slightly higher in Wheel Poker than in standard games.
2.  **The Flush "Penalty":** In 7/5 or 8/5 games, the lower payout for Flushes and Full Houses means you should lean even more heavily into strategies that prioritize Three-of-a-Kind and Royal Flush draws.

## Conclusion: Which Should You Play?

Always look for the 9/6, but understand that in the world of "feature" poker, an **8/5 Wheel Poker** machine can actually be more profitable than a **9/6 Standard** machine if you factor in the massive 2,000-coin wheel potential. However, avoid **7/5** unless the wheel values are significantly boosted, as the base game "bleed" will likely outpace your wheel winnings.

Always check the pay table before you insert your first credit—in Wheel Poker, the numbers on the screen are only half the story.
