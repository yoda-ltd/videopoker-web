# The Evolution and History of Microgaming Video Poker: The Digital Revolution

In 1994, a company called **Microgaming** changed the gambling world forever. While the giants like **IGT** and **Si Redd** were still focusing on land-based casinos, Microgaming launched the first true online casino. This move started a second **video poker software history**—one that existed entirely in the digital realm.

## The 1994 Breakthrough: From Physical Cabinets to Browsers
Microgaming’s primary innovation was the move away from physical hardware logic. Instead of EPROMs, they developed a centralized game server architecture. 

*   **API Integration:** Early Microgaming video poker was among the first to use **API Integration** to link player accounts across different websites. This allowed for the creation of massive progressive jackpots, such as "Jackpot Deuces."
*   **The PRNG Revolution:** Microgaming's **Random Number Generator (RNG)** was built from the ground up to handle the unique challenges of the internet. Their software underwent rigorous **RNG Certification** to prove to a skeptical public that online gambling wasn't a "black box."

## The Technical Leap: Downloadable vs. Flash vs. HTML5
The history of Microgaming’s video poker software is a history of web technology:

1.  **The Download Era (1990s):** Players had to download a massive software package to their PCs. This software contained the local graphics and sound, while the actual win/loss outcome was determined on Microgaming's secure servers.
2.  **The Flash Era (2000s):** The "Instant Play" era allowed video poker to run directly in a browser. Microgaming’s "Viper" software became the industry standard for speed and stability.
3.  **The HTML5 Era (2010s-Present):** Today, Microgaming (and its successors like Games Global) uses HTML5 to ensure their games—like the iconic "Aces and Faces" or "Deuces Wild"—run perfectly on both mobile phones and desktop computers.

## Security and Standards: GLI-11 and eCOGRA
Microgaming was a co-founder of **eCOGRA** (eCommerce Online Gaming Regulation and Assurance). This body ensures that online video poker software meets standards similar to the **GLI-11 Standards** used in physical casinos. 

Their **Source Code** is regularly audited to ensure that every deal is truly random and that the virtual 52-card deck is not "weighted" in any way. This transparency is why Microgaming is often cited as one of the **best video poker game providers** in history.

## IGT vs. Microgaming: Two Different Philosophies
While **IGT** dominated the physical "Game King" market with Si Redd’s vision, Microgaming dominated the "Virtual" market. 
*   **IGT focus:** Cabinet reliability, physical security, and land-based casino floor management.
*   **Microgaming focus:** Scalability, cross-device compatibility, and large-scale progressive networks.

## Conclusion: The Mobile-First Future
Today, the line between land-based and online video poker is blurring. Modern machines in Las Vegas often use software architectures inspired by the digital-first approach that Microgaming pioneered. As we look at the **video poker software history**, it's clear that Microgaming's 1994 gamble on the internet paved the way for the mobile-first, cloud-based world of gaming we live in today.
