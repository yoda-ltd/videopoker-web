# Strategy Differences: Moving from Super Double Bonus to Five-Deck Poker
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Super Double Bonus** to **Five-Deck Poker** is a shift from a "High Bonus" natural variant to a "Multi-Deck" variant. This represents a significant **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your hands and the potential for multi-deck connectivity.

### The Comparison Matrix: Super Double Bonus vs. Five-Deck Poker

| Feature | Super Double Bonus (10/7) | Five-Deck Poker (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.17% (Full Pay) | 99.40% |
| **Variance Index** | 28.5 | 20.0 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target High-Payout Natural Quads | Target 5-Card Hands with Multi-Deck Connectivity |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.17% (Player Edge) | 0.60% |

### Strategic Divergence: Bonus Quads vs. Five Decks

In **Super Double Bonus Poker**, you are looking for natural four-of-a-kind hands that pay a massive bonus. In **Five-Deck Poker**, you are looking for the best five-card combination across five independent decks. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through multi-deck connectivity. Five-Deck Poker's variance (20.0) is lower than Super Double Bonus Poker (28.5), providing a smoother **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 60% overlap on the core hand results. However, in Five-Deck Poker, you are looking for Straights and Flushes much more frequently because you have five independent decks to draw from. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the connectivity in Five-Deck Poker dominates the EV landscape.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Multi-Deck Connectivity:** You are looking for Straights and Flushes much more frequently.
2.  **High Pair Priority:** In Five-Deck Poker, you are looking for natural high pairs that can hit across multiple decks.
3.  **Bonus Removal:** You no longer need to manage a high-bonus four-of-a-kind payout. Your strategy is hand-by-hand.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that Super Double Bonus Poker offers a player advantage in its full-pay version, but Five-Deck Poker provides a smoother ride with lower variance. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are top-tier choices for anyone looking for a player advantage.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Super Double Bonus to Royal Flush Bonus
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Super Double Bonus** to **Royal Flush Bonus** is a shift from a "High Bonus" natural variant to a "Targeted Jackpot" natural variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your hands and the potential for a Royal Flush jackpot.

### The Comparison Matrix: Super Double Bonus vs. Royal Flush Bonus

| Feature | Super Double Bonus (10/7) | Royal Flush Bonus (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.17% | 99.80% |
| **Variance Index** | 28.5 | 32.0 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target High-Payout Natural Quads | Target Royal Flush Jackpot |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.17% (Player Edge) | 0.20% |

### Strategic Divergence: Bonus Quads vs. Royal Jackpot

In **Super Double Bonus Poker**, you are looking for natural four-of-a-kind hands that pay a massive bonus. In **Royal Flush Bonus**, the game is natural, but the payout for a Royal Flush is significantly increased. This creates a slightly higher **Volatility Normalization** (32.0 vs 28.5).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 80% overlap on the core hand results. However, in Royal Flush Bonus, you will prioritize Royal Flush cards (A, K, Q, J, 10). This is the essence of **Payout Compression** where the "Top Tier" hands (Royal Flush) are the focus in Royal Flush Bonus.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Royal Flush Priority:** In Royal Flush Bonus, you will break a Full House or a Flush to hold 3-to-a-Royal much more frequently.
2.  **Bonus Devaluation:** In Royal Flush Bonus, four-of-a-kind hands are only valuable for a standard payout. They no longer offer a special bonus.
3.  **Natural Connectivity:** In Royal Flush Bonus, you are looking for natural straights and flushes.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games are highly rewarding for the expert player, but Super Double Bonus Poker offers a player advantage in its full-pay version. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are excellent choices for the disciplined player.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Super Double Bonus to Triple Aces
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Super Double Bonus** to **Triple Aces** is a shift from one "High Bonus" natural variant to a "Targeted Bonus" variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value specific card ranks and their related bonuses.

### The Comparison Matrix: Super Double Bonus vs. Triple Aces

| Feature | Super Double Bonus (10/7) | Triple Aces (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.17% | 99.60% |
| **Variance Index** | 28.5 | 25.0 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target High-Payout Natural Quads | Target Aces Bonus |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.17% (Player Edge) | 0.40% |

### Strategic Divergence: Broad Bonus Quads vs. Ace Jackpot

In **Super Double Bonus Poker**, all four-of-a-kind hands are "Doubled" compared to standard Jacks or Better. In **Triple Aces Poker**, the focus is specifically on achieving Three or Four Aces. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through specific high-tier combinations. Triple Aces' variance (25.0) is lower than Super Double Bonus Poker (28.5), providing a smoother **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 80% overlap on the core hand results. However, in Triple Aces, you will prioritize Aces above any other natural card. This is the essence of **Payout Compression** in Ace-based games.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Ace Priority:** In both games, Aces are the highest-paying quads. You prioritize them over almost any other card rank.
2.  **Broad Quad Devaluation:** In Triple Aces, other quad tiers are only valuable for a standard payout. They no longer offer a special bonus.
3.  **Full House Break:** You will break a Full House containing three Aces to draw for the fourth Ace in both games.

A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are solid games for the expert player, offering competitive returns with unique gameplay. For players who enjoy the Ace focus of Super Double Bonus, Triple Aces Poker is a fun, high-action alternative for **EV Maximization**.
