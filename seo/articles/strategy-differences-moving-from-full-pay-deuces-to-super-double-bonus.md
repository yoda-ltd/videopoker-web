# Strategy Differences: Moving from Full Pay Deuces to Super Double Bonus

Transitioning from a Deuces-based game like Full Pay Deuces Wild (FPDW) to a Jacks-based variant like Super Double Bonus (SDB) requires more than just a change in mindset—it requires a total overhaul of your mathematical approach. This **best video poker strategy comparison** highlights the critical **Strategy Bifurcation** that occurs when moving between wild card and non-wild card environments.

## The Core Mathematical Shift

In Full Pay Deuces Wild, the "Deuce" is the engine of the game. Your **video poker house edge analysis** is built around the frequency of wild cards, which make up roughly 7.7% of the deck. In FPDW, a significant portion of your return comes from hands that simply don't exist or aren't prioritized in SDB, such as "Four Deuces" or "Wild Royals."

When you move to Super Double Bonus, you enter a world of **Payout Compression** on the mid-tier hands (like Flushes and Straights) to pay for massive bonuses on Four of a Kind (Quads). SDB is famous for its "nested" bonus structure, where 4 Aces pay more than 4 Kings, which pay more than 4 Jacks.

## Strategy Bifurcation: From Survival to Hunting

1.  **Low Pair Priority:** In FPDW, you never hold a single high card, and you rarely hold a small pair if a Deuce is present. In SDB, "Jacks or Better" is the baseline. You must hold low pairs to chase Quads, but you also must respect the high-card threshold for "money back" returns.
2.  **The Flush/Straight Gap:** In Deuces Wild, you rarely draw to inside straights or flushes unless they are part of a wild card combination. In SDB, the **EV Gap** for flushes is narrower, making them more viable targets depending on the specific pay table (e.g., 9/5 vs 8/5).
3.  **Variance Scaling:** FPDW is actually a relatively "smooth" game if you find a true 100.76% table. SDB, however, has much higher **Variance Scaling**. You will experience much longer losing streaks in SDB because you are waiting for specific Quads to "even out" the math.

## Bankroll Considerations

A player moving from Deuces to SDB must increase their bankroll buffer by at least 50% to handle the increased volatility. The "churn" of small wins in Deuces keeps you in the game; the "jackpot" nature of SDB requires you to survive until the Aces or Jacks hit. Understanding these **strategy differences** is the key to maintaining your edge when switching variants.
