# Video Poker Host Etiquette: Negotiating Back-End Comps

In the world of high-limit video poker, the player's card is only the first step. The real "Comp Hustle" happens in the host's office. Understanding **Video Poker Host Etiquette** and how to negotiate "Back-End Comps" is essential for **how to get free rooms in Las Vegas video poker** experts.

## 1. What is a Casino Host?

A host is a casino employee tasked with building relationships with high-value players. Their job is to ensure you keep coming back. They have the power to "wipe" your bill (discretionary comps) based on your **ADT (Average Daily Theoretical)**.

## 2. When do you need a Host?

You don't need a host if you are a $0.25 player. However, if you are playing $1, $5, or $10 denominations, you are a "Host-Level" player.

*   **The Trigger:** Most casinos require a certain amount of coin-in per trip (e.g., $10,000 to $25,000) before a host will "look" at your account.
*   **The First Meeting:** Do not wait for them to find you. Visit the host office on your first day and introduce yourself. Ask, "What is the requirement for a room comp at this level?"

## 3. The Art of the "Back-End" Negotiation

"Back-End Comps" are credits applied to your bill *after* you have finished playing, but before you check out.

*   **The Ask:** On your final morning, call or visit your host. Say: "I've had a great trip and put in some good volume. Is there anything you can do for my room charges?"
*   **The Math:** A host will generally look at your "Theo" (Theoretical Loss). If you played $50,000 coin-in on a 2% rated machine, your Theo is $1,000. Most hosts can give back 30-40% of that ($300-$400) toward your food, spa, or show tickets.
*   **Actual vs. Theo:** A good host cares about **Theoretical Loss Optimization**. Even if you *won* money, they may still comp your bill because your "Theo" was high.

## 4. Host Etiquette: The Dos and Don'ts

To maintain **best video poker rewards strategy** continuity, you must treat your host with respect.

*   **DO: Tip your host.** While they cannot accept cash in many jurisdictions, a gift card or a nice bottle of wine (where legal) goes a long way.
*   **DO: Be honest about your play.** If you plan to play less this trip, tell them. They appreciate the heads-up.
*   **DON'T: Be a "Comp Leecher."** If you ask for everything and give nothing back in volume, you will be "fired" by your host.
*   **DON'T: Forget the "Zero-Play Day."** If your host sees a day of $0 play on your account, they cannot help you. It tanks your **ADT Scoping** and makes their job impossible.
