# The History and Evolution of Aces and Faces Video Poker

Aces and Faces is one of the "Founding Fathers" of the Bonus Poker movement. Developed in the early days of the digital casino revolution, it sought to simplify the "Bonus" concept by making all "High Cards" (Aces, Kings, Queens, and Jacks) the heroes of the game.

## The "Face Card" Focus

In the early 1990s, most video poker machines were standard Jacks or Better. Aces and Faces was developed as a way to "Upgrade" the experience. The idea was simple: If you get Four of a Kind using "Face Cards," you get a bonus payout.

This was a massive hit with players who enjoyed the "Power" of the high cards. It allowed them to play a game that felt like Jacks or Better but with the added excitement of "Jackpot" potential on every high pair.

## Evolutionary Progression

Aces and Faces underwent several iterations as it moved from physical cabinets to the internet:

1.  **Microgaming's Influence:** Aces and Faces became a cornerstone of the early online casino world, with companies like Microgaming and Playtech creating iconic versions of the game.
2.  **Tiered Payouts:** The game evolved to have two distinct bonus tiers:
    *   **Tier 1:** Four Aces (The Top Bonus).
    *   **Tier 2:** Four Kings, Queens, or Jacks (The "Face Card" Bonus).
3.  **Low Card "Stability":** Unlike Double Double Bonus, which aggressively cuts the payouts for 2s through 10s, Aces and Faces maintained a relatively stable pay table for low quads, making it a "Friendly" bonus game.

## Video Poker Terms Every Player Should Know

When searching the complete video poker glossary, these terms are vital for Aces and Faces:

*   **Bankroll:** This game is "Lower Variance" than most bonus games. You can play it with a moderate bankroll and still expect a long session.
*   **Full Pay:** The classic Full Pay version of Aces and Faces offers a return of 99.26% (8/5 table).
*   **Action:** Because high cards are so important, the game feels "Active." You are constantly holding cards that have the potential to hit a bonus.

## Strategy and "High Card Mastery"

The evolution of Aces and Faces created a specific "High Card" strategy. Players learned to never discard a Face Card or an Ace if they had a pair, and they became more aggressive in holding single high cards compared to the standard Jacks or Better strategy.

## Modern Status

Today, Aces and Faces is the "Standard" bonus game for many European and International casinos. While American players often prefer "Double Double Bonus," international markets value the stability and "High Card" focus of Aces and Faces.

The history of Aces and Faces is a reminder that simplicity is often the key to longevity. By focusing on the cards that players already love, it became a permanent fixture in the global "Complete Video Poker Glossary."
