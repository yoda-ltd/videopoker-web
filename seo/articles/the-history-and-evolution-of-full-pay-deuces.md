# The History and Evolution of Full Pay Deuces Wild

Deuces Wild is arguably the second most popular video poker game in history, and its "Full Pay" version (FPDW) is the holy grail for professional players. Understanding its history is a lesson in the battle of wits between casinos and "advantage players."

## The Birth of the "Wild" Card

Video poker began with "Jacks or Better," but by the early 1980s, developers realized that adding wild cards could create a much more exciting, fast-paced game. Deuces Wild was the result. By making all four 2s (Deuces) wild, the game transformed from a game of "pairs and triplets" into a game of "straights, flushes, and quads."

## The Legend of "Full Pay" (FPDW)

The term "Full Pay" refers to the specific pay table that offers a theoretical return of **100.76%** with perfect strategy. This means that, over the long run, the player actually has a mathematical edge over the house—a rarity in the casino world.

### The 103/25/15/9/5/3/2 Pay Table
The classic Full Pay Deuces Wild pay table is identified by these payouts:
*   **Natural Royal Flush:** 800 (per coin, at max bet)
*   **Four Deuces:** 200
*   **Wild Royal Flush:** 25
*   **Five of a Kind:** 15
*   **Straight Flush:** 9
*   **Four of a Kind:** 5
*   **Full House:** 3
*   **Flush:** 2

## The Great "FPDW" Migration

In the 1990s and early 2000s, Full Pay Deuces Wild was common in Las Vegas, particularly in "locals" casinos. However, as information about the player's edge spread through books and early internet forums, casinos began "tightening" the machines.

This led to the "evolution" of Short Pay versions:
*   **"Illinois Deuces":** A slightly lower return version (98.9%).
*   **"Not-So-Ugly" Deuces (NSUD):** A version that still offers around 99.7%, making it a favorite for modern players.

## Essential Terms for Deuces Wild Players

To navigate the world of wild cards, here are video poker terms every player should know:

*   **Expected Value (EV):** In FPDW, the EV is positive (>100%), which is why these machines are often capped at lower denominations.
*   **Variance:** Deuces Wild has very high variance. You can go hours without seeing a Deuce, or hit Four Deuces twice in ten minutes.
*   **Bankroll:** Because of the volatility, a significant bankroll is required to survive the "dry spells" between Four Deuce hits.

## Legacy

Full Pay Deuces Wild remains the benchmark for video poker excellence. While it is increasingly difficult to find in modern casinos, its existence continues to drive players to study strategy and search the "complete video poker glossary" for any advantage they can find. It is the ultimate "Player vs. House" showdown.
