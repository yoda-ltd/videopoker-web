# Strategy Differences: Moving from Triple Bonus Plus to Full Pay Deuces
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Triple Bonus Plus** to **Full Pay Deuces** is a shift from a "High Bonus" natural variant to a "Multi-Wild" variant. This represents a significant **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your hands versus the wild card features.

### The Comparison Matrix: Triple Bonus Plus vs. Full Pay Deuces

| Feature | Triple Bonus Plus (Full Pay) | Full Pay Deuces (100.76%) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.58% | 100.76% |
| **Variance Index** | 35.0 | 25.8 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target High-Payout Bonus Quads | Target Deuce-Based Wilds |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.42% | -0.76% (Player Edge) |

### Strategic Divergence: Triple Bonus Quads vs. Wild Deuces

In **Triple Bonus Plus**, the payouts for all four-of-a-kind hands are significantly increased (tripled) compared to standard games. In **Full Pay Deuces**, the four 2s are your most powerful tools, and you are hunting for the Four Deuces jackpot. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through wild card connectivity. Full Pay Deuces' variance (25.8) is lower than Triple Bonus Plus (35.0), providing a smoother **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is minimal (less than 10%). In Triple Bonus Plus, you would never discard an Ace. In Full Pay Deuces, you break almost every natural winner to hunt for Deuces. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the 2s in Full Pay Deuces dominate the EV landscape even more than the bonus quads in Triple Bonus Plus.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Deuce Obsession:** Every Deuce is a keeper. Three Deuces is a massive hold, even if you have a "Natural" winner.
2.  **Bonus Devaluation:** In Full Pay Deuces, Aces and other high-rank cards are only valuable for a high pair or as part of a Deuce-heavy draw. They no longer offer a special bonus.
3.  **Connectivity Strategy:** In Full Pay Deuces, you are looking for wild card connectivity to complete straights and flushes.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games are highly rewarding for the expert player, but Full Pay Deuces offers a significant player advantage in its full-pay version. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are top-tier choices for anyone looking for a player advantage.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Triple Bonus Plus to Super Double Bonus
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Triple Bonus Plus** to **Super Double Bonus** is a shift from one "High Bonus" natural variant to another. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value specific card ranks and their related bonuses.

### The Comparison Matrix: Triple Bonus Plus vs. Super Double Bonus

| Feature | Triple Bonus Plus (Full Pay) | Super Double Bonus (10/7) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.58% | 100.17% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 35.0 | 28.5 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target High-Payout Bonus Quads | Target Specific High-Payout Quads |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.42% | -0.17% (Player Edge) |

### Strategic Divergence: Triple Bonus vs. Super Double Bonus

In **Triple Bonus Plus**, all four-of-a-kind hands pay a significant bonus. In **Super Double Bonus Poker**, the payouts are tiered: Four Aces pay more than Four 2s-4s, which pay more than Four 5s-Ks. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through tiered quads. Super Double Bonus Poker's variance (28.5) is lower than Triple Bonus Plus (35.0), providing a smoother **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is high strategy overlap (approximately 80%) because both are 52-card natural games. However, in Super Double Bonus, you will prioritize Aces and other high-bonus quads more than in Triple Bonus Plus. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the high-tier hands in Super Double Bonus dominate the EV landscape.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Tiered Quad Awareness:** In Super Double Bonus, you must be aware of the bonus payouts for all quad tiers.
2.  **Ace Priority:** In both games, Aces are the highest-paying quads. You prioritize them over almost any other card rank.
3.  **Full House Break:** You will break a Full House containing three Aces to draw for the fourth Ace in both games. This is a common **EV Maximization** play for high-bonus variants.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games are excellent for the expert player, but Super Double Bonus Poker offers a player advantage in its full-pay version. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are excellent choices for the disciplined player.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Triple Bonus Plus to Triple Aces
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Triple Bonus Plus** to **Triple Aces** is a shift from a "Broad Bonus" natural variant to a "Targeted Bonus" variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value specific card ranks and their related bonuses.

### The Comparison Matrix: Triple Bonus Plus vs. Triple Aces

| Feature | Triple Bonus Plus (Full Pay) | Triple Aces (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.58% | 99.60% |
| **Variance Index** | 35.0 | 25.0 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target High-Payout Bonus Quads | Target Aces Bonus |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.42% | 0.40% |

### Strategic Divergence: Broad Bonus Quads vs. Ace Jackpot

In **Triple Bonus Plus**, all four-of-a-kind hands pay a significant bonus. In **Triple Aces Poker**, the focus is specifically on achieving Three or Four Aces. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through specific high-tier combinations. Triple Aces' variance (25.0) is much lower than Triple Bonus Plus (35.0), providing a very stable **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 80% overlap on the core hand results. However, in Triple Aces, you will prioritize Aces above any other natural card. This is the essence of **Payout Compression** in Ace-based games.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Ace Priority:** In both games, Aces are the highest-paying quads. You prioritize them over almost any other card rank.
2.  **Broad Quad Devaluation:** In Triple Aces, other quad tiers are only valuable for a standard payout. They no longer offer a special bonus.
3.  **Natural Connectivity:** In both games, you are looking for natural straights and flushes.

A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are solid games for the expert player, offering competitive returns with unique gameplay. For players who enjoy the high-action strategy of Triple Bonus Plus, Triple Aces Poker is a fun, targeted alternative for **EV Maximization**.
