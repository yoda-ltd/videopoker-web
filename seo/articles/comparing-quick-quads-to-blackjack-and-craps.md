# Comparing Quick Quads to Blackjack and Craps

Quick Quads is a unique video poker variant where a 3-of-a-kind plus two other cards that sum to the same rank counts as a 4-of-a-kind. But how does this innovative game compare to the classic table games?

### RTP and the Quick Quads Feature
- **Quick Quads**: The **Theoretical Return** depends on the base game plus the added value of the quick-quads feature. A good machine can offer 99% or higher.
- **Blackjack**: Offers a 99.5% RTP (0.5% house edge) with perfect basic strategy.
- **Craps (Pass Line)**: Offers a 98.59% RTP (1.41% house edge).

While Blackjack still holds a slight edge on base RTP, Quick Quads provides a much more mentally engaging experience and the chance for a 4-of-a-kind on many more hands.

### Variance and Sustainability
Quick Quads has higher **Variance** than both Blackjack and Craps because you have to place an extra bet (usually 6 coins instead of 5) to activate the quick-quads feature. To achieve **Session Sustainability**, you need a larger bankroll for **Variance Buffering** than you would for a standard game of Blackjack. Without the extra bankroll, the cost of the 6th coin can be punishing.

### "Is Max Bet Always Better for Bankroll Longevity?"
In Quick Quads, you must play the 6th coin to get the advertised RTP. If you only play 5 coins, you are playing a standard game on a machine with a lower base pay table. For those looking for **video poker tips for beginners**, our advice is to only play Quick Quads if you can afford the 6th coin. Without it, you're better off playing Craps.

### Skill and Decision Making
Unlike Blackjack, where you must make the right decision on every hand, Quick Quads requires a unique type of **Hit Frequency Optimization** that involves summing the cards to see if you can make a quad. This is more mentally challenging than Blackjack and significantly more complex than the decisions in Craps.

### RNG vs. Physical Odds
While Craps relies on physical dice and Blackjack on a physical or shuffled deck, Quick Quads uses **Pseudo-Randomness** through an RNG. This ensures that every hand is fair and independent, just like the roll of the dice in a certified game of Craps.

If you want to know **how to win video poker daily**, understanding these comparisons is vital. Quick Quads offers a great balance of risk and reward for those who enjoy a mental challenge.
