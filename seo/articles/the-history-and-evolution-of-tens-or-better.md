# The History and Evolution of Tens or Better

Tens or Better is the "Friendliest" game in the history of video poker. It represents a brief but important moment in the early 1980s when developers were trying to find the "Sweet Spot" for player retention—making a game that was almost impossible to lose quickly.

## The "Low-Threshold" Concept

Introduced by IGT as a direct "Softer" alternative to Jacks or Better, Tens or Better sought to lower the "Barrier to Winning." In standard video poker, you need a pair of Jacks to get your money back. In Tens or Better, you only need a pair of 10s.

This simple evolution changed the "Psychology" of the game. Players felt like they were "Winning" more often, which led to longer "Time on Device," a key metric for casino success.

## How the Game Evolved

Tens or Better evolved from a "Starter Game" into a "Stable Classic" through several key phases:

1.  **The "High-Frequency" Standard:** By adding two more "Winning" cards ( the two 10s), the frequency of winning hands jumped significantly. This made Tens or Better the "Low-Volatility" king of the early video poker floor.
2.  **The "6/5" Pay Table:** To balance the higher frequency of winning hands, casinos had to reduce the payouts for the "Full House" and "Flush." The classic Full Pay version of Tens or Better is the "6/5" table, which offers a return of 99.14%.
3.  **Digital Integration:** Tens or Better became a favorite for early "Hand-held" video poker games and home computer simulations because it provided a more "Rewarding" experience for casual players who weren't masters of strategy.

## Essential Video Poker Terminology

For players exploring the "Friendly" side of the floor, these are the video poker terms every player should know:

*   **Bankroll Management:** Tens or Better is the "Safest" game for a small bankroll. Its "Low Variance" means your credits will last longer than in almost any other video poker variant.
*   **Variance:** This is the "Lowest Variance" game on the floor. It is designed for steady, consistent play.
*   **Expected Value (EV):** The strategy for Tens or Better is nearly identical to Jacks or Better, but with one obvious change: You "Hold" a pair of 10s. This makes it the easiest game to learn for a beginner.

## Strategy and "The Beginner's Path"

The evolution of Tens or Better created a "Foundation" for the industry. Many of today's "High-Limit" professionals started their careers playing Tens or Better at a 5-cent or 25-cent machine, learning the basic "Rhythm" of the game without the fear of a rapid "Bust Out."

## Legacy and Modern Status

Today, Tens or Better is a "Legacy" game. While it is less common on the main floors of modern "Mega-Resorts" (who prefer the higher-margin "Double Double Bonus"), it remains a staple on online "Micro-Gaming" sites and in older "Downtown" Las Vegas casinos.

The history of Tens or Better is a reminder that in the world of video poker, "Winning is Fun." By making it just a little bit easier to "Get Your Money Back," developers created a "Friendly" classic that remains a cornerstone of the "Complete Video Poker Glossary."
