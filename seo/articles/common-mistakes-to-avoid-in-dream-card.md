# Common Mistakes to Avoid in Dream Card Video Poker

Dream Card is a high-stakes, high-action variant of video poker that offers a "Dream Card" (a card of your choice) on approximately 25% of your hands. While the **Theoretical Return** can be incredibly high, the cost and complexity lead to frequent mistakes.

### The Cost of the Dream
The most common mistake in Dream Card is failing to account for the extra bet. To activate the Dream Card feature, you must bet 10 coins per line instead of the usual 5. If you don't have the bankroll for this, you should not be playing. This extra cost significantly increases the **Variance**, requiring much more substantial **Variance Buffering** for **Session Sustainability**.

### Misusing the Dream Card
When the Dream Card appears, you get to choose exactly what it is. Beginners often make the mistake of choosing a card to complete a low-paying hand (like a Flush) when they should be aiming for a high-paying hand (like Four-of-a-Kind or a Royal Flush). **Hit Frequency Optimization** in Dream Card is all about maximizing the potential of that one free card.

### "Is Max Bet Always Better for Bankroll Longevity?"
In Dream Card, there is no "max bet" in the traditional sense—there is only the 10-coin bet or a losing bet. If you play only 5 coins, you are playing a standard game but on a machine often programmed with a worse base pay table. For those asking **how to win video poker daily**, the answer in Dream Card is: 10 coins or nothing.

### Strategy Confusion
Because you are often starting with a guaranteed "good" card, the standard strategy charts for Jacks or Better or Deuces Wild don't apply. Using the wrong strategy will tank your RTP. If you are looking for **video poker tips for beginners**, our number one tip for Dream Card is to use a specific Dream Card strategy app.

### Pseudo-Randomness vs. True Randomness
Some players believe the Dream Card is "rigged" to only appear on low-paying hands. In reality, the RNG determines when the Dream Card appears using **Pseudo-Randomness** that is just as fair as the dealing of the cards themselves. The frequency (roughly 1 in 4 hands) is a mathematical constant, not a variable controlled by the casino to spite you.

Dream Card is a game for the serious player with a large bankroll. Avoid these mistakes, and the "dream" can become a reality.
