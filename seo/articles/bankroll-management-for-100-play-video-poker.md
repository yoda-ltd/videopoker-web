# Bankroll Management for 100-Play Video Poker: Managing Combinatorial Volatility

100-Play video poker is the ultimate test of a professional's financial discipline. While the underlying math of the game remains the same, the **Combinatorial Volatility** of playing 100 hands simultaneously creates a unique bankroll challenge. To survive the "century grind," you must master the **best video poker for high volume** techniques, specifically addressing the **Shared-Base Variance** that defines multi-hand play.

## The Math of 100-Play: Understanding Variance Aggregation

The biggest misconception in multi-hand video poker is that playing more hands "smooths out" the variance. In reality, while it speeds up the "Long Run," it increases the **Standard Deviation per Deal** through a phenomenon known as **Multi-Hand Volatility Scaling**.

### 1. The Shared Base Hand Effect
In 100-Play, your variance is heavily "front-loaded" on the initial deal. This creates a mathematical correlation across all hands.
- **Correlation:** If you are dealt a "Pat" hand (like a Flush or Full House), your variance for that deal is zero—you win on all 100 lines. This is **Positive Covariance**.
- **Independent Draws:** If you are dealt a "Draw" hand (like 4 to a Royal), the 100 **Independent Draw Randomness** events provide a "Smoothing Effect." You have 100 independent chances to hit the big one. While the result is binary (hit or miss) for each hand, the aggregate outcome across 100 hands typically converges toward the mean of the draw's probability.

### 2. Combinatorial Convergence
The **Variance Index** for 100-Play is not simply the single-play index times 100. Because the initial five cards are identical for all hands, the outcomes are not fully independent.
- For Jacks or Better (19.5 index), the 100-play variance per deal is significantly higher than single-play, but the variance *per coin wagered* actually decreases slightly as N (number of hands) increases, due to **Combinatorial Convergence**.
- To maintain a **Risk of Ruin (RoR)** below 1%, you need a bankroll that accounts for the "cluster" risk of multiple bad base hands in a row.

## Bankroll Requirements: Jacks or Better vs. Triple Double Bonus

When selecting the **best video poker for high volume**, the game's intrinsic volatility determines your survival requirements.

### 100-Play Jacks or Better (JoB)
- **Variance Index:** ~19.5 (Single) -> ~1,600 (100-Play per Deal).
- **Bankroll Requirement:** A professional "safety" bankroll for 100-play JoB should be approximately **3,000 to 4,000 Royal Flush Equivalents (RFEs)**. 
- **The Grind:** JoB is the preferred choice for high-volume grinders because the high frequency of Two Pair and Jacks-or-Better payouts provides a constant "rebate" on the 100-coin-per-line bet.

### 100-Play Triple Double Bonus (TDB)
- **Variance Index:** ~100 (Single) -> ~8,500+ (100-Play per Deal).
- **Bankroll Requirement:** For TDB, the volatility is extreme. You need a bankroll at least **3 to 4 times larger** than JoB.
- **The Risk:** In TDB, much of the RTP is concentrated in Four-of-a-Kind with a Kicker. If you go 500 deals without a "Four-of-a-Kind" Base Hand, your 100-play bankroll will vanish at an alarming rate. This is where **Multi-Hand Volatility Scaling** becomes a double-edged sword.

## Multi Hand Video Poker Strategy and Exposure

Applying a professional **multi hand video poker strategy** requires a shift in perspective. You are not betting 5 coins; you are betting **500 coins** per "game cycle."

- **Bet Sizing:** If your total "Life-Of-Bankroll" is $100,000, playing $0.25 denomination ($125 per deal) represents a standard risk. However, many players fail to realize that $1.00 denomination ($500 per deal) on a 100-play machine is the equivalent of "High Roller" status on a single-line machine.
- **The Smoothing Effect in Practice:** While the "Shared Base Hand" creates spikes, the **Independent Draw Randomness** means that over a 4-hour session, you will see your results for common draws (like holding a Low Pair) move very close to the expected 12% Three-of-a-Kind hit rate.

100-Play video poker is a high-speed engine for generating EV, but it requires a massive "fuel tank."
