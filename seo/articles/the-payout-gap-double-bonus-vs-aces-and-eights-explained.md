# The Payout Gap: Double Bonus vs. Aces and Eights Explained
Category: Comparison

Understanding the **Strategic Divergence** between **Double Bonus** and **Aces and Eights** is key to mastering these high-payout variants. This comparison focuses on how the paytables differ and why those differences lead to unique **EV Maximization** paths.

### The Comparison Matrix: Double Bonus vs. Aces and Eights

| Feature | Double Bonus (10/7) | Aces and Eights (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.17% (Full Pay) | 99.78% |
| **Variance Index** | 28.5 | 21.8 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | High-Payout Quad Bonuses | Targeted Quad Bonuses (Aces/8s/7s) |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.17% (Player Edge) | 0.22% |

### The Bonus Gap: Broad vs. Targeted

In **Double Bonus Poker**, almost all four-of-a-kind hands are "Doubled" compared to standard games, with tiered payouts for different ranks. In **Aces and Eights**, the rewards are tiered specifically for Aces, 8s, and 7s. This leads to a higher **Volatility Normalization** (28.5 vs 21.8) in Double Bonus.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is high strategy overlap (approximately 80%) because both are based on a 52-card natural deck. However, in Double Bonus, you prioritize Aces and other high-bonus quads much more than in Aces and Eights. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the high-tier quads in Double Bonus dominate the EV landscape.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Ace Priority:** In Double Bonus, Aces are the highest-paying quads. You prioritize them over almost any other card rank.
2.  **Face Card Focus:** In Double Bonus, Kings, Queens, and Jacks are building blocks for a bonus payout. In Aces and Eights, they are simply "high cards."
3.  **Return Priority:** Double Bonus offers a superior theoretical return (100.17%), making it the better choice for the expert player.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that Double Bonus Poker is the superior game for the expert player seeking an edge, while Aces and Eights is better for those who want a stable return. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are elite choices for those who master their respective strategies.

# The Payout Gap: Double Bonus vs. Aces and Faces Explained
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Double Bonus** to **Aces and Faces** is a shift from one "High Bonus" variant to another, but with a different ranking focus. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your hands and the volatility that comes with them.

### The Comparison Matrix: Double Bonus vs. Aces and Faces

| Feature | Double Bonus (10/7) | Aces and Faces (8/5) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.17% | 99.26% |
| **Variance Index** | 28.5 | 22.1 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | High-Payout Quad Bonuses | Face Card Quad Bonuses |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.17% (Player Edge) | 0.74% |

### The Rank Gap: Broad vs. Faces

In **Double Bonus**, the rewards are extended across all quad tiers. In **Aces and Faces**, the rewards are concentrated on Achieving four-of-a-kind with face cards. This leads to a lower **Volatility Normalization** (22.1 vs 28.5).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is high strategy overlap (approximately 80%). However, in Aces and Faces, you prioritize face cards much more than in Double Bonus. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the face cards in Aces and Faces dominate the EV landscape for middle-tier wins.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Face Card Priority:** In Aces and Faces, prioritize Kings, Queens, and Jacks much more than in Double Bonus.
2.  **Ace Priority:** In both games, Aces are the highest-paying quads.
3.  **Return Priority:** Double Bonus offers a superior theoretical return (100.17%), making it the better choice for the advantage player.

A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that Double Bonus is a superior choice for the expert player who wants a high return with an edge. The **best video poker strategy comparison** highlights that high-payout quads are the ultimate tool for **EV Maximization** in Double Bonus.

# The Payout Gap: Double Bonus vs. Triple Double Bonus Explained
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Double Bonus** to **Triple Double Bonus** involves moving from a "Standard High Bonus" variant to a "Kicker-Based Jackpot" variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you approach **Volatility Normalization**.

### The Comparison Matrix: Double Bonus vs. Triple Double Bonus

| Feature | Double Bonus (10/7) | Triple Double Bonus (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.17% | 99.58% |
| **Variance Index** | 28.5 | 98.0 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | High-Payout Quad Bonuses | Kicker-Based Quad Jackpots |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.17% (Player Edge) | 0.42% |

### The Kicker Gap: Natural vs. Kicker Jackpot

In **Double Bonus**, the reward is concentrated in Achieving natural quads. In **Triple Double Bonus Poker**, the reward for Four Aces with a kicker is 4,000 credits—the same as a Royal Flush! This creates extreme **Volatility Normalization** (98.0 vs 28.5).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 70% overlap. In Double Bonus, you would never hold a kicker with a pair. In Triple Double Bonus, you prioritize kicker combinations above all else, often breaking winners to maximize the kicker potential. This is the essence of **EV Maximization** in high-variance kicker games.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Kicker Awareness:** You must learn which cards are "Kickers" for each quad tier.
2.  **Ace-Kicker Priority:** Holding a pair of Aces with a low kicker is a valid strategy in Triple Double Bonus.
3.  **Variance Management:** Triple Double Bonus is much more "swingy" than Double Bonus. You need a massive bankroll to withstand the swings.

Through a detailed **video poker house edge analysis**, it's clear that while Triple Double Bonus offers exciting jackpot potential, Double Bonus is the more reliable choice for the advantage player. The **best video poker strategy comparison** for this transition is: Use the natural quads for the edge, or hunt the kickers for the jackpot.
