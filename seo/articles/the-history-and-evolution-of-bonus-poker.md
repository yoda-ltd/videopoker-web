# The History and Evolution of Bonus Poker

Bonus Poker is widely considered the "Father" of all modern video poker variants. It was the first game to successfully challenge the dominance of "Jacks or Better," proving that players were willing to accept slightly lower payouts on common hands in exchange for a "Bonus" on rare ones.

## The First "Bonus" Experiment

Introduced in the early 1980s by IGT (International Game Technology), Bonus Poker was a revolutionary response to the growing popularity of digital casino games. At the time, every video poker machine was a "Jacks or Better" clone.

The primary evolution was the introduction of **Tiered Four of a Kind Payouts**. By making Four Aces pay more than other quads, developers created a game that felt more like "Real" poker, where Aces are the most powerful cards in the deck.

## How the Game Evolved

Bonus Poker evolved from a single experimental machine into a global standard through several key phases:

1.  **The "Aces" Era:** Originally, the only "Bonus" was for Four Aces. This was later expanded to include a secondary bonus for Four 2s, 3s, and 4s.
2.  **The "8/5" Standard:** As the game's popularity grew, casinos settled on the "8/5" pay table (8 for a Full House, 5 for a Flush) as the "Full Pay" version of Bonus Poker, offering a healthy return of 99.17%.
3.  **Multi-Hand Integration:** With the advent of the 21st century, Bonus Poker became the "baseline" game for Triple-Play and Five-Play machines, as its balanced volatility made it perfect for multi-hand play.

## Essential Video Poker Terminology

When consulting the complete video poker glossary, these are the video poker terms every player should know for Bonus Poker:

*   **Bankroll:** Bonus Poker is a "Low-to-Medium" variance game. It is the perfect entry point for players moving up from Jacks or Better.
*   **Action:** Because the bonus quads occur relatively frequently, the game provides consistent "Action" and positive reinforcement for the player.
*   **Expected Value (EV):** The strategy for Bonus Poker is very similar to Jacks or Better, but with a slight "tilt" toward holding Aces. This makes it an easy game to master for professional players.

## Strategy and the "Balanced" Approach

The evolution of Bonus Poker created the "Balanced" approach to strategy. Unlike "Triple Double Bonus" where you are constantly "Gambling" for a big hit, Bonus Poker strategy is about "Stability." You still get the excitement of the 400-credit Ace win, but the rest of the pay table is "Fair" enough to keep you in the game for hours.

## Legacy of the "Original Bonus"

Every "Bonus" game you see on a casino floor today—from "Double Double Bonus" to "Super Aces"—owes its existence to the original Bonus Poker. It was the game that proved video poker could be more than just a 52-card simulation; it could be a "Feature-Rich" gaming experience.

Today, Bonus Poker remains one of the most played games in the world. It is the "Jacks or Better" of the modern era—a reliable, fair, and exciting game that remains a cornerstone of the "Complete Video Poker Glossary."
