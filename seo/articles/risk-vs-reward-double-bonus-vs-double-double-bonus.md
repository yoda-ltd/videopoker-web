# Double Bonus vs. Double Double Bonus: Strategy Overlap and Hand-Ranking Priorities

In the specialized world of video poker, the comparison between **Double Bonus (DB)** and **Double Double Bonus (DDB)** is a fascinating study in **Strategy Convergence** and **Optimal Hold Variance**. While both games are "Bonus" variants that pay extra for specific 4-of-a-kind hands, they distribute their return-to-player (RTP) and prioritize their "Kicker" bonuses differently. Understanding the **Hand-Ranking Inversion** between these two is essential for anyone looking for the **best video poker strategy comparison** and learning **how to play video poker optimally**.

## The Core Conflict: 4-of-a-Kind vs. Kicker 4-of-a-Kind

The primary driver of the strategy shift between Double Bonus and Double Double Bonus is the "Kicker" system and the Straight payout. Double Bonus (typically 10/7 or 9/6) is a "top-heavy" game that pays only 1-for-1 for Two Pair, but compensates with 5-for-1 for a Straight and 160-for-1 for 4 Aces. Double Double Bonus (typically 9/6 or 8/5) also pays 1-for-1 for Two Pair, but compensates with massive kicker-dependent payouts for 4 Aces (400-for-1).

| Hand | Double Bonus (9/6) | Double Double Bonus (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Two Pair** | 1-for-1 | 1-for-1 |
| **Straight** | 5-for-1 | 4-for-1 |
| **Flush** | 6-for-1 | 6-for-1 |
| **Full House** | 9-for-1 | 9-for-1 |
| **4 Aces w/ 2,3,4 Kicker** | 160-for-1 | 400-for-1 |

The reduction of the Straight payout from 5-for-1 to 4-for-1 in DDB and the kicker-dependent quads are the single most significant **Strategy Convergence** breakers.

## 1. Strategy Overlap: High Strategy Convergence in Foundations

If you are coming from a Double Bonus background, you can reuse about 70% of your strategy for Double Double Bonus. The **Strategy Convergence** is high because both games pay only 1-for-1 for Two Pair, creating a shared "quad-centric" mindset.

*   **High Pair Priority:** In both games, starting with a pair of Jacks or Better is a foundational hold.
*   **Three of a Kind:** In both games, Three of a Kind is a premium situation, always drawing for the 4th card.
*   **Royal Flush Draws:** The priority of a 4-card Royal Flush remains at the top of the list in both games.

## 2. Hand-Ranking Inversion: The Straight and Kicker Pivot

The real strategy change comes from the bonuses in each game. This causes a **Hand-Ranking Inversion** where certain cards become more valuable than others.

*   **The Straight Pivot:** In Double Bonus, the Straight pays 5-for-1. This is a "win" that sustains your bankroll. In Double Double Bonus, the Straight only pays 4-for-1 (even money). This means it is no longer a "win," but a "push." Because of this, the math often dictates breaking a potential Straight draw in DDB to hold a lone Ace or a pair of Jacks. This is the definition of **Optimal Hold Variance**.
*   **The Kicker Dominance:** In Double Double Bonus, hitting 4 Aces with a 2, 3, or 4 kicker is a massive 400-for-1 win. In Double Bonus, hitting 4 Aces is 160-for-1. This means you will hold onto kicker cards (2, 3, 4) alongside Aces much more aggressively in DDB.

## 3. Penalty Card Impact: The Bonus Hunt

Professional players focus on **Penalty Card Impact** when hunting for those specific quads.

*   **In Double Bonus:** You are looking for Aces. Discarding a card that could have completed a Straight but "penalizes" your Ace pair potential is common.
*   **In Double Double Bonus:** You are looking for Aces and kicker cards (2, 3, 4). Discarding a card that could have completed a Straight but "penalizes" your kicker potential is the rule, not the exception.

## 4. Optimal Hold Variance: Medium vs. High Volatility

Double Double Bonus is significantly more volatile than Double Bonus.

*   **Double Bonus Volatility:** ~28.3 (Medium). Your bankroll will experience moderate swings as you hunt for the 160-for-1 Aces.
*   **Double Double Bonus Volatility:** ~42.0 (High). Your bankroll will experience long "dry spells," punctuated by massive, life-changing wins.

## Conclusion: How to Transition

When moving from Double Bonus to Double Double Bonus, the **best video poker strategy comparison** shows that you must become even more "quad-centric" and "kicker-focused." 

1.  **Devalue the Straight:** In Double Double Bonus, a Straight is just a push. Don't chase inside draws as aggressively as you would in Double Bonus.
2.  **Hunt the Kicker:** In Double Double Bonus, understand that hitting 4 Aces with a 2, 3, or 4 kicker is the primary goal. Don't ignore those kicker cards.
3.  **Bankroll Management:** You need a larger bankroll for Double Double Bonus (at least 2x what you need for Double Bonus) to survive the volatility.

By mastering these **Hand-Ranking Priorities**, you can navigate the shift from the Straight-focused world of Double Bonus to the kicker-driven world of Double Double Bonus and learn **how to play video poker optimally**.
