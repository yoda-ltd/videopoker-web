# Bonus Poker vs. Double Bonus Poker: Strategy Overlap and Hand-Ranking Priorities

For the video poker enthusiast, the choice between **Bonus Poker** and **Double Bonus Poker** is a lesson in extreme **Optimal Hold Variance**. While both games are "Bonus" variants that pay extra for specific 4-of-a-kind hands, they distribute their return-to-player (RTP) and prioritize their "Aces" bonuses differently. Understanding the **Hand-Ranking Inversion** between these two is essential for anyone looking for the **best video poker strategy comparison** and learning **how to play video poker optimally**.

## The Core Conflict: Stability vs. The Bonus Push

The primary driver of the strategy shift between Bonus Poker and Double Bonus is the pay table. Bonus Poker (typically 8/5) is a "balanced" game that pays 2-for-1 for Two Pair and 8-for-1 for a Full House. Double Bonus (typically 10/7 or 9/6) is a "top-heavy" game that pays only 1-for-1 for Two Pair, but compensates with 5-for-1 for a Straight and 160-for-1 for 4 Aces.

| Hand | Bonus Poker (8/5) | Double Bonus (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Two Pair** | 2-for-1 | 1-for-1 |
| **Straight** | 4-for-1 | 5-for-1 |
| **Flush** | 5-for-1 | 6-for-1 |
| **Full House** | 8-for-1 | 9-for-1 |
| **4 Aces** | 80-for-1 | 160-for-1 |

The reduction of the Two Pair payout from 2-for-1 to 1-for-1 in DB is the single most significant **Strategy Convergence** breaker in video poker history.

## 1. Strategy Overlap: High Strategy Convergence in Foundations

If you are coming from a Jacks or Better background, you can reuse about 60% of your strategy for both Bonus Poker and Double Bonus. The **Strategy Convergence** is high because the basic hand rankings remain mostly traditional.

*   **High Pair Priority:** In both games, starting with a pair of Jacks or Better is a foundational hold.
*   **Three of a Kind:** In both games, Three of a Kind is a premium situation, always drawing for the 4th card.
*   **Royal Flush Draws:** The priority of a 4-card Royal Flush remains at the top of the list in both games.

## 2. Hand-Ranking Inversion: The Two Pair and Aces Pivot

The real strategy change comes from the bonuses in each game. This causes a **Hand-Ranking Inversion** where certain cards become more valuable than others.

*   **The Two Pair Pivot:** In Bonus Poker, you ALWAYS hold Two Pair. It pays 2-for-1, which is a "win." In Double Bonus, Two Pair only pays 1-for-1 (even money). This means it is no longer a "win," but a "push." Because of this, the math often dictates breaking Two Pair in DB if it contains a pair of Aces. You hold the pair of Aces and discard the other pair to maximize your chances of hitting 4 Aces (160-for-1). This is the definition of **Optimal Hold Variance**.
*   **The Aces Dominance:** In Double Bonus, hitting 4 Aces is a massive 160-for-1 win. In Bonus Poker, it is 80-for-1. This means you will hold onto a lone Ace much more aggressively in Double Bonus.

## 3. Penalty Card Impact: The Bonus Hunt

Professional players focus on **Penalty Card Impact** when hunting for those specific quads.

*   **In Bonus Poker:** You are looking for Aces and 2-4s. Discarding a card that could have completed a Straight but "penalizes" your Ace pair potential is more common.
*   **In Double Bonus:** You are looking for Aces. Discarding a card that could have completed a Straight but "penalizes" your Ace pair potential is even more common.

## 4. Optimal Hold Variance: Stability vs. Bonus Potential

Double Bonus is significantly more volatile than Bonus Poker.

*   **Bonus Poker Volatility:** ~20.9 (Low-Medium). Your bankroll will experience moderate swings as you hunt for the 80-for-1 Aces and 40-for-1 low quads.
*   **Double Bonus Volatility:** ~28.3 (Medium). Your bankroll will experience more "dry spells," but your wins will be larger.

## Conclusion: How to Transition

When moving from Bonus Poker to Double Bonus, the **best video poker strategy comparison** shows that you must become more "quad-centric." 

1.  **Devalue the Two Pair:** If you have Aces and another pair, keep the Aces and throw away the other pair in DB. The 160-for-1 potential is mathematically superior to the 1-for-1 push.
2.  **Respect the Straight:** In Double Bonus, the 5-for-1 Straight is a significant win. Don't ignore those straight draws.
3.  **Bankroll Management:** You need a larger bankroll for Double Bonus (at least 2x what you need for Bonus Poker) to survive the volatility of the 1-for-1 Two Pair payout.

By mastering these **Hand-Ranking Priorities**, you can navigate the shift from the 2-for-1 Two Pair world of Bonus Poker to the quad-focused world of Double Bonus and learn **how to play video poker optimally**.
