# Bonus Poker Deluxe vs. Quick Quads: House Edge and Strategy Synergy

In the ultimate "best video poker strategy comparison," **Bonus Poker Deluxe vs. Quick Quads** is a battle of bonus quads vs. innovative draws. One game rewards you with a massive bonus for hitting any 4-of-a-kind (Bonus Poker Deluxe); the other introduces a unique "Quick Quads" rule where hitting a 3-of-a-kind and having the other two cards add up to the rank of the 3-of-a-kind counts as a 4-of-a-kind. This creates a massive **Strategy Bifurcation** and alters the fundamental **Payout Weighting** of each game.

## The House Edge Analysis: Bonus Aces vs. Quick Quads

To perform a professional **video poker house edge analysis**, we must examine the "Full Pay" versions of both.

| Game | Full Pay Version | Return (RTP) | House Edge |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bonus Poker Deluxe** | 9/6 (99.64%) | 99.64% | 0.36% |
| **Quick Quads** | 8/5 (99.62%) | 99.62% | 0.38% |

Both games offer nearly identical house edges in their "Full Pay" form, but the **EV Gap** between the two is often closed by how you play the quads. **Bonus Poker Deluxe** is a "pure" bonus game, paying 80-for-1 for *any* 4-of-a-kind. **Quick Quads** pays 400-for-1 for 4 Aces, but significantly less for other quads.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Quad" vs. "Quick Quad" Hunt

The most significant **Strategy Bifurcation** occurs in the way you prioritize 4-of-a-kind.

*   **Bonus Poker Deluxe Strategy:** Because *any* 4-of-a-kind pays 80-for-1, you will aggressively hold *any* pair. If you have a choice between a 4-card Straight and a low pair (2s-10s), the **Optimal Hold** in Bonus Poker Deluxe is always the pair. The goal is to maximize your chances of hitting any 4-of-a-kind.
*   **Quick Quads Strategy:** In Quick Quads, you are hunting for 3-of-a-kind hands and specific "kickers" that add up to the rank of the 3-of-a-kind. This means you will prioritize holding certain low cards (2s-10s) that could complete a "Quick Quad." For example, if you have 8-8-8-5-3, the 5 and 3 add up to 8, making it a 4-of-a-kind. This is a classic example of **Payout Weighting**—the "Quick Quad" payout is so valuable that it overrides the "safe" pair hold.

## Strategy Synergy: Where the Games Align

Despite the different payout structures, there is some **Strategy Synergy** in the "Base Game" logic:

1.  **Royal Flush Priority:** In both games, a 4-card Royal Flush is the top-tier hold.
2.  **Aces are Key:** In both games, 4 Aces pay the largest bonus. You always hold a pair of Aces.
3.  **High Pair Stability:** Both games pay 1-for-1 for a pair of Jacks or better, providing a stable floor for your bankroll.

## Variance Inversion: Bonus Quads vs. Quick Quad Draws

The most jarring difference for players is the **Variance Inversion**.

*   **Bonus Poker Deluxe:** The volatility is high (~32.0). Because you are chasing 80-for-1 payouts on any 4-of-a-kind, you will experience longer losing streaks between "big" wins. However, the frequent 80-for-1 payouts can quickly recover your bankroll.
*   **Quick Quads:** The volatility is also high (~30.0), but more "balanced." Because you have the extra "Quick Quad" rule, you will hit 4-of-a-kind hands more frequently than in Bonus Poker Deluxe, even if they are smaller.

## Conclusion: Which to Choose?

If you enjoy the simplicity of a game where *any* 4-of-a-kind is a massive win, **Bonus Poker Deluxe** is an excellent choice.

However, if you enjoy the tactical complexity of chasing "Quick Quads" and holding specific cards to complete 4-of-a-kind hands, **Quick Quads** offers a unique challenge. For a complete **video poker house edge analysis**, always check the pay table: a Full Pay 9/6 machine for Bonus Poker Deluxe is a must, but Quick Quads' innovative rule is the holy grail for many video poker players.
