# How to Play Triple Double Bonus Video Poker: Beginner's Guide
Category: Variant Guide

Triple Double Bonus (TDB) is one of the most exciting and high-variance variants of video poker. It is a "kicker" game, meaning the payouts for Four of a Kind are heavily influenced by the fifth card in the hand. For beginners, TDB offers the thrill of massive payouts that rival the Royal Flush, but it requires a disciplined approach to **multi-hand bankroll management**.

## The Core Payouts: Why Triple Double Bonus?
What sets TDB apart from standard Double Double Bonus (DDB) is the massive payout for Four Aces when the kicker is a 2, 3, or 4. In TDB, this hand typically pays **4,000 coins** (at max bet)—the same as a Royal Flush.

*   **Four Aces (with 2, 3, 4 kicker):** 4,000 coins
*   **Four 2s, 3s, or 4s (with A, 2, 3, 4 kicker):** 2,000 coins
*   **Four Aces (no kicker):** 800 coins

## Variance per Hand: The Double-Edged Sword
In professional gambling terminology, TDB is known for its extreme **variance per hand**. Because so much of the return is tied up in these rare, high-paying Four of a Kind hands, the "base" hands (like Two Pair) often pay less (typically only 1-to-1, returning your bet).

This means you will experience longer losing streaks than in Jacks or Better. If you are playing Triple Play or Ten Play TDB, the **1-to-10 hand multiplier effect on volatility** can be staggering. You might go many rounds without a significant win, only to hit a massive "kicker" jackpot that puts you deep in the black.

## Strategy for Beginners
The strategy for Triple Double Bonus is more aggressive than other games. You are often "hunting" for those Aces and kickers.

1.  **Hold the Kickers:** If you are dealt Three Aces and a 2, 3, or 4, you MUST hold the kicker. In other games, you might discard it to maximize the chance of a fourth Ace, but in TDB, the kicker is where the value lies.
2.  **Aces are King:** Always prioritize holding a single Ace over almost any other single card (except a high-ranking card for a potential Royal Flush).
3.  **Use a Trainer:** Because the strategy is counter-intuitive compared to Jacks or Better, using a **triple play video poker trainer** is essential. It will correct you when you reflexively discard a kicker or a lone Ace.

## Sustaining Your Play
Because of the high volatility, **bankroll sustainability** is your primary concern. 
*   **Lower the Denomination:** If you usually play quarters, consider nickels for TDB.
*   **Maximum Coins:** Always play 5 coins. The 4,000-coin payout for Four Aces with a kicker is only available at max bet. Playing fewer coins drastically increases the house edge.

Triple Double Bonus is not for the faint of heart, but for those who enjoy the "jackpot" chase, it is the premier choice in the video poker world.
