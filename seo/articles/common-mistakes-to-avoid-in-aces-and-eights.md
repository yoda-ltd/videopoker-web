# Common Mistakes to Avoid in Aces and Eights Video Poker

Aces and Eights is a popular variation of Bonus Poker that focuses on the high payouts for quads involving Aces and Eights (and Sevens). Mastering the **Aces and Eights strategy chart 2026** is essential for **EV Maximization** in this high-variance game. To learn **how to play video poker like a pro**, you must first eliminate these three common "Pro-Level" errors.

## Analysis: 3 Most Common "Pro-Level" Errors in Aces and Eights

### 1. Misvaluing the Eight as a "Kicker"
In many video poker variants, high pairs (J-A) are the target. In Aces and Eights, the 8-8 pair pays no more than a 2-2 pair on its own, but its potential for 4-of-a-kind is significantly higher.

**The Error:** Discarding a pair of 8s for a single Ace.
**The Pro Reality:** A pair of 8s in Aces and Eights has more "equity" than a single Ace because the 4-of-a-kind payout for 8s is 250 credits (on a 5-coin bet), whereas for other cards (except 7s), it's only 125 credits. **Rank-Based Priority** in 2026 dictates that you should prioritize the "Bonus" quads (A, 8, 7) over standard high cards.

### 2. Overvaluing the Straight vs. 3 to a Royal
Because Aces and Eights is a bonus poker variant, the payout for a Straight is often lower than in standard Jacks or Better.

**The Error:** Keeping a "made" Straight (e.g., `8-9-10-J-Q`) instead of drawing to 3 to a Royal Flush.
**The Pro Reality:** In Aces and Eights, the **Expected Return Differential** between a small "made" hand like a straight and a three-card Royal Flush draw can be significant. If the Straight payout is only 4-to-1, the long-term EV of the Royal Flush draw is often higher. Pro players calculate this "Strategy Fine-Tuning" based on the specific pay table of the machine.

### 3. Ignoring the Seven for 4-of-a-Kind
Many players forget that Sevens also have a bonus payout in this game.

**The Error:** Discarding a pair of 7s in a hand where you have two high cards.
**The Pro Reality:** Like 8s, a pair of 7s has a higher 4-of-a-kind payout (125 credits) than most other low cards. **Rank-Based Priority** in Aces and Eights means you should always hold the pair of 7s. The potential for the bonus quads outweighs the probability of hitting a high pair with a single card.
