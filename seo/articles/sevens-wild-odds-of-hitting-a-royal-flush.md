# Sevens Wild: Odds of Hitting a Royal Flush

In the world of video poker, Sevens Wild is a unique and exciting variant where all four Sevens (7s) in the deck act as wild cards. This dramatically changes the mathematics of the game, especially when you are hunting for the most coveted hand in gambling—the Royal Flush.

## Understanding the Sevens Wild Deck
Unlike standard Jacks or Better, which uses a 52-card deck, Sevens Wild uses the same 52 cards but designates all 7s as wild. This means there are two types of Royal Flushes:
1.  **Natural Royal Flush:** Ten, Jack, Queen, King, and Ace of the same suit (no Sevens).
2.  **Wild Royal Flush:** A Royal Flush completed using one or more Sevens.

In most pay tables, the Natural Royal Flush pays the jackpot (usually 800-for-1 or 4000 coins on a 5-coin bet), while the Wild Royal Flush pays significantly less (often 25-for-1 or 125 coins on a 5-coin bet).

## The Mathematical Odds
The odds of hitting a natural Royal Flush in Sevens Wild are slightly different than in standard games due to the 7s being wild.

*   **Natural Royal Flush Odds:** Approximately 1 in 45,000 hands (depending on the specific variant and strategy used).
*   **Wild Royal Flush Odds:** Approximately 1 in 5,000 hands.

Because the Sevens can substitute for any card, your frequency of hitting "big" hands like Four of a Kind and Straight Flushes increases significantly, but the house compensates for this by requiring a higher minimum hand to push or win (usually Three of a Kind).

## Impact of the Pay Table: 9/6 vs. 8/5
There are two primary versions of Sevens Wild found in high-limit rooms and online platforms:
*   **9/6 Sevens Wild:** This version has a lower variance and is more common. It offers a return to player (RTP) of around 99% to 100% on "Full Pay" schedules.
*   **8/5 Sevens Wild:** This variant is harder to win on but often offers boosted payouts for other hands like Four of a Kind or Full Houses to compensate for the stricter opening requirement.

## Strategy for Hunting the Royal
When playing Sevens Wild, your strategy changes the moment a Seven appears in your hand. 
*   **With a Seven:** You should almost never discard a Seven. If you have four cards to a Natural Royal Flush and a Seven, you have a guaranteed Wild Royal Flush, but you are also drawing for the Natural Royal.
*   **Without a Seven:** The strategy closely mirrors standard video poker, but you must be aware that the Sevens being wild slightly dilutes the probability of drawing specific cards.

## Conclusion
While the Sevens make it easier to complete a Wild Royal, the Natural Royal remains the ultimate goal. By understanding the wild card mechanics and choosing the right pay table (preferably 9/6 or 20/7/5 configurations), you can maximize your chances of hitting the big one.
