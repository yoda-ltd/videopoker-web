# The History and Evolution of Aces and Eights Video Poker

Aces and Eights is a legendary variant of video poker that pays homage to one of the most famous stories in gambling history—the "Dead Man's Hand." This article explores the history and evolution of this high-profile variant.

## The "Dead Man's Hand" Legacy

The term "Aces and Eights" refers to the hand allegedly held by Wild Bill Hickok when he was shot in the back in Deadwood, South Dakota, in 1876. While the original hand was a pair of Black Aces and Black Eights (the fifth card is still debated), the "Aces and Eights" name became a gambling legend.

In the late 1990s, game developers (primarily IGT and Realtime Gaming) decided to capitalize on this legend. They created "Aces and Eights" to provide a thematic, slightly different alternative to "Bonus Poker."

## Evolution of the Pay Table

Originally, Aces and Eights was designed to be a "Fair" game for the player. It became famous for offering high payouts for three distinct categories of Four of a Kind:
1.  **Aces and Eights:** The highest bonus.
2.  **Sevens:** A mid-tier bonus.
3.  **Other Quads:** The standard payout.

This was a departure from "Aces and Faces" (which focused on face cards) and "Bonus Poker" (which focused only on Aces). The inclusion of 8s as a high-value bonus card was a unique thematic evolution.

## Strategic Shifts in the 21st Century

As Aces and Eights evolved, it became a favorite for "Grinders"—players who wanted a game with slightly higher volatility than Jacks or Better but more stability than Double Double Bonus.

The strategy for Aces and Eights is unique:
*   **The "Hickok" Hold:** Players learned that holding a pair of 8s was suddenly more valuable than holding a pair of 10s or Jacks, a concept that exists in no other poker game.
*   **Sevens as the "Secret" Payout:** The high payout for Four Sevens meant that players had to treat a pair of 7s with extra respect, creating a strategic "pocket" in the middle of the deck.

## Essential Terms for Aces and Eights Players

When consulting the complete video poker glossary, these are the video poker terms every player should know:

*   **Bankroll:** Since Aces and Eights is a "Bonus" game, you need a slightly larger bankroll than you would for Jacks or Better.
*   **Variance:** Aces and Eights is considered "Medium Variance." It offers frequent enough quads to keep your bankroll moving.
*   **Full Pay:** A "Full Pay" version of Aces and Eights (paying 80-for-1 on Aces/Eights and 50-for-1 on Sevens) offers an excellent return of 99.78%.

## Legacy and the "Dead Man's" Future

Today, Aces and Eights is a staple in both land-based and online casinos. It has been incorporated into various multi-game platforms and remains popular because of its "storyteller" appeal.

The history of Aces and Eights is more than just a pay table evolution—it's a reminder of the "Old West" roots of American gambling. It remains the only video poker game where you can "Win Like a Legend" (or hopefully, better than Wild Bill).
