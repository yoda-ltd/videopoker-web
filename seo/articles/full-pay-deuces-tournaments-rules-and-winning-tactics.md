## The Mechanics: Timed vs. Credit-Based Formats

Deuces Wild tournaments typically fall into two categories, each requiring a distinct approach to **Hand-Per-Hour Optimization**.

### 1. Timed Tournaments (The Sprint)
In a timed session (e.g., 15 minutes), you have effectively unlimited credits. Here, your total score is limited only by your physical speed. You must maximize your hands-per-minute to increase the raw probability of hitting the high-payout milestones: **Four Deuces (1,000 coins)** and the **Natural Royal (4,000 coins)**.

### 2. Credit-Based Tournaments (The Marathon)
If you are given a fixed credit balance (e.g., 5,000 credits), speed is secondary to **Session ROI**. Every mistake reduces your total potential score. In this format, you play a nearly perfect "Full Pay" strategy, only deviating when the leaderboard standings dictate a high-risk gamble.

## Speed Strategy: Sacrificing EV for Volume

In a timed Full Pay Deuces tournament, the "Speed Strategy" becomes your primary weapon. This involves making "Instant Holds" to keep your **Speed-Play Index** high.

*   **Hand-Per-Hour Optimization:** Professional tournament players target 1,000 to 1,200 hands per hour. At this speed, you cannot spend time analyzing complex Deuces Wild draws. You must develop muscle memory for the "Big Hits."
*   **The "Deuce Priority" Rule:** If you see a Deuce, hold it instantly and draw. In a tournament, a single Deuce is your gateway to Four Deuces, which is the "Leaderboard Buster" you need to win.
*   **Mathematically Correct Misplays:** In the final 120 seconds of a timed tournament, if you are outside the prize money, it is mathematically correct to discard a "Winning" Straight or Flush if it doesn't contain a Deuce. Why? Because a 10-coin win won't move you up the leaderboard, but a redrawn Four Deuces (1,000 coins) will.

The volatility of Deuces Wild is your best friend in a tournament. **Leaderboard Variance** measures the "swing" potential of a player's score.

2.  **Tracking Session ROI:** Monitor your progress relative to the clock. If you have 5 minutes left and are at 50% of the projected winning score, you must transition to "Aggressive Mode"—holding only Deuces and Royal pieces, discarding everything else.
3.  **The "Four Deuces" Threshold:** Hitting Four Deuces once usually puts you in the top 5% of a field. Hitting it twice almost guarantees a win. Your entire strategy should be optimized to maximize the *number* of times you draw to Four Deuces.
