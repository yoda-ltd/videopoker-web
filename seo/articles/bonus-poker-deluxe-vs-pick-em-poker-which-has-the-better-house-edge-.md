# Bonus Poker Deluxe vs. Pick'em Poker: House Edge and Strategy Synergy

In the ultimate "best video poker strategy comparison," **Bonus Poker Deluxe vs. Pick'em Poker** is a battle of bonus quads vs. simplified strategy. One game rewards you with a massive bonus for hitting any 4-of-a-kind (Bonus Poker Deluxe); the other simplifies the game into a "Pick'em" format where you only choose between two cards to complete your hand. This creates a massive **Strategy Bifurcation** and alters the fundamental **Payout Weighting** of each game.

## The House Edge Analysis: Bonus Aces vs. Simplified Strategy

To perform a professional **video poker house edge analysis**, we must examine the "Full Pay" versions of both.

| Game | Full Pay Version | Return (RTP) | House Edge |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bonus Poker Deluxe** | 9/6 (99.64%) | 99.64% | 0.36% |
| **Pick'em Poker** | 99.95% (Full Pay) | 99.95% | 0.05% |

While **Full Pay Pick'em Poker** is one of the rare games that can offer a near-zero house edge, it comes with a high price in terms of gameplay flexibility. **Bonus Poker Deluxe** is the "pure" bonus game, paying 80-for-1 for *any* 4-of-a-kind.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Quad" vs. "Simplified" Hunt

The most significant **Strategy Bifurcation** occurs in the way you prioritize 4-of-a-kind vs. simplified decision-making.

*   **Bonus Poker Deluxe Strategy:** Because *any* 4-of-a-kind pays 80-for-1, you will aggressively hold *any* pair. If you have a choice between a 4-card Straight and a low pair (2s-10s), the **Optimal Hold** in Bonus Poker Deluxe is always the pair. The goal is to maximize your chances of hitting any 4-of-a-kind.
*   **Pick'em Poker Strategy:** In Pick'em Poker, you are dealt two cards and then shown two more potential "3rd cards." You choose one. This means your strategy is much more limited and "pre-calculated." You aren't "drawing" in the traditional sense; you are "picking" the card that gives you the highest Expected Value (EV).

## Strategy Synergy: Where the Games Align

Despite the different payout structures, there is some **Strategy Synergy** in the "Base Game" logic:

1.  **Royal Flush Priority:** In both games, a Royal Flush is the top-tier hold.
2.  **Aces are Key:** In both games, 4 Aces pay the largest bonus. You always prioritize Aces.
3.  **High Pair Stability:** Both games pay 1-for-1 for a pair of high cards (Jacks or Better in one, Tens or Better in the other), providing a stable floor for your bankroll.

## Variance Inversion: Bonus Quads vs. Constant Pair Wins

The most jarring difference for players is the **Variance Inversion**.

*   **Bonus Poker Deluxe:** The volatility is high (~32.0). Because you are chasing 80-for-1 payouts on any 4-of-a-kind, you will experience longer losing streaks between "big" wins. However, the frequent 80-for-1 payouts can quickly recover your bankroll.
*   **Pick'em Poker:** The volatility is very low (~15.0). Because you are winning on a pair of Tens or Better and have a limited number of choices, you will hit a winning hand on about 45% of your deals. This is the lowest volatility game in the video poker world, making it ideal for clearing casino bonuses.

## Conclusion: Which to Choose?

If you enjoy the simplicity of a game where *any* 4-of-a-kind is a massive win, **Bonus Poker Deluxe** is an excellent choice.

However, if you are a "bonus hunter" or a casual player who wants to minimize risk and play for a long time on a small bankroll, **Pick'em Poker** is the superior choice. For a complete **video poker house edge analysis**, always check the pay table: a Full Pay 9/6 machine for Bonus Poker Deluxe is a must, but Pick'em Poker's near-zero house edge is the holy grail for most video poker players.
