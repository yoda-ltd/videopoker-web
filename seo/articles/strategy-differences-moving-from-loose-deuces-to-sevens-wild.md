# Strategy Differences: Moving from Loose Deuces to Sevens Wild
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Loose Deuces** to **Sevens Wild** is a shift from one "Multi-Wild" variant to another. This represents a significant **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your wild cards and their potential for massive jackpots.

### The Comparison Matrix: Loose Deuces vs. Sevens Wild

| Feature | Loose Deuces (Full Pay) | Sevens Wild (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 101.60% | 100.56% |
| **Variance Index** | 200.0+ | 32.0 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Discard Everything for Deuces | Target Sevens as Wild Cards |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -1.60% (Player Edge) | -0.56% (Player Edge) |

### Strategic Divergence: Deuces vs. Sevens

In **Loose Deuces**, the four 2s are your most powerful tools, and you are hunting for the Four Deuces jackpot. This creates extreme **Volatility Normalization** (200.0+ vs 32.0). In **Sevens Wild**, the four 7s are your most powerful tools, but the payout for Four Sevens is not as high as for Four Deuces. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through multi-wild card connectivity.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 80% overlap because both are multi-wild games. However, in Loose Deuces, you break almost every natural winner to hunt for Deuces. In Sevens Wild, the decision is slightly more balanced. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the 2s in Loose Deuces dominate the EV landscape even more than the 7s in Sevens Wild.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Deuce Devaluation:** When moving to Sevens Wild, 2s are just another card in the deck. Your focus shifts entirely to the 7s.
2.  **Seven is Everything:** A single 7 is almost always held.
3.  **Variance Normalization:** Sevens Wild has much lower variance (32.0) than Loose Deuces (200.0+). This means your bankroll will last much longer in Sevens Wild.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games offer a player advantage in their full-pay versions, but Sevens Wild provides a much smoother ride with lower variance. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are top-tier choices for the disciplined player.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Loose Deuces to Super Aces Bonus
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Loose Deuces** to **Super Aces Bonus** is a shift from a "Multi-Wild" variant to a "Targeted Bonus" natural variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value specific card ranks and their related bonuses.

### The Comparison Matrix: Loose Deuces vs. Super Aces Bonus

| Feature | Loose Deuces (Full Pay) | Super Aces Bonus (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 101.60% | 99.94% |
| **Variance Index** | 200.0+ | 60.0+ |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Deuce-Based Wilds | Target Aces Bonus with Kicker |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -1.60% (Player Edge) | 0.06% |

### Strategic Divergence: Wild Connectivity vs. Ace Jackpot

In **Loose Deuces**, you are looking for 2s to complete your hands. In **Super Aces Bonus**, you are looking for natural Four Aces with a kicker for a massive bonus. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through high-tier natural quads. Super Aces Bonus's variance (60.0+) is lower than Loose Deuces (200.0+), providing a smoother **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is minimal (less than 10%). In Loose Deuces, you would never discard a 2. In Super Aces Bonus, you will hold Aces with a kicker for the massive bonus. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the Aces in Super Aces Bonus dominate the EV landscape.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Deuce Devaluation:** In Super Aces Bonus, 2s are only valuable if you have a pair or more. They no longer complete straights and flushes.
2.  **Ace Priority:** In Super Aces Bonus, Aces are the most important natural cards. You prioritize them over almost any other card rank.
3.  **Kicker Knowledge:** You must learn which cards are "Kickers" for the Ace bonus.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games are highly rewarding for the expert player, but Super Aces Bonus provides a smoother ride with lower variance than Loose Deuces. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are top-tier choices for anyone looking for a player advantage.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Loose Deuces to Tens or Better
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Loose Deuces** to **Tens or Better** is a shift from a "Multi-Wild" variant to a "Natural" variant with a lower high-pair floor. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your hands versus the wild card features.

### The Comparison Matrix: Loose Deuces vs. Tens or Better

| Feature | Loose Deuces (Full Pay) | Tens or Better (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 101.60% | 99.14% |
| **Variance Index** | 200.0+ | 19.5 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Discard Everything for Deuces | Target Tens or Better High Pairs |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -1.60% (Player Edge) | 0.86% |

### Strategic Divergence: Wild vs. Natural High Pair

In **Loose Deuces**, the four 2s are your focus. In **Tens or Better**, you are looking for natural high pairs starting at 10s. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through volume and natural pairs. Tens or Better's variance (19.5) is extremely low compared to Loose Deuces (200.0+), providing a very stable **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is zero (0%). Both are fundamentally different games. Loose Deuces is a multi-wild jackpot game; Tens or Better is a low-variance high-pair game. In Loose Deuces, you would never hold a pair of 10s unless it was part of a Deuce-heavy draw. In Tens or Better, you always hold 10s.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Deuce Devaluation:** In Tens or Better, 2s are just another card in the deck.
2.  **High Card Floor Shift:** In Tens or Better, the floor is 10s. This means you hold 10s, Jacks, and Queens much more frequently.
3.  **Natural Connectivity:** You are looking for natural straights and flushes.

A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are solid games for the expert player, offering competitive returns with unique gameplay. For players who find the wild card strategy of Loose Deuces too volatile, Tens or Better is a simple, high-action alternative for **EV Maximization**.
