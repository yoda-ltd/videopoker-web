# Strategy Differences: Moving from Aces and Eights to All American

If you are a regular **Aces and Eights** player, you are used to a game that rewards you for hitting specific Four of a Kinds (Aces, 8s, and 7s). Moving to **All American** can be a culture shock. While the games look similar, the underlying "math engine" is completely different.

In this guide, we explain the key strategy shifts you need to make to transition successfully from Aces and Eights to All American.

## 1. The "Two Pair" Mindset Shift

In Aces and Eights, Two Pair pays **2:1**. In All American, Two Pair only pays **1:1**.

*   **Aces and Eights Strategy:** You are happy to hit Two Pair. It doubles your money and keeps you in the game.
*   **All American Strategy:** Two Pair is a "push." Because it pays so little, you should be much more willing to **break** a potential Two Pair if you have a 4-card draw to a Royal Flush or a Straight Flush.

## 2. Aggression on Flushes and Straights

This is the biggest difference. In Aces and Eights, a Straight pays 4:1 and a Flush pays 5:1. In All American, they both typically pay **8:1**.

*   **Aces and Eights Strategy:** You only keep 4-card flushes or straights if you have nothing else (like a high card or a pair).
*   **All American Strategy:** A 4-card Flush or 4-card Straight is **Gold**. You should prioritize these draws over keeping a "Low Pair" (2s through 10s). This is a "heretical" move for most poker players, but it is mathematically required in All American.

## 3. The Straight Flush Focus

Aces and Eights pays 50:1 for a Straight Flush. All American pays a massive **200:1**.

*   **Aces and Eights Strategy:** You rarely "chase" a 3-card Straight Flush unless you have literally nothing else in your hand.
*   **All American Strategy:** A 3-card Straight Flush (with no gaps) is a powerful hand. You should often keep it over a single high card (like an Ace or King). The 200:1 potential is a huge part of your long-term return.

## 4. De-valuing the "Bonus" Cards

In Aces and Eights, you go out of your way to keep Aces, 8s, and 7s because of the quad bonuses.

*   **Aces and Eights Strategy:** You might keep a single Ace over a 3-card straight draw.
*   **All American Strategy:** An Ace is just an Ace. While it still pays "Jacks or Better" (1:1), it doesn't have the "hidden value" it has in Aces and Eights. Don't be afraid to discard an Ace to chase an 8:1 Straight or Flush draw.

## 5. Variance Preparation

*   **Aces and Eights:** Variance is ~21.8. It’s a relatively steady game.
*   **All American:** Variance is ~30.0. It is a much "bumpier" ride.

**The Transition Tip:** When you move to All American, don't be surprised if your bankroll disappears faster than usual during a "cold" streak. You need about 50% more bankroll for All American than you do for Aces and Eights at the same stake.

## Summary: The "Mental Cheat Sheet"

1.  **Stop** worrying about Two Pair; it’s just a push.
2.  **Start** chasing 4-card Flushes and Straights aggressively (they pay 8:1!).
3.  **Look** for 3-card Straight Flush draws; they are worth more than you think (200:1).
4.  **Trust** the math, even when it tells you to break a pair.

## Conclusion

Transitioning from Aces and Eights to All American is like moving from a "steady job" to a "high-commission sales job." You’ll have more "zero" days, but your "big" days will be much, much larger. By focusing on the 8:1 and 200:1 payouts, you can master the All American dream.
