# The Payout Gap: Bonus Poker vs Double Bonus Explained

In the world of **best odds video poker**, players often find themselves choosing between two of the most popular machines on the floor: Bonus Poker and Double Bonus. While they look similar, the **EV Gap** between them depends entirely on their specific pay tables and your tolerance for **House Edge Variance**.

## The Best Odds Index: 8/5 Bonus vs 10/7 Double Bonus
In the **highest return video poker games** 2026 ranking, both machines offer strong professional returns when found in their "Full Pay" configurations:
*   **8/5 Bonus Poker:** 99.17% RTP.
*   **10/7 Double Bonus:** 100.17% RTP.

At first glance, Double Bonus appears to be the superior choice. However, Double Bonus offers a "Bonus" for hitting Four of a Kind (specifically Four Aces, which pays 160-for-1 instead of 80-for-1). This change in the pay table structure shifts the **Theoretical Return Convergence**.

## Analysis of "Short Pay" Predation
The real "Payout Gap" occurs when casinos offer sub-optimal versions of these games. 
*   **Bonus Poker (6/5):** RTP falls to 96.87%.
*   **Double Bonus (8/5):** RTP falls to 94.19%.

If you find an 8/5 Bonus machine next to an 8/5 Double Bonus machine, the 4.98% **EV Gap** is predatory. A professional player would never touch the 8/5 Double Bonus machine in this scenario. However, if the choice is between 6/5 Bonus (96.87%) and 10/7 Double Bonus (100.17%), the Double Bonus machine is actually the much better bet.

## House Edge Variance and Strategy
Double Bonus has higher variance than Bonus Poker. This is because more of the return is tied up in the Four of a Kind payouts. In Bonus Poker, the Two Pair payout (2-for-1) is the engine that keeps your bankroll alive. 
*   **Bonus Poker Strategy:** Focuses on bankroll preservation and hitting the Four of a Kind.
*   **Double Bonus Strategy:** Slightly adjusts to account for the higher value of Four of a Kind, particularly Aces.
