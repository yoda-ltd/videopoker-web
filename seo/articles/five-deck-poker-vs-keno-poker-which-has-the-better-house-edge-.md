# Five Deck Poker vs. Keno Poker: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Comparing **Five Deck Poker** and **Keno Poker** is a comparison of two massive mathematical systems. While Five Deck Poker increases the complexity of the cards through parallel decks, Keno Poker blends standard poker with a completely separate keno lottery system. To choose the right game, you must understand how **video poker fairness and regulation** ensures the integrity of these high-stakes mathematical models.

## The Mathematical Framework: Decks vs. Numbers

The house edge in these games is determined by the "Coverage" of winning outcomes.

*   **Five Deck Poker:** Uses five separate 52-card decks for the draw stage. This allows for hands like "Five-of-a-Kind," which are impossible in standard poker. The edge is concentrated in the skill of holding cards across multiple parallel deck shuffles.
*   **Keno Poker:** Blends a 52-card poker hand with a 20-number keno draw (from 80 numbers). The house edge is split between the card-draw logic and the keno-pattern logic.

## House Edge Analysis: The Accuracy of the Math

The accuracy of the advertised edge in these variants is guaranteed by **Return to Player (RTP) Verification** during independent **Lab Audits**.

| Metric | Typical Five Deck Poker | Typical Keno Poker |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Typical RTP** | **98.5% - 99.4%** | **95.0% - 97.0%** |
| **House Edge** | **0.6% - 1.5%** | **3.0% - 5.0%** |
| **Volatility** | Medium-High | Very High |

**The Verdict:** **Five Deck Poker** offers a vastly superior house edge for skilled players. Its return-to-player is closer to professional Jacks or Better, while **Keno Poker** is designed as a "High-Risk, High-Reward" lottery hybrid with a higher house tax.

## 1. Technical Standards for Complex Logic

Both variants must adhere to strict **Technical Standards** enforced by regulators like the **NGCB** or **UKGC**.

*   **Five Deck Poker:** The **Random Number Generator (RNG)** must provide five truly independent shuffles. Firms like **GLI** audit the code to ensure that a card drawn in deck 1 has no statistical correlation with deck 5.
*   **Keno Poker:** Regulators verify that the card-draw RNG and the keno-ball RNG are separate and independent. The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures that hitting a big poker win doesn't "block" you from hitting a keno pattern.

## 2. Payout Analysis: The "Jackpot" Burden

In Keno Poker, the house edge is higher because of the "Jackpot Tax" required to fund the astronomical keno payouts.
- In Five Deck Poker, the payout for "Five-of-a-Kind" is a natural extension of the multi-deck probability and doesn't require a high house tax.
- In Keno Poker, you are effectively paying a premium for the chance to win two different games on a single wager.

For the player who wants a lower house edge and values strategic precision, **Five Deck Poker** is the superior choice. Its high RTP makes it a fair game for professional-grade bankrolls.

However, if you want the excitement of a lottery-style "Mega Hit" alongside your poker hand, **Keno Poker** provides a fair and regulated experience, despite the higher house edge.
