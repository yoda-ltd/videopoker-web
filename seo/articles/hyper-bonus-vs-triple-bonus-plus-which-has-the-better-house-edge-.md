# Hyper Bonus vs. Triple Bonus Plus: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Two of the most popular high-volatility video poker variants are **Hyper Bonus** and **Triple Bonus Plus**. Both games reward specific four-of-a-kind (Quads) combinations, making them exciting choices for players looking for a big score. But which one offers a better long-term **Expected Value (EV)**, and how do they differ in strategy?

## The Payout Breakdown

Both games are variations of Jacks or Better, but they differ significantly in how they pay for Quads.

### Triple Bonus Plus (9/6 Pay Table)
*   **4 Aces:** 800 coins (on a 5-coin bet)
*   **4 2s, 3s, or 4s:** 400 coins
*   **4 5s through Kings:** 250 coins
*   **Full House:** 9 coins
*   **Flush:** 6 coins

### Hyper Bonus (9/6 Pay Table)
*   **4 Aces:** 1200 coins (on a 5-coin bet)
*   **4 2s, 3s, or 4s:** 800 coins
*   **4 5s through Kings:** 500 coins
*   **Full House:** 9 coins
*   **Flush:** 6 coins

## House Edge and Return to Player (RTP)

The house edge is the long-term mathematical advantage the casino has over the player. In video poker, we often talk in terms of RTP (Return to Player).

*   **9/6 Triple Bonus Plus:** Offers an RTP of approximately **99.58%**. This is a powerhouse for skilled players.
*   **9/6 Hyper Bonus:** Offers an RTP of approximately **99.75%**.

**The Verdict:** By a thin margin, **Hyper Bonus** (in its full-pay version) generally offers a better long-term EV. However, these "full-pay" tables are becoming increasingly rare. You must always check the specific pay table on the machine. An 8/5 Hyper Bonus (97.80% RTP) is far worse than a 9/6 Triple Bonus Plus.

## Variance: The High Volatility Factor

Both games are considered **high volatility video poker strategy** tests. Because so much of the RTP is tied up in rare four-of-a-kind hands, you will experience longer losing streaks compared to standard Jacks or Better.

*   **Triple Bonus Plus:** Focuses its highest bonuses on Aces and low cards (2, 3, 4).
*   **Hyper Bonus:** Takes those bonuses to the extreme. The 1200-coin bonus for Aces and the 800-coin bonus for low Quads (2, 3, 4) are massive. This makes the distribution of big wins even more dramatic than in Triple Bonus Plus.

## Strategy Comparison: How to Hold

When playing either of these games, using a **triple bonus poker trainer** or a strategy calculator is essential.

1.  **Breaking a Full House for Aces:** In Hyper Bonus, you should almost always break a Full House if it includes three Aces. The potential 1200-coin payoff for the fourth Ace is far superior to the 9-coin Full House.
2.  **Hold Pair of 2s, 3s, 4s over High Cards:** In Hyper Bonus, the 800-coin bonus for low Quads makes them more valuable than in Triple Bonus Plus (where they pay 400). This shifts your hold priorities even more aggressively toward low pairs.
3.  **The Kicker Rule:** Neither of these games uses "kickers" (a specific fifth card) to boost the payout of a Quad. This simplifies the strategy significantly compared to Double Double Bonus.

## Conclusion

If you can find a full-pay 9/6 Hyper Bonus machine, it's technically the superior choice. However, 9/6 Triple Bonus Plus is nearly as good and often easier to find in many casinos. Both games demand a disciplined approach and a large enough bankroll to survive the high-variance nature of the bonus payouts. Whichever you choose, ensure you've mastered the specific hold patterns for that game's pay table to minimize the house edge.
