# Ultimate X vs. Dream Card: Which has the better house edge?

Ultimate X and Dream Card are two "premium" video poker variants that require an extra bet to activate their special features. While both appeal to players looking for an edge, they function in completely different ways, particularly regarding **EV preservation** and **Next-Hand Value**.

## Ultimate X: The Multiplier Grind
In Ultimate X, your extra 5-coin bet buys you **Accumulated Multipliers** for the *next* hand.
*   **Edge:** On a 9/6 pay table, the edge is very thin (approx. 0.2% with perfect play).
*   **The Vulture Factor:** Ultimate X has a "persistent state," meaning you can use the **Vulture Strategy**. If you know **how to find vulture machines**, you can start your session with a massive mathematical advantage.

## Dream Card: The Ideal Hold
Dream Card requires a 10-coin bet (double the standard). In exchange, the machine will occasionally give you the "best possible" fifth card for your hand before the draw.
*   **Edge:** Dream Card's edge is slightly higher than Ultimate X on most pay tables. The "Dream Card" doesn't appear on every hand, making the 10-coin cost harder to justify over a long session.
*   **No Persistence:** There are no "Next Hand" bonuses in Dream Card. Once a hand is over, it’s over. You can't "vulture" a Dream Card machine.

## Comparing 3-Play, 5-Play, and 10-Play
Ultimate X excels in **10-play** because the multiplier effect can "cluster," leading to massive swings. Dream Card is often played as a 3-play or 5-play game, as the 10-coin cost per line for 100% of the hands is incredibly expensive.
