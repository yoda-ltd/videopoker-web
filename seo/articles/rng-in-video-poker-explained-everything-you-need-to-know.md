# RNG in Video Poker: The Science Behind the Shuffle

When you sit down at a video poker machine, you're not just playing a game—you're interacting with a complex piece of software governed by strict **video poker fairness and regulation**. At the heart of this software is the **Random Number Generator (RNG)**. To understand how video poker really works, we need to dive into the technology that ensures every deal is backed by a **trusted video poker RNG certification**.

## What is an RNG?
The RNG used in video poker is a **PRNG (Pseudo-Random Number Generator)**. A PRNG is a computer algorithm that uses a starting number (a "seed") and a mathematical formula to generate a sequence of numbers that appear to be completely random.

In professional gaming environments, these algorithms run continuously, cycling through millions of numbers every second. To satisfy **Technical Standards**, these RNGs must be "non-deterministic," meaning a player cannot predict the next sequence of numbers based on previous outcomes.

## How RNG Mapping and Jurisdictional Mandates Work
Video poker machines are governed by a **PAR Sheet** (Probability Accounting Report). This document is the mathematical blueprint for the game. **Jurisdictional Mandates** from regulators like the **NGCB** or **UKGC** require that the RNG's numerical output corresponds exactly to a physical 52-card deck.

This ensures that:
*   The chance of drawing any specific card is exactly **1 in 52**.
*   The chance of being dealt a Royal Flush on the initial deal is always **1 in 649,740**.
*   The game provides an honest, unbiased representation of card probability.

## The Truth About Fairness: Lab Audits and Integrity
The most common myth in gambling is that machines can be "tight" or "loose" based on how much they have recently paid out. In reality, **game integrity** is maintained through rigorous **Lab Audits** performed by independent testing labs like **GLI (Gaming Laboratories International)** and **BMM Testlabs**.

1.  **Independence of Events:** Every hand is an independent event. The RNG doesn't "remember" previous wins or losses.
2.  **RTP Verification:** Lab engineers simulate millions of hands to ensure that the actual performance of the machine matches the theoretical **Return to Player (RTP)** listed in the software's specifications.
3.  **Code Verification:** During a **Lab Audit**, the actual **Source Code** of the game is inspected to ensure there are no hidden subroutines that could alter the randomness of the deal.

## The Evolution of Hardware: From EPROMs to Servers
In the early days of video poker, the RNG and game logic were stored on physical **EPROM** chips. Today, the industry has shifted toward **Server-Based Gaming (SBG)** and cloud-based deployments. While the hardware has changed, the **Technical Standards** have only become more stringent. Modern server-based systems allow for real-time monitoring of RNG performance, providing an even higher level of transparency and security for the player.
