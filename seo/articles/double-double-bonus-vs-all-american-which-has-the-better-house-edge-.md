# Double Double Bonus vs. All American: Strategy Overlap and Hand-Ranking Priorities

In the specialized world of video poker, the comparison between **Double Double Bonus (DDB)** and **All American** is a fascinating study in **Strategy Convergence** and **Optimal Hold Variance**. While both games are "Static Strategy" variants—meaning no wild cards—they distribute their return-to-player (RTP) in fundamentally different ways. Understanding the **Hand-Ranking Inversion** between these two is essential for anyone looking for the **best video poker strategy comparison** and learning **how to play video poker optimally**.

## The Core Conflict: Kicker Quads vs. The Draw Push

The primary driver of the strategy shift between Double Double Bonus and All American is the "Kicker" system and the Draw payouts. Double Double Bonus (typically 9/6 or 8/5) is a "top-heavy" game that pays only 1-for-1 for Two Pair, but compensates with massive payouts for 4-of-a-kind (especially with a kicker). All American (typically 8/8/8) is a "heavy" game that pays 8-for-1 for a Full House, 8-for-1 for a Flush, and 8-for-1 for a Straight.

| Hand | Double Double Bonus (9/6) | All American (8/8) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Two Pair** | 1-for-1 | 1-for-1 |
| **Straight** | 4-for-1 | 8-for-1 |
| **Flush** | 6-for-1 | 8-for-1 |
| **Full House** | 9-for-1 | 8-for-1 |
| **4 Aces w/ 2,3,4 Kicker** | 400-for-1 | 35-for-1 |

This increase in the kicker-dependent 4 Aces payouts in Double Double Bonus and the 8-for-1 Straight/Flush in All American create massive **Strategy Convergence** breakers.

## 1. Strategy Overlap: High Strategy Convergence in Foundations

If you are coming from a Jacks or Better background, you can reuse about 40% of your strategy for both Double Double Bonus and All American. The **Strategy Convergence** is limited by the "quad" vs. "draw" hunt.

*   **Royal Flush Draws:** The priority of a 4-card Royal Flush remains at the top of the list in both games.
*   **Three of a Kind:** In both games, Three of a Kind is a premium situation, always drawing for the 4th card.
*   **Two Pair Pivot:** Both games pay only 1-for-1 for Two Pair, meaning you will often break Two Pair containing Aces in both games, though for different reasons (hunting the kicker quad in DDB vs. hunting the flush/straight in All American).

## 2. Hand-Ranking Inversion: The Kicker vs. Draw Pivot

The real strategy change comes from the bonuses in DDB and the 8-for-1 Straight/Flush in All American. This causes a **Hand-Ranking Inversion** where certain cards become more valuable than others.

*   **The Kicker Dominance:** In Double Double Bonus, the 5th card (the kicker) is everything for the bonus wins. You will hold onto a lone Ace or a pair of Aces much more aggressively in DDB to hit the 400-for-1 win.
*   **The Draw Dominance:** In All American, the 8-for-1 Straight and 8-for-1 Flush are your primary goals. You will often choose to hold a 4-card Flush over a High Pair (Jacks-Aces) in All American, whereas in DDB, you would prioritize the Aces.

## 3. Penalty Card Impact: The Bonus vs. Draw Hunt

Professional players focus on **Penalty Card Impact** when hunting for those specific wins.

*   **In Double Double Bonus:** You are looking for Aces and kicker cards (2, 3, 4). Discarding a card that could have completed a Straight but "penalizes" your kicker potential is more common.
*   **In All American:** A single "penalty card" can flip the strategy. You are more likely to discard a potential pair to chase the 8-for-1 Straight or Flush.

## 4. Optimal Hold Variance: Stability vs. Bonus Potential

Both games are relatively high-volatility variants.

*   **Double Double Bonus Volatility:** ~42.0 (High). Your bankroll will experience long "dry spells," punctuated by massive, life-changing wins.
*   **All American Volatility:** ~30.0 (Medium-High). Because you are chasing high-payout Straights and Flushes, this is one of the more volatile "Static Strategy" variants available.

## Conclusion: How to Transition

When moving between Double Double Bonus and All American, the **best video poker strategy comparison** shows that you must shift your focus between "quad-hunting" and "draw-completing." 

1.  **Hunt the Kicker in DDB:** Understand that hitting 4 Aces with a 2, 3, or 4 kicker is the primary goal in DDB.
2.  **Respect the Straight and Flush in All American:** If the machine pays 8 for both, the 4-card Straight and 4-card Flush are your primary goals.
3.  **Bankroll Management:** Both games require a large bankroll to survive the volatility of the 1-for-1 Two Pair payout and the chasing of high-value wins.

By mastering these **Hand-Ranking Priorities**, you can navigate the shift from the quad-focused world of Double Double Bonus to the draw-driven world of All American and learn **how to play video poker optimally**.
