# Ultimate Super Double Bonus Strategy Chart for 2026

Super Double Bonus (SDB) is a volatile variant that rewards aggressive play on specific four-of-a-kind combinations, particularly Aces. Understanding the **Super Double Bonus strategy chart 2026** is essential for **EV Maximization** in the modern casino landscape. If you want to learn **how to play video poker like a pro**, you must understand the **Expected Return Differential** that separates SDB from other bonus poker variants.

## Analysis: 3 Most Common "Pro-Level" Errors in Super Double Bonus

### 1. Miscalculating EV on Aces vs. Full Houses
In many variants, holding a Full House is a given. However, in SDB, the payout for four Aces is significantly elevated (often 800 credits on a 5-coin bet).

**The Error:** Keeping the Full House (e.g., `A-A-A-K-K`).
**The Pro Reality:** Under many SDB pay tables, the **EV Maximization** strategy dictates breaking the Full House to draw for the fourth Ace. If the payout for 4 Aces is 800 and the Full House is only 45, the mathematical "Expected Return Differential" favors the draw. Pro players recognize that the long-term equity of the quad Aces outweighs the short-term safety of the Full House.

### 2. Ignoring Rank-Based Priority in Straight Flush Draws
Many players treat all Open-Ended Straight Flush draws (OESFD) with equal weight. In SDB, **Rank-Based Priority** is crucial.

**The Error:** Holding a low-rank OESFD (e.g., `4-5-6-7` of Spades) over a high-rank pair or a high-card Ace.
**The Pro Reality:** Because SDB heavily weights the top of the pay table, a single Ace often has more long-term value than a low-rank draw that doesn't include high-card "kickers" for potential high-pair payouts. Strategy fine-tuning requires recognizing that the "cost" of missing a straight flush is higher when you sacrifice a high-card hold.

### 3. Failure in Strategy Fine-Tuning for "Kicker" Games
SDB is often confused with Double Double Bonus (DDB). In DDB, kickers (2, 3, or 4) matter immensely for the 4-of-a-kind payout. In Super Double Bonus, the payouts are usually fixed for the quads regardless of the kicker.

**The Error:** Holding a kicker with a pair of Aces (e.g., `A-A-2`) hoping for the DDB-style bonus.
**The Pro Reality:** Holding the kicker reduces your chances of drawing the 3rd and 4th Aces. Since SDB doesn't reward the kicker, you are effectively lowering your EV for no reason. Pros discard the kicker to maximize the number of available cards that complete the quad.

In Super Double Bonus, your primary objective is to secure the high-payout quads while maintaining a decent return from high pairs. The **Strategy Fine-Tuning** required for SDB is more aggressive than in Jacks or Better. To play like a pro, you must transition from a "Value-Based" mindset (holding what pays now) to an "Equity-Based" mindset (holding what yields the highest average return).
