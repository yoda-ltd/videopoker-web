# Video Poker: Pseudo-Randomness vs. True Randomness Explained

A common concern among players is whether video poker machines are "fair." This often leads to discussions about **Pseudo-Randomness vs. True Randomness**. To understand if the games are rigged, we have to look at the math and the law.

### What is the RNG?
The heart of a video poker machine is the Random Number Generator (RNG). This is a microprocessor that constantly generates numbers, even when no one is playing. Each number corresponds to a card in a 52-card deck (or 53 for Joker Poker).

### Pseudo-Randomness vs. True Randomness
- **True Randomness**: Found in nature (like radioactive decay). It is impossible to predict.
- **Pseudo-Randomness**: Generated by a mathematical formula. If you knew the formula and the "seed" (the starting point), you could predict the outcome.

While the RNG is technically **Pseudo-Random**, modern algorithms (like the Mersenne Twister) are so complex that for a human player, they are indistinguishable from True Randomness. There is no "pattern" to find, and the machine doesn't "know" it's time for a Royal Flush.

### Hit Frequency Optimization and Fairness
Casinos are heavily regulated. In jurisdictions like Nevada or New Jersey, the RNG must be certified to ensure that every card has an equal 1/52 chance of being dealt. This ensures that your **Hit Frequency Optimization** is based on pure probability, not a programmed win/loss cycle.

### Addressing the "Rigged" Concerns
Many players ask, "Why do I keep losing on 9/6 machines?" and assume the RNG is working against them. In reality, it's just **Variance**. The machine doesn't care about your **video poker tips for beginners** or your "gut feeling." It simply executes the math.

To learn **how to win video poker daily**, you must accept that the RNG is fair and focus entirely on your own strategy. The cards are random; your holds should not be.
