# Strategy Differences: Moving from Sevens Wild to Bonus Poker Deluxe
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Sevens Wild** to **Bonus Poker Deluxe** is a shift from a "Multi-Wild" variant to a "Natural" variant with a uniform quad bonus. This represents a significant **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your hands versus the wild card features.

### The Comparison Matrix: Sevens Wild vs. Bonus Poker Deluxe

| Feature | Sevens Wild (Full Pay) | Bonus Poker Deluxe (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.56% | 99.64% |
| **Variance Index** | 32.0 | 30.7 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Sevens as Wild Cards | Target ANY Natural Quad Bonus |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.56% (Player Edge) | 0.36% |

### Strategic Divergence: Wild Connectivity vs. Uniform Quads

In **Sevens Wild**, the four 7s are the most powerful cards in the deck, allowing you to complete hands that would otherwise be worthless. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through multi-wild card connectivity. In **Bonus Poker Deluxe**, all four-of-a-kind hands are "Doubled" compared to standard Jacks or Better, paying a uniform 400 credits. This creates a much lower **Volatility Normalization** (30.7 vs 32.0).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 40% overlap. In Sevens Wild, you will hold a single 7 almost as if it were a high pair. In Bonus Poker Deluxe, you only care about 7s if you have a pair or more. This creates a shift in **Payout Compression** where the "Middle Tier" hands (Straights/Flushes) are the focus in Sevens Wild, but the "Top Tier" hands (Four of a Kind) are the focus in Bonus Poker Deluxe.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Seven Devaluation:** When moving to Bonus Poker Deluxe, 7s are just another card in the deck. They no longer offer a wild card feature.
2.  **Natural High Pair Priority:** In Bonus Poker Deluxe, Jacks, Queens, Kings, and Aces are your primary focus. In Sevens Wild, they are almost entirely ignored unless they support a 4-to-a-Royal with Sevens.
3.  **Five of a Kind Removal:** This hand doesn't exist in Bonus Poker Deluxe. You are now looking for natural four-of-a-kind combinations.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games are excellent for players who enjoy bonus payouts, but Bonus Poker Deluxe provides a smoother ride with lower variance. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are top-tier choices for anyone looking for a player advantage.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Sevens Wild to Super Aces Bonus
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Sevens Wild** to **Super Aces Bonus** is a shift from a "Multi-Wild" variant to a "High Bonus" natural variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value four-of-a-kind hands and the volatility that comes with them.

### The Comparison Matrix: Sevens Wild vs. Super Aces Bonus

| Feature | Sevens Wild (Full Pay) | Super Aces Bonus (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Sevens as Wild Cards | Target Aces Bonus with Kicker |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.56% (Player Edge) | 0.06% |

### Strategic Divergence: Wild Connectivity vs. Ace Jackpot

In **Sevens Wild**, you are looking for 7s to complete your hands. In **Super Aces Bonus**, you are looking for natural Four Aces with a kicker for a massive bonus. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through high-tier natural quads. Super Aces Bonus's variance (60.0+) is higher than Sevens Wild (32.0), requiring careful **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is minimal (less than 10%). In Sevens Wild, you would never discard a 7. In Super Aces Bonus, you will hold Aces with a kicker for the massive bonus. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the Aces in Super Aces Bonus dominate the EV landscape.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Seven Devaluation:** In Super Aces Bonus, 7s are only valuable if you have a pair or more. They no longer complete straights and flushes.
2.  **Ace Priority:** In Super Aces Bonus, Aces are the most important natural cards. You prioritize them over almost any other card rank.
3.  **Natural Connectivity:** In Super Aces Bonus, you are looking for natural straights and flushes. In Sevens Wild, you are looking for wild card connectivity.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games are highly rewarding for the expert player, but Super Aces Bonus provides a more intense experience with higher variance. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are top-tier choices for anyone looking for a player advantage.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Sevens Wild to Tens or Better
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Sevens Wild** to **Tens or Better** is a shift from a "Multi-Wild" variant to a "Natural" variant with a lower high-pair floor. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your hands versus the wild card features.

### The Comparison Matrix: Sevens Wild vs. Tens or Better

| Feature | Sevens Wild (Full Pay) | Tens or Better (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.56% | 99.14% |
| **Variance Index** | 32.0 | 19.5 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Sevens as Wild Cards | Target Tens or Better High Pairs |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.56% (Player Edge) | 0.86% |

### Strategic Divergence: Wild vs. Natural High Pair

In **Sevens Wild**, the four 7s are your focus. In **Tens or Better**, you are looking for natural high pairs starting at 10s. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through volume and natural pairs. Tens or Better's variance (19.5) is lower than Sevens Wild (32.0), providing a very stable **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is roughly 30%. In Sevens Wild, you would never discard the 7s. In Tens or Better, you will hold a pair of 10s as a winner. This is the essence of **Payout Compression** in high-pair games.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Seven Devaluation:** In Tens or Better, 7s are just another card in the deck.
2.  **High Card Floor Shift:** In Tens or Better, the floor is 10s. This means you hold 10s, Jacks, and Queens much more frequently.
3.  **Natural Connectivity:** You are looking for natural straights and flushes.

A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are solid games for the expert player, offering competitive returns with unique gameplay. For players who find the wild card strategy of Sevens Wild too complex, Tens or Better is a simple, high-action alternative for **EV Maximization**.
