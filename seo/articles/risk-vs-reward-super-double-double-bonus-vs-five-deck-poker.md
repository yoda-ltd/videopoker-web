# Risk vs. Reward: Super Double Double Bonus vs. Five-Deck Poker
Category: Comparison

Choosing between **Super Double Double Bonus** and **Five-Deck Poker** involves balancing the rewards of "Kicker-Based Natural Quads" against the "Multi-Deck Connectivity" of Five-Deck Poker. This creates a significant **Strategic Divergence** in how you approach **Volatility Normalization**.

### Strategic Divergence: Kickers vs. Connectivity

In **Super Double Double Bonus Poker**, the reward is concentrated in Achieving natural four-of-a-kind hands with a kicker for a massive bonus. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through rank-based holds. In **Five-Deck Poker**, the reward is spread across natural combinations across five independent decks. This leads to a much lower **Volatility Normalization** (20.0 vs 45.0+).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 60% overlap on the core hand results. However, in Five-Deck Poker, you focus on Natural connectivity across all five decks, while in Super Double Double Bonus, you prioritize kicker-based quads. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the high-tier kicker quads in Super Double Double Bonus dominate the EV landscape.

### Key Risk/Reward Factors
1.  **Super Double Double Bonus Reward:** A superior "Jackpot" potential for Aces and kickers, with the excitement of the kicker-based draw.
2.  **Five-Deck Reward:** A consistent, lower-variance return with frequent natural wins across decks.
3.  **Bankroll Requirement:** Because of the Variance Index of 45.0+, Super Double Double Bonus requires a much larger bankroll than Five-Deck Poker.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games are highly engaging for those who enjoy natural quad bonuses. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both offer excellent returns if you play the maximum bet and master the specialized strategies.
