# The Payout Gap: Triple Aces vs. Wheel Poker Explained

The "Payout Gap" between Triple Aces and Wheel Poker represents the two most popular ways to "Target" specific mid-tier wins: "Specialized Payouts" versus "Feature Spins." This **best video poker strategy comparison** breaks down how **Payout Compression** and **Variance Scaling** define these two "High-Reward" formats.

## The Mathematical Foundation: Bonus vs. Feature

**Triple Aces** is a "Bonus" game. It is a Jacks or Better variant that pays a massive premium for 4 Aces (often 120-to-1 or 600 coins). To fund this, the game uses **Payout Compression** on other Quads and potentially on mid-tier hands. The **video poker house edge analysis** is dominated by the frequency of hitting 4 Aces.

**Wheel Poker** is a "Feature" game. It typically takes a standard game (like Jacks or Better) and adds a "Bonus Wheel" feature. To trigger the "Spin," you must typically bet an "Extra" 5 coins. This is the ultimate form of **Payout Compression**. You are betting 10 coins for a 5-coin pay table. The **video poker house edge analysis** is based on the "Wheel Frequency" and the "Spin Payouts."

## Variance Scaling and the EV Gap

1.  **Triple Aces:** The variance is high (around 40-50). It is a "Hunting" game. The **Variance Scaling** is steep because the game relies on the Aces to achieve its theoretical return. The **EV Gap** between a session with Aces and one without is massive.
2.  **Wheel Poker:** The variance is also high. You are "Pre-paying" for the wheel. If the wheel doesn't trigger frequently enough, or if it lands on small amounts, the **EV Gap** will empty your bankroll. Your bankroll moves in "Jumps" based on the "Spin Strike."

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Ace" vs. The "Spin"

The **Strategy Bifurcation** is a shift in "Player Focus":
*   In **Triple Aces**, you are an "Ace Specialist." You will hold a single Ace with extreme priority. You treat the Ace as a "Jackpot" trigger. You will break apart "Winning" high pairs to chase the Aces.
*   In **Wheel Poker**, the strategy usually remains identical to the "Base" game. The "Wheel" doesn't change *how* you play; it just changes *how much* you pay for the "Rush."

If you enjoy the "Strike" of a massive natural payout for 4 Aces, Triple Aces is your game. If you enjoy the "Rush" of a random multiplier wheel that can turn any win into a huge one, Wheel Poker offers a more "Exhilarating" (albeit more expensive) experience. Both require a disciplined approach and an understanding of the "Cost" of the feature (or the bonus).
