# Bonus Poker Odds of Hitting a Royal Flush: Mathematical Breakdown and RTP Strategy

Bonus Poker is often called the "sweet spot" for video poker enthusiasts. It offers higher-value Four of a Kind payouts than Jacks or Better, while maintaining a much lower **Variance Index** than high-volatility games like Double Double Bonus. This guide explores the **video poker odds and probability** of Bonus Poker and why it remains one of the **highest return video poker games** in any casino.

## The Mathematical Framework of Bonus Poker

Bonus Poker (specifically the "Full Pay" 8/5 version) returns a theoretical **99.17%** with perfect strategy. Its structure is almost identical to Jacks or Better, with one key difference: higher payouts for Four Aces (400-for-5), Four 2s, 3s, or 4s (200-for-5), and other Quads (125-for-5).

### Royal Flush Probability and Combinatorial Analysis
The probability of hitting a Royal Flush in Bonus Poker is approximately **1 in 40,391 hands**. 
- **The "Cycle":** The Royal Flush cycle in Bonus Poker is identical to Jacks or Better because the optimal strategy for the Royal Flush rarely conflicts with the bonus quad strategy.
- **EV Calculation:** In Bonus Poker, the Royal Flush accounts for approximately **1.98%** of the total RTP. This means that if you never hit a Royal, you still have a strong 97.19% return, provided you play perfectly.

## Low Variance and the Variance Index

Bonus Poker is a low-variance game. While Jacks or Better has a **Variance Index** of 19.5, Full Pay Bonus Poker is only slightly higher at **20.9**.

### Standard Deviation and Statistical Convergence
The relatively low **Standard Deviation** in Bonus Poker is driven by the 2-for-1 payout on Two Pair. This frequent payout acts as a "buffer" for your bankroll while you wait for a big hand.
- **Impact on Bankroll:** Unlike high-variance games that only pay 1-for-1 on Two Pair, Bonus Poker allows you to play for long periods with a moderate bankroll.
- **Convergence:** Because of the low variance, **Statistical Convergence** (where your actual results match the theoretical 99.17% RTP) is achieved relatively quickly. A professional player might only need to play over **500,000 hands** to see their results reliably "even out" near the 99% mark.

## The Royal Flush Cycle and Strategy Precision

The "Royal Flush Cycle" is the average interval between Royal hits. In Bonus Poker, this cycle is highly dependent on **strategy precision**, specifically when drawing for the bonus quads.

### Strategic Shifts in Bonus Poker
To achieve the highest return, you must adjust your strategy slightly away from the Jacks or Better baseline:
- **Prioritizing Aces:** Because Four Aces pay so much (400 coins), the strategy for drawing toward a single Ace is slightly more aggressive than in Jacks or Better.
- **Holding for Quads:** The 125-for-5 payout on all other Quads is enough to shift the EV in certain marginal situations, but it rarely impacts the decision to hold for a Royal Flush.
- **Combinatorial Analysis:** Professional players use complex algorithms to determine when the "Royal Flush Cycle" should be sacrificed to chase a high-value quad, but in Bonus Poker, these conflicts are rare.
