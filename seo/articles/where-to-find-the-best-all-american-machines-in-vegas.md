# Where to Find the Best All American Machines in Vegas

Las Vegas is the world capital of Video Poker, but not all machines are created equal. For an **All American** enthusiast, the city is a scavenger hunt for the legendary **8/8/8 "Full Pay"** pay table. While the "Strip" casinos often have poorer odds, the "Locals" and "Downtown" casinos still hide some of the best mathematical bets in the world.

Here is your guide to finding the best All American action in Las Vegas for 2026.

## 1. Downtown Las Vegas: The "Advantage" Hub

Downtown (Fremont Street) is traditionally where the best Video Poker is found.
*   **The California (The Cal):** Known for its "Hawaiian" hospitality and its excellent Video Poker. Look for their older "I-Game" or "Game King" machines. They often keep Full Pay All American active to attract serious grinders.
*   **Main Street Station:** One of the most beautiful casinos in Vegas, it is also famous for its pay tables. Check the machines near the "Boar's Head Bar."
*   **The D Las Vegas:** Head to the second floor (the "Vintage Vegas" section). You might find some older coin-operated or early TITO machines that still offer 100%+ returns.

## 2. The "Locals" Casinos (Off-Strip)

Casinos that cater to Las Vegas residents must offer better odds to keep people coming back.
*   **South Point:** Located south of the Strip on Las Vegas Blvd, South Point is a Video Poker mecca. They have thousands of machines, and you can almost always find 8/8/8 All American if you are willing to walk the floor.
*   **Station Casinos (Palace, Red Rock, Green Valley Ranch):** Look for machines labeled **"100% Payback"** or **"Great Stuff."** These banks of machines are specifically designed to offer a player advantage. Check the "All American" option on these multi-game units.

## 3. The Las Vegas Strip: Be Selective

Finding good All American on the Strip is difficult but not impossible.
*   **The Cosmopolitan:** Known for a high-end Video Poker scene. While the main floor is tough, their high-limit room sometimes features "Full Pay" options at higher denominations ($5, $10, $25).
*   **The Strat:** Often overlooked, the Stratosphere (now The Strat) offers better odds than the "Mega-Resorts" in the center of the Strip. 
*   **Ellis Island:** Just a block behind the Strip (near Bally's/Horseshoe), this tiny casino is a legend for its low-stakes, high-paying Video Poker and cheap beer.

In Vegas, All American is usually part of a **"Game King"** multi-game machine.

3.  **DO NOT HIT DEAL YET.** Look at the pay table on the screen.
