### The Ace Gap: Targeted vs. Uniform quads

In **Super Aces Bonus**, the reward is concentrated in Achieving Four Aces with a kicker (Ace, 2, 3, or 4) for a massive bonus. **Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 80% overlap on the core hand results. However, in Super Aces Bonus, you prioritize Aces and kickers above any other natural card. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the Aces in Super Aces Bonus dominate the EV landscape. In Bonus Poker Deluxe, any quad is a major win.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Ace Priority:** In Super Aces Bonus, prioritize Aces above all other ranks.
2.  **Kicker Awareness:** Learn which cards are "Kickers" for the Ace bonus in Super Aces Bonus.
3.  **Return Priority:** Super Aces Bonus offers a superior theoretical return (99.94%), making it the better choice for the long-term player who can handle high volatility.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that Super Aces Bonus is the superior game for the expert player seeking a higher return, while Bonus Poker Deluxe is better for those who want a stable ride. In **Super Aces Bonus**, the rewards are concentrated on Achieving Four Aces with a kicker. In **Double Jackpot Poker**, the rewards are tiered across Aces, Faces, and low-rank quads. This leads to a much lower **Volatility Normalization** (28.5 vs 60.0+).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is high strategy overlap (approximately 80%). However, in Double Jackpot Poker, you prioritize face cards much more than in Super Aces Bonus. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the high-tier quads in both games dominate their respective EV landscapes.

2.  **Kicker Removal:** You no longer need to manage kickers for the bonus in Double Jackpot.
3.  **Return Priority:** Super Aces Bonus offers a superior theoretical return (99.94%), making it the better choice for the advantage player.

A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both games offer excellent returns, but Double Jackpot Poker is the more straightforward choice for the expert player who wants natural action. # The Payout Gap: Super Aces Bonus vs. Pick 'em Poker Explained
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Super Aces Bonus** to **Pick 'em Poker** involves moving from a "High-Volatility Draw" variant to a "Low-Volatility Choice" variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you approach **Volatility Normalization**.

### The Comparison Matrix: Super Aces Bonus vs. Pick 'em Poker

| Feature | Super Aces Bonus (Full Pay) | Pick 'em Poker (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.94% | 99.95% |
| **Variance Index** | 60.0+ | 15.0 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | Four Aces with Kicker | High-Payout High Pair Connectivity |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.06% | 0.05% |

### The Choice Gap: Draw vs. Choice

In **Super Aces Bonus**, the reward is concentrated in Achieving natural quads through drawing cards. In **Pick 'em Poker**, the focus is on choosing the better of two fixed outcome stacks. This leads to an extreme **Volatility Normalization** (15.0 vs 60.0+).

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is zero (0%). Both are fundamentally different games. Super Aces Bonus is a draw game; Pick 'em Poker is a choice-based game. In Super Aces Bonus, you prioritize natural quad potential. In Pick 'em Poker, you always choose the highest EV stack. This is the essence of **EV Maximization** in high-volume games.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Simplify Everything:** You are only making one choice in Pick 'em Poker: left or right.
2.  **High Pair Priority:** In Pick 'em Poker, prioritize natural high pairs starting at 10s.
3.  **Return Priority:** Both games offer excellent theoretical returns, making them top-tier choices for the expert player.

Through a detailed **video poker house edge analysis**, it's clear that while both games offer similar returns, Pick 'em Poker is the more reliable choice for those who want a stable return. The **best video poker strategy comparison** for this transition is: Use the draw for the action, or the choice for the return.
