# The History and Evolution of Jacks or Better: The Gold Standard

Among all the variants of video poker, none is as iconic or foundational as **Jacks or Better**. Often referred to as "Draw Poker" in its early days, it is the game that launched a thousand casino careers and remains the baseline for every other variant on the market. Let's dive into the **video poker history facts** that made Jacks or Better a legend.

## The Birth of a Legend (1979)
The story of Jacks or Better begins with William "Si" Redd and his company, **SIRCOMA** (Si Redd's Coin Machines). In 1979, SIRCOMA released a machine called **Draw Poker**. Unlike the mechanical "poker" machines of the late 19th century, this was a fully digital game that used a television monitor and a solid-state computer to deal cards.

Initially, players were skeptical. Why play poker against a machine when you could play against people? However, the privacy and speed of the machine eventually won them over. When SIRCOMA changed its name to **International Game Technology (IGT)** in 1980, Jacks or Better became its flagship product.

## The Mathematical Evolution: From EPROMs to PRNGs
The early Jacks or Better machines used **EPROM** (Erasable Programmable Read-Only Memory) chips to store the game's logic. These chips contained the "PAR Sheets" (Probability Accounting Reports) that dictated the house edge and payout structures. 

At the heart of these machines was the **PRNG (Pseudo-Random Number Generator)**. This algorithm ensures that every hand is dealt from a virtual 52-card deck with the same randomness as a physical shuffle. 

### Is Video Poker Rigged? Explained
A common question among new players is: **is video poker rigged explained** simply? For Jacks or Better, the answer is no. Because the game's mechanics are so transparent—a 52-card deck and a visible payout table—regulatory bodies can easily audit the PRNG code to ensure it meets strict randomness standards. Every hand is an independent event, and there are no "hot" or "cold" cycles.

## The 9/6 Full Pay Revolution
In the 1980s, the "9/6" version of Jacks or Better became the "Gold Standard" for serious players. A "9/6" machine pays:
*   **9 coins** for a Full House
*   **6 coins** for a Flush

With perfect **Expert Logic** and strategy, a 9/6 Jacks or Better machine has a Return to Player (RTP) of **99.54%**. This incredibly low house edge (0.46%) made it one of the few games in the casino where a skilled player could nearly break even over the long term.

## The Impact on Modern Gaming
Jacks or Better laid the groundwork for everything that followed:
*   **Bonus Poker Variants:** Games like Double Bonus Poker and Deuces Wild are simply Jacks or Better with modified payout tables.
*   **Multi-Play Machines:** The "Triple Play" and "Five Play" machines that are common today all owe their existence to the original 1979 SIRCOMA design.
*   **Online Video Poker:** The transition to online and mobile gaming in the 2000s saw Jacks or Better as the first game to be ported to digital platforms.
