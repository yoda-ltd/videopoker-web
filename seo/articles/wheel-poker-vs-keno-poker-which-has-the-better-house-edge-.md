# Wheel Poker vs. Keno Poker: Which Has the Better House Edge?

In the casino, players are often torn between the strategic depth of video poker and the "big win" potential of Keno. Branded variants like **Wheel Poker** and **Keno Poker** try to bridge this gap. But which one offers the better house edge? This comparison will look at the **Bonus Event EV** of the Wheel versus the "Catch" probability of Keno to find the winner.

## Wheel Poker: The Poker-First Choice

Wheel Poker is 100% video poker. You play the cards, and if you get a Three-of-a-Kind, you spin the wheel for a bonus.
*   **Trigger:** Natural Three-of-a-Kind.
*   **Bonus Event EV:** ~470 coins on a 2,000-coin max wheel.
*   **Strategy:** Pure video poker. Follow a **wheel poker strategy guide** to maximize your RTP.

The house edge in Wheel Poker is based on the poker pay table. If you play optimally, the edge is often as low as 1.5%.

## Keno Poker: The Hybrid Gamble

Keno Poker (sometimes called "Keno Draw") allows you to pick numbers like a Keno game, and then those numbers are "matched" against the cards dealt in your poker hand (or a secondary draw).
*   **The Problem:** Keno is historically one of the worst games in the casino for the player, with house edges often exceeding 20%. 
*   **The Hybrid Edge:** Even when combined with video poker, the "Keno" portion of the game drags down the overall RTP.

## Head-to-Head: House Edge Comparison

### 1. Skill vs. Luck
*   **Wheel Poker:** Highly skill-dependent. Your ability to hold the right cards directly affects how often you trigger the wheel and your overall return.
*   **Keno Poker:** Mostly luck-based. No amount of skill can help you "pick" the right numbers in Keno.

### 2. Transparency
In Wheel Poker, you can see the pay table and calculate the edge. In Keno Poker, the "Keno" payouts are often obscured, making it difficult to know exactly how much the house is taking.

## Comparison Table

| **Typical House Edge** | 1.5% - 2.5% | 5.0% - 15.0% |

## Why Wheel Poker is the Clear Winner

If you care about your bankroll, **Wheel Poker** is the only logical choice. Keno is a "money pit" designed for casual players who don't mind a high house edge in exchange for a lottery-style win. Wheel Poker gives you the same "big wheel" excitement but keeps the house edge within the realm of professional-grade video poker.

## Conclusion

Don't be seduced by the "cross-over" appeal of Keno Poker. The "Keno" element is almost always a tax on the player. Stick to the **wheel poker strategy guide**, master your holds, and let the wheel provide the bonuses. You'll get more play for your money and a much fairer shot at a winning session. In the battle of the house edge, the Wheel crushes the Keno ticket every time.
