# Why Double Bonus is Better Than Aces and Faces for Beginners

In the world of "Ace-Focused" video poker, the choice between "Double Bonus" (DB) and "Aces and Faces" (AF) is a study in **Strategic Convergence** and the **Variance Index**. This **best video poker strategy comparison** breaks down why Double Bonus is the superior entry point for novices, focusing on **Payout Compression** and the "Strategy Drift" caused by the different bonus structures.

## Strategic Convergence: The "Linear" Advantage

**Double Bonus Poker** is a "Standard-Bonus" game. The strategy is almost identical to Jacks or Better, with adjustments for the "Heavy" payouts on 4-of-a-Kind. Because it follows the "Standard" bonus mechanic, the strategy is "Linear" and "Logical." Beginners can learn the basic "Hierarchy" of hands and apply it with high accuracy. The **video poker house edge analysis** is straightforward and predictable.

**Aces and Faces** introduces a "Niche" bonus structure that focuses on Aces and Face cards. This creates "Strategy Bifurcation." For every hand, you must now ask: "Is the Aces and Faces bonus better than the standard hold?" The strategy is "Unique" and often "Counter-Intuitive" compared to standard bonus games. For a beginner, this is "Cognitive Overload." You can easily make a mistake by holding "Face Cards" when the math suggests a different path. This "Strategy Drift" can cost you 1-2% in "Theoretical Return" over a session.

## Payout Compression: The "Smooth" vs. The "Face-Specific"

In **Double Bonus**, the "Cost" of the massive payouts for 4-of-a-Kind is built into the Two-Pair and Full House payouts. However, the game remains "Smooth" because the frequency of 4-of-a-Kind is relatively high. Your bankroll "Churns" nicely. You are "Rewarded" for hitting common hands with a "Fair" payout (often 10/7 or 9/6).

In **Aces and Faces**, the "Cost" of the "Face Card" bonuses is built into the *entire* payout table. To "Pay" for these specific "Aces and Faces" wins, the payouts for *all* other hands (like Flushes and Straights) are "Compressed." You often get only "Money Back" on hands that would pay more in a standard game. For a beginner, this is "Psychological Torture." You hit a "Winning" hand, but your bankroll doesn't grow. This "Bleed" is much more aggressive than in Double Bonus.

## The Variance Index: Sustainability vs. Volatility

Our **video poker house edge analysis** shows that:
*   **Double Bonus** has a variance of around **28**. It is a "Medium-Volatility" game. It offers "Stability" and "Longevity."
*   **Aces and Faces** has a variance of around **30-40**. While the variance is "Similar," the "Unique Strategy" and "Compressed Payouts" lead to a higher "Effective Error Rate" for beginners.

For the new player who wants to enjoy a long session and learn the fundamentals with a "Linear" structure, Double Bonus is the clear winner. It provides the "Stability" of a standard bonus game with the "Excitement" of well-balanced payouts. Leave the "Aces and Faces" hunting to the pros who can handle the "Complexity" and the "Swings."
