## The Math of the Multi-Strike Royal Flush

In a standard 52-card deck, the odds of hitting a Royal Flush are approximately 1 in 40,000. However, in Multi-Strike, the "effective" odds of hitting a Royal Flush at the 8x multiplier (Level 4) are much steeper.

### 1. Sequential Probability
To reach Level 4, you must either win the previous level or receive a "Free Ride." 
- **The Advancement Filter:** If your average win rate is ~45%, your probability of naturally reaching Level 4 is approximately $0.45^3 \approx 9\%$. 
- **Effective Odds:** This means your chances of hitting a Royal Flush at the 8x level are roughly 10 times harder than on Level 1. This is a form of **Multi-Hand Volatility Scaling** where the variance is stacked vertically rather than horizontally.

### 2. Independent Draw Randomness
Every level in Multi-Strike is an **Independent Trial**. 
- Even though the game is played on a ladder, the RNG for Level 4 has zero memory of Level 1. The **Independent Draw Randomness** ensures that once you are at Level 4, your probability of hitting a Royal Flush is the standard 1/40,000 for that specific hand.

## Combinatorial Volatility and the "Level 4" Chasing Strategy

- **Levels 1-3 (The Climb):** Your goal is advancement. You play a "conservative" strategy to ensure you reach the next multiplier.
- **Level 4 (The Peak):** Once you reach the 8x level, there is no higher level to advance to. Your strategy shifts to "Aggressive Chasing." This is the only time the **Independent Draw Randomness** should be leveraged to chase low-probability, high-EV hands like 3-to-a-Royal.
