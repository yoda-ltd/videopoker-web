# Loose Deuces Payout Table Explained: 25/15 vs 20/12 and More

In the **best odds video poker** world, Loose Deuces is one of the most exciting variants because of its massive payout for Four Deuces. For the professional player, this represents a unique opportunity for **Pay Table Optimization**.

## The Best Odds Index: 25/15 Loose Deuces (101.60%)
In the **highest return video poker games** 2026 index, Loose Deuces is a top-tier choice. The "Full Pay" version offers a **Theoretical Return Convergence of 101.60%**.

To identify this machine, look for these specific payouts:
*   **Four Deuces:** 500-for-1 (instead of 200).
*   **Five of a Kind:** 25-for-1.
*   **Wild Royal Flush:** 25-for-1.
*   **Four of a Kind:** 15-for-1.
*   **Full House:** 10-for-1.
*   **Flush:** 8-for-1.

Wait, why do we call it "25/15" Loose Deuces? It's the pay table that pays **25 coins for a Five of a Kind AND 15 coins for a Four of a Kind**.

## Analysis of "Short Pay" Predation
Because 101.60% gives the player a massive edge, casinos often use "Short Pay" tables to reverse it.
*   **20/12 Loose Deuces:** Paying only 20 for Five of a Kind and 12 for Four of a Kind drops the RTP to **99.20%**.
*   **15/10 Loose Deuces:** Paying 15 for Five of a Kind and 10 for Four of a Kind drops the RTP to **97.80%**.
*   **Common Short Pay (12/8):** This variant drops the RTP below **96%**.

The **EV Gap** between the 101.60% version and the 96% version is over 5.6%. If you are playing 800 hands per hour at a $1 machine, that’s $56 per hour you’re losing simply by picking the wrong machine. This "predatory" short-pay table kills the professional edge.

## House Edge Variance in Loose Deuces
Loose Deuces has higher **House Edge Variance** than Jacks or Better. This is because the game's return is heavily weighted towards the Four Deuces. If you don't hit the Four Deuces at the mathematically expected frequency, your actual return will lag behind the 101.60% theoretical return. Professional bankroll management is essential.
