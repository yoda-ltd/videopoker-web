# The Payout Gap: Bonus Poker vs. Aces and Faces Explained
Category: Comparison

Understanding the **Strategic Divergence** between **Bonus Poker** and **Aces and Faces** is key to mastering these high-payout variants. This comparison focuses on how the paytables differ and why those differences lead to unique **EV Maximization** paths.

### The Comparison Matrix: Bonus Poker vs. Aces and Faces

| Feature | Bonus Poker (8/5) | Aces and Faces (8/5) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.17% | 99.26% |
| **Variance Index** | 20.9 | 22.1 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | Targeted Bonuses (Aces/2s-4s) | Face Card Quad Bonuses |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.83% | 0.74% |

### The Rank Gap: Aces vs. Faces

In **Bonus Poker**, the payouts for four-of-a-kind with Aces or 2s-4s are significantly increased. In **Aces and Faces**, the rewards are extended to include Kings, Queens, and Jacks. This leads to a similar **Volatility Normalization** (22.1 vs 20.9).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is high strategy overlap (approximately 85%) because both are based on a 52-card natural deck. However, in Aces and Faces, you prioritize face cards much more than in Bonus Poker. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the face cards in Aces and Faces dominate the EV landscape for middle-tier wins.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Face Card Priority:** In Aces and Faces, prioritize Kings, Queens, and Jacks much more than in Bonus Poker.
2.  **Tiered Quad Awareness:** In Bonus Poker, you must be aware of the specific bonuses for 2s-4s.
3.  **Return Priority:** Aces and Faces offers a superior theoretical return (99.26%), making it the better choice for the long-term player.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that Aces and Faces is the superior game for the player looking for the lowest house edge, while Bonus Poker is better for those who enjoy the excitement of low-rank bonuses. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are solid choices for the recreational player.

# The Payout Gap: Bonus Poker vs. Double Bonus Explained
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Bonus Poker** to **Double Bonus** is a shift from a "Moderate Bonus" variant to a "High Bonus" variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your hands and the volatility that comes with them.

### The Comparison Matrix: Bonus Poker vs. Double Bonus

| Feature | Bonus Poker (8/5) | Double Bonus (10/7) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.17% | 100.17% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 20.9 | 28.5 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | Moderate Quad Bonuses | High-Payout Quad Bonuses |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.83% | -0.17% (Player Edge) |

### The Bonus Gap: Moderate vs. High

In **Bonus Poker**, the rewards are frequent but moderate. In **Double Bonus Poker**, the rewards are significantly higher, with all quads paying more. This leads to a much higher **Volatility Normalization** (28.5 vs 20.9).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is high strategy overlap (approximately 80%). However, in Double Bonus, you prioritize Aces and other high-bonus quads much more than in Bonus Poker. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the high-tier quads in Double Bonus dominate the EV landscape.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Ace Priority:** In Double Bonus, Aces are the highest-paying quads. You prioritize them over almost any other card rank.
2.  **Full House Break:** In Double Bonus, you break a Full House containing three Aces to draw for the fourth Ace.
3.  **Variance Management:** Double Bonus is much more "swingy" than Bonus Poker. You need a larger bankroll to withstand the swings.

A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that Double Bonus is a superior choice for the expert player who wants a high return with an edge. The **best video poker strategy comparison** highlights that high-payout quads are the ultimate tool for **EV Maximization** in Double Bonus.

# The Payout Gap: Bonus Poker vs. Double Double Bonus Explained
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Bonus Poker** to **Double Double Bonus** involves moving from a "Standard Bonus" variant to a "Kicker-Based" variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you approach **Volatility Normalization**.

### The Comparison Matrix: Bonus Poker vs. Double Double Bonus

| Feature | Bonus Poker (8/5) | Double Double Bonus (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.17% | 98.98% |
| **Variance Index** | 20.9 | 42.0 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | Moderate Quad Bonuses | Quads with Kickers |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.83% | 1.02% |

### The Kicker Gap: Natural vs. Kicker

In **Bonus Poker**, the reward is concentrated in Achieving natural quads. In **Double Double Bonus Poker**, the reward depends on the fifth card in the hand (the "Kicker"). This creates a much higher **Volatility Normalization** (42.0 vs 20.9).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 70% overlap. In Bonus Poker, you would never hold a kicker with a pair. In Double Double Bonus, you prioritize kicker combinations above all else, often breaking pat winners to maximize the kicker potential. This is the essence of **EV Maximization** in kicker-based games.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Kicker Awareness:** You must learn which cards are "Kickers" for each quad tier.
2.  **Ace-Kicker Priority:** Holding a pair of Aces with a low kicker is a valid strategy in Double Double Bonus.
3.  **Variance Management:** Double Double Bonus is much more "swingy" than Bonus Poker. You need a larger bankroll to withstand the swings.

Through a detailed **video poker house edge analysis**, it's clear that while Double Double Bonus is the most popular game, it is financially taxing. The **best video poker strategy comparison** for this transition is: Use the kickers to find the massive bonuses, but only if you can handle the 42.0 variance.
