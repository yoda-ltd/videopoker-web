# Is Max Bet Always Better for Bankroll Longevity?

One of the most debated topics among casual players is whether to bet 1 coin or 5 coins. Specifically, "Is max bet always better for bankroll longevity?" The answer is more nuanced than a simple yes or no.

### The Royal Flush Bonus
In almost all video poker games, betting the 5th coin unlocks a massive bonus for the Royal Flush. 
- 1 Coin: 250 units
- 2 Coins: 500 units
- 3 Coins: 750 units
- 4 Coins: 1000 units
- **5 Coins: 4000 units** (A significant jump from the linear 1250!)

This bonus accounts for approximately **1% to 1.5% of the total RTP**. If you play 1 coin, you are essentially playing a machine with a 1.5% higher house edge.

### Variance vs. Sustainability
While max bet offers the highest **Theoretical Return**, it also increases your **Variance**. If you have $100 and play $1 denomination at 1 coin ($1/hand), you have 100 bets. If you play $1 denomination at 5 coins ($5/hand), you only have 20 bets. 
For **Session Sustainability**, it is always better to play a lower denomination at max bet ($0.25 at 5 coins = $1.25/hand) than a higher denomination at single coin ($1 at 1 coin = $1/hand).

### Strategy Adjustments
When you bet the maximum, your strategy changes. You become more aggressive in chasing the Royal Flush because the payoff is so much higher. If you are a beginner looking for **video poker tips for beginners**, understand that "Max Bet" is the only way to reach the advertised RTP of the machine.

### The Bottom Line
If you want to know **how to win video poker daily**, you must play max bet. Playing anything less is giving the casino an unnecessary advantage. If the max bet is too high for your bankroll, move to a cheaper machine, but keep that 5-coin button pressed.
