# All American Payout Table Explained: 9/6 vs 8/5 and More

When it comes to **best odds video poker**, All American Poker is a hidden gem that many recreational players avoid because of its strange payout structure. For the savvy player, this represents a unique opportunity for **Pay Table Optimization**.

## The Best Odds Index: 9/6 All American (100.72%)
In the **highest return video poker games** 2026 index, 9/6 All American is a top-tier choice. It offers a **Theoretical Return Convergence of 100.72%**.

The "9/6" designation here is unique because All American pays:
*   **Straight Flush:** 200 coins per 1 coin bet (huge!).
*   **Four of a Kind:** 34 coins per 1 coin bet.
*   **Full House:** 8 coins per 1 coin bet.
*   **Flush:** 8 coins per 1 coin bet.
*   **Straight:** 8 coins per 1 coin bet.

Wait, why do we call it "9/6" All American? Historically, it's the pay table that pays **9 coins for a Full House AND 6 coins for a Flush**. In the 2026 version of All American, look for the "Full Pay" 8/8/8 (Full House/Flush/Straight) table.

## Analysis of "Short Pay" Predation
Because 100.72% gives the player the edge, casinos often use "Short Pay" tables to reverse it.
*   **8/8/7 All American:** Paying only 7 coins for a Straight drops the RTP to **99.60%**.
*   **8/7/7 All American:** Paying 7 for Flush and 7 for Straight drops the RTP to **98.49%**.
*   **Common Short Pay (8/5/5):** This variant drops the RTP below **96%**.

The **EV Gap** between the 100.72% version and the 96% version is over 4.7%. If you are playing 800 hands per hour at a $1 machine, that’s $47 per hour you’re losing simply by picking the wrong machine. This "predatory" short-pay table kills the professional edge.

## House Edge Variance in All American
All American has higher **House Edge Variance** than Jacks or Better. This is because the game's return is heavily weighted towards the Straight Flush (200-for-1). If you don't hit the Straight Flush at the mathematically expected frequency, your actual return will lag behind the 100.72% theoretical return. Professional bankroll management is essential.
