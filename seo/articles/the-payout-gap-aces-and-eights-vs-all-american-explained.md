# The Payout Gap: Aces and Eights vs. All American Explained
Category: Comparison

Understanding the **Strategic Divergence** between **Aces and Eights** and **All American** is key to mastering these high-payout variants. This comparison focuses on how the paytables differ and why those differences lead to unique **EV Maximization** paths.

### The Comparison Matrix: Aces and Eights vs. All American

| Feature | Aces and Eights (9/6) | All American (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.78% | 100.72% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 21.8 | 60.0 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | Quad Bonuses (Aces/8s/7s) | High Payouts for Straights/Flushes |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.22% | -0.72% (Player Edge) |

### The Connectivity Gap: Straights and Flushes

In **All American**, the payouts for Straights and Flushes are significantly increased (34-for-1 and 8-for-1 respectively), compared to the standard 4-for-1 and 6-for-1 in Aces and Eights. This creates a massive **Volatility Normalization** shift. In All American, you are looking for connectivity above all else, while in Aces and Eights, you are hunting for specific quad tiers.

**Strategy Overlap:** Approximately 50% of your Jacks or Better strategy applies, but you must unlearn the habit of holding low pairs over 4-to-a-flush in All American. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the high-volume draws in All American dominate the EV landscape.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Connectivity Priority:** In All American, prioritize 4-to-a-flush and 4-to-a-straight over small pairs.
2.  **Quad Devaluation:** In All American, quads pay the standard 125 credits. In Aces and Eights, they are your primary "Bonus" target.
3.  **Bankroll Requirement:** Because of the Variance Index of 60.0, All American requires a much larger bankroll to withstand the swings of high-payout draws.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that All American is the superior game for the professional player looking for an edge, while Aces and Eights is better for the recreational player seeking a stable return. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are elite choices for those who master their respective strategies.

# The Payout Gap: Aces and Eights vs. Joker Poker Explained
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Aces and Eights** to **Joker Poker** involves a shift from a "Natural" variant to a "Wild Card" variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your hands and the volatility that comes with them.

### The Comparison Matrix: Aces and Eights vs. Joker Poker

| Feature | Aces and Eights (9/6) | Joker Poker (Kings or Better) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.78% | 100.64% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 21.8 | 15.5 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | Quad Bonuses | Wild Card Connectivity (Joker) |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.22% | -0.64% (Player Edge) |

### The Wild Gap: The 53rd Card

In **Joker Poker**, the Joker is your most powerful tool, allowing you to complete hands that would otherwise be worthless. In **Aces and Eights**, all 52 cards are natural, and you are looking for specific bonuses on four-of-a-kind hands. This creates a lower **Volatility Normalization** (15.5 vs 21.8).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 65% overlap. In Aces and Eights, you would never discard a high pair. In Joker Poker, you prioritize the Joker above all else, often breaking pairs to maximize the wild card's potential. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the wild card in Joker Poker dominates the EV landscape.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Joker Supremacy:** Never discard a Joker. It is the single most valuable card for **EV Maximization**.
2.  **High Card Floor:** In Joker Poker, the minimum for a return is often Kings or Better. This means you hold Kings and Aces much more frequently.
3.  **Five of a Kind Target:** This hand becomes your secondary target. In Aces and Eights, your goal is the "Special Quad." In Joker Poker, you are looking for Five of a Kind using the Joker.

A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that Joker Poker is a superior choice for the expert player who wants a high return with low variance. The **best video poker strategy comparison** highlights that the wild card is the ultimate tool for **Volatility Normalization**.

# The Payout Gap: Aces and Eights vs. Loose Deuces Explained
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Aces and Eights** to **Loose Deuces** is one of the most extreme shifts in payout structures. You are moving from a low-volatility, quad-focused game to a high-volatility, wild-card jackpot game. This is the ultimate test of **Strategic Divergence**.

### The Comparison Matrix: Aces and Eights vs. Loose Deuces

| Feature | Aces and Eights (9/6) | Loose Deuces (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.78% | 101.60% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 21.8 | 200.0+ |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | Quad Bonuses | Four Deuces Jackpot |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.22% | -1.60% (Player Edge) |

### The Jackpot Gap: Deuce Hunting

In **Aces and Eights**, the rewards are frequent and moderate. In **Loose Deuces**, the reward is massive but rare (the Four Deuces payout). This creates extreme **Volatility Normalization** (200.0+ vs 21.8). You are no longer playing "poker"; you are playing a jackpot simulation.

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is minimal (less than 10%). In Aces and Eights, you would never discard a pat winner. In Loose Deuces, you break almost every pat winner (except a Royal) to hunt for Deuces. This is the ultimate form of **EV Maximization** in high-variance games.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Deuce Obsession:** Every Deuce is a keeper. Three Deuces is a massive hold, even if you have a "Natural" winner.
2.  **Quad Devaluation:** In Loose Deuces, natural quads are almost irrelevant compared to the Four Deuces.
3.  **Variance Management:** Because of the Variance Index of 200+, Loose Deuces requires a massive bankroll to withstand the swings.

Through a detailed **video poker house edge analysis**, it's clear that while Loose Deuces has the highest RTP, it is emotionally and financially taxing. The **best video poker strategy comparison** for this transition is: Forget the bonuses; find the Deuces, or lose.
