# Double Jackpot Poker vs. Super Star Poker: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Double Jackpot Poker and Super Star Poker represent two different ways casinos package video poker. Double Jackpot is a specific **game variant** with its own rules and pay table, while Super Star Poker is a **multi-game platform** that often includes Double Jackpot among dozens of other options.

To find the best house edge, you need to understand the difference between the game and the machine it's played on.

## Double Jackpot Poker: The High-Return Variant

Double Jackpot Poker is a "Bonus" style game that focuses on premiums for face cards and Aces.

*   **Four Aces:** 160-for-1.
*   **Four J, Q, K:** 80-for-1.
*   **Theoretical RTP (Full Pay 9/6):** **99.63%**
*   **House Edge:** 0.37%

Double Jackpot is one of the most player-friendly games in the casino, provided you find the 9/6 version.

## Super Star Poker: The Multi-Game Machine

Super Star Poker is a brand of multi-game machines (similar to Game King) produced by IGT. These machines offer a variety of games, including:
*   Standard Video Poker (Jacks or Better, Double Bonus, etc.)
*   Multi-Hand Poker (3-play, 5-play, 10-play)
*   Specialty Games (Ultimate X, Super Times Pay)
*   Video Blackjack and Keno

### The House Edge on Super Star Poker
The house edge on a Super Star Poker machine isn't fixed. It depends on which specific game you select from the menu.
*   **Multi-Play Advantage:** Many games on Super Star Poker offer "Multi-Play" (playing 3 or more hands at once). While this doesn't change the *percentage* house edge, it increases your total wagering and can lead to much faster bankroll swings.
*   **The "Convenience" Tax:** Because Super Star Poker machines are popular and expensive for casinos to lease, they often feature "short-pay" tables for the standard games. It is common to see a 8/5 Double Jackpot game on a Super Star machine even if there is a 9/6 standalone machine across the aisle.

## Comparison: Head-to-Head

### 1. Mathematical Return (House Edge)
If you play Double Jackpot on a standalone machine vs. Double Jackpot on a Super Star Poker machine, **the standalone machine almost always wins.**
Standalone machines are more likely to offer "Full Pay" tables (9/6), while multi-game machines like Super Star Poker often default to "Short Pay" (8/5 or 7/5) to maximize casino profit across all the included games.

### 2. Variety and Features
*   **Double Jackpot (Standalone)** is for the purist. You sit down, you play one hand at a time, and you focus on the strategy for that specific game.
*   **Super Star Poker** is for the player who wants variety. You can play Double Jackpot for 10 minutes, then switch to Ultimate X or even a round of Blackjack without moving seats.

### 3. Bankroll Requirements
*   **Double Jackpot** standalone play is predictable. You bet 5 coins per hand.
*   **Super Star Poker** can be a bankroll killer. If you choose a "Multi-Hand" or "Ultimate X" version of Double Jackpot, you could be betting 30 or 50 coins per round.

## Verdict: Which Should You Play?

*   **Play Double Jackpot (Standalone) if** you can find a machine with a 9/6 pay table. This is your best chance to play with a house edge of under 0.5%.
*   **Play Super Star Poker if** you want to play multiple hands at once or if you enjoy the specialty features (like multipliers) that standalone machines don't offer. 

**Pro Tip:** If you use a Super Star Poker machine, **always check the pay table for every game you play.** Just because the Jacks or Better game has a good pay table doesn't mean the Double Jackpot game on the same screen will! Casinos "mix and match" pay tables on multi-game machines to trick players. Always look for the 9 and the 6!
