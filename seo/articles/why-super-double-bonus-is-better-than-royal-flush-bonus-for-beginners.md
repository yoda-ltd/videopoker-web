# Why Super Double Bonus is Better Than Royal Flush Bonus for Beginners

For the beginning video poker player, the "Bonus" world can be confusing. Two of the most common "Bonus" variants are Super Double Bonus (SDB) and Royal Flush Bonus (RFB). This **best video poker strategy comparison** breaks down why Super Double Bonus is almost always the superior choice for a beginner, focusing on **Variance Scaling** and **Payout Compression**.

## Variance Scaling: The "Balanced" vs. The "Top-Heavy"

**Super Double Bonus** is a "Balanced" bonus game. It pays "Nested" bonuses for different sets of four-of-a-kind (Quads). 4 Aces pay more than 4 Kings, which pay more than 4 Jacks. This means you have "Stair-step" goals. You can hit *any* set of Quads and feel like you've won a "Bonus." The **Variance Scaling** is manageable because the "Bonus" is distributed across multiple hands.

**Royal Flush Bonus**, as the name implies, is "Top-Heavy." It typically puts a huge "Bonus" on the Royal Flush (often 4,000 coins + a progressive or extra bonus). To fund this, the game uses heavy **Payout Compression** on the mid-tier hands (like Flushes and Straights). For a beginner, this is "Mathematical Suicide." You will go through thousands of hands without hitting the Royal, "Bleeding" bankroll on the compressed lower hands.

## Payout Compression and the "Money Back" Threshold

In **Super Double Bonus**, you typically still get "Money Back" on Two-Pair (depending on the specific pay table, but often 1-to-1). This is the cornerstone of bankroll preservation. 

In **Royal Flush Bonus**, the **Payout Compression** is often so severe that Two-Pair only pays 1-to-1, *and* the Flush/Straight payouts are reduced to 4/3 or 5/4. This creates a massive **EV Gap** during your standard playtime. Beginners often don't realize that they are paying a "Royal Tax" on every single hand they play.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Royal" Trap

The **Strategy Bifurcation** is most dangerous in Royal Flush Bonus:
*   In **Royal Flush Bonus**, the "Bonus" might tempt you to play "Recklessly" for the Royal. You might break a "High Pair" to chase a 3-card Royal just because the "Bonus" is so big. This is a "Psychological EV Trap."
*   In **Super Double Bonus**, the strategy is more "Logical." You are playing for "Quads," which are much more frequent than Royals. This is much easier for a beginner to master and enjoy.

For the new player who wants to enjoy a long session and hit a "Bonus" more than once every 40,000 hands, Super Double Bonus is the clear winner. Leave the "Royal" hunting to the professionals.
