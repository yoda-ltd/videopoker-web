# Ultimate All American Strategy Chart for 2026

All American Video Poker is not for the faint of heart. Its unique payout structure—which heavily rewards Straights, Flushes, and Straight Flushes—requires an aggressive strategy that often contradicts the "common sense" of Jacks or Better. This 2026 guide provides the mathematical hierarchy needed to conquer this high-variance game and secure the **best odds for All American**.

## The All American Strategy Hierarchy

This hierarchy is optimized for "Full Pay" All American machines where Straights and Flushes pay 8-for-1.

1.  **Royal Flush, Straight Flush, 4 of a Kind.**
2.  **4 to a Royal Flush.**
3.  **Straight, Flush, Full House:** Unlike other games, keep these pat hands over almost any draw.
4.  **3 of a Kind.**
5.  **4 to a Straight Flush.**
6.  **4 to a Flush:** This is a much higher priority in All American than in other variants.
7.  **4 to a Straight:** Open-ended or inside, the high payout makes the chase viable.
8.  **3 to a Royal Flush.**
9.  **High Pair (Jacks, Queens, Kings, Aces):** Note that High Pairs are lower on the list here because the "bonus" value is in the Straights and Flushes.
10. **3 to a Straight Flush.**
11. **Two Pair:** Only keep if no better draws exist, as it only pays 1-for-1.
12. **Low Pair (2s through 10s).**

## Analyzing the Volatility Index

All American has one of the highest **Volatility Indexes** among non-wild-card games. Because Two Pair only pays even money (1-for-1), your bankroll will experience significant "bleed" during standard play. You are essentially waiting for the "strike"—a high-paying Straight or Flush—to put you back in the black.

## The EV Differential of "Breaking Pairs"

One of the most difficult skills to master in an **All American video poker trainer** is knowing when to break a pair. In Jacks or Better, you would almost never break a High Pair to chase a 4-card Flush. In All American, the **EV Differential** actually favors the Flush draw. The math proves that the 8-to-1 payout on the Flush is so valuable that it's worth abandoning the "safe" return of the High Pair.

## Pay Table Verification: Hunting for the Best Odds

To find the **best odds for All American**, you must verify the payouts for the "Middle Three":
*   **Full House:** 8 (or 9)
*   **Flush:** 8
*   **Straight:** 8

If you see a machine paying 6 for a Straight or 5 for a Flush, walk away. The house edge on these "short-pay" machines is predatory and cannot be overcome even with perfect strategy.
