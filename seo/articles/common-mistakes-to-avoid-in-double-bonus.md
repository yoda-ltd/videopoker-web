# Common Mistakes to Avoid in Double Bonus: Professional Grinder's Warning

Double Bonus (DB) is a "Variance Monster." Offering a theoretical return of over 100% in its "Full Pay" version (10/7), it is a favorite for those learning **how to play video poker like a pro**. However, the game's unique paytable—which significantly boosts the payout for a Straight Flush—creates a strategic landscape that is radically different from any other variant. To achieve **EV Maximization**, you must identify and eliminate the specific errors that recreational players make when confronted with the "Double Bonus" math.

## Analysis: The "Straight-Flush" Squeeze and Pro Errors

### 1. The "Straight-Flush" Blindness: Under-Valuing the 50:1 Payout
The most common mistake in Double Bonus is failing to adjust for the massive increase in the Straight Flush payout.
- **The Error:** Holding a High Pair over a 4-to-a-Straight Flush.
- **The Pro Reality:** In Double Bonus, the **EV Maximization** rules shift dramatically. A 4-to-a-Straight Flush (and even some 3-to-a-Straight Flush draws) takes precedence over hands that would be "automatic" holds in Jacks or Better.
- **The Strategy Tip:** If you are dealt a pair of Kings and 4-to-a-Straight Flush, the pro **discards the Kings**. This is a classic **Hand-Ranking Inversion** that amateurs find counter-intuitive but which is mathematically essential for a professional edge.

### 2. The "Full House" Trap: Misjudging 10/7 Equity
In "Full Pay" Double Bonus, the Full House pays a generous 10:1. This leads to a unique error.
- **The Error:** Holding a Full House that includes three Aces.
- **The Pro Reality:** You **always** break a Full House to draw for Quad Aces in Double Bonus.
- **The Math:** The payout for Quad Aces (160:1) is so high that the "Theoretical Return" of the draw outweighs the 10:1 guaranteed win. This is a difficult play for "scared" players, but it's the only way to realize the game's full potential.

### 3. Mismanaging the "Two-Pair" Engine
Unlike Jacks or Better, Double Bonus only pays 1-for-1 for Two Pair.
- **The Error:** Assuming Double Bonus has the same "Smoothing Effect" as JoB.
- **The Pro Reality:** The 1-for-1 Two Pair payout is a "Bankroll Killer." It means the game has much higher **Theoretical Return Variance**.
- **The Strategy Tip:** To maintain **Session Sustainability**, you must be more aggressive in your pursuit of Quads and Straight Flushes. You are not "grinding" for Two Pair; you are "surviving" for the bonus hands.

## EV Maximization and Bankroll Sustainability

Success in Double Bonus requires a massive bankroll and a cool head.
- **Mistake:** Playing with a "Standard" bankroll (e.g., 2,000 units).
- **The Consequence:** Double Bonus has a **Standard Deviation** that is nearly 50% higher than JoB. You need at least 7,000 units to avoid "Bankroll Evaporation" during the long stretches between Straight Flushes and Quad Aces.
- **The Pro Tip:** Use "Variance Buffering." If you are playing $1 denomination, a $7,000 bankroll is the *minimum* starting point for a professional grinder.

## Analysis of Hand-Ranking Inversion: The DB Specifics

In Double Bonus, the "High Card" strategy is more complex due to the Straight Flush potential.
- **The Decision:** You are dealt 10-J-Q (suited) and a pair of 2s.
- **The Pro Play:** You discard the pair of 2s and hold the 3-to-a-Royal/Straight Flush.
- **The Reasoning:** In Bonus Poker, you hold the 2s. In Double Bonus, the combined 50:1 (Straight Flush) and 800:1 (Royal) potential makes the suited high cards the superior path to **EV Maximization**.
