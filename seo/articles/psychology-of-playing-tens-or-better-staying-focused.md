# Psychology of Playing Tens or Better: Staying Focused

Tens or Better is the most conservative variant of video poker, even more so than Jacks or Better. While its low volatility makes it easier on the bankroll, it presents its own set of psychological challenges. Mastering Tens or Better requires a unique form of **Professional Discipline** and a long-term **Pro Mindset**.

## The Trap of Boredom: Maintaining Focus in Low Volatility

In Tens or Better, the frequent small wins (like a pair of Tens) can lead to a psychological state of boredom. This is the opposite of the "high" found in games like Double Double Bonus.

- **Session Fatigue**: When the game feels "easy" and the results are predictable, your focus can slip. **Session Fatigue** in Tens or Better doesn't manifest as frustration, but as autopilot clicking.
- **The Dopamine Loop**: The "dopamine hit" in Tens or Better is weak. This can lead players to play too fast, increasing their error rate and donating their edge back to the house.
- **Expert Video Poker Strategy Tips**: To succeed, you must treat every hand with the same level of care, even if you are just holding a pair of Tens for a 1-to-1 payout.

## The "Royal Desert" in a Slow-Moving Game

Because Tens or Better pays out frequently, you can play for a long time on a small bankroll. However, this means you will spend more time in the **Royal Desert** (going 100,000+ hands without hitting a Royal Flush).

1. **Cognitive Resilience**: You must be able to endure thousands of small wins without ever hitting the "Big One." This requires the patience of a marathon runner.
2. **Loss Aversion Bias**: Even in a low-volatility game, losing streaks occur. Players who are used to winning small may "Tilt" when they lose back-to-back sessions, leading to "chasing" behavior.
3. **Tilt Mitigation**: When the cards aren't falling, you must be able to step away. **Tilt Control** is just as important in a slow-moving game as it is in a volatile one.

## Professional Discipline: The Assembly Line Approach

To learn **how to become a professional video poker player**, you must treat Tens or Better like an assembly line. 

- **The Assembly Line**: Every hand is a transaction. There is no "feeling" that a Tens or Better pair is "due."
- **Focus Audit**: Periodically ask yourself, "Am I still checking for penalty cards?" If the answer is no, you are too bored to play correctly.
- **Pro Mindset**: A pro judges their performance by the correctness of their holds, not by how much "fun" they had or how much money they made.

## Responsible Play in a Low-Stakes Game

**Responsible video poker playing** in Tens or Better means recognizing when the game's low stakes are causing you to take it less seriously.

- **Bankroll is Still King**: Even in a low-volatility game, you need a bankroll. Playing with "scared money" will still cause you to make errors.
- **Tilt Control**: If you find yourself increasing your bet just to "feel something," you are in a state of Tilt and must stop immediately.

## Conclusion
Tens or Better is a test of mental endurance. By focusing on **Tilt Control** and maintaining a rigorous **Pro Mindset**, you can navigate the slow grind and maintain your professional edge in this classic game.
