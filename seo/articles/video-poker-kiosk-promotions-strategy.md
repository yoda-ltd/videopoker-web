## 1. What are Kiosk Promotions?

Kiosk promotions are digital games, multipliers, or gift giveaways that are managed through the casino's electronic kiosks. They are designed to drive "Daily Visits" and increase your **ADT (Average Daily Theoretical)**.

## 2. Types of Kiosk-Based EV

### A. Mystery Multipliers
These are the most lucrative for video poker grinders. You swipe your card, and a digital game (like a "Spin the Wheel") determines your point multiplier for the day (e.g., 2x to 100x).
*   **The Strategy:** Only put in your high-volume play *after* you have secured a high multiplier. If you spin a 2x, it might be a **Zero-Play Day**. If you spin a 10x, it's time to grind.

### B. Swipe and Win / Earn and Get
These require a certain amount of coin-in (e.g., $500) before you can swipe for a prize.
*   **The Comps:** Prizes can include Free Play, Dining Credits, or physical gifts (electronics, cookware).
*   **The Math:** If $500 coin-in earns you a $20 "Dining Credit," you just added 4% to your RTP for that session.

### C. Daily Slot Tournaments
Many kiosks offer a free "Daily Tournament" entry. 
*   **The ROI:** While high-variance, these cost nothing but 2 minutes of your time. Top prizes are often $500 to $1,000 in Free Play.

## 3. The "Kiosk Activation" Trap

The single biggest error in **Theoretical Loss Optimization** is forgetting to "Activate" your offers.

*   **The Rule:** Always swipe your card at the kiosk *before* you sit down at a video poker machine. 
*   **Why?** Many point multipliers are not retroactive. If you play $5,000 and then swipe for a 10x multiplier, you earned 0 extra points for that $5,000. 

## 4. Gift Giveaways: The Hidden Cash Value

Casinos frequently offer physical gifts (e.g., a "KitchenAid Mixer" or a "Tumi Carry-On") for a set amount of coin-in.

*   **Comp Hustler Tip:** If you don't need the gift, check the "Secondary Market." You can often sell these brand-new items on eBay or Facebook Marketplace.
