# Video Poker Tips for Beginners: A Roadmap to Professional Play

If you're just starting out, "video poker tips for beginners" usually revolve around simple advice like "don't discard a pair." However, to achieve **Session Sustainability**, you need to look deeper into how the machine actually functions.

### The Foundation: Theoretical Return (RTP)
Every video poker machine has a **Theoretical Return**. This is the percentage of wagered money the machine is programmed to pay back over millions of hands. A 9/6 Jacks or Better machine has a 99.54% RTP with perfect strategy. As a beginner, your goal is to get as close to that number as possible by minimizing strategy errors.

### Hit Frequency Optimization
New players often chase big hands, but **Hit Frequency Optimization** is about maintaining your bankroll through small, frequent wins (like Jacks or Better or Two Pair). These smaller payouts buffer your bankroll while you wait for a high-value hand.

### Is Max Bet Always Better for Bankroll Longevity?
This is a common niche question. While betting the maximum (5 coins) activates the royal flush bonus (moving the payout from 250-per-coin to 800-per-coin), it also increases your **Variance**. If you have a limited bankroll, playing a lower denomination at max bet is almost always superior to playing a higher denomination at a single coin.

### Tips for Longevity:
1. **Always use a Player's Card**: This adds roughly 0.1% to 0.5% in "back-end" value.
2. **Master one variant**: Don't jump between Deuces Wild and Jacks or Better; the strategies are fundamentally different.
3. **Slow down**: The machine doesn't care how fast you play. Taking your time reduces the number of hands played per hour, which preserves your bankroll during cold streaks.

If you want to learn **how to win video poker daily**, you must transition from a "gambler" mindset to an "operator" mindset. Focus on the math, and the results will follow.
