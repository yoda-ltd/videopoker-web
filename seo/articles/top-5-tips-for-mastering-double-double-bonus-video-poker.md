# Top 5 Tips for Mastering Double Double Bonus Video Poker

Double Double Bonus (DDB) is one of the most exciting and widely played video poker variants in the world. It is known for its high volatility and the "all-or-nothing" nature of its massive payouts for Four-of-a-Kind with a "Kicker." To truly master this game, you must be prepared for the swings and trust in the rigorous **video poker fairness and regulation** that ensures the integrity of the deal.

Here are the top five professional tips for mastering Double Double Bonus, backed by modern **Technical Standards**.

## 1. Respect the "Kicker" Mechanic

The defining feature of DDB is the payout boost for Four-of-a-Kind when the fifth card (the kicker) is an Ace, 2, 3, or 4. 
- **The Tip:** If you are dealt Three-of-a-Kind (Aces through 4s) and the fifth card is a valid kicker, you must hold the kicker. In most other games, you would discard it.
- **Integrity Note:** The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures that the "kicker" is drawn from a fair 52-card deck and is not manipulated by the software to prevent a high payout.

## 2. Prepare for High Volatility

DDB is significantly more "swingy" than Jacks or Better. You will experience longer losing streaks, but the "hits" will be much larger.
- **The Tip:** Ensure your **bankroll management** is adjusted for DDB. A larger buffer (e.g., 5,000 units) is recommended to weather the variance.
- **Fairness Note:** Regulators like the **NGCB** or **UKGC** require that the machine’s **Source Code** and volatility are accurately reflected in the advertised payout percentages.

## 3. Prioritize Single Aces

In DDB, a lone Ace is the most valuable single card in the deck because it is the gateway to the 800-coin (no kicker) or 2,000-coin (with kicker) quad payouts.
- **The Tip:** If you have a hand with no other pairs or draws, always hold a lone Ace. It is often more valuable than holding two high cards of different suits.
- **Integrity Note:** Independent **Lab Audits** by firms like **GLI** verify that the RNG provides an unbiased distribution of Aces across all deals and draws.

## 4. Play Only "9/6" Full Pay Machines

The profitability of DDB hinges on the pay table for Full Houses and Flushes. 
- **The Tip:** Look for the "9/6" version (9 for Full House, 6 for Flush). Avoid 8/5 or 7/5 versions, as they significantly lower your theoretical **Return to Player (RTP)**.
- **Fairness Note:** **Jurisdictional Mandates** require that casinos clearly display these payout ratios so players can verify the mathematical quality of the game.

## 5. Trust the Independent Draw

A common myth is that if you hit a big "Kicker" win, the machine will "lock you out" from another big win. 
- **The Tip:** Every hand in DDB is an independent event. Your chance of hitting 4 Aces with a Kicker is the same on every hand.
- **Integrity Note:** **RTP Verification** ensures that the game’s logic remains consistent, regardless of your previous winnings. The RNG is continuously cycling to provide a truly random experience.

## Conclusion
Mastering Double Double Bonus requires a mix of aggressive play, solid bankroll management, and a deep understanding of the kicker mechanic. By targeting the bonus cards and playing on machines with a **trusted video poker RNG certification**, you can take advantage of the massive jackpot potential that DDB offers. Remember: in DDB, the kicker is king—play it right!
