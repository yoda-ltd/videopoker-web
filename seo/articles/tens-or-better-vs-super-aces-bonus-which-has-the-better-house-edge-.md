# Tens or Better vs. Super Aces Bonus: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Comparing **Tens or Better** and **Super Aces Bonus** is a comparison of low-volatility stability vs. extreme rank specialization. While Tens or Better lowers the qualification for a winning hand to a pair of 10s, Super Aces Bonus rewards you massively for hitting Four Aces. To choose the right game, you must understand how **video poker fairness and regulation** ensures the integrity of these contrasting mathematical models.

## The Mathematical Framework: Frequency vs. Single-Rank Focus

The house edge in these games is determined by how the "Return" is awarded across the ranks.

*   **Tens or Better:** Value is spread across frequent, small wins. By paying for a pair of 10s, the game ensures a higher "Hit Frequency," keeping your bankroll stable.
*   **Super Aces Bonus:** Value is concentrated at the very top. Hitting Four Aces can pay as much as a Royal Flush, making it a high-variance "Jackpot-Grinder" variant.

## House Edge Analysis: The Accuracy of the Math

The accuracy of the advertised edge in these variants is guaranteed by **Return to Player (RTP) Verification** during independent **Lab Audits**.

| Metric | Typical Tens or Better | Typical Super Aces Bonus |

| **Typical RTP** | **99.1% - 99.4%** | **98.8% - 99.6%** |
| **House Edge** | **0.6% - 0.9%** | **0.4% - 1.2%** |

**The Verdict:** **Super Aces Bonus** generally offers a slightly better house edge for skilled players who enjoy high-stakes targeting. However, **Tens or Better** provides a much more stable bankroll ride, making it the better choice for beginners or those who want to maximize their playtime.

## 1. Technical Standards for Fairness

Both variants must adhere to strict **Technical Standards** enforced by regulators like the **NGCB** or **UKGC**.

*   **Tens or Better:** The **Random Number Generator (RNG)** must provide an honest deal of 10s. Firms like **GLI** audit the code to ensure the game honors the lower qualification threshold fairly.
*   **Super Aces Bonus:** Regulators verify that every Ace has an exactly 1-in-52 chance of appearing. The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures that the machine doesn't "starve" the player of high-value quads to protect the jackpot.

## 2. Payout Analysis: The "Action" Tax

In these games, you are paying a "tax" for your chosen style of play.
- In Tens or Better, the base Full House/Flush payouts are lower than standard to fund the 10s wins.
- In Super Aces Bonus, Two Pair only pays 1-for-1. This funds the massive 2,000-coin bonus for Four Aces.

However, if you want a stable, low-risk introduction to the game and value consistent winning hands, **Tens or Better** provides a fair and rewarding experience.
