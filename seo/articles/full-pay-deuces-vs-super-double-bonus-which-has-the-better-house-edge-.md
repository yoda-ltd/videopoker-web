# Full Pay Deuces vs. Super Double Bonus: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Video poker players are often torn between the "Wild Card" games and the "Bonus" games. Two of the most popular high-return options are **Full Pay Deuces Wild (100.76% EV)** and **Super Double Bonus (99.69% EV)**. This article breaks down the house edge, volatility, and math to see which game deserves your action.

## Theoretical Return (EV) and House Edge

### 1. Full Pay Deuces Wild
*   **Return (EV):** 100.76%
*   **House Edge:** -0.76% (Player Edge)
*   **Math:** The game pays out over 100% because of the 200:1 Four Deuces and 800:1 Natural Royal.

### 2. Full Pay Super Double Bonus
*   **Return (EV):** 99.69%
*   **House Edge:** 0.31% (Casino Edge)
*   **Math:** This game is a variant of Jacks or Better that pays extra for various Four-of-a-Kind combinations (e.g., Four Aces pays 160:1).

**The Verdict on EV:** Full Pay Deuces Wild is the clear winner. It is one of the few games where the player has a mathematical advantage over the casino.

## Pay Table Comparison: Bonus vs. Wild
The appeal of Super Double Bonus is the "Bonus" payouts for quads, but Deuces Wild offers "Wild" flexibility.

| Hand | Deuces Wild (Full Pay) | Super Double Bonus (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Natural Royal Flush** | 800:1 | 800:1 |
| **Four Aces** | (Included in 4-of-a-Kind) | **160:1** |
| **Four 2s, 3s, or 4s** | **200:1 (for 2s only)** | 80:1 |
| **Four 5s-Ks** | 5:1 | 50:1 |
| **Straight** | 2:1 | 4:1 |

In Super Double Bonus, the **Straight** pays 4-for-1, which is a massive boost to your bankroll sustainability. In Deuces Wild, the Straight only pays 2-for-1, making the game feel "stingy" until you hit a big hand.

## Volatility and Bankroll
*   **Deuces Wild Variance:** ~25.8
*   **Super Double Bonus Variance:** ~28.5

Surprisingly, Super Double Bonus is slightly **more volatile** than Full Pay Deuces Wild. This is because so much of the return in Super Double Bonus is tied up in specific Four-of-a-Kind hands (Aces, 2s, 3s, 4s). If you go a long time without hitting Four Aces, your bankroll will vanish quickly.

## Strategy: Which is Harder?
**Deuces Wild** strategy is famous for being counter-intuitive. You throw away "good" hands like Two Pair because they don't exist in the pay table.

**Super Double Bonus** strategy is a modified version of Jacks or Better. It's more logical to most players (e.g., holding a pair of Jacks), but the "Aces" vs. "Full House" hold decisions can be tricky.

### Why You Need a Trainer
Regardless of which game you choose, you cannot achieve these returns without perfect play. 
*   A **Video Poker Trainer** for Deuces Wild will teach you when to hold a single Deuce over a 3-card Royal.
*   A trainer for Super Double Bonus will teach you when to break a Full House to go for Four Aces.

## House Edge Analysis: The "Full Pay" Reality
The biggest problem for players is finding the "Full Pay" versions of these games.
*   **Short Pay Deuces (98.9%):** Common in many casinos.
*   **Short Pay Super Double Bonus (97.7%):** Found when the Straight payout is lowered from 4:1 to 3:1.

If you are looking at a 98.9% Deuces Wild machine vs. a 99.69% Super Double Bonus machine, the **Super Double Bonus game becomes the better choice**, even though its "peak" EV is lower.

## Conclusion
If you can find both in their "Full Pay" forms, **Full Pay Deuces Wild (100.76%) is the superior choice**. It offers a positive return and slightly lower volatility. However, Super Double Bonus (99.69%) is an excellent alternative for players who prefer a "Natural" game without wild cards and like the thrill of chasing 160-to-1 Four Aces.
