# The Payout Gap: All American vs. Joker Poker Explained
Category: Comparison

Understanding the **Strategic Divergence** between **All American** and **Joker Poker** is key to mastering these high-payout variants. This comparison focuses on how the paytables differ and why those differences lead to unique **EV Maximization** paths.

### The Comparison Matrix: All American vs. Joker Poker

| Feature | All American (9/6) | Joker Poker (Kings or Better) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.72% (Full Pay) | 100.64% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 60.0 | 15.5 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | Straight/Flush Connectivity | Wild Card Connectivity (Joker) |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.72% (Player Edge) | -0.64% (Player Edge) |

### The connectivity Gap: Natural vs. Wild

In **All American**, the payouts for Straights and Flushes are significantly increased (34-for-1 and 8-for-1 respectively). In **Joker Poker**, the Joker is your most powerful tool, allowing you to complete hands that would otherwise be worthless. This creates a massive **Volatility Normalization** shift. In All American, you are looking for natural connectivity above all else.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 50% overlap. In All American, you prioritize the Straight and Flush draws, often breaking pairs to maximize the high-payout connectivity. In Joker Poker, you prioritize the Joker above all else. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the high-volume draws in All American and the Joker in Joker Poker dominate their respective EV landscapes.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Connectivity Priority:** In All American, prioritize 4-to-a-flush and 4-to-a-straight over small pairs.
2.  **Joker Supremacy:** In Joker Poker, never discard a Joker. It is your ultimate tool for **EV Maximization**.
3.  **Variance Management:** All American is much more "swingy" than Joker Poker. You need a much larger bankroll to withstand the swings of high-payout draws.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games are elite choices for the expert player, but Joker Poker provides a much more stable ride. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both offer a significant player advantage in their full-pay versions.

# The Payout Gap: All American vs. Loose Deuces Explained
Category: Comparison

Moving from **All American** to **Loose Deuces** is a shift from one high-volatility variant to another, but with a different focus. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your hands and the volatility that comes with them.

### The Comparison Matrix: All American vs. Loose Deuces

| Feature | All American (9/6) | Loose Deuces (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.72% | 101.60% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 60.0 | 200.0+ |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | Straight/Flush Connectivity | Four Deuces Jackpot |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.72% (Player Edge) | -1.60% (Player Edge) |

### The Jackpot Gap: Connectivity vs. Deuces

In **All American**, the rewards are frequent but swingy through natural Straights and Flushes. In **Loose Deuces**, the reward is massive but rare (the Four Deuces payout). This creates extreme **Volatility Normalization** (200.0+ vs 60.0). You are no longer looking for natural connectivity; you are playing a jackpot simulation.

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is minimal (less than 10%). In All American, you would never discard a pat winner. In Loose Deuces, you break almost every pat winner (except a Royal) to hunt for Deuces. This is the essence of **EV Maximization** in high-variance games.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Deuce Obsession:** Every Deuce is a keeper. Three Deuces is a massive hold, even if you have a "Natural" winner.
2.  **Connector Devaluation:** In Loose Deuces, natural straight and flush draws are almost irrelevant compared to the Four Deuces.
3.  **Variance Management:** Loose Deuces requires a massive bankroll to survive the long droughts between hitting the Deuces.

A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that while Loose Deuces has the highest RTP, it is emotionally and financially taxing. The **best video poker strategy comparison** for this transition is: Forget the straights; find the Deuces, or lose.

# The Payout Gap: All American vs. Sevens Wild Explained
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **All American** to **Sevens Wild** involves moving from a "Connectivity-Focused" natural variant to a "Multi-Wild" variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you approach **Volatility Normalization**.

### The Comparison Matrix: All American vs. Sevens Wild

| Feature | All American (9/6) | Sevens Wild (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.72% | 100.56% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 60.0 | 32.0 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | Straight/Flush Connectivity | Multi-Wild Connectivity (Sevens) |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.72% (Player Edge) | -0.56% (Player Edge) |

### The Wild Gap: Natural vs. Wild Connectivity

In **All American**, the reward is concentrated in natural combinations. In **Sevens Wild**, the four 7s are your most powerful tools. This leads to a lower **Volatility Normalization** (32.0 vs 60.0).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 40% overlap. In All American, you prioritized the natural connectivity. In Sevens Wild, you prioritize the 7s above all else, often breaking natural connections to maximize the wild card's potential. This is the essence of **EV Maximization** in wild card games.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Seven Supremacy:** A single 7 is almost always held.
2.  **Connector Devaluation:** In Sevens Wild, natural straight and flush draws are only valuable if they can be completed with a 7.
3.  **Five of a Kind Target:** This hand becomes your secondary target in Sevens Wild, whereas it doesn't exist in All American.

Through a detailed **video poker house edge analysis**, it's clear that Sevens Wild offers a superior return with lower variance than All American. The **best video poker strategy comparison** for this transition is: Use the sevens to find the edge with less stress.
