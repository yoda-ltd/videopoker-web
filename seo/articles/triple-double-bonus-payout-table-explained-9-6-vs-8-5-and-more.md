# Triple Double Bonus Payout Table Explained: 9/7 vs 9/5 and More

In the **best odds video poker** world, Triple Double Bonus Poker is the fan favorite because of its "kicker" bonus payouts for Four of a Kind. For the professional player, this represents a unique opportunity for **Pay Table Optimization**.

## The Best Odds Index: 9/7 Triple Double Bonus (99.58%)
In the **highest return video poker games** 2026 index, 9/7 Triple Double Bonus is a top-tier choice. It offers a **Theoretical Return Convergence of 99.58%**.

The "9/7" designation here refers to:
*   **Full House:** 9 coins per 1 coin bet.
*   **Flush:** 7 coins per 1 coin bet.

Wait, why do we call it "9/7" Triple Double Bonus? It's the pay table that pays **9 coins for a Full House AND 7 coins for a Flush**.

## Analysis of "Short Pay" Predation
Because 99.58% gives the player a strong return, casinos often use "Short Pay" tables to reverse it.
*   **9/6 Triple Double Bonus:** Paying only 6 coins for a Flush drops the RTP to **98.15%**.
*   **9/5 Triple Double Bonus:** Paying 5 coins for a Flush drops the RTP to **97.02%**.
*   **Common Short Pay (8/5):** This variant drops the RTP below **95.97%**.

The **EV Gap** between the 99.58% version and the 95.97% version is over 3.61%. If you are playing 800 hands per hour at a $1 machine, that’s $36.1 per hour you’re losing simply by picking the wrong machine. This "predatory" short-pay table kills the professional edge.

## House Edge Variance in Triple Double Bonus
Triple Double Bonus has higher **House Edge Variance** than Jacks or Better. This is because the game's return is heavily weighted towards the Four of a Kind with a kicker. If you don't hit the Four of a Kind with a kicker at the mathematically expected frequency, your actual return will lag behind the 99.58% theoretical return. Professional bankroll management is essential.
