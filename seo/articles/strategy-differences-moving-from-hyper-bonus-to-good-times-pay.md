# Strategy Differences: Moving from Hyper Bonus to Good Times Pay

The transition from **Hyper Bonus** to **Good Times Pay** is a move from "High-Speed Intensity" to "Multiplier Relaxation." Both are Action Gaming patents that define the "rare video poker variants strategy" on the **best video poker machines in 2026**, but they offer two very different ways to experience **Accumulated Multipliers**.

## The Multiplier Trigger: Dealt vs. Random
The fundamental difference is *when* the multiplier appears.

- **Hyper Bonus (The Aggressive Feature):** You bet 10 coins per line to trigger multipliers *based on the dealt cards*. If you are dealt a winning hand, you get a multiplier. It rewards "Strength on the Deal."
- **Good Times Pay (The Serendipitous Feature):** You bet a 6th or 10th coin (depending on the version) to trigger a random "Multiplier Event." A character or animation appears at random and awards a 2x to 5x multiplier for the *current* hand.

## Key Strategic Adjustments

### 1. From "Hold-for-Multiplier" to "Pure Strategy"
- **In Hyper Bonus:** You must be aware of which dealt hands trigger multipliers. This often causes you to deviate from standard strategy to "Capture" a multiplier.
- **In Good Times Pay:** The multiplier is random and determined independently of your cards. Therefore, there is zero strategy change. You play "Pure EV" strategy for the underlying game. The multiplier is a "Gift" that doesn't change your decision-making.

### 2. Variance Normalization and Bankroll Management
- **Hyper Bonus Shift:** High variance. You are "Paying for Performance." If you aren't dealt winning hands, you are losing 10 coins per hand with no benefit.
- **Good Times Pay Shift:** Moderate variance. Because the multiplier is random, it "Smooths Out" the dry spells of standard video poker. 
- **The Adjustment:** Good Times Pay is a "Friendlier" game. It doesn't require the intense focus of Hyper Bonus. It's a great "Step-Down" game for players who find the 10-coin requirement of Hyper Bonus too taxing on their bankroll.

### 3. Feature Hit Frequency
- **Hyper Bonus:** The frequency depends on the pay table (how often you are dealt winning hands).
- **Good Times Pay:** The frequency is fixed (usually once every 4-7 hands).
- **The Shift:** In Hyper Bonus, you feel in "Control" of the bonus. In Good Times Pay, you are "Waiting" for the bonus. Moving to Good Times Pay requires a shift from "Action" to "Enjoyment."

## Why the Transition Matters in 2026
In 2026, **Good Times Pay** remains a staple in "Recreational" zones of the casino, while **Hyper Bonus** dominates the "High-Limit" and "Fast-Play" pits. 
- **Expert Tip:** If you've had a long, high-stress session on Hyper Bonus, moving to Good Times Pay is an excellent way to maintain a high RTP (usually over 99%) while reducing the "Mental Fatigue" of multiplier-tracking.

## Conclusion
Moving from Hyper Bonus to Good Times Pay is a shift from "Earning it" to "Receiving it." Master the transition, and you'll find that "Good Times" are much easier to sustain when you don't have to calculate your own multipliers!
