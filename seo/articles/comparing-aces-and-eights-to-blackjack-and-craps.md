Each game offers a unique path to **EV Maximization**, but the mechanics and house edges differ significantly.

### Strategic Divergence: Skill vs. Luck

In **Aces and Eights**, your return is entirely dependent on your ability to make the mathematically correct hold decisions. This is a game of **Volatility Normalization** through disciplined play. In **Blackjack**, your return is similarly skill-dependent, but you also have the ability to "Double Down" or "Split," which adds a layer of bet management. In **Craps**, your return is primarily determined by the specific bets you place (like Pass Line with Odds), with minimal skill required once the bet is made.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is zero (0%) strategy overlap between these games. While they all aim for **EV Maximization**, the methods are entirely different. In Aces and Eights, you are holding cards; in Blackjack, you are hitting or standing; in Craps, you are placing chips.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Aces and Eights Skill:** You must master the exact strategy for bonus quads (Aces, 8s, 7s).
2.  **Blackjack Discipline:** You must follow Basic Strategy perfectly to achieve the 0.50% house edge.
3.  **Craps Simplicity:** To achieve the best odds in Craps, you simply place the Pass Line bet and back it with the maximum allowed "Odds" bet. This is a form of **Payout Compression**—the "Odds" bet has zero house edge.

Through a detailed **video poker house edge analysis**, it's clear that Aces and Eights offers a superior return compared to standard Blackjack and Craps, provided you are willing to master the complex strategy. However, Blackjack offers a much lower variance, making it a smoother ride for the risk-averse player.
