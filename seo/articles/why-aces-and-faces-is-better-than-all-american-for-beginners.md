# Why Aces and Faces is Better Than All-American for Beginners

In the world of "Strategy-Rich" video poker, the choice between "Aces and Faces" (AF) and "All-American" (AA) is a study in **Strategic Convergence** and the **Variance Index**. This **best video poker strategy comparison** breaks down why Aces and Faces is the superior entry point for novices, focusing on **Payout Compression** and the "Strategy Drift" caused by the different card values and bonus structures.

## Strategic Convergence: The "Linear-Bonus" Advantage

**Aces and Faces Poker** is a "Standard-Draw" game with "Bonus" payouts for specific 4-of-a-Kind hands. The strategy is almost identical to Jacks or Better, with minor adjustments for the "Face Card" bonuses. Because it follows the "Standard" draw mechanic, the strategy is "Linear" and "Logical." Beginners can learn the basic "Hierarchy" of hands and apply it with high accuracy. The **video poker house edge analysis** is straightforward and predictable.

**All-American Poker** introduces a "Niche" bonus structure that focuses on Flushes and Straights. This creates "Strategy Bifurcation." For every hand, you must now ask: "Is the All-American bonus better than the standard hold?" The strategy is "Unique" and often "Counter-Intuitive" compared to standard bonus games. For a beginner, this is "Cognitive Overload." You can easily make a mistake by holding "Connectors" when the math suggests a different path. This "Strategy Drift" can cost you 1-2% in "Theoretical Return" over a session.

## Payout Compression: The "Smooth" vs. The "Straight-Specific"

In **Aces and Faces**, the "Cost" of the "Face Card" bonuses is built into the payout table. However, the game remains "Smooth" because the "Base Payouts" are standard for a bonus-style game. Your bankroll "Churns" nicely. You are "Rewarded" for hitting common hands with a "Fair" payout.

In **All-American**, the "Cost" of the "Straight and Flush" bonuses is built into the Two-Pair and Full House payouts. To "Pay" for these specific "All-American" wins, the payouts for *all* other hands (like Full Houses) are "Compressed." You often get only "Money Back" on Two-Pair. For a beginner, this is "Psychological Torture." You hit a "Winning" hand, but your bankroll doesn't grow. This "Bleed" is much more aggressive than in Aces and Faces.

## The Variance Index: Sustainability vs. Volatility

Our **video poker house edge analysis** shows that:
*   **Aces and Faces** has a variance of around **30-40**. It is a "Medium-Volatility" game. It offers "Stability" and "Longevity."
*   **All-American** has a variance of around **30-40**. While the variance is "Similar," the "Unique Strategy" and "Compressed Payouts" lead to a higher "Effective Error Rate" for beginners who make mistakes.

For the new player who wants to enjoy a long session and learn the fundamentals with a "Linear-Bonus" structure, Aces and Faces is the clear winner. It provides the "Stability" of a standard game with the "Excitement" of well-balanced payouts. Leave the "All-American" hunting to the pros who can handle the "Complexity" and the "Swings."
