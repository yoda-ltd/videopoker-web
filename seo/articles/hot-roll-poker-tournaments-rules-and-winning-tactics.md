## The Rules of the Roll

Tournament Hot Roll Poker typically involves a timed session where you attempt to rack up the highest score. 
*   **The Multiplier Advantage:** Multipliers are triggered randomly. In a tournament, the "lucky" player who gets a 10x or 12x multiplier during a Four-of-a-Kind or Royal Flush will almost certainly win.
*   **Betting:** 10-coin max bets are standard and required to keep the Hot Roll feature active.

## Analysis of Hot Roll's Dice Multiplier (Up to 12x)

Understanding the **Multiplier Roll Probability** is your secret weapon. Since the most common roll is a 7 (16.67%), you can expect most of your "boosted" hands to be at the 7x level. 

### Bankroll Volatility in Tournaments
In a tournament, "bankroll" is just a score. You don't care about losing it; you only care about the peak. This means:
1.  **High-Risk Holds:** You should be more willing to break a winning hand (like a Flush) to chase a Royal Flush if you have a 10x or 12x multiplier active.
2.  **Speed Play:** You need to maximize the number of "Hot Roll" events you see. The more hands you play, the more chances you have at a 12x multiplier.

## Winning Tactics: Leveraging the Multiplier

1.  **Multiplier-Specific Strategy:** When a 12x multiplier is rolled, the "Value" of every card in the deck effectively multiplies by 12. If you are using a **hot roll video poker trainer**, practice hands with high multipliers to see how your "Expectation" changes. You might find that chasing a 3-card Royal Flush becomes mathematically superior to holding a High Pair when the payout is 12x.
2.  **The "7x" Grind:** Don't wait for the 12x to play well. Since 7x is the most frequent multiplier, your tournament success will likely be decided by how many "Full Houses" and "Four-of-a-Kinds" you can land during the 7x rolls.
3.  **End-Game Desperation:** In the final 60 seconds of a tournament, if you are not in the top 3, abandon all "safe" play. Only hold cards that can lead to a Royal Flush or a high-value Four-of-a-Kind.

## Feature Hit Frequency vs. Speed

In Hot Roll Poker, the feature hits about 16% of the time. In a 500-hand tournament session, you will see roughly 80 "Hot Roll" hands. 
*   If you play at 400 hands per hour, you get fewer chances.
*   If you play at 800 hands per hour, you double your chances of hitting that 12x Royal Flush.
