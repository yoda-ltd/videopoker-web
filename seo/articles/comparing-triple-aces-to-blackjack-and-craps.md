# Comparing Triple Aces to Blackjack and Craps

Triple Aces is a high-action video poker variant that offers massive payouts for three or four Aces. How does this high-octane game compare to the "big two" of the casino: Blackjack and Craps?

### Theoretical Return Comparison
- **Triple Aces**: The **Theoretical Return** can be very high, often exceeding 99% on well-configured machines.
- **Blackjack**: Offers a 99.5% RTP (0.5% house edge) with perfect basic strategy.
- **Craps (Pass Line)**: Offers a 98.59% RTP (1.41% house edge).

While Blackjack still holds a slight edge on base RTP, Triple Aces provides the chance for a massive payout (the Aces or the Royal Flush) that Blackjack simply doesn't offer on a single bet.

### Variance and Sustainability
Triple Aces has much higher **Variance** than both Blackjack and Craps because a significant portion of its return is tied up in the Aces bonus payouts. To achieve **Session Sustainability**, you need a much larger bankroll for **Variance Buffering** than you would for a slow-paced game of Blackjack. Without the extra bankroll, the long wait for the Aces can be punishing.

### The Max Bet Essential
"Is max bet always better for bankroll longevity?" In Blackjack and Craps, your bet size doesn't change the house edge. In Triple Aces, the 5th coin is mandatory to get the full Royal Flush and Aces bonuses. If you aren't playing max bet, the RTP of this game drops significantly, making it a worse bet than Craps.

### Skill and Strategy
Triple Aces requires a very specific and aggressive strategy that focuses on **Hit Frequency Optimization** for the Aces. This is more complex than Blackjack's basic strategy. While Blackjack and Craps rely on physical randomness, Triple Aces uses **Pseudo-Randomness** through a certified RNG. This ensures that every hand is fair and independent.
