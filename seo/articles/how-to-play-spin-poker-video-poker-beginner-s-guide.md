Spin Poker is the ultimate crossover for fans of both slot machines and video poker. ## What is Spin Poker?

In traditional video poker, you play one hand at a time. In Spin Poker, you play 9 hands simultaneously, arranged in a 3x5 grid that looks exactly like a classic 9-payline slot machine. 

The core mechanic is simple: you are dealt five cards on the center line. When you choose which cards to hold, those holds are applied to all 9 paylines. When you hit "Draw," the remaining positions spin like slot reels to reveal the final cards for all lines.

## Mechanics: Spin Poker vs. Triple Play

Many players confuse Spin Poker with "Triple Play" or "Five Play" Draw Poker. While both involve multiple hands, the mechanics differ significantly:

1.  **Reel Strips vs. Independent Decks**: In Triple Play, each hand is drawn from its own independent 52-card deck (minus the cards you held). In Spin Poker, the game uses a system of "Reel Strips." While the result still mirrors a standard deck's probability, the visual representation and the way **RNG Cross-Pollination** occurs across the 9 paylines are unique.
2.  **Payline Volatility**: Because many paylines share the same vertical reel positions, your wins are often "clumped." If you hit a big card on the first reel, it might complete winning combinations on three different paylines at once. This creates higher **Payline Volatility** compared to the more distributed risk of Triple Play.

## Professional Terminology & Math

To play like a pro, you need to understand the underlying math of hybrid games:

*   **Hybrid RTP (Return to Player)**: Spin Poker generally carries the same RTP as the base game (e.g., 9/6 Jacks or Better), but because you are betting 9 times the credits, your bankroll will experience much sharper swings.
*   **Side-Bet EV**: Some Spin Poker Deluxe machines offer additional paylines or bonus features. Always calculate if the extra cost per spin provides a positive **Side-Bet EV** (Expected Value) or if it simply increases the house edge.

## Quick Tips for Beginners

*   **Hold for the Center**: Always make your holding decisions based on the center line.
*   **Bet Max Lines**: To achieve the best statistical return, you should usually bet the maximum 9 lines. Playing fewer lines doesn't change the odds of a single hand, but it reduces the "slot-like" advantage of the game's design.
*   **Check the Pay Table**: Spin Poker machines often have different pay tables for different denominations. A "9/6" table (9 credits for a Full House, 6 for a Flush) is the gold standard for Jacks or Better.
