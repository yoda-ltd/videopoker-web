# Aces and Eights Video Poker: Tournaments, Rules, and Winning Tactics

Video poker tournaments are high-energy competitions that offer a different challenge than the solo play you experience at a regular machine. In an Aces and Eights tournament, you are racing against both the clock and your opponents to achieve the highest possible score within a set number of hands or time limit.

If you are looking to take your game to the next level, understanding the specific rules and tactics for Aces and Eights tournaments is crucial.

## The Rules: How It Works

Tournament rules can vary by casino (live or online), but most follow a standard format:

1.  **Entry Fee:** You pay a buy-in fee to participate. All players receive an equal number of credits (e.g., 1,000 credits).
2.  **Time Limit:** You are typically given a specific amount of time (e.g., 15 or 20 minutes) to play.
3.  **Hand Limit:** Alternatively, you might be given a set number of hands (e.g., 200 or 500 hands) to complete.
4.  **No Cash Value:** The credits you use during the tournament have no monetary value. You cannot cash them out.
5.  **Ranking:** Your final score is the total number of credits you have remaining at the end of the tournament. The top players on the leaderboard win a portion of the prize pool.

## Aces and Eights Strategy in a Tournament

The strategy for an Aces and Eights tournament differs significantly from standard play. In a regular game, you are playing for long-term expected value (EV). In a tournament, you are playing for **volatility** and **maximum score**.

### 1. Speed is Your Best Friend
If the tournament is time-based, you must play as fast as possible. Any unplayed credits at the end of the time limit are essentially wasted opportunities. You need to develop "muscle memory" for standard hands so you don't waste precious seconds thinking.

### 2. High-Volatility Strategy
In a standard Aces and Eights game, you might choose the safer play for a long-term return. In a tournament, you need to "go big or go home."

*   **Chasing the Royal Flush:** If you have 3 to a Royal Flush, you should hold them and discard the other two cards, even if you have a pair of Jacks.
*   **The Four-of-a-Kind Bonuses:** Aces and Eights pays more for Four Aces, Eights, and Sevens. In a tournament, these hands are your ticket to the top of the leaderboard. If you have a single Ace or Eight, you might choose to hold it over a low pair in hopes of hitting those high-value quads.

### 3. Maximum Coin Play
This should go without saying, but always play the maximum amount of coins (typically 5). You need that 800-to-1 Royal Flush bonus to have any chance of winning the tournament.

### 4. Know Your Position
If you can see the leaderboard while you play, use it to your advantage. If you are near the end of the tournament and far behind the leaders, you should take even more aggressive risks to hit a "monster" hand. If you are already at the top, you might play a slightly more conservative, standard strategy to maintain your lead.

## Tactical Tips for Success

*   **Keep Your Hands Moving:** In live tournaments, avoid talking to neighbors or looking around. Keep your eyes on the screen and your hands on the buttons.
*   **Don't Overthink:** Most video poker decisions are straightforward. If you have a winning hand that is better than two pair, you should almost always keep it unless you have a 4-to-a-Royal draw.
*   **Focus on the Bonuses:** Remember that Four Aces and Four Eights pay 80-to-1, and Four Sevens pay 50-to-1. These are the hands that will separate you from the pack in an Aces and Eights tournament.
*   **Practice Speed:** Before entering a tournament, use a video poker trainer to practice your strategy until you can make decisions almost instantly without errors.

## Conclusion: Volatility Wins Tournaments

An Aces and Eights tournament is a test of both skill and speed. While luck plays a major role, your ability to play fast and take calculated risks for high-value hands will give you a significant edge over more cautious players. Focus on those four-of-a-kind bonuses and the Royal Flush, and you might just find yourself at the top of the leaderboard!
