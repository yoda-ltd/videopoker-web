# Psychology of Playing All American: Staying Focused

All American Poker is a variant of Jacks or Better that offers significantly higher payouts for Straights, Flushes, and Straight Flushes. This makes the game more volatile and requires a more aggressive strategy. To succeed, you must cultivate the **Pro Mindset** and practice extreme **Professional Discipline**.

## The Straight and Flush Trap: The Dopamine Loop of All American

The defining feature of All American is the increased reward for hands that are normally "low-tier" in other games. This creates a powerful **Dopamine Loop** that can lead to disastrous strategy errors.

- **The Dopamine Loop**: Hitting a Straight or Flush in All American feels twice as good because it pays twice as much as in Jacks or Better. This can lead to "chasing" behavior where players "fish" for Straights and Flushes when it's not the correct play.
- **Expert Video Poker Strategy Tips**: A pro NEVER "fishes" for a Straight or Flush unless it is mathematically correct (which is rare). 
- **Loss Aversion Bias**: The higher volatility of All American means you will lose back-to-back sessions more frequently. This triggers **Loss Aversion Bias**, where players become "desperate" to hit a big hand to "make it all back." 

## Navigating the "Royal Desert" and the "Straight Flush" Drought

In All American, your return is heavily weighted towards the big hands—not just the Royal Flush, but also the Straight Flush.

1. **The Royal Desert**: You can easily go 100,000 hands without a Royal Flush in All American. This is where your **Cognitive Resilience** is truly tested.
2. **The Straight Flush Desert**: Hitting a Straight Flush in All American is a major payout. If you don't hit one for several thousand hands, your bankroll will take a massive hit. This is the "All American Grind."
3. **Tilt Mitigation**: When your bankroll is melting, the urge to play faster or "gamble" on draws becomes intense. **Tilt Control** is your only defense.

## Professional Discipline: The Assembly Line of All American

To learn **how to become a professional video poker player**, you must treat All American as a cold, calculating process.

- **The Assembly Line**: Every hand is just another calculation. There is no "feeling" that the Straights are coming.
- **Session Fatigue**: All American has one of the most aggressive strategies. **Session Fatigue** after 3-4 hours can lead to costly errors in penalty card management or straight-flush holds.
- **Pro Mindset**: A pro judges their performance by the correctness of their holds, not by the session total.

## Responsible Play in a High-Variance Game

**Responsible video poker playing** in All American requires a massive bankroll and the discipline to walk away when the "edge" is gone.

- **Bankroll is King**: If your bankroll is too small, you will play "scared," which leads to strategy errors.
- **Tilt Control**: If you find yourself angry at the machine for not "giving" you a Straight or Flush, you are in a state of Tilt and must stop immediately.

## Conclusion
All American is a game of incredible highs and devastating lows. By focusing on **Tilt Control** and maintaining a rigorous **Pro Mindset**, you can harness the power of the "Straight and Flush" and navigate the volatility of this high-octane game.
