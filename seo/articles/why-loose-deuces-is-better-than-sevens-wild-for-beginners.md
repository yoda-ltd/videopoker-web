# Why Loose Deuces is Better Than Sevens Wild for Beginners

In the world of "Wild Card" video poker, the choice between "Loose Deuces" (LD) and "Sevens Wild" (SW) is a study in **Strategic Convergence** and the **Variance Index**. This **best video poker strategy comparison** breaks down why Loose Deuces is the superior entry point for novices, focusing on **Payout Compression** and the "Strategy Drift" caused by the different wild cards.

## Strategic Convergence: The "Deuces-Standard" Advantage

**Loose Deuces Poker** is a "Deuces Wild" variant. The strategy is almost identical to standard Deuces Wild, with adjustments for the "Heavy" payouts on 4-of-a-Kind Deuces. Because the "Wild Card" is the Deuce, the strategy is "Standard" and "Well-Documented." Beginners can learn the basic "Hierarchy" of hands and apply it with high accuracy. The **video poker house edge analysis** is straightforward and predictable.

**Sevens Wild** introduces "Sevens" as the wild card. This creates "Strategy Bifurcation." For every hand, you must now ask: "Is the Seven wild?" The presence of the Seven as a wild card fundamentally changes the "Value" of every card. For a beginner, this is "Cognitive Overload." You can easily make a mistake by holding a "Natural" hand when the "Seven" suggests a different path. This "Strategy Drift" can cost you 1-2% in "Theoretical Return" over a session.

## Payout Compression: The "Smooth" vs. The "Wild"

In **Loose Deuces**, the "Cost" of the massive payout for 4-of-a-Kind Deuces is built into the other 4-of-a-Kind and Straight Flush payouts. However, the game remains "Smooth" because the frequency of wild cards is relatively high. Your bankroll "Churns" nicely. You are "Rewarded" for hitting common hands with a "Fair" payout.

In **Sevens Wild**, the "Wild Seven" allows for "Extreme" hands. To "Pay" for these rare "Wild" wins, the payouts for common hands are "Compressed." You often get only "Money Back" on hands that would pay more in a standard game. For a beginner, this is "Psychological Torture." You hit a "Winning" hand, but your bankroll doesn't grow. This "Bleed" is much more aggressive than in Loose Deuces.

## The Variance Index: Sustainability vs. Volatility

Our **video poker house edge analysis** shows that:
*   **Loose Deuces** has a variance of around **30-40**. It is a "Medium-to-High Volatility" game. It offers "Excitement" without the "Punishing Swings."
*   **Sevens Wild** has a variance of around **50-60**. It is a "High-Volatility" game. The "Gaps" between big wins are much longer.

For the new player who wants to enjoy a long session and learn the fundamentals without the "Seven" confusion, Loose Deuces is the clear winner. It provides the "Stability" of standard wild-card games with the "Enhanced" rewards of a specialty game. Leave the "Seven" hunting to the pros who can handle the "Complexity" and the "Swings."
