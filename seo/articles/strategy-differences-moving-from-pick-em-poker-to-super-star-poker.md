# Strategy Differences: Moving from Pick'em Poker to Super Star Poker

Transitioning from Pick'em Poker to Super Star Poker is a unique step in the video poker world. Both games are niche variants that offer a "simplified" or "multi-game" experience. This guide provides the best video poker strategy comparison to help you understand how to play video poker like a pro during this transition.

## The Pick'em Poker Baseline

In Pick'em Poker, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is built on the fact that any pair of 9s or better is a winning hand. This creates a very stable, low-volatility experience. When moving to Super Star Poker, that "safety net" is removed. You now need at least a pair of Jacks to get your money back.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from Pick'em Poker to Super Star Poker, you'll find that **80% of your strategy remains the same**. The **20% that changes** is entirely focused on the "Pair threshold." This 20% shift causes the most **Bankroll Drain** if not corrected.

### The 20% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Pair of 9s/10s Devaluation:** In Pick'em Poker, a pair of 9s or 10s is a winning hand. In Super Star Poker, it's a "trash" hand that you should rarely hold unless you're drawing for something better (like a three-card Royal Flush).
2.  **Super Star Focus:** Super Star Poker is named "Super Star" because it offers a variety of games in one machine. This makes four-of-a-kind and specific straights much more valuable than in Pick'em Poker.
3.  **Two Pair Strategy:** While Two Pair pays 2-for-1 in both games, the higher payout for specific "Super Star" hands in Super Star Poker means you might break a Full House to draw for a fifth card more often than you would in Pick'em Poker.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

Super Star Poker introduces **Volatility Scaling**. Without the 9s-or-better push, your bankroll will experience more frequent dips. **EV Erosion** occurs when a Pick'em-Poker player instinctively holds a pair of 9s or 10s in Super Star Poker. This is a "dead hand" and holding it is a direct loss of expected value.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Jacks or Better are your new minimum "win."
*   **Modify:** Discard any pair of 9s or 10s unless they are part of a better draw.
*   **Target:** Aggressively pursue the "Super Star" bonuses; they are the heart of the Super Star Poker pay table.

By recognizing that the "safety net" has moved, you can successfully transition to Super Star Poker without suffering from significant EV Erosion.
