# Ultimate Loose Deuces Strategy Chart for 2026

Loose Deuces is a high-payout variation of Deuces Wild that focuses on the massive payout for four Deuces. Mastering the **Loose Deuces strategy chart 2026** is essential for **EV Maximization** in this high-variance game. To learn **how to play video poker like a pro**, you must first eliminate these three common "Pro-Level" errors.

## Analysis: 3 Most Common "Pro-Level" Errors in Loose Deuces

### 1. Misvaluing the Deuce as a "Kicker"
The most significant difference in Loose Deuces is that the payout for 4 Deuces is often 2,500 credits (on a 5-coin bet).

**The Error:** Holding a high card (like an Ace) along with a Deuce (e.g., `2-A`).
**The Pro Reality:** In Loose Deuces, a Deuce with one high card is almost always a "drawing" hand. Strategy fine-tuning dictates that you should only hold the Deuce by itself (or with other cards that complete a strong "made" hand like a flush or straight). The Deuce is so powerful that holding it with a non-matching card lowers your overall probability of drawing into more valuable combinations.

### 2. Overvaluing "Natural" Hands in Loose Deuces
Players often forget that the payouts in Loose Deuces are adjusted to account for the Wild card.

**The Error:** Keeping a pair of 2s (Deuces) in Loose Deuces.
**The Pro Reality:** Wait, you *always* keep a pair of Deuces. But what about a pair of Kings? In Loose Deuces, a pair of Kings pays *zero*. The **Rank-Based Priority** in 2026 dictates that if you have no Deuces, you should be much more aggressive in drawing for them. Often, discarding a low pair is better than holding it, as the low pair has almost no path to a "Wild" bonus that actually pays out.

### 3. Mismanaging the 4-to-a-Royal Flush Draw
The presence of the Deuce creates a "Wild Royal Flush" category, which pays significantly less than a "Natural Royal Flush."

**The Error:** Breaking a strong 4-of-a-kind (including a Deuce) to draw for a Natural Royal Flush.
**The Pro Reality:** A Natural Royal Flush is incredibly rare. The **Expected Return Differential** strongly favors the "made" 4-of-a-kind over the slim chance of a Natural Royal. Pro players treat the Deuce as the most valuable card and rarely discard it, even for a potential Royal.
