# Strategy Differences: Moving from Double Bonus to Aces and Eights
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Double Bonus** to **Aces and Eights** is a shift from a "High Bonus" variant to a "Targeted Bonus" variant. This represents a significant **Strategic Divergence** in how you value specific four-of-a-kind hands and the volatility that comes with them.

### The Comparison Matrix: Double Bonus vs. Aces and Eights

| Feature | Double Bonus (10/7) | Aces and Eights (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.17% (Full Pay) | 99.78% |
| **Variance Index** | 28.5 | 21.8 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Broad-Scale Bonus Quads | Target Specific Aces/8s/7s Bonuses |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.17% (Player Edge) | 0.22% |

### Strategic Divergence: The Bonus Payout Tiers

In **Double Bonus Poker**, almost all four-of-a-kind hands are "Doubled" compared to standard Jacks or Better, with Aces, 2s-4s, and 5s-Ks having their own tiers. In **Aces and Eights**, the "Faces" lose their special status and are replaced by 8s and 7s. This is a critical **EV Maximization** point: if you are holding a pair of Jacks in Aces and Eights, you treat them like a standard high pair. In Double Bonus, they are a building block for a bonus payout.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is high strategy overlap (approximately 80%) because both are 52-card natural games. However, in Aces and Eights, you will break a Full House to hold three Aces less frequently than in Double Bonus. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the high-tier hands in Double Bonus dominate the EV landscape.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Devaluation of Faces:** When moving to Aces and Eights, Jacks, Queens, and Kings are simply "high cards" for pair purposes. They no longer offer a special quad bonus.
2.  **Valuation of 8s and 7s:** In Aces and Eights, a pair of 8s or 7s is mathematically more valuable than in Double Bonus because of the **Payout Compression** at the four-of-a-kind level.
3.  **Full House Strategy:** In 10/7 Double Bonus, you break a Full House to hold three Aces. In Aces and Eights, the decision is much closer and often depends on the exact paytable.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that Double Bonus Poker offers a player advantage in its full-pay version, but Aces and Eights provides a smoother ride with lower variance. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are top-tier choices for anyone looking for a player advantage.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Double Bonus to Aces and Faces
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Double Bonus** to **Aces and Faces** is a shift from a "High Bonus" variant to a "Faces Bonus" variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value specific card ranks and their related bonuses.

### The Comparison Matrix: Double Bonus vs. Aces and Faces

| Feature | Double Bonus (10/7) | Aces and Faces (8/5) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.17% | 99.26% |
| **Variance Index** | 28.5 | 22.1 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target High-Payout Natural Quads | Target Face Card Quads |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.17% (Player Edge) | 0.74% |

### Strategic Divergence: Bonus Tiers vs. Faces Focus

In **Double Bonus**, you are looking for natural four-of-a-kind hands that pay a massive bonus. In **Aces and Faces**, you are looking for Four Aces or Four "Faces" (Kings, Queens, Jacks). This is a game of **EV Maximization** through specific card ranks. Double Bonus's variance (28.5) is higher than Aces and Faces (22.1), requiring careful **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 80% overlap. In Double Bonus, you would break a Full House to hold three Aces. In Aces and Faces, the decision is similar, but you also prioritize pairs of Kings, Queens, and Jacks much more than in Double Bonus. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the face cards in Aces and Faces dominate the EV landscape.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Ace Priority:** In both games, Aces are the highest-paying quads. You prioritize them over almost any other card rank.
2.  **Face Card Focus:** Kings, Queens, and Jacks are the next tier in Aces and Faces. In Double Bonus, they are just another "High Bonus" quad.
3.  **Full House Break:** You will break a Full House containing three Aces to draw for the fourth Ace in both games. This is a common **EV Maximization** play for high-bonus variants.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that Double Bonus Poker offers a player advantage in its full-pay version, but Aces and Faces provides a competitive return with lower variance. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are excellent choices for the disciplined player.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Double Bonus to Double Double Bonus
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Double Bonus** to **Double Double Bonus** is a shift from a "High Bonus" variant to a "Kicker-Based" bonus variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value specific card combinations and their related "Kickers."

### The Comparison Matrix: Double Bonus vs. Double Double Bonus

| Feature | Double Bonus (10/7) | Double Double Bonus (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.17% | 98.98% |
| **Variance Index** | 28.5 | 42.0 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target High-Payout Natural Quads | Target Quads with Kickers |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.17% (Player Edge) | 1.02% |

### Strategic Divergence: Natural Quad vs. Kicker Jackpot

In **Double Bonus**, the payout for four-of-a-kind hands is fixed. In **Double Double Bonus Poker**, the payout for Four Aces, 2s, 3s, and 4s depends on the fifth card in the hand (the "Kicker"). This is a game of **EV Maximization** through specific high-tier combinations, leading to extreme **Volatility Normalization** (42.0 vs 28.5).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 70% overlap. In Double Bonus, you would never hold a kicker with a pair. In Double Double Bonus, if you have three Aces and a low kicker (2, 3, 4), you will keep the kicker to draw for the massive 2,000-credit bonus. This is the essence of **Payout Compression** in kicker-based games.

### Critical Strategy Shifts
1.  **Kicker Knowledge:** You must learn which cards are "Kickers" for each quad tier (Aces, 2s, 3s, 4s).
2.  **Ace-Kicker Priority:** Holding a pair of Aces with a low kicker is a valid strategy in Double Double Bonus, whereas in Double Bonus, you'd only hold the Aces.
3.  **Variance Management:** Double Double Bonus has a very high variance (42.0). You need a larger bankroll to withstand the swings of "Kicker Hunting."

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that while Double Double Bonus is the most popular game in many casinos, it requires a higher degree of mental flexibility to master the "Natural" kicker strategy. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that it offers a competitive return if you play the maximum bet and master the kicker strategy.
