# The Payout Gap: Double Bonus vs Double Double Bonus Explained

In the world of **best odds video poker**, players often find themselves choosing between two of the most popular machines on the floor: Double Bonus and Double Double Bonus. While they look similar, the **EV Gap** between them depends entirely on their specific pay tables and your tolerance for **House Edge Variance**.

## The Best Odds Index: 10/7 Double Bonus vs 9/6 Double Double Bonus
In the **highest return video poker games** 2026 ranking, both machines offer strong professional returns when found in their "Full Pay" configurations:
*   **10/7 Double Bonus:** 100.17% RTP.
*   **9/6 Double Double Bonus:** 98.98% RTP.

At first glance, Double Bonus appears to be the superior choice. However, Double Double Bonus offers a "Bonus" for hitting Four of a Kind with a kicker (specifically Four Aces with a 2, 3, or 4 kicker, which pays 400-for-1 instead of 160-for-1). This change in the pay table structure shifts the **Theoretical Return Convergence**.

## Analysis of "Short Pay" Predation
The real "Payout Gap" occurs when casinos offer sub-optimal versions of these games. 
*   **Double Bonus (8/5):** RTP falls to 94.19%.
*   **Double Double Bonus (7/5):** RTP falls to 95.71%.

If you find an 8/5 Double Bonus machine next to a 7/5 Double Double Bonus machine, the 1.52% **EV Gap** is predatory. A professional player would never touch the 8/5 Double Bonus machine in this scenario. However, if the choice is between 8/5 Double Bonus (94.19%) and 9/6 Double Double Bonus (98.98%), the Double Double Bonus machine is actually the much better bet.

## House Edge Variance and Strategy
Double Double Bonus has higher variance than Double Bonus. This is because more of the return is tied up in the Four of a Kind with a kicker payouts. In Double Bonus, the Straight payout (5-for-1) is the engine that keeps your bankroll alive. 
*   **Double Bonus Strategy:** Focuses on bankroll preservation and hitting the Four of a Kind.
*   **Double Double Bonus Strategy:** Slightly adjusts to account for the higher value of Four of a Kind with a kicker.
