# Bonus Poker vs. Double Double Bonus: Strategy Overlap and Hand-Ranking Priorities

For the video poker enthusiast, the choice between **Bonus Poker** and **Double Double Bonus (DDB)** is a lesson in extreme **Optimal Hold Variance**. While both games are "Bonus" variants that pay extra for specific 4-of-a-kind hands, they distribute their return-to-player (RTP) and prioritize their "Kicker" bonuses differently. Understanding the **Hand-Ranking Inversion** between these two is essential for anyone looking for the **best video poker strategy comparison** and learning **how to play video poker optimally**.

## The Core Conflict: Stability vs. The Kicker Push

The primary driver of the strategy shift between Bonus Poker and Double Double Bonus is the "Kicker" system and the Two Pair payout. Bonus Poker (typically 8/5) is a "balanced" game that pays 2-for-1 for Two Pair and 8-for-1 for a Full House. Double Double Bonus (typically 9/6 or 8/5) is a "top-heavy" game that pays only 1-for-1 for Two Pair, but compensates with massive payouts for 4-of-a-kind (especially with a kicker).

| Hand | Bonus Poker (8/5) | Double Double Bonus (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Two Pair** | 2-for-1 | 1-for-1 |
| **Flush** | 5-for-1 | 6-for-1 |
| **Full House** | 8-for-1 | 9-for-1 |
| **4 Aces w/ 2,3,4 Kicker** | 80-for-1 | 400-for-1 |

The reduction of the Two Pair payout from 2-for-1 to 1-for-1 in DDB is the single most significant **Strategy Convergence** breaker in video poker history.

## 1. Strategy Overlap: High Strategy Convergence in Foundations

If you are coming from a Jacks or Better background, you can reuse about 50% of your strategy for both Bonus Poker and Double Double Bonus. The **Strategy Convergence** is high because the basic hand rankings remain mostly traditional.

*   **High Pair Priority:** In both games, starting with a pair of Jacks or Better is a foundational hold.
*   **Three of a Kind:** In both games, Three of a Kind is a premium situation, always drawing for the 4th card.
*   **Royal Flush Draws:** The priority of a 4-card Royal Flush remains at the top of the list in both games.

## 2. Hand-Ranking Inversion: The Two Pair and Kicker Pivot

The real strategy change comes from the bonuses in each game. This causes a **Hand-Ranking Inversion** where certain cards become more valuable than others.

*   **The Two Pair Pivot:** In Bonus Poker, you ALWAYS hold Two Pair. It pays 2-for-1, which is a "win." In Double Double Bonus, Two Pair only pays 1-for-1 (even money). This means it is no longer a "win," but a "push." Because of this, the math often dictates breaking Two Pair in DDB if it contains a pair of Aces. You hold the pair of Aces and discard the other pair to maximize your chances of hitting 4 Aces with a 2, 3, or 4 kicker (400-for-1). This is the definition of **Optimal Hold Variance**.
*   **The Kicker Dominance:** In Double Double Bonus, hitting 4 Aces with a 2, 3, or 4 kicker is a massive 400-for-1 win. In Bonus Poker, hitting 4 Aces is 80-for-1. This means you will hold onto a lone Ace or a pair of Aces much more aggressively in DDB.

## 3. Penalty Card Impact: The Bonus Hunt

Professional players focus on **Penalty Card Impact** when hunting for those specific quads.

*   **In Bonus Poker:** You are looking for Aces and 2-4s. Discarding a card that could have completed a Straight but "penalizes" your Ace pair potential is more common.
*   **In Double Double Bonus:** You are looking for Aces and kicker cards (2, 3, 4). Discarding a card that could have completed a Straight but "penalizes" your kicker potential is the rule, not the exception.

## 4. Optimal Hold Variance: Stability vs. Bonus Potential

Double Double Bonus is significantly more volatile than Bonus Poker.

*   **Bonus Poker Volatility:** ~20.9 (Low-Medium). Your bankroll will experience moderate swings as you hunt for the 80-for-1 Aces and 40-for-1 low quads.
*   **Double Double Bonus Volatility:** ~42.0 (High). Your bankroll will experience long "dry spells," punctuated by massive, life-changing wins.

## Conclusion: How to Transition

When moving from Bonus Poker to Double Double Bonus, the **best video poker strategy comparison** shows that you must become "quad-centric" and "kicker-focused." 

1.  **Devalue the Two Pair:** If you have Aces and another pair, keep the Aces and throw away the other pair in DDB. The 400-for-1 potential is mathematically superior to the 1-for-1 push.
2.  **Hunt the Kicker:** In Double Double Bonus, understand that hitting 4 Aces with a 2, 3, or 4 kicker is a massive win. Don't ignore those kicker cards.
3.  **Bankroll Management:** You need a much larger bankroll for Double Double Bonus (at least 3-4x what you need for Bonus Poker) to survive the volatility.

By mastering these **Hand-Ranking Priorities**, you can navigate the shift from the 2-for-1 Two Pair world of Bonus Poker to the quad-focused world of Double Double Bonus and learn **how to play video poker optimally**.
