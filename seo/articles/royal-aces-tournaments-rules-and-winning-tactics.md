# Royal Aces Tournaments: Rules and Winning Tactics
Category: Tournament Guide

Video poker tournaments bring a different flavor to the casino floor, transforming a solitary "man vs. machine" struggle into a high-octane competitive event. When those tournaments feature **Royal Aces**, the stakes and strategies shift dramatically. Because of the game's unique 2,000-coin bonus for Quad Aces, a **royal aces video poker strategy** for tournaments looks very different from standard "cash" play.

## Tournament Rules: The Basics

Most Royal Aces tournaments follow a standard format:
1.  **Timed Sessions:** You have a set amount of time (e.g., 15 or 20 minutes) and a set number of "credits" (e.g., 1,000 credits).
2.  **Use It or Lose It:** Credits have no cash value outside the tournament. Your goal is to accumulate the highest possible score before time runs out.
3.  **No Strategy Cards:** Most tournaments forbid external aids. You must know your **Aces-heavy EV** by heart.

## Winning Tactics: The "Aces or Bust" Mentality

In a tournament, "playing it safe" is the fastest way to lose. You are competing against hundreds of other players, several of whom *will* hit a Royal Flush or Quad Aces. To win, you must also hit those big hands.

### 1. Speed is Your Best Friend
In a timed tournament, every second you spend thinking is a second you aren't dealing cards. You need to play at a blistering pace—ideally 600-800 hands per hour. If you don't use all your credits, you have essentially forfeited your chance to win.

### 2. Radical Aces-Heavy EV
In "cash" play, you might keep a Full House or a Flush. In a **Royal Aces** tournament, if you are behind in the standings, you must be willing to break a "guaranteed" win to chase the 2,000-coin Quad Aces.
*   **Example:** Dealt AAAKK (Full House). In a tournament, if you need a huge score to catch the leaders, you discard the KK and draw for the 4th Ace. The 2,000-coin payout is the only thing that will move the needle.

### 3. Kicker Dependencies? Forget Them.
Royal Aces tournaments are about pure volume. Don't waste time looking for "lucky" kickers. Hold your Aces and hammer the "Deal" button.

### 4. The 2,000-Coin Bonus vs. The Royal Flush
In Royal Aces, the Quad Aces payout (2,000) is exactly half of a Royal Flush (4,000). However, you are statistically far more likely to hit the Aces. In a tournament setting:
*   Always hold a pair of Aces over 3 cards to a Royal Flush.
*   The frequency of the 2,000-coin bonus is what wins Royal Aces tournaments, not the rare Royal Flush.

## Managing the Leaderboard

Most tournaments display a live leaderboard. 
*   **If you are in the Top 3:** You can shift slightly toward a more "conservative" strategy to maintain your lead.
*   **If you are in the "Middle of the Pack":** You must go "Full Turbo." Break everything that isn't a 4-of-a-kind to hunt for those Aces.

## Conclusion

Royal Aces tournaments are won by the fastest players who are willing to take the most risks. By embracing an aggressive **royal aces video poker strategy**, prioritizing the 2,000-coin bonus, and maintaining a high hand-per-minute rate, you give yourself the best chance to climb the leaderboard. Remember: in a tournament, finishing with 100 "safe" credits is a failure. Use every credit, chase every Ace, and play for the win.
