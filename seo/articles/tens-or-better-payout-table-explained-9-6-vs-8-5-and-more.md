# Tens or Better Payout Table Explained: 6/5 vs 5/5 and More

In the **best odds video poker** world, Tens or Better is a common variant because it offers a lower pair requirement for a payout. For the professional player, this represents a unique opportunity for **Pay Table Optimization**.

## The Best Odds Index: 6/5 Tens or Better (99.14%)
In the **highest return video poker games** 2026 index, 6/5 Tens or Better is a top-tier choice. It offers a **Theoretical Return Convergence of 99.14%**.

The "6/5" designation here refers to:
*   **Full House:** 6 coins per 1 coin bet.
*   **Flush:** 5 coins per 1 coin bet.

Wait, why do we call it "6/5" Tens or Better? It's the pay table that pays **6 coins for a Full House AND 5 coins for a Flush**.

## Analysis of "Short Pay" Predation
Because 99.14% gives the player a strong return, casinos often use "Short Pay" tables to reverse it.
*   **5/5 Tens or Better:** Paying only 5 coins for a Full House drops the RTP to **98.05%**.
*   **6/4 Tens or Better:** Paying 4 for Flush drops the RTP to **98.00%**.
*   **Common Short Pay (5/4):** This variant drops the RTP below **97.00%**.

The **EV Gap** between the 99.14% version and the 97% version is over 2.14%. If you are playing 800 hands per hour at a $1 machine, that’s $21.4 per hour you’re losing simply by picking the wrong machine. This "predatory" short-pay table kills the professional edge.

## House Edge Variance in Tens or Better
Tens or Better has lower **House Edge Variance** than Jacks or Better. This is because the game's return is more evenly distributed due to the lower pair requirement. If you don't hit the top hands at the mathematically expected frequency, your actual return will lag behind the 99.14% theoretical return. Professional bankroll management is essential.
