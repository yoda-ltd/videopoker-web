# Video Poker Glossary: Terms Every Player Should Know

To master the machines, you must first master the language. This complete video poker glossary covers the essential video poker terms every player should know, from the basic mechanics to advanced strategic concepts.

## Professional Video Poker Terminology

### Action
**Definition:** The total amount of money wagered over a specific period. For example, if you play 500 hands at $1.25 per hand, your total action is $625.
**Pro Tip:** Casinos base your comps (rewards) on your total action, not your wins or losses. To maximize rewards, focus on high-volume play on machines with the lowest house edge.

### Bankroll
**Definition:** The dedicated fund of money a player sets aside specifically for gambling, separate from daily living expenses.
**Pro Tip:** A proper bankroll should be large enough to withstand the natural variance of the game. For Jacks or Better, a bankroll of 3-5 royal flush payouts (12,000 - 20,000 credits) is recommended for long-term professional play.

### Cycle
**Definition:** The theoretical number of hands it takes for a specific outcome to occur based on probability. The most common "cycle" discussed is the Royal Flush cycle, which is approximately once every 40,000 hands.
**Pro Tip:** Remember that a cycle is a statistical average, not a guarantee. You might hit two royals in 5,000 hands, or go 100,000 hands without one. Never chase a hand just because you feel it is "due."

### Full Pay
**Definition:** A pay table that offers the highest possible return for a specific game variant. In Jacks or Better, this is the "9/6" version (paying 9 for a Full House and 6 for a Flush).
**Pro Tip:** Always check the pay table before inserting money. Playing a "Short Pay" machine (like 8/5 Jacks or Better) instead of Full Pay can drop your expected return by over 1%.

### Short Pay
**Definition:** Any pay table where the payouts for certain hands (usually the Full House and Flush) have been reduced from the standard professional (Full Pay) levels.
**Pro Tip:** Short pay machines are the "hidden tax" of the casino. If you can't find a Full Pay machine, consider moving to a different casino or playing a lower denomination where the pay table might be better.

### Expected Value (EV)
**Definition:** The mathematical average amount a player can expect to win or lose on a specific hand if played perfectly.
**Pro Tip:** Strategic decisions should always be based on maximizing EV. Sometimes the "safe" play has a lower EV than a "risky" play that has a higher potential payout.

### Variance
**Definition:** The statistical measure of the "swings" in your bankroll. High variance games (like Triple Double Bonus) have frequent dry spells punctuated by massive payouts, while low variance games (like Jacks or Better) provide a steadier experience.
**Pro Tip:** Match your game choice to your bankroll. If you have a small bankroll, stick to low-variance games to avoid going broke before hitting a big hand.

### RNG (Random Number Generator)
**Definition:** The computer algorithm that constantly shuffles the virtual deck, ensuring that every hand dealt is independent and random.
**Pro Tip:** There is no such thing as a "hot" or "cold" machine. The RNG doesn't know if you just won or lost; your odds are exactly the same on every single spin.

### House Edge
**Definition:** The mathematical advantage that the casino holds over the player, expressed as a percentage of each bet.
**Pro Tip:** In video poker, the house edge is not fixed—it depends on your skill. By using perfect strategy on a Full Pay machine, you can often reduce the house edge to less than 0.5%, or even gain a slight edge over the casino.

## Complete Video Poker Glossary: Additional Terms

*   **Credits:** The unit of betting on the machine.
*   **Draw:** The second part of the hand where you replace discarded cards.
*   **Hold:** The cards you choose to keep before the draw.
*   **Max Bet:** Wager the maximum number of coins (usually 5) to activate the highest payout for a Royal Flush.
*   **Pat Hand:** A hand that is already a winner before the draw (e.g., being dealt a Straight).
*   **Pay Table:** The chart on the machine that lists the payout for every winning hand combination.
*   **Tilt:** A state of emotional frustration that leads to poor strategic decisions (common after a "bad beat").
