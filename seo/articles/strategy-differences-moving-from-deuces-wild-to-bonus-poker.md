# Strategy Differences: Moving from Deuces Wild to Bonus Poker
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Deuces Wild** to **Bonus Poker** is a shift from a "Wild Card" variant to a "Natural" variant. This represents a significant **Strategic Divergence** in how you value wild cards versus standard hand ranks.

### The Comparison Matrix: Deuces Wild vs. Bonus Poker

| Feature | Deuces Wild (9/6) | Bonus Poker (8/5) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 98.91% | 99.17% |
| **Variance Index** | 25.8 | 20.9 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Prioritize Deuces as Wild Cards | Target Natural Bonus Quads |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 1.09% | 0.83% |

### Strategic Divergence: The Wild Factor

In **Deuces Wild**, the four 2s are the most powerful cards in the deck, allowing you to complete hands that would otherwise be worthless. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through wild card connectivity. In **Bonus Poker**, all 52 cards are natural, and you are looking for specific bonuses on four-of-a-kind hands. This creates a much lower **Volatility Normalization** (20.9 vs 25.8).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 40% overlap. In Deuces Wild, you will hold a single 2 almost as if it were a high pair. In Bonus Poker, you only care about 2s if you have a pair or more. This creates a shift in **Payout Compression** where the "Middle Tier" hands (Straights/Flushes) are the focus in Deuces Wild, but the "Top Tier" hands (Four of a Kind) are the focus in Bonus Poker.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Deuce Devaluation:** When moving to Bonus Poker, 2s are just another card in the deck. They no longer offer a wild card feature.
2.  **High Card Supremacy:** In Bonus Poker, Jacks, Queens, Kings, and Aces are your primary focus. In Deuces Wild, they are almost entirely ignored unless they support a 4-to-a-Royal with Deuces.
3.  **Five of a Kind Removal:** This hand doesn't exist in Bonus Poker. You are now looking for natural four-of-a-kind combinations.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games are excellent for players who enjoy bonus payouts, but Bonus Poker provides a smoother ride with lower variance. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are top-tier choices for anyone looking for a player advantage.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Deuces Wild to Double Bonus Poker
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Deuces Wild** to **Double Bonus Poker** is a shift from a "Wild Card" variant to a "High Bonus" natural variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value four-of-a-kind hands and the volatility that comes with them.

### The Comparison Matrix: Deuces Wild vs. Double Bonus Poker

| Feature | Deuces Wild (9/6) | Double Bonus Poker (10/7) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 98.91% | 100.17% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 25.8 | 28.5 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Deuce-Based Wilds | Target High-Payout Natural Quads |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 1.09% | -0.17% (Player Edge) |

### Strategic Divergence: Wild Connectivity vs. Bonus Quads

In **Deuces Wild**, you are looking for 2s to complete your hands. In **Double Bonus Poker**, you are looking for natural four-of-a-kind hands that pay a massive bonus. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through high-tier natural quads. Double Bonus Poker's variance (28.5) is slightly higher than Deuces Wild (25.8), requiring careful **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 30% overlap. In Deuces Wild, you would never discard a 2. In Double Bonus Poker, you will break a Full House to hold three Aces much more frequently than in any other game. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the high-tier hands in Double Bonus dominate the EV landscape.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Deuce Devaluation:** In Double Bonus Poker, 2s are only valuable if you have a pair or more. They no longer complete straights and flushes.
2.  **Ace Supremacy:** In Double Bonus Poker, Aces are the highest-paying quads. You prioritize them over almost any other card rank.
3.  **Full House Break:** You will break a Full House containing three Aces to draw for the fourth Ace. This is a major departure from Deuces Wild.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that Double Bonus Poker offers a player advantage in its full-pay version, but the volatility is higher. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are top-tier choices for the disciplined player.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Deuces Wild to Double Double Bonus Poker
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Deuces Wild** to **Double Double Bonus Poker** is a shift from a "Wild Card" variant to a "Kicker-Based" natural variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value specific card combinations and their related "Kickers."

### The Comparison Matrix: Deuces Wild vs. Double Double Bonus Poker

| Feature | Deuces Wild (9/6) | Double Double Bonus (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 98.91% | 98.98% |
| **Variance Index** | 25.8 | 42.0 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Deuce Connectivity | Target Quads with Kickers |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 1.09% | 1.02% |

### Strategic Divergence: Wild Card vs. Kicker Jackpot

In **Deuces Wild**, the four 2s are your most powerful tools. In **Double Double Bonus Poker**, the payout for Four Aces, 2s, 3s, and 4s depends on the fifth card in the hand (the "Kicker"). This is a game of **EV Maximization** through specific high-tier combinations, leading to extreme **Volatility Normalization** (42.0 vs 25.8).

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is minimal (less than 20%). In Deuces Wild, you would never hold a kicker with a pair. In Double Double Bonus, if you have three Aces and a low kicker (2, 3, 4), you will keep the kicker to draw for the massive 2,000-credit bonus. This is the essence of **Payout Compression** in kicker-based games.

### Critical Strategy Shifts
1.  **Kicker Knowledge:** You must learn which cards are "Kickers" for each quad tier (Aces, 2s, 3s, 4s).
2.  **Ace-Kicker Priority:** Holding a pair of Aces with a low kicker is a valid strategy in Double Double Bonus, whereas in Deuces Wild, you'd only hold the Aces (and the 2s).
3.  **Variance Management:** Double Double Bonus has a very high variance (42.0). You need a larger bankroll to withstand the swings of "Kicker Hunting."

A **video poker house edge analysis** indicates that while Double Double Bonus is the most popular game in many casinos, it requires a higher degree of mental flexibility to master the "Natural" kicker strategy. The **best video poker strategy comparison** for this transition is: Forget the deuces; find the Aces and the Kickers.
