# Strategy Differences: Moving from Hot Roll Poker to Dice Poker

## Multipliers vs. Dice as Cards
The core difference is that in Dice Poker, the dice *are* the cards.

- **Hot Roll Poker (The Multiplier Variant):** You play standard video poker (cards). The dice are a side-feature that multiplies your card-based win.
- **Dice Poker (The Fixed-Outcome Variant):** You don't use cards. Instead, you are "Dealt" five dice. You choose which to hold and which to "Roll" again to form hands like "Small Straight," "Full House," or "Five of a Kind."

## Key Strategic Adjustments

### 1. The Death of the "Royal Flush" Chase
- **In Hot Roll Poker:** You are always hunting for the Royal Flush or the Four-of-a-Kind. The strategy is built on standard poker rankings.
- **In Dice Poker:** There is no "Royal Flush." The top payout is usually for "Five of a Kind" (all five dice showing the same number).
- **The Shift:** You must learn a completely new hand-ranking table. A "Full House" in Dice Poker is much easier to hit than in card-based poker, and its payout reflects that lower volatility.

### 2. Variance Normalization and Bankroll Management
- **Hot Roll Poker Shift:** High variance. You bet 10 coins per hand (5 + 5 side-bet) and hope for a high multiplier.
- **Dice Poker Shift:** Low to Moderate variance. Dice Poker usually has a very flat payout structure. Because the probabilities of dice outcomes are different from cards (only 6 possible ranks vs. 13), the game produces many small wins.
- **The Adjustment:** Dice Poker is a "Bankroll Preservation" game. It's often used by players who want to "Kill Time" or enjoy the physical sensation of rolling dice without the high-stakes pressure of a 10-coin Hot Roll multiplier.

### 3. Side-Bet EV vs. Base-Game EV
- **Hot Roll Poker:** The 5-coin side-bet is mandatory to trigger the dice. Without it, the RTP is poor.
- **Dice Poker:** There is usually no side-bet. The "Dice" feature is the base game.
- **The Shift:** In Hot Roll, you are "Paying for a Bonus." In Dice Poker, you are "Playing the Game." This makes Dice Poker a much more transparent experience for the player.
