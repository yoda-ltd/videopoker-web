# Ultimate Joker Poker Strategy Chart for 2026

Joker Poker (often referred to as "Kings or Better") is a unique "Wild Card" variant that adds a 53rd card to the deck: the Joker. This small change completely transforms the **EV Maximization** landscape. To learn **how to play video poker like a pro**, you must first understand the **Expected Return Differential** between a Joker-led hand and a "Natural" hand.

## Analysis: 3 Most Common "Pro-Level" Errors in Joker Poker

### 1. Misvaluing the Joker as a "Kicker"
A common error even for intermediate players is treating the Joker like a high card rather than a Wild card.

**The Error:** Holding a Joker with a high card (like an Ace) (e.g., `Joker-A`).
**The Pro Reality:** In Joker Poker, a Joker with one high card is almost always a "drawing" hand. Strategy fine-tuning dictates that you should only hold the Joker by itself (or with other cards that complete a strong "made" hand like a flush or straight). The Joker is so powerful that holding it with a non-matching card lowers your overall probability of drawing into more valuable combinations.

### 2. Overvaluing "Natural" Hands in Joker Poker
Players often forget that the payouts in Joker Poker are adjusted to account for the Wild card.

**The Error:** Keeping a pair of 2s in Joker Poker.
**The Pro Reality:** In "Kings or Better" Joker Poker, a pair of 2s pays *zero*. The **Rank-Based Priority** in 2026 dictates that if you have no Joker, you should be much more aggressive in drawing for it. Often, discarding a low pair is better than holding it, as the low pair has almost no path to a "Wild" bonus that actually pays out.

### 3. Mismanaging the 4-to-a-Royal Flush Draw
The presence of the Joker creates a "Wild Royal Flush" category, which pays significantly less than a "Natural Royal Flush."

**The Error:** Breaking a strong 4-of-a-kind (including a Joker) to draw for a Natural Royal Flush.
**The Pro Reality:** A Natural Royal Flush is incredibly rare. The **Expected Return Differential** strongly favors the "made" 4-of-a-kind over the slim chance of a Natural Royal. Pro players treat the Joker as the most valuable card and rarely discard it, even for a potential Royal.

## Hand-Ranking Priorities in Joker Poker
The "Hand-Ranking Priority" in Joker Poker is unique: **The Joker > Everything else.** Because the Joker is so powerful, your primary objective is to maximize the frequency of hands that include it. **Strategy Fine-Tuning** in Joker Poker requires a "Wild-Focused" mindset. If you want to play like a pro, you must be comfortable discarding "good" hands in Jacks or Better to hunt for the elusive Joker that defines this game.
