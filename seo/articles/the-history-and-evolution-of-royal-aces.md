# The History and Evolution of Royal Aces Video Poker

Royal Aces is a specialized variant that sits at the intersection of "Bonus Poker" and "Jacks or Better." Its history is rooted in the competitive Las Vegas market of the late 20th century, where casinos were constantly tweaking pay tables to attract "locals"—players who knew the game inside and out.

## The "Royal" Concept

The name "Royal Aces" is a bit of a marketing flourish. It combines the two most desirable hands in video poker: The Royal Flush and Four Aces. In this variant, the Four Aces payout is "elevated" to a status almost equal to the Royal Flush, hence the name.

The game first appeared in "locals-focused" casinos like the Station Casinos and Coast Casinos in Las Vegas. These venues needed games that offered something more exciting than the standard "9/6 Jacks or Better" to keep their regulars coming back.

## How the Game Evolved

Royal Aces introduced several key changes to the standard video poker model:

1.  **Ace-Centric Payouts:** The primary evolution was the creation of a "top-heavy" pay table. While most quads (5s through Kings) paid the standard amount, Aces were boosted significantly.
2.  **Trade-offs in the Pay Table:** To afford the "Royal" Ace payouts, casinos typically reduced the payouts for the Full House and Flush. This was the birth of the "weighted" pay table, a concept now found in the complete video poker glossary.
3.  **Strategy Divergence:** As Royal Aces evolved, it became clear that "Jacks or Better" strategy wouldn't work. Players had to learn to "over-hold" Aces, a strategy that increased the "Variance" of the game.

## Video Poker Terms Every Player Should Know

When playing Royal Aces, these terms are crucial:

*   **Bankroll:** Since the game is "top-heavy," your bankroll will experience steeper declines while you wait for those "Royal Aces."
*   **Action:** Royal Aces is considered a "high-action" game. The potential for a massive payout on any given hand keeps the energy high.
*   **Expected Value (EV):** For the professional, the EV of Royal Aces is often higher than standard Bonus Poker, provided you can find a machine with a decent Full House/Flush payout.

## Royal Aces in the Digital Age

With the transition to modern IGT and Scientific Games platforms, Royal Aces has been incorporated into various multi-game "bundles." It remains a favorite for players who enjoy the "hunt" for Aces but find the more complex games like "Double Double Bonus" (with its kicker requirements) too tedious.

The history of Royal Aces is a reminder of the "Goldilocks" principle in game design—finding the version that is "just right" for players who want high rewards without high complexity. It remains a cornerstone of the modern video poker experience.
