# Common Mistakes to Avoid in Triple Bonus Plus

Triple Bonus Plus is a high-variance video poker variant that offers significantly increased payouts for various Four-of-a-Kind hands. While the potential for big wins is high, the "top-heavy" pay table leads to several common mistakes.

### Chasing the Bonus Hands
The most frequent mistake in Triple Bonus Plus is over-valuing the bonus hands (Four-of-a-Kind). Players often break apart winning hands (like a Straight or a Flush) to chase a Four-of-a-Kind. This destroys your **Hit Frequency Optimization**. Unless the strategy chart explicitly tells you to break a hand, you should always keep your guaranteed wins to maintain **Session Sustainability**.

### Ignoring the Base Pay Table
Just because a machine says "Triple Bonus Plus" doesn't mean it's a good bet. Casinos often lower the payout for the smaller, more common hands (like Two Pair or Three-of-a-Kind) to compensate for the higher bonus payouts. If the base pay table is too weak, the overall **Theoretical Return** will be lower than a standard Jacks or Better machine. To learn **how to win video poker daily**, you must look at the whole table.

### Bankroll Mismanagement
Triple Bonus Plus has much higher **Variance** than standard video poker. Because a larger portion of the return is tied up in the bonus hands, you will experience longer and deeper losing streaks. For **Session Sustainability**, you need a substantial bankroll for **Variance Buffering**. If you don't have enough units to weather the "dry spells," you will bust out.

### "Is Max Bet Always Better for Bankroll Longevity?"
In Triple Bonus Plus, the answer is an absolute yes. The bonus payouts for the Four-of-a-Kind and the Royal Flush are only fully available at the 5-coin level. For those looking for **video poker tips for beginners**, our number one tip for Triple Bonus Plus is: 5 coins or find a different game.

### Misunderstanding the RNG
Some players believe the bonus hands are "due" to hit after a long period without one. This is a misunderstanding of **Pseudo-Randomness**. The RNG deals the cards fairly for every hand. A bonus hand can hit twice in a row or not appear for thousands of hands.

Triple Bonus Plus is a game of high risk and high reward. Avoid these mistakes, play with discipline, and you'll find it to be a highly rewarding addition to your video poker repertoire.
