# Strategy Differences: Moving from Tens or Better to White Hot Aces

Transitioning from Tens or Better to White Hot Aces is a major shift in how you play video poker. Tens or Better is a very stable, low-volatility experience. White Hot Aces is a volatile variant that rewards you for getting four Aces with a kicker. This guide provides the best video poker strategy comparison to help you understand how to play video poker like a pro during this transition.

## The Tens or Better Baseline

In Tens or Better, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is built on the fact that any pair of 10s or higher is a winning hand. This creates a very stable experience. When moving to White Hot Aces, that "safety net" is removed. You now need at least a pair of Aces to get your money back.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from Tens or Better to White Hot Aces, you'll find that **only about 40% of your strategy is portable**. This is why this transition causes such massive **Bankroll Drain** for Tens or Better experts. The **60% that changes** is what separates a professional White Hot Aces player from a hobbyist.

### The 60% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Pair of 10s/Jacks/Queens/Kings Devaluation:** In Tens or Better, a pair of 10s, Jacks, Queens, or Kings is a winning hand. In White Hot Aces, it's a "trash" hand that you should rarely hold unless you're drawing for something better (like a three-card Royal Flush).
2.  **White Hot Aces Focus:** White Hot Aces is named "White Hot Aces" because it rewards you specifically for getting four Aces with a kicker. This makes four-of-a-kind and specific straights much more valuable than in Tens or Better.
3.  **Two Pair Strategy:** While Two Pair pays 2-for-1 in both games, the higher payout for specific "White Hot Aces" hands in White Hot Aces means you might break a Full House to draw for a fifth card more often than you would in Tens or Better.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

White Hot Aces introduces **Volatility Scaling**. Without the 10s-or-better push, your bankroll will experience more frequent dips. **EV Erosion** occurs when a Tens-or-Better player instinctively holds a pair of 10s, Jacks, Queens, or Kings in White Hot Aces. This is a "dead hand" and holding it is a direct loss of expected value.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Aces or Better are your new minimum "win."
*   **Modify:** Discard any pair of 10s, Jacks, Queens, or Kings unless they are part of a better draw.
*   **Target:** Aggressively pursue the "White Hot Aces" bonuses; they are the heart of the White Hot Aces pay table.

By recognizing that the "safety net" has moved, you can successfully transition to White Hot Aces without suffering from significant EV Erosion.
