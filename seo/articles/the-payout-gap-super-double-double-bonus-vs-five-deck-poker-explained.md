# The Payout Gap: Super Double Double Bonus vs. Five-Deck Poker Explained
Category: Comparison

Understanding the **Strategic Divergence** between **Super Double Double Bonus** and **Five-Deck Poker** is key to mastering these high-action variants. This comparison focuses on how the paytables differ and why those differences lead to unique **EV Maximization** paths.

| :--- | :--- | :--- |

### The Kicker Gap: Natural Kickers vs. 5 Decks

In **Five-Deck Poker**, the reward is concentrated in Achieving high-payout hands across five independent decks. In **Super Double Double Bonus Poker**, the reward is concentrated in Achieving natural four-of-a-kind hands with a kicker for a massive bonus. This creates a significant **Volatility Normalization** shift. In Five-Deck Poker, you focus on natural connectivity across decks, while in Super Double Double Bonus, you prioritize kicker-based quads.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 60% overlap on the core hand results. However, in Five-Deck Poker, you are looking for Straights and Flushes much more frequently because you have five independent decks to draw from. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the connectivity in Five-Deck Poker dominates the EV landscape.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Kicker Awareness:** Always be aware of the kicker for the bonus in Super Double Double Bonus.
2.  **Connectivity Priority:** In Five-Deck Poker, prioritize 4-to-a-flush and 4-to-a-straight across multiple decks.
3.  **Return Priority:** Super Double Double Bonus offers a slightly higher theoretical return (99.69%), making it the better choice for the long-term player.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that Super Double Double Bonus Poker is a high-action game that rewards kicker hunting, but Five-Deck Poker provides a smoother ride with lower variance. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are excellent choices for the disciplined player.
