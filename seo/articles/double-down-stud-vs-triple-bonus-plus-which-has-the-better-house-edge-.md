# Double Down Stud vs. Triple Bonus Plus: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Double Down Stud and Triple Bonus Poker Plus are two high-action video poker games that appeal to "bonus hunters." Double Down Stud allows you to double your bet on high-potential hands, while Triple Bonus Poker Plus offers massive payouts for four-of-a-kind, even higher than standard Double Bonus games.

Which one should you play for the best chance of winning? Let's analyze the house edge.

## Triple Bonus Poker Plus: The Jackpot Heavyweight

Triple Bonus Poker Plus is one of the most volatile games in the casino. It takes the "Bonus" concept and cranks it up to 11.

*   **Four Aces:** Typically pays **240-for-1** (1,200 coins for 5).
*   **Four 2s, 3s, or 4s:** Typically pays **120-for-1** (600 coins).
*   **Four 5s through Kings:** Typically pays **50-for-1** (250 coins).
*   **Two Pair:** Pays **1-for-1**.
*   **Full Pay RTP (9/5):** **99.8%**
*   **House Edge:** 0.2%

The 240-for-1 payout for Aces is massive—nearly a third of the way to a Royal Flush. This makes Triple Bonus Plus an incredibly exciting game for players who love "big hits."

## Double Down Stud: The Betting Strategy Game

Double Down Stud is a unique game with no draws. You see four cards and decide whether to double your bet before the fifth card is revealed.

*   **Full Pay RTP (9/6):** **98.49%**
*   **House Edge:** 1.51%

## Comparing the House Edge

### 1. Theoretical Return (RTP)
In a head-to-head comparison of "Full Pay" machines, **Triple Bonus Poker Plus is the far superior game.**
*   Triple Bonus Poker Plus: **99.8% RTP**
*   Double Down Stud: **98.49% RTP**

A 99.8% return is one of the best you can find in any casino. It means that for every $1,000 you bet, you only "expect" to lose $2. In Double Down Stud, you would expect to lose $15.10 for that same $1,000 bet.

### 2. Strategy Complexity
*   **Triple Bonus Poker Plus strategy** is extremely difficult. Because the payouts for Aces and low cards are so high, you will often make "crazy" holds, like keeping a single Ace over a pair of Kings. 
*   **Double Down Stud strategy** is simple. You just have to know when a four-card hand is strong enough to justify doubling your bet. There are no cards to hold or discard.

### 3. Volatility (The "Bust" Factor)
*   **Triple Bonus Plus** is "off the charts" in terms of volatility. Because so much of the return is tied up in the 240-for-1 and 120-for-1 bonus hands, you will experience very long "dry spells." You need a very large bankroll to survive this game.
*   **Double Down Stud** is also volatile due to the 10-coin bet, but the payouts are more "flat," so the swings aren't as extreme as hitting a 1,200-coin Ace bonus.

## Verdict: Which Should You Play?

*   **Play Triple Bonus Poker Plus if** you are an experienced player with a large bankroll and want the best mathematical return. A 99.8% machine is a fantastic find.
*   **Play Double Down Stud if** you want a simpler game or if the only Triple Bonus Plus machines available are "short-pay" (returning 97% or less).

**Pro Tip:** Triple Bonus Plus is often "hidden" under poor pay tables. If you see a version where the Full House/Flush is 8/5 or 7/5, the RTP drops to **96% or lower**. In those cases, a 9/6 **Double Down Stud machine actually becomes the better mathematical bet.** Always check the numbers before you start playing!
