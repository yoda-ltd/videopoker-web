# Good Times Pay vs. Triple Bonus Plus: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Comparing **Good Times Pay** and **Triple Bonus Plus** is a study in random multipliers vs. tiered rank bonuses. While Good Times Pay offers high-multiplier excitement on any winning hand, Triple Bonus Plus rewards the player for hitting specific high-ranking Four-of-a-Kind hands. To choose the right game, you must understand how **video poker fairness and regulation** ensures the integrity of these specialized "Bonus-Focused" models.

## The Mathematical Framework: Multipliers vs. Quad Bonuses

The house edge in these games is determined by the "Payout Concentration."

*   **Good Times Pay:** The value is spread across all winning hands via a random multiplier (up to 10x). This increases the variance of standard hands like Two Pair and Full Houses.
*   **Triple Bonus Plus:** The value is concentrated in the top of the pay table. Four Aces, for example, typically pays 120-for-1 or more, with other tiered bonuses for 2s, 3s, and 4s.

The accuracy of the advertised edge in these variants is guaranteed by **Return to Player (RTP) Verification** during independent **Lab Audits**.

## 1. Technical Standards for Random Triggers

Both variants must adhere to strict **Technical Standards** enforced by regulators like the **NGCB** or **UKGC**.

*   **Good Times Pay:** The **Random Number Generator (RNG)** must provide a fair distribution of multipliers. Firms like **GLI** audit the code to ensure the 10x multiplier is truly random and independent of the cards.
*   **Triple Bonus Plus:** Regulators verify that the high-ranking cards (Aces through 4s) are dealt with exactly the same frequency as other ranks. The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures that the machine doesn't "starve" the bonus combinations.

## 2. Payout Analysis: The "Jackpot" Funding

In these games, you are effectively paying a "tax" for access to the massive hits.
- In Triple Bonus Plus, the payouts for Two Pair may be lower (1-for-1) to fund the 120-for-1 Aces.
- In Good Times Pay, the base Full House/Flush payouts are lower to fund the average value of the multipliers.
