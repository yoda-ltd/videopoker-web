# Bonus Poker vs. Aces and Faces: House Edge and Strategy Synergy

In the landscape of "best video poker strategy comparison," **Bonus Poker vs. Aces and Faces** is a battle of bonus quads vs. high-card rewards. Both games belong to the "Bonus" category of Jacks or Better variants, and they both prioritize 4 Aces as the top payout below a Royal Flush. However, they distribute their return-to-player (RTP) through different **Payout Weighting** systems for the remaining quads, creating a subtle but critical **Strategy Bifurcation** for pro players.

## The House Edge Analysis: Bonus Aces vs. Balanced Quads

To perform a professional **video poker house edge analysis**, we must examine the "Full Pay" versions of both games.

| Game | Full Pay Version | Return (RTP) | House Edge |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bonus Poker** | 8/5 (99.17%) | 99.17% | 0.83% |
| **Aces and Faces** | 8/5 (99.26%) | 99.26% | 0.74% |

While **Full Pay Aces and Faces** technically offers a lower house edge in its purest form, the **EV Gap** between the two is often closed by the fact that Bonus Poker pays 40-for-1 for 4 2s, 3s, and 4s, which are more common than 4 face cards.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Low Card" vs. "High Card" Hold

The most significant **Strategy Bifurcation** occurs in the way you handle the "Low" cards vs. the "Face" cards.

*   **Bonus Poker Strategy:** Because 4 2s, 3s, or 4s pay 40-for-1 (vs. the standard 25-for-1), you will more aggressively hold pairs of low cards. If you have a choice between a 4-card Straight and a low pair (2s-4s), the **Optimal Hold** in Bonus Poker is always the pair.
*   **Aces and Faces Strategy:** In this game, 4 Jacks, Queens, or Kings pay 40-for-1. This creates a unique **Hand-Ranking Inversion**. You will hold a pair of face cards over a 4-card Straight or Flush, a move that would be mathematically incorrect in almost any other video poker variant.

## Strategy Synergy: Where the Games Align

Despite the different quad bonuses, there is massive **Strategy Synergy** in the "Base Game" logic:

1.  **Aces are King:** In both games, 4 Aces pay 80-for-1. This is your primary goal. You always hold a pair of Aces, and you never break them to chase a Flush or Straight.
2.  **Royal Flush Priority:** In both games, a 4-card Royal Flush is the top-tier hold.
3.  **Jacks or Better Foundation:** Both games pay 1-for-1 for a pair of Jacks or better, providing a stable floor for your bankroll.

## Variance Inversion: Bonus Quads vs. Frequent Pair Wins

The most jarring difference for players is the **Variance Inversion** caused by the pay table.

*   **Bonus Poker:** The variance is spread more evenly across the low and high cards. Because you get a bonus for any 4-of-a-kind involving 2s, 3s, 4s, or Aces, you hit "significant" wins more frequently.
*   **Aces and Faces:** The volatility is slightly higher because the bonus is concentrated in Aces, Jacks, Queens, and Kings. You will go for longer stretches without a bonus quad, but the face card payout is larger (40-for-1 vs. the standard 25-for-1).

## Conclusion: Which to Choose?

If you prefer a game with a lower house edge and don't mind the "face-card" focus, **Aces and Faces** is the superior choice for long-term play.

However, if you enjoy the familiarity of holding low pairs and want a slightly more "frequent" bonus experience, **Bonus Poker** is an excellent alternative. For a complete **video poker house edge analysis**, always check the pay table: an 8/5 Aces and Faces machine will almost always outperform a standard Bonus Poker machine in the long run.
