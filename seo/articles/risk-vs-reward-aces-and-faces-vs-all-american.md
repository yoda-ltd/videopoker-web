# Risk vs. Reward: Aces and Faces vs. All American

When comparing **Aces and Faces** with **All American**, you are looking at two variants that take the standard Jacks or Better model and "boost" specific hand payouts. Aces and Faces rewards high-card Four of a Kinds, while All American rewards the "Mid-Tier" hands like Straights and Flushes.

Which one offers a better balance of risk and reward for your gambling dollar? Let’s break down the math.

## 1. Payout Philosophies

### Aces and Faces
*   **The Bonus:** Higher payouts for Four of a Kind involving "Faces" (J, Q, K) and "Aces."
*   **Aces Quad Payout:** Often 80:1.
*   **Faces Quad Payout:** Often 40:1.
*   **Strategy:** You prioritize high-value cards (Aces and Face cards) even more than in standard Jacks or Better.

### All American
*   **The Bonus:** Higher payouts for Straight Flushes (200:1), Straights (8:1), and Flushes (8:1).
*   **Strategy:** You prioritize "Connectors" and "Suited Cards." You are looking to build sequences rather than just matching high-card ranks.

## 2. House Edge and RTP Comparison

| Game | Full Pay RTP | House Edge |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **All American (8/8/8)** | **100.72%** | **-0.72% (Player Advantage)** |
| **Aces and Faces (8/5)** | **99.26%** | **0.74%** |

**The Verdict:** **All American is the mathematically superior game.** It is one of the very few games in the world where the player has a theoretical edge over the house. Aces and Faces is a great game with a sub-1% house edge, but it can’t compete with the 100%+ return of All American.

## 3. Variance and Bankroll Risk

*   **Aces and Faces Variance:** ~19.8 (Low). Because this game pays 2:1 for Two Pair, it is a very "steady" game. You win often, and your bankroll doesn't fluctuate wildly.
*   **All American Variance:** ~30.0 (High). Because Two Pair only pays 1:1, you will experience more "losing" hands. You rely on hitting 8:1 Flushes/Straights and 200:1 Straight Flushes to stay ahead.

**The Verdict:** **All American is significantly riskier.** You need a much larger bankroll to play All American at the same stakes as Aces and Faces.

## 4. Strategy Comparison

*   **Aces and Faces Strategy:** **Easy**. It is very similar to Jacks or Better. If you can play Jacks or Better, you can play Aces and Faces with almost no learning curve.
*   **All American Strategy:** **Difficult**. The strategy is counter-intuitive. Breaking a high pair to chase a 4-card flush is something most players find hard to do, but it is required for optimal play.

## 5. Which One Should You Play?

**Choose All American if:**
*   You are a "Grinder" looking for a mathematical edge.
*   You have a large bankroll and can handle long losing streaks.
*   You enjoy the challenge of a complex, non-standard strategy.

**Choose Aces and Faces if:**
*   You want a "Low Stress" session with frequent small wins.
*   You love hitting Four of a Kind with high cards.
*   You are a beginner or recreational player who wants their money to last as long as possible.

## Conclusion

If you are playing to **Win Money** in the long run, **All American (8/8/8)** is the only choice. The 100.72% return is a gift from the gambling gods. However, if you are playing for **Entertainment** and want to maximize your time at the machine without too much risk, **Aces and Faces** is a fantastic, low-variance alternative.
