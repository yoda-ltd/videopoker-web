# The Payout Gap: Jacks or Better vs Double Bonus Explained

In the world of **best odds video poker**, players often find themselves choosing between two of the most popular machines on the floor: Jacks or Better and Double Bonus. While they look similar, the **EV Gap** between them depends entirely on their specific pay tables and your tolerance for **House Edge Variance**.

## The Best Odds Index: 9/6 Jacks vs 10/7 Double Bonus
In the **highest return video poker games** 2026 ranking, both machines offer strong professional returns when found in their "Full Pay" configurations:
*   **9/6 Jacks or Better:** 99.54% RTP.
*   **10/7 Double Bonus:** 100.17% RTP.

At first glance, Double Bonus appears to be the superior choice. However, Double Bonus offers a "Bonus" for hitting Four of a Kind (specifically Four Aces, which pays 160-for-1 instead of 25-for-1). This change in the pay table structure shifts the **Theoretical Return Convergence**.

## Analysis of "Short Pay" Predation
The real "Payout Gap" occurs when casinos offer sub-optimal versions of these games. 
*   **Jacks or Better (8/5):** RTP falls to 97.30%.
*   **Double Bonus (8/5):** RTP falls to 94.19%.

If you find a 9/6 Jacks machine next to an 8/5 Double Bonus machine, the 5.35% **EV Gap** is predatory. A professional player would never touch the 8/5 Double Bonus machine in this scenario. However, if the choice is between 8/5 Jacks (97.30%) and 10/7 Double Bonus (100.17%), the Double Bonus machine is actually the much better bet.

## House Edge Variance and Strategy
Double Bonus has higher variance than Jacks or Better. This is because more of the return is tied up in the Four of a Kind payouts. In Jacks or Better, the Two Pair payout (2-for-1) is the engine that keeps your bankroll alive. 
*   **Jacks or Better Strategy:** Focuses on bankroll preservation and hitting the Royal Flush.
*   **Double Bonus Strategy:** Slightly adjusts to account for the higher value of Four of a Kind, particularly Aces.
