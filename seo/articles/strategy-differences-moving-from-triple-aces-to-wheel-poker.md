# Strategy Differences: Moving from Triple Aces to Wheel Poker

Transitioning from Triple Aces to Wheel Poker is a unique step in the video poker world. Both games are niche variants that offer a "high-speed" or "bonus" experience. This guide provides the best video poker strategy comparison to help you understand how to play video poker like a pro during this transition.

## The Triple Aces Baseline

In Triple Aces, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is built on the fact that any pair of Aces or better is a winning hand. This creates a very stable, low-volatility experience. When moving to Wheel Poker, that "safety net" is removed. You now need at least a pair of Queens or Kings to get your money back.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from Triple Aces to Wheel Poker, you'll find that **80% of your strategy remains the same**. The **20% that changes** is entirely focused on the "Pair threshold." This 20% shift causes the most **Bankroll Drain** if not corrected.

### The 20% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Pair of Aces Devaluation:** In Triple Aces, a pair of Aces is a winning hand. In Wheel Poker, it's a "trash" hand that you should rarely hold unless you're drawing for something better (like a three-card Royal Flush).
2.  **Wheel Bonus Focus:** Wheel Poker is named "Wheel" because it rewards you specifically for getting a winning hand and then spinning a wheel for additional prizes. This makes four-of-a-kind and specific straights much more valuable than in Triple Aces.
3.  **Two Pair Strategy:** While Two Pair pays 2-for-1 in both games, the higher payout for spinning the wheel in Wheel Poker means you might break a Full House to draw for a fifth card more often than you would in Triple Aces.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

Wheel Poker introduces **Volatility Scaling**. Without the Aces-or-better push, your bankroll will experience more frequent dips. **EV Erosion** occurs when a Triple-Aces player instinctively holds a pair of Aces in Wheel Poker. This is a "dead hand" and holding it is a direct loss of expected value.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Queens or Kings or Better are your new minimum "win."
*   **Modify:** Discard any pair of Aces unless they are part of a better draw.
*   **Target:** Aggressively pursue the "Wheel" bonuses; they are the heart of the Wheel Poker pay table.

By recognizing that the "safety net" has moved, you can successfully transition to Wheel Poker without suffering from significant EV Erosion.
