# Triple Bonus Plus Pay Table Analysis: 9/6 vs 8/5 vs 7/5

Triple Bonus Plus is a favorite among high-volatility video poker enthusiasts. Unlike standard Jacks or Better, this variant heavily rewards specific four-of-a-kind (Quads) combinations, significantly altering the **Expected Value (EV)** and variance of the game. Understanding the nuances between the 9/6, 8/5, and 7/5 pay tables is crucial for any player looking to optimize their return.

## The Payout Structure: Why Quads Matter

In Triple Bonus Plus, the "Triple" refers to the massive bonuses awarded for specific Quads. While a standard game might offer 125 coins for any four-of-a-kind, Triple Bonus Plus breaks them down:

*   **Four Aces:** Typically pays 800 coins (on a 5-coin bet).
*   **Four 2s, 3s, or 4s:** Typically pays 400 coins.
*   **Four 5s through Kings:** Typically pays 250 coins.

This structure creates a high-variance environment. To fund these massive payouts, the game often reduces the payout for a Full House or Flush, which is where the 9/6 vs. 8/5 distinction becomes vital.

## Comparing the Pay Tables

The numbers "9/6" refer to the payouts for a Full House and a Flush, respectively, per coin wagered.

### Return to Player (RTP) Analysis

*   **9/6 Triple Bonus Plus:** The gold standard, offering an RTP of approximately **99.58%** with perfect strategy.
*   **8/5 Triple Bonus Plus:** A common "short-pay" table. The reduction in Full House and Flush payouts drops the RTP to roughly **97.67%**.
*   **7/5 Triple Bonus Plus:** Often found in high-traffic or tourist areas. This table is significantly worse, with an RTP plummeting to around **96.55%**.

## High Volatility Video Poker Strategy

Because of the weighted Quad payouts, your strategy must shift away from the "grind" of Jacks or Better. You are essentially hunting for Aces and low Quads.

1.  **Prioritize Aces:** In a 9/6 game, holding a single Ace is often more valuable than holding a low pair because the 800-coin payoff for Quad Aces is so dominant.
2.  **The Flush Trap:** On an 8/5 or 7/5 table, the value of chasing a Flush is diminished. You should be more willing to break a potential Flush to hold a high-value Quad kicker (like an Ace).
3.  **Variance Management:** Triple Bonus Plus is not for the faint of heart. The "swings" are much larger than in Double Bonus. You need a larger bankroll to weather the droughts between hitting the big bonus hands.
