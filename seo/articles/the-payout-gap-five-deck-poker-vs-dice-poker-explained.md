# The Payout Gap: Five-Deck Poker vs. Dice Poker Explained

The "Payout Gap" between Five-Deck Poker and Dice Poker represents the two most "Exotic" ways to "Re-imagine" video poker. One uses "Depth" (adding more cards to the deck), while the other uses "RNG Simulation" (using dice to represent cards). This **best video poker strategy comparison** examines the **Variance Scaling** and **Payout Compression** inherent in these two "Unconventional" formats.

## The Mathematical Foundation: Broadening vs. Simulating

**Five-Deck Poker** is a "Broadening" game. By simulating multiple decks, the game changes the fundamental probabilities of poker. Hands like "Five-of-a-Kind" become possible. To fund these new "Super" hands, the game uses heavy **Payout Compression** on "Natural" Flushes and Straights. The **video poker house edge analysis** is complex because you are no longer playing with a standard 52-card probability set.

**Dice Poker** is a "Simulating" game. It uses a dice-based RNG (Random Number Generator) to represent poker cards. Because the probability of a "Face" appearing on a die (1 in 6) is different than a "Card" appearing in a deck (1 in 13 or 1 in 52), the entire **video poker house edge analysis** must be rewritten. The **Payout Compression** is built into the dice themselves—some "Hands" are much harder to "Roll" than to "Draw."

## Variance Scaling and the EV Gap

1.  **Five-Deck Poker:** The variance is extremely high. You are playing for "Unicorn" hands. The **EV Gap** during standard play is massive. You can go through thousands of "Standard" hands without seeing the "Five-Deck" hands hit. This requires a massive bankroll.
2.  **Dice Poker:** The variance depends on the specific "Dice" implementation. If the game is designed to hit "Six-of-a-Kind" frequently, the **Variance Scaling** might actually be lower than standard poker, but the payouts will be correspondingly "Compressed."

## Strategy Bifurcation: Stacking vs. Rolling

The **Strategy Bifurcation** is a total departure from standard logic:
*   In **Five-Deck Poker**, your "Hold" logic must account for the possibility of duplicate cards. You are "Stacking" rather than "Ranking." 
*   In **Dice Poker**, you aren't playing "Poker" in the traditional sense. You are playing "Probability Sets." You hold "Faces" based on their frequency on the dice rather than their "Rank" in a deck.

For the serious advantage player, both of these games offer challenges. Five-Deck requires a new mathematical model. Dice Poker requires a deep understanding of "Dice Probability"—is the "Roll" worth the "Risk"? For most, these games are for "Entertainment" rather than "Advantage Play" due to the high house edge and complex strategies.
