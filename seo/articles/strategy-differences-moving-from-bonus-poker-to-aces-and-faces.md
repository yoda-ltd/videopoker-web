# Strategy Differences: Moving from Bonus Poker to Double Bonus Poker
Category: Comparison

The transition from **Bonus Poker** to **Double Bonus Poker** is a shift from a "Moderate Bonus" variant to a "High Bonus" variant. This represents a significant **Strategic Divergence** in how you value four-of-a-kind hands and the volatility that comes with them.

### The Comparison Matrix: Bonus Poker vs. Double Bonus Poker

| Feature | Bonus Poker (8/5) | Double Bonus Poker (10/7) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.17% | 100.17% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 20.9 | 28.5 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Aces/2s-4s/5s-Ks | Target Higher Bonus Payouts |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.83% | -0.17% (Player Edge) |

### Strategic Divergence: The Bonus Multiplier

In **Bonus Poker**, Four Aces pay 400 credits (for a 5-coin bet). In **Double Bonus Poker**, Four Aces pay 800 credits. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through high-tier quads. In Double Bonus Poker, the payouts for all four-of-a-kind hands are "doubled" compared to standard Jacks or Better, leading to a much higher **Volatility Normalization** (28.5 vs 19.5).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is high strategy overlap (approximately 80%) because both are 52-card natural games. However, in Double Bonus Poker, you will break a Full House to hold three Aces much more frequently than in Bonus Poker. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the high-tier hands in Double Bonus dominate the EV landscape.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Ace Supremacy:** In Double Bonus Poker, Aces are the highest-paying quads. You prioritize them over almost any other card rank.
2.  **Full House Break:** You will break a Full House containing three Aces to draw for the fourth Ace. This is a major departure from Bonus Poker.
3.  **Flush and Straight Focus:** In 10/7 Double Bonus, the payouts for Flushes and Straights are increased, which significantly changes your **EV Maximization** on connected cards.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that Double Bonus Poker offers a player advantage in its full-pay version, but the volatility is much higher. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are excellent choices for the disciplined player.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Bonus Poker to Double Double Bonus Poker
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Bonus Poker** to **Double Double Bonus Poker** is a shift from a "Standard Bonus" variant to a "Kicker Bonus" variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value specific card combinations and their related "Kickers."

### The Comparison Matrix: Bonus Poker vs. Double Double Bonus Poker

| Feature | Bonus Poker (8/5) | Double Double Bonus (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.17% | 98.98% |
| **Variance Index** | 20.9 | 42.0 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Aces/2s-4s/5s-Ks | Target Quads with Kickers |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.83% | 1.02% |

### Strategic Divergence: The Kicker Factor

In **Double Double Bonus Poker**, the payout for Four Aces, 2s, 3s, and 4s depends on the fifth card in the hand (the "Kicker"). For example, Four Aces with an Ace, 2, 3, or 4 kicker pays a massive 2,000 credits. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through specific high-tier combinations, leading to extreme **Volatility Normalization** (42.0 vs 20.9).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 70% overlap. In Bonus Poker, you would never hold a kicker with a pair. In Double Double Bonus, if you have three Aces and a low kicker (2, 3, 4), you will keep the kicker to draw for the massive 2,000-credit bonus. This is the essence of **Payout Compression** in kicker-based games.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Kicker Knowledge:** You must learn which cards are "Kickers" for each quad tier (Aces, 2s, 3s, 4s).
2.  **Ace-Kicker Priority:** Holding a pair of Aces with a low kicker is a valid strategy in Double Double Bonus, whereas in Bonus Poker, you'd only hold the Aces.
3.  **Variance Management:** Double Double Bonus has a very high variance (42.0). You need a larger bankroll to withstand the swings of "Kicker Hunting."

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that Double Double Bonus is the most popular game in many casinos due to its "Jackpot" potential. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that it offers a competitive return if you play the maximum bet and master the kicker strategy.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Bonus Poker to Triple Double Bonus Poker
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Bonus Poker** to **Triple Double Bonus Poker** is one of the most extreme shifts in volatility a player can make. You are moving from a "Moderate Bonus" game to a "Kicker-Heavy Jackpot" game. This is the ultimate test of **Strategic Divergence**.

### The Comparison Matrix: Bonus Poker vs. Triple Double Bonus Poker

| Feature | Bonus Poker (8/5) | Triple Double Bonus (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.17% | 99.58% |
| **Variance Index** | 20.9 | 98.0 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Standard Bonus Quads | Target Quads with Specific Kickers |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.83% | 0.42% |

### Volatility Normalization: The Kicker Jackpot

In **Triple Double Bonus Poker**, the payout for Four Aces with an Ace, 2, 3, or 4 kicker is 4,000 credits—the same as a Royal Flush! This creates massive **Payout Compression** across the rest of the table. In Bonus Poker, a "Full House" is a great win; in Triple Double Bonus, it is merely a way to survive until you hit the Kicker Jackpot.

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is minimal (less than 30%). In Bonus Poker, you would never discard a Full House. In Triple Double Bonus, you break a Full House if it contains three Aces and a low kicker to draw for the 4,000-credit bonus. This is the ultimate form of **EV Maximization** in high-variance kicker games.

### Critical Strategy Shifts
1.  **Ace-Kicker Obsession:** Every Ace and kicker combination is a keeper. Three Aces and a low kicker is a massive hold, even if you have a "Natural" winner.
2.  **Middle Quad Devaluation:** In Bonus Poker, Four 5s through Kings are valuable. In Triple Double Bonus, they are almost entirely ignored in favor of the Aces/2s-4s/Kickers.
3.  **Bankroll Requirement:** Because of the Variance Index of 98.0, you need a massive bankroll to withstand the swings of Triple Double Bonus. This is a core part of **Volatility Normalization**.

A **video poker house edge analysis** indicates that while Triple Double Bonus has a competitive RTP, it is emotionally taxing. The **best video poker strategy comparison** for this transition is: Find the Aces and the Kickers, or lose.
