# The Payout Gap: Deuces Wild vs Double Bonus Explained

In the world of **best odds video poker**, players often find themselves choosing between two of the most popular machines on the floor: Deuces Wild and Double Bonus. While they look similar, the **EV Gap** between them depends entirely on their specific pay tables and your tolerance for **House Edge Variance**.

## The Best Odds Index: Full Pay Deuces vs 10/7 Double Bonus
In the **highest return video poker games** 2026 ranking, both machines offer strong professional returns when found in their "Full Pay" configurations:
*   **Full Pay Deuces Wild:** 100.76% RTP.
*   **10/7 Double Bonus:** 100.17% RTP.

At first glance, Deuces Wild appears to be the superior choice. However, Deuces Wild offers a "Bonus" for hitting Four Deuces (specifically Four Deuces, which pays 200-for-1 instead of 160-for-1). This change in the pay table structure shifts the **Theoretical Return Convergence**.

## Analysis of "Short Pay" Predation
The real "Payout Gap" occurs when casinos offer sub-optimal versions of these games. 
*   **Airport Deuces:** RTP falls to 94.82%.
*   **Double Bonus (8/5):** RTP falls to 94.19%.

If you find an Airport Deuces machine next to an 8/5 Double Bonus machine, the 0.63% **EV Gap** is predatory. A professional player would never touch the 8/5 Double Bonus machine in this scenario. However, if the choice is between Airport Deuces (94.82%) and 10/7 Double Bonus (100.17%), the Double Bonus machine is actually the much better bet.

## House Edge Variance and Strategy
Deuces Wild has higher variance than Double Bonus. This is because more of the return is tied up in the Four Deuces and Natural Royal Flush payouts. In Double Bonus, the Straight payout (5-for-1) is the engine that keeps your bankroll alive. 
*   **Deuces Wild Strategy:** Slightly adjusts to account for the higher value of Four Deuces, particularly deuces.
*   **Double Bonus Strategy:** Focuses on bankroll preservation and hitting the Four of a Kind.
