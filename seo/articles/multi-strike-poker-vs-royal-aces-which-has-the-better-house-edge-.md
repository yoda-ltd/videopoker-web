# Multi-Strike Poker vs. Royal Aces: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Comparing **Multi-Strike Poker** and **Royal Aces** is a comparison of tiered persistence vs. rank specialization. While Multi-Strike rewards survival through four levels of increasing multipliers, Royal Aces rewards you for targeting the top of the deck (Aces). To choose the right game, you must understand how **video poker fairness and regulation** ensures the integrity of these specialized "Advantage-Play" models.

## The Mathematical Framework: Ladders vs. High Quads

The house edge in these games is determined by where the mathematical value is "Concentrated."

*   **Multi-Strike Poker:** Value is distributed across a tiered ladder (1x, 2x, 4x, 8x). The edge comes from the player's ability to maximize their "Ladder EV" by reaching Level 4 with high-frequency winning hands.
*   **Royal Aces:** Value is concentrated in the Four Aces payout. The edge comes from the player's ability to prioritize Aces and the RNG's ability to deliver them in a statistically random sequence.

## 1. Technical Standards for Randomness

Both variants must adhere to strict **Technical Standards** enforced by regulators like the **NGCB** or **UKGC**.

*   **Multi-Strike Poker:** The **Random Number Generator (RNG)** must provide independent shuffles for each level. Firms like **GLI** audit the code to ensure that being dealt an Ace on Level 1 doesn't "starve" the machine of Aces on Level 4.
*   **Royal Aces:** Regulators verify that every Ace has an exactly 1-in-52 chance of being dealt. The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures that the Aces aren't "weighted" to appear less frequently than other cards.
