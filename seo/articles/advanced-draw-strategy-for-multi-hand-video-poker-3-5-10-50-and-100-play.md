# Advanced Draw Strategy for Multi-Hand Video Poker: Mastering 3, 5, 10, 50, and 100 Play

In the realm of professional video poker, the move to multi-hand variants (3, 5, 10, 50, and 100-play) is the ultimate method for increasing volume and accelerating **Expected Return (ER)**. ## The Mechanics of the "Shared Base Hand" vs. "Independent Draw"

The most critical concept in any multi-hand variant is the distinction between the "Base Hand" (the deal) and the "Replacement Phase" (the draw). 

1.  **The Shared Base Hand:** You are dealt one initial five-card hand from a single virtual deck. This hand is replicated across all active lines (3 to 100).
2.  **The Lock-In:** The cards you choose to "Hold" are locked across all hands. This creates a mathematical correlation—your session's potential is "seeded" by this single deal.
3.  **Independent Draw Randomness:** For every individual hand, the remaining empty slots are filled from a *fresh, independent deck*. This is the **Smoothing Effect**.

This means that every single hand in a 100-play machine is an **Independent Trial**. If you hold 4 cards to a Royal Flush, you are running 100 independent 1/47 trials simultaneously.

## Best Video Poker Draw Strategy: Does Volume Change the Hold?

A common myth among amateur players is that you should "play tighter" in 100-play to protect your bankroll. **This is a mathematical error.**

Because each draw is an independent event with its own **Independent Draw Randomness**, the **Expected Return (ER)** of any specific hold is identical whether you are playing 1 hand or 1,000. 
- **The Golden Rule:** Always follow the strategy for the highest **Expected Value (EV)**. 
- **The "Smoothing Effect":** While the **Shared Base Hand** creates spikes, the 100 independent draws will "smooth out" the results for common draws (like holding a Low Pair). Over time, your actual results for these draws will converge very closely to the theoretical frequency (e.g., hitting a Set about 12% of the time).

## Combinatorial Convergence: The "Century" Advantage

**Combinatorial Convergence** is the reason professionals prefer the **best video poker for high volume**. By playing 100 hands at a time, you achieve statistical convergence 100 times faster than a single-line player.
- **Volume:** You can play 50,000 hands in a few hours.
- **Accuracy:** Because the results "smooth out" across the 100 draws, you can actually see your **ER** manifesting in real-time. This reduces the "perceived" variance of common draws, even as it increases the **Multi-Hand Volatility Scaling** of the base hand.

## Strategic Lock-Ins: The "Pocket" Advantage

One of the most significant events in professional play is being dealt a high-value hand or a strong draw "in the pocket" (on the deal).

### Chasing the Royal: The 4-to-a-Royal Scenario
When you hold 4 cards to a Royal Flush:
*   In **Single Play**, you have a 2.12% chance (1/47) of hitting the jackpot.
*   In **100-Play**, you have 100 independent 2.12% trials. 
*   **The Probability:** $1 - (46/47)^{100} \approx 88.5\%$. 
This 88.5% probability is why 100-play is the **best video poker for high volume** for those chasing the ultimate payout.
