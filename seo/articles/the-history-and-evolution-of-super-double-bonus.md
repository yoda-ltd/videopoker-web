# The History and Evolution of Super Double Bonus Video Poker

Super Double Bonus (SDB) is a heavyweight in the "Bonus" category of video poker. It represents a specific evolutionary step where game developers moved beyond simply boosting Aces and began creating high-value "pockets" of payouts across the entire deck.

## Beyond Double Bonus

To understand Super Double Bonus, one must first look at its predecessor: Double Bonus. Double Bonus made Aces, 2s, 3s, and 4s more valuable. Super Double Bonus took this concept and applied "Super" multipliers to specific sets of quads.

The game became a favorite in the late 1990s as a high-volatility alternative to Jacks or Better. It was designed for the player who found the standard game too "boring" but found "Deuces Wild" too confusing.

## Key Evolutionary Features

1.  **Tiered Quad Payouts:** SDB is famous for its distinct tiers. Four Aces pay the most, followed by Four Kings, Queens, or Jacks. This created a "High Card" focus that shifted the strategy away from low pairs.
2.  **Trade-off in common hands:** To fund the "Super" bonuses, the payout for "Two Pair" was often reduced to 1-for-1 (even money). This was a massive shift in the "Cycle" of the game, making the bankroll much more volatile.
3.  **Technological Shift:** SDB was one of the first games to truly benefit from the transition to color CRT screens, using bright, "Super" graphics to highlight the bonus quads.

## Video Poker Terms Every Player Should Know

When exploring the complete video poker glossary, keep these terms in mind for Super Double Bonus:

*   **Bankroll:** Because Two Pair only pays even money, you will lose credits much faster than in Jacks or Better. A "Large Bankroll" is mandatory for SDB.
*   **Variance:** This is a high-variance game. The "swings" are significant, and the game is won or lost on your ability to hit those "Super" quads.
*   **Expected Value (EV):** SDB strategy is highly specific. Because J, Q, and K quads are worth more than 2-10 quads, you must adjust your "Hold" decisions accordingly.

## The Strategy Evolution

The most significant change SDB brought to the world of video poker was the "Two Pair Dilemma." In most games, Two Pair is a reliable way to slowly grow your bankroll. In Super Double Bonus, Two Pair is barely a win. This forced players to evolve their strategy to be more "Aggressive," often discarding a potential Full House to chase a Four of a Kind.

## Super Double Bonus Today

Today, SDB remains one of the most popular "Bonus" variants on the market. It is featured on almost every multi-game machine in Las Vegas and is a staple of online casinos. Its history is a testament to the fact that players are willing to accept lower frequency wins in exchange for "Super" sized payouts.

For players looking to master the game, understanding the "Super Double Bonus" evolution is key to navigating the high-stakes, high-reward world of modern video poker.
