## What is High-Limit Multi-Strike Poker?

### Tiered Multipliers and "Ladder EV"
Multi-Strike is a 20-coin bet game where you play up to 4 levels of hands. A winning hand on Level 1 (1x multiplier) moves you to Level 2 (2x), then Level 3 (4x), and finally Level 4 (8x). 
- **The Similarities**: Like all high-limit games, Multi-Strike requires a large bankroll. A $1 denomination machine requires a $20 bet per game. 
- **The Differences**: In Multi-Strike, your goal is to reach the 8x multiplier on Level 4. The **Ladder EV** is much higher when you focus on "Survival Play" (prioritizing high-probability wins) at Level 1 and 2. A Royal Flush at Level 4 (8x multiplier) is a massive 32,000-coin jackpot ($32,000 on a $1 machine).

### The "Free Ride" and "Bonus Streak" Edge

A key differentiator for Multi-Strike is the "Free Ride" and "Bonus Streak" mechanics. These features ensure you advance to the next level regardless of the hand's outcome, drastically improving your **Hand-Over-Hand ROI**.
- **Free Ride Impact**: In Multi-Strike, a Free Ride is a "guaranteed win" for advancement purposes. There is no equivalent in standard video poker.
- **Bonus Streak Tips**: Many Multi-Strike machines feature "Bonus Streak" payouts for consecutive wins. Using **bonus streak video poker tips** to maintain these multipliers can lead to a much higher **Bonus Trigger Frequency** than you'll find at standard video poker machines.

## Strategy for High-Limit Multi-Strike

### "Survival Play" at Lower Levels
On Level 1 and Level 2, your focus must be on **Survival**. 
- **The Correct Play**: Prioritize high-probability winning hands at the bottom of the ladder to reach the 8x multiplier on Level 4.
- **The Pitfall**: Chasing a low-probability, high-payout hand on Level 1.

### Aggressive Strategy at Level 4
Once you reach Level 4, every payout is multiplied by 8. 
- **Strategy Shift**: At Level 4, your **multi strike video poker strategy** becomes aggressive. Since you can't advance any further, your only goal is to maximize the payout of the current hand.
