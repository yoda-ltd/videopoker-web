# Bankroll Management for Super Double Bonus Grinders: Strategizing the Extreme Quad Returns

Super Double Bonus (SDB) is a high-variance video poker variant that offers significantly enhanced payouts for Four-of-a-Kind hands, particularly for Aces and Face cards (J, Q, K). For the professional grinder, SDB offers a volatility profile that rivals Double Double Bonus. Mastering the **best video poker bankroll management** for Super Double Bonus requires a deep understanding of its extreme **Standard Deviation**.

## The Mathematics of Super Double Bonus: Variance Index

Super Double Bonus is a high-variance game. While Jacks or Better has a Variance Index of 19.5, Super Double Bonus typically sits around **40.0 to 45.0**.

### 1. The "Two Pair" Tax
In most versions of Super Double Bonus, Two Pair only pays **1-for-1**. This is the primary driver of the game's volatility. Without the 2-for-1 "safety net," your bankroll will experience faster "burn periods" while you wait for the high-paying quads.

### 2. Standard Deviation and Risk of Ruin (RoR)
The standard deviation for Super Double Bonus is roughly 6.50 to 7.00 per hand.
- To maintain a **Risk of Ruin** below 1%, a professional needs a bankroll of at least **8,000 to 12,000 units**.
- This is significantly higher than standard Bonus Poker. If you are playing $1 units ($5/bet), a **Life-Of-Bankroll** of $40,000 to $60,000 is the minimum for professional security.

## Extreme Quad Payouts: The SDB Advantage

In Super Double Bonus, the Four-of-a-Kind payouts are structured to reward high-rank cards:
- **Four Aces:** Usually 160-for-1.
- **Four Faces (J, Q, K):** Usually 120-for-1.
- **Other Quads:** Usually 50-for-1.

These "Super Quads" are the "lifeblood" of the grinder. A Four-of-a-Kind occurs roughly every 420 hands. However, hitting the "Aces" or "Faces" quads specifically takes much longer, which creates a "bumpy" bankroll curve.
