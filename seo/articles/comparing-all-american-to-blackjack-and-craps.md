# Comparing All American to Blackjack and Craps

When gamblers look for the best odds in a casino, the conversation usually centers on three games: **Blackjack**, **Craps**, and **Video Poker** (specifically All American). All three are "skill-influenced" games that offer some of the lowest house edges on the floor.

But which one is right for you? In this guide, we compare All American Video Poker to its table-game rivals to help you find your best mathematical edge.

## 1. House Edge Comparison

The house edge is the percentage of each bet that the casino expects to keep over the long run.

| Game | House Edge (Optimal Play) | Complexity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **All American (8/8/8)** | **-0.72% (Player Advantage)** | High |
| **Blackjack (3:2, S17)** | **~0.50%** | Medium |
| **Craps (Pass Line w/ Odds)** | **~0.40% to 1.41%** | Low/Medium |
| **All American (8/5)** | **~1.50%** | High |

**The Verdict:** If you can find a Full Pay (8/8/8) All American machine, it is **mathematically superior** to both Blackjack and Craps. It is one of the only games where the player has a theoretical advantage.

## 2. Skill vs. Luck

### All American
Video Poker is a game of pure mathematics. Every decision has a single "correct" answer based on probability. Unlike Blackjack, there is no "dealer" to worry about, and unlike Craps, there are no complex physics involved. However, the strategy is much more difficult to memorize than Blackjack's Basic Strategy.

### Blackjack
Blackjack is often considered the most "skillful" table game. By using Basic Strategy and potentially counting cards, players can reduce the house edge to nearly zero. However, "rules" like 6:5 payouts and continuous shufflers have made it harder to win in recent years.

### Craps
Craps is a game of "Smart Betting." The skill lies not in how you throw the dice (despite what some "dice controllers" claim), but in which bets you place. If you stick to the Pass Line and Max Odds, the house edge is incredibly low.

## 3. Variance and "The Swing"

### All American (High Variance)
All American is a "roller coaster." Because so much of the return is tied up in rare hands like the Straight Flush and Royal Flush, you will have many sessions where you lose. But when you win, you win big.

### Blackjack (Low/Medium Variance)
Blackjack is a steady game. You win about 42% of your hands, lose 48%, and push 10%. Your bankroll will fluctuate, but rarely in the massive "bursts" seen in Video Poker.

### Craps (Low/High Variance)
Craps variance depends on your betting style. If you only play the Pass Line, it's steady. If you start betting on "Yo-leven" or "Snake Eyes" (proposition bets), the variance (and the house edge) skyrockets.

## 4. The "Social" Factor

*   **All American:** Solo experience. It’s just you and the machine. Great for players who want to focus, listen to music, and avoid the "pressure" of a table.
*   **Blackjack:** Semi-social. You are playing with others against the dealer. There is often pressure from other players if you make a "wrong" move (even though it doesn't actually affect their odds).
*   **Craps:** The most social game in the casino. When the table is "hot," everyone is winning together. It’s loud, fast, and high-energy.

## 5. Rewards and Comps

*   **All American:** Machines track your play perfectly. You earn points for every dollar cycled. This makes it easy to earn "Cash Back" and "Free Play."
*   **Blackjack/Craps:** Players are "rated" by a pit boss who estimates your average bet. This is often less accurate and can lead to fewer comps for low-stakes players compared to Video Poker.

## Final Recommendation

*   **Choose All American if:** You want the absolute best mathematical return and enjoy solitary, high-speed, strategic play.
*   **Choose Blackjack if:** You enjoy the battle against the dealer and prefer a steady, lower-variance session.
*   **Choose Craps if:** You want the "casino experience"—noise, crowds, and the thrill of the dice—while still keeping the house edge low.

**Pro Tip:** If you see an All American machine with an 8/8/8 pay table, stop whatever you're doing and play it. That is a better "job" than playing Blackjack or Craps!
