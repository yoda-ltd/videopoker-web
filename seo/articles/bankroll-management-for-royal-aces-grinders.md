# Bankroll Management for Royal Aces Grinders

Royal Aces is a variant that significantly increases the payout for a Royal Flush, often at the expense of the smaller hands. This "top-heavy" structure makes it a high-variance game where **Bankroll Management** is the difference between a big win and a quick exit.

### The High Cost of the Royal
In Royal Aces, the **Theoretical Return** is heavily dependent on hitting that massive top payout. This means your daily results will often be negative unless you hit the Royal. To achieve **Session Sustainability**, you need a much larger bankroll than you would for Jacks or Better. We suggest a **Variance Buffering** of at least 2,000 units for a serious session.

### Is Max Bet Always Better?
A common niche question among players is: "Is max bet always better for bankroll longevity?" In Royal Aces, the answer is a resounding yes. The entire point of the game is the boosted Royal Flush payout, which is almost exclusively tied to the 5th coin. If you aren't playing max bet, you are playing a game with a significantly lower RTP and no upside.

### Hit Frequency Optimization
Because you are chasing a massive payout, your **Hit Frequency Optimization** strategy will be more aggressive. You will be holding cards that have even a remote chance of becoming a Royal Flush more often than in other games. For those looking for **video poker tips for beginners**, this can be a difficult transition to make, as it feels "wrong" to discard winning pairs to chase a long shot.

### Professional Daily Play
To learn **how to win video poker daily** on a Royal Aces machine, you must accept that "winning daily" actually means "playing perfectly every day until you hit the big one." It is a game of patience and deep pockets.

### RNG Fairness
As with all certified machines, Royal Aces uses **Pseudo-Randomness** to ensure every card is dealt fairly. The machine doesn't "know" it's paying out a bonus Royal; it just follows the math. Don't fall for the trap of thinking a machine is "hot" because someone else just hit a Royal—every hand is independent.
