## Multipliers vs. Bingo Patterns
The core difference lies in the distribution of the "Bonus" value.

- **Hot Roll Poker (The Volatility King):** In Hot Roll, the dice roll applies to your *current* win. It's a "Vertical" boost to your existing hand value.
- **Bingo Poker (The Pattern Collector):** In Bingo Poker, you play a standard game of video poker, but every winning hand (Pair, Flush, Full House) corresponds to a number on a 5x5 Bingo board on the screen. Filling a row, column, or diagonal on that board triggers a "Bingo Jackpot."

## Key Strategic Adjustments

### 1. From "Pure Card EV" to "Board-Aware Strategy"
- **In Hot Roll Poker:** The dice roll is random and doesn't change your card strategy. You use standard "Pure EV" (Jacks or Better or DDB) to maximize the base hand, which the dice then multiply.
- **In Bingo Poker:** You must constantly monitor the Bingo board. If you have a choice between holding a "Standard EV" pair and holding a single card that corresponds to a needed "Bingo Number," the board may override standard play. 
- **The Shift:** You are moving from a single-layer strategy to a dual-layer strategy. In 2026, the **Bingo Poker Logic** requires you to calculate the "Hidden EV" of the bingo card before every discard.

### 2. Variance Normalization and Bankroll Management
- **Hot Roll Poker Shift:** Hot Roll is extremely high-variance. You are betting 10 coins per hand, and the multipliers are "All or Nothing." Your bankroll will experience massive, violent swings.
- **Bingo Poker Shift:** Bingo Poker has a "Slower Burn." While you are still betting a side-bet to activate the board, the rewards are more "Accumulated." You are slowly filling the board over dozens of hands. 
- **The Adjustment:** Bingo Poker requires more patience. You won't see the "12x Jackpot" of Hot Roll, but you will see the steady progression of the bingo board. Your bankroll management should be focused on "Time on Device" (TOD) to ensure you complete the patterns.

### 3. Feature Hit Frequency
- **Hot Roll Poker:** High frequency (dice roll every ~6 hands), but low win-correlation.
- **Bingo Poker:** Low frequency (completing a bingo pattern happens every ~150-200 hands), but high win-certainty.
- **The Shift:** In Hot Roll, the "Feature" is the roll. In Bingo Poker, the "Feature" is the payout. Moving to Bingo Poker requires a shift from "Instant Gratification" to "Long-Term Planning."
