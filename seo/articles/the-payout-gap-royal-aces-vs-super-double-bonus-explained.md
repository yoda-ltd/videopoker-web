# The Payout Gap: Royal Aces vs. Super Double Bonus Explained

The "Payout Gap" between Royal Aces and Super Double Bonus (SDB) illustrates a fascinating trend in modern video poker: the "Super-Charging" of specific card combinations. This **best video poker strategy comparison** looks at how **Payout Compression** is applied to different tiers of "Bonus" hands to create unique **Variance Scaling** profiles.

## The Bonus Structure: Aces vs. Royals

In **Super Double Bonus**, the "Bonus" is nested. 4 Aces pay more than 4 Kings/Queens/Jacks, which pay more than 4 Threes/Fours/Fives. It is a balanced "Quad" game that rewards you for hitting any set of four-of-a-kind.

**Royal Aces**, as the name suggests, puts all its "Eggs" in one basket: the Aces. While it typically pays a premium for 4 Aces, it often reduces the payouts for other Quads or even "Full House" hands to compensate. This creates a massive **EV Gap** when you aren't hitting Aces. If you hit 4 Kings in Royal Aces, you might feel "Short-changed" compared to a session on SDB.

## Variance Scaling and Bankroll Management

1.  **Super Double Bonus:** The variance is relatively high (around 45). It is a "Stair-step" game. You can survive on the lower Quad payouts while you wait for the "Big" Aces. 
2.  **Royal Aces:** This is a "Binary" game. Either you hit the Aces and win, or you don't and lose. The **Variance Scaling** is much steeper because the game doesn't provide the same "Safety Net" on non-Ace Quads. Your **video poker house edge analysis** will show that Royal Aces is a much more "Violent" game for your bankroll.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Ace" Obsession

The **Strategy Bifurcation** is most evident in the "Hold" rules:
*   In **Royal Aces**, you will hold a single Ace with much higher priority than in SDB. You might even break a "Winning" Two-Pair to keep a lone Ace if the pay table is aggressive enough. 
*   In **Super Double Bonus**, you play a more "Balanced" game. You respect the Ace, but you don't "Chasing" it to the point of destroying your mid-tier EV. You still value high pairs and "Royal Flush" draws as your primary goals.

If you are a "Specialist" who loves the thrill of the "Ace" hit, Royal Aces is your game. If you prefer a more "Democratic" distribution of Quad bonuses, Super Double Bonus is the mathematically superior choice for long-term play.
