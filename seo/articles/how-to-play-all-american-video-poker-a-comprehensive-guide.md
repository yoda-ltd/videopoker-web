# How to Play All American Video Poker: A Comprehensive Guide

All American Video Poker is a high-octane variant that rewards bold play and a deep understanding of card probabilities. Unlike standard Jacks or Better, All American features a restructured pay table that emphasizes Straights and Flushes, creating a unique **Volatility Index** that appeals to seasoned gamblers. In this guide, we’ll break down the mechanics, the math, and how to find the **best odds for All American**.

## The Evolution of All American

All American was developed as a specialized variant of Jacks or Better. The goal was to create a game that felt more like a "chase" for medium-tier hands. Historically, it appeared on specialized machines in Atlantic City and Las Vegas before finding a permanent home in the digital libraries of online casinos. It is often favored by players who find the standard "Jacks or Better" grind too predictable.

## Pay Table Verification: 9/6 vs. 8/5 All American

In All American, **Pay Table Verification** is unique. Because the payouts for Straights, Flushes, and Full Houses are often identical (usually 8-for-1 or 9-for-1), the "9/6" vs "8/5" terminology refers to the relative strength of the entire table.

*   **Full Pay All American:** Pays 8-for-1 (or sometimes 9) for Flushes, Straights, AND Full Houses. With perfect play, the RTP can exceed 100.7% on certain older variations, though modern online versions typically sit around 99.38%.
*   **Low Pay All American:** Payouts for the Straight or Flush are reduced to 5 or 6, which drastically creates a negative **EV Differential**.

## All American vs. Jacks or Better: Comparative Analysis

The fundamental difference lies in the payout structure:
1.  **Straights and Flushes:** In Jacks or Better, a Flush pays more than a Straight. In All American, they often pay the same high amount.
2.  **Two Pair:** In All American, Two Pair usually only pays 1-for-1 (the same as a Pair of Jacks), whereas in Jacks or Better, it pays 2-for-1.
3.  **Volatility:** Because Two Pair only returns your bet, All American has a much higher **Volatility Index**. You will see your bankroll dip more frequently, but the high payouts for Straights and Flushes offer massive recovery potential.
