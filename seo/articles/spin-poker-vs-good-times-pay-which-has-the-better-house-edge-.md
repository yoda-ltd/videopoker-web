# Spin Poker vs Good Times Pay: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Two of the most popular video poker variants are Spin Poker and Good Times Pay. Both offer exciting multi-hand formats and unique "game-within-a-game" mechanics. Let's compare them to see which one provides the better **Slot-Hybrid EV** and which one might be better for your style of play.

## Spin Poker: The Multi-Payline Multi-Hand Variant

Spin Poker is the hybrid between video poker and a slot machine. It uses a 9-payline structure on a 3x5 grid.

1.  **Payline Mechanics:** You play one "hold" decision that propagates across 9 active lines. This creates a high level of **Multi-Hand Draw Variance**.
2.  **Increased Payouts:** If you hit a big hand on the "center" row, it's very common to hit multiple winners across the other lines, leading to huge total wins.
3.  **Analysis:** The house edge in Spin Poker is identical to the base game (e.g., Jacks or Better). If you play on a 9/6 machine, the house edge is only ~0.46%. It's a high-volatility game with a very low edge.

## Good Times Pay: The Bonus Feature Mechanic

Good Times Pay is a unique video poker variant where, on certain deals, the machine will apply a multiplier to your next hand.

1.  **The Good Times Pay Cost:** To enable the Good Times Pay feature, you must bet 10 coins per hand (compared to the standard 5 coins). This extra 5 coins is the "price" you pay for the feature.
2.  **Increased Multipliers:** When the Good Times Pay is active, the machine will randomly apply a multiplier to your next hand, which can significantly boost your score.
3.  **Analysis:** The **Slot-Hybrid EV** of Good Times Pay is actually quite favorable if played correctly. The house edge can be as low as 0.25% to 0.5%, making it one of the best video poker variants in the casino.

## Comparing the Two: Which Should You Play?

*   **For Lowest House Edge:** Good Times Pay usually wins. The mechanic of having a multiplier applied to your next hand is incredibly powerful and, when accounted for with the 10-coin bet, it often results in a higher overall return than Spin Poker.
*   **For More Variety:** Spin Poker is the better choice. It offers more games and more "bonus" features that keep the gameplay fresh.
*   **For Strategy Difficulty:** Both require a specific approach. You should consult a **super star poker strategy guide** or specialized Good Times Pay trainer to master the "advance" vs. "hold" decisions. Spin Poker strategy is identical to the base game.

If you're a pure "math" player looking for the lowest possible house edge, Good Times Pay is the clear winner. However, if you're looking for an exciting game with more variety and don't mind a slightly higher house edge, Spin Poker is the game for you.
