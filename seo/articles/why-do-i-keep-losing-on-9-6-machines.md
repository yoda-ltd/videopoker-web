# Why Do I Keep Losing on 9/6 Machines?

It's the ultimate frustration for the informed player: you found a 9/6 Jacks or Better machine (99.54% RTP), you're using a strategy card, but you're still losing. "Why do I keep losing on 9/6 machines?" The answer lies in the intersection of **Variance** and **Sample Size**.

### Variance Buffering
9/6 Jacks or Better is a "low variance" game, but "low" is relative. You can easily go 500 hands without a Four-of-a-Kind, and 40,000 hands without a Royal Flush. If you don't have enough **Variance Buffering** (i.e., a large enough bankroll), you will "bust out" before the math has a chance to even out.

### The Myth of Rigging: Pseudo-Randomness vs. True Randomness
Many players believe that if they lose for three hours straight, the machine must be "rigged" or "due" for a win. This stems from a misunderstanding of **Pseudo-Randomness vs. True Randomness**. 
- Modern machines use a Random Number Generator (RNG). 
- While technically "Pseudo-Random" (generated by an algorithm), for the purposes of gambling, it is effectively **True Randomness**. 
- The RNG does not know if you just won or lost; every hand is an independent event.

### Common Errors That Sink RTP
Even if the machine is 9/6, your personal return will drop if you make small errors. 
- **The "High Card" Trap**: Holding a Jack and a Queen when the strategy dictates holding just the Jack (in certain variants).
- **The "Flush Draw" vs. "Small Pair"**: In Jacks or Better, you always hold the small pair over a 4-card flush. Ignoring this reduces your **Hit Frequency Optimization**.

### How to Fix Your Game
If you're asking "how to win video poker daily," the first step is to stop blaming the machine and start auditing your play. Use a video poker trainer app to see if your "perfect strategy" is actually perfect. Most players who think they play perfectly are actually making 1-2 errors per hour, which is enough to turn a 99.5% game into a 98% game.

Remember: 9/6 machines are the gold standard, but they are not a "get rich quick" scheme. They are a "lose slowly until you hit big" scheme.
