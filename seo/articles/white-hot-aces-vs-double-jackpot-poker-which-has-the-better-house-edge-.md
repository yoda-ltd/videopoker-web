# White Hot Aces vs. Double Jackpot Poker: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Comparing **White Hot Aces** and **Double Jackpot Poker** is a study in rank-specific specialization. While White Hot Aces concentrates its value in hitting Four Aces (usually 240-for-1), Double Jackpot Poker rewards you for high-ranking quads across several faces (Aces, face cards) and often includes a bonus for holding a valid kicker. To choose the right game, you must understand how **video poker fairness and regulation** ensures the integrity of these specialized "Jackpot-Centric" models.

## The Mathematical Framework: Aces vs. Multiple Faces

The house edge in these games is determined by how the "Big Wins" are distributed.

*   **White Hot Aces:** Value is heavily weighted toward hitting Four Aces. The 1,200-coin payout (at max bet) is the primary driver of the RTP. The edge is tied to the player's ability to prioritize Aces above all else.
*   **Double Jackpot Poker:** Value is distributed more broadly across Aces, Kings, Queens, and Jacks. You receive high payouts for any of these high-value quads, often with a "Double" bonus for a kicker.

In both games, you are paying a "tax" on common hands to fund the big hits.
- In both variants, Two Pair almost always pays 1-for-1. This funds the massive bonuses for Aces and Faces.
- In White Hot Aces, the base pay table for non-Ace quads is often lower to fund the 240-to-1 Ace hit.
