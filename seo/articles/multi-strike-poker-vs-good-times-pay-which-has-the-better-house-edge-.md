# Multi-Strike Poker vs. Good Times Pay: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Comparing **Multi-Strike Poker** and **Good Times Pay** is a comparison of tiered ladders vs. random multipliers. While Multi-Strike rewards survival and advancement through four levels of increasing payouts, Good Times Pay relies on random multipliers assigned to winning hands. To choose the right game, you must understand how **video poker fairness and regulation** ensures the integrity of these specialized "Multiplier-Heavy" models.

## The Mathematical Framework: Ladders vs. Randomness

The house edge in these games is determined by how the "Multiplied Value" is awarded.

*   **Multi-Strike Poker:** You must "Strike" (win) to move to the next level. Payouts are 1x, 2x, 4x, and 8x. The edge comes from the player's ability to maximize their "Ladder EV" through survival-based strategy on lower levels.
*   **Good Times Pay:** A multiplier (up to 10x) is randomly applied to any winning hand. The player has no control over the trigger, making it a high-excitement, variance-driven variant.

## 2. Payout Analysis: The "Ladder" Tax

In these games, you are paying a premium for the "Action."
- In Multi-Strike, you bet 20 coins (5 for each of the 4 levels). This wager funds the 8x multiplier on Level 4.
- In Good Times Pay, the base pay table for common wins is lower to fund the average value of the random multipliers.
