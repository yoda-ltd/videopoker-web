# Bonus Poker Deluxe Pay Table Analysis: 9/6 vs. 8/5 vs. 7/5

Bonus Poker Deluxe is the "simplest" of the high-paying quad games. It offers a single, large bonus for any Four of a Kind (80-for-1). However, this simplicity can be a trap. Because the "Bonus" is so visible and attractive, players often fail to perform a proper **Pay Table Verification** on the Full House and Flush payouts. In this analysis, we'll show you why the "middle" of the table matters more than the "bonus" at the top.

## The 80-for-1 Allure

In Bonus Poker Deluxe, all four-of-a-kind hands pay 80 coins (for a 1-coin bet). This is a massive jump from the 25 coins paid in Jacks or Better. However, as any **Bonus Poker Deluxe video poker trainer** will show you, quads only account for a small percentage of your total hands. The bulk of your return comes from Full Houses, Flushes, and Straights.

## Comparing the Tables

### 1. The "Full Pay" 9/6 Bonus Poker Deluxe
*   **Full House:** 9
*   **Flush:** 6
*   **Theoretical RTP:** 99.64%
*   **Volatility Index:** High

This is one of the best games in the casino. A 99.64% return is world-class, but remember: the **Volatility Index** is much higher than Jacks or Better because Two Pair only pays 1-for-1. You need a significant bankroll to play this game properly.

### 2. The "Short Pay" 8/6 Bonus Poker Deluxe
*   **Full House:** 8
*   **Flush:** 6
*   **Theoretical RTP:** 98.49%

Reducing the Full House by one credit drops the return by 1.15%. This is a significant **EV Differential**. For a high-volume player, this could represent thousands of dollars in lost expected value over a year.

### 3. The "Low Pay" 8/5 Bonus Poker Deluxe
*   **Full House:** 8
*   **Flush:** 5
*   **Theoretical RTP:** 97.40%

This is where the game starts to become "dangerous" for your bankroll. Combined with the high volatility (Two Pair paying only 1-for-1), an 8/5 machine will drain your funds very quickly during "dry" spells where quads don't appear.

### 4. The "Greedy" 7/5 Bonus Poker Deluxe
*   **Full House:** 7
*   **Flush:** 5
*   **Theoretical RTP:** 96.25%

At 96.25%, you are better off playing almost any other video poker variant. The "Bonus" of 80-for-1 is not enough to compensate for these poor middle payouts.

## Finding the Best Odds for Bonus Poker Deluxe

To find the **best odds for Bonus Poker Deluxe**, you must be disciplined.
1.  **Look for 9/6:** If you don't see 9 for a Full House and 6 for a Flush, keep walking.
2.  **Verify the Quad:** Ensure it pays 80-for-1. Some "Deluxe" clones only pay 60 or 70.
3.  **Check Two Pair:** Ensure it pays at least 1-for-1 (Even Money).

## The Role of the Video Poker Trainer

If you are unsure about a machine, use a **Bonus Poker Deluxe video poker trainer** on your phone to check the RTP of the specific numbers you see on the screen. The trainer will also help you adjust your strategy; on a lower-pay machine, you might actually play *more* aggressively for quads because the "safe" hands (Full Houses) are worth less.

## Conclusion

Bonus Poker Deluxe is a fantastic game for those who love the thrill of the "Quad." But don't let the 80-for-1 payout blind you to the math. Always perform your **Pay Table Verification** and stick to 9/6 machines to ensure you are getting the **best odds for Bonus Poker Deluxe**.
