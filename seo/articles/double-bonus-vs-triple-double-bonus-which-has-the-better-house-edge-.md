# Double Bonus vs. Triple Double Bonus: Strategy Overlap and Hand-Ranking Priorities

In the professional video poker landscape, the transition from **Double Bonus (DB)** to **Triple Double Bonus (TDB)** represents the ultimate test of **Optimal Hold Variance**. While both games are "Bonus" variants that pay extra for specific 4-of-a-kind hands, the mathematical profile and the **Hand-Ranking Inversion** between them are stark. Understanding these differences is crucial for anyone looking for the **best video poker strategy comparison** and learning **how to play video poker optimally**.

## The Core Conflict: 4-of-a-Kind vs. Full House/Flush/Two Pair

The primary driver of the strategy shift between Double Bonus and Triple Double Bonus is the "Triple Kicker" system. In Double Bonus, all 4 Aces pay 160-for-1. In Triple Double Bonus, 4 Aces with a 2, 3, or 4 kicker pay an astronomical 800-for-1 (the same as a Royal Flush). To fund these massive payouts, the casino must take from the mid-level hands.

| Hand | Double Bonus (9/6) | Triple Double Bonus (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Two Pair** | 1-for-1 | 1-for-1 |
| **Flush** | 6-for-1 | 6-for-1 |
| **Full House** | 9-for-1 | 9-for-1 |
| **Straight** | 5-for-1 | 4-for-1 |
| **4 Aces w/ 2,3,4 Kicker** | 160-for-1 | 800-for-1 |

The reduction of the Straight payout from 5-for-1 to 4-for-1 and the "Triple Kicker" potential are the single most significant **Strategy Convergence** breakers in video poker history.

## 1. Strategy Overlap: What Stays the Same?

Even with the massive difference in volatility, you can reuse about 40% of your strategy for Triple Double Bonus if you're coming from a Double Bonus background.

*   **Three of a Kind:** In both games, Three of a Kind is a premium hold. You are always drawing for the 4th card, and in TDB, you are also hoping for the right kicker.
*   **Royal Flush Draws:** The priority of a 4-card Royal Flush remains at the top of the list in both games.
*   **Flush Priority:** Both games (in 9/6 versions) value the Flush at 6-for-1, meaning 4-card flush draws are significant.

## 2. Hand-Ranking Inversion: The Straight Draw Pivot

The most significant **Hand-Ranking Inversion** occurs at the Straight level.

*   **Double Bonus:** The Straight pays 5-for-1. This is a "win" that sustains your bankroll. Because of this, you hold "Inside Straight" draws with 3 High Cards (J, Q, K) much more frequently.
*   **Triple Double Bonus:** The Straight only pays 4-for-1 (even money). This means it is no longer a "win," but a "push." Because of this, the math often dictates breaking a straight draw to hold a lone Ace or a pair of Jacks. This is the definition of **Optimal Hold Variance**.

## 3. Penalty Card Impact: The Triple Kicker Hunt

Professional players focus on **Penalty Card Impact** when hunting for those 800-for-1 payouts.

*   **In Double Bonus:** You don't care about the 5th card (the kicker) when you have 4-of-a-kind. It doesn't change the payout.
*   **In Triple Double Bonus:** The 5th card is everything. If you have Three Aces, you are not just looking for the 4th Ace; you are also looking for a 2, 3, or 4 to turn an 800-unit win into a 4,000-unit win (at max coins). This creates a **Penalty Card Impact** where you might discard a card that could have completed a minor win to keep your "kicker slots" open.

## 4. Optimal Hold Variance: The Quest for Quads

The volatility of Triple Double Bonus is the highest in the video poker world (outside of some niche progressive games).

*   **Double Bonus Volatility:** ~28.3 (Medium). Your bankroll will last longer, and you'll have more frequent "small wins."
*   **TDB Volatility:** ~98.0 (Extreme). You will experience long "dry spells" where your bankroll depletes rapidly, punctuated by massive, life-changing wins (comparable to hitting multiple Royal Flushes).

## Conclusion: How to Transition

When moving from Double Bonus to Triple Double Bonus, the **best video poker strategy comparison** shows that you must become "quad-centric." 

1.  **Devalue the Straight:** If the machine only pays 4 for a Straight, don't chase inside draws. Focus on the higher-frequency, higher-payout quad potential.
2.  **Hunt the Triple Kicker:** Understand that in TDB, the 2, 3, and 4 are special cards. They are your "boosters" for the Aces and low quads.
3.  **Bankroll Management:** You need a massive bankroll for TDB (at least 4-6x what you need for Double Bonus) to survive the volatility of the 4-for-1 Straight payout and the kicker-dependent quads.

By mastering these **Hand-Ranking Priorities**, you can navigate the shift from stable Double Bonus to the extreme, high-octane world of Triple Double Bonus and learn **how to play video poker optimally**.
