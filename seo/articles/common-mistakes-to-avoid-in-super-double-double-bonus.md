# Common Mistakes to Avoid in Super Double Double Bonus Video Poker

Super Double Double Bonus (SDDB) is a volatile variation of Bonus Poker that focuses on high payouts for quads involving Aces and specific "kickers" (2, 3, or 4). Mastering the **Super Double Double Bonus strategy chart 2026** is essential for **EV Maximization** in this high-variance environment. To learn **how to play video poker like a pro**, you must first eliminate these three common "Pro-Level" errors that are unique to kicker-based games.

## Analysis: 3 Most Common "Pro-Level" Errors in Super Double Double Bonus

### 1. Misvaluing the Kicker with a Pair
The most significant difference in SDDB is that a 4-of-a-kind involving Aces with a 2, 3, or 4 kicker pays a massive bonus.

**The Error:** Holding a kicker along with a pair of Aces (e.g., `A-A-2`).
**The Pro Reality:** In Super Double Double Bonus, **Strategy Fine-Tuning** in 2026 dictates that you should *never* keep a kicker unless you already have the 4-of-a-kind. Holding the kicker actually *reduces* your probability of drawing the 3rd or 4th matching card. The **Expected Return Differential** strongly favors drawing for the quads first.

### 2. Overvaluing the Straight vs. 3 to a Royal
Because Super Double Double Bonus is a bonus poker variant, the payout for a Straight is often lower than in standard Jacks or Better.

**The Error:** Keeping a "made" Straight (e.g., `8-9-10-J-Q`) instead of drawing to 3 to a Royal Flush.
**The Pro Reality:** In SDDB, the **Expected Return Differential** between a small "made" hand like a straight and a three-card Royal Flush draw can be significant. If the Straight payout is only 4-to-1, the long-term EV of the Royal Flush draw is often higher. Pro players calculate this "Strategy Fine-Tuning" based on the specific pay table of the machine.

### 3. Ignoring the 3 Aces Hand-Ranking Priority
In SDDB, three Aces are incredibly valuable because of the potential for the massive quad Aces with a kicker.

**The Error:** Breaking three Aces to draw for a Royal Flush (e.g., `A-A-A-K-Q` of Hearts).
**The Pro Reality:** In Super Double Double Bonus, three Aces are a "stop" hand. You never break three Aces for anything other than a guaranteed Royal Flush. The **Rank-Based Priority** in 2026 dictates that you should prioritize the Aces above almost everything else.

If you want to play like a pro, you must be comfortable prioritizing these specific ranks over the standard "Jacks or Better" logic.
