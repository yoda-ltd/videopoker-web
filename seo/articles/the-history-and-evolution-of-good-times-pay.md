# The History and Evolution of Good Times Pay Video Poker

Good Times Pay is a fascinating "feature-driven" variant of video poker that represents the era of "Multiplier Mania." Its history is a testament to the creativity of developers who sought to make the "boring" parts of the game—the non-winning hands—more exciting.

## The Multiplier Revolution

In the early 2000s, video poker was facing stiff competition from the new generation of "bonus-rich" video slots (like *Wheel of Fortune*). To stay relevant, developers introduced "Bonus Bets." Good Times Pay was one of the first successful "Bonus Bet" games.

By betting a 6th coin (or 10 coins in some versions), players activated the "Good Times Pay" feature. This feature randomly awarded multipliers on winning hands, turning a standard 125-credit Flush into a "Good Times" 625-credit win.

## Key Evolutionary Milestones

1.  **The "Bonus Bet" Mechanic:** This game helped normalize the idea of betting more than 5 coins per hand, a concept now found in most high-end video poker machines.
2.  **Visual Excitement:** Good Times Pay machines were famous for their bright, 70s-style "Retro" graphics and upbeat music, signaling a "Good Time" whenever the multiplier feature was active.
3.  **Randomized Rewards:** Unlike games where the payout is fixed on the pay table, Good Times Pay introduced "Stochastic" (random) multipliers, adding a layer of slot-machine style "luck" to the skill-based poker game.

## Video Poker Terms Every Player Should Know

When playing Good Times Pay, these terms from the complete video poker glossary are essential:

*   **Action:** The bonus bet significantly increases your "Action" per hour, which can lead to more casino comps (rewards).
*   **Variance:** Because the multipliers are random, the variance is high. You might hit a multiplier on a "Jacks or Better" hand (small win) or a "Four of a Kind" (massive win).
*   **Expected Value (EV):** The "Bonus Bet" usually increases the overall EV of the machine, but only if you play with perfect strategy. The "cost" of the 6th coin is offset by the potential for 5x or 10x multipliers.

## Strategy Divergence

The evolution of Good Times Pay changed how players looked at the deck. While the basic "Hold" strategy remained largely the same as Jacks or Better or Bonus Poker, the *feeling* of the game changed. Players weren't just "playing for quads" anymore—they were "playing for the multiplier."

## Legacy of "Good Times"

Good Times Pay paved the way for modern blockbusters like "Super Times Pay" and "Double Super Times Pay." It proved that video poker players enjoyed a "surprise" element in their game.

Today, while the original standalone "Good Times Pay" cabinets are becoming rare, the multiplier mechanic it pioneered is a standard feature on most multi-game platforms. It remains a reminder that sometimes, the best way to win is to have a "Good Time."
