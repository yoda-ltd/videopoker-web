# Double Bonus Video Poker: Odds of Hitting a Royal Flush and Volatility Analysis

Double Bonus Poker is a fan favorite among high-stakes players because it offers some of the **highest return video poker games** available in the industry. However, its mathematical structure is built on a foundation of "increased payouts" for certain Four of a Kind hands, which fundamentally changes the **video poker odds and probability** landscape compared to Jacks or Better.

## The Mathematical Engine of Double Bonus

In Double Bonus Poker (specifically the "Full Pay" 10/7 version), the game returns a theoretical **100.17%** with perfect strategy. This puts the player in a mathematically advantageous position over the house.

### Royal Flush Probability and Combinatorial Analysis
The probability of hitting a Royal Flush in Double Bonus Poker is approximately **1 in 48,048 hands**. 
- **The "Cycle":** The Royal Flush cycle in Double Bonus is slightly longer than in Jacks or Better (~40,000 hands) because the optimal strategy sometimes requires breaking a winning hand to chase a high-value Four of a Kind.
- **EV Calculation:** In Double Bonus, the Royal Flush accounts for approximately **1.66%** of the total RTP. This means that if you never hit a Royal, you still have a strong 98.51% return, provided you hit your Bonus Quads correctly.

## High Variance and the Variance Index

Double Bonus is a high-variance game. While Jacks or Better has a **Variance Index** of 19.5, Full Pay Double Bonus jumps to approximately **28.3**.

### Standard Deviation and Statistical Convergence
The high **Standard Deviation** in Double Bonus is driven by the weighted payouts for Four Aces (800-for-5), Four 2s, 3s, and 4s (400-for-5), and other Quads (250-for-5).
- **Impact on Bankroll:** You will experience more "swings" and deeper losing streaks than in lower-variance games.
- **Convergence:** Because of the high variance, **Statistical Convergence** (where your actual results match the theoretical 100.17% RTP) takes much longer to achieve. A professional player might need to play over **1,000,000 hands** to see their results reliably "even out" near the 100% mark.

## The Royal Flush Cycle and Strategy Precision

The "Royal Flush Cycle" is the average interval between Royal hits. In Double Bonus, this cycle is highly dependent on **strategy precision**. 

### Strategic Shifts in Double Bonus
To achieve the 100.17% RTP, you must adjust your strategy away from the Jacks or Better baseline:
- **Breaking Straight Flushes:** In some rare instances, the EV of holding for a specific Bonus Quad or Royal Flush may exceed holding a made Straight Flush.
- **The "High Card" Priority:** Because Aces pay so much (800 coins for four), the strategy for drawing toward a Royal often competes with the strategy for drawing toward Four Aces. 
- **Combinatorial Analysis:** Professional players use complex algorithms to determine when the "Royal Flush Cycle" should be sacrificed to chase the more frequent (and highly lucrative) Four Aces.

## Bankroll Management for High-Variance Play

Because the **video poker odds and probability** are skewed toward high-value, low-frequency hands, a significant bankroll is required:
- **Risk of Ruin:** A player with a small bankroll has a high "risk of ruin" in Double Bonus, even though the game is technically +EV.
- **The "Grind" vs. The "Jackpot":** You must be able to sustain your play through thousands of hands where the 1-for-1 payout on Two Pair (a key difference from Jacks or Better) will deplete your bankroll faster.

## Conclusion

Double Bonus is the ultimate "expert's" game. It offers one of the **highest return video poker games** for those who have the bankroll to withstand the volatility and the discipline to execute perfect strategy. By understanding the math of the **Variance Index** and the extended **Royal Flush Cycle**, you can turn the mathematical edge in your favor and capitalize on the game's unique payout structure.
