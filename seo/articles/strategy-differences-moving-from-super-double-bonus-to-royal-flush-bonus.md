# Strategy Differences: Moving from Super Double Bonus to Royal Flush Bonus Poker

Transitioning from Super Double Bonus to Royal Flush Bonus Poker is a unique step in the video poker world. Both games are popular variants that offer specific payouts for Four of a Kind. This guide provides the best video poker strategy comparison to help you understand how to play video poker like a pro during this transition.

## The Super Double Bonus Baseline

In Super Double Bonus, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is built on the fact that any pair of Jacks or better is a winning hand. This creates a very stable, low-volatility experience. When moving to Royal Flush Bonus Poker, that "safety net" is removed. You now need at least a pair of Queens or Kings to get your money back.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from Super Double Bonus to Royal Flush Bonus Poker, you'll find that **80% of your strategy remains the same**. The **20% that changes** is entirely focused on the "Pair threshold." This 20% shift causes the most **Bankroll Drain** if not corrected.

### The 20% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Pair of Jacks Devaluation:** In Super Double Bonus, a pair of Jacks is a winning hand. In Royal Flush Bonus Poker, it's a "trash" hand that you should rarely hold unless you're drawing for something better (like a three-card Royal Flush).
2.  **Royal Flush Bonus Focus:** Royal Flush Bonus Poker is named "Royal Flush Bonus" because it rewards you specifically for getting a Royal Flush. This makes Royal Flushes and four-of-a-kind much more valuable than in Super Double Bonus.
3.  **Two Pair Strategy:** While Two Pair pays 2-for-1 in both games, the higher payout for specific "Royal Flush" hands in Royal Flush Bonus Poker means you might break a Full House to draw for a fifth card more often than you would in Super Double Bonus.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

Royal Flush Bonus Poker introduces **Volatility Scaling**. Without the Jacks-or-better push, your bankroll will experience more frequent dips. **EV Erosion** occurs when a Super-Double-Bonus player instinctively holds a pair of Jacks in Royal Flush Bonus Poker. This is a "dead hand" and holding it is a direct loss of expected value.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Queens or Kings or Better are your new minimum "win."
*   **Modify:** Discard any pair of Jacks unless they are part of a better draw.
*   **Target:** Aggressively pursue the "Royal Flush" bonuses; they are the heart of the Royal Flush Bonus Poker pay table.

By recognizing that the "safety net" has moved, you can successfully transition to Royal Flush Bonus Poker without suffering from significant EV Erosion.
