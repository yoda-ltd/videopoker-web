## Tournament Rules: The Basics

### Tiered Multipliers and "Ladder EV"
Multi-Strike is a 20-coin bet game where you play up to 4 levels of hands. A winning hand on Level 1 (1x multiplier) moves you to Level 2 (2x), then Level 3 (4x), and finally Level 4 (8x). 
- **The Similarities**: Like all video poker games, Multi-Strike requires a specialized **multi strike video poker strategy** that prioritizes "Survival Play" at Level 1 and 2. 
- **The Differences**: In tournaments, your goal is to reach the 8x multiplier on Level 4. The **Ladder EV** is much higher when you focus on "Survival Play" (prioritizing high-probability wins) at Level 1 and 2. A Royal Flush at Level 4 (8x multiplier) is a massive 32,000-coin jackpot.

### The "Free Ride" and "Bonus Streak" Edge

A key differentiator for Multi-Strike is the "Free Ride" and "Bonus Streak" mechanics. These features ensure you advance to the next level regardless of the hand's outcome, drastically improving your **Hand-Over-Hand ROI**.
- **Free Ride Impact**: In Multi-Strike, a Free Ride is a "guaranteed win" for advancement purposes. 
- **Bonus Streak Tips**: Many Multi-Strike machines feature "Bonus Streak" payouts for consecutive wins. Using **bonus streak video poker tips** to maintain these multipliers can lead to a much higher **Bonus Trigger Frequency**.

## Winning Tactics for Tournaments

### "Survival Play" at Lower Levels
On Level 1 and Level 2, your focus must be on **Survival**. 
- **The Correct Play**: Prioritize high-probability winning hands at the bottom of the ladder to reach the 8x multiplier on Level 4.
- **The Pitfall**: Chasing a low-probability, high-payout hand on Level 1.

### Aggressive Strategy at Level 4
Once you reach Level 4, every payout is multiplied by 8. 
- **Strategy Shift**: At Level 4, your **multi strike video poker strategy** becomes aggressive. Since you can't advance any further, your only goal is to maximize the payout of the current hand. In tournaments, this is the only time you should aggressively chase big hands.
