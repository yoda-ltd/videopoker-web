# Bankroll Management for Double Bonus Grinders: Mastering the 10/7 High-Variance Grind

Double Bonus (DB) is a legendary game in the video poker world, particularly the "Full Pay" 10/7 version, which offers a theoretical RTP of 100.17%. For the professional grinder, Double Bonus represents one of the best "non-wild" opportunities in the casino, but it comes with a significantly higher **Variance Index** than standard Bonus Poker. Mastering the **best video poker bankroll management** for DB is the key to capturing that elusive +EV.

## The Mathematics of Double Bonus: Variance Index

Double Bonus is a high-variance game. While Jacks or Better has a **Variance Index** of 19.5, Double Bonus typically sits around **28.0 to 30.0**. 

### 1. The Strategy-Variance Interplay
Double Bonus strategy is famously difficult. You will often break apart a Full House to chase Four-of-a-Kind, or hold a lone Ace over a High Pair. These "aggressive" plays increase your variance and your **Ruin Probability** if your bankroll isn't properly sized.

### 2. Standard Deviation and Volatility Buffering
The standard deviation for Double Bonus is approximately 5.30 per hand. 
- Because the strategy is top-heavy (focusing on Aces and Quads), you will experience longer "losing streaks" than in Jacks or Better.
- To maintain a **Risk of Ruin** below 1% in a +EV game like 10/7 DB, a professional needs a bankroll of at least **5,000 to 7,000 units**.

## Kelly Criterion Application in Double Bonus

Double Bonus is one of the few games where the **Kelly Criterion** can be applied to a "clean" edge (the cards themselves).

- **Formula:** $f* = (bp - q) / b$
- With a 0.17% edge on 10/7 DB, a "Full Kelly" approach would suggest a massive bankroll to minimize the risk of " Gambler's Ruin." 
- Most professionals use a "Half-Kelly" or "Quarter-Kelly," which mandates a **Life-Of-Bankroll** of $25,000 to $35,000 for $1 denomination play ($5/bet).

## Session-Based vs. Life-Of-Bankroll Strategies

- **Session-Based Bankroll:** For a 10/7 DB session, do not walk into the casino with less than 1,000 units. The game "eats" bankrolls during the dry spells where the Aces aren't appearing.
- **Life-Of-Bankroll:** Use a **video poker bankroll calculator** specifically calibrated for DB's unique pay table. The calculator will show that because you are breaking many "made" hands to chase bonus quads, your session-to-session swings will be much more violent than in standard Bonus Poker.

## The Royal Flush Cycle and Bonus Quads

The **Royal Flush Cycle** in Double Bonus is approximately 1 in 48,000 hands (slightly longer than JoB due to the aggressive strategy). 
- However, the game offers high payouts for quads:
  - **4 Aces:** 160-for-1.
  - **4 2s, 3s, 4s:** 80-for-1.
  - **Other Quads:** 50-for-1.

These bonus quads are the "lifeblood" of the DB grinder. A single hit on 4 Aces (160 units) can instantly replenish a session bankroll that was on the verge of collapse. 

## Strategic Precision and Variance

Double Bonus is a game of "perfect play." A single mistake—like holding the wrong kicker or failing to break a Flush to chase a Royal—can turn a +EV game into a -EV game.
- **Precision:** Errors in DB are highly penalizing. 
- **The Buffer:** Always add a 10-20% "safety buffer" to your **video poker bankroll calculator** results to account for human fatigue and strategic errors during long sessions.

## Conclusion

Double Bonus is the ultimate professional's game. It offers a mathematical edge but demands absolute discipline and a massive bankroll. By maintaining a strong **Life-Of-Bankroll** and respecting the **Variance Index**, you can survive the DB swings and capture the long-term profit.
