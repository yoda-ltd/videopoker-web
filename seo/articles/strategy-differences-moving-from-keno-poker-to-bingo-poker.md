# Strategy Differences: Moving from Keno Poker to Bingo Poker

Transitioning from Keno Poker to Bingo Poker is a unique step in the video poker world. Both games are niche variants that combine the classic 52-card deck with additional "numbers" from keno or bingo. This shift in **Baseline Hand Ranking** is one of the most difficult to master for a casual player.

## The Keno Poker Baseline

In Keno Poker, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is built on the fact that any pair of Jacks or better is a winning hand. This creates a very stable, low-volatility experience. When moving to Bingo Poker, that "safety net" is removed. You now need at least a pair of Queens or Kings to get your money back.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from Keno Poker to Bingo Poker, you'll find that **80% of your strategy remains the same**. The **20% that changes** is entirely focused on the "Pair threshold." This 20% shift causes the most **Bankroll Drain** if not corrected.

### The 20% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Pair of Jacks Devaluation:** In Keno Poker, a pair of Jacks is a winning hand. In Bingo Poker, it's a "trash" hand that you should rarely hold unless you're drawing for something better (like a three-card Royal Flush).
2.  **Bingo Bonus Focus:** Bingo Poker is named "Bingo" because it pays extra for specific "Bingo" patterns on your cards. This makes four-of-a-kind and specific straights much more valuable than in Keno Poker.
3.  **Two Pair Strategy:** While Two Pair pays 2-for-1 in both games, the higher payout for specific "Bingo" hands in Bingo Poker means you might break a Full House to draw for a fifth card more often than you would in Keno Poker.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

Bingo Poker introduces **Volatility Scaling**. Without the Jacks-or-better push, your bankroll will experience more frequent dips. **EV Erosion** occurs when a Keno-Poker player instinctively holds a pair of Jacks in Bingo Poker. This is a "dead hand" and holding it is a direct loss of expected value.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Queens or Kings or Better are your new minimum "win."
*   **Modify:** Discard any pair of Jacks unless they are part of a better draw.
*   **Target:** Aggressively pursue the "Bingo" bonuses; they are the heart of the Bingo Poker pay table.

By recognizing that the "safety net" has moved, you can successfully transition to Bingo Poker without suffering from significant EV Erosion.
