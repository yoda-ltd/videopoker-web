# Common Mistakes to Avoid in Multi-Strike Poker
Category: Strategy & Tips

Multi-Strike Poker is a high-octane variant that can be incredibly lucrative, but its unique structure often trips up even experienced video poker players. To master the **multi strike video poker strategy**, you must avoid several common pitfalls that can significantly lower your **Hand-Over-Hand ROI**.

## Mistake 1: Treating Each Level the Same

The most significant mistake in Multi-Strike is playing "Standard Strategy" for every level. Multi-Strike is a tiered game where the goal is to reach the 8x multiplier on Level 4.
- **The Pitfall**: Chasing a low-probability, high-payout hand (like a 4-card Flush) on Level 1.
- **The Correct Play**: On Levels 1 and 2, prioritize **Survival Play**. Any winning hand (even a low pair) guarantees advancement to the next level. The **Ladder EV** is much higher when you focus on "winning" rather than "hitting big" at the bottom of the ladder.

## Mistake 2: Ignoring the "Free Ride" Mechanic

The "Free Ride" is a critical feature in Multi-Strike. When it's awarded, you advance to the next level regardless of the outcome of your current hand. 
- **The Pitfall**: Continuing to play conservatively when a Free Ride is active.
- **The Correct Play**: When a Free Ride is active, your **strategy shifts** to aggressive "Jackpot Chasing." Since advancement is guaranteed, your focus moves from "Survival" to maximizing the payout of the *current* level. This is the only time you should revert to standard or high-volatility strategy on lower levels.

## Mistake 3: Underestimating Bankroll Volatility

Multi-Strike requires a 20-coin bet to play all four levels (5 coins per level). 
- **The Pitfall**: Playing with a bankroll designed for standard 5-coin video poker.
- **The Correct Play**: Multi-Strike is an extreme variance game. To withstand the "dry spells" where you fail to reach Level 2 or 3, you need a bankroll of at least 1,500 to 2,000 full bets (30,000 to 40,000 coins). 

## Mistake 4: Missing Out on "Bonus Streak" Opportunities

Many Multi-Strike machines also feature "Bonus Streak" mechanics, where consecutive wins trigger even higher multipliers.
- **The Pitfall**: Failing to adjust your strategy to keep a streak alive.
- **The Correct Play**: When a streak is active, your goal is **Multiplier Retention**. You may need to play even more conservatively to ensure you don't break the streak and lose the multiplier. These **bonus streak video poker tips** are essential for maximizing your long-term earnings.

## Mistake 5: Playing Inferior Paytables

Like all video poker games, the paytable is the single biggest factor in your overall RTP. 
- **The Pitfall**: Playing on a machine with a 7/5 or 6/5 (Full House/Flush) paytable.
- **The Correct Play**: Always seek out 9/6 (or better) paytables. The **Ladder EV** drops significantly on lower paytables, making it nearly impossible to overcome the house edge, even with perfect play.

## Conclusion

By avoiding these common mistakes and adopting a tiered, level-specific strategy, you can master the **multi strike video poker strategy** and capitalize on the massive payouts that make this game a favorite among video poker grinders. Remember: focus on survival early, and go for the kill when the 8x multiplier is active!
