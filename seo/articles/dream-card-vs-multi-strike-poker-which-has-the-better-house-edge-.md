# Dream Card vs. Multi-Strike Poker: Which Has the Better House Edge?
Category: Variant Comparisons

Two of the most popular video poker variants are **Dream Card** and **Multi-Strike Poker**. Both are IGT-patented games that add a unique layer of strategy to standard video poker. While Dream Card offers a "guaranteed winning hand," Multi-Strike is a tiered game with increasing multipliers. When comparing the two, several factors determine the better house edge.

## Skill vs. Chance: The Strategy Divide

### Multi-Strike Poker: Tiered Multipliers
Multi-Strike is a 20-coin bet game where you play up to 4 levels of hands. A winning hand on Level 1 (1x) moves you to Level 2 (2x), then Level 3 (4x), and finally Level 4 (8x). 
- **The Similarities**: Both games require a "side-bet" to unlock their features. For Multi-Strike, this is the 5 coins per level for 4 levels. 
- **The Differences**: In Multi-Strike, your goal is to reach the 8x multiplier on Level 4. The **Ladder EV** is much higher when you focus on "Survival Play" (prioritizing high-probability wins) at Level 1 and 2. A Royal Flush at Level 4 (8x multiplier) is a massive 32,000-coin jackpot.

### Dream Card: The "Guaranteed Winning Hand"
Dream Card is a 10-coin bet game where the "Dream Card" mechanic is triggered about 5% of the time. When active, it gives you a card that completes the best possible hand.
- **The Similarities**: Both games have high volatility and require specialized strategy. For Dream Card, this involves a "Dream Card Strategy" that shifts based on which card is awarded. 
- **The Differences**: Dream Card is a "one-off" bonus, while Multi-Strike is a "ladder" of bonuses.

## The "Free Ride" and "Bonus Streak" Edge

A key differentiator for Multi-Strike is the "Free Ride" and "Bonus Streak" mechanics. These features ensure you advance to the next level regardless of the hand's outcome, drastically improving your **Hand-Over-Hand ROI**.
- **Free Ride Impact**: In Multi-Strike, a Free Ride is a "guaranteed win" for advancement purposes. There is no equivalent in Dream Card.
- **Bonus Streak Tips**: Many Multi-Strike machines feature "Bonus Streak" payouts for consecutive wins. Using **bonus streak video poker tips** to maintain these multipliers can lead to a much higher **Bonus Trigger Frequency** than you'll find in Dream Card.

## House Edge and RTP Comparison

The house edge for both games is heavily dependent on the base paytable. 
- **Multi-Strike RTP**: With a 9/6 (Full House/Flush) paytable and perfect play, Multi-Strike can have an RTP of up to 99.7%. This is because the 8x multiplier on Level 4 significantly offsets the 20-coin bet.
- **Dream Card RTP**: With a 9/6 paytable and perfect play, Dream Card's RTP is typically around 99.4%. The 10-coin bet for the Dream Card feature is slightly less efficient than the Multi-Strike side-bet.

## Summary
While Dream Card offers a unique "guaranteed win" mechanic, **Multi-Strike Poker** generally has a better house edge when played with a specialized **multi strike video poker strategy**. The tiered multipliers (up to 8x) and "Free Ride" features provide a more consistent long-term ROI for the disciplined player.
