# The Payout Gap: Five Deck Poker vs. Wheel Poker Explained

For the **Advantage Player (AP)**, understanding the difference between high-feature video poker variants like Five Deck Poker and Wheel Poker is the difference between a high-variance gambling session and a calculated **Return on Investment (ROI)**. Both games offer a "bonus feature" for an additional bet, but the way they generate **Expected Value (EV)** is fundamentally different.

## Five Deck Poker: The "Duplicate" Bonus

In Five Deck Poker, each card in your hand is drawn from a separate 52-card deck. This changes the entire mathematical landscape of the game.
- **The Bonus Mechanism:** Because each card comes from a different deck, you can hit "Five of a Kind" (five identical cards). This is impossible in standard 52-card poker. The "Expected Value (EV)" of the hands you can complete with a single card is so high that it pulls the strategy toward the top of the deck.
- **The AP Edge:** The primary advantage in Five Deck Poker comes from the "Jackpot Potential" of the five decks. An AP looks for Five Deck Poker machines where the paytable hasn't been severely "downgraded" to pay for the five-deck feature. If the base game is "8/5" (8 coins Full House, 5 for Flush), but the five-deck feature adds 0.8% to the RTP, the game might be viable.

## Wheel Poker: The "Royal" Bonus

In Wheel Poker, you typically pay a 5-coin surcharge (total 10-coin bet) to activate a wheel spin whenever you hit a "Four of a Kind" (or occasionally other hands, depending on the variant).
- **The Bonus Mechanism:** The wheel offers a random prize—usually a large coin amount or a multiplier. This means the feature is **Outcome-Driven**. You only get the benefit when you hit a rare, high-value hand.
- **The AP Edge:** Wheel Poker is attractive to APs because it adds a layer of "Wheel EV" to every Four of a Kind. The strategy is generally the same as the underlying game, but the higher **Volatility** requires a more robust bankroll.

## Comparing the Payout Gap

The "Payout Gap" between these two games is found in how they distribute their **RTP (Return to Player)**.
1. **Frequency of Bonus:** Five Deck Poker features trigger much more frequently than the "Four of a Kind" required for Wheel Poker. This makes Five Deck a "smoother" experience, although still higher variance than standard video poker.
2. **Top-End Potential:** Wheel Poker has a higher "Single-Hand ROI" potential. A wheel spin can result in 2,000, 4,000, or even 8,000 coins on top of your Four of a Kind. Five Deck typically caps Five-of-a-Kind at 800-for-1.
3. **Strategic Adjustment:** In Five Deck Poker, there is a significant change to the base strategy. You must hold for "Five of a Kind" over a traditional Royal Flush. In Wheel Poker, the strategy remains standard, but the "value" of each Four of a Kind is higher, which can make a difference if there is a progressive involved.

## Advantage Play Recommendation

- **Choose Five Deck Poker if:** You prefer more frequent bonuses and a slightly lower variance than Wheel Poker. It's often easier to find "Full Pay" or near-Full Pay variants of Five Deck Poker.
- **Choose Wheel Poker if:** You have a massive bankroll and are "chasing" a massive top-end win. The **Standard Deviation** is much higher here.

## Conclusion: Mastering the Bonus Surcharge

Both Five Deck Poker and Wheel Poker require a 10-coin bet per line to be mathematically optimal. For an AP, this means your hourly "action" is doubled. Before sitting down, ensure the "Bonus RTP" added by the five decks or the wheel actually compensates for the 5-coin surcharge. If the base game is too poor, no amount of duplicate cards or spinning wheels will make the game profitable. Focus on the EV, master the volatility, and treat every surcharge as a calculated business expense.
