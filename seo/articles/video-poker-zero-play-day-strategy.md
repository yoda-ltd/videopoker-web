## 1. What is a "Zero-Play" Day?

A zero-play day occurs when you are on a casino property—specifically when you are staying in a room on your player's card—and you do not record any significant gambling volume.

*   **The Computer's View:** The casino's database sees that you occupied a room worth $200 (Market Rate) and gave back $0 in **Theoretical Loss (Theo)**.
*   **The Impact:** This "Zero" is averaged into your **ADT (Average Daily Theoretical)**. If you had an ADT of $400 over 2 days, and then record a Zero on Day 3, your 3-day ADT drops to **$266**.

## 2. Why "Zeros" Destroy Your Mailers

**Mailer Logic** is built on averages. Casinos send offers for Free Play and Suites based on your *per-day* value.

*   **Scenario A:** You stay 2 nights, play $10,000 coin-in. ADT = $5,000. Mailer = 3 nights free + $200 Free Play.
*   **Scenario B:** You stay 4 nights (using the same 2-night free mailer + 2 paid nights), play the same $10,000 coin-in. ADT = $2,500. Mailer = 2 nights free + $50 Free Play.
*   **The Lesson:** By staying longer without increasing your play, you "diluted" your value and lost hundreds of dollars in future **Mailer Equity**.
