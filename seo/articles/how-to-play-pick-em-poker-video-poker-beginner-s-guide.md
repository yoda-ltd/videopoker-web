## What is Pick'em Poker?

In Pick'em Poker, you don't choose which of five cards to keep. Instead, you are given two fixed cards and two "piles" to choose from. It is a "non-wild" game based on a standard 52-card virtual deck, managed by a **trusted video poker RNG certification**.

## How the Deal Works: A Unique Format

1.  **The Deal:** You are dealt **two fixed cards** on the left. These are your "base" and are always kept.
2.  **The Piles:** On the right, you see **two separate cards** (let's call them Pile A and Pile B). 
3.  **The Hidden Cards:** Each pile represents a three-card set, but only the *top card* of each pile is visible. 
4.  **The Choice:** You must "Pick" either Pile A or Pile B.
5.  **The Result:** The machine reveals the two hidden cards in your chosen pile. Your final five-card hand consists of the two fixed cards on the left, the visible card you chose, and the two hidden cards from that pile.

The randomness of the hidden cards is guaranteed by **RTP Verification** and periodic **Lab Audits** by firms like **GLI**.

## Basic Strategy: The "Golden Rule" of the Pick

Since you only have to choose between two piles, your strategy is focused on which pile completes the better poker hand. To satisfy **Technical Standards** for optimal play:
- **Prioritize Pairs:** If one pile gives you a pair of 9s or higher, pick it.
- **High Card Value:** If no pairs are available, choose the pile with the highest ranking card (Aces are best).
- **Straight/Flush Draws:** If a pile gives you a 3-card Royal Flush or a 3-card Straight Flush draw, it is often more valuable than a low pair.
