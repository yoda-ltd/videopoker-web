# Strategy Differences: Moving from White Hot Aces to Pick'em Poker

Transitioning from White Hot Aces to Pick'em Poker is a massive shift in how you play video poker. White Hot Aces is a volatile variant that rewards you for getting four Aces with a kicker. Pick'em Poker is a much more simplified and less volatile game. This guide provides the best video poker strategy comparison to help you understand how to play video poker like a pro during this transition.

## The White Hot Aces Baseline

In White Hot Aces, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is built on the fact that any pair of Aces or better is a winning hand. This creates a very stable, low-volatility experience. When moving to Pick'em Poker, that "safety net" is removed. You now need at least a pair of Jacks to get your money back.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from White Hot Aces to Pick'em Poker, you'll find that **only about 40% of your strategy is portable**. This is why this transition causes such massive **Bankroll Drain** for White Hot Aces experts. The **60% that changes** is what separates a professional Pick'em player from a hobbyist.

### The 60% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Pair of Aces Devaluation:** In White Hot Aces, a pair of Aces is a winning hand. In Pick'em Poker, it's a "trash" hand that you should rarely hold unless you're drawing for something better (like a three-card Royal Flush).
2.  **Pick'em Focus:** Pick'em Poker is named "Pick'em" because you get to pick which of two three-card hands you want to complete. This makes four-of-a-kind and specific straights much more valuable than in White Hot Aces.
3.  **Two Pair Strategy:** While Two Pair pays 2-for-1 in both games, the higher payout for specific "Pick'em" hands in Pick'em Poker means you might break a Full House to draw for a fifth card more often than you would in White Hot Aces.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

Pick'em Poker introduces **Volatility Scaling**. Without the Aces-or-better push, your bankroll will experience more frequent dips. **EV Erosion** occurs when a White-Hot-Aces player instinctively holds a pair of Aces in Pick'em Poker. This is a "dead hand" and holding it is a direct loss of expected value.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Jacks or Better are your new minimum "win."
*   **Modify:** Discard any pair of Aces unless they are part of a better draw.
*   **Target:** Aggressively pursue the "Pick'em" bonuses; they are the heart of the Pick'em Poker pay table.

By recognizing that the "safety net" has moved, you can successfully transition to Pick'em Poker without suffering from significant EV Erosion.
