# Strategy Differences: Moving from Standard to Ultimate X

Many players assume that because they have mastered Jacks or Better, they can jump right into Ultimate X. This is a dangerous assumption. While the hand rankings are the same, the introduction of **Accumulated Multipliers** and **Next-Hand Value** changes the "mathematically correct" play in several key scenarios.

## The Multiplier Impact on Strategy
In standard video poker, your goal is to maximize the payout of the *current* hand. In Ultimate X, you are also playing for the *future* multipliers.

### Key Tactical Shifts:
*   **Flush Draws:** In many Ultimate X games, a Flush triggers a 10x or 11x multiplier for the next hand. This makes 4-to-a-flush significantly more valuable than in standard poker.
*   **Full House vs. Three of a Kind:** A Full House often triggers a 12x multiplier, while 3-of-a-kind might only trigger a 4x. This gap encourages holding for the Full House more aggressively to ensure **EV preservation**.
*   **Breaking Pairs:** There are rare situations where breaking a low pair to go for a higher multiplier trigger is the "pro" move.

## 3-Play, 5-Play, and 10-Play Complexity
The strategy doesn't just change from standard to Ultimate X; it scales with the number of lines.
*   **3-Play:** Closer to standard strategy, but with a slight leaning toward multiplier hands.
*   **10-Play:** Extreme focus on "volatility management." The goal is to keep as many multipliers active as possible to capitalize on the 10-coin premium you are paying on every line.
