# Strategy Differences: Moving from Super Aces Bonus to Bonus Poker Deluxe
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Super Aces Bonus** to **Bonus Poker Deluxe** is a shift from a "Targeted Bonus" natural variant to a "Uniform Bonus" variant. This represents a significant **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your hands and the potential for bonus quads.

### The Comparison Matrix: Super Aces Bonus vs. Bonus Poker Deluxe

| Feature | Super Aces Bonus (Full Pay) | Bonus Poker Deluxe (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.94% | 99.64% |
| **Variance Index** | 60.0+ | 30.7 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Aces Bonus with Kicker | Target ANY Natural Quad Bonus |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.06% | 0.36% |

### Strategic Divergence: Aces Focus vs. Uniform Quads

In **Super Aces Bonus**, you are looking for natural Four Aces with a kicker for a massive bonus. In **Bonus Poker Deluxe**, all four-of-a-kind hands are "Doubled" compared to standard Jacks or Better, paying a uniform 400 credits. This creates a much lower **Volatility Normalization** (30.7 vs 60.0+).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 80% overlap on the core hand results. However, in Super Aces Bonus, you will prioritize Aces above any other natural card. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the Aces in Super Aces Bonus dominate the EV landscape. In Bonus Poker Deluxe, any quad is just as good as any other.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Ace Devaluation:** When moving to Bonus Poker Deluxe, Aces are just another card in the deck. They no longer offer a special bonus.
2.  **Kicker Removal:** You no longer need to manage kickers for the bonus. Your strategy is hand-by-hand.
3.  **Uniform Quad Focus:** In Bonus Poker Deluxe, you are looking for ANY natural four-of-a-kind combination.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games are excellent for players who enjoy bonus payouts, but Bonus Poker Deluxe provides a smoother ride with lower variance. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are top-tier choices for anyone looking for a player advantage.

In **Double Jackpot Poker**, the payouts are tiered: Four Aces pay more than Four Faces (K, Q, J), which pay more than Four 2s-10s. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through tiered quads. Double Jackpot Poker's variance (28.5) is much lower than Super Aces Bonus (60.0+), providing a smoother **Volatility Normalization**.

This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the Aces in Super Aces Bonus dominate the EV landscape. In Double Jackpot Poker, the faces are also a major target.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Face Card Priority:** In Double Jackpot Poker, you prioritize Kings, Queens, and Jacks much more than in Super Aces Bonus.
2.  **Kicker Removal:** You no longer need to manage kickers for the bonus. Your strategy is hand-by-hand.
3.  **Tiered Quad Focus:** In Double Jackpot Poker, you are looking for specific quad tiers for the jackpot.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Super Aces Bonus to Pick 'em Poker
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Super Aces Bonus** to **Pick 'em Poker** is a shift from a "Draw" variant to a "Fixed Outcome" variant. This represents a total **Strategic Divergence** in how you interact with the machine and value your decisions.

### The Comparison Matrix: Super Aces Bonus vs. Pick 'em Poker

| Feature | Super Aces Bonus (Full Pay) | Pick 'em Poker (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.94% | 99.95% |
| **Variance Index** | 60.0+ | 15.0 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Aces Bonus with Kicker | Pick 1 of 2 Hidden Stacks |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.06% | 0.05% |

### Strategic Divergence: Drawing vs. Picking

In **Super Aces Bonus**, you are looking for the best five-card combination by drawing cards. In **Pick 'em Poker**, you are shown two fixed cards and must choose between two "stacks" of three cards. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through simple choice, not complex drawing.

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is zero (0%). Both are fundamentally different games. Super Aces Bonus is a draw game; Pick 'em Poker is a choice-based game. In Super Aces Bonus, you are looking for the **best video poker strategy comparison** for your initial deal; in Pick 'em Poker, you are choosing between two fixed outcomes.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Simplify Everything:** You are only making one choice in Pick 'em Poker: left or right.
2.  **High Pair Priority:** In Pick 'em Poker, you are looking for natural high pairs starting at 10s. The EV math is much simpler.
3.  **Variance Normalization:** Pick 'em Poker has a very low variance (15.0) compared to Super Aces Bonus (60.0+). This means your bankroll will last much longer.

A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that Pick 'em Poker is one of the "loosest" games in the casino, often returning almost 100%. For players who find the drawing strategy of Super Aces Bonus too volatile, Pick 'em Poker is the ultimate alternative for **EV Maximization**.
