# Why Double Bonus is Better than Aces and Eights for Beginners
Category: Comparison

For players new to the world of high-payout video poker, choosing the right starting point is a matter of **Strategic Divergence**. This guide explains why **Double Bonus** is often the superior choice for beginners over the tiered-payout **Aces and Eights**.

### The Comparison Matrix: Double Bonus vs. Aces and Eights

This **best video poker strategy comparison** highlights why beginners should prioritize broad rewards.

| Feature | Double Bonus (10/7) | Aces and Eights (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.17% (Full Pay) | 99.78% |
| **Variance Index** | 28.5 | 21.8 |
| **Primary Focus** | High-Payout Quad Bonuses | Targeted Quad Bonuses (Aces/8s/7s) |
| **Learning Curve** | Gentle | Moderate |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.17% (Player Edge) | 0.22% |

### The Reward Gap: Broad vs. Targeted Bonuses

The single biggest reason **Double Bonus** is better for beginners is its broad reward structure. In Double Bonus, almost all four-of-a-kind hands pay a significant bonus compared to standard games. In **Aces and Eights**, you must remember to prioritize only 8s and 7s for their special tiered bonuses. This leads to a lower cognitive load for **EV Maximization** in Double Bonus for a new player.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is high strategy overlap (approximately 80%) between Jacks or Better and Double Bonus. For a beginner, this means their existing knowledge is highly transferable. In Aces and Eights, the focus on specific ranks requires more unlearning of standard poker "common sense." This is the essence of **Volatility Normalization** for each game.

### Why Beginners Win with Double Bonus
1.  **High Theoretical Return:** The 100.17% RTP provides a player edge in full-pay versions.
2.  **Frequent Large Payouts:** High-payout quads occur across multiple ranks, keeping the game exciting.
3.  **Transferable Skills:** What you learn in Double Bonus applies to almost all other high-payout natural games.

While both games offer excellent returns, the broad and intuitive nature of high-payout quads in Double Bonus makes it the more enjoyable choice for those just starting their video poker journey.

# Why Double Bonus is Better than Aces and Faces for Beginners
Category: Comparison

Moving from a face-card-focused game to a broad-bonus game is a major **Strategic Divergence**. For beginners, **Double Bonus** offers a much more rewarding path to mastery than **Aces and Faces**.

### The Comparison Matrix: Double Bonus vs. Aces and Faces

| Feature | Double Bonus (10/7) | Aces and Faces (8/5) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.17% | 99.26% |
| **Variance Index** | 28.5 | 22.1 |
| **Primary Focus** | High-Payout Quad Bonuses | Face Card Quad Bonuses |
| **Strategy Type** | Natural/Targeted | Natural/Rank |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.17% (Player Edge) | 0.74% |

### The Payout Gap: High Payouts vs. Rank Focus

In **Aces and Faces**, you are primarily looking for four-of-a-kind with Kings, Queens, or Jacks. In **Double Bonus**, the payouts for these quads are significantly higher, and the bonus is extended to other ranks as well. For a beginner, this means more frequent and larger rewards for simple hold decisions. This leads to a higher **EV Maximization** potential.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is high strategy overlap (approximately 80%). However, in Double Bonus, the rewards for natural quads are so high that they dominate the EV landscape. For a beginner, this simplifies the goal: find any four-of-a-kind. This is the essence of **Volatility Normalization**.

### Why Beginners Win with Double Bonus
1.  **Bankroll Growth:** The higher theoretical return allows for more frequent winning sessions.
2.  **Exciting Draws:** The potential for a massive payout on any natural quad keeps the game engaging.
3.  **Low Complexity:** You don't have to learn complex rank-based exceptions to standard poker logic.

Through a detailed **video poker house edge analysis**, it's clear that while Aces and Faces is a solid game, Double Bonus offers a superior return and more excitement for those just starting their video poker journey.

# Why Double Bonus is Better than Triple Double Bonus for Beginners
Category: Comparison

Transitioning to a kicker-based variant like **Triple Double Bonus** is an extreme leap in volatility that most beginners are not prepared for. For those new to the game, **Double Bonus** provides a much better introduction to high-action video poker.

### The Comparison Matrix: Double Bonus vs. Triple Double Bonus

| Feature | Double Bonus (10/7) | Triple Double Bonus (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.17% | 99.58% |
| **Variance Index** | 28.5 | 98.0 |
| **Primary Focus** | High-Payout Quad Bonuses | Kicker-Based Quad Jackpots |
| **Risk Level** | Moderate/High | Extreme |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.17% (Player Edge) | 0.42% |

### The Jackpot Gap: Natural vs. Kicker Strategy

In **Triple Double Bonus**, the reward for Four Aces with a kicker is 4,000 credits—the same as a Royal Flush! For a beginner, this strategy is counter-intuitive and emotionally taxing. You must break almost every winning hand to chase the kicker jackpot. In **Double Bonus**, you play a high-payout natural game with much lower volatility. This leads to a much healthier **Volatility Normalization** (28.5 vs 98.0).

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is roughly 70%. In Double Bonus, you prioritize natural quads. In Triple Double Bonus, that logic is second to kicker hunting. For a beginner, this difference is crucial for Achieving a high return without losing their entire bankroll.

### Why Beginners Win with Double Bonus
1.  **Bankroll Protection:** The 28.5 variance is far more manageable than the 98.0 of Triple Double Bonus.
2.  **Transferable Skills:** What you learn in Double Bonus applies to almost all other natural high-payout variants.
3.  **Low Stress Action:** You don't have to worry about the "all-or-nothing" nature of kicker hunting.

A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that while Triple Double Bonus offers exciting jackpot potential, most beginners will find Double Bonus to be the smarter, more rewarding path to **EV Maximization**.
