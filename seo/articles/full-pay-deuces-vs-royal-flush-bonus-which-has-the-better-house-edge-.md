# Full Pay Deuces vs. Royal Flush Bonus: Which Has the Better House Edge?

When walking through a casino, you'll often see "Deuces Wild" machines alongside "Royal Flush Bonus" (or similar Bonus Poker) machines. Both offer big jackpots, but the underlying math and house edge are worlds apart. This article compares **Full Pay Deuces Wild (100.76% EV)** against **Royal Flush Bonus** games to see which offers the better return for your bankroll.

## Understanding the Theoretical Return (EV)

### 1. Full Pay Deuces Wild
*   **Return (EV):** 100.76%
*   **House Edge:** -0.76% (Player Advantage)
*   **Key Feature:** The "jackpot" is spread between Four Deuces (200:1) and the Natural Royal (800:1).
*   **Volatility:** High.

### 2. Royal Flush Bonus Games
"Royal Flush Bonus" is usually a variation of Jacks or Better or Double Bonus where the Royal Flush payout is increased (e.g., from 800:1 to 1000:1 or via a progressive).
*   **Return (EV):** Typically ranges from **98.5% to 99.8%**, even with the bonus.
*   **House Edge:** 0.2% to 1.5%.
*   **Key Feature:** Massive reliance on a single, rare hand (the Royal).

## The Math: Why Deuces Wild Usually Wins
A Royal Flush occurs approximately once every **40,000 to 45,000 hands**. 

In a **Royal Flush Bonus** game, if the payout is bumped to 1,000:1 (up from 800:1), it adds about **0.5%** to the total return. However, casinos often lower the payout for the **Full House** or **Flush** to compensate. 
*   Example: A 9/6 Jacks or Better (99.54%) becomes an 8/5 Royal Flush Bonus (97.8% + bonus = ~98.3%).

In **Full Pay Deuces Wild**, you don't just rely on the Royal. The **Four Deuces (200:1)** occur every **4,900 hands**. This means you hit a "mini-jackpot" nearly 9 times more often than you do in a Royal Flush Bonus game.

## Strategy Complexity Comparison
Which game is easier to play perfectly?

| Feature | Full Pay Deuces Wild | Royal Flush Bonus (Jacks/Better) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strategy Difficulty** | High (Counter-intuitive) | Low (Logical) |
| **Penalty for Errors** | High | Low |
| **Optimal Hold** | Hold 2s over almost everything | Chase Royal pieces aggressively |

**The Verdict:** While Full Pay Deuces has a better mathematical return, it is much harder to play. A single mistake in Deuces Wild (like throwing away a Deuce) can cost you more in EV than the entire house edge of a Bonus Poker game.

## House Edge Analysis: The "Hidden" Costs
In Royal Flush Bonus games, the "Bonus" is often a distraction. You might be playing a machine with a 97% base return, and the bonus only brings it up to 99%. 

In contrast, Full Pay Deuces is a "pure" table. If you see the **5-for-1 Four of a Kind** and **15-for-1 Five of a Kind**, you know exactly what the math is: 100.76%. There are no hidden progressives or bonus triggers required to hit that number.

## When to Choose Which Game?

**Choose Full Pay Deuces Wild if:**
*   You have mastered the strategy via a trainer.
*   You want a mathematical edge over the casino.
*   You have a large enough bankroll to handle the volatility.

**Choose Royal Flush Bonus if:**
*   You want a simpler, more "relaxing" game.
*   The progressive jackpot is exceptionally high (e.g., a $1.25 bet paying $2,500+).
*   You prefer the "Natural" game of Jacks or Better without wild cards.

## Conclusion
Mathematically, **Full Pay Deuces Wild is the superior game**. It offers a positive return (100.76%) that most Royal Flush Bonus games can't touch. However, the "Better House Edge" only exists if you play perfectly. If you aren't prepared to study strategy charts, the Royal Flush Bonus game might actually be "cheaper" for you in the long run due to its lower complexity.
