# All American Pay Table Analysis: 9/6 vs. 8/5 vs. 7/5

All American Video Poker is famous for its "balanced" pay table, where Straights, Flushes, and Full Houses often pay the same high amount. However, this balance is fragile. Small changes to these payouts can have a massive impact on the **Volatility Index** and your overall return. In this analysis, we will explore the different versions of the game and how to find the **best odds for All American**.

## Understanding All American Math

In All American, the "9/6" label is slightly different than in Jacks or Better. While it still refers to the Full House and Flush, you must also pay close attention to the Straight payout. To achieve a high RTP, All American requires all three—Full House, Flush, and Straight—to be high.

## Comparing the Tables

### 1. The "Full Pay" All American (9/8/8)
*   **Full House:** 9
*   **Flush:** 8
*   **Straight:** 8
*   **Theoretical RTP:** 99.38%
*   **Volatility Index:** High

This is the version you want. The high payouts for Straights and Flushes compensate for the fact that Two Pair only pays 1-for-1. It provides an exciting, high-variance experience where big wins can come from relatively common hands.

### 2. The "Short Pay" All American (8/8/8)
*   **Full House:** 8
*   **Flush:** 8
*   **Straight:** 8
*   **Theoretical RTP:** 98.49%

Reducing the Full House by just one credit drops the return by nearly 1%. While still playable for some, it increases the "grind" and makes it harder to stay profitable.

### 3. The "Low Pay" All American (8/6/6)
*   **Full House:** 8
*   **Flush:** 6
*   **Straight:** 6
*   **Theoretical RTP:** 95.80%

This is the version often found in "greedy" online casinos or low-limit airport machines. By dropping the Straight and Flush payouts, the casino destroys the **EV Differential** that makes All American worth playing. At this level, the house edge is over 4%, which is far too high for a game with this much volatility.

## Volatility and the "Two Pair" Factor

The most important thing to remember in any All American **Pay Table Verification** is that Two Pair only pays 1-for-1. This is the source of the game's high **Volatility Index**. On a 9/8/8 machine, you can handle the "Two Pair" tax because the Straights and Flushes pay so well. On an 8/6/6 machine, you are paying the tax but getting no reward, leading to a rapid bankroll collapse.

## Hunting for the Best Odds for All American

To maximize your success, always use an **All American video poker trainer** to simulate play on different tables. You will quickly see that your strategy must change based on the payouts. On a high-pay machine, you will aggressively chase Straight draws. On a low-pay machine, the math often forces you into a "defensive" style that ruins the fun of the game.

## Conclusion

All American is a game of "all or nothing." If you find a 9/8/8 machine, it is one of the most rewarding and mathematically interesting games in the casino. If you can only find 8/6/6, you are better off playing standard Jacks or Better. Always perform your **Pay Table Verification** before you play to ensure you are getting the **best odds for All American**.
