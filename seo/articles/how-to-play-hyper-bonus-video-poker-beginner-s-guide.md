## What is Hyper Bonus?
Hyper Bonus is a multi-hand game (typically 3, 5, or 10 play) that features an optional side-bet to activate **Accumulated Multipliers**. Unlike Ultimate X, where multipliers are earned on the *previous* hand, Hyper Bonus awards multipliers on the *current* hand based on the quality of your dealt cards.

### The 10-Coin Bet: Side-Bet EV
To play Hyper Bonus correctly, you must bet 10 coins per line (5 for the base game, 5 for the feature). 
- **The Mechanic:** If you are dealt a winning hand (or a specific "bonus-qualifying" hand like a pair), the game applies a multiplier to the payouts for that hand across all your active lines.
- **RTP Impact:** Playing with only 5 coins is a mistake. The base game usually has a lower pay table than standalone games; the 10th coin is what brings the theoretical RTP back up to the 99% range by introducing the multiplier feature.

## How the Multipliers Work
The core of Hyper Bonus is the **Feature Hit Frequency**. Multipliers are awarded for "Pat" hands on the deal:
- **Three-of-a-Kind Dealt:** Often triggers a 3x or 5x multiplier for the draw.
- **Four-to-a-Flush/Straight:** Can trigger 2x multipliers.
- **Full House/Four-of-a-Kind:** These high-value "Pat" hands receive massive multipliers (up to 12x or more depending on the version), resulting in astronomical payouts.

## Strategic Shifts for Beginners
Hyper Bonus strategy differs from standard play because of **Variance Normalization**. You are essentially "pre-paying" for the chance to hit a multiplier.

1.  **Aggressive Dealt-Hand Holding:** In Hyper Bonus, you are looking for any combination that triggers a multiplier. If a pair of 8s triggers a multiplier but a single King does not, the value of the 8s increases significantly compared to standard play.
2.  **Multi-Hand Focus:** Since multipliers apply to *all* lines, the strategy favors "broad" draws. If you have a 3x multiplier active on 10-play, every single "High Card" draw is 3 times more valuable than usual.
3.  **Bankroll Awareness:** Because you are betting 10 coins per line, a 10-play machine costs 100 coins per spin. Beginners should start at the "penny" or "nickel" denomination to avoid exhausting their bankroll before the multipliers hit.
