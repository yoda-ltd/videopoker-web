# Bankroll Management for Jacks or Better Grinders: The Math of Sustainability

For the Jacks or Better grinder, video poker is less a game of chance and more an exercise in capital management. Unlike "one-shot" recreational players who might burn through a few hundred dollars for entertainment, the professional grinder views their bankroll as a tool of the trade. Success is defined by surviving the variance to realize the game's theoretical Expected Value (EV).

## The Kelly Criterion and Video Poker

In professional gambling finance, the **Kelly Criterion** is the gold standard for determining optimal bet sizing. While video poker typically involves a fixed 5-coin bet to maximize the Royal Flush payout, the Kelly Criterion helps determine the total bankroll required to minimize the **Risk of Ruin (RoR)**.

For a 9/6 Jacks or Better machine (99.54% ER), the variance is relatively low (19.5), but the "lumpy" nature of the payout—where a significant portion of the return is tied to a 1-in-40,000 hand—means you cannot simply play with a "session budget."

## Bankroll for the Long Run vs. One-Shot Sessions

There is a fundamental mathematical divide between how a pro and a casual player approach the machine.

### 1. One-Shot Sessions (Recreational)
Recreational players often use a "stop-loss" or a fixed daily budget. Mathematically, this is inefficient. If you are playing a positive EV game (through a combination of pay tables and casino comps/promotions), every hand you *don't* play is a loss of theoretical profit. 

### 2. The Long Run (Professional)
A professional "bankroll for the long run" is designed to withstand a 3-standard-deviation swing. For Jacks or Better, a common rule of thumb is to have a bankroll of **3 to 5 Royal Flushes** for the denomination you are playing.
- **Quarters ($1.25/bet):** $12,000 - $20,000 bankroll.
- **Dollars ($5.00/bet):** $48,000 - $80,000 bankroll.

## Utilizing a Video Poker Bankroll Calculator

To move beyond rules of thumb, serious players use a **video poker bankroll calculator**. These tools allow you to input the specific pay table, your desired "Risk of Ruin" (e.g., 1%), and the number of hands you intend to play. 

The calculator will reveal that to achieve a 99% confidence level of not going broke over 100,000 hands of 9/6 Jacks or Better, you need a significantly larger cushion than most players realize. This is because "Session Tracking" often reveals long droughts where even small wins (High Pairs, Two Pair) fail to appear, depleting the bankroll before the next "big" hit.

## How to Track Video Poker Winnings for Taxes and Growth

Sustainability isn't just about the math at the machine; it’s about the accounting at the desk. Professional grinders must know **how to track video poker winnings for taxes** to ensure the IRS doesn't take a bite out of their "gross" winnings without accounting for the "net" reality.

- **The Daily Log:** Record every session's start/end time, machine ID, and net result.
- **The Variance Buffer:** Keep your gambling funds separate from personal finances. A "leak" in your personal budget is a "Risk of Ruin" for your gambling business.
- **Comp Valuation:** Treat free play, meals, and room offers as part of your bankroll's return. In many cases, the "grind" is break-even on the cards but highly profitable on the back-end rewards.
