## 1. The Foundation: Tier Matching and Reciprocity

The "Loop" begins with **Tier Matching**. Most major casino operators in Las Vegas will match the status you have earned at a competing property to lure your business. 

*   **The Catalyst:** Earn "Diamond" status at Caesars through a concentrated video poker "Diamond in a Day" (DIAD) run.

## 2. ADT Scoping: The Math of the Stay

To stay for free indefinitely, you must master your **ADT (Average Daily Theoretical)**. This is the amount the casino *expects* to win from you per day.

*   **Scoping the Property:** To get a free room at Flamingo (Caesars), you might need an ADT of $150. To get one at Bellagio (MGM), you might need $500.
*   **The Strategy:** Always play at a level that slightly exceeds the requirement for the property you *want* to stay at next. If you want a suite at Caesars Palace, your video poker volume must reflect "Palace-level" ADT.

## 3. Advanced Mailer Optimization: Triggering the "Free"

The lifeblood of the "Vegas Loop" is the **Bounce Back** mailer. Casinos use **Mailer Logic** to look at your play history and send offers for future free stays.

*   **The Gap Strategy:** Stay 4 nights at an MGM property using their mailer. During those 4 nights, put in your required video poker volume. 
*   **The Pivot:** For your *next* 4 nights, move to a Caesars property using *their* mailer.
*   **The Cycle:** While you are at Caesars, the MGM system sees you as "away" and generates a new offer to bring you back. By alternating every 3-5 days, you ensure that you never pay for a room and your ADT never looks "diluted" by low-play days.

## 4. Avoiding the "Zero-Play Day" Trap

The biggest mistake "Loop" players make is the **Zero-Play Day**. If you are staying at Caesars on a comped room and decide to take a day off to visit the Grand Canyon, *do not use your card for anything* that day. 

If you swipe your card at a restaurant or a penny slot, the computer records a "Day" with $0 play. This drags your ADT down significantly, which can kill your mailers and break the loop.

## 5. Cash Back vs. Free Play: Evaluating the EV

As you chain these rewards, you must decide how to take your "Rebate."
*   **Free Play:** Often has a higher face value but must be played through the machine, introducing variance.
*   **Cash Back:** Lower face value but realized immediately. 
*   **The Pro Tip:** Use your Free Play on high-volatility machines like **Triple Double Bonus** to hunt for a jackpot, as the "money" isn't yours yet anyway.
