# Loose Deuces vs. Super Aces Bonus: Which Has the Better House Edge?

When it comes to high-octane video poker, few games compare to **Loose Deuces** and **Super Aces Bonus**. Loose Deuces offers the legendary 500-for-1 payout for Four Deuces, while Super Aces Bonus offers a massive 160-for-1 for Four Aces. This article compares the house edge, math, and volatility of these two powerhouse games.

## Theoretical Return (EV) and House Edge

### 1. Full Pay Loose Deuces
*   **Return (EV):** 100.97%
*   **House Edge:** -0.97% (Player Advantage)
*   **Core Math:** High return driven by the 500:1 Four Deuces and a strong 100.97% theoretical limit.

### 2. Full Pay Super Aces Bonus
*   **Return (EV):** 99.94%
*   **House Edge:** 0.06% (Virtually Break-Even)
*   **Core Math:** High return driven by the 160:1 payout for Four Aces and the 80:1 payout for Four 2s, 3s, or 4s.

**The Winner on EV:** Full Pay Loose Deuces (100.97%) is the better game mathematically, offering nearly a 1% edge to the skilled player.

## Pay Table Analysis: Wild vs. Natural

The primary difference is that Loose Deuces uses **Wild Cards**, whereas Super Aces Bonus is a **Natural** game. 

| Hand | Loose Deuces (100.97%) | Super Aces Bonus (99.94%) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Natural Royal** | 800:1 | 800:1 |
| **Four Deuces** | **500:1** | 80:1 |
| **Four Aces** | (Included in 4-of-a-Kind) | **160:1** |
| **Full House** | 3:1 | 8:1 |
| **Flush** | 2:1 | 5:1 |

In Super Aces Bonus, the **Full House (8:1)** and **Flush (5:1)** payouts are much higher than in Loose Deuces. This means your "base" play is more rewarding in Super Aces, while Loose Deuces is a "Jackpot or Bust" style game.

## Volatility and Variance
*   **Loose Deuces Variance:** ~74
*   **Super Aces Bonus Variance:** ~60

**Loose Deuces is significantly more volatile.** While Super Aces has its share of swings (waiting for those 160:1 Aces), the 500:1 payout in Loose Deuces is a much rarer event (1 in 4,800 hands). In Super Aces, you hit Four Aces approximately every **4,200 hands**, and because the base payouts (Full House/Flush) are better, your bankroll won't bleed out as fast.

## Strategy: The Trainer's Role
*   **Loose Deuces Strategy:** Highly aggressive. You often discard everything to hold a single Deuce.
*   **Super Aces Strategy:** Similar to Jacks or Better but with a massive emphasis on holding lone Aces. You will often break Two Pair to keep a single Ace.

**Pro Tip:** If you use a **Video Poker Trainer**, make sure to toggle between "Wild" and "Standard" modes. The logic for holding a "Kicker" in Super Aces is non-existent (you never hold a kicker with Aces), which simplifies the game compared to the wild-card logic of Loose Deuces.

## House Edge Analysis: Finding the Best Table
The 100.97% Loose Deuces and 99.94% Super Aces are rare. More commonly, you will find:
*   **Loose Deuces (98.2%):** Found when the Straight Flush or Wild Royal payouts are lowered.
*   **Super Aces (98.8%):** Found when the Full House/Flush drops to 7/5 or 6/5.

At these "Short Pay" levels, **Super Aces (98.8%) usually has a lower house edge** than the common version of Loose Deuces (98.2%).

## Which Game Should You Play?

**Choose Loose Deuces if:**
*   You found a machine that actually offers the 100.97% return.
*   You have a large bankroll and a "high-risk, high-reward" mindset.
*   You enjoy the flexibility of wild cards.

**Choose Super Aces Bonus if:**
*   You prefer "Natural" video poker (no wild cards).
*   You want a game that is almost break-even (99.94%) with lower volatility than Loose Deuces.
*   You love the thrill of seeing that fourth Ace pop up for a 160-to-1 win.

## Conclusion
While **Loose Deuces (100.97%)** is the king of theoretical returns, **Super Aces Bonus (99.94%)** is often the more practical choice for most players. It offers a near-perfect return with lower variance and a more logical strategy. However, if you have the skill and the bankroll, the 1% edge in Loose Deuces is the ultimate prize in the casino.
