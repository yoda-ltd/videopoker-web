# Loose Deuces vs. Sevens Wild: Which Has the Better House Edge?

For fans of wild-card video poker, the two heavy hitters are **Loose Deuces** and **Sevens Wild**. Both games use a 52-card deck where four cards are wild, but their pay tables and strategies differ significantly. This article analyzes the house edge and math to determine which game gives the player a better chance to win.

## Theoretical Return (EV) Comparison

### 1. Full Pay Loose Deuces
*   **Return (EV):** 100.97%
*   **House Edge:** -0.97% (Player Advantage)
*   **The Hook:** 500-for-1 payout on Four Deuces.

### 2. Full Pay Sevens Wild
*   **Return (EV):** 100.56%
*   **House Edge:** -0.56% (Player Advantage)
*   **The Hook:** 1,000-for-1 payout on Four Sevens (depending on the version, sometimes 400:1).

**The Verdict on EV:** Full Pay Loose Deuces (100.97%) is mathematically superior to Sevens Wild. However, Sevens Wild is often easier to find in modern casinos.

## Pay Table Math: The Wild Card Difference

In **Loose Deuces**, the focus is entirely on the "Deuce." Because the 2s are the lowest cards in the deck, they aren't "missed" as much in straights or flushes. 

In **Sevens Wild**, the 7 is a middle-value card. When you hold a 7 as a wild card, you are removing a crucial "connector" from the deck for straights (5-6-7-8-9).

### Full Pay Sevens Wild Table (100.56%)
*   Natural Royal: 800
*   **Four Sevens: 1000** (This is the "Full Pay" differentiator)
*   Wild Royal: 25
*   Five of a Kind: 15

If you find a Sevens Wild machine where Four Sevens only pays **400:1**, the EV drops to **99.1%**, making it much worse than even a "Short Pay" Loose Deuces machine.

## Volatility and Variance
*   **Loose Deuces Variance:** ~74
*   **Sevens Wild Variance:** ~115 (extremely high)

Sevens Wild is significantly more volatile than Loose Deuces. This is because a huge portion of the payback is tied up in the **1,000-to-1 Four Sevens** jackpot. You will go through your bankroll much faster in Sevens Wild while waiting for that rare 1-in-4,800 hand.

## Strategy: Which is Harder?

| Feature | Loose Deuces | Sevens Wild |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Hold** | Any Deuce | Any Seven |
| **Complexity** | High | Very High |
| **Strategy Shift** | Focus on 500:1 hits | Focus on 1000:1 hits |

**Strategy Note:** In Sevens Wild, the strategy for "Inside Straights" and "Double Inside Straights" is more complex because of where the 7 sits in the deck. Using a trainer is mandatory for both, but the learning curve for Sevens Wild is steeper.

## House Edge Analysis: The Reality Check
Most casinos do not offer the 100.97% Loose Deuces or the 100.56% Sevens Wild. 

*   **Common Loose Deuces (98.2%):** A house edge of 1.8%.
*   **Common Sevens Wild (97.5%):** A house edge of 2.5%.

In the "Short Pay" world, Loose Deuces usually maintains a slightly better return than Sevens Wild.

## Which Should You Play?

**Play Loose Deuces if:**
*   You want the highest possible theoretical return (100.97%).
*   You prefer a game where the wild card is at the "bottom" of the deck.
*   You have a moderate-to-large bankroll.

**Play Sevens Wild if:**
*   You are a "jackpot hunter" looking for that 1,000:1 payout without needing a Royal Flush.
*   You enjoy the highest possible volatility.
*   You have found a rare machine that actually pays 1,000-for-1 for the Four Sevens.

## Conclusion
While both games offer player-advantage versions, **Loose Deuces is the better overall game**. It has a higher EV (100.97% vs 100.56%) and lower variance (74 vs 115). Sevens Wild is a thrilling ride, but for serious players looking to grind out a profit, the math points squarely at the Deuces.
