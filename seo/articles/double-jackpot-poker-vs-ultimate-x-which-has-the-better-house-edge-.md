# Double Jackpot Poker vs. Ultimate X: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Double Jackpot Poker and Ultimate X are two of the most popular choices in modern casinos, but they represent two entirely different philosophies of video poker. Double Jackpot is a high-payout "base" game, while Ultimate X is a "feature" game that adds multipliers to your play.

To find the best house edge, you have to understand the "cost" of the multipliers.

## Double Jackpot Poker: The Pure Math Play

Double Jackpot is a standard "Bonus" style game that focuses on premiums for Four of a Kind hands involving face cards and Aces.

*   **Four Aces:** 160-for-1.
*   **Four J, Q, K:** 80-for-1.
*   **Two Pair:** 1-for-1.
*   **Theoretical RTP (Full Pay 9/6):** **99.63%**
*   **House Edge:** 0.37%

On a standalone 9/6 machine, Double Jackpot is one of the best bets in the casino.

## Ultimate X: The High-Octane Multiplier Game

Ultimate X is not a single game; it is a "skin" that can be applied to many different games, including Double Jackpot. When you play Ultimate X:
1.  You must bet **10 coins** per hand (instead of the usual 5).
2.  The extra 5 coins go toward "buying" multipliers.
3.  If you get a winning hand (like a Flush), your **next hand** will have a multiplier (e.g., 7x) applied to its win.

### The House Edge on Ultimate X
The theoretical RTP of Ultimate X is usually slightly **lower** than the base game it is attached to. 
*   **Ultimate X Double Jackpot (9/6):** **99.30%**
*   **House Edge:** 0.70%

While 99.30% is still a great return, you are paying a "premium" of about 0.33% for the excitement of the multipliers.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

### 1. Mathematical Return (House Edge)
**Double Jackpot (Standalone) is the superior mathematical bet.** 
A return of 99.63% is objectively better than 99.30%. Over thousands of hands, that 0.33% difference adds up to a significant amount of money.

### 2. Strategy Complexity
*   **Double Jackpot** uses standard draw poker strategy.
*   **Ultimate X strategy** is extremely complex. Because your current win creates a multiplier for the *future*, the strategy for "holding" cards changes based on whether you currently have an active multiplier. You need a specialized Ultimate X strategy sheet to play this game correctly; playing with standard strategy will drop your RTP by 2-3%.

### 3. Volatility (The "Rollercoaster")
*   **Double Jackpot** is high volatility due to the 1-for-1 Two Pair payout.
*   **Ultimate X** is "off the charts" volatility. You are betting 10 coins per hand, and your biggest wins only happen when you hit a strong hand (like Four of a Kind) while a 10x or 12x multiplier is active. You can go through a $500 bankroll in 15 minutes on a quarter Ultimate X machine if the multipliers don't line up.

## Verdict: Which Should You Play?

*   **Play Double Jackpot (Standalone) if** you are an advantage player looking for the lowest possible house edge and a more manageable level of volatility.
*   **Play Ultimate X if** you are a "jackpot hunter" who wants the chance to win $1,000 or more on a single hand. Just be sure you have a massive bankroll and the correct strategy sheet.
