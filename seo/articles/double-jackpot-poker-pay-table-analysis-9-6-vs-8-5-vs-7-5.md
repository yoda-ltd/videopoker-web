# Double Jackpot Poker Pay Table Analysis: 9/6 vs 8/5 vs 7/5

Double Jackpot Poker is a favorite among video poker enthusiasts who want the excitement of bonus payouts but prefer a game with lower volatility than Triple Double Bonus. As with all video poker, the "Return to Player" (RTP) is determined by the specific pay table on the machine.

In this analysis, we look at the common 9/6, 8/5, and 7/5 versions of Double Jackpot Poker to see which one offers the best mathematical value.

## The Key to Double Jackpot: Face Cards and Aces

In Double Jackpot Poker, the payouts for Four of a Kind are split into three tiers:
1.  **Four Aces:** 160-for-1.
2.  **Four Jacks, Queens, or Kings:** **80-for-1**.
3.  **Four 2s through 10s:** 50-for-1.

The "Double Jackpot" refers to the fact that the face cards pay significantly more than the lower ranks (80 vs 50). This makes it a great game for those who like holding high cards.

## The Variable Numbers: Full House and Flush

Casinos adjust the house edge by changing the payouts for the **Full House** and the **Flush**. These are the "9" and "6" in a 9/6 machine.

*   **9/6:** Full House (9), Flush (6)
*   **8/5:** Full House (8), Flush (5)
*   **7/5:** Full House (7), Flush (5)

## Return to Player (RTP) Comparison

| Version | Full House | Flush | Theoretical RTP | House Edge |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **9/6 (Full Pay)** | 9 | 6 | **99.63%** | 0.37% |
| **8/5** | 8 | 5 | **97.46%** | 2.54% |
| **7/5** | 7 | 5 | **96.41%** | 3.59% |

### 9/6 Double Jackpot (The Gold Standard)
The 9/6 version is one of the best games in the casino. A 99.63% RTP is extremely high, meaning you only "lose" 37 cents for every $100 you bet over the long run. If you find a 9/6 machine, you are playing a near "break-even" game that offers high excitement.

### 8/5 Double Jackpot
The 8/5 version is a significant step down. The 2% drop in RTP effectively **multiplies the house edge by seven times**. You will feel your bankroll disappearing much faster on an 8/5 machine, as the frequent wins on Full Houses and Flushes are worth less.

### 7/5 Double Jackpot
With a 96.41% RTP, the 7/5 version is one of the worst bets in video poker. At this level, the house edge is approaching that of many slot machines. You are better off looking for a standard Jacks or Better machine with a better pay table.

## Why Two Pair Still Pays 1:1

Like most "Bonus" variants, Double Jackpot Poker pays **1-for-1 on Two Pair**. 
This is the trade-off for the high Four of a Kind payouts. Because Two Pair happens often (once every 8 hands), paying only 1-for-1 instead of 2-for-1 increases the game's volatility. You will have more losing sessions, but your winning sessions will be much larger thanks to the Face Card bonuses.

## Strategy Adjustments

*   **On 9/6 Machines:** You can afford to be more aggressive with Flush draws because the 6-for-1 payout justifies the risk.
*   **On 7/5 Machines:** Flushes are undervalued. Your strategy should focus almost entirely on building pairs and high cards to hit the Double Jackpot bonuses.

## Summary Checklist

Before playing Double Jackpot Poker, look at the screen and check:
1.  **Full House:** Is it 9?
2.  **Flush:** Is it 6?
3.  **Four Jacks/Queens/Kings:** Does it pay 400 coins (for a 5-coin bet)?

**Verdict:** Only play **9/6 Double Jackpot Poker** if you want the best possible return. A 9/6 machine is a world-class gambling value. Anything lower (8/5 or 7/5) is a "sucker bet" that should be avoided in favor of better-paying machines nearby.
