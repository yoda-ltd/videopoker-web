# Common Mistakes to Avoid in Double Jackpot Poker Video Poker

Double Jackpot Poker (DJP) is a variation of Bonus Poker that focuses on high payouts for quads involving Kings or Jacks (the "Jackpot" cards) and Aces. Mastering the **Double Jackpot Poker strategy chart 2026** is essential for **EV Maximization** in this high-variance game. To learn **how to play video poker like a pro**, you must first eliminate these three common "Pro-Level" errors.

## Analysis: 3 Most Common "Pro-Level" Errors in Double Jackpot Poker

### 1. Misvaluing the Jackpot Cards (Jacks and Kings)
The most significant difference in DJP is that 4-of-a-kind involving Jacks or Kings pays a massive bonus, often 400 credits (on a 5-coin bet).

**The Error:** Treating a pair of Jacks the same as a pair of Queens.
**The Pro Reality:** In Double Jackpot Poker, a pair of Jacks has more "equity" than a pair of Queens or Kings (Wait, Kings also pay the bonus!). Let's be precise: **Rank-Based Priority** in 2026 dictates that you should prioritize the "Jackpot" quads (A, K, J) in your drawing strategy. If you have a pair of Jacks and a single Ace, the Jacks are more valuable due to the potential for the 400-credit payout.

### 2. Overvaluing the Straight vs. 3 to a Royal
Because Double Jackpot Poker is a bonus poker variant, the payout for a Straight is often lower than in standard Jacks or Better.

**The Error:** Keeping a "made" Straight (e.g., `8-9-10-J-Q`) instead of drawing to 3 to a Royal Flush.
**The Pro Reality:** In DJP, the **Expected Return Differential** between a small "made" hand like a straight and a three-card Royal Flush draw can be significant. If the Straight payout is only 4-to-1, the long-term EV of the Royal Flush draw is often higher. Pro players calculate this "Strategy Fine-Tuning" based on the specific pay table of the machine.

### 3. Ignoring the Ace for 4-of-a-Kind
In Double Jackpot Poker, Aces also have a bonus payout (800 credits), which is even higher than the Jacks or Kings.

**The Error:** Discarding a single Ace in a hand where you have two other high cards.
**The Pro Reality:** The Ace is the most valuable non-wild card in the deck. **Rank-Based Priority** in Double Jackpot Poker means you should always prioritize the Ace. The potential for the 800-credit quad Aces outweighs almost any other drawing combination.

## Hand-Ranking Priorities in Double Jackpot Poker
The "Hand-Ranking Priority" in Double Jackpot Poker is unique: **Aces > Kings = Jacks > Queens.** Because of the bonus payouts for these quads, your primary objective is to maximize the frequency of hands that include them. **Strategy Fine-Tuning** in Double Jackpot Poker requires a "Jackpot-Focused" mindset. If you want to play like a pro, you must be comfortable prioritizing these specific ranks over the standard "Jacks or Better" logic.
