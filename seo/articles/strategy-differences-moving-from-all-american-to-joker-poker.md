# Strategy Differences: Moving from All American to Joker Poker
Category: Comparison

Moving from **All American** to **Joker Poker** is a shift from a "Straight/Flush Heavy" variant to a "Wild Card" variant. This represents a significant **Strategic Divergence** in how you value connected cards versus wild card potential.

### The Comparison Matrix: All American vs. Joker Poker

| Feature | All American (9/6) | Joker Poker (Kings or Better) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.72% (Full Pay) | 100.64% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 60.0 | 15.5 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Straight/Flush Draws | Prioritize Joker Connectivity |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.72% (Player Edge) | -0.64% (Player Edge) |

### Strategic Divergence: Connectors vs. Wilds

In **All American**, you are looking for four-to-a-flush or four-to-a-straight, because these hands pay significantly more than in standard Jacks or Better. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through volume and connected draws. In **Joker Poker**, the Joker is the ultimate connector, leading to a much lower **Volatility Normalization** (15.5 vs 60.0).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 50% overlap. In All American, you will hold "Open-Ended Straight Draws" almost as if they were a pair. In Joker Poker, you only care about these draws if you have the Joker to complete them. This creates a shift in **Payout Compression** where the "Middle Tier" hands (Straights/Flushes) are the focus in All American, but the "Top Tier" hands (Five of a Kind/Wild Royal) are the focus in Joker Poker.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Straight/Flush Devaluation:** When moving to Joker Poker, you stop chasing every inside straight draw. You only chase them if the Joker is involved or if they are 4-to-a-Royal.
2.  **Joker Supremacy:** The Joker is never discarded. It is your most powerful tool for **EV Maximization**.
3.  **High Card Floor:** In All American, Jacks are valuable. In Joker Poker, the minimum for a return is often Kings or Better. This means you must discard Jacks and Queens more frequently.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games offer a player advantage in their full-pay versions, but the "feel" of the games is entirely different due to the variance of All American's straight-heavy paytable. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are elite choices for the professional player.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from All American to Loose Deuces
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **All American** to **Loose Deuces** is a massive leap in volatility and strategy. This shift represents moving from a game of "Connectivity" to a game of "Wild Jackpots," requiring a complete overhaul of your **Volatility Normalization** tactics.

### The Comparison Matrix: All American vs. Loose Deuces

| Feature | All American (9/6) | Loose Deuces (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.72% | 101.60% |
| **Variance Index** | 60.0 | 200.0+ |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Hold Connectors for Straights/Flushes | Discard Everything for Deuces |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.72% (Player Edge) | -1.60% (Player Edge) |

### Volatility Normalization: The Jackpot Simulation

In **Loose Deuces**, you are no longer playing a "strategic" game of poker in the sense of finding marginal EV in straight draws. You are hunting for the Four Deuces. This creates extreme **Payout Compression**. In All American, a "Flush" is your bread and butter; in Loose Deuces, it is a secondary concern.

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is extremely low (less than 10%). In All American, you would never discard a Straight or a Flush. In Loose Deuces, you break these hands if you have a single Deuce and can draw for more. This is the ultimate form of **EV Maximization** in high-variance wild card games.

### Critical Strategy Shifts
1.  **The Deuce is Everything:** Every Deuce is a keeper. Three Deuces is a massive hold, even if you have a "Natural" winner.
2.  **Connector Devaluation:** In All American, four-to-a-straight is gold. In Loose Deuces, it is worthless unless it is a 4-to-a-Royal with Deuces involved.
3.  **Variance Management:** Because of the Variance Index of 200+, you need a massive bankroll to withstand the swings. This is the core of **Volatility Normalization**.

A **video poker house edge analysis** indicates that while Loose Deuces has a significantly higher RTP, it is emotionally taxing. The **best video poker strategy comparison** for this transition is: Forget the straights; find the Deuces.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from All American to Sevens Wild
Category: Comparison

Moving from **All American** to **Sevens Wild** involves moving from a "Connectivity" variant to a "Wild Card" variant. While both have moderate volatility, Sevens Wild requires a shift from "Natural" straights to "Wild" combinations.

### The Comparison Matrix: All American vs. Sevens Wild

| Feature | All American (9/6) | Sevens Wild (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.72% | 100.56% |
| **Variance Index** | 60.0 | 32.0 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Straight/Flush Payouts | Prioritize Sevens as Wilds |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.72% (Player Edge) | -0.56% (Player Edge) |

### Strategic Divergence: The Power of the Seven

In All American, the number 7 is just another card in a sequence. In **Sevens Wild**, the number 7 becomes the most powerful card in the deck. This creates a shift in **EV Maximization**; you are no longer looking for "Natural" strength but for "Wild" connectivity.

**Strategy Overlap:** You can reuse about 40% of your knowledge. However, the presence of four wild cards means that hands like "Three of a Kind" occur much more frequently, leading to **Payout Compression** for lower-tier hands. You must adjust your **Volatility Normalization** to account for the fact that a "Full House" is common and no longer a "big" win.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Seven is Everything:** A single 7 is almost always held.
2.  **Breaking Natural Connections:** If holding a natural straight draw prevents you from drawing to a 7-heavy Wild Flush or Wild Royal, you break the draw. This is a major departure from All American.
3.  **Five of a Kind Target:** This is your new secondary target. In All American, your highest non-Royal is Four of a Kind. In Sevens Wild, Five of a Kind is a major goal.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games are highly rewarding for the expert player, but Sevens Wild provides a smoother ride with lower variance. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are top-tier choices for anyone looking for a player advantage.
