# The History and Evolution of All American Video Poker

All American Video Poker is a unique "high-volatility" variant that celebrates the American spirit of "Go Big or Go Home." Its history is a fascinating study of how changing a single payout level—the "Flush"—can completely transform the strategy and feel of a game.

## The "Straight Flush" Pioneer

Introduced in the early 1990s, All American was designed to be the "Aggressive" version of Jacks or Better. While standard games focused on the "Full House" and "Two Pair," All American shifted the prize pool toward the "Top" of the deck.

The game became famous for its **Massive Straight Flush** and **Flush** payouts. In an era where most machines paid 6-for-1 for a Flush, All American often paid 8-for-1 or even 10-for-1, creating a "patriotic" surge of excitement for mid-range hands.

## Evolution of the "8/8" Pay Table

The "All American" brand is synonymous with the "8/8" or "9/9" pay table. This refers to the payouts for the Straight, Flush, and Full House. By making the Straight and Flush pay the same amount as the Full House, the game completely altered the "Expected Value" (EV) of drawing for straights and flushes.

This was a radical evolution. In any other video poker game, drawing for an "Inside Straight" is a cardinal sin. in All American, because the payout is so high, it sometimes becomes the mathematically correct play.

## Essential Video Poker Terminology

For players exploring the All American way, these are the video poker terms every player should know:

*   **Bankroll:** All American is a "High Variance" game. Because the payouts for Two Pair and Full House are often reduced to compensate for the high Straight/Flush wins, your bankroll will experience massive swings.
*   **Action:** This is a "Fast Action" game. You are constantly chasing "Long-Shot" hands like the Straight Flush, making every hand a high-stakes moment.
*   **Full Pay:** A "Full Pay" All American machine is a professional's dream, often offering a return of 100.72%—putting the player in a state of mathematical advantage over the house.

## The Strategy Revolution

All American evolved a "Straight-Heavy" strategy. Players learned to prioritize "Flush Draws" and "Straight Draws" over "Low Pairs," a move that would be considered "crazy" in Jacks or Better. This "Break the Rules" mentality is what gave the game its "All American" identity.

## Legacy and the Global Market

While it bears a US-centric name, All American is actually one of the most popular variants in European online casinos (often powered by NetEnt or Playtech). It remains the go-to choice for players who are tired of the "grind" of standard poker and want to play for "The Big Hit."

The history of All American is a reminder that in video poker, a small change in the pay table can lead to a completely different world of strategy. It remains a cornerstone of the "Complete Video Poker Glossary."
