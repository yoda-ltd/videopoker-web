# Royal Aces vs. Super Double Double Bonus: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Comparing **Royal Aces** and **Super Double Double Bonus (SDDB)** is a comparison of two "Jackpot-Grinder" models. While Royal Aces targets a single rank (Aces) for massive payouts, SDDB targets several ranks (Aces, 2s, 3s, 4s) and adds a "Kicker" requirement to reach the highest rewards. To choose the right game, you must understand how **video poker fairness and regulation** ensures the integrity of these high-volatility mathematical models.

## The Mathematical Framework: Focus vs. Breadth

The house edge in these games is determined by the "Bonus Hit Frequency."

*   **Royal Aces:** Value is concentrated in hitting Four Aces (no kicker required). The edge comes from the player's ability to prioritize Aces above all else in their strategic model.
*   **Super Double Double Bonus:** Value is spread across multiple "Kicker-Dependent" quads. The edge comes from the player's ability to manage the increased complexity of holding the right kickers alongside their quads.
