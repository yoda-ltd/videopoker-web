# Hyper Bonus Pay Table Analysis: 9/6 vs 8/5 vs 7/5

Hyper Bonus Video Poker is a high-octane variant that combines the foundational strategy of Jacks or Better with a random or fixed multiplier mechanism that can skyrocket your winnings. For serious players, the primary question is always: "What is the EV (Expected Value) and how does the pay table impact my bottom line?"

In this article, we'll break down the **9/6**, **8/5**, and **7/5** pay tables and how the Hyper Bonus mechanics (up to 8x multipliers) change the math of the game.

## Understanding the Hyper Bonus Pay Table
In standard Jacks or Better, a "9/6" table means you get 9 coins for a Full House and 6 coins for a Flush (per coin bet).

| Hand | 9/6 Table | 8/5 Table | 7/5 Table |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Full House | 9x | 8x | 7x |
| Flush | 6x | 5x | 5x |
| **Total RTP (Standard)** | **99.54%** | **97.30%** | **96.15%** |

In Hyper Bonus, however, the **multi-level multipliers (2x, 4x, 8x levels)** are applied to these base numbers. The stakes are much higher because a small change in the pay table is multiplied by the bonus.

## The Multiplier Impact: 2x, 4x, 8x levels
Hyper Bonus rewards players who hit winning hands consecutively or buy-in for a bonus round. Let's see how a "Royal Flush" (typically 800x) looks at an 8x level:
- **Royal Flush (1x):** 4,000 coins (Max Bet)
- **Royal Flush (8x):** 32,000 coins (Max Bet)

While the big wins get the headlines, the real difference is in the common hands like a Full House.
- **9/6 Full House at 8x:** 72 coins.
- **7/5 Full House at 8x:** 56 coins.

You lose **16 coins** on a single hand just by choosing a 7/5 table over a 9/6 table! Over an hour of play (600+ hands), this "small" difference can cost you hundreds of dollars in EV.

## Strategy: Advancing Through Levels vs Single-Hand Play
To truly master Hyper Bonus, you need to think like a professional. Standard Jacks or Better strategy is not enough.

1.  **Level 1 Strategy:** Your goal is to trigger the "Hyper" mode. This means you might play more conservatively, holding a pair of Jacks instead of chasing a long-shot straight flush draw.
2.  **Hyper Mode Strategy:** Once the 8x multiplier is active, you apply a **high limit multiplier strategy**. Your goal shifts to maximum EV for that specific hand. This is the moment to take aggressive draws for a Royal or Quads because the payoff is massive.

## 9/6 vs 8/5 vs 7/5: Which is Best for Hyper Bonus?
-   **9/6 Hyper Bonus:** The ideal table. If you find this at a casino, stick to it. The RTP is typically over **99.5%**, giving you the best chance of a winning session.
-   **8/5 Hyper Bonus:** A significant drop. The house edge increases by about 2%. This is standard for most Las Vegas Strip casinos.
-   **7/5 Hyper Bonus:** A "bankroll killer." The house edge is too high for the volatility of Hyper Bonus. You'll likely see your money disappear before you can hit a big multiplier win.
