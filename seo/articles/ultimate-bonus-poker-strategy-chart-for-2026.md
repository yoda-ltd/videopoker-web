## Analysis: 3 Most Common "Pro-Level" Errors in Bonus Poker

### 1. Miscalculating EV on Aces vs. Other Quads
The most significant difference in Bonus Poker is the tiered payout for 4-of-a-kind. Aces pay 80 credits (on a 5-coin bet), 2s-4s pay 40, and 5s-Ks pay 25.

**The Error:** Treating all 4-of-a-kind draws with the same weight.
**The Pro Reality:** In Bonus Poker, a single Ace has more "equity" than a single King or Queen. **Rank-Based Priority** in 2026 dictates that you should prioritize the "Bonus" quads (A, 2, 3, 4) in your drawing strategy. If you have two high cards and one of them is an Ace, the Ace is much more valuable due to the potential for the 80-credit payout.

### 2. Overvaluing the Two Pair Hand
In some video poker variants, Two Pair is a cornerstone of the player's return. In Bonus Poker, the payout is 2-to-1, which is the same as in Jacks or Better.

**The Error:** Keeping Two Pair instead of a three-card Royal Flush draw.
**The Pro Reality:** In Bonus Poker, the **Expected Return Differential** between a "made" Two Pair and a high-potential draw like a three-card Royal Flush can be narrow. Pro players use **Strategy Fine-Tuning** to recognize when the long-term equity of the Royal Flush outweighs the short-term safety of the Two Pair payout.

### 3. Mismanaging 3 to a Straight Flush
Many players discard three-card straight flush draws in favor of a single high card.

**The Error:** Holding an Ace and discarding `4-5-6` of Spades.
**The Pro Reality:** A three-card straight flush draw has a higher EV than a single high card in Bonus Poker. The potential for a straight, a flush, or a straight flush (the "Royal's little brother") is too valuable to ignore. **Rank-Based Priority** in 2026 dictates keeping the draw.
