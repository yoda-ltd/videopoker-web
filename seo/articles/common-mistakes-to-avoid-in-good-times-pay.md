# Common Mistakes to Avoid in Good Times Pay

Good Times Pay is a video poker variant that adds a multiplier feature to the game. While the prospect of multiplying your wins is exciting, the added cost and complexity lead to several common player mistakes.

### Ignoring the Extra Bet
To activate the "Good Times" multiplier feature, you must place an extra bet (usually 10 coins total instead of 5). The most common mistake is playing the machine without this extra bet. If you don't bet the full amount, you are playing a standard game on a machine that often has a lower-than-average base **Theoretical Return**. This makes it a losing proposition.

### Bankroll Mismanagement
The multiplier feature increases the **Variance** of the game significantly. You will have more losing sessions, but your winning sessions will be larger. For **Session Sustainability**, you need a much larger bankroll for **Variance Buffering** than you would for a standard game. If you don't have enough units to weather the "dry spells" between multipliers, you will bust out.

### "Is Max Bet Always Better for Bankroll Longevity?"
In Good Times Pay, there is no middle ground. You must play the maximum bet (including the multiplier bet) to get the advertised RTP. If the cost is too high for your bankroll, move to a lower denomination. For those looking for **video poker tips for beginners**, our number one tip for Good Times Pay is: play it at the 10-coin level or not at all.

### Strategy Confusion
Many players use a standard Jacks or Better strategy for Good Times Pay. This is a mistake. The multiplier feature changes the value of certain hands, and your **Hit Frequency Optimization** must reflect that. To learn **how to win video poker daily** on a Good Times machine, you must use a strategy specifically designed for this variant.

### Misunderstanding the RNG
Some players believe the multiplier is "due" to appear after a long losing streak. This is a misunderstanding of **Pseudo-Randomness**. The multiplier is determined by the RNG independently for each hand. There is no pattern, and the machine doesn't "know" you're having a bad time.

Good Times Pay can lead to great results if you play it correctly. Avoid these mistakes, and the good times will eventually roll.
