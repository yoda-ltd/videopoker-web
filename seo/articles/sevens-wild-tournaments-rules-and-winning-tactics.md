# Sevens Wild Tournaments: Rules and Winning Tactics

Video poker tournaments are becoming increasingly popular in both online and land-based casinos, and Sevens Wild is one of the most exciting variants for these events. The addition of all four Sevens (7s) as wild cards increases the frequency of big hands, which can be the difference between winning a prize and going home empty-handed.

## Understanding the Tournament Rules
In a Sevens Wild tournament, you typically have a set amount of time (e.g., 15 minutes) or a set number of hands (e.g., 200 hands) to play. The player with the highest total score at the end wins the top prize.

Unlike regular video poker, where you are playing against the house and trying to maximize your return to player (RTP), in a tournament, you are playing against other players. This means your strategy must adapt to the scoreboard.

## Winning Tactics for Sevens Wild Tournaments
### 1. The Power of the Sevens
In a tournament, the Sevens are more than just wild cards; they're your ticket to high-point hands. 
*   **Always Hold All Sevens:** This should go without saying, but in the heat of a tournament, never discard a Seven.
*   **Prioritize Big Hands:** In standard Sevens Wild, you might play conservatively for a Three of a Kind or higher to preserve your bankroll. In a tournament, however, a Three of a Kind won't help you much. You need to chase the **Natural Royal Flush** and **Five of a Kind** to climb the leaderboard.

### 2. High-Speed Play
Speed is often a crucial factor in tournaments. If you have a time limit, you need to play as many hands as possible. This is where **video poker training tools** become invaluable. These tools help you internalize optimal Sevens Wild strategy so you can make split-second decisions without sacrificing accuracy.

### 3. Strategy Adjustments for Tournaments
*   **Ignore the Bankroll:** In a tournament, your credits have no cash value. Don't be afraid to take risks that you wouldn't take in a regular cash game.
*   **Focus on the Big Win:** If you are behind on the leaderboard, you must be more aggressive. For example, you might discard a winning Three of a Kind if you have three cards to a Natural Royal Flush.
*   **Watch the Clock:** If you have only a few minutes left and are nowhere near the leaders, you must go for the long shots (like drawing to a Royal) every single time.

## Sevens Wild vs. Other Tournament Variants
Many players prefer Sevens Wild tournaments over Jacks or Better because the four wild cards increase the "volatility" and allow for bigger point jumps. Hitting a **Wild Royal Flush** (worth 25 points/coins) or **Five of a Kind** (worth 15 points/coins) can propel you into the lead instantly.
