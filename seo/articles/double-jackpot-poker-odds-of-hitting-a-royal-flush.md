# Double Jackpot Poker Odds of Hitting a Royal Flush

Double Jackpot Poker is a popular video poker variant that belongs to the "Bonus" family. It offers specialized payouts for Four of a Kind hands involving face cards and Aces. But how do these specialized rules affect the most coveted hand in the game: the Royal Flush?

## The Baseline Odds

Like all standard 52-card video poker games, the probability of being dealt a Royal Flush in your initial five cards is exactly:

**1 in 649,740**

However, video poker is a game of draws. Your actual odds of hitting a Royal Flush during play depend on your strategy.

## Royal Flush Frequency in Double Jackpot Poker

On a Full Pay Double Jackpot Poker machine, using perfect strategy, you will hit a Royal Flush approximately **once every 40,000 to 45,000 hands.**

This frequency is very similar to Jacks or Better. Unlike Double Bonus or Double Double Bonus, where the aggressive pursuit of Aces can slightly lower the frequency of Royal Flushes, Double Jackpot strategy is more "balanced."

## Odds of Completing the Royal Flush

When you are dealt a partial Royal Flush, your odds of completing it on the draw are:

1.  **4 Cards to a Royal:** You need 1 specific card out of the 47 remaining. 
    *   **Odds: 1 in 47**
2.  **3 Cards to a Royal:** You need 2 specific cards. 
    *   **Odds: 1 in 1,081**
3.  **2 Cards to a Royal:** You need 3 specific cards. 
    *   **Odds: 1 in 16,215**

## Strategy: Royal Flush vs. Face Card Bonuses

Double Jackpot Poker is unique because it pays a "Double Jackpot" for Four of a Kind hands involving Jack, Queen, or King, and a separate bonus for Aces.

*   **Four Aces:** 160-for-1
*   **Four Jacks, Queens, or Kings:** **80-for-1**
*   **Other Four of a Kind:** 50-for-1

### The Conflict
Because the payouts for high face cards (J, Q, K) are so high (80-for-1 vs. 25-for-1 in standard games), you will hold onto them more often. 
*   **In Jacks or Better:** You might discard a single Jack to draw for a better hand.
*   **In Double Jackpot:** You will almost always hold that Jack. 

This "High Card" focus actually **helps** your Royal Flush frequency. Because you are holding onto Jacks, Queens, Kings, and Aces more often to chase the "Double Jackpot" bonuses, you are naturally in a better position to complete a Royal Flush if the right cards come up on the draw.

## Payout and RTP Impact

In a standard 9/6 Double Jackpot game (99.63% RTP), the Royal Flush accounts for approximately **1.95%** of the total return.

This means if you play 5 coins (and get the 4,000-coin Royal Flush bonus), the Royal Flush is a critical part of your long-term success. If you only play 1-4 coins, the Royal Flush payout drops to 250-for-1, and your total RTP drops by over 1%, making the game much harder to beat.

## Summary: Odds at a Glance

| Scenario | Odds |
| :--- | :--- |
| Dealt naturally | 1 in 649,740 |
| Overall (Perfect Strategy) | **~1 in 42,000** |
| Drawing to 4-card Royal | 1 in 47 |
| Drawing to 3-card Royal | 1 in 1,081 |

**The Bottom Line:** Double Jackpot Poker is a great game for Royal Flush hunters. The strategy naturally encourages you to hold the very cards (A, K, Q, J) that lead to a Royal Flush, while providing high-paying "consolation prizes" in the form of Double Jackpot bonuses on face cards. Always play max coins to ensure you get the full value for the ultimate hand!
