# Triple Aces vs. Royal Flush Bonus: Which Has the Better House Edge?
Category: Comparison Guide

Choosing between **Triple Aces** and **Royal Flush Bonus** (often part of the "Bonus Poker" family) is a choice between two different philosophies of video poker. One rewards the "frequent" big hand (Triple/Quad Aces), while the other focuses on the "ultimate" hand (The Royal Flush). This **triple aces payout analysis** will break down the house edge, variance, and strategic differences between these two popular variants.

## The Core Philosophy: "The Grind" vs. "The Jackpot"

The primary difference lies in where the casino "hides" its return.

1.  **Triple Aces:** Shifts value into the **3-of-a-kind Aces** (the "Triple Aces bump") and the **Quad Aces** (the 2,000-coin bonus). It is a game for players who want to see their bankroll jump frequently.
2.  **Royal Flush Bonus:** As the name suggests, this variant adds a "progressive" or "static" bonus to the Royal Flush, sometimes pushing the payout from 4,000 coins to 5,000 or even 8,000+ coins.

## House Edge Analysis: The Numbers

To determine which has the better house edge, we must look at the "base" pay table (the Full House/Flush payouts).

### Typical Return to Player (RTP) Comparison
| Variant | 9/6 Table RTP | 8/5 Table RTP |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Triple Aces | ~99.4% | ~97.5% |
| Royal Flush Bonus | ~99.1% (Base) | ~97.2% (Base) |

*Note: Royal Flush Bonus RTP increases as the bonus grows. If the Royal Flush pays 8,000 coins, the RTP can exceed 100%!*

**The Verdict:** In a standard "static" environment, **Triple Aces usually has a slightly better base house edge** than Royal Flush Bonus. However, if the Royal Flush Bonus is part of a progressive jackpot that has climbed significantly, the Royal Flush Bonus becomes the mathematically superior game.

## Variance and Short-Term Bankroll

This is where the **triple aces payout analysis** really matters for the average player.

*   **Triple Aces (Lower Variance):** Because you hit 3-of-a-kind and 4-of-a-kind Aces much more often than you hit a Royal Flush, Triple Aces feels "smoother." The 45-60 coin payout for Triple Aces helps sustain your session.
*   **Royal Flush Bonus (Extreme Variance):** This game is the definition of "top-heavy." You are playing with a worse base return for the *tiny* chance of hitting a massive Royal Flush bonus. Your bankroll will likely bleed out much faster here than in Triple Aces.

## Strategic Implications: Aces-Heavy EV

*   **Triple Aces Strategy:** You must play an "Aces-heavy" strategy. You will break Full Houses (if they include a pair of Aces) or Two Pair more often to chase the Triple/Quad Ace payout. 
*   **Royal Flush Bonus Strategy:** Your strategy remains close to standard Jacks or Better, with a slightly higher emphasis on holding suited high cards to chase the Royal. You rarely "break" strong hands to chase a Royal unless the bonus is astronomically high.

## Conclusion: Which Should You Choose?

*   **Choose Triple Aces if:** You want a longer play session and enjoy the frequent "mini-jackpots" associated with Aces. It is the better "daily driver" for the serious video poker enthusiast.
*   **Choose Royal Flush Bonus if:** You are "swinging for the fences." This is a "lottery-style" video poker game. If the jackpot is high enough, it’s worth a few rounds, but it shouldn't be your primary game for bankroll longevity.

In the end, the **triple aces payout analysis** favors the player who prefers consistent, high-value hits over the "once-in-a-lifetime" score. Triple Aces provides a balanced, engaging experience that rewards skillful "Aces-heavy" play.
