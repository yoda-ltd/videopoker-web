# Five Deck Poker vs. Hot Roll Poker: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Comparing **Five Deck Poker** and **Hot Roll Poker** is a study in how mathematical "Add-Ons" affect your long-term return. While Five Deck Poker changes the fundamental probability of the deck, Hot Roll Poker adds a random multiplier stage to a standard poker hand. To choose the right game, you must understand how **video poker fairness and regulation** ensures the integrity of these specialized "Enhanced" models.

## The Mathematical Framework: Parallel Decks vs. Random Multipliers

The house edge in these games is determined by how the "Bonus" value is distributed.

*   **Five Deck Poker:** Uses five separate 52-card decks for the draw stage. This allows for "Five of a Kind" and provides a higher strategic ceiling. The bonus is "intrinsic" to the card math.
*   **Hot Roll Poker:** Uses a standard 52-card deck but occasionally triggers a virtual dice roll after a win to multiply your payout. The bonus is "extrinsic" to the card math.
