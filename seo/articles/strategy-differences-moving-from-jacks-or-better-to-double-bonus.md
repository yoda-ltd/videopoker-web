# Strategy Differences: Moving from Jacks or Better to Double Bonus Poker

Transitioning from the industry standard Jacks or Better (JoB) to Double Bonus Poker is a classic step for players looking for more volatility and higher payouts on premium hands. However, failing to account for **Strategy Drift** during this transition can lead to significant **EV Erosion**. This guide provides the best video poker strategy comparison to help you understand how to play video poker like a pro when moving between these two popular variants.

## The Jacks or Better Baseline

In Jacks or Better, the strategy is balanced and focuses on the high frequency of "push" hands (jacks or better pair). When moving to Double Bonus, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** must undergo a specific recalibration. 

The primary change in the pay table is the increased payout for Four of a Kind (specifically Aces), but this comes at the cost of a reduced payout for Two Pair (usually 1-for-1 instead of 2-for-1). This single change in the pay table forces a massive shift in how you value drawing hands.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

When transitioning, you'll find that **80% of your JoB strategy stays the same**. You are still holding onto high cards, pairs, and obvious four-card flushes or straights. However, the **20% that changes** is where most players lose their bankroll.

### The 20% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Two Pair Devaluation:** In JoB, Two Pair is a solid winning hand. In Double Bonus, it's just a return of your bet. You should no longer treat it as a "safe" harbor.
2.  **Straight Draw Aggression:** Double Bonus pays more for a Straight (usually 5-for-1 vs 4-for-1). This makes open-ended straight draws more valuable than they are in JoB.
3.  **Full House vs. Flush:** Depending on the specific pay table (e.g., 10/7 vs 9/6), the value of holding 3-to-a-flush vs 3-to-a-straight-flush shifts significantly.

## Volatility Scaling

Double Bonus introduces **Volatility Scaling**. While JoB is relatively "low and slow," Double Bonus is designed to have deeper swings. To play like a pro, you must accept that you will go longer periods without a significant win, compensated by the massive 160-for-1 or 400-for-1 payouts on Four Aces.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Be more aggressive with four-card straights.
*   **Modify:** De-prioritize Two Pair in your mental "strength" map.
*   **Target:** Keep a sharp eye on lone Aces; their value in a potential 4-of-a-kind is much higher here than in JoB.

By mastering these subtle shifts, you ensure that your transition between variants is profitable rather than a drain on your resources.
