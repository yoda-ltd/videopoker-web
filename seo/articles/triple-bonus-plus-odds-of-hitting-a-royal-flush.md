## The Probability of a Royal Flush

In a standard 52-card deck, there are 2,598,960 possible five-card hands. Out of these, only four are Royal Flushes (one for each suit). This means the raw odds of being dealt a Royal Flush on the initial draw are 1 in 649,740.

However, video poker isn't a single-draw game. You have the chance to discard and draw new cards. In a typical Jacks or Better game, the odds of hitting a Royal Flush (with perfect strategy) are approximately **1 in 40,391 hands**.

Why do the odds increase? Because you are more likely to break a potential Royal Flush draw (like holding a 10, J, Q of the same suit) to hold a pair of Aces, which has a significant bonus payout in this game.

## The Strategy: To Draw or Not to Draw?

The **high volatility video poker strategy** of Triple Bonus Plus dictates how you should chase a Royal Flush. Here are some key strategy points:

1.  **Hold Three to a Royal:** If you have three cards to a Royal Flush, you should almost always hold them and discard the other two. This is standard across most video poker games.
2.  **Aces vs. Royal Draws:** This is where Triple Bonus Plus gets tricky. If you have a single Ace that is part of a potential Royal Flush (e.g., A, K, Q of Spades) along with another Ace of a different suit (a pair of Aces), the correct play in Triple Bonus Plus is often to hold the pair of Aces. The potential 800-coin bonus for Quad Aces is too valuable to ignore.
3.  **Four to a Royal:** This is a "no-brainer." Always hold four cards to a Royal Flush, even if it means breaking a made Straight or Flush. The 800-to-1 payout (4,000 coins on a 5-coin bet) is far superior to the smaller payouts.
