# Keno Poker: Odds of Hitting a Royal Flush

The ultimate goal in any Keno Poker variant is the Royal Flush. However, in Keno Poker, the addition of Keno-style side bets creates a more complex set of probabilities. This guide breaks down the **Drawing Probability** of the Royal Flush and how the Keno component affects your overall game strategy.

## Royal Flush Odds: The Poker Base

In Keno Poker, the base game uses a standard 52-card deck. The odds of being dealt a Royal Flush on the initial 5-card deal are:
- **Odds:** 1 in 649,740.

With optimal play (holding the correct cards and discarding the rest), the **Drawing Probability** of hitting a Royal Flush in Keno Poker is approximately 1 in 40,000 hands. This is the same as in traditional video poker, but the *value* of the Royal Flush is different because of the Keno side bet.

## The Keno Factor: Hybrid RTP Analysis

Keno Poker machines allow you to select "Keno spots" (numbers) before each deal. When you hit a Royal Flush, the cards in your hand are often cross-referenced with your Keno spots. This leads to a **Hybrid RTP Analysis** that incorporates both the poker payout and the Keno bonus.

### Keno Multipliers and Side Bets
- **Multipliers:** If you hit a Royal Flush and your selected Keno spots also match the numbers on the cards, the payout can be multiplied by 5x, 10x, or even 100x.
- **Keno Bonus:** Some Keno Poker variants award a massive bonus for hitting a Royal Flush while matching all of your Keno spots. This drastically reduces the **Cross-Game Variance** by providing a massive, multi-tiered jackpot.
