# Ultimate Aces and Faces Strategy Chart for 2026

Aces and Faces is a game that rewards those who understand the value of high-card Quads. By shifting the payout structure to favor Aces, Kings, Queens, and Jacks, this variant demands a subtle shift in strategy compared to traditional Jacks or Better. This 2026 guide breaks down the optimal holds and the **Pay Table Verification** steps required to find the **best odds for Aces and Faces**.

## The Aces and Faces Strategy Hierarchy

This strategy is designed for the 8/5 "Full Pay" version of the game.

1.  **Royal Flush, Straight Flush.**
2.  **4 Aces:** The highest payout below the Royal and Straight Flush (usually 80-for-1).
3.  **4 Kings, Queens, or Jacks:** High-tier quads (usually 40-for-1).
4.  **4 to a Royal Flush.**
5.  **4 of a Kind (2s through 10s):** Standard quad payout (25-for-1).
6.  **Full House, Flush, Straight.**
7.  **3 of a Kind:** A powerful draw, especially when holding Aces or Face cards.
8.  **4 to a Straight Flush.**
9.  **Two Pair:** Prioritize keeping these, but be aware of the "Quad potential" of the higher pair.
10. **Pair of Aces:** Higher priority than other High Pairs due to the 80-for-1 Quad potential.
11. **High Pair (K, Q, J).**
12. **3 to a Royal Flush.**
13. **4 to a Flush.**
14. **Low Pair (2s through 10s).**

## Understanding the Volatility Index

Aces and Faces sits comfortably between Jacks or Better and the high-variance "Double Bonus" games. Its **Volatility Index** is moderate. You won't experience the extreme "feast or famine" cycles of Double Double Bonus, but you will see more variation than in Tens or Better. The game is balanced around the fact that you get a "bonus" for high-rank quads, but you "pay" for it with slightly lower payouts for Full Houses or Flushes.
