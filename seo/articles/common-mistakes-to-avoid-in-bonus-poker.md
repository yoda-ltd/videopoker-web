# Common Mistakes to Avoid in Bonus Poker: Professional Grinder's Warning

Bonus Poker is the workhorse of the professional video poker world. Its deceptive similarity to Jacks or Better (JoB) leads many players to treat it with a "close enough" strategy, which is the fastest way to bleed EV. To truly master **how to play video poker like a pro**, one must understand that the "Bonus" in the name isn't just for show—it requires a fundamental shift in **EV Maximization** and **Hand-Ranking Inversion**.

## Analysis: The "JoB-Clone" Trap and Other Errors

### 1. The "Generic High Pair" Fallacy: Treating Aces Like Kings
The most common mistake for players transitioning from Jacks or Better is failing to differentiate between Aces and other High Pairs.
- **The Error:** Holding a pair of Kings with the same priority as a pair of Aces.
- **The Pro Reality:** In Bonus Poker, Quad Aces pay 80:1, while Quad Kings pay only 25:1. 
- **The Strategy Tip:** The "Expected Value" (EV) of a pair of Aces is significantly higher. In certain scenarios, a professional will hold a lone Ace over a "gapped" 4-to-a-Straight, a play that would be a mistake in JoB. This is a subtle but critical **Hand-Ranking Inversion**.

### 2. The "Kicker Logic" Carryover: Holding Unnecessary Cards
Players coming from Double Double Bonus (DDB) often carry over "Kicker Logic" where it doesn't belong.
- **The Error:** Holding an Ace or 2 as a "kicker" with three Aces or three 2s.
- **The Pro Reality:** There is no kicker bonus in standard Bonus Poker. Quad Aces pay 80:1 regardless of the fifth card.
- **The Math:** Holding a kicker reduces your draw space and lowers your probability of hitting the fourth matching rank. The professional rule is simple: **In Bonus Poker, never hold a kicker.**

### 3. Miscalculating the "2 Pair" Engine: The Volatility Misjudgment
Bonus Poker is often perceived as a "low variance" game, leading to bankroll complacency.
- **The Error:** Playing with a bankroll that is too small (e.g., 500 - 1,000 units).
- **The Pro Reality:** While more stable than DDB, Bonus Poker still features a "lumpy" paytable due to the 80:1 and 40:1 quads. 
- **The Strategy Tip:** To maintain **Session Sustainability**, a professional maintains a bankroll of at least 3,000 units. This "Variance Buffering" is essential to survive the "Aces Droughts" that characterize the Bonus Poker experience.

## EV Maximization and Paytable Awareness

A professional's edge is found in the "thinnest" of margins. 
- **Mistake:** Playing on "Short Pay" machines (like 7/5 or 6/5 Bonus Poker).
- **The Consequence:** The difference between 8/5 Bonus Poker (99.17%) and 7/5 is nearly 1%. For a pro playing 800 hands an hour at $1 denomination, that 1% error costs $40 per hour.
- **Expert Video Poker Strategy Tip:** "How to play video poker like a pro" starts before you even sit down. If the machine doesn't offer the "Full Pay" 8/5 (or better) table, the pro walks away.

## Analysis of Hand-Ranking Inversion: Bonus vs. JoB

In Bonus Poker, the premium on 2s, 3s, and 4s (40:1) leads to a specific inversion.
- **The Decision:** You are dealt a pair of 2s and two high cards (like J-Q).
- **The Pro Play:** In Bonus Poker, the **Pair of 2s** takes precedence.
- **The Reasoning:** In JoB, the high cards might be the superior play to stay in "High Pair" territory. In Bonus Poker, the 40:1 Quad potential of the 2s provides a higher long-term **Theoretical Return**.
