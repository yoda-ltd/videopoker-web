# Double Down Stud vs. Royal Aces: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Double Down Stud and Royal Aces (a variant of Bonus Poker) offer two very different approaches to video poker. Double Down Stud is a betting-focused game with no draws, while Royal Aces is a specialized game that pays a massive premium for a specific type of Four of a Kind: Aces.

Which one should you choose for the best mathematical return?

## Royal Aces: The "Ace-Centric" Bonus Game

Royal Aces is a high-volatility variant that takes the "Bonus Poker" concept and pushes it to the limit. 

*   **Four Aces:** Typically pays **400-for-1** (2,000 coins for 5). This is the same as the "Four Aces with a Kicker" payout in Double Double Bonus, but without the kicker requirement!
*   **Other Four of a Kind:** Pays a standard 25-for-1.
*   **Two Pair:** Pays **2-for-1**.
*   **Full Pay RTP (9/6):** **99.4%**
*   **House Edge:** 0.6%

The defining feature of Royal Aces is that it rewards you massively for hitting Four Aces, but because Two Pair still pays 2-for-1, it is much less "punishing" than other bonus games.

## Double Down Stud: The Betting Strategy Game

In Double Down Stud, you see four cards and decide whether to double your bet before the fifth card is revealed. There are no draws.

*   **Top Payout:** Royal Flush (800-for-1).
*   **Full Pay RTP (9/6):** **98.49%**
*   **House Edge:** 1.51%

## Comparing the House Edge

### 1. Theoretical Return (RTP)
In a head-to-head comparison of Full Pay machines, **Royal Aces is the superior game**. 
*   Royal Aces: **99.4% RTP**
*   Double Down Stud: **98.49% RTP**

A 0.9% difference is significant. In Royal Aces, you are playing a game with a house edge of about half a percent. In Double Down Stud, the house edge is triple that.

### 2. Volatility and Bankroll
*   **Royal Aces** has high volatility because a large portion of the return is tied up in that rare 400-for-1 Aces payout. However, the 2-for-1 payout on Two Pair helps keep you afloat.
*   **Double Down Stud** volatility comes from the "Double Down" mechanic. Because you are often betting 10 coins instead of 5, your bankroll can swing wildly depending on whether those fifth cards hit or miss.

### 3. Strategy Complexity
*   **Royal Aces strategy** is relatively simple. It is very similar to Bonus Poker, but you are even more aggressive about holding a single Ace or a pair of Aces.
*   **Double Down Stud strategy** is the simplest in the casino. You don't have to worry about discards; you just have to know when the math justifies doubling your bet.

## Verdict: Which Should You Play?

*   **Play Royal Aces if** you want the best possible return. A 99.4% RTP is an excellent game that can provide hours of play and the chance for a huge 2,000-coin "mini-jackpot" on Four Aces.
*   **Play Double Down Stud if** you are tired of the "hold and draw" mechanic of standard video poker and want a fast-paced game where you are "betting" rather than "playing." 

**Pro Tip:** Be careful with the pay tables! Many Royal Aces machines are "short-pay" (e.g., 8/5 or 7/5), which drops the RTP to 97% or lower. If you find a short-pay Royal Aces machine and a Full Pay (9/6) Double Down Stud machine, the **Double Down Stud machine actually becomes the better mathematical bet.** Always look at the Full House and Flush payouts before you start!
