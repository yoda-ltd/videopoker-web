## Skill vs. Chance: The Strategy Divide

### Multi-Strike vs. Blackjack
Both Multi-Strike Poker and Blackjack are "skill-based" games where player decisions directly impact the outcome. 
- **The Similarities**: In both games, you can follow a "basic strategy" to minimize the house edge. For Multi-Strike, this involves a specialized **multi strike video poker strategy** that prioritizes "Survival Play" at Level 1 to reach the 8x multiplier at Level 4.
- **The Differences**: Blackjack is a one-on-one game against the dealer, where the odds are fairly consistent. Multi-Strike, however, is a tiered game where the **Ladder EV** shifts with each level. A Royal Flush at Level 4 (8x multiplier) is a massive 32,000-coin jackpot, something you'll never see in a standard game of Blackjack.

### Multi-Strike vs. Craps
Craps is a fast-paced game of pure chance, while Multi-Strike is a skill-based video poker variant.
- **The Similarities**: Both games offer a "thrill ride" with high volatility. In Craps, a "Hot Hand" can lead to massive wins. In Multi-Strike, a "Free Ride" or a "Bonus Streak" can trigger huge payouts at the 4x and 8x levels.
- **The Differences**: Craps is a community game with dozens of bet types, most of which have a high house edge. Multi-Strike is a solitary game where, with perfect play on a 9/6 (Full House/Flush) paytable, the house edge can be as low as 0.3%.
