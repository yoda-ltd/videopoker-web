# Triple Bonus Plus vs. Super Double Double Bonus: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Comparing **Triple Bonus Plus** and **Super Double Double Bonus (SDDB)** is a comparison of tiered rank bonuses against high-payout kicker-logic. While Triple Bonus Plus rewards you for hitting high-ranking quads across multiple ranks, SDDB adds a "Kicker" requirement to reach its massive top payouts. To choose the right game, you must understand how **video poker fairness and regulation** ensures the integrity of these specialized high-volatility models.

## The Mathematical Framework: Ranks vs. Kickers

The house edge in these games is determined by the "Path to the Jackpot."

*   **Triple Bonus Plus:** Value is concentrated in the quads themselves. You receive tiered bonuses (e.g., 120-for-1 for Aces) regardless of the fifth card. The edge is tied to your ability to prioritize the correct ranks in your strategic holds.
*   **Super Double Double Bonus:** Value is concentrated in "Kicker-Dependent" quads. You receive the highest payouts (e.g., 2,000 coins for 4 Aces) only if you hold a valid kicker (2, 3, or 4). The edge is tied to your mastery of the complex kicker strategy and bankroll management.
