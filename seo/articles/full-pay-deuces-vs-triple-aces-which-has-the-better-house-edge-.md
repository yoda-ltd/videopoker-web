# Full Pay Deuces vs. Triple Aces: Which Has the Better House Edge?

For video poker players who enjoy chasing high-value quads, the choice often boils down to **Full Pay Deuces Wild (100.76% EV)** or **Triple Aces** (a variant of Triple Bonus Poker, often returning **99.9% EV**). While Deuces Wild uses wild cards, Triple Aces uses a specialized pay table to reward Four Aces with a massive payout. This article analyzes the house edge, math, and strategy for both games.

## Theoretical Return (EV) and House Edge

### 1. Full Pay Deuces Wild
*   **Return (EV):** 100.76%
*   **House Edge:** -0.76% (Player Edge)
*   **The Math:** Driven by the 200:1 Four Deuces and 800:1 Natural Royal.

### 2. Triple Aces (Triple Bonus Poker Plus)
*   **Return (EV):** 99.94%
*   **House Edge:** 0.06% (Casino Edge)
*   **The Math:** Driven by a **240-for-1** payout for Four Aces and an **80-for-1** payout for Four 2s, 3s, or 4s.

**The Winner on EV:** Full Pay Deuces Wild is the clear winner, as it provides a positive expected return. However, Triple Aces is nearly a break-even game (99.94%), which is exceptionally good for a "Natural" poker game.

## Pay Table Comparison: Wild vs. Bonus

| Hand | Deuces Wild (100.76%) | Triple Aces (99.94%) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Natural Royal** | 800:1 | 800:1 |
| **Four Deuces** | **200:1** | 80:1 |
| **Four Aces** | 5:1 (as 4-of-a-kind) | **240:1** |
| **Full House** | 3:1 | 10:1 |
| **Flush** | 2:1 | 6:1 |

In Triple Aces, the **Full House (10:1)** and **Flush (6:1)** payouts are massive compared to Deuces Wild. This provides a much smoother experience for the player, as small wins occur more frequently and for larger amounts.

## Volatility and Variance
*   **Deuces Wild Variance:** ~25.8
*   **Triple Aces Variance:** ~52.0

**Triple Aces is significantly more volatile than Full Pay Deuces Wild.** This might be surprising to some, but because such a large chunk of the return in Triple Aces is tied up in the 240:1 Four Aces and 80:1 small quads, you will see your bankroll fluctuate wildly. Deuces Wild, despite its wild-card nature, has a more "compact" variance because the Four Deuces (200:1) hit more often than the Four Aces (240:1) do in a Natural game.

## Strategy: Which is Harder?
**Deuces Wild** strategy is counter-intuitive. You must ignore traditional poker rankings to hold single deuces.

**Triple Aces** strategy is an "aggressive" version of Jacks or Better. 
*   **Hold Decision:** You will often break a Full House to keep three Aces in hopes of hitting the 240:1 payout.
*   **Hold Decision:** You never keep a "Kicker" with your Aces; you want maximum draw potential for that fourth Ace.

### Using a Video Poker Trainer
Both games require perfect play to reach their theoretical returns. 
1.  **For Deuces Wild:** Practice the "1 Deuce vs. 3-Card Royal" holds.
2.  **For Triple Aces:** Practice the "Triple Bonus" specific holds where you prioritize Aces over almost everything else (including made straights in some scenarios).

## House Edge Analysis: The Reality of the Floor
*   **Full Pay Deuces (100.76%):** Rare. Most common is 98.9%.
*   **Triple Aces (99.94%):** Usually found in high-limit rooms. If the Full House drops to 9:1, the EV falls to around 98.5%.

If you find a 99.9% Triple Aces machine and only a 98.9% Deuces Wild machine, the **Triple Aces game is the superior choice**, provided you have the bankroll to handle the 52.0 variance.

## Which Game Should You Play?

**Choose Full Pay Deuces Wild if:**
*   You found the rare machine that offers a 100.76% return.
*   You want lower volatility (25.8 vs. 52.0).
*   You enjoy the "Wild Card" mechanic.

**Choose Triple Aces if:**
*   You prefer "Natural" poker without wild cards.
*   You want a near break-even game (99.94%) with huge quad payouts.
*   You have a large bankroll and can handle the "Triple Bonus" swings.

## Conclusion
For the professional looking for an edge, **Full Pay Deuces Wild (100.76%)** is the gold standard. However, for the player who prefers the mechanics of Jacks or Better but wants the high-reward potential of quad Aces, **Triple Aces (99.94%)** is one of the best games in the casino. Just be prepared for the volatility—Triple Aces is a high-speed chase for those 240-to-1 payouts.
