# Strategy Differences: Moving from Super Double Double Bonus to Triple Aces Poker

Transitioning from Super Double Double Bonus (SDDB) to Triple Aces Poker is a unique step in the video poker world. Both games are popular variants that offer specific payouts for Four of a Kind. This guide provides the best video poker strategy comparison to help you understand how to play video poker like a pro during this transition.

## The Super Double Double Bonus Baseline

In Super Double Double Bonus, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is built on the fact that any pair of Jacks or better is a winning hand. This creates a very stable, low-volatility experience. When moving to Triple Aces Poker, that "safety net" is removed. You now need at least a pair of Aces to get your money back.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from Super Double Double Bonus to Triple Aces Poker, you'll find that **80% of your strategy remains the same**. The **20% that changes** is entirely focused on the "Pair threshold." This 20% shift causes the most **Bankroll Drain** if not corrected.

### The 20% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Pair of Jacks Devaluation:** In Super Double Double Bonus, a pair of Jacks is a winning hand. In Triple Aces Poker, it's a "trash" hand that you should rarely hold unless you're drawing for something better (like a three-card Royal Flush).
2.  **Triple Aces Focus:** Triple Aces Poker is named "Triple Aces" because it rewards you specifically for getting three or four Aces. This makes four-of-a-kind and specific straights much more valuable than in Super Double Double Bonus.
3.  **Two Pair Strategy:** While Two Pair pays 2-for-1 in both games, the higher payout for specific "Triple Aces" hands in Triple Aces Poker means you might break a Full House to draw for a fifth card more often than you would in Super Double Double Bonus.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

Triple Aces Poker introduces **Volatility Scaling**. Without the Jacks-or-better push, your bankroll will experience more frequent dips. **EV Erosion** occurs when a SDDB player instinctively holds a pair of Jacks in Triple Aces Poker. This is a "dead hand" and holding it is a direct loss of expected value.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Aces or Better are your new minimum "win."
*   **Modify:** Discard any pair of Jacks unless they are part of a better draw.
*   **Target:** Aggressively pursue the "Triple Aces" bonuses; they are the heart of the Triple Aces Poker pay table.

By recognizing that the "safety net" has moved, you can successfully transition to Triple Aces Poker without suffering from significant EV Erosion.
