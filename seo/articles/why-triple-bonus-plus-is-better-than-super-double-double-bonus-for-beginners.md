# Why Triple Bonus Plus is Better Than Super Double Double Bonus for Beginners

In the world of "Bonus" video poker, the choice between "Triple Bonus Plus" (TBP) and "Super Double Double Bonus" (SDDB) is a study in **Strategic Convergence** and the **Variance Index**. This **best video poker strategy comparison** breaks down why Triple Bonus Plus is the superior entry point for novices, focusing on **Payout Compression** and the "Strategy Drift" caused by the kicker features.

## Strategic Convergence: The "Linear" Advantage

**Triple Bonus Plus Poker** is a "Bonus" game. The strategy is almost identical to Jacks or Better, with adjustments for the "Heavy" payouts on 4-of-a-Kind. Because there are no "Kickers" to worry about, the strategy is "Linear" and "Logical." Beginners can learn the basic "Hierarchy" of hands and apply it with high accuracy. The **video poker house edge analysis** is straightforward and predictable.

**Super Double Double Bonus** introduces "Kickers." This creates "Strategy Bifurcation." For every 4-of-a-Kind, you must now ask: "Do I have the kicker?" The presence of the kicker fundamentally changes the "Value" of every card. For a beginner, this is "Cognitive Overload." You can easily make a mistake by holding a "Natural" hand when the "Kicker" suggests a different path. This "Strategy Drift" can cost you 1-2% in "Theoretical Return" over a session.

## Payout Compression: The "Smooth" vs. The "Kicker"

In **Triple Bonus Plus**, the "Cost" of the massive payouts for 4-of-a-Kind is built into the Two-Pair and Full House payouts. However, the game remains "Smooth" because the frequency of 4-of-a-Kind is relatively high. Your bankroll "Churns" nicely. You are "Rewarded" for hitting common hands with a "Fair" payout.

In **Super Double Double Bonus**, the "Kicker" allows for "Extreme" hands. To "Pay" for these rare "Kicker" wins, the payouts for common hands are "Compressed." You often get only "Money Back" on hands that would pay more in a standard game. For a beginner, this is "Psychological Torture." You hit a "Winning" hand, but your bankroll doesn't grow. This "Bleed" is much more aggressive than in Triple Bonus Plus.

## The Variance Index: Sustainability vs. Volatility

Our **video poker house edge analysis** shows that:
*   **Triple Bonus Plus** has a variance of around **35**. It is a "Medium-to-High Volatility" game. It offers "Excitement" without the "Punishing Swings."
*   **Super Double Double Bonus** has a variance of around **50-60**. It is a "High-Volatility" game. The "Gaps" between big wins are much longer.

For the new player who wants to enjoy a long session and learn the fundamentals without the "Kicker" confusion, Triple Bonus Plus is the clear winner. It provides the "Stability" of standard games with the "Enhanced" rewards of a specialty game. Leave the "Kicker" hunting to the pros who can handle the "Complexity" and the "Swings."
