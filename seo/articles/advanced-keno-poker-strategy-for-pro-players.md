# Advanced Keno Poker Strategy for Pro Players: Grid-Based EV Analysis

Keno Poker (or Poker Keno) is a "Hybrid" variant where the "Draw" cards are selected from a larger "Keno Grid" (often 80 numbers) but evaluated using "Poker Ranks." ## Grid-Based EV Analysis: The "Large-Sample" Advantage

In Keno Poker, you might draw 10 to 20 numbers from an 80-number grid, and the machine then looks for the "Best 5-card Poker Hand" within those numbers.

1.  **The "Cluster-Selection" Strategy**: In **advanced video poker strategy**, you are not just hoping for a "Draw." You are hoping for a "Cluster." If you are allowed to "Pick" numbers (like in Keno), a professional uses **Recursion Analysis** to calculate the "Overlap Probability." If you pick numbers 1-10, you are maximizing the probability of a "Straight" or "Straight Flush" within the 10-card sample.
2.  **Grid-Based EV Analysis**: A professional calculates the "Theoretical Yield" of the "Best Hand." Because you have 10-20 cards to choose from, the probability of a "Pair" or "Three of a Kind" is nearly 100%. The "Real Game" is hitting "Quads," "Royals," and "Straight Flushes."

## Recursion Analysis: The "Multi-Hand Overlap"

In Keno Poker, the machine often pays for *multiple* poker hands found within the sample.

- **The "High-Tier" Priority**: If you draw 20 numbers and find two "4-of-a-kinds," you are paid for both. This creates a "Magnification Effect."
