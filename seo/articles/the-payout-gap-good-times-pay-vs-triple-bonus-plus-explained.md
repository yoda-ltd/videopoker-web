# The Payout Gap: Good Times Pay vs. Triple Bonus Plus Explained

The "Payout Gap" between Good Times Pay and Triple Bonus Plus represents the two most common ways to "Amplify" your video poker wins: "Multipliers" versus "Static Bonuses." This **best video poker strategy comparison** explores how **Payout Compression** and **Variance Scaling** define these two "High-Reward" formats.

## The Mathematical Foundation: Feature vs. Table

**Good Times Pay** is a "Feature" game. It typically takes a standard game (like Jacks or Better) and adds a multiplier (2x to 10x). To fund this "Feature," you must typically bet an "Extra" 5 coins. This is the ultimate form of **Payout Compression**. You are betting 10 coins but only getting "5-coin" value on hands where the multiplier doesn't trigger. The **video poker house edge analysis** is based on the "Multiplier Frequency."

**Triple Bonus Plus** is a "Table" game. The "Bonus" is built into the pay table itself. It is a Jacks or Better variant that pays massive "Triple" bonuses for Four-of-a-Kind (Quads). To fund these "Bonuses," the game uses **Payout Compression** on common hands like Two-Pair (often reduced to 1-to-1).

## Variance Scaling and the EV Gap

1.  **Good Times Pay:** The variance is high. You are "Pre-paying" for the multiplier. If the "Good Times" don't roll, the **EV Gap** will empty your wallet quickly. Your bankroll moves in "Jumps" based on the feature.
2.  **Triple Bonus Plus:** The variance is also high (around 45-50). It is a "Hunting" game. The **Variance Scaling** is steep because the game doesn't provide the same "Safety Net" on non-Quad hands. Your bankroll moves in "Jumps" based on the Quads.

## Strategy Bifurcation: Feature vs. Target

The **Strategy Bifurcation** is a shift in player focus:
*   In **Good Times Pay**, the strategy usually remains identical to the "Base" game. The "Multipliers" don't change *how* you play; they just change *how much* you pay.
*   In **Triple Bonus Plus**, the strategy changes to "Quad Hunting." You will hold small pairs and high pairs with extreme priority. You accept the "Money Back" nature of Two-Pair as the price of "Chasing" the jackpot.

If you enjoy the "Rush" of a random multiplier that can turn any win into a huge one, Good Times Pay is your game. If you prefer the "Strategic Goal" of hitting massive Quad payouts, Triple Bonus Plus offers a more "Targeted" high-risk experience. Both require a disciplined bankroll and an understanding of the "Cost" of the feature.
