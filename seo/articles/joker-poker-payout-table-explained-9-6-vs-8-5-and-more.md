# Joker Poker Payout Table Explained: 20/7 vs 18/7 and More

In the **best odds video poker** world, Joker Poker (also known as Joker Wild) is a fascinating variant because it adds a 53rd card to the deck—the Joker. For the professional player, this represents a unique opportunity for **Pay Table Optimization**.

## The Best Odds Index: Kings or Better Joker Poker (100.65%)
In the **highest return video poker games** 2026 index, the "Kings or Better" variant of Joker Poker is a top-tier choice. The "Full Pay" version offers a **Theoretical Return Convergence of 100.65%**.

To identify this machine, look for these specific payouts:
*   **Sequential Royal Flush:** 20,000 coins (if applicable).
*   **Royal Flush (no Joker):** 800-for-1.
*   **Five of a Kind:** 200-for-1.
*   **Wild Royal Flush:** 100-for-1.
*   **Four of a Kind:** 20-for-1.
*   **Full House:** 7-for-1.
*   **Flush:** 5-for-1.

## Analysis of "Short Pay" Predation
Because 100.65% gives the player the edge, casinos often use "Short Pay" tables or "Aces or Better" versions to reverse it.
*   **Aces or Better Joker Poker:** By requiring a pair of Aces instead of Kings to push/win, the RTP drops significantly, often to **98.60%**.
*   **18/7 Joker Poker:** Reducing the Four of a Kind payout from 20 to 18 drops the RTP to **99.29%**.
*   **Common Short Pay (15/7):** This variant drops the RTP below **98%**.

The **EV Gap** between the 100.65% version and the 98% version is over 2.6%. If you are playing 800 hands per hour at a $1 machine, that’s $26 per hour you’re losing simply by picking the wrong machine. This "predatory" short-pay table kills the professional edge.

## House Edge Variance in Joker Poker
Joker Poker has higher **House Edge Variance** than Jacks or Better. This is because the game's return is heavily weighted towards the top hands like Five of a Kind and the Royal Flush. If you don't hit these rare hands at the mathematically expected frequency, your actual return will lag behind the theoretical return.
