# Bonus Poker Payout Table Explained: 8/5 vs 7/5 and More

In the **best odds video poker** world, Bonus Poker is the fan favorite because of its "kicker" bonus payouts for Four of a Kind. For the professional player, this represents a unique opportunity for **Pay Table Optimization**.

## The Best Odds Index: 8/5 Bonus Poker (99.17%)
In the **highest return video poker games** 2026 index, 8/5 Bonus Poker is a top-tier choice. It offers a **Theoretical Return Convergence of 99.17%**.

The "8/5" designation here refers to:
*   **Full House:** 8 coins per 1 coin bet.
*   **Flush:** 5 coins per 1 coin bet.

Wait, why do we call it "8/5" Bonus Poker? It's the pay table that pays **8 coins for a Full House AND 5 coins for a Flush**.

## Analysis of "Short Pay" Predation
Because 99.17% gives the player a strong return, casinos often use "Short Pay" tables to reverse it.
*   **7/5 Bonus Poker:** Paying only 5 coins for a Flush drops the RTP to **98.01%**.
*   **6/5 Bonus Poker:** Paying 6 for Full House and 5 for Flush drops the RTP to **96.87%**.
*   **Common Short Pay (8/4):** This variant drops the RTP below **98.04%**.

The **EV Gap** between the 99.17% version and the 96.87% version is over 2.3%. If you are playing 800 hands per hour at a $1 machine, that’s $23 per hour you’re losing simply by picking the wrong machine. This "predatory" short-pay table kills the professional edge.

## House Edge Variance in Bonus Poker
Bonus Poker has higher **House Edge Variance** than Jacks or Better. This is because the game's return is heavily weighted towards the Four of a Kind. If you don't hit the Four of a Kind at the mathematically expected frequency, your actual return will lag behind the 99.17% theoretical return. Professional bankroll management is essential.
