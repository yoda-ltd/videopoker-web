# Why All American is Better Than Loose Deuces for Beginners

If you are new to video poker, you might be tempted by **Loose Deuces**. After all, the name implies "loose" (easy to win) and it features four wild cards. However, for a beginner, **All American is a much better choice**. 

Loose Deuces is a "Jackpot-heavy" game that can wipe out a beginner’s bankroll in minutes. All American, while still exciting, offers a much more stable and educational experience. Here is why you should start with the red, white, and blue.

## 1. The Variance Trap

"Variance" is a measure of how much your bankroll will swing up and down.
*   **Loose Deuces Variance:** **~190.0** (Extreme). Most of the game's payout is tied up in the "Four Deuces" and "Royal Flush" jackpots. If you don't hit those, you will lose your money very, very fast.
*   **All American Variance:** **~30.0** (High, but manageable). While All American is more "swingy" than standard poker, it is nowhere near the extreme volatility of Loose Deuces.

**The Lesson:** A beginner playing Loose Deuces will often go broke before they even understand the rules. All American gives you more "playtime" for your dollar.

## 2. Learning "Real" Poker Strategy

Loose Deuces is a wild-card game. The strategy involves holding things that would be insane in a regular poker game (like keeping a single 2 and discarding four high cards).
*   **All American:** Teaches you the fundamental values of a 52-card deck. You learn to recognize flushes, straights, and pairs—skills that are transferable to almost every other poker game.
*   **Loose Deuces:** Teaches you "Deuces Wild" strategy, which is useless if you ever sit down at a Texas Hold'em table or a Jacks or Better machine.

## 3. Payout Frequency

Because Loose Deuces has wild cards, the "Bottom" of the pay table is gutted. 
*   **In Loose Deuces:** You get **nothing** for Two Pair or a High Pair. You must have at least Three of a Kind to win anything.
*   **In All American:** You win with a High Pair (Jacks or Better) or Two Pair.
*   **Why it matters:** For a beginner, winning *something* frequently is better for morale and learning than losing 10 hands in a row and then hitting one big hand.

## 4. Strategy Complexity

*   **Loose Deuces Strategy:** Extremely complex. You have to learn five different sets of rules based on how many "Deuces" you have in your hand (0, 1, 2, 3, or 4).
*   **All American Strategy:** Straightforward. You are always looking for the same hands, and the "break the pair" rules are easy to memorize.

## 5. Bankroll Requirements

*   **Loose Deuces:** To play safely, you need a bankroll of at least **5,000 credits**.
*   **All American:** You can have a great session with **1,000 to 2,000 credits**.

**For a beginner on a budget, All American is the clear winner.**

## Summary Comparison

| Feature | All American | Loose Deuces |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Volatilty** | Moderate-High | **Extreme** |
| **Min. Winning Hand** | Pair of Jacks | 3 of a Kind |
| **Transferable Skill** | Yes (High) | No (Low) |
| **Fun Factor** | Strategic Hunt | Jackpot Chase |

## Conclusion

Loose Deuces is a game for experts and high-rollers who are chasing a specific jackpot. **All American** is a game for players who want to learn the art of Video Poker, enjoy a mathematical edge, and have their bankroll last longer than a few minutes. If you are just starting out, skip the "Loose" wild cards and stick to the All American dream!
