# Sevens Wild vs. Tens or Better: Which Has the Better House Edge?

In the world of video poker, players often compare variants that use wild cards. Two of the most popular are **Sevens Wild** and **Tens or Better**. While both offer a wild card, the strategy and house edge (return to player or RTP) of each game differ significantly.

## Sevens Wild: The Power of Four Wilds
In Sevens Wild, all four Sevens (7s) are wild. This creates a much more volatile game than Tens or Better. The house edge in Sevens Wild can be even lower than in Tens or Better, provided you find a "Full Pay" machine.

*   **RTP of Sevens Wild:** On a Full Pay Sevens Wild machine, the RTP can reach as high as **100.56%**, meaning the player has a mathematical advantage over the casino.
*   **Strategy for Sevens Wild:** The strategy is much more complex than Tens or Better. You are frequently discarding high cards to draw for Sevens, which is counterintuitive to most video poker players.

## Tens or Better: The Lower Entry Threshold
In Tens or Better, a pair of 10s or higher is a winning hand. This creates a much more stable game than Sevens Wild. The house edge in Tens or Better can be even lower than in Sevens Wild, provided you find a "Full Pay" machine.

*   **RTP of Tens or Better:** On a Full Pay Tens or Better machine, the RTP is approximately **99.14%**.
*   **Strategy for Tens or Better:** The strategy is much more simple than Sevens Wild. You are frequently holding 10s to ensure a win, which is intuitive to most video poker players.

## House Edge and Return to Player (RTP) Comparison
When comparing Sevens Wild and Tens or Better, the key is the **house edge**.
*   **Sevens Wild:** On a Full Pay machine, the house edge is negative, meaning the player has a **+0.56%** edge. However, on "Short Pay" Sevens Wild machines (the most common version), the house edge can climb to **2% or higher**.
*   **Tens or Better:** On a Full Pay machine, the house edge is around **0.86%**. However, on "Short Pay" Tens or Better machines (the most common version), the house edge can climb to **2% or higher**.

## Volatility and Bankroll Considerations
Sevens Wild is a much more volatile game. You will have long losing streaks punctuated by big wins (like hitting four Sevens). Tens or Better is a more stable game with a higher hit frequency for smaller hands (pair of 10s).

*   **Bankroll Management:** For a 100-hand session at a $1 denomination, Tens or Better is safer. For professional players with a large bankroll and a focus on long-term profits, Sevens Wild is the better choice, provided they can find a Full Pay machine.

## Conclusion: Which Should You Play?
If you are looking for a game with a manageable strategy and a low house edge, **Tens or Better** is an excellent choice, especially in **high-limit video poker** areas. However, if you are a professional player looking for a mathematical edge and can handle high volatility, **Sevens Wild** is the superior game.
