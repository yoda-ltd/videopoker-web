# Strategy Differences: Moving from Dice Poker to Keno Poker

Transitioning from Dice Poker to Keno Poker is a unique step in the video poker world. Both games are niche variants that combine the classic 52-card deck with an additional "bonus" mechanism (dice or keno numbers). This guide provides the best video poker strategy comparison to help you understand how to play video poker like a pro during this transition.

## The Dice Poker Baseline

In Dice Poker, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is built on the fact that any pair of Queens or Kings or better is a winning hand. This creates a relatively stable experience. When moving to Keno Poker, that "safety net" is removed. You now need at least a pair of Jacks to get your money back.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from Dice Poker to Keno Poker, you'll find that **80% of your strategy remains the same**. The **20% that changes** is entirely focused on the "Pair threshold." This 20% shift causes the most **Bankroll Drain** if not corrected.

### The 20% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Pair of Queens/Kings Devaluation:** In Dice Poker, a pair of Queens or Kings is a winning hand. In Keno Poker, it's still a winning hand, but you can also win with a pair of Jacks. This makes Jacks much more valuable than in Dice Poker.
2.  **Keno Bonus Focus:** Keno Poker is named "Keno" because it rewards you specifically for getting keno numbers on your cards. This makes four-of-a-kind and specific straights much more valuable than in Dice Poker.
3.  **Two Pair Strategy:** While Two Pair pays 2-for-1 in both games, the higher payout for specific "Keno" hands in Keno Poker means you might break a Full House to draw for a fifth card more often than you would in Dice Poker.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

Keno Poker introduces **Volatility Scaling**. With the Jacks-or-better push, your bankroll will experience more frequent small wins. **EV Erosion** occurs when a Dice-Poker player instinctively devalues a pair of Jacks in Keno Poker. This is a "winning hand" and not holding it is a direct loss of expected value.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Jacks or Better are your new minimum "win."
*   **Modify:** Value a pair of Jacks more highly than you did in Dice Poker.
*   **Target:** Aggressively pursue the "Keno" bonuses; they are the heart of the Keno Poker pay table.

By recognizing that the "safety net" has moved, you can successfully transition to Keno Poker without suffering from significant EV Erosion.
