# Strategy Differences: Moving from Bonus Poker to Double Double Bonus
Category: Advanced Strategy

The leap from Bonus Poker (BP) to Double Double Bonus (DDB) is the most common transition for video poker players looking for "big win" potential. However, failing to adjust for the **EV Differential** and the "Kicker" mechanics can lead to rapid bankroll depletion. This guide breaks down the essential strategy shifts.

## 1. Adjusting for Variance Scaling

The most important "meta-strategy" when moving to DDB is bankroll management. DDB has a variance of approximately 42, compared to BP's 21. This means you need **double the bankroll** to play the same stakes in DDB as you did in BP to have the same risk of ruin. 

## 2. The Kicker Mechanic and Hand-Ranking Priorities

The "Kicker" (the 5th card in a 4-of-a-kind hand) is the heart of DDB strategy. It creates several unique **Hand-Ranking Priorities**:

*   **Holding Kickers with Aces**: In Bonus Poker, if you have a pair of Aces and a "Kicker" (2, 3, or 4), you discard the kicker. In Double Double Bonus, you *still* discard the kicker with a pair of Aces, but if you have **Three Aces**, the math changes. You hold the Aces and the Kicker to lock in the potential for the 2000-coin payout.
*   **Breaking Full Houses?**: No, you generally do not break a Full House to chase Quads in DDB, but the *pain* of hitting a Full House when you needed that one specific card for a 2000-coin Quad is much higher.

## 3. The "Two Pair Trap" Re-Visited

Like Double Bonus, DDB only pays **1-for-1 on Two Pair**. 

*   In **Bonus Poker**, Two Pair is a "safety net."
*   In **Double Double Bonus**, Two Pair is a "placeholder."

This shift means you must play "Offense" more than "Defense." You are no longer trying to "not lose"; you are trying to "hit big." This leads to a **Strategy Convergence** where DDB players will more aggressively discard cards that might lead to a Two Pair in favor of cards that have a higher "Quad Bonus" potential.

## 4. Video Poker House Edge Analysis: The Strategy Error Gap

A common pitfall in **video poker house edge analysis** is assuming that "Bonus is Bonus." If you use a Bonus Poker strategy on a DDB machine, your actual return will drop by nearly 1%. This is because BP strategy overvalues Two Pair and undervalues the "Kicker" bonuses.

### Key Strategy Shift Summary
| Hand | Bonus Poker Action | DDB Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Two Pair** | Hold and take the 2-for-1 profit. | Hold, but acknowledge it's only a 1-for-1 push. |
| **Three Aces + Kicker** | Discard Kicker. | **HOLD Kicker.** |
| **Lone Ace** | Standard High Card play. | Highly aggressive hold to chase Quad Aces. |

## Conclusion: The Professional Comparison

When performing a **best video poker strategy comparison**, DDB stands out as the ultimate "High Reward, High Risk" variant. The transition from Bonus Poker requires a mental shift: you are moving from a game of "Percentage Grinding" to a game of "Bonus Hunting." Understanding the **Variance Scaling** is the only way to survive the hunt.
