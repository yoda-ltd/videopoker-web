# Full Pay Deuces Pay Table Analysis: 9/6 vs 8/5 vs 7/5

In the world of video poker, "Full Pay Deuces Wild" is a specific term that refers to a pay table offering a theoretical return of **100.76%**. However, casinos often "short-pay" players by tweaking the payouts for certain hands. This article analyzes the math behind the 9/6, 8/5, and 7/5 variations of Deuces Wild and why they are vastly different from the true Full Pay version.

## Defining "Full Pay" Deuces Wild
Before looking at the 9/6 or 8/5 versions, we must understand the baseline for Full Pay Deuces Wild. In Deuces Wild, the most critical payouts are for the **Four of a Kind**, **Full House**, and **Flush**.

True Full Pay Deuces Wild (100.76%) is often called "25/15/9/5" because:
*   Wild Royal: 25
*   5 of a Kind: 15
*   Straight Flush: 9
*   **4 of a Kind: 5**

## The Confusion with 9/6, 8/5, and 7/5
In games like Jacks or Better, 9/6 refers to the Full House and Flush payouts. In Deuces Wild, these labels are often used loosely by players to describe the **Straight Flush** and **Four of a Kind** payouts.

### 1. The 100.76% Table (The Professional Standard)
*   **4 of a Kind:** 5
*   **Full House:** 3
*   **Flush:** 2
*   **EV:** 100.76%
*   **Strategy:** High-level professional play. Requires a trainer to master.

### 2. The 9/6 Version (Often "Airport Deuces")
If a machine pays 9 for a Straight Flush and 6 for a Four of a Kind (but lowers other payouts like Five of a Kind), it might seem better, but usually, it's a trap. 
*   **Note:** Always check the **Five of a Kind (15)** and **Wild Royal (25)**. If those are lowered to 12 and 20 respectively, your EV drops to around **98.9%**.

### 3. The 8/5 Version (The Trap)
In this version, the Straight Flush pays 8 and the Four of a Kind pays 5. 
*   **Impact:** Losing that 1 unit on the Straight Flush (9 vs 8) might seem small, but since you hit a Straight Flush approximately once every 450 hands, it adds up.
*   **Expected Value:** Approximately **99.7%** (assuming other payouts remain standard). While better than many slots, it's no longer a "player advantage" game.

### 4. The 7/5 Version (The Avoid List)
This is a common "Short Pay" table found in high-traffic tourist areas. 
*   **EV:** Roughly **98.8%**. 
*   **House Edge:** 1.2%. At this level, you are losing money significantly faster than at the 100.76% machine.

## Mathematical Breakdown of the "4 of a Kind" Payout
The single most important number in Deuces Wild is the payout for **Four of a Kind**.
*   In Full Pay, it pays **5-for-1**.
*   In many Vegas Strip casinos, it is lowered to **4-for-1**.

**The Math:** You hit Four of a Kind about **once every 15 hands**. Lowering the payout from 5 to 4 is a **6.5% reduction in total return**. This single change turns one of the best games in the casino into one of the worst.

## Strategy Adjustments for Short Pay Tables
If you must play a 9/6 or 8/5 table instead of Full Pay:
1.  **Lower Volatility:** These tables often have lower volatility but a higher house edge. 
2.  **Trainer Practice:** Use a trainer like Video Poker for Winners and input the specific "Short Pay" table. The strategy for holding "Inside Straights" changes when the Straight Flush payout drops from 9 to 8 or 7.

## Conclusion
Don't be fooled by labels. A "9/6" Deuces Wild machine is not necessarily a good game. Always look for the **5-for-1 Four of a Kind** and the **15-for-1 Five of a Kind**. If you can't find those, you aren't playing Full Pay Deuces, and the house has the edge.
