# The Payout Gap: Bonus Poker Deluxe vs. Double Jackpot Poker Explained
Category: Comparison

Understanding the **Strategic Divergence** between **Bonus Poker Deluxe** and **Double Jackpot Poker** is key to mastering these high-payout variants. This comparison focuses on how the paytables differ and why those differences lead to unique **EV Maximization** paths.

### The Quad Gap: Uniform vs. Tiered Bonuses

In **Bonus Poker Deluxe**, all four-of-a-kind hands pay a uniform 400 credits (for a 5-coin bet). In **Double Jackpot Poker**, the payouts are tiered: Four Aces pay more than Four Faces (K, Q, J), which pay more than Four 2s-10s. This creates a significant **Volatility Normalization** shift. In Double Jackpot Poker, you are looking specifically for tiered quads, while in Bonus Poker Deluxe, any quad is a winner.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is high strategy overlap (approximately 80%) because both are based on a 52-card natural deck. However, in Double Jackpot Poker, you prioritize Aces and face cards much more than in Bonus Poker Deluxe. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the high-tier quads in Double Jackpot dominate the EV landscape.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Ace Priority:** In Double Jackpot Poker, Aces are the highest-paying quads. You prioritize them over almost any other card rank.
2.  **Face Card Focus:** Kings, Queens, and Jacks are the next tier in Double Jackpot. In Bonus Poker Deluxe, they are only valuable for a standard payout.
3.  **Low Card Value:** In Double Jackpot Poker, low-rank quads pay significantly less than in Bonus Poker Deluxe.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games are highly engaging for those who enjoy quad bonuses. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both offer excellent returns if you play the maximum bet and follow the rank-based strategy.

# The Payout Gap: Bonus Poker Deluxe vs. Pick 'em Poker Explained
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Bonus Poker Deluxe** to **Pick 'em Poker** is a shift from a "Draw-Based" variant to a "Choice-Based" variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your decisions and the volatility that comes with them.

### The Comparison Matrix: Bonus Poker Deluxe vs. Pick 'em Poker

| Feature | Bonus Poker Deluxe (9/6) | Pick 'em Poker (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.64% | 99.95% |
| **Variance Index** | 30.7 | 15.0 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | ANY Natural Quad Bonus | High-Payout High Pair Connectivity |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.36% | 0.05% |

### The Mechanical Gap: Drawing vs. Choosing

In **Bonus Poker Deluxe**, the rewards are concentrated in Achieving natural quads through drawing cards. In **Pick 'em Poker**, the focus is on choosing the better of two fixed outcome stacks. This leads to a much lower **Volatility Normalization** (15.0 vs 30.7).

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is zero (0%). Both are fundamentally different games. Bonus Poker Deluxe is a draw game; Pick 'em Poker is a choice-based game. In Bonus Poker Deluxe, you prioritize natural quad potential. In Pick 'em Poker, you prioritize the highest EV stack. This is the essence of **EV Maximization** through simple choice.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Simplify Everything:** You are only making one choice in Pick 'em Poker: left or right.
2.  **High Pair Priority:** In Pick 'em Poker, you prioritize natural high pairs starting at 10s.
3.  **Variance Management:** Pick 'em Poker is much more stable than Bonus Poker Deluxe. You need a much smaller bankroll to withstand the swings.

A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that Pick 'em Poker is one of the "loosest" games in the casino, often returning almost 100%. The **best video poker strategy comparison** highlights that choice is the ultimate tool for **EV Maximization** in Pick 'em Poker.

# The Payout Gap: Bonus Poker Deluxe vs. Quick Quads Explained
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Bonus Poker Deluxe** to **Quick Quads** involves moving from a "Standard Bonus" variant to an "Additive Bonus" variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you approach **Volatility Normalization**.

### The Comparison Matrix: Bonus Poker Deluxe vs. Quick Quads

| Feature | Bonus Poker Deluxe (9/6) | Quick Quads (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.64% | 99.60% |
| **Variance Index** | 30.7 | 35.0 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | ANY Natural Quad Bonus | Additive Quad Payouts |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.36% | 0.40% |

### The Arithmetic Gap: Natural vs. Additive

In **Bonus Poker Deluxe**, the reward is concentrated in Achieving natural quads. In **Quick Quads**, you can also achieve quads through additive combinations (e.g., three 8s and a 5 and a 3). This creates a higher **Volatility Normalization** (35.0 vs 30.7).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 75% overlap. In Bonus Poker Deluxe, you only care about natural pairs. In Quick Quads, you prioritize additive combinations above all else, often breaking natural connections to maximize the arithmetic potential. This is the essence of **EV Maximization** in additive games.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Arithmetic Awareness:** You must always be looking for cards that sum to your three-of-a-kind rank.
2.  **Low Card Value:** In Quick Quads, low cards (2, 3, 4) are vital for Achieving additive quads.
3.  **Six-Coin Bet:** Quick Quads requires a 6th coin to activate the additive feature. This is the core of the **video poker house edge analysis**—you must pay to play the feature.

Through a detailed **video poker house edge analysis**, it's clear that Quick Quads offers a competitive return with higher action than Bonus Poker Deluxe. The **best video poker strategy comparison** highlights that arithmetic is the ultimate tool for **EV Maximization** in Quick Quads.
