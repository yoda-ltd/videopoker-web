# Risk vs. Reward: Tens or Better vs. Double Jackpot Poker

Tens or Better and Double Jackpot Poker represent two very different ways to play the "Jacks or Better" base game. One is a "Safety" game, and the other is a "Jackpot" game. This **best video poker strategy comparison** examines the **Variance Scaling** and **Payout Compression** that occur when you trade stability for "Jackpot" potential.

## The Mathematical Foundation: Safety vs. Strike

**Tens or Better** is designed for maximum stability. By paying you for a pair of Tens (instead of Jacks), the game provides a massive "Safety Net." This leads to **Payout Compression** on "Flush" and "Full House" hands, meaning your "Big" natural hands pay less to fund your frequent "Small" wins.

**Double Jackpot Poker** is designed for the "Strike." It typically keeps the "Jacks or Better" minimum win but introduces massive "Double" bonuses for specific four-of-a-kind (Quads). To fund these jackpots, the game uses **Payout Compression** on Two-Pair (often reducing it to 1-to-1). This is a massive **EV Gap** for the "Average" session.

## Variance Scaling and the Bankroll Buffer

1.  **Tens or Better:** The variance is low (around 18-20). It is a "Low-Risk" game. Your bankroll will move slowly, making it perfect for "Grinding" points or enjoying a long session. The **video poker house edge analysis** is very stable.
2.  **Double Jackpot Poker:** The variance is high (around 40-50). This is a "High-Risk" game. Because you are only getting "Money Back" on Two-Pair, your bankroll will "Bleed" until you hit a "Double" Quad. This requires a much larger bankroll buffer.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Hold" Logic

The **Strategy Bifurcation** is a total reversal of "Risk" tolerance:
*   In **Tens or Better**, you play "Cautiously." You hold high cards (Tens and up) with extreme priority to get your money back. You are playing for "Time on Device." 
*   In **Double Jackpot Poker**, you play "Aggressively." You hold high pairs and Aces with higher priority than you would in a standard game, hoping to trigger the "Double" payout. You accept the "Money Back" nature of Two-Pair as the price of "Chasing" the jackpot.

For the beginner or the "Safe" player, Tens or Better is the clear winner. For the "Jackpot Hunter" who wants the "Rush" of a 400-to-1 or 800-to-1 Quad payout, Double Jackpot Poker offers the ultimate "Strike" potential.
