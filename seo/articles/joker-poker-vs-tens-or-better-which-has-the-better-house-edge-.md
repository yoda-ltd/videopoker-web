# Joker Poker vs. Tens or Better: Which Has the Better House Edge?

In the world of video poker, players often compare variants that use wild cards. Two of the most popular are **Joker Poker** and **Tens or Better**. While both offer a wild card, the strategy and house edge (return to player or RTP) of each game differ significantly.

## Joker Poker: The 53rd Card
In Joker Poker, there is one wild card: the Joker. This makes it a 53-card deck. The inclusion of the Joker makes it easier to complete hands like a Full House or a Flush, and it also introduces the possibility of a **Five of a Kind**.

*   **RTP of Joker Poker:** On a 9/6 (Full Pay) Kings or Better machine, the RTP is approximately **99.29%**.
*   **Strategy for Joker Poker:** The strategy is relatively straightforward, but it shifts based on whether you are dealt the Joker. Using a **video poker training tool** can help you master the subtle nuances of Joker Poker strategy.

## Tens or Better: The Lower Entry Threshold
In Tens or Better, a pair of 10s or higher is a winning hand. This creates a much more stable game than Joker Poker. The house edge in Tens or Better can be even lower than in Joker Poker, provided you find a "Full Pay" machine.

*   **RTP of Tens or Better:** On a Full Pay Tens or Better machine, the RTP can reach as high as **99.14%**, meaning the player has a mathematical advantage over the casino.
*   **Strategy for Tens or Better:** The strategy is much more simple than Joker Poker. You are frequently holding 10s to ensure a win, which is intuitive to most video poker players.

## House Edge and Return to Player (RTP) Comparison
When comparing Joker Poker and Tens or Better, the key is the **house edge**.
*   **Joker Poker:** The house edge is typically around **0.71%**.
*   **Tens or Better:** On a Full Pay machine, the house edge is around **0.86%**. However, on "Short Pay" Tens or Better machines (the most common version), the house edge can climb to **2% or higher**.

## Volatility and Bankroll Considerations
Tens or Better is a much more stable game. You will have shorter losing streaks punctuated by smaller wins (like hitting a pair of 10s). Joker Poker is a more volatile game with a higher hit frequency for bigger hands (Natural Royal Flush).

*   **Bankroll Management:** For a 100-hand session at a $1 denomination, Tens or Better is safer. For professional players with a large bankroll and a focus on long-term profits, Joker Poker is the better choice, provided they can find a Full Pay machine.

## Conclusion: Which Should You Play?
If you are looking for a game with a manageable strategy and a low house edge, **Joker Poker** is an excellent choice, especially in **high-limit video poker** areas. However, if you are a professional player looking for a mathematical edge and can handle high volatility, **Tens or Better** is the superior game.
