## The Multiplier vs. The Static Pay Table
The core difference is *how* the game reaches its high payouts.

- **Hyper Bonus (The Dynamic Accelerator):** You bet 10 coins per line. The game "Multiplies" the base pay table on the fly based on your dealt cards. It is a "Feature-First" game.

## Key Strategic Adjustments

### 1. From "Hold-for-Multiplier" to "Hold-for-Quads"
- **In Hyper Bonus:** Your hold strategy is slightly warped to capture multipliers on the deal. You are looking for "Any" winning combination that triggers a 2x-12x boost.
- **In Triple Bonus Plus:** Your strategy is radically focused on Four-of-a-Kind. You will often break Full Houses or Two Pair to draw for the specific "Bonus" Quads (Aces-4s). The "Triple" in the title refers to the massive 240-to-1 or 400-to-1 payouts for these hands.

### 2. Variance Normalization and Bankroll Management
- **Hyper Bonus Shift:** High variance. You are betting double (10 coins) and hunting for multipliers. The swings are fast and violent.
- **Triple Bonus Plus Shift:** High but "Predictable" variance. Because you are only betting 5 coins, your bankroll lasts twice as long in terms of "Hands Played" (HOP) compared to Hyper Bonus. However, the RTP is concentrated in the Quads, meaning you will still experience long periods of "Bleeding" bankroll between big hits.
- **The Adjustment:** Triple Bonus Plus is a "Better Value" for players on a budget. You get the high-payout thrill of Hyper Bonus without the mandatory 10-coin side-bet "Tax."

### 3. Feature Hit Frequency
- **Hyper Bonus:** High frequency (something "special" happens every ~6 hands).
- **Triple Bonus Plus:** Low frequency (the "Triple" payouts for Quads happen roughly once every 400-500 hands).
- **The Shift:** Moving to Triple Bonus Plus requires more patience. You are trading the "Instant Feedback" of Hyper Bonus for the "Long-Term Payout" of a superior pay table.
