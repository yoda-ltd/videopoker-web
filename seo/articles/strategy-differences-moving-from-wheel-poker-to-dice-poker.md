# Strategy Differences: Moving from Wheel Poker to Dice Poker

Transitioning from Wheel Poker to Dice Poker is a unique step in the video poker world. Both games are niche variants that combine the classic 52-card deck with an additional "bonus" mechanism (a wheel or dice). This guide provides the best video poker strategy comparison to help you understand how to play video poker like a pro during this transition.

## The Wheel Poker Baseline

In Wheel Poker, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is built on the fact that any pair of Jacks or better is a winning hand. This creates a very stable, low-volatility experience. When moving to Dice Poker, that "safety net" is removed. You now need at least a pair of Queens or Kings to get your money back.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from Wheel Poker to Dice Poker, you'll find that **80% of your strategy remains the same**. The **20% that changes** is entirely focused on the "Pair threshold." This 20% shift causes the most **Bankroll Drain** if not corrected.

### The 20% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Pair of Jacks Devaluation:** In Wheel Poker, a pair of Jacks is a winning hand. In Dice Poker, it's a "trash" hand that you should rarely hold unless you're drawing for something better (like a three-card Royal Flush).
2.  **Dice Bonus Focus:** Dice Poker is named "Dice" because it rewards you for getting specific dice rolls on your cards. This makes four-of-a-kind and specific straights much more valuable than in Wheel Poker.
3.  **Two Pair Strategy:** While Two Pair pays 2-for-1 in both games, the higher payout for specific "Dice" hands in Dice Poker means you might break a Full House to draw for a fifth card more often than you would in Wheel Poker.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

Dice Poker introduces **Volatility Scaling**. Without the Jacks-or-better push, your bankroll will experience more frequent dips. **EV Erosion** occurs when a Wheel-Poker player instinctively holds a pair of Jacks in Dice Poker. This is a "dead hand" and holding it is a direct loss of expected value.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Queens or Kings or Better are your new minimum "win."
*   **Modify:** Discard any pair of Jacks unless they are part of a better draw.
*   **Target:** Aggressively pursue the "Dice" bonuses; they are the heart of the Dice Poker pay table.

By recognizing that the "safety net" has moved, you can successfully transition to Dice Poker without suffering from significant EV Erosion.
