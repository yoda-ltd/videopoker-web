# How to Play Bonus Poker Deluxe: A Comprehensive Guide

Bonus Poker Deluxe is the ultimate variant for "Quad Hunters." While most Bonus games differentiate between the rank of the four-of-a-kind (paying more for Aces or 2s-4s), Bonus Poker Deluxe levels the playing field by paying a high, flat rate for *any* four-of-a-kind. In this guide, we'll dive into the mechanics, the mathematics of the **EV Differential**, and how to identify the **best odds for Bonus Poker Deluxe**.

## A Brief History of Bonus Poker Deluxe

Bonus Poker Deluxe was introduced to fill a specific niche in the casino floor. Players loved the "Bonus" payouts for four-of-a-kind, but many found it frustrating to hit a quad of 10s only to receive a standard payout while a quad of 4s paid a premium. Manufacturers responded with "Deluxe," which simplified the bonus structure. It has since become a cult classic, known for its straightforward yet high-variance gameplay.

## Pay Table Verification: 9/6 vs. 8/5 Bonus Poker Deluxe

As with Jacks or Better, the strength of a Bonus Poker Deluxe machine is primarily determined by the Full House and Flush payouts.

*   **9/6 Bonus Poker Deluxe:** Pays 9 for a Full House and 6 for a Flush. This is the gold standard and offers an RTP of 99.64% with perfect play.
*   **8/5 Bonus Poker Deluxe:** This version is much more common in modern casinos but drops the RTP significantly to 97.40%.

Understanding **Pay Table Verification** is crucial here because the "Deluxe" 4-of-a-kind payout (usually 80-for-1 for *any* rank) is so attractive that players often ignore the "leak" in the middle of the pay table. ## Bonus Poker Deluxe vs. Jacks or Better: Comparative Analysis

The primary difference is where the "value" of the game is stored:
1.  **Four of a Kind:** In Jacks or Better, all quads pay 25-for-1. In Bonus Poker Deluxe, all quads pay 80-for-1.
2.  **Two Pair:** To fund that massive quad boost, Two Pair only pays 1-for-1 (Even Money). In Jacks or Better, it pays 2-for-1.

This shift creates a much higher **Volatility Index**. Your bankroll will deplete faster on a Deluxe machine because Two Pairs (which occur frequently) don't provide any profit—they only return your bet. You are essentially paying a "volatility tax" for the chance to hit those frequent 80-for-1 payouts.
