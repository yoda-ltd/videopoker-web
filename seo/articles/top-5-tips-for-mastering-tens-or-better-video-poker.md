# Top 5 Tips for Mastering Tens or Better Video Poker

Tens or Better is often recommended as the best starting point for new video poker players. Because the minimum winning hand is lower than in Jacks or Better (a pair of 10s instead of Jacks), the game features lower volatility and more frequent winning sessions. However, mastering Tens or Better still requires discipline and a commitment to the mathematical framework of **video poker fairness and regulation**.

Here are the top five professional tips for mastering Tens or Better, supported by modern **Technical Standards**.

## 1. Respect the "Tens" Threshold

The biggest difference in this variant is the value of the 10. 
- **The Tip:** Unlike standard poker, a lone 10 is now a "high card." If you have a hand with no pairs and a lone 10, you should hold it just as you would an Ace or King. 
- **Integrity Note:** The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures that 10s are not "weighted" to appear less frequently than Jacks or higher cards. Every rank has an exactly equal chance of being dealt.

## 2. Unlearn the Jacks or Better Strategy

Many players mistakenly use Jacks or Better strategy for Tens or Better. 
- **The Tip:** If you are dealt a pair of 10s, hold them. In other games, you might discard them to chase a higher-value draw, but in Tens or Better, they are a guaranteed win. 
- **Fairness Note:** Independent **Lab Audits** by firms like **GLI** verify that holding a pair of 10s provides a higher mathematical Expected Value (EV) in this game than discarding them.

## 3. Prioritize Bankroll Longevity

Tens or Better is a "low variance" game, meaning it is designed for steady, long-term play. 
- **The Tip:** Use this game to "churn" comps and earn casino loyalty points. Because the volatility is low, your bankroll will last longer, allowing you to maximize your hourly return from secondary benefits.
- **Integrity Note:** **Return to Player (RTP) Verification** ensures that the "Low Variance" nature of the game is consistent across millions of simulated hands.

## 4. Always Play 5 Coins (Max Bet)

Regardless of the variant, the "5-Coin Rule" remains supreme. 
- **The Tip:** The jackpot for a Royal Flush jumps from 250-for-1 to 800-for-1 when you play the maximum coins. Never bet fewer than 5 coins; if the stakes are too high, find a machine with a lower denomination.
- **Security Note:** Regulators like the **NGCB** or **UKGC** ensure that the machine correctly awards the 5-coin bonus as described on the pay table.

## 5. Play Only "Full Pay" Machines

To get the most out of Tens or Better, you must find a machine with a high payout for Full Houses and Flushes. 
- **The Tip:** Look for "6/5" or higher pay tables. Because Tens or Better is easier to win, the payouts are naturally lower than Jacks or Better, but playing on "Short Pay" machines will still erode your edge.
- **Fairness Note:** **Jurisdictional Mandates** require casinos to accurately display these payout tables. Always verify the pay table before you begin your session.

Mastering Tens or Better is about embracing the lower threshold for success and staying disciplined with your high-card holds.
