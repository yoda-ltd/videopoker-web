# Royal Aces vs. Super Double Bonus: Which Has the Better House Edge?
Category: Comparison Guide

For fans of high-volatility video poker, the choice often comes down to two heavyweights: **Royal Aces** and **Super Double Bonus (SDB)**. Both games offer massive payouts for 4-of-a-kind hands, but they distribute their Expected Value (EV) in very different ways. This article provides a **royal aces video poker strategy** comparison to help you decide which game offers the better house edge and better fit for your playstyle.

## The Mathematical Core: Where the Money Goes

The primary difference between these two games is how they reward "Quads."

### Quad Payouts (5-Coin Bet)
| Hand | Royal Aces | Super Double Bonus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Four Aces | 2,000 Coins | 800 Coins |
| Four 2s-4s | 400 Coins | 400 Coins |
| Four 5s-Ks | 250 Coins | 250 - 400 Coins |

**Super Double Bonus** is more "balanced" across all 4-of-a-kind hands. It rewards you significantly for hitting *any* set of quads. **Royal Aces**, on the other hand, is "Aces-heavy." It puts a massive premium (2,000 coins) on the Four Aces while keeping other quads relatively standard.

## House Edge Comparison: 9/6 Pay Tables

When comparing the "Full Pay" (9/6) versions of both games:

1.  **Super Double Bonus (9/6):** Typically offers a Return to Player (RTP) of **99.69%**.
2.  **Royal Aces (9/6):** Typically offers a Return to Player (RTP) of **99.20%**.

**The Verdict on House Edge:** On paper, **Super Double Bonus (9/6) has the better house edge** (0.31% vs. 0.80%). 

However, "Full Pay" machines are becoming rare. In most casinos, you are more likely to find 8/5 or 7/5 versions. If you find a 9/6 Royal Aces machine next to an 8/5 Super Double Bonus machine, the Royal Aces game becomes the superior mathematical choice.

## Variance and Bankroll: The "Swing" Factor

While SDB has a slightly better house edge, the **variance** in Royal Aces is unique. 

*   **Royal Aces Strategy:** Because of the 2,000-coin bonus for Quad Aces, your **Aces-heavy EV** is through the roof. You will find yourself discarding "winning" hands (like a low pair) to chase a single Ace more often in Royal Aces than in SDB. 
*   **SDB Strategy:** SDB is more about the "overall" quad hunt. You are less incentivized to "break" hands for Aces because the gap between Quad Aces (800) and Quad Jacks (400) isn't as massive as the 2,000 vs. 250 gap in Royal Aces.

## Kicker Dependencies

*   **Royal Aces:** Usually does not feature kicker bonuses. You just need the four cards.
*   **Super Double Bonus:** Also usually avoids kicker bonuses (unlike its cousin, Double Double Bonus), making the strategy "cleaner" but still high-variance.

## Which Should You Play?

*   **Play Super Double Bonus if:** You want the best possible mathematical return (in 9/6 form) and prefer a game that rewards all 4-of-a-kind hands generously.
*   **Play Royal Aces if:** You are a "jackpot hunter." The 2,000-coin payout for Aces is 50% of a Royal Flush and occurs 10-12 times more frequently. If you can handle the extreme variance and the "Aces-or-bust" mentality, Royal Aces is the more thrilling game.

## Conclusion

While Super Double Bonus wins on pure RTP in its 9/6 format, **Royal Aces video poker strategy** offers a specialized "Aces-heavy" experience that many players find more rewarding in short sessions. Always check the pay table—a 9/6 version of either game is a rarity that should be played immediately, but always be aware that Royal Aces requires a significantly larger bankroll to survive the hunt for those 2,000 coins.
