# Why All American is Better Than Sevens Wild for Beginners

When a beginner steps up to a Video Poker machine, they are often drawn to "Wild Card" games like **Sevens Wild**. The idea that any 7 can become any card you need feels like a huge advantage. However, for the smart beginner, **All American is the much better choice**.

In this guide, we’ll explain why the "Safe" path of All American is actually more profitable and more educational than the "Wild" path of Sevens Wild.

## 1. The Trap of the Wild Card

Wild cards are a "tax" on your bankroll. To pay for the fact that 7s are wild, the casino reduces the payouts for almost every other hand.
*   **Sevens Wild:** You typically need **Three of a Kind** just to get your money back. You get nothing for a High Pair or Two Pair.
*   **All American:** You get your money back for a **High Pair (Jacks or Better)**. 
*   **The Beginner’s Reality:** As a beginner, you will naturally hit pairs and two-pairs much more often than Three of a Kind. In All American, these hands keep you in the game. In Sevens Wild, they are "dead" hands that take your money.

## 2. Transferable Skills

Learning Video Poker is like learning a language. 
*   **All American:** Uses the standard "Language" of poker. The skills you learn (hand rankings, deck probabilities) apply to Jacks or Better, Bonus Poker, and even live poker games.
*   **Sevens Wild:** Uses a "Slang" that only works in Sevens Wild. If you learn to play Sevens Wild and then try to play a regular poker game, you will be completely lost.

## 3. Higher Mathematical Return (RTP)

If you are playing to win, the numbers don't lie.
*   **All American (8/8/8):** **100.72% RTP**. (The player has the advantage).
*   **Sevens Wild (Full Pay):** **~100.56% RTP**.
*   **The Reality:** While both are good, All American offers a slightly higher theoretical return. More importantly, finding an 8/8/8 All American machine is often easier than finding the "Perfect" Sevens Wild table.

## 4. Simplified Strategy

Playing with wild cards is mentally exhausting for a beginner.
*   **In Sevens Wild:** You have to change your strategy every hand based on whether you have zero, one, two, or three 7s. 
*   **In All American:** The strategy is consistent. Once you learn the "Priority List" (e.g., holding 4-card flushes over low pairs), you can play with 100% accuracy for hours.

## 5. Better "Mid-Tier" Payouts

All American is famous for its **8:1 payouts** for Straights and Flushes.
*   **In Sevens Wild:** Because wild cards make Straights and Flushes easier to hit, the payouts are much lower (usually 2:1 or 3:1).
*   **The Feeling:** There is nothing more satisfying in All American than hitting a Flush and seeing your credits jump by 40 units (on a 5-coin bet). In Sevens Wild, hitting a flush feels like a minor "pity prize."

## Summary Table: All American vs. Sevens Wild

| Feature | All American | Sevens Wild |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Minimum Win** | Pair of Jacks | 3 of a Kind |
| **Strategy Style** | Consistent/Strategic | Varied/Complex |
| **Skill Building** | Excellent | Poor |
| **Bankroll Survival** | High | Low (High Volatility) |

## Conclusion

If you want to "play around" and see some wild cards, Sevens Wild is fine. But if you want to **become a better poker player** and give yourself the **best chance to win**, All American is the superior choice. It rewards your skill, builds your poker foundation, and offers the highest payouts for the hands you’ll hit most often. Start with All American!
