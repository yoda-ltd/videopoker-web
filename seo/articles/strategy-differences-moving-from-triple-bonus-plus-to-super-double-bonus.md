# Strategy Differences: Moving from Triple Bonus Plus to Super Double Bonus

The move from Triple Bonus Plus to Super Double Bonus (SDB) is a move from "Ultra-High Variance" to "Balanced High Variance." This **best video poker strategy comparison** examines the **Strategy Bifurcation** and **Variance Scaling** involved in moving between two of the most popular "Bonus" variants in the casino.

## The Mathematical Foundation: Triple vs. Nested

**Triple Bonus Plus** is an "All-in" game. It pays massive "Triple" bonuses for Four-of-a-Kind (Quads). To fund these 400-to-1 or 800-to-1 payouts, the game uses heavy **Payout Compression** on common hands like Two-Pair (often reduced to 1-to-1) and potentially on the "Full House/Flush" payouts.

**Super Double Bonus** is a "Nested" game. It pays different bonuses for different sets of Quads. 4 Aces pay more than 4 Kings/Queens/Jacks, which pay more than 4 Threes/Fours/Fives. This creates a more "Balanced" distribution of the return. While the Quads pay less than the "Triple" bonuses, they hit more "Uniformly" across the deck.

## Variance Scaling and the Bankroll Buffer

1.  **Triple Bonus Plus:** The variance is extremely high (often 50+). It is a "Boom or Bust" game. The **Variance Scaling** is steep because so much of the return is tied to rare Quad hits. You need a massive bankroll to survive the "Dry" spells.
2.  **Super Double Bonus:** The variance is high (around 45) but more "Predictable." The **Variance Scaling** is slightly smoother because you are "Hunting" multiple different Quad bonuses. The **EV Gap** in an average session is slightly smaller than in Triple Bonus Plus.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Hold" Logic

The transition requires a shift in "Targeting":
*   In **Triple Bonus Plus**, you are a "Specialist." You hold small pairs and high pairs with extreme priority, hoping for that "Triple" hit. You accept the "Money Back" nature of Two-Pair as the price of admission.
*   In **Super Double Bonus**, you are a "Generalist." You still hold pairs to chase Quads, but you also respect the "High Card" values more. You might hold a "High Pair" (Jacks-Kings) with more confidence in SDB because the "Bonus" is still significant but the "Safety" of the base payout is more important.

If you are a "Jackpot Hunter" who wants the biggest possible hit, stay with Triple Bonus Plus. If you are looking for a more "Sustainable" bonus experience that still offers huge payouts, Super Double Bonus is the mathematically superior choice for long-term play.
