# Strategy Differences: Moving from Tens or Better to Bonus Poker Deluxe

Tens or Better is widely regarded as one of the most forgiving video poker variants. It's the "entry-level" game for many. Transitioning to Bonus Poker Deluxe (BPD) requires a shift in how you value pairs and four-of-a-kind. This guide provides the best video poker strategy comparison to help you understand how to play video poker like a pro during this transition.

## The Tens or Better Baseline

In Tens or Better, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is built on the fact that any pair of 10s or higher is a push (1-for-1). This creates a very stable, low-volatility experience. When moving to Bonus Poker Deluxe, that "safety net" is removed. You now need at least a pair of Jacks to get your money back.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from Tens or Better to BPD, you'll find that **85% of your strategy remains the same**. The **15% that changes** is entirely focused on the "Pair threshold." This 15% shift causes the most **Bankroll Drain** if not corrected.

### The 15% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Pair of Tens Devaluation:** This is the most critical shift. In Tens or Better, a pair of 10s is a winning hand. In BPD, it's a "trash" hand that you should rarely hold unless you're drawing for something better (like a three-card Royal Flush).
2.  **Four of a Kind Focus:** BPD is named "Deluxe" because it pays a flat 80-for-1 for *any* Four of a Kind. This makes four-of-a-kind much more valuable than in Tens or Better.
3.  **Two Pair Strategy:** While Two Pair pays 2-for-1 in both games, the higher payout for Four of a Kind in BPD means you might break a Full House to draw for a fourth card more often than you would in Tens or Better.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

Bonus Poker Deluxe introduces **Volatility Scaling**. Without the 10s-or-better push, your bankroll will experience more frequent dips. **EV Erosion** occurs when a Tens-or-Better player instinctively holds a pair of 10s in BPD. This is a "dead hand" and holding it is a direct loss of expected value.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Jacks or Better are your new minimum "win."
*   **Modify:** Discard any pair of 10s unless they are part of a better draw.
*   **Target:** Aggressively pursue Four of a Kind; they are the heart of the BPD pay table.

By recognizing that the "safety net" has moved, you can successfully transition to BPD without suffering from significant EV Erosion.
