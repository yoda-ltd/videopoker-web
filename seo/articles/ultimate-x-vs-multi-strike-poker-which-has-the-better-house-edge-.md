# Ultimate X vs. Multi-Strike Poker: Which has the better house edge?

Ultimate X and Multi-Strike Poker are two of the most popular "gimmick" video poker games that offer much more than standard play. Both are favorites for advantage players, but they approach **EV preservation** and variance in very different ways.

## Ultimate X: The Multiplier Loop
Ultimate X is built around the **Next-Hand Value**. By paying a premium, you earn **Accumulated Multipliers** for your subsequent round of play.
*   **House Edge:** On a 9/6 pay table with perfect play (practiced on an **ultimate x video poker trainer**), the edge is extremely low, often near 0.2%.
*   **The Vulture Factor:** The house edge can be virtually eliminated—or turned into a player advantage—if you know **how to find vulture machines**. Entering a game with pre-existing multipliers is the ultimate pro move.

## Multi-Strike Poker: The Level Up
Multi-Strike Poker is a 4-level game. If you win on Level 1, you move to Level 2 where payouts are 2x. This continues up to Level 4 (8x payouts).
*   **House Edge:** Multi-Strike also offers a low house edge on good pay tables, but it lacks the "persistent" state of Ultimate X. 
*   **Persistence:** In Ultimate X, the multipliers stay on the machine if you leave. In Multi-Strike, once your round is over, it’s over. You can't "vulture" a Multi-Strike machine in the same way.

## Comparing 3-Play, 5-Play, and 10-Play
Ultimate X is typically played in **3-play, 5-play, or 10-play** formats, which distributes the multiplier effect across multiple lines. Multi-Strike is a vertical game where you focus on a single hand at a time to "climb the ladder."

## Which is Better?
*   **Advantage:** Ultimate X. The ability to use the **Vulture Strategy** gives it a unique "scoutable" advantage that Multi-Strike doesn't have.
*   **Variance:** Both are high, but 10-play Ultimate X is arguably the most volatile video poker game in the casino.
