# Bonus Poker vs. Double Bonus: House Edge and Strategy Synergy

In the landscape of "best video poker strategy comparison," **Bonus Poker vs. Double Bonus** is a battle of bonus quads vs. high-card volatility. Both games belong to the "Bonus" category of Jacks or Better variants, and they both prioritize 4 Aces as the top payout below a Royal Flush. However, they distribute their return-to-player (RTP) through different **Payout Weighting** systems for the remaining quads, creating a massive **Strategy Bifurcation** and altering the fundamental **Payout Weighting** of each game.

## The House Edge Analysis: Bonus Aces vs. Double Payouts

To perform a professional **video poker house edge analysis**, we must examine the "Full Pay" versions of both.

| Game | Full Pay Version | Return (RTP) | House Edge |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bonus Poker** | 8/5 (99.17%) | 99.17% | 0.83% |
| **Double Bonus** | 10/7 (100.17%) | 100.17% | -0.17% (Player Edge!) |

While **Full Pay Double Bonus** is one of the rare games that can offer a positive return to the player (over 100%), it comes with a high price in terms of bankroll management and **Optimal Hold Variance**. **Bonus Poker** is the "safer" alternative, designed for players who want more frequent, albeit smaller, wins.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Safe" vs. "Aggressive" Hunt

The most significant **Strategy Bifurcation** occurs in the way you prioritize 4-of-a-kind.

*   **Bonus Poker Strategy:** Because *any* 4-of-a-kind pays 25-for-1 (or more for Aces, 2s, 3s, 4s), you will aggressively hold *any* pair. If you have a choice between a 4-card Straight and a low pair (2s-10s), the **Optimal Hold** in Bonus Poker is always the pair. The goal is to maximize your chances of hitting any 4-of-a-kind.
*   **Double Bonus Strategy:** In Double Bonus, 4 Aces pay a whopping 160-for-1. This means you will prioritize holding Aces over almost any other hand, including made Straights and Flushes. You will often break a made hand to chase a 4-card Royal Flush or 4 Aces, a move that would be mathematically incorrect in almost any other video poker variant.

## Strategy Synergy: Where the Games Align

Despite the different payout structures, there is some **Strategy Synergy** in the "Base Game" logic:

1.  **Royal Flush Priority:** In both games, a 4-card Royal Flush is the top-tier hold.
2.  **Aces are Key:** In both games, 4 Aces pay the largest bonus. You always prioritize Aces.
3.  **High Pair Stability:** Both games pay 1-for-1 for a pair of high cards (Jacks or Better), providing a stable floor for your bankroll.

## Variance Inversion: Bonus Quads vs. Extreme Volatility

The most jarring difference for players is the **Variance Inversion**.

*   **Bonus Poker:** The volatility is high (~20.0), but more "balanced." Because you have multiple tiers of bonus payouts (80-for-1, 40-for-1, 25-for-1), you will hit "significant" wins more frequently than in Double Bonus.
*   **Double Bonus:** The volatility is extreme (~28.0). Because your payouts are heavily dependent on hitting 4 Aces or 4 2s-4s, you will experience very long losing streaks and very massive wins. This is one of the highest volatility games in the video poker world, making it ideal for professional players with large bankrolls.

## Conclusion: Which to Choose?

If you enjoy the simplicity of a game where *any* 4-of-a-kind is a massive win and want to minimize risk, **Bonus Poker** is an excellent choice.

However, if you are a professional player with a large bankroll and don't mind the extreme volatility of chasing 160-for-1 Aces, **Double Bonus** offers one of the best returns in the casino. For a complete **video poker house edge analysis**, always check the pay table: a Full Pay 10/7 machine for Double Bonus is a must, but Bonus Poker's stability is the holy grail for many video poker players.
