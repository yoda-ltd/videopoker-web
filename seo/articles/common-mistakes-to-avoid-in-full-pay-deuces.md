# Common Mistakes to Avoid in Full Pay Deuces

Full Pay Deuces Wild is the "Holy Grail" of video poker, offering a **Theoretical Return** of 100.76%. However, most players actually lose money on these machines because they make critical strategy errors.

### Overvaluing the Deuces
The most common mistake is holding a single Deuce when the strategy dictates discarding everything. While a Deuce is powerful, it is not always worth keeping if the rest of the hand is garbage. **Hit Frequency Optimization** in Deuces Wild requires a very specific set of rules that differ significantly from Jacks or Better.

### Breaking a Natural Straight or Flush
In many video poker games, you never break a Straight or a Flush. In Full Pay Deuces, there are situations where you *do* break them—specifically if you have four cards to a Royal Flush. Failing to make these "aggressive" moves will lower your RTP below 100%.

### Ignoring Bankroll Requirements
Because so much of the return in Deuces Wild is tied up in the Four Deuces (payout 200) and the Royal Flush (payout 800), the **Variance** is extremely high. Without proper **Variance Buffering**, you will bust out before you hit the big hands. For **Session Sustainability**, you need a much larger bankroll than you would for Jacks or Better.

### The Max Bet Question
"Is max bet always better for bankroll longevity?" In Full Pay Deuces, if you don't bet the 5th coin, the RTP drops from 100.76% to around 99%. You are literally turning a winning game into a losing one by not pressing that max bet button.

### Beginner Pitfalls
If you are looking for **video poker tips for beginners**, avoid Full Pay Deuces until you have mastered a simpler game. The strategy is counter-intuitive and easy to mess up. To learn **how to win video poker daily**, you need the discipline to stick to a complex strategy chart without second-guessing it.

### RNG Reality
Don't fall for the "the machine is hiding the deuces" myth. The **Pseudo-Randomness** of the RNG ensures that every card has an equal chance of appearing. If you haven't seen a Deuce in 20 hands, it's just a statistical fluctuation, not a conspiracy.

Master the strategy, manage your bankroll, and Full Pay Deuces can be your path to professional play.
