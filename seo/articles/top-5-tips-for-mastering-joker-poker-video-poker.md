# Top 5 Tips for Mastering Joker Poker Video Poker

Joker Poker (often called Joker Wild) is a unique variant that adds a 53rd card—the Joker—to the standard deck. This extra card introduces a "Wild" element that significantly changes the math of the game. To master Joker Poker, you must understand how to balance the "Wild" potential with standard poker strategy, all while relying on the framework of **video poker fairness and regulation**.

Here are the top five tips for mastering Joker Poker, backed by modern **Technical Standards**.

## 1. Respect the 53rd Card Probability

The most fundamental shift in Joker Poker is the deck size. 
- **The Tip:** Adjust your expectations for drawing specific natural cards. In a 53-card deck, the probability of drawing a natural Ace is slightly lower than in a 52-card deck.
- **Integrity Note:** The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures that the Joker has exactly the same 1-in-53 chance of appearing as any other card. The machine never "hides" the Joker to lower your returns.

## 2. Never Discard the Joker

This may seem obvious, but it is the golden rule of the game. 
- **The Tip:** If you are dealt the Joker, it MUST be part of your final hand. The only decision is which other cards to hold alongside it. 
- **Fairness Note:** Independent **Lab Audits** by firms like **GLI** verify that holding the Joker always provides a higher mathematical Expected Value (EV) than discarding it.

## 3. Prioritize "Kings or Better" Strategy

In the most common version of Joker Poker, the minimum winning hand is a pair of Kings. 
- **The Tip:** Do not hold a lone Ace or a lone King if you have no Joker. You need a pair of Kings or better to win. If you have a pair of Queens or Jacks, hold them only as a draw toward Three-of-a-Kind.
- **Integrity Note:** **RTP Verification** ensures that these "entry requirements" are balanced by the increased hit frequency provided by the Joker.

## 4. Master the "Wild" Royal Flush Strategy

Joker Poker has two Royal Flush payouts: Natural and Wild.
- **The Tip:** If you have the Joker and three cards to a Royal Flush, you have a guaranteed win. However, do not break a high-paying hand (like Four-of-a-Kind) to chase a Wild Royal unless the math specifically favors it. 
- **Security Note:** Regulators like the **NGCB** or **UKGC** ensure that the "Wild" logic in the game's **Source Code** correctly identifies and pays out the highest possible hand for the player.

## 5. Play Only "Full Pay" Kings-or-Better

The profitability of Joker Poker varies wildly between versions. 
- **The Tip:** Look for the "Kings or Better" version that pays 20-for-1 for Four-of-a-Kind and 7-for-1 for a Full House. Avoid "Aces or Better" versions unless they offer significantly boosted payouts for other hands.
- **Fairness Note:** **Jurisdictional Mandates** require casinos to display these payout tables clearly. Always verify the pay table before you deposit.

Mastering Joker Poker is about understanding the power of the 53rd card and being disciplined with your "Kings or Better" entry.
