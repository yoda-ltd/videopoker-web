# The Payout Gap: Double Jackpot Poker vs Super Star Poker Explained

In the world of professional video poker, understanding the "payout gap" between two similar-looking machines is the key to maintaining a long-term edge. Today we compare **Double Jackpot Poker** and **Super Star Poker**, focusing on the **Probability Distribution** of their top prizes and how to apply a winning **progressive video poker strategy**.

## Double Jackpot Poker: The King of Kickers

Double Jackpot Poker is a "Bonus" style game that pays extra for Quads (Four of a Kind) based on the "Kicker" (the 5th card). 

*   **Mathematical Convergence:** Because a significant portion of the RTP is tied up in these specific Quad+Kicker combinations, the game has higher volatility than Jacks or Better.
*   **Royal Flush Cycle:** The Royal Flush payout remains the standard 800-for-1 (at max bet), with a cycle of roughly 1 in 40,000 hands.

## Super Star Poker: Multi-Game Volatility

Super Star Poker isn't just one game; it's a platform that often includes multipliers and "Super" bonus rounds. The "gap" here is often found in the cost of the features.

*   **Expected Value (EV):** To get the "Super Star" bonuses, you often have to bet more than 5 coins. This fundamentally changes your **Progressive Reset EV**.
*   **The Payout Gap:** If you play Super Star Poker without the bonus bet, the pay table is often "crippled" (e.g., 8/5 instead of 9/6). This creates a massive gap in the **Break-even Meter** requirements compared to a standalone Double Jackpot machine.

## Chasing the Royal: Strategy Differences

When it comes to **how to hit a royal flush in video poker**, the two machines require different mentalities.

### 1. Breaking Hands for the Royal
*   **In Double Jackpot:** Since the Quads pay so well, you are less likely to break a "Trip" (Three of a Kind) to chase a 3-card Royal, unless the progressive jackpot is extremely high.
*   **In Super Star:** If you have a multiplier active, the value of the Royal Flush skyrockets. A 5x multiplier on a Royal is a "life-changing" event. In these cases, your strategy shifts entirely toward **Jackpot Chasing**.

### 2. The Math of the Multiplier
In Super Star Poker, the multiplier affects the **Probability Distribution** of your returns. If you have a 10x multiplier, the "Value" of the Royal Flush in your EV calculation isn't 4,000 coins; it's 40,000 coins. 
*   **The Result:** At 10x, you should break almost *any* hand (including a Straight Flush!) to chase even a 3-card Royal.

## The Break-Even Meter Comparison

To find a 100%+ RTP game, you must look at the meters:

| Game Type | Standard Break-even (Royal) | Feature Cost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Double Jackpot (9/6)** | 10,790 coins | 5 Coins |
| **Super Star (9/6)** | 13,500 coins | 6-10 Coins |

The "Gap" exists because Super Star Poker requires more capital to activate the high-EV features, meaning the jackpot must be much higher to compensate for the "Feature Tax."

## Conclusion

If you prefer a steady chase with high Quad payouts, **Double Jackpot Poker** is your game. However, if you are a "Whale" looking for the absolute highest peak in **progressive video poker strategy**, the multipliers of **Super Star Poker** offer a higher (though more expensive) ceiling. Understanding **how to hit a royal flush in video poker** is about more than just cards; it's about knowing which machine's gap is easier to bridge.
