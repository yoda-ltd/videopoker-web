# Double Bonus Tournaments: Rules and Winning Tactics

Video poker tournaments offer a unique twist on the standard game. Instead of playing against the house edge for your own money, you compete against other players for a prize pool. Double Bonus Poker is a frequent choice for tournament directors because its high volatility and large bonus payouts create exciting, come-from-behind victories.

## How Double Bonus Tournaments Work

In a typical Double Bonus tournament:
1.  **Entry Fee:** Players pay a set fee to enter.
2.  **Starting Credits:** Everyone starts with the same number of credits (e.g., 1,000 or 2,000).
3.  **Time Limit:** You have a set amount of time (e.g., 15 or 20 minutes) or a set number of hands to play.
4.  **Ranking:** Players are ranked based on their ending credit balance. The top finishers share the prize pool.

## Key Differences from Standard Play

Tournament strategy is fundamentally different from cash game strategy. In a cash game, you want to maximize your long-term mathematical return (RTP). In a tournament, you want to maximize your **peak score** within a limited timeframe.

### 1. Speed is Essential
In most tournaments, the goal is to play as many hands as possible before time runs out. If you leave credits on the machine, you've wasted opportunities to hit a big hand.
*   **Tip:** Practice playing quickly. Don't overthink marginal decisions.
*   **Tactical Note:** If you hit a massive hand (like a Royal Flush) early and are far ahead of the field, you might actually slow down to "protect your lead," but this is rare.

### 2. High Variance is Your Friend
In a cash game, variance is something to be managed. In a tournament, especially if you are behind, you **need** variance.
*   **Tactical Note:** Because Double Bonus pays 800-for-1 for Four Aces and 400-for-1 for Four 2s-4s, it is the perfect tournament game. One lucky draw can catapult you from last place to first.

### 3. Strategy Adjustments for Tournaments

In a tournament, you may need to deviate from "perfect" math to chase the big scores that will actually win the prize.

*   **Chasing the Royal:** In a cash game, you might hold a high pair over a 3-card Royal Flush. In a tournament, if you need a huge score to catch up, you should almost always hold the Royal Flush draw.
*   **The "Aces" Strategy:** Since Four Aces pays so much in Double Bonus, tournament players often prioritize holding a single Ace or a pair of Aces much more aggressively than in a cash game.
*   **Ignoring Small Wins:** If you are nearing the end of the tournament and need 2,000 credits to reach the top 3, a 5-credit "Jacks or Better" win doesn't help you. In desperate situations, players might break winning hands (like a Straight) to chase a Royal Flush or Four Aces.

## Top Tactics for Winning

### The "Early Game" (First 75% of Time)
Play with a standard, aggressive Double Bonus strategy. Focus on speed and accuracy. You want to build a solid base and hope for one of the "Double" bonus hands (Four of a Kind) to put you in the top tier of players.

### The "End Game" (Last 25% of Time)
Check the leaderboard if it's visible.
*   **If you are in the Lead:** Play standard strategy, but perhaps slightly more conservatively. Don't take unnecessary risks.
*   **If you are in the Middle:** Look for opportunities to "swing" for a big hand. Start holding Royal Flush and Ace draws more aggressively.
*   **If you are far Behind:** Go for broke. Discard everything except cards that can lead to a Royal Flush or Four Aces. You won't win with Straights or Full Houses; you need a "Tournament Killer" hand.

## Summary

Double Bonus tournaments are about **explosive potential**. Unlike slower-paced games, Double Bonus allows players to leapfrog the competition in a single hand. By combining high-speed play with a strategy that favors the highest payouts, you give yourself the best chance to take home the trophy.

**Pro Tip:** Always check if the tournament machine uses a "Tournament Pay Table." Sometimes these tables are different from the ones on the casino floor to increase the frequency of big hits for spectator excitement!
