# Royal Flush Chasing: Strategy for High-Stakes Multi-Hand Video Poker

In the world of high-stakes video poker, the Royal Flush is the ultimate "Jackpot" event. While many amateur players rely on luck, professional "Royal Flush Chasing" is a disciplined pursuit that involves calculating **Expected Return (ER)**, understanding **Independent Draw Randomness**, and managing **Multi-Hand Volatility Scaling**. This guide explores the "best video poker for high volume" strategy for hitting a Royal Flush in 50-play and 100-play environments.

## The Mathematical Probability of a Royal Flush

Before you begin "chasing" the Royal, you must understand the core probabilities in a standard 52-card deck.

*   **Initial Deal:** 1 in 649,740.
*   **Holding 4 Cards to a Royal Flush:** 1 in 47 (approx. 2.12%).
*   **Holding 3 Cards to a Royal Flush:** 1 in 1,081 (approx. 0.09%).

For the professional, chasing a Royal Flush is about identifying when the **ER** of the draw exceeds the value of a "made" hand.

## How to Hit a Royal Flush in Video Poker: The Multi-Hand Advantage

The most effective way to hit a Royal Flush is to play multi-hand variants (3, 5, 10, 50, or 100-play). This is because each "click" of the draw button initiates multiple **Independent Trials** based on **Independent Draw Randomness**.

### 100-Play Royal Flush Strategy
In 100-play video poker, if you are dealt 4 cards to a Royal Flush in the **Shared Base Hand** (the deal), you have 100 independent chances to hit.
- **The Probability:** $1 - (46/47)^{100} \approx 88.5\%$. 
- **The "Smoothing Effect":** This high probability means that in 100-play, a 4-to-a-Royal deal is almost a "locked-in" win, significantly reducing the session-to-session variance of the Royal Flush cycle.

## Royal Flush In-the-Pocket: The Ultimate Lock-In

In high-stakes multi-hand play, the term **"Royal Flush In-the-Pocket"** refers to being dealt all 5 cards of a Royal Flush on the initial **Shared Base Hand**. 

- **The Correlation:** Because all 100 hands share the same deal, you "lock in" 100 Royal Flushes instantly. This results in a **400,000-coin win**. 
- **The Volatility Spike:** This is the peak of **Multi-Hand Volatility Scaling**. One single deal can change your life, but the probability remains 1 in 649,740.

## Best Video Poker Draw Strategy: Chasing vs. Playing Safe

Should you always "chase" the Royal Flush? The answer depends on the **Expected Return (ER)** and the **Combinatorial Convergence** of your session.

### Scenario: Holding 4 to a Royal vs. A Made Flush
In standard Jacks or Better, a Flush pays 6-for-1. 
*   **Holding the Flush:** Guaranteed 30 coins (on a 5-coin bet).
*   **Chasing the Royal (Holding 4):** ER is roughly 85.1 coins.
*   **The Decision:** Since 85.1 > 30, the "best video poker for high volume" strategy is to break the Flush and chase the Royal.

### Scenario: Holding 3 to a Royal vs. A High Pair
In Jacks or Better, a High Pair pays 1-for-1.
*   **Holding the Pair:** ER is roughly 7.5 coins total.
*   **Chasing the Royal (Holding 3):** ER is roughly 6.5 coins total.
*   **The Decision:** Keep the High Pair. The **Smoothing Effect** of the 100 independent draws on a High Pair hold is more valuable than the low-probability Royal chase.

## Multi Hand Video Poker Strategy and Bankroll

When "chasing" in multi-hand play, you must be prepared for the **Shared-Base Variance** to work against you.

- **Correlated Risk:** A string of "bad" base hands (e.g., being dealt 2, 5, 8, 10, Q across 100 lines) will deplete your bankroll 100 times faster than single-play.
- **Combinatorial Convergence:** Over a long session, the 100 independent draws will bring your results closer to the theoretical **ER**, but your bankroll must be deep enough to reach that point. 
- **Bankroll Benchmark:** For serious "Royal Flush Chasing" in 100-play, you should have at least **3,000 to 5,000 Royal Flush Equivalents (RFEs)**.
