# Bonus Poker Tournaments: Rules and Winning Tactics
Category: Tournament Strategy

Bonus Poker is the "goldilocks" of video poker tournaments—it offers higher payouts for Four of a Kind than Jacks or Better, but with less volatility than Double Double Bonus. For players looking to master **video poker tournament strategy**, Bonus Poker is the perfect training ground to balance **Session Speed** and point optimization.

## The Bonus Poker Speed-Play Index

In Bonus Poker, Four Aces pay 80-to-1, while Four 2s, 3s, or 4s pay 40-to-1. All other Quads pay 25-to-1. This simplified structure allows for a very high **Speed-Play Index**. Because there are no "Kickers" to worry about, you can maintain a blistering pace of 800+ hands per hour.

### Maximizing Tournament Points

The key to winning a Bonus Poker tournament is the "Quad Frequency." Since you don't need a kicker, your **Hand-Over-Hand ROI** is driven by how many times you can trigger the "Bonus" payouts.
*   **The "Ace-High" Bias:** In a tournament, if you are dealt a high pair (J, Q, K) vs. a single Ace, the decision is usually to hold the pair. However, some aggressive tournament strategies favor the single Ace to chase the 80-to-1 "Tournament Points" of Four Aces.
*   **Speed Efficiency:** Because the strategy is so similar to Jacks or Better, you can play this game "on autopilot," which is essential for maintaining your speed in a timed tournament heat.

## Video Poker Etiquette for Beginners: The Professional Floor

Even in the friendly atmosphere of a Bonus Poker bank, the "Unwritten Rules" of the casino floor apply.

1.  **Slot Attendant Relationships:** In a tournament, the **Slot Attendant** is your referee. If your machine freezes (a "tilt"), raise your hand and stay seated. Do not try to fix the machine yourself, as this can lead to disqualification.
2.  **Machine Reservation:** Outside of tournament hours, if you are playing Bonus Poker, it is standard to reserve your machine for a quick bathroom break by leaning the chair. But never do this during a tournament session—once you leave the chair, your session is over.
3.  **Social Play vs. Silence:** Many Bonus Poker players are "grinders" who prefer a quiet environment. If you are engaging in **Social Play**, keep your voice at a moderate level. The "Unwritten Rule" is that the person playing the fastest has the right to the least distraction.
4.  **Tipping on the "Small" Quads:** While a $1,200+ jackpot requires a tip, many players also give a small "hand-to-hand" tip to attendants if they hit a "dry" machine that suddenly turns "hot." This is part of the "Good Karma" etiquette of the floor.

## Tournament Tactics: The "Full House" Trap

In a standard game, a Full House is a great win. In a **video poker tournament strategy**, a Full House can actually be a "trap." It stops the clock for a "win animation," but doesn't provide enough points to win the tournament. Skilled tournament players often look for ways to minimize the "dead time" of these mid-tier win animations to get back to the "Deal" faster.

## Conclusion

Bonus Poker tournaments are a test of endurance and speed. By focusing on your **Speed-Play Index** and knowing which Quads carry the "Bonus" points, you can outpace the competition. Adhering to proper **video poker etiquette for beginners** will ensure you are respected by the staff and other players as you climb to the top of the leaderboard. Speed is king, but the "Bonus" is the crown!
