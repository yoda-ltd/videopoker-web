# Hot Roll Poker Pay Table Analysis: 9/6 vs. 8/5 vs. 7/5

In the world of high-volatility video poker, Hot Roll Poker stands as a titan. But even with 12x multipliers, the foundation of your success is built on the pay table. For those using a **hot roll video poker trainer**, the first lesson is learning how to spot the difference between a "player-friendly" machine and a "house-heavy" one. 

## The Hot Roll "Multiplier Tax"

Hot Roll Poker requires a 10-coin bet per line to activate the random dice multiplier. This multiplier appears on roughly 1 in 6 hands. Because the **Multiplier Roll Probability** includes the chance for a 12x boost, the base pay tables are often tighter than standard machines.

### Evaluating the Numbers:
*   **9/6 (Full House/Flush):** The gold standard. Rare in Hot Roll, but offers near 100% RTP when the multiplier is factored in.
*   **8/5:** The most common "fair" table found in high-limit rooms.
*   **7/5:** Common in "penny" or "nickel" denominations. This table requires a high frequency of multipliers just to break even.

## Analysis of Hot Roll's Dice Multiplier (Up to 12x)

The 12x multiplier is the "jackpot" of the feature, but it only has a **2.78% chance** of appearing when the Hot Roll is triggered. Most of your value comes from the 6x, 7x, and 8x rolls.

### Impact on Bankroll Volatility
The difference between a 9/6 and a 7/5 table is magnified by the multipliers. 
*   On a **9/6 table**, a 12x Full House pays **108 coins per coin bet** (540 coins total).
*   On a **7/5 table**, a 12x Full House pays only **84 coins per coin bet** (420 coins total).

That 120-coin difference on a single hand illustrates why the pay table matters *more* in Hot Roll Poker than in standard games.

## Strategy for Different Pay Tables

When using a **hot roll video poker trainer**, pay attention to how your "Hold" strategy might shift based on the table:

1.  **High-Tier (9/6):** Play standard optimal strategy. The high base payouts mean you don't need to "gamble" on long shots; the math is already in your favor.
2.  **Low-Tier (7/5):** The "Bleed" is real. You are essentially paying a high premium for the *chance* of a multiplier. In these games, hitting a "Natural" Royal Flush is your only real path to a winning session, as the small hands (Full House/Flush) won't sustain your bankroll.

## Conclusion

Hot Roll Poker is designed to distract you with the flash of rolling dice and the promise of 12x wins. However, a smart player looks past the dice at the Full House and Flush payouts. If you see a **7/5** table, proceed with caution—the volatility will be brutal. If you find an **8/5** or better, the Hot Roll feature provides a thrilling and mathematically sound way to chase the big multipliers.

Remember: The dice add the excitement, but the pay table provides the profit.
