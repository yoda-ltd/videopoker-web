# The Father of Video Poker: The Legacy of Si Redd and SIRCOMA

No exploration of the gambling industry is complete without discussing **William "Si" Redd**. Often called the "King of Slots" or the "Father of Video Poker," Redd’s vision in the late 1970s transformed the casino industry by introducing the world to **video poker fairness and regulation** through software-driven gaming.

## From Bally to SIRCOMA (1970s)
In the early 1970s, Si Redd was a distributor for Bally Manufacturing. He had a vision for a new kind of game: a video-based poker machine. At the time, Bally’s leadership was skeptical; they believed that players would never trust a machine to "shuffle" cards. 

Redd struck a deal: he kept the patent for the video poker game in exchange for a promise not to compete with Bally in the mechanical slot market. He founded **SIRCOMA** (Si Redd's Coin Machines) and began a revolution that would eventually necessitate the creation of modern **Technical Standards**.

## The Technological Leap: The First RNGs
In 1979, SIRCOMA released **Draw Poker**. This wasn't just a new game; it was the birth of regulated gaming software. 

*   **The First Digital Shuffles:** SIRCOMA’s machines used primitive **Random Number Generators (RNG)** burned into physical **EPROM** chips. 
*   **The Foundation of Integrity:** These early chips were the precursors to the **trusted video poker RNG certification** we see today. For the first time, a player could play a game where the "dealer" was a mathematical algorithm that could be audited for fairness.

## The Birth of IGT and Global Regulation (1980s)
SIRCOMA went public in 1980 and changed its name to **International Game Technology (IGT)**. Under Redd’s leadership, IGT refined the software logic of video poker. This growth forced regulators in Nevada and beyond to create the first **Jurisdictional Mandates** for electronic gaming. 

The industry began to rely on independent **Lab Audits** to ensure that IGT's software was truly random. This era established the roles of testing labs like **GLI (Gaming Laboratories International)**, which became the guardians of **game integrity** on the casino floor.

## Why Si Redd’s Logic Still Matters in 2026
The "Draw Poker" logic developed by Redd’s team remains the blueprint for modern video poker regulation:
1.  **The Deal:** A random 5-card hand is drawn from a virtual 52-card deck, verified by **RNG Certification**.
2.  **The Hold:** The player selects cards to keep using a digital interface.
3.  **The Draw:** The remaining cards are replaced by new cards from the same virtual deck, a process governed by **Technical Standards** to ensure no cards are "re-used" or "hidden."

This transparent, mathematical process is what makes the game fair and beatable by skilled players. Today, whether you are playing an HTML5 game or a server-based terminal, you are interacting with the software legacy of Si Redd.

## Conclusion: A Visionary’s Success
Si Redd passed away in 2003, but his impact is felt every time a player hits the "Deal" button. He took video poker from a niche experiment to the most played game on the casino floor by proving that software could be just as fair as a human dealer—if not more so. His commitment to mathematical integrity is the reason we have the robust **video poker fairness and regulation** framework that protects players today.
