# Risk vs. Reward: Sevens Wild vs. Super Aces Bonus

Sevens Wild and Super Aces Bonus (SAB) represent two different ways to "Amplify" your video poker wins. One uses "Wild Cards" (Sevens), while the other uses "Jackpot Payouts" (Aces). This **best video poker strategy comparison** examines the **Variance Scaling** and **Payout Compression** of both "High-Reward" formats.

## The Mathematical Foundation: Wild vs. Bonus

**Sevens Wild** is a "Wild Card" game. The four Sevens (7s) are the engine of the game. The "Reward" is hitting "Four Sevens" (200-to-1) or a "Wild Royal Flush." The "Risk" is that you get "Nothing" for anything less than Three-of-a-Kind. This is the ultimate form of **Payout Compression**.

**Super Aces Bonus** is a "Bonus" game. It is a Jacks or Better variant that pays a massive premium for 4 Aces (often 160-to-1 or 800 coins). To fund this, the game uses **Payout Compression** on other Quads and potentially on mid-tier hands. The "Reward" is a massive payout for a specific "Natural" combination.

## Variance Scaling and the Bankroll Buffer

1.  **Sevens Wild:** The variance is high. You are "Locked" into the math of the Sevens. The **Variance Scaling** is steep because the game doesn't provide a "Safety Net" for standard pairs. You can go hundreds of hands without hitting a "Winning" hand.
2.  **Super Aces Bonus:** The variance is also high (around 40-50). It is a "Hunting" game. The **Variance Scaling** is steep because the game relies on the Aces to achieve its theoretical return. The **EV Gap** between a session with Aces and one without is massive.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Seven" vs. The "Ace"

The **Strategy Bifurcation** is a total shift in "Target" logic:
*   In **Sevens Wild**, the "Seven" is king. You will hold a single Seven over *everything* else. You treat the Seven as a "Value" multiplier that builds every hand.
*   In **Super Aces Bonus**, the "Ace" is the goal. You will hold a single Ace with extreme priority. You treat the Ace as a "Jackpot" trigger.

If you enjoy the "Rush" of building hands with wild cards, Sevens Wild is your game. If you prefer the "Strike" of a massive natural payout for 4 Aces, Super Aces Bonus offers the ultimate "Ace-Hunting" experience. Both require a disciplined bankroll and a deep understanding of the "Target" hand's frequency.
