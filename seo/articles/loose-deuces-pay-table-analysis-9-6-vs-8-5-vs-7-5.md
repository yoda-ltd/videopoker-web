# Loose Deuces Pay Table Analysis: 9/6 vs 8/5 vs 7/5

Loose Deuces is a "specialty" video poker game famous for its massive **500-for-1 payout for Four Deuces**. However, like all video poker variants, there is a "Full Pay" version and several "Short Pay" versions. Understanding the difference between a 9/6, 8/5, or 7/5 structure in Loose Deuces is vital because the high volatility of the game can bankrupt a player quickly if the house edge is too high.

## The Full Pay Loose Deuces Standard
The "Full Pay" version of Loose Deuces offers a theoretical return of **100.97%**. This is even higher than Full Pay Deuces Wild (100.76%). 

To get this 100.97% EV, the pay table must look like this:
*   **Four Deuces:** 500
*   **Wild Royal:** 25
*   **Five of a Kind:** 12
*   **Straight Flush:** 10
*   **Four of a Kind:** 4

## Analysis of the 10/4 vs. 9/4 vs. 8/4 Tables
In Loose Deuces, players often refer to the "Straight Flush" and "Four of a Kind" payouts when comparing machines.

### 1. The 10/4 Table (The 100.97% Gold Standard)
*   **Straight Flush:** 10
*   **Four of a Kind:** 4
*   **EV:** 100.97%
*   **Strategy:** This game requires an extremely aggressive strategy for holding deuces. If you have 3 to a Royal and a Deuce, you throw away the Royal pieces and just keep the Deuce.

### 2. The 9/4 Table (The "Good" Commercial Game)
*   **Straight Flush:** 9
*   **Four of a Kind:** 4
*   **EV:** Approximately **99.5%**.
*   **Analysis:** By dropping the Straight Flush from 10 to 9, the house takes away about 0.5% of the return. While this is no longer a "player advantage" game, it is still significantly better than most slot machines.

### 3. The 8/4 Table (The Common Short-Pay)
*   **Straight Flush:** 8
*   **Four of a Kind:** 4
*   **EV:** Approximately **98.2%**.
*   **Analysis:** This is where the game becomes dangerous. Loose Deuces is already high-volatility. When you drop the EV to 98.2%, the frequency of the 500-to-1 jackpot isn't enough to compensate for the rapid loss of your bankroll on the base hands.

## Why 7/5 or 9/6 Labels Don't Always Apply
In Loose Deuces, you will rarely see a "5" for Four of a Kind. If you do, check the other payouts—usually, the **Five of a Kind** or **Straight Flush** will be severely gutted (e.g., Five of a Kind paying only 10 or 8).

### The "Wild Royal" Factor
Always check the **Wild Royal Flush** payout. 
*   **Full Pay:** 25-for-1
*   **Short Pay:** 20-for-1
If the Wild Royal drops to 20, the EV takes a massive **1.2% hit**. A 10/4 table with a 20-unit Wild Royal is actually worse than an 8/4 table with a 25-unit Wild Royal.

## House Edge and Volatility Math
Loose Deuces has a **Variance of approximately 74**. For comparison, Jacks or Better has a variance of 19.5. 

| Table | EV | House Edge | House Edge (on $1.25 bet) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Full Pay (10/4)** | 100.97% | -0.97% (Player Edge) | +$0.012 per hand |
| **Short Pay (8/4)** | 98.20% | 1.80% | -$0.022 per hand |

On a "Short Pay" machine, you are losing money twice as fast while still enduring the massive swings of a high-variance game.

## Training and Strategy
Because Loose Deuces strategy is so counter-intuitive (e.g., discarding a pat Straight to keep a single Deuce), you **must** use a trainer. 
1.  Open your software (e.g., WinPoker or Video Poker Trainer).
2.  Manually edit the pay table to match the machine in front of you (9/4, 8/4, etc.).
3.  The trainer will show you that as the Straight Flush payout drops, your willingness to chase it also drops, making the "Deuce-only" hold even more frequent.

## Conclusion
Loose Deuces is a thrill-seeker's game. To survive the swings, you need the highest pay table possible. Look for the **10/4** structure with a **25-unit Wild Royal**. Avoid 8/4 or 7/5 variations, as the math simply doesn't support the risk.
