# Why Tens or Better is Better Than Super Aces Bonus for Beginners

In the world of "Entry-Level" video poker, the choice between "Tens or Better" (ToB) and "Super Aces Bonus" (SAB) is a study in **Strategic Convergence** and the **Variance Index**. This **best video poker strategy comparison** breaks down why Tens or Better is the superior entry point for novices, focusing on **Payout Compression** and the "Strategy Drift" caused by the different minimum win and bonus structures.

## Strategic Convergence: The "Low-Complexity" Advantage

**Tens or Better** is the "Simplest" form of video poker. The strategy is almost identical to Jacks or Better, with a lower minimum win requirement (Tens). Because it follows the "Standard" draw mechanic with a very "Broad" winning range, the strategy is "Linear" and "Logical." Beginners can learn the basic "Hierarchy" of hands and apply it with extremely high accuracy. The **video poker house edge analysis** is the most straightforward.

**Super Aces Bonus** is a "High-Volatility" game with "Extreme" bonuses for 4 Aces. This creates "Strategy Bifurcation." For every hand, you must play with "Perfect" accuracy to achieve the theoretical return, while also managing "Ace" holds. For a beginner, this is "High-Pressure." One mistake on an "Ace" hand can cost you significantly more than a mistake in Tens or Better, where the "Lower Minimum Win" provides a "Safety Net." This "Strategy Drift" in SAB often leads to higher losses for new players.

## Payout Compression: The "Smooth" vs. The "Ace-Specific"

In **Tens or Better**, the "Cost" of the "Lower Minimum Win" is built into the *entire* payout table (especially the Full House and Flush). However, the game remains "Smooth" because the frequency of winning hands is the highest in the casino. Your bankroll "Churns" beautifully. You are "Rewarded" for hitting common hands very frequently.

In **Super Aces Bonus**, the "Cost" of the "Ace" bonuses is built into the Two-Pair and Full House payouts. To "Pay" for these rare "Ace" wins, the payouts for common hands are "Compressed." You often get only "Money Back" on Two-Pair. For a beginner, this is "Psychological Torture." You hit a "Winning" hand, but your bankroll doesn't grow. This "Bleed" is much more aggressive than in Tens or Better.

## The Variance Index: Sustainability vs. Volatility

Our **video poker house edge analysis** shows that:
*   **Tens or Better** has a variance of around **12-15**. It is the "Lowest-Volatility" game in the casino. It offers "Maximum Stability" and "Longevity."
*   **Super Aces Bonus** has a variance of around **60-80**. It is an "Extreme-Volatility" game. The "Gaps" between "Ace" hits are much longer.

For the new player who wants to enjoy a long session and learn the fundamentals with "Maximum Stability," Tens or Better is the clear winner. It provides the "Stability" of the lowest-volatility game with the "Safety Net" of frequent wins. Leave the "Ace-Only" hunting to the pros who can handle the "Pressure" and the "Swings."
