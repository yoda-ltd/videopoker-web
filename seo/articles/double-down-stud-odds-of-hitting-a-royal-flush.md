# Double Down Stud Odds of Hitting a Royal Flush

Double Down Stud is a unique video poker variant that plays differently than almost any other game in the casino. Because players only get to see four cards before deciding whether to "Double Down" their bet, the strategy and the resulting odds of hitting a Royal Flush are distinct from games like Jacks or Better or Deuces Wild.

## How Double Down Stud Works

In Double Down Stud:
1.  You are dealt one card face up.
2.  Three more cards are dealt face up.
3.  You have a choice: **Double your bet** or stay with your original wager.
4.  The fifth and final card is dealt.
5.  There are **no discards or draws**. You simply get the 5 cards you are dealt.

## The Basic Probability

Because there is no "draw" phase (where you can discard cards to improve your hand), the odds of hitting a Royal Flush in Double Down Stud are mathematically identical to being dealt a Royal Flush "on the fly" in a standard 52-card deck.

The number of possible 5-card hands in a 52-card deck is **2,598,960**.
There are only **4** ways to make a Royal Flush.

**The odds of a Royal Flush are 1 in 649,740.**

## Why the Odds are "Worse" than Standard Video Poker

In standard video poker (Draw Poker), you can discard cards. This allows you to "chase" a Royal Flush if you are dealt 3 or 4 cards of the sequence. This drastically increases the frequency of Royal Flushes to approximately **once every 40,000 to 45,000 hands**.

In Double Down Stud, you cannot discard. If you are dealt 4 cards to a Royal Flush, you just have to pray the 5th card is the one you need. You can't throw away a "junk" 5th card to try again. 

Therefore, **you will see roughly 15 times fewer Royal Flushes in Double Down Stud** than you would in a standard draw poker game.

## The "Double Down" Strategy for Royals

While you can't improve your *odds* of hitting a Royal Flush, you can improve your *payout* when you have a chance at one.

### When to Double Down
The "Double Down" decision is made after the 4th card is dealt. 
*   **If you have 4 cards to a Royal Flush:** You MUST Double Down. You have a **1 in 48** chance (since 4 cards are known and 48 are left in the deck) of hitting the Royal Flush on the final card. 
*   **If you have 3 cards to a Royal Flush:** Some strategies suggest Doubling Down if you have a high-potential hand (like 10-J-Q of the same suit), but generally, the math only favors doubling when you already have a "Winning Hand" (a pair of 6s or better in most versions).

## Payout Structure

A Royal Flush in Double Down Stud typically pays **800-for-1**. 
*   If you bet 5 coins and **don't** Double Down, you win **4,000 coins**.
*   If you bet 5 coins and **do** Double Down (making it a 10-coin total bet), you win **8,000 coins**.

Because hitting a Royal Flush is so rare (1 in 649,740), the "Royal Flush" makes up a much smaller percentage of the total Return to Player (RTP) in Double Down Stud than it does in Draw Poker. In this game, your profit comes from doubling down on pairs and two-pair hands, not from chasing the Royal.

## Summary: Royal Flush Frequency

| Event | Odds |
| :--- | :--- |
| Dealt 4 to a Royal | 1 in 13,536 |
| Hitting the 5th card (after Doubling) | 1 in 48 |
| **Total Royal Flush Frequency** | **1 in 649,740** |

**The Bottom Line:** Don't play Double Down Stud if you are "Royal Flush hunting." The game is designed around the unique betting mechanic, and the Royal Flush is a once-in-a-lifetime bonus rather than a strategic goal. If you want to see Royals more often, stick to Double Bonus or Jacks or Better.
