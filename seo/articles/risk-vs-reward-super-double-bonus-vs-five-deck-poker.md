# Risk vs. Reward: Super Double Bonus vs. Five-Deck Poker
Category: Comparison

Choosing between **Super Double Bonus** and **Five-Deck Poker** involves balancing the rewards of "High-Payout Natural Quads" against the "Multi-Deck Connectivity" of Five-Deck Poker. This creates a significant **Strategic Divergence** in how you approach **Volatility Normalization**.

### The Comparison Matrix: Super Double Bonus vs. Five-Deck Poker

| Feature | Super Double Bonus (10/7) | Five-Deck Poker (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.17% (Full Pay) | 99.40% |
| **Variance Index** | 28.5 | 20.0 |
| **Reward Focus** | High-Payout Natural Quads | Multi-Deck Connectivity (5 Decks) |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.17% (Player Edge) | 0.60% |

### Strategic Divergence: Quads vs. Connectivity

In **Super Double Bonus Poker**, the reward is concentrated in Achieving natural four-of-a-kind hands that pay a massive bonus. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through rank-based holds. In **Five-Deck Poker**, the reward is spread across natural combinations across five independent decks. This leads to a lower **Volatility Normalization** (20.0 vs 28.5).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 60% overlap on the core hand results. However, in Five-Deck Poker, you focus on Natural connectivity across all five decks, while in Super Double Bonus, you prioritize high-bonus natural quads. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the high-tier quads in Super Double Bonus dominate the EV landscape.

### Key Risk/Reward Factors
1.  **Super Double Bonus Reward:** A significant player advantage (0.17%) in the full-pay version, but with higher volatility.
2.  **Five-Deck Reward:** A consistent, lower-variance return with frequent natural wins across decks.
3.  **Strategy Complexity:** Super Double Bonus rewards those who can master the tiered quad strategy for the highest payouts.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that Super Double Bonus is the superior game for the expert player seeking an edge, while Five-Deck Poker is better for those who want a stable ride with unique deck features. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are elite choices for those who master their respective strategies.

### Strategic Divergence: Broad vs. Focused Bonuses
