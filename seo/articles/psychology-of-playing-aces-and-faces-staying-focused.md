# Psychology of Playing Aces and Faces: Staying Focused

Aces and Faces is a variant of Jacks or Better that offers bonus payouts for Four of a Kind in Aces, Kings, Queens, or Jacks. This creates a unique psychological landscape that requires a specific **Pro Mindset** and intense **Professional Discipline**.

## The Face Card Trap: The Dopamine Loop of Aces and Faces

In Aces and Faces, the extra rewards for Aces and Face Cards (Kings, Queens, Jacks) can lead to a powerful **Dopamine Loop**. 

- **The Dopamine Loop**: The "Bonus" in Aces and Faces is designed to trigger a stronger neurochemical response when you hit the big hand.
- **Expert Video Poker Strategy Tips**: To succeed, you must avoid the "Chasing" mentality. Do not hold two Aces in Aces and Faces because you "feel" like four Aces are coming. Stick to the mathematically correct play every time.
- **Loss Aversion Bias**: Players who lose back-to-back sessions in Aces and Faces are more likely to "Tilt" because they feel they "should" have hit a Four of a Kind by now. 

## Handling the "Royal Desert" and "Face Card" Droughts

The Royal Flush is still the largest payout in Aces and Faces, but the game is less reliant on it than other variants. However, going on a **Royal Desert** can still be a major psychological blow.

1. **Cognitive Resilience**: You must be able to withstand 100,000 hands without a Royal Flush, provided you are hitting enough Bonus hands to keep your bankroll healthy.
2. **The Bonus Drought**: If you go several hours without hitting four-of-a-kind in Aces or Face Cards, your bankroll will take a massive hit. This is the "Aces and Faces Grind."
3. **Tilt Mitigation**: When your bankroll is melting, the urge to play faster or "gamble" on draws becomes intense. **Tilt Control** is your only defense.

## Professional Discipline: The Assembly Line Approach

To learn **how to become a professional video poker player**, you must treat Aces and Faces like an assembly line. 

- **The Assembly Line**: Every hand is a transaction. There is no "feeling" that the Aces are coming.
- **Session Fatigue**: Because the strategy for Aces and Faces differs slightly from Jacks or Better, **Session Fatigue** is a major risk. A single error can wipe out your entire hourly edge.
- **Pro Mindset**: A pro judges their performance by the correctness of their holds, not by the session total.

## Responsible Play and the Pro Boundary

**Responsible video poker playing** in Aces and Faces means recognizing when the bonus payouts are causing you to take the strategy less seriously.

- **Bankroll is King**: If your bankroll is too small, you will play "scared," which leads to strategy errors.
- **Tilt Control**: If you find yourself angry at the machine for not "giving" you the bonus payout, you are in a state of Tilt and must stop immediately.

## Conclusion
Aces and Faces is a game of highs and lows. By focusing on **Tilt Control** and maintaining a rigorous **Pro Mindset**, you can harness the power of the "Bonus" and maintain your professional edge in this classic game.
