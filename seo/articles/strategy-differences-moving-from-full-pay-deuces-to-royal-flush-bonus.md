# Strategy Differences: Moving from Full Pay Deuces to Royal Flush Bonus
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Full Pay Deuces** to **Royal Flush Bonus** is a shift from a "Wild Card" variant to a "Natural" variant with a targeted jackpot. This represents a significant **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your hand results versus the additional features of the jackpot.

### The Comparison Matrix: Full Pay Deuces vs. Royal Flush Bonus

| Feature | Full Pay Deuces (100.76%) | Royal Flush Bonus (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.76% | 99.80% |
| **Variance Index** | 25.8 | 32.0 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Prioritize Deuces as Wild Cards | Target Royal Flush Jackpot |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.76% (Player Edge) | 0.20% |

### Strategic Divergence: Wild Connectivity vs. Royal Jackpot

In **Full Pay Deuces**, the four 2s are the most powerful cards in the deck, allowing you to complete hands that would otherwise be worthless. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through wild card connectivity. In **Royal Flush Bonus**, the game is natural, but the payout for a Royal Flush is significantly increased. This creates a much higher **Volatility Normalization** (32.0 vs 25.8).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 40% overlap. In Full Pay Deuces, you will hold a single 2 almost as if it were a high pair. In Royal Flush Bonus, you only care about Royal Flush cards (A, K, Q, J, 10). This creates a shift in **Payout Compression** where the "Middle Tier" hands (Straights/Flushes) are the focus in Deuces Wild, but the "Top Tier" hands (Royal Flush) are the focus in Royal Flush Bonus.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Deuce Devaluation:** When moving to Royal Flush Bonus, 2s are just another card in the deck. They no longer offer a wild card feature.
2.  **Royal Flush Priority:** In Royal Flush Bonus, you will break a Full House or a Flush to hold 3-to-a-Royal much more frequently than in any other game.
3.  **Natural Connectivity:** In Royal Flush Bonus, you are looking for natural straights and flushes. In Full Pay Deuces, you are looking for wild card connectivity.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games offer a player advantage in their full-pay versions, but Royal Flush Bonus provides a smoother ride with lower variance. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are top-tier choices for anyone looking for a player advantage.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Full Pay Deuces to Super Double Bonus
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Full Pay Deuces** to **Super Double Bonus** is a shift from a "Wild Card" variant to a "High Bonus" natural variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value four-of-a-kind hands and the volatility that comes with them.

### The Comparison Matrix: Full Pay Deuces vs. Super Double Bonus

| Feature | Full Pay Deuces (100.76%) | Super Double Bonus (10/7) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.76% | 100.17% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 25.8 | 28.5 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Deuce-Based Wilds | Target High-Payout Natural Quads |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.76% (Player Edge) | -0.17% (Player Edge) |

### Strategic Divergence: Wild Connectivity vs. Bonus Quads

In **Full Pay Deuces**, you are looking for 2s to complete your hands. In **Super Double Bonus Poker**, you are looking for natural four-of-a-kind hands that pay a massive bonus. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through high-tier natural quads. Super Double Bonus Poker's variance (28.5) is higher than Full Pay Deuces (25.8), requiring careful **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 30% overlap. In Full Pay Deuces, you would never discard a 2. In Super Double Bonus Poker, you will break a Full House to hold three Aces much more frequently than in any other game. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the high-tier hands in Super Double Bonus dominate the EV landscape.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Deuce Devaluation:** In Super Double Bonus Poker, 2s are only valuable if you have a pair or more. They no longer complete straights and flushes.
2.  **Ace Supremacy:** In Super Double Bonus Poker, Aces are the highest-paying quads. You prioritize them over almost any other card rank.
3.  **Full House Break:** You will break a Full House containing three Aces to draw for the fourth Ace. This is a major departure from Full Pay Deuces.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that Super Double Bonus Poker offers a player advantage in its full-pay version, but the volatility is higher. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are top-tier choices for the disciplined player.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Full Pay Deuces to Triple Aces
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Full Pay Deuces** to **Triple Aces** is a shift from a "Wild Card" variant to a "Targeted Bonus" natural variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value specific card ranks and their related bonuses.

### The Comparison Matrix: Full Pay Deuces vs. Triple Aces

| Feature | Full Pay Deuces (100.76%) | Triple Aces (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.76% | 99.60% |
| **Variance Index** | 25.8 | 25.0 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Deuce Connectivity | Target Aces Bonus |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.76% (Player Edge) | 0.40% |

### Strategic Divergence: Wild Card vs. Ace Jackpot

In **Full Pay Deuces**, the four 2s are your most powerful tools. In **Triple Aces Poker**, the focus is on achieving Three or Four Aces. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through specific high-tier combinations. Triple Aces' variance (25.0) is similar to Full Pay Deuces (25.8), providing a consistent **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is minimal (less than 20%). In Full Pay Deuces, you would never discard a 2. In Triple Aces, if you have two Aces and a kicker, you will keep the Aces to draw for the massive bonus. This is the essence of **Payout Compression** in Ace-based games.

### Critical Strategy Shifts
1.  **Ace Priority:** In Triple Aces, Aces are the most important natural cards. You prioritize them over almost any other card rank.
2.  **Deuce Devaluation:** In Triple Aces, 2s are just another card in the deck. They no longer offer a wild card feature.
3.  **Natural Connectivity:** In Triple Aces, you are looking for natural straights and flushes. In Full Pay Deuces, you are looking for wild card connectivity.

A **video poker house edge analysis** indicates that while Triple Aces is a fun, high-action game, it requires a higher degree of mental flexibility to master the "Natural" Ace strategy. The **best video poker strategy comparison** for this transition is: Forget the deuces; find the Aces.
