Keno Poker tournaments combine the traditional poker math with the fast-paced nature of Keno-style side bets. This guide provides a comprehensive overview of the rules and advanced tactics you need to dominate the leaderboard and maximize your **Hybrid RTP Analysis**.

## Basic Tournament Rules

In a Keno Poker tournament, all participants typically start with the same number of credits and a set amount of time (e.g., 20 minutes) to play as many hands as possible. The goal is to accumulate the highest total score, which is a combination of your poker hand rankings and Keno spot matches.

1. **Timed Sessions:** Speed is critical. Every second counts. Players who can quickly identify the best poker discards while simultaneously tracking their Keno spots will have a significant advantage.
2. **Fixed Credits:** Players cannot add more credits, making bankroll management within the tournament structure essential.
3. **Simultaneous Scoring:** Points are awarded for each poker hand (Jacks or Better, Three of a Kind, etc.) and each Keno spot match.
