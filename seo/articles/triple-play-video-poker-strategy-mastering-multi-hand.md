# Triple Play Video Poker Strategy: Mastering Multi-Hand Logic

Multi-hand video poker, specifically the popular Triple Play format, introduces a layer of **Multi-Hand Volatility Scaling** and excitement that single-hand games cannot match. When you play three hands at once, you aren't just tripling your bet; you are fundamentally changing the **Shared-Base Variance** of your session. To succeed, you must implement a disciplined **multi hand video poker strategy** that accounts for the **Smoothing Effect** of independent draws.

## The Triple Play Math: Shared Base Hand & Smoothing

In Triple Play, you are dealt one "Base Hand." Whatever cards you choose to hold are replicated across all three hands. The remaining cards for each hand are then drawn from three separate independent decks.

### 1. The Shared Base Hand Effect
Triple Play is significantly more volatile than single-hand play because of the **Shared Base Hand**.
- **Correlation:** If you are dealt a strong starting hand (like Three of a Kind), you are guaranteed three winning hands.
- **Volatility Scaling:** If the deal is poor, all three bets are at risk simultaneously. This "front-loads" the variance onto the initial deal.

### 2. Independent Draw Randomness
While the starting hand is shared, the draws are **Independent Trials**. This provides a subtle "Smoothing Effect."
- If you hold 4 cards to a Flush, each of the three hands has an independent 19.1% chance to complete the hand. 
- Over thousands of hands, this **Independent Draw Randomness** ensures that your results for common draws will move toward the theoretical **Expected Return (ER)**.

## Multi Hand Video Poker Strategy: The Professional Approach

1.  **Bankroll Scaling:** Professionals do not simply triple their bankroll for Triple Play. To handle the **Shared-Base Variance**, you should apply a bankroll multiplier of approximately **2.5x to 3.0x** your standard single-hand requirement for the same total "coin-in."
2.  **Best Video Poker for High Volume:** Triple Play is often the preferred starting point for players moving into high-volume play because it offers a balance between the speed of **Combinatorial Convergence** and manageable volatility.
3.  **Hold Strategy:** Your strategy never changes based on the number of hands. If the optimal play for the pay table is to hold a High Card, it remains the optimal play in Triple Play. The **ER** per hand remains identical.
4.  **Combinatorial Convergence:** By playing three hands per deal, you are reaching the "long run" three times faster than a single-line player. This is the primary advantage of multi-hand play.

## Surviving the Swings: Royal Flush Equivalents (RFEs)

In multi-hand formats, bankroll depth is measured in **Royal Flush Equivalents (RFEs)**. For Triple Play, hitting a Royal on the draw usually means hitting it on one hand, but hitting it on the deal (a "Pocket Royal") means hitting it on all three.

*   **Bankroll Goal:** Aim for a minimum of **2,000 to 3,000 RFEs** in your total bankroll when playing Triple Play professionally. This provides the necessary cushion to withstand the "clusters" of losing base hands that characterize **Multi-Hand Volatility Scaling**.

## Conclusion

Triple Play is the "gateway" to advanced multi-hand video poker. By understanding the math behind the **Shared Base Hand** and the **Independent Draw Randomness**, you can enjoy the faster action without falling victim to the increased volatility. Respect the **Shared-Base Variance**, manage your bankroll for **Multi-Hand Volatility Scaling**, and keep your eyes on the optimal **multi hand video poker strategy** for every deal.
