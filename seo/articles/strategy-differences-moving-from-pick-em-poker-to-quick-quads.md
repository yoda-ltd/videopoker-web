# Strategy Differences: Moving from Pick 'em Poker to Quick Quads
Category: Comparison

### Strategic Divergence: Picking vs. Drawing

In **Pick 'em Poker**, you are shown two fixed cards and must choose between two "stacks" of three cards. In **Quick Quads**, you are looking for the best five-card combination by drawing up to five new cards. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through drawing and additive combinations. Quick Quads' variance (35.0) is much higher than Pick 'em Poker (15.0), requiring careful **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is zero (0%). Both are fundamentally different games. Pick 'em Poker is a choice-based game; Quick Quads is a draw game with an additive twist. ### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Additive Knowledge:** You must learn the exact additive combinations in Quick Quads.
2.  **Drawing Priority:** In Quick Quads, you are looking for pairs and trips based on the rank. In Pick 'em Poker, you are always choosing between two fixed outcomes.
3.  **Variance Normalization:** Quick Quads has much higher variance (35.0) than Pick 'em Poker (15.0). This means your bankroll will last longer in Pick 'em Poker, but your wins will be more structured.

A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are solid games for the expert player, offering competitive returns with unique gameplay. For players who find the picking strategy of Pick 'em Poker too simplistic, Quick Quads Poker is the ultimate alternative for **EV Maximization**.
