# Why Aces and Faces is Better Than Aces and Eights for Beginners

In the world of "Ace-Focused" video poker, the choice between "Aces and Faces" (AF) and "Aces and Eights" (AE) is a study in **Strategic Convergence** and the **Variance Index**. This **best video poker strategy comparison** breaks down why Aces and Faces is the superior entry point for novices, focusing on **Payout Compression** and the "Strategy Drift" caused by the different bonus structures.

## Strategic Convergence: The "Linear-Bonus" Advantage

**Aces and Faces Poker** is a "Standard-Draw" game with "Bonus" payouts for specific 4-of-a-Kind hands (Aces and Face cards). The strategy is almost identical to Jacks or Better, with minor adjustments for the "Quad" hands. Because it follows the "Standard" draw mechanic with a "Linear" bonus structure, the strategy is "Linear" and "Logical." Beginners can learn the basic "Hierarchy" of hands and apply it with high accuracy. The **video poker house edge analysis** is straightforward and predictable.

**Aces and Eights** introduces a "Niche" bonus structure that focuses on Aces, Eights, and Sevens. This creates "Strategy Bifurcation." For every hand, you must now ask: "Is the Aces and Eights bonus better than the standard hold?" The strategy is "Unique" and often "Counter-Intuitive" compared to standard bonus games. For a beginner, this is "Cognitive Overload." You can easily make a mistake by holding "Sevens" when the math suggests a different path. This "Strategy Drift" can cost you 1-2% in "Theoretical Return" over a session.

## Payout Compression: The "Smooth" vs. The "Niche-Specific"

In **Aces and Faces**, the "Cost" of the "Face Card" bonuses is built into the payout table. However, the game remains "Smooth" because the "Base Payouts" are standard for a bonus-style game. Your bankroll "Churns" nicely. You are "Rewarded" for hitting common hands with a "Fair" payout.

In **Aces and Eights**, the "Cost" of the "Niche" bonuses is built into the *entire* payout table (especially the Full House and Flush). To "Pay" for these specific "Aces and Eights" wins, the payouts for *all* other hands are "Compressed." You often get only "Money Back" on hands that would pay more in a standard game. For a beginner, this is "Psychological Torture." You hit a "Winning" hand, but your bankroll doesn't grow. This "Bleed" is much more aggressive than in Aces and Faces.

## The Variance Index: Sustainability vs. Volatility

Our **video poker house edge analysis** shows that:
*   **Aces and Faces** has a variance of around **30-40**. It is a "Medium-Volatility" game. It offers "Stability" and "Longevity."
*   **Aces and Eights** has a variance of around **25-30**. While "Lower," the "Unique Strategy" and "Compressed Payouts" lead to a higher "Effective Loss Rate" for beginners who make mistakes.

For the new player who wants to enjoy a long session and learn the fundamentals with a "Linear-Bonus" structure, Aces and Faces is the clear winner. It provides the "Stability" of a standard game with the "Excitement" of well-balanced payouts. Leave the "Aces and Eights" hunting to the pros who can handle the "Complexity" and the "Swings."
