# Common Mistakes to Avoid in Triple Aces Video Poker

Triple Aces is a specialized variation of Bonus Poker that focuses on the high payouts for quads involving Aces. Mastering the **Triple Aces strategy chart 2026** is essential for **EV Maximization** in this high-variance game. To learn **how to play video poker like a pro**, you must first eliminate these three common "Pro-Level" errors that are specific to Ace-heavy games.

## Analysis: 3 Most Common "Pro-Level" Errors in Triple Aces

### 1. Misvaluing the Triple Ace vs. Full House
In Triple Aces, the payout for three Aces is significantly higher than in standard Bonus Poker, reflecting the game's focus on the top of the pay table.

**The Error:** Keeping a Full House that includes 3 Aces (e.g., `A-A-A-5-5`).
**The Pro Reality:** In Triple Aces, the **Expected Return Differential** between a guaranteed Full House and drawing for the 4th Ace is heavily weighted toward the draw. **Rank-Based Priority** in 2026 dictates breaking the Full House to go for the quad Aces. Pro players recognize that the long-term equity of the 4th Ace outweighs the small, immediate payout of the Full House.

### 2. Overvaluing the Straight vs. 3 to a Royal
Because Triple Aces is a bonus poker variant, the payout for a Straight is often lower than in standard Jacks or Better.

**The Error:** Keeping a "made" Straight (e.g., `8-9-10-J-Q`) instead of drawing to 3 to a Royal Flush.
**The Pro Reality:** In Triple Aces, the **Expected Return Differential** between a small "made" hand like a straight and a three-card Royal Flush draw can be significant. If the Straight payout is only 4-to-1, the long-term EV of the Royal Flush draw is often higher. Pro players calculate this "Strategy Fine-Tuning" based on the specific pay table of the machine.

### 3. Ignoring the 3 Aces Hand-Ranking Priority
In Triple Aces, three Aces are incredibly valuable because of the potential for the massive quad Aces payout.

**The Error:** Breaking three Aces to draw for a Royal Flush (e.g., `A-A-A-K-Q` of Hearts).
**The Pro Reality:** In Triple Aces, three Aces are a "stop" hand. You never break three Aces for anything other than a guaranteed Royal Flush. The **Rank-Based Priority** in 2026 dictates that you should prioritize the Aces above almost everything else.

## Hand-Ranking Priorities in Triple Aces
The "Hand-Ranking Priority" in Triple Aces is unique: **Aces > Everything else.** Because of the massive bonus payouts for quad Aces, your primary objective is to maximize the frequency of hands that include them. **Strategy Fine-Tuning** in Triple Aces requires an "Ace-Focused" mindset. If you want to play like a pro, you must be comfortable prioritizing this specific rank over the standard "Jacks or Better" logic.
