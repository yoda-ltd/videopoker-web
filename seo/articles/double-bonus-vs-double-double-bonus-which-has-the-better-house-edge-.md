# Double Bonus vs. Double Double Bonus: House Edge and Strategy Synergy

In the landscape of "best video poker strategy comparison," **Double Bonus vs. Double Double Bonus** is a battle of bonus quads vs. kicker-driven volatility. Both games belong to the "Bonus" category of Jacks or Better variants, and they both prioritize 4 Aces as the top payout below a Royal Flush. However, they distribute their return-to-player (RTP) through different **Payout Weighting** systems for the remaining quads and "kickers," creating a massive **Strategy Bifurcation** and altering the fundamental **Payout Weighting** of each game.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Aggressive" vs. "Kicker" Hunt

The most significant **Strategy Bifurcation** occurs in the way you prioritize 4-of-a-kind.

*   **Double Bonus Strategy:** In Double Bonus, 4 Aces pay a whopping 160-for-1. This means you will prioritize holding Aces over almost any other hand, including made Straights and Flushes. You will often break a made hand to chase 4 Aces, a move that would be mathematically incorrect in almost any other video poker variant.
*   **Double Double Bonus Strategy:** In Double Double Bonus, 4 Aces with a kicker (2, 3, or 4) pay a whopping 400-for-1. This means you will prioritize holding Aces over almost any other hand, including made Straights and Flushes. You will often break a made hand to chase 4 Aces with a kicker, a move that would be mathematically incorrect in almost any other video poker variant.

## Strategy Synergy: Where the Games Align

Despite the different payout structures, there is some **Strategy Synergy** in the "Base Game" logic:

1.  **Aces are King:** In both games, 4 Aces pay a bonus. You always prioritize Aces.
2.  **Royal Flush Priority:** In both games, a 4-card Royal Flush is the top-tier hold.
3.  **High Pair Stability:** Both games pay 1-for-1 for a pair of high cards (Jacks or Better), providing a stable floor for your bankroll.

## Variance Inversion: Double Bonus vs. Extreme Kicker Volatility

The most jarring difference for players is the **Variance Inversion**.

*   **Double Bonus:** The volatility is extreme (~28.0). Because your payouts are heavily dependent on hitting 160-for-1 Aces or 80-for-1 2s-4s, you will experience very long losing streaks and very massive wins.
*   **Double Double Bonus:** The volatility is even more extreme (~42.0). Because your payouts are heavily dependent on hitting 400-for-1 Aces with a kicker or 160-for-1 2s-4s with a kicker, you will experience very long losing streaks and very massive wins.

If you are a professional player with a large bankroll and don't mind the extreme volatility of chasing 160-for-1 Aces, **Double Bonus** offers one of the best returns in the casino.

However, if you enjoy the thrill of chasing 400-for-1 Aces with a kicker and have the bankroll to handle the volatility, **Double Double Bonus** offers a unique challenge.
