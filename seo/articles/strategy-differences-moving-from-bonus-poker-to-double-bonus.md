# Strategy Differences: Moving from Bonus Poker to Double Bonus
Category: Advanced Strategy

Transitioning between variants requires more than just knowing the pay table; it requires a deep understanding of the **EV Differential** created by subtle payout shifts. When moving from Bonus Poker (BP) to Double Bonus (DB), the most jarring change for many players is the "Two Pair Trap."

## The Two Pair Trap Analysis

In the **best video poker strategy comparison**, the Two Pair payout is the "canary in the coal mine." 

*   **Bonus Poker (8/5)**: Two Pair pays **2-to-1**.
*   **Double Bonus (10/7)**: Two Pair pays **1-to-1**.

This change effectively doubles the cost of missing a higher-ranking hand while holding Two Pair. In Double Bonus, Two Pair is no longer a "winning" hand in terms of profit; it is merely a "push." This necessitates a shift in **Hand-Ranking Priorities**.

## Subtle Strategy Shifts

### 1. The Value of the Ace
In Double Bonus, the Ace becomes a much more powerful card due to the 160-for-1 payout for Quad Aces. This leads to several strategy divergences:
*   **Holding a Lone Ace**: In DB, you will hold a lone Ace more frequently than in BP, especially against other high cards, because the "kicker" potential for a 400-coin (on 5 coins) quad is so high.
*   **Breaking Two Pair**: While rare, there are specific +EV situations in extreme high-variance DB versions where breaking a Two Pair (containing Aces) to chase the Quad Aces becomes a viable consideration, a move that is strictly "strategy suicide" in Bonus Poker.

### 2. The Straight Flush Incentive
Double Bonus typically pays 50-for-1 for a Straight Flush, but more importantly, the Straight itself often pays 5-for-1. This **Variance Scaling** means you will chase 4-card straights and 3-card straight flushes with significantly more frequency in DB than in the more conservative BP.

## Video Poker House Edge Analysis

The **video poker house edge analysis** shows that while 10/7 DB has a higher theoretical return, the "Strategy Convergence" between the two is low. A player using a perfect Bonus Poker strategy on a Double Bonus machine will lose significantly more than the theoretical house edge suggests, often surrendering over 1.5% in "strategy error" EV.

### Summary of Shifts
| Feature | Bonus Poker (BP) | Double Bonus (DB) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Two Pair** | Defense (2-for-1) | Push (1-for-1) |
| **Straight** | 4-for-1 | 5-for-1 |
| **Quad Aces** | 80-for-1 | 160-for-1 |
| **Strategy Style** | Bankroll Grind | Aggressive Bonus Chasing |

Mastering the transition means accepting that your "safe" hands in BP are no longer safe in DB. You are trading the safety of the 2-for-1 Two Pair for the explosive potential of the 160-for-1 Quad Aces. Always consult a specific **best video poker strategy comparison** chart for the exact pay table you are playing to ensure you aren't falling victim to variant-specific traps.
