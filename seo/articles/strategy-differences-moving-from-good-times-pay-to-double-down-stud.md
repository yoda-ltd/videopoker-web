# Strategy Differences: Moving from Good Times Pay to Double Down Stud
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Good Times Pay** to **Double Down Stud** is a shift from a "Multi-Hand Multiplier" variant to a "Bet Multiplier" variant. This represents a significant **Strategic Divergence** in how you interact with the machine and value your initial cards.

### The Comparison Matrix: Good Times Pay vs. Double Down Stud

| Feature | Good Times Pay (Full Pay) | Double Down Stud (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.70% | 99.20% |
| **Variance Index** | 40.0+ | 18.0 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Multiplier Results | Target 4-Card Hands with Doubling Bet |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.30% | 0.80% |

### Strategic Divergence: Multipliers vs. Double Bet

In **Good Times Pay**, your results trigger multipliers that apply to your next several hands. In **Double Down Stud**, you are shown four cards and must decide whether to "Double Down" (double your bet) before the fifth card is revealed. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through bet sizing.

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is zero (0%). Both are fundamentally different games. Good Times Pay is a draw game with a multiplier twist; Double Down Stud is a fixed-outcome game with a betting twist. In Good Times Pay, you are looking for the **best video poker strategy comparison** for your four cards across multiple hands; in Double Down Stud, you are deciding whether to risk more on the fifth card.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Multiplier Management:** In Good Times Pay, you must always be looking for how multipliers improve your draw across multiple hands.
2.  **Double Down Math:** You must learn the exact EV of your four-card hand before deciding to double your bet.
3.  **Variance Normalization:** Double Down Stud has a much lower variance (18.0) than Good Times Pay (40.0+). This means your bankroll will last longer in Double Down Stud, but your wins will be more structured.

A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are solid games for the expert player, offering competitive returns with unique gameplay. For players who find the multiplier strategy of Good Times Pay too volatile, Double Down Stud Poker is the ultimate alternative for **EV Maximization**.

3.  **Connectivity Strategy:** In Full Pay Deuces, you are looking for wild card connectivity to complete straights and flushes.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that Full Pay Deuces offers a significant player advantage in its full-pay version, but the volatility is high. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are excellent choices for the disciplined player.
