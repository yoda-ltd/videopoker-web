# Royal Aces vs. Triple Bonus Plus: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Comparing **Royal Aces** and **Triple Bonus Plus** is a comparison of two "High-Quad" mathematical models. While Royal Aces targets a single rank (Aces) for astronomical payouts, Triple Bonus Plus uses a tiered system to reward several high-ranking hands (Aces, 2s, 3s, 4s) with significant bonuses. To choose the right game, you must understand how **video poker fairness and regulation** ensures the integrity of these specialized "Bonus-Centric" models.

## The Mathematical Framework: Focus vs. Distribution

The house edge in these games is determined by how the "Big Wins" are distributed across the ranks.

*   **Royal Aces:** Value is concentrated at the very top. Hitting Four Aces can pay 800-for-1, making it a "winner-take-all" rank strategy.
*   **Triple Bonus Plus:** Value is distributed more evenly across the high ranks. Payouts for Aces are high (e.g., 120-for-1), but you also receive strong bonuses for 2s, 3s, and 4s.

## House Edge Analysis: The Accuracy of the Math

The accuracy of the advertised edge in these variants is guaranteed by **Return to Player (RTP) Verification** during independent **Lab Audits**.

**The Verdict:** **Triple Bonus Plus** generally offers a slightly better house edge for most players. The tiered bonus structure provides a more stable mathematical return than the "Aces-or-Nothing" focus of Royal Aces.

## 1. Technical Standards for Fairness

Both variants must adhere to strict **Technical Standards** enforced by regulators like the **NGCB** or **UKGC**.

*   **Royal Aces:** The **Random Number Generator (RNG)** must provide an honest deal of Aces. Firms like **GLI** audit the code to ensure the 1-in-52 probability for an Ace remains constant regardless of the jackpot size.
*   **Triple Bonus Plus:** Regulators verify that all bonus-eligible ranks (Aces through 4s) are dealt with exactly the same frequency as other ranks. The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures the machine doesn't "starve" the bonus combinations.

## 2. Payout Analysis: The "Jackpot" Burden

In both games, you are paying a "tax" on common wins to fund the big hits.
- In Triple Bonus Plus, the payout for Two Pair is almost always 1-for-1 (Even Money). This tax funds the 120-for-1 and 80-for-1 bonus quads.
- In Royal Aces, the base Full House/Flush payouts are lower to fund the 800-coin Four Aces win.

For the player who wants the lowest house edge and values a more consistent hit frequency for big hands, **Triple Bonus Plus** is the superior choice. Its 99.5%+ potential return is excellent for skilled players.

However, if you enjoy the high-stakes thrill of a single-rank jackpot, **Royal Aces** provides a fair and rewarding experience.
