# Common Mistakes to Avoid in All American

All American Video Poker is a "Specialty" variant that rewards players for hitting Straights and Flushes with inflated payouts. Its "Bottom-Heavy" pay table leads to frequent errors, as players often use standard Jacks or Better logic in a game that requires a completely different approach. For the professional "Grinder," All American is about "Structural EV Maximization." 

If you want to know **how to become a professional video poker player**, you must avoid these "All American" traps.

## 1. The "High-Pair" Over-Valuation

The most common mistake in All American is over-valuing "High Pairs" at the expense of "Straight and Flush" draws.

- **The Mistake**: Holding a pair of Jacks while discarding a "Four-Card Flush" or a "Four-Card Open-Ended Straight."
- **The Pro Secret**: In All American, Flushes and Straights pay 8-for-1. This is much higher than the 6-for-1 and 4-for-1 payouts in Jacks or Better. Therefore, the "Expected Value" (EV) of a Straight or Flush draw is often higher than the EV of a high pair. Professionals follow the **EV Maximization** hierarchy, which prioritizes "Structural Draws" (Straights and Flushes) over "Pair Draws." One of the **pro video poker player secrets** is the "Structural Bias"—you will often "Break" a winning high pair to draw for a Straight or Flush in All American.

## 2. Ignoring "Penalty Card" Awareness in "Structural" Mode

Because the return in All American is so dependent on hitting Straights and Flushes, your discards are critical.

- **The Mistake**: Discarding a card of the same suit as your "Flush Draw" without checking for "Suit Penalties."
- **The Pro Secret**: **Penalty Card Awareness** is essential for "Flush and Straight Optimization." If you discard a card that would have been a "Natural Out" for your draw, you are "Penalizing" the deck's potential to give you a winning hand. Pros use strategy software to understand how "Suit Density" and "Rank Density" affect their long-term return. Even a 0.05% shift in probability is enough to change a "Hold" to a "Fold" for a professional.

## 3. The "Full House" and "Two Pair" Under-Valuation

In All American, the payouts for Full Houses and Two Pairs are often reduced to fund the large Straight and Flush payouts.

- **The Mistake**: Expecting the "Small Wins" (Two Pair) to sustain your bankroll.
- **The Pro Secret**: A pro is an "EV Purist." They know that the "Real Money" in All American is in the Straights, Flushes, and Straight Flushes. They will follow the math that dictates "Ignoring" the two-pair draw to pursue a "Structural Draw" in almost every configuration. One of the **pro video poker player secrets** is "Structural Tunnel Vision"—focusing on the 8-for-1 payouts at the expense of the smaller, traditional wins.

## 4. Failure in "Variance Buffering"

All American is a "High-Variance" game. Its **Standard Deviation** is roughly 30.0.

- **The Mistake**: Thinking you can "Grind" All American with a "JoB-sized" bankroll.
- **The Pro Secret**: Professionals use **Variance Buffering**. You need a bankroll of at least **6,000 to 7,000 units** to safely play. While the Straights and Flushes pay well, they don't hit often enough to prevent significant "Downswings." You need a buffer to survive the "No-Flush" sessions. Pros also maximize their "Slot Club" points and "Bounce Back" cash to push their total return closer to 100%.

## 5. Violating "Unwritten Pro Rules"

All American is a "Fast-Paced" game, often favored by "Expert Grinders."

- **The Mistake**: Breaking the "Rhythm" of the floor or being "Too Slow" in your decision-making.
- **The Pro Secret**: Follow the **unwritten pro rules**. This includes "Machine Protection" and "Social Etiquette." If you are playing 1,000 hands per hour, you want to be in a "Flow State." **Tipping attendants** on your "Straight Flush" hand-pays is essential for ensuring that your machine is never "Down" for long. A well-tipped attendant will "Protect" your machine if you need to take a quick break, ensuring your **Session Longevity**.

## Summary: The All American Professional

To succeed in All American, you must be a "Structural Specialist." This requires:
1. **Draw-Centric Discipline**: Prioritizing Straights and Flushes above all else.
2. **Clinical Execution**: 100% accuracy on every hold/discard decision.
3. **Volume Focus**: Playing at maximum speed to "Realize" the 8-for-1 payouts.

By avoiding these mistakes, you protect your **0.05% margin** and ensure your success as a professional All American grinder.
