# Triple Double Bonus vs. All American: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Comparing **Triple Double Bonus (TDB)** and **All American** is a comparison of Quad specialization vs. Sequence specialization. While TDB rewards you massively for hitting high-ranking Four-of-a-Kind hands with kickers, All American focuses on boosted payouts for Straights and Flushes. To choose the right game, you must understand how **video poker fairness and regulation** ensures the integrity of these contrasting mathematical models.

## The Mathematical Framework: Quads vs. Sequences

The house edge in these games is determined by which hands drive the "Return."

*   **Triple Double Bonus:** Value is heavily concentrated at the very top of the pay table. Four Aces with a kicker (2, 3, or 4) can pay as much as a Royal Flush. The edge comes from the player's ability to master the aggressive kicker strategy.
*   **All American:** Value is distributed more evenly through mid-tier hands. Straights and Flushes both pay 8-for-1 (instead of the usual 4 or 6). The edge comes from the player's ability to prioritize "Sequence draws" over low pairs.

The accuracy of the advertised edge in these variants is guaranteed by **Return to Player (RTP) Verification** during independent **Lab Audits**.

**The Verdict:** **Full Pay All American** is the mathematical winner. It is one of the few games where a skilled player holds the edge over the house. **Triple Double Bonus** is a "Fair-House" variant but requires much more specific hits (kicker-quads) to realize its full potential.

## 1. Technical Standards for Fairness

Both variants must adhere to strict **Technical Standards** enforced by regulators like the **NGCB** or **UKGC**.

*   **Triple Double Bonus:** The **Random Number Generator (RNG)** must provide an honest deal for kickers. Firms like **GLI** audit the code to ensure the machine isn't "starving" the player of the 2, 3, or 4 needed for the top payouts.
*   **All American:** Regulators verify that the shuffle is unbiased, ensuring that Straights and Flushes occur with the correct statistical frequency to support the high RTP. The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures that hitting the "Sequence" bonus is a fair statistical event.

## 2. Payout Analysis: The "Jackpot" Tax

In both games, you are paying a "tax" for access to the big hits.
- In both variants, Two Pair only pays 1-for-1 (Even Money). 
- In TDB, this tax funds the massive 4,000-coin kicker bonuses.
- In All American, this tax funds the boosted 8-for-1 Straight and Flush payouts.

For the player who wants the absolute best math and is willing to master an aggressive sequence-based strategy, **Full Pay All American** is the superior choice. Its 100.7%+ return is a mathematical fortress for skilled gamblers.

However, if you thrive on the high-stakes thrill of hitting quads that pay like a Royal Flush, **Triple Double Bonus** provides a fair and rewarding experience.
