# Strategy Differences: Moving from Super Star Poker to Hyper Bonus Poker

Transitioning from Super Star Poker to Hyper Bonus Poker is a unique step in the video poker world. Both games are niche variants that offer a "high-speed" or "bonus" experience. This guide provides the best video poker strategy comparison to help you understand how to play video poker like a pro during this transition.

## The Super Star Poker Baseline

In Super Star Poker, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is built on the fact that any pair of Jacks or better is a winning hand. This creates a very stable, low-volatility experience. When moving to Hyper Bonus Poker, that "safety net" is removed. You now need at least a pair of Queens or Kings to get your money back.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from Super Star Poker to Hyper Bonus Poker, you'll find that **80% of your strategy remains the same**. The **20% that changes** is entirely focused on the "Pair threshold." This 20% shift causes the most **Bankroll Drain** if not corrected.

### The 20% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Pair of Jacks Devaluation:** In Super Star Poker, a pair of Jacks is a winning hand. In Hyper Bonus Poker, it's a "trash" hand that you should rarely hold unless you're drawing for something better (like a three-card Royal Flush).
2.  **Hyper Bonus Focus:** Hyper Bonus Poker is named "Hyper Bonus" because it rewards you specifically for getting multiple winning hands in a row at a high speed. This makes four-of-a-kind and specific straights much more valuable than in Super Star Poker.
3.  **Two Pair Strategy:** While Two Pair pays 2-for-1 in both games, the higher payout for specific "Hyper Bonus" hands in Hyper Bonus Poker means you might break a Full House to draw for a fifth card more often than you would in Super Star Poker.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

Hyper Bonus Poker introduces **Volatility Scaling**. Without the Jacks-or-better push, your bankroll will experience more frequent dips. **EV Erosion** occurs when a Super-Star-Poker player instinctively holds a pair of Jacks in Hyper Bonus Poker. This is a "dead hand" and holding it is a direct loss of expected value.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Queens or Kings or Better are your new minimum "win."
*   **Modify:** Discard any pair of Jacks unless they are part of a better draw.
*   **Target:** Aggressively pursue the "Hyper Bonus" bonuses; they are the heart of the Hyper Bonus Poker pay table.

By recognizing that the "safety net" has moved, you can successfully transition to Hyper Bonus Poker without suffering from significant EV Erosion.
