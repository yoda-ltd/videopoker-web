# Top 5 Tips for Mastering Aces and Faces Video Poker

Aces and Faces is an action-oriented variant that rewards players for hitting Four-of-a-Kind with—you guessed it—Aces and the "Face" cards (Jacks, Queens, and Kings). To master this game, you must understand how to prioritize these high-ranking cards without neglecting the fundamental poker math that keeps your bankroll alive. 

Here are the top five professional tips for mastering Aces and Faces, all backed by the high standards of **video poker fairness and regulation**.

## 1. Prioritize High Pair Retention

The defining feature of Aces and Faces is the tiered payout for Quads: 80-for-1 for Aces and 40-for-1 for Jacks, Queens, or Kings. 
- **The Tip:** If you have a pair of Aces or a pair of any Face card, hold them and draw for the others. These cards are significantly more valuable than lower pairs. 
- **Integrity Note:** The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures that Aces and Face cards are dealt with a fair, statistically random distribution. The machine never "hides" these cards to prevent a bonus payout.

## 2. Unlearn Standard "Flush Chase" Habits

In many video poker variants, chasing a Flush is a top priority. In Aces and Faces, the high payout for Face-based Quads changes the math. 
- **The Tip:** If you have a choice between holding a pair of Jacks and chasing a marginal Flush draw, the "Face" pair often has a higher mathematical Expected Value (EV). 
- **Fairness Note:** Independent **Lab Audits** by firms like **GLI** verify that the "Face-first" strategy aligns with the theoretical return of the game's **Source Code**.

## 3. Verify the Full House and Flush Payouts

The profitability of Aces and Faces depends on the pay table for Full Houses and Flushes. 
- **The Tip:** Look for "8/5" or "7/6" versions. Avoid "5/4" or "6/5" machines, as they significantly lower your **Return to Player (RTP)** and cannot be offset by the bonus Quad payouts.
- **Fairness Note:** **Jurisdictional Mandates** require casinos to display these payout values clearly. Always verify the pay table before you begin your session.

## 4. Master the "Max Bet" Strategy

To achieve the best possible odds, you must play with **5 coins**. 
- **The Tip:** The bonus for a Royal Flush at 5 coins (usually 4,000 coins) provides a massive boost to your theoretical return. If 5 coins are too expensive, move to a machine with a lower denomination rather than betting fewer coins.
- **Integrity Note:** **RTP Verification** confirms that the machine provides a fair chance at hitting the Royal Flush bonus, regardless of how recently the jackpot was won.

## 5. Trust the Random Number Generator

A common myth in video poker is that machines become "due" for a Face-card bonus if one hasn't appeared in a while. 
- **The Tip:** Every hand in Aces and Faces is an independent event. The RNG does not remember previous hands. Focus on perfect execution of strategy rather than trying to "time" the machine.
- **Security Note:** Regulators like the **NGCB** or **UKGC** perform regular audits to ensure the RNG remains statistically neutral, providing a fair and unbiased experience for every player on every deal.

## Conclusion
Mastering Aces and Faces is about targeting the top of the deck and being disciplined with your high-card holds. By prioritizing Aces and Faces and playing on platforms with a **trusted video poker RNG certification**, you can take advantage of the increased excitement and high returns this variant offers. Remember: in this game, the faces are your best path to victory!
