# Joker Poker: 9/6 vs 8/5 vs 7/5 Pay Table Analysis

For any video poker player, the pay table is the single most important factor in determining the house edge. In Joker Poker (often called Joker Wild), the most common variants are the **9/6**, **8/5**, and **7/5** payout schedules. These numbers refer to the payout for a Full House and a Flush, respectively.

## Comparison of Payouts
The table below shows the typical differences for a 1-coin bet across common schedules (Kings or Better version):

| Hand | 9/6 (Full Pay) | 8/5 (Mid Pay) | 7/5 (Low Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Natural Royal Flush** | 800 | 800 | 800 |
| **Five of a Kind** | 200 | 200 | 200 |
| **Wild Royal Flush** | 100 | 100 | 100 |
| **Straight Flush** | 50 | 50 | 50 |
| **Four of a Kind** | 20 | 20 | 15 |
| **Full House** | **9** | **8** | **7** |
| **Flush** | **6** | **5** | **5** |
| **Straight** | 3 | 3 | 3 |
| **Three of a Kind** | 2 | 2 | 2 |
| **Two Pair** | 1 | 1 | 1 |
| **Kings or Better** | 1 | 1 | 1 |

## The Impact on Return to Player (RTP)
*   **9/6 Joker Poker:** Often considered the "Full Pay" version, this variant has an RTP of approximately **99.29%** when played with optimal strategy.
*   **8/5 Joker Poker:** The reduction in the Full House and Flush payouts drops the RTP to roughly **98.44%**.
*   **7/5 Joker Poker:** This version is common in many land-based casinos and has a significantly lower RTP, often around **97.19%**.

The difference between a 9/6 and a 7/5 game might seem small, but over a session of 1,000 hands at a $1 denomination, a 2% difference in RTP translates to a $20 difference in expected loss.

## High-Limit Joker Poker Considerations
In **high-limit video poker** lounges, you are more likely to find 9/6 or even 20/7/5 schedules. High rollers should always scan the pay table before sitting down, as the volatility of Joker Poker can be high due to the 53rd card (the Joker) making bigger hands like Five of a Kind possible.

## Strategy Adjustments
Your strategy must change slightly depending on the pay table. In a 9/6 game, you can afford to hold onto certain draws (like three to a Flush) more aggressively than in a 7/5 game where the Flush payout is reduced.

To master these nuances, we recommend using **video poker training software**. These tools can run simulations on specific Joker Poker pay tables, highlighting exactly where your strategy needs to shift to maintain the highest possible RTP.

## Conclusion: Which Should You Play?
The answer is simple: always seek out the 9/6 Joker Poker machine. If only 8/5 or 7/5 are available, your house edge increases significantly. Always remember that the payout for the "Natural Royal Flush" is only 800-for-1 when you bet 5 coins; a 1-coin bet usually only pays 250-for-1, making the max bet essential for professional-level play.
