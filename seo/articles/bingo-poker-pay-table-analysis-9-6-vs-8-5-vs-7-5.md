# Bingo Poker Pay Table Analysis: 9/6 vs 8/5 vs 7/5

To understand the true value of Bingo Poker, a professional player must look beyond the base game. Unlike standard video poker, Bingo Poker incorporates a grid-based side game where each card drawn helps complete a Bingo pattern. This requires a **Hybrid RTP Analysis** that integrates the poker hand rankings with the Bingo payout system.

## Comparison Table: 9/6 vs 8/5 vs 7/5 Bingo Poker

The following table compares the base game payouts for a Full House and a Flush across three common pay tables found in casinos today.

| Pay Table | Full House | Flush | Base RTP |
|-----------|------------|-------|----------|
| 9/6 | 9 coins | 6 coins | ~99.5% |
| 8/5 | 8 coins | 5 coins | ~97.3% |
| 7/5 | 7 coins | 5 coins | ~96.1% |

### 9/6 Bingo Poker: The Low-Variance Professional Choice
The 9/6 variant is the most player-friendly. In this version, the **Drawing Probability** of sustaining your bankroll through a long session is high. When combined with the Bingo side game, a 9/6 Bingo Poker machine can sometimes reach an RTP of over 100.2% during promotional periods or when the Bingo jackpot is high.

### 8/5 Bingo Poker: The Most Common Casino Floor Variant
You will most likely encounter the 8/5 version in major casino hubs. While the lower payouts for Full Houses and Flushes increase the house edge, the Bingo side game is often more aggressive, with more frequent (though smaller) payouts for "Lines" and "Corners."

### 7/5 Bingo Poker: The High-Variance "Jackpot" Version
A 7/5 pay table is common on machines with massive progressive Bingo jackpots. In these games, the **Cross-Game Variance** is extreme. You are sacrificing base-game stability for the slim chance of hitting a Royal Flush that *also* completes a "Blackout" on your Bingo card.

## Integrating Bingo Patterns into Your Strategy

A **bingo poker strategy guide** will emphasize that your decision-making should shift based on the state of your Bingo grid.

1. **Lines and Corners:** If you are one card away from a "Diagonal Line" or "Four Corners" Bingo payout, it may be mathematically correct to break a low pair in the poker game to chase that specific Bingo card.
2. **The "Wait" Factor:** In Bingo Poker, some patterns persist across multiple hands. This creates a "persistent state" where the current machine's EV is higher than the base pay table suggests.

## Conclusion

When choosing between 9/6, 8/5, and 7/5 Bingo Poker, always start with the 9/6 variant if available. However, because of the Bingo component, even a 7/5 machine can be profitable if the Bingo grid is "nearly full." Always consult a **bingo poker strategy guide** to understand these complex situational shifts and maximize your total RTP.
