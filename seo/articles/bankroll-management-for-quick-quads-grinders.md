Category: Variant Guide

Quick Quads is an "Action Gaming" masterpiece that transforms the traditional hunt for four-of-a-kind into a high-frequency summation game. However, for the "grinder" looking to sustain a professional career, the bankroll requirements are significantly different from standard Jacks or Better. Understanding the **Payline Variance** and **Side-Bet EV** is critical to survival.

## The 6-Coin Reality

In Quick Quads, the mandatory 6th coin (to activate the "summation" bonus) increases your "per-hand" cost by 20%. While this improves your **Bonus Trigger Frequency** (hitting quads roughly 3x more often), it also accelerates your "burn rate" during cold streaks.

### 1. The "Quad-Heavy" Return
Quick Quads derives a larger percentage of its total Expected Return (ER) from the 4-of-a-kind category.
- **Standard Poker**: Quads account for ~6% of return.
- **Quick Quads**: Quads account for ~15% to 18% of return.
If you go on a "Quad Drought," your bankroll will evaporate much faster than in a balanced game like 9/6 JoB.

## Side-Bet EV vs. Total Exposure

The 6th coin is not "lost money." It is an investment in a higher-return game. When searching for the **best video poker machines in 2026**, Quick Quads often ranks high because the bonus mechanic is "fixed" math rather than a random multiplier.

## Rare Video Poker Variants Strategy: The "Sum" Bankroll
When playing Quick Quads, your bankroll is essentially a "Summation Reserve." You are betting on the fact that your 3-of-a-kind holds will convert into "Quick Quads" at a predictable rate. 

1. **Avoid "Under-Betting"**: Never play 5 coins on a Quick Quads machine. The ER drops by over 1.5%, making it one of the worst bets in the house.
2. **Denomination Discipline**: If your bankroll is $5,000, stay at the nickel or dime level ($0.30 - $0.60 per hand). Moving to quarters with only $5,000 is a "Gambler's Ruin" scenario for Quick Quads.

To succeed as a Quick Quads grinder in 2026, you must respect the 6-coin cost. By maintaining a 7,000-unit buffer and strictly following the "summation" strategy, you can capitalize on the high quad frequency without falling victim to the variance.
