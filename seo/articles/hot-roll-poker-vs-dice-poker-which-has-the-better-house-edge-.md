# Hot Roll Poker vs. Dice Poker: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Wait, aren't they the same thing? Not quite. While **Hot Roll Poker** is a specific, branded variant from IGT, "Dice Poker" is a broader category that includes various games where dice determine multipliers or bonus wins. If you are using a **hot roll video poker trainer**, it is important to know if the math you are learning applies to the specific machine in front of you.

## Hot Roll Poker: The IGT Standard

Hot Roll Poker is famous for its "Pre-Hand" multiplier. You roll the dice, get a multiplier (2x to 12x), and then play your hand.
*   **Multiplier Roll Probability:** Fixed at roughly 1 in 6 hands.
*   **Bonus Event EV:** High, but concentrated in the 12x multiplier.
*   **Analysis of Hot Roll's Dice Multiplier (Up to 12x):** The 7 is the most common roll, making the "average" multiplier during a feature hand roughly 7x.

## Generic Dice Poker: The Variations

Other "Dice Poker" machines might trigger a roll *after* a win (like a double-up feature) or use dice to determine a fixed coin bonus instead of a multiplier.
*   **The Difference:** A "Fixed Bonus" (e.g., Roll a 12 to get 500 coins) is much less valuable than a "Multiplier" (Roll a 12 to get 48,000 coins on a Royal Flush).
*   **House Edge:** Fixed-bonus dice games often have a higher house edge because the "upside" is capped.

## House Edge Analysis

### 1. Multiplier vs. Fixed Payout
Hot Roll Poker's multiplier applies to the *entire* win, including a Royal Flush. This significantly lowers the house edge for skilled players who can hit those big hands. Generic Dice Poker games with fixed payouts often have much lower RTPs because the bonus doesn't scale with the rarity of the poker hand.

### 2. Volatility
*   **Hot Roll Poker:** Very High. Your RTP is heavily dependent on hitting a good hand during a high-multiplier roll.
*   **Generic Dice Poker:** Medium. If the bonus is a fixed coin amount, your bankroll will be steadier, but you lose the "Life-Changing Win" potential.
