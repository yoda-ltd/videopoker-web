# Common Mistakes to Avoid in Super Double Bonus Video Poker

Mastering **Super Double Bonus strategy chart 2026** requires more than just knowing which cards to hold; it requires understanding the **Expected Return Differential** that separates casual players from the elite. Super Double Bonus (SDB) is a volatile variant that rewards aggressive play on specific four-of-a-kind combinations, particularly Aces.

To learn **how to play video poker like a pro**, you must eliminate these three high-level "Pro-Level" errors that often plague even experienced gamblers.

## 1. Miscalculating EV Maximization on Aces vs. Full Houses
In standard Jacks or Better, holding a Full House is a no-brainer. However, in Super Double Bonus, the payout for four Aces is significantly elevated. A common "Pro-Level" error occurs when a player is dealt a hand like `A-A-A-K-K`. 

**The Error:** Keeping the Full House for a guaranteed payout.
**The Pro Reality:** Under many SDB pay tables, the **EV Maximization** strategy dictates breaking the Full House to draw for the fourth Ace. If the payout for 4 Aces is 800 credits (on a 5-coin bet) and the Full House is only 45, the mathematical "Expected Return Differential" favors the draw. Pro players calculate the risk of "bricking" the hand against the massive windfall of the quad Aces.

## 2. Ignoring Rank-Based Priority in Straight Flush Draws
Many players treat all Open-Ended Straight Flush draws (OESFD) with equal weight. In SDB, **Rank-Based Priority** is crucial. 

**The Error:** Holding a low-rank OESFD (e.g., `4-5-6-7` of Spades) over a high-rank pair or a high-card Ace.
**The Pro Reality:** Because SDB heavily weights the top of the pay table, a single Ace often has more long-term value than a low-rank draw that doesn't include high-card "kickers" for potential high-pair payouts. Strategy fine-tuning requires recognizing that the "cost" of missing a straight flush is higher when you sacrifice a high-card hold.

## 3. Failure in Strategy Fine-Tuning for "Kicker" Games
SDB is often confused with Double Double Bonus (DDB). In DDB, kickers (2, 3, or 4) matter immensely for the 4-of-a-kind payout. In Super Double Bonus, the payouts are usually fixed for the quads regardless of the kicker.

**The Error:** Holding a kicker with a pair of Aces (e.g., `A-A-2`) hoping for the DDB-style bonus.
**The Pro Reality:** Holding the kicker reduces your chances of drawing the 3rd and 4th Aces. Since SDB doesn't reward the kicker, you are effectively lowering your EV for no reason. Pros discard the kicker to maximize the number of available cards that complete the quad.
