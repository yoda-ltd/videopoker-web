# Loose Deuces vs. White Hot Aces: Which Has the Better House Edge?

For video poker players who crave big payouts for Four-of-a-Kind, the choice often comes down to **Loose Deuces** or **White Hot Aces**. One uses wild cards to reach a 500:1 jackpot, while the other uses "Triple" payouts for Aces to deliver a 240:1 punch. This article compares the house edge, math, and volatility of these two aggressive games.

## Theoretical Return (EV) and House Edge

### 1. Full Pay Loose Deuces
*   **Return (EV):** 100.97%
*   **House Edge:** -0.97% (Player Advantage)
*   **The Catch:** Extremely rare to find the 100.97% version; usually found at 98.2%.

### 2. Full Pay White Hot Aces
*   **Return (EV):** 99.57%
*   **House Edge:** 0.43% (Casino Edge)
*   **The Catch:** Requires a 9/5 pay table (9 for Full House, 5 for Flush).

**The Winner on EV:** Full Pay Loose Deuces (100.97%) is the superior mathematical game, offering the player a significant edge.

## Pay Table Comparison: Wild Deuces vs. White Hot Aces

| Hand | Loose Deuces (100.97%) | White Hot Aces (99.57%) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Natural Royal** | 800:1 | 800:1 |
| **Four Deuces** | **500:1** | 80:1 |
| **Four Aces** | (Included in 4-of-a-Kind) | **240:1** |
| **Four 2s, 3s, or 4s** | 5:1 | 120:1 |
| **Full House** | 3:1 | 9:1 |

In White Hot Aces, the **Full House (9:1)** and **Flush (5:1)** payouts are much more generous than in Loose Deuces. However, Loose Deuces makes up for this with the massive 500:1 jackpot for the deuces.

## Volatility and Variance
*   **Loose Deuces Variance:** ~74
*   **White Hot Aces Variance:** ~47

**Loose Deuces is much more volatile than White Hot Aces.** While a 47 variance is high for a "Natural" game, it doesn't compare to the 74 variance of Loose Deuces. In White Hot Aces, the "Triple" payouts for Aces (240:1) and 2s-4s (120:1) happen more frequently than the 500:1 hit in Loose Deuces. This means White Hot Aces players will experience fewer "total bankroll meltdowns" than Loose Deuces players.

## Strategy Complexity: Wild vs. Natural
*   **Loose Deuces:** You must learn a completely new way of playing poker. Holding a single Deuce is often better than holding a pat Straight.
*   **White Hot Aces:** This is a variant of Bonus Poker. The strategy is mostly logical (keep pairs, chase flushes), with the only major shift being an extreme focus on holding any lone Ace.

**The Trainer's Edge:** Because Loose Deuces strategy is so counter-intuitive, players make more mistakes on it. If you use a **Video Poker Trainer**, you might find that you can play White Hot Aces at **99.5% accuracy** much faster than you can master Loose Deuces at the same level.

## House Edge Analysis: Finding the Right Machine
Most casinos offer "Short Pay" versions:
*   **Loose Deuces (98.2%):** Common in Vegas locals casinos.
*   **White Hot Aces (98.4%):** Found when the pay table is 8/5 or 7/5 instead of 9/5.

At these levels, the two games are very similar in terms of total return. Your choice should depend on your **risk tolerance**.

## Which Game Should You Play?

**Choose Loose Deuces if:**
*   You are playing at a casino that offers the 100.97% table (e.g., some Sam's Town or South Point machines in the past).
*   You want the "Home Run" potential of a 500:1 hit.
*   You have the discipline to follow a difficult wild-card strategy.

**Choose White Hot Aces if:**
*   You prefer "Natural" poker and don't like wild cards.
*   You want a high-return game (99.57%) with lower volatility than Loose Deuces.
*   You love the "Triple Bonus" aspect of hitting big payouts for Aces and small quads.

## Conclusion
Mathematically, **Loose Deuces (100.97%)** is the better game. However, **White Hot Aces (99.57%)** is much more accessible for the average player. It offers a professional-grade return with lower variance and a more intuitive strategy. If you want the best odds, study Loose Deuces; if you want the best experience with a solid return, stick with the White Hot Aces.
