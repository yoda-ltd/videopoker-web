# Double Jackpot Poker vs. Pick'em Poker: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Double Jackpot Poker and Pick'em Poker are two very different approaches to the video poker experience. Double Jackpot is a standard "hold and draw" game with high-paying bonuses for face cards, while Pick'em Poker is a unique variant with limited choices and very different mathematical properties.

Which one should you play for the best return? Let's compare the house edge.

## Pick'em Poker: The Strategy Master's Choice

Pick'em Poker (also known as Pick-a-Pair) is unique because you don't choose which cards to discard.
1.  You are dealt two cards on the left.
2.  You are presented with two "piles" of cards on the right. Only the top card of each pile is visible.
3.  You choose one of the two piles.
4.  The chosen pile reveals two more cards, giving you a 5-card hand.

Because your choices are limited to "Option A or Option B," the strategy is much simpler to play perfectly.

### The House Edge (Full Pay)
*   **Theoretical RTP:** **99.95%**
*   **House Edge:** 0.05%
*   **Volatility:** Low (Compared to Bonus games)

Pick'em Poker is legendary for its 99.95% return, making it one of the closest games to "break-even" in the entire casino.

## Double Jackpot Poker: The Bonus Hunter's Choice

Double Jackpot Poker is a standard draw game that pays premiums for Four of a Kind involving face cards (Jack, Queen, King) and Aces.

*   **Four Aces:** 160-for-1.
*   **Four J, Q, K:** 80-for-1.
*   **Two Pair:** 1-for-1.
*   **Theoretical RTP (9/6):** **99.63%**
*   **House Edge:** 0.37%

Double Jackpot is a more "traditional" and exciting game, but it carries higher volatility because of the 1-for-1 payout on Two Pair.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

### 1. Mathematical Return (House Edge)
**Pick'em Poker is the superior game.** 
A 99.95% RTP is nearly perfect. While 99.63% (Double Jackpot) is excellent, you will still lose money about seven times faster on the Double Jackpot machine over the long run.

### 2. Strategy and Ease of Use
*   **Pick'em Poker** is much easier to play perfectly. Since you only have two choices, you can use a simple strategy card to make the right decision every time. 
*   **Double Jackpot Poker** requires standard draw poker strategy, which is more complex. You have to decide which cards to hold out of all five, leading to more potential for human error.

### 3. Volatility and "The Big Hit"
*   **Double Jackpot Poker** is for those who want the "Double Jackpot" payout. Hitting Four Kings for 400 coins is a huge thrill.
*   **Pick'em Poker** is a much more "flat" game. The top payouts are rarer, and the game is designed to give you frequent, smaller wins to keep you playing. It is much less likely to "bankrupt" you in 20 minutes than Double Jackpot.

## Verdict: Which Should You Play?

*   **Play Pick'em Poker if** you want the best possible math and the longest play time. If you find a 99.95% machine, you are playing one of the fairest games ever designed.
*   **Play Double Jackpot Poker if** you find the "Pick'em" mechanic boring and want the traditional video poker experience with the chance for high-paying face card bonuses.

**Final Note:** Beware of the pay tables! Pick'em Poker is often "short-pay" in modern casinos, with returns dropping to 97%. If you see a Pick'em machine where the Full House/Flush payout is low, and a 9/6 Double Jackpot machine next to it, the **Double Jackpot machine becomes the better mathematical choice!** Always check the payouts before you commit.
