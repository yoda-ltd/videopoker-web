# Sevens Wild vs. White Hot Aces: Which Has the Better House Edge?

In the world of video poker, players often compare variants that use wild cards. Two of the most popular are **Sevens Wild** and **White Hot Aces**. While both offer a wild card, the strategy and house edge (return to player or RTP) of each game differ significantly.

## Sevens Wild: The Power of Four Wilds
In Sevens Wild, all four Sevens (7s) are wild. This creates a much more volatile game than White Hot Aces. The house edge in Sevens Wild can be even lower than in White Hot Aces, provided you find a "Full Pay" machine.

*   **RTP of Sevens Wild:** On a Full Pay Sevens Wild machine, the RTP can reach as high as **100.56%**, meaning the player has a mathematical advantage over the casino.
*   **Strategy for Sevens Wild:** The strategy is much more complex than White Hot Aces. You are frequently discarding high cards to draw for Sevens, which is counterintuitive to most video poker players.

## White Hot Aces: The Power of Four Aces
In White Hot Aces, all four Aces (As) are high-paying. This makes it a 52-card deck. The strategy is much more straightforward than Sevens Wild. The house edge in White Hot Aces can be even lower than in Sevens Wild, provided you find a "Full Pay" machine.

*   **RTP of White Hot Aces:** On a Full Pay White Hot Aces machine, the RTP is approximately **99.57%**.
*   **Strategy for White Hot Aces:** The strategy is relatively straightforward. You are frequently holding Aces to ensure a win, which is intuitive to most video poker players.

## House Edge and Return to Player (RTP) Comparison
When comparing Sevens Wild and White Hot Aces, the key is the **house edge**.
*   **Sevens Wild:** On a Full Pay machine, the house edge is negative, meaning the player has a **+0.56%** edge. However, on "Short Pay" Sevens Wild machines (the most common version), the house edge can climb to **2% or higher**.
*   **White Hot Aces:** On a Full Pay machine, the house edge is around **0.43%**. However, on "Short Pay" White Hot Aces machines (the most common version), the house edge can climb to **1% or higher**.

## Volatility and Bankroll Considerations
Sevens Wild is a much more volatile game. You will have long losing streaks punctuated by big wins (like hitting four Sevens). White Hot Aces is a more stable game with a higher hit frequency for smaller hands (Jacks or Better).

*   **Bankroll Management:** For a 100-hand session at a $1 denomination, White Hot Aces is safer. For professional players with a large bankroll and a focus on long-term profits, Sevens Wild is the better choice, provided they can find a Full Pay machine.

## Conclusion: Which Should You Play?
If you are looking for a game with a manageable strategy and a low house edge, **White Hot Aces** is an excellent choice, especially in **high-limit video poker** areas. However, if you are a professional player looking for a mathematical edge and can handle high volatility, **Sevens Wild** is the superior game.
