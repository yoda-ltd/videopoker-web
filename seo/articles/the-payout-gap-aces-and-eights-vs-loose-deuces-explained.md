# The Payout Gap: Aces and Eights vs. Loose Deuces Explained

The "Payout Gap" between Aces and Eights and Loose Deuces represents the two extremes of video poker: "Natural Bonus" versus "Wild Card Chaos." This **best video poker strategy comparison** breaks down how **Payout Compression** and **Variance Scaling** create two radically different gambling experiences from the same basic deck of cards.

## The Mathematical Foundation: Bonus vs. Wild

**Aces and Eights** is a "Bonus" game. It is a Jacks or Better variant that pays fixed bonuses for 4 Aces, 4 Eights, and 4 Sevens. It is a "Pure" game with a 52-card deck and no wild cards. The **video poker house edge analysis** is based on the frequency of hitting these specific "Natural" Quads. Many versions are "Player-Friendly" with 2-to-1 payouts for Two-Pair.

**Loose Deuces** is a "Wild" game. The four Deuces (2s) are wild. To fund the massive "Four Deuces" payout (often 500-to-1 or 2,500 coins), the game uses extreme **Payout Compression**. You typically don't get paid at all for anything less than Three-of-a-Kind. This is a 52-card deck where the Deuces "Break" the standard probability model.

## Variance Scaling and the Bankroll Buffer

1.  **Aces and Eights:** The variance is moderate (around 25-30). It is a "Smoother" bonus game. Because the Two-Pair payout is often higher (or the Quad bonuses are more frequent), your bankroll "Churns" effectively. It is a great game for "Grinding" and long sessions.
2.  **Loose Deuces:** The variance is high (often 50-70). It is a "Volatile" game. The **Variance Scaling** is steep. Because you lose your bet on every hand that isn't a Three-of-a-Kind or better, your bankroll will "Liquidate" rapidly if you don't hit the Deuces. This requires a massive bankroll buffer.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Hold" Factor

The **Strategy Bifurcation** is a total shift in "Card Value" logic:
*   In **Aces and Eights**, you play "Standard Bonus" strategy. You hold high pairs and target the Aces, Eights, and Sevens. It is very close to standard Jacks or Better.
*   In **Loose Deuces**, you play "Wild Hunting." The "Deuce" is king. You will hold a single Deuce over *everything* else (except a natural Royal draw). You will break apart "Winning" natural hands to chase the Deuces.

If you enjoy the "Stability" of a natural bonus game and want to "Grind" for a long time, Aces and Eights is the mathematically superior choice. If you enjoy the "Rush" of wild card jackpots and have the "Nerves" (and the bankroll) for high volatility, Loose Deuces offers the ultimate "Wild" experience.
