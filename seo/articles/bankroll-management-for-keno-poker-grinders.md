# Bankroll Management for Keno Poker Grinders

Keno Poker is a unique variant that blends the high-variance nature of Keno with the decision-making of Video Poker. For the professional grinder, **Bankroll Management** is the single most important factor for long-term survival.

### Understanding Variance in Keno Poker
Keno Poker typically has a higher **Variance** than standard Jacks or Better. This means your bankroll will experience more significant swings. To achieve **Session Sustainability**, you must have a larger reserve. A standard recommendation for Keno Poker is at least 4x the bankroll you would carry for a low-variance game like Jacks or Better.

### The Importance of Theoretical Return
Before you start grinding, you must identify the **Theoretical Return** of the Keno Poker variant you are playing. Many Keno variants have significantly lower RTPs (often in the 92-95% range) compared to high-end Video Poker (99%+). If the RTP is too low, no amount of bankroll management can make you a winner.

### Hit Frequency Optimization
In Keno Poker, you are often looking for specific "matches." **Hit Frequency Optimization** involves choosing the number of spots that gives you the best balance between frequent small wins and the occasional large payout. 

### Professional Tips:
1. **Max Bet and Longevity**: Many players ask, "Is max bet always better for bankroll longevity?" In Keno Poker, the bonus for a max bet might not be as pronounced as the Royal Flush bonus in Video Poker, but it still often provides a critical boost to the RTP.
2. **Video Poker Tips for Beginners**: If you are new to Keno Poker, start at the lowest denomination possible to get a feel for the "dry spells" that characterize this game.
3. **Daily Goals**: To learn **how to win video poker daily** (or Keno Poker), you must set strict loss limits. If you lose 10% of your total bankroll in one day, walk away.

Success in Keno Poker grinding comes down to discipline and a deep understanding of **Pseudo-Randomness vs. True Randomness**. The machine is random; your bankroll strategy must be methodical.
