# Loose Deuces vs. Tens or Better: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Video poker players often face a choice between high-volatility "Wild" games like **Loose Deuces** and low-volatility "Entry-level" games like **Tens or Better**. One is built for jackpot hunters, while the other is built for sustainability. This article compares the house edge, math, and strategy of these two very different games.

## Theoretical Return (EV) and House Edge

### 1. Full Pay Loose Deuces
*   **Return (EV):** 100.97%
*   **House Edge:** -0.97% (Player Advantage)
*   **The Hook:** 500-for-1 for Four Deuces.

### 2. Full Pay Tens or Better
*   **Return (EV):** 99.14%
*   **House Edge:** 0.86% (Casino Edge)
*   **The Hook:** You get paid for a pair of Tens (unlike Jacks or Better).

**The Winner on EV:** Full Pay Loose Deuces (100.97%) is mathematically superior. It offers a significant player advantage, whereas Tens or Better is a "losing" game even at its best.

## Pay Table Comparison: Jackpot vs. Sustainability

| Hand | Loose Deuces (100.97%) | Tens or Better (99.14%) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Natural Royal** | 800:1 | 800:1 |
| **Four Deuces** | **500:1** | (N/A) |
| **Full House** | 3:1 | 6:1 |
| **Flush** | 2:1 | 5:1 |
| **Pair (10s or higher)** | (N/A) | **1:1** |

In Tens or Better, you are getting paid on a huge percentage of hands because a pair of 10s is very common. In Loose Deuces, you get paid for nothing less than Three-of-a-Kind. This means Tens or Better is designed to keep you playing for a long time, while Loose Deuces is designed to either bankrupt you or make you rich.

## Volatility and Variance
*   **Loose Deuces Variance:** ~74.0
*   **Tens or Better Variance:** ~12.6

**Tens or Better is one of the lowest-volatility games in the casino.** Its variance (12.6) is less than one-fifth that of Loose Deuces (74.0). 
*   In **Tens or Better**, your bankroll will stay relatively flat for a long time. 
*   In **Loose Deuces**, your bankroll will likely plummet rapidly until you hit Four Deuces or a Royal Flush.

## Strategy Complexity
**Loose Deuces** strategy is complex and counter-intuitive. You must learn when to hold a single wild card and when to discard "natural" winners to chase the 500:1 jackpot.

**Tens or Better** strategy is incredibly simple. It is almost identical to Jacks or Better, except you hold any pair of 10s. It is the perfect game for a beginner to play without needing a strategy card or trainer.

### Why Use a Trainer?
Even though Tens or Better is "easy," you still need a **Video Poker Trainer** if you want to hit that 99.14% return. For Loose Deuces, a trainer is absolutely non-negotiable—without one, you will likely play at a 2-3% disadvantage.

## House Edge Analysis: The Reality of the Floor
*   **Full Pay Loose Deuces (100.97%):** Very rare. Most machines are 98.2%.
*   **Full Pay Tens or Better (99.14%):** Common in many casinos. If the Full House drops to 5:1, the EV falls to around 97.8%.

Even at the "Short Pay" level, **Loose Deuces (98.2%)** is often better than a mediocre Tens or Better machine (97.8%). However, you must be able to withstand the 74.0 variance.

## Which Game Should You Play?

**Choose Loose Deuces if:**
*   You are a professional or serious hobbyist looking for an edge.
*   You have a large bankroll and can handle massive losing streaks.
*   You enjoy the excitement of wild cards and 500:1 payouts.

**Choose Tens or Better if:**
*   You are a beginner or a "casual" player.
*   You want your $100 to last for several hours of play.
*   You don't want to memorize complex strategy charts.

## Conclusion
Mathematically, **Loose Deuces (100.97%)** is the better game by a wide margin. It is a "professional" game. **Tens or Better (99.14%)** is a "recreational" game. If your goal is to win money over the long term, you must master Loose Deuces. If your goal is to enjoy a few drinks and play some poker without losing too much too fast, Tens or Better is the perfect choice.
