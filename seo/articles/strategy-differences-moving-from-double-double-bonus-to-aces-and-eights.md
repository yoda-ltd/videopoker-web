# Strategy Differences: Moving from Double Double Bonus to Aces and Eights

Transitioning from Double Double Bonus (DDB) to Aces and Eights (A8) is a unique step in the video poker world. Both games are popular variants that offer specific payouts for Four of a Kind. This guide provides the best video poker strategy comparison to help you understand how to play video poker like a pro during this transition.

## The Double Double Bonus Baseline

In Double Double Bonus, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is built on the fact that any pair of Jacks or better is a winning hand. This creates a very stable, low-volatility experience. When moving to Aces and Eights, that "safety net" is removed. You now need at least a pair of Queens or Kings to get your money back.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from Double Double Bonus to Aces and Eights, you'll find that **80% of your strategy remains the same**. The **20% that changes** is entirely focused on the "Pair threshold." This 20% shift causes the most **Bankroll Drain** if not corrected.

### The 20% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Pair of Jacks Devaluation:** In Double Double Bonus, a pair of Jacks is a winning hand. In Aces and Eights, it's a "trash" hand that you should rarely hold unless you're drawing for something better (like a three-card Royal Flush).
2.  **Aces and Eights Focus:** Aces and Eights is named "Aces and Eights" because it rewards you specifically for getting four Aces or four 8s. This makes four-of-a-kind and specific straights much more valuable than in Double Double Bonus.
3.  **Two Pair Strategy:** While Two Pair pays 2-for-1 in both games, the higher payout for specific "Aces and Eights" hands in Aces and Eights means you might break a Full House to draw for a fifth card more often than you would in Double Double Bonus.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

Aces and Eights introduces **Volatility Scaling**. Without the Jacks-or-better push, your bankroll will experience more frequent dips. **EV Erosion** occurs when a DDB player instinctively holds a pair of Jacks in Aces and Eights. This is a "dead hand" and holding it is a direct loss of expected value.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Queens or Kings or Better are your new minimum "win."
*   **Modify:** Discard any pair of Jacks unless they are part of a better draw.
*   **Target:** Aggressively pursue the "Aces and Eights" bonuses; they are the heart of the Aces and Eights pay table.

By recognizing that the "safety net" has moved, you can successfully transition to Aces and Eights without suffering from significant EV Erosion.
