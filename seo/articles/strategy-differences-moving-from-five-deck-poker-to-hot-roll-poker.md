## Deck Complexity vs. Multiplier Intensity
The fundamental difference lies in the composition of the cards and the reward structure.

- **Five Deck Poker (The Probability Shift):** In Five Deck Poker, each card in your hand comes from a separate 52-card deck. This allows for "impossible" hands like five Aces of Spades. The strategy is entirely focused on the increased probability of Quads and specific "Five-of-a-Kind" payouts.
- **Hot Roll Poker (The Multiplier Specialist):** Hot Roll uses a standard single 52-card deck (per hand). The complexity isn't in the cards, but in the **10-coin side-bet** that triggers a dice roll multiplier (2x to 12x) on random deals.

## Key Strategic Adjustments

### 1. From "Five-of-a-Kind" Hunting to "Pure EV"
- **In Five Deck Poker:** Your strategy is radically different from standard poker. You are hunting for duplicated cards. Holding a single Ace of Spades and an Ace of Hearts from different decks is the start of a massive payout.
- **In Hot Roll Poker:** You return to "Standard Video Poker Strategy" (e.g., Jacks or Better or Double Double Bonus). The deck behaves normally. You don't hunt for duplicates because they don't exist. Your goal is simply to play perfect standard strategy to maximize the value of the random multipliers.

### 2. Variance Normalization and Bankroll Management
- **Five Deck Poker Shift:** Five Deck Poker has unique variance. While Quads are more common, the "top-heavy" nature of Five-of-a-Kind payouts creates long dry spells.
- **Hot Roll Poker Shift:** Hot Roll has high, aggressive variance. You are betting 10 coins per hand (double the standard). The multipliers are frequent (roughly 1 in 6 hands), but if you aren't winning when the "12x" roll hits, your bankroll will evaporate quickly.
- **The Adjustment:** Hot Roll requires a more traditional "Multi-Play" bankroll. You must be prepared for the 10-coin-per-line cost. Five Deck Poker requires a "Niche Specialist" bankroll focused on chasing those rare multi-deck combinations.

### 3. Feature Hit Frequency
- **Five Deck Poker:** The "Feature" is the deck itself—it is active on every hand.
- **Hot Roll Poker:** The "Feature" (the dice roll) is active on roughly 16% of hands.
- **The Shift:** Moving to Hot Roll satisfies the need for "Action" in a different way. Instead of the constant, subtle probability shift of Five Deck, you get the "Flash and Bang" of the Hot Roll dice.
