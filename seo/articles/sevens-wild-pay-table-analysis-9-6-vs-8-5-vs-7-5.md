# Sevens Wild: 9/6 vs 8/5 vs 7/5 Pay Table Analysis

For any video poker player, the pay table is the single most important factor in determining the house edge. In Sevens Wild, a unique variant where all 7s are wild, the most common payout schedules are the **9/6**, **8/5**, and **7/5** variants. These numbers refer to the payout for a Full House and a Flush, respectively.

The table below shows the typical differences for a 1-coin bet across common schedules (Kings or Better version):

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Natural Royal Flush** | 800 | 800 | 800 |

## The Impact on Return to Player (RTP)
*   **9/6 Sevens Wild:** Often considered the "Full Pay" version, this variant has an RTP of approximately **99.29%** when played with optimal strategy.
*   **8/5 Sevens Wild:** The reduction in the Full House and Flush payouts drops the RTP to roughly **98.44%**.
*   **7/5 Sevens Wild:** This version is common in many land-based casinos and has a significantly lower RTP, often around **97.19%**.

The difference between a 9/6 and a 7/5 game might seem small, but over a session of 1,000 hands at a $1 denomination, a 2% difference in RTP translates to a $20 difference in expected loss.

## High-Limit Sevens Wild Considerations
In **high-limit video poker** lounges, you are more likely to find 9/6 or even 20/7/5 schedules. High rollers should always scan the pay table before sitting down, as the volatility of Sevens Wild can be high due to the four wild cards (the Sevens) making bigger hands like Five of a Kind possible.

## Strategy Adjustments
Your strategy must change slightly depending on the pay table. In a 9/6 game, you can afford to hold onto certain draws (like three to a Flush) more aggressively than in a 7/5 game where the Flush payout is reduced.

## Conclusion: Which Should You Play?
The answer is simple: always seek out the 9/6 Sevens Wild machine. If only 8/5 or 7/5 are available, your house edge increases significantly.
