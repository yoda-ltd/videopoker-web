# The Payout Gap: Aces and Faces vs. Aces and Eights Explained
Category: Comparison

Understanding the **Strategic Divergence** between **Aces and Faces** and **Aces and Eights** is key to mastering these high-payout variants. This comparison focuses on how the paytables differ and why those differences lead to unique **EV Maximization** paths.

### The Comparison Matrix: Aces and Faces vs. Aces and Eights

| Feature | Aces and Faces (8/5) | Aces and Eights (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.26% | 99.78% |
| **Variance Index** | 22.1 | 21.8 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | Face Card Quad Bonuses | Targeted Quad Bonuses (Aces/8s/7s) |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.74% | 0.22% |

### The Rank Gap: Faces vs. Tiers

In **Aces and Faces**, the payouts for all face-card quads (Kings, Queens, Jacks) are significantly increased. In **Aces and Eights**, the "Faces" lose their special status and are replaced by tiered bonuses for Aces, 8s, and 7s. This leads to a similar **Volatility Normalization** (21.8 vs 22.1).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is high strategy overlap (approximately 85%) because both are based on a 52-card natural deck. However, in Aces and Faces, you prioritize face cards much more than in Aces and Eights. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the face cards in Aces and Faces dominate the EV landscape for middle-tier wins.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Face Devaluation:** When moving to Aces and Eights, Jacks, Queens, and Kings are simply "high cards" for pair purposes.
2.  **Tiered Quad Awareness:** In Aces and Eights, you must be aware of the specific bonuses for 8s and 7s.
3.  **Return Priority:** Aces and Eights offers a superior theoretical return (99.78%), making it the better choice for the long-term player.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that Aces and Eights is the superior game for the player looking for the lowest house edge, while Aces and Faces is better for those who enjoy the excitement of face-card bonuses. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are solid choices for the recreational player.

# The Payout Gap: Aces and Faces vs. All American Explained
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Aces and Faces** to **All American** is a shift from a "Quad-Focused" variant to a "Connectivity-Focused" variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your hands and the volatility that comes with them.

### The Comparison Matrix: Aces and Faces vs. All American

| Feature | Aces and Faces (8/5) | All American (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.26% | 100.72% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 22.1 | 60.0 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | Face Card Quad Bonuses | Straight/Flush connectivity |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.74% | -0.72% (Player Edge) |

### The Connectivity Gap: Straights and Flushes

In **All American**, the payouts for Straights and Flushes are significantly increased (34-for-1 and 8-for-1 respectively). In **Aces and Faces**, these hands pay the standard 4-for-1 and 5-for-1. This creates a massive **Volatility Normalization** shift. In All American, you are looking for connectivity above all else.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 50% overlap. In Aces and Faces, you would never discard a high pair. In All American, you prioritize the Straight and Flush draws, often breaking pairs to maximize the high-payout connectivity. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the connectivity in All American dominates the EV landscape.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Connectivity Priority:** In All American, prioritize 4-to-a-flush and 4-to-a-straight over small pairs.
2.  **Face Devaluation:** In All American, face-card quads are only valuable for a standard payout.
3.  **Bankroll Requirement:** Because of the Variance Index of 60.0, All American requires a much larger bankroll to withstand the swings.

A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that All American is a superior choice for the expert player who wants a high return with an edge. The **best video poker strategy comparison** highlights that connectivity is the ultimate tool for **EV Maximization** in All American.

# The Payout Gap: Aces and Faces vs. Joker Poker Explained
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Aces and Faces** to **Joker Poker** involves a shift from a "Natural" variant to a "Wild Card" variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your hands and the volatility that comes with them.

### The Comparison Matrix: Aces and Faces vs. Joker Poker

| Feature | Aces and Faces (8/5) | Joker Poker (Kings or Better) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.26% | 100.64% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 22.1 | 15.5 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | Face Card Quad Bonuses | Wild Card Connectivity (Joker) |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.74% | -0.64% (Player Edge) |

### The Wild Gap: The 53rd Card

In **Joker Poker**, the Joker is your most powerful tool. In **Aces and Faces**, all 52 cards are natural, and you are looking for specific bonuses on face-card quads. This leads to a lower **Volatility Normalization** (15.5 vs 22.1).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 60% overlap. In Aces and Faces, you prioritize face cards. In Joker Poker, you prioritize the Joker above all else, often breaking face pairs to maximize the wild card's potential. This is the essence of **EV Maximization** in wild card games.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Joker Supremacy:** Never discard a Joker.
2.  **High Card Floor Shift:** In Joker Poker, the floor is Kings. This means you hold Kings and Aces much more frequently than in Aces and Faces.
3.  **Natural Connectivity:** In Joker Poker, you are looking for wild card connectivity to complete hands.

Through a detailed **video poker house edge analysis**, it's clear that Joker Poker offers a superior return with lower variance. The **best video poker strategy comparison** for this transition is: Use the Joker to smooth out the swings and find the edge.
