# Ultimate Triple Double Bonus Strategy Chart for 2026

Triple Double Bonus (TDB) is the "Nuclear" version of video poker. It is known for its high volatility and its massive "kicker" rewards that transform the entire **EV Maximization** landscape. To understand **how to play video poker like a pro**, you must first understand why TDB is fundamentally different from Jacks or Better or other bonus poker variants.

## Analysis: 3 Most Common "Pro-Level" Errors in Triple Double Bonus

### 1. Misunderstanding the "Kicker" and Quads
The most significant difference in TDB is that a 4-of-a-kind (Aces, 2s, 3s, 4s) with a kicker (A-4) pays a massive 800 credits on a 5-coin bet (4,000 credits).

**The Error:** Keeping a kicker with only 2 or 3 of a kind.
**The Pro Reality:** A common "Pro-Level" mistake is holding an Ace and a 2 (e.g., `A-A-2`) from the initial deal. In TDB, **Strategy Fine-Tuning** dictates that you *never* keep a kicker unless you already have the 4-of-a-kind. Holding the kicker actually *reduces* your probability of drawing the 3rd or 4th matching card. The **Expected Return Differential** strongly favors drawing for the quads first.

### 2. Breaking Three of a Kind for the Royal Flush
Many players, blinded by the massive Royal payout, will break a high-ranking 3-of-a-kind (like Aces) to draw for 3 to a Royal Flush.

**The Error:** Discarding `A-A` from an `A-A-A-K-Q` (all Hearts) hand to draw for a Royal Flush.
**The Pro Reality:** In TDB, three Aces are incredibly valuable because of the potential for the 800-credit quad Aces. **Rank-Based Priority** in TDB is heavily weighted toward the quads. You *never* break three Aces for anything other than a guaranteed Royal Flush.

### 3. Misjudging Full House Equity
In many video poker variants, a Full House is a "stop" hand (you keep it). In TDB, the Full House payout is often relatively low compared to the potential for quad Aces.

**The Error:** Keeping a Full House that includes 3 Aces (e.g., `A-A-A-5-5`).
**The Pro Reality:** This is a classic **EV Maximization** conflict. If the payout for 4 Aces is 800 credits and the Full House is only 45, the mathematical "Expected Return Differential" dictates breaking the Full House to go for the quads. Pro players consistently discard the pair in a Full House with three Aces.

## Hand-Ranking Priorities in Triple Double Bonus
The "Hand-Ranking Priority" in TDB is unique: **Aces > Everything else.** Because of the massive 800-to-1 payout for quad Aces with a kicker, an Ace has higher "equity" than almost any other card in the deck. **Strategy Fine-Tuning** in TDB requires a "Quad-Focused" mindset. If you want to learn **how to play video poker like a pro**, you must be willing to sacrifice small, guaranteed payouts for the high-variance, high-reward quads that define this game.
