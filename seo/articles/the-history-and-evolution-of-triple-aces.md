# The History and Evolution of Triple Aces Bonus Poker

Triple Aces Bonus Poker is a specialized evolution within the "Bonus Poker" family, designed to satisfy the cravings of players who prioritize the most powerful card in the deck. This variant represents a refinement of the "Ace-centric" game design that began in the early 1990s.

## The "Aces" Lineage

To understand Triple Aces, one must look at the progression of video poker:
1.  **Jacks or Better:** All Four of a Kind hands pay the same.
2.  **Bonus Poker:** Four Aces pay a premium.
3.  **Double Bonus Poker:** Four Aces pay double the standard bonus.
4.  **Triple Aces:** Pushes the payout structure even further, often creating a tiered system where Aces are in a league of their own.

Triple Aces was introduced to provide a "middle ground" jackpot. It specifically caters to players who find the 800-to-1 odds of a Royal Flush too daunting and prefer to chase the much more common Four Aces.

## Technological Evolution

The rise of Triple Aces coincided with the shift from analog "drum" machines to digital "screen" machines. Digital RNGs allowed developers to create complex pay tables that could sustain the high payouts of Triple Aces by slightly reducing the payouts of common hands like Two Pair or the Flush.

## Essential Video Poker Terminology

For players exploring this variant, these are video poker terms every player should know:

*   **Bankroll:** Chasing "Triple Aces" requires a resilient bankroll. Because so much of the machine's return is tied up in the Four Aces payout, you will experience more "losing sessions" between the big hits.
*   **Full Pay:** Finding a "Full Pay" Triple Aces machine is the key to professional success. Check the complete video poker glossary to understand how a "9/6" vs "8/5" table impacts your bottom line.
*   **Action:** Triple Aces is designed to keep players in their seats. The frequent "small" wins (like Three of a Kind) provide enough "action" to keep the bankroll moving while waiting for the Aces.

## Strategy and "The Chase"

The evolution of Triple Aces created a new breed of "Ace Chasers." These players use a modified strategy where they will hold a single Ace over almost any other single high card, and will frequently break up low pairs if they have an Ace to "go with it."

## Modern Availability

Today, Triple Aces is most commonly found as a sub-menu option on "All-Star Poker" and "Game King" multi-game platforms. It remains popular in both land-based casinos and online platforms because it offers a clear, achievable goal: **Get the Aces.**

Understanding the history of Triple Aces is essential for any player looking to master the "Bonus" style of video poker. It is a game of focus, patience, and—above all—Aces.
