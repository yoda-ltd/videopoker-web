# Bankroll Management for Double Jackpot Poker Grinders: Strategizing the High-Multiplier Quads

Double Jackpot Poker is a compelling video poker variant that offers significantly enhanced payouts for Four-of-a-Kind hands, particularly those with high-rank cards (J, Q, K, A). For the professional grinder, this game offers a high-action volatility profile similar to Double Bonus. Mastering the **best video poker bankroll management** for Double Jackpot Poker requires a focus on Quad-Frequency and **Volatility Buffering**.

## The Mathematics of Double Jackpot: Variance Index

Double Jackpot Poker is a high-variance game. Its **Variance Index** typically ranges from **28.0 to 35.0**, making it more volatile than Jacks or Better (19.5) but more stable than the extreme "Triple" variants.

### 1. The "Two Pair" Tax
In many versions of Double Jackpot, Two Pair only pays **1-for-1**. This is the primary driver of the game's volatility. Without the 2-for-1 "safety net," your bankroll will experience faster "burn periods" while you wait for the high-paying quads.

### 2. Standard Deviation and Risk of Ruin (RoR)
The standard deviation for Double Jackpot is roughly 5.50 to 6.20 per hand.
- To maintain a **Risk of Ruin** below 1%, a professional needs a bankroll of at least **5,000 to 8,000 units**.
- For a $0.25 player ($1.25/bet), a **Life-Of-Bankroll** of $1,500 to $2,000 is the minimum for professional security.

## High-Multiplier Quads: The Core Payouts

In Double Jackpot Poker, the payouts for Four-of-a-Kind are structured to favor "Jackpot" cards:
- **Four Aces:** 160-for-1 (or higher depending on the pay table).
- **Four Faces (J, Q, K):** 80-for-1.
- **Other Quads:** 40-for-1 to 50-for-1.

These "Jackpot Quads" are the "lifeblood" of the grinder. A Four-of-a-Kind occurs roughly every 420 hands. However, hitting the "Aces" or "Faces" quads specifically takes much longer, which creates a "bumpy" bankroll curve.

The **Royal Flush Cycle** remains standard at 1 in ~40,000 hands.
