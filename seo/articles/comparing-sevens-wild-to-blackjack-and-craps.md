# Comparing Sevens Wild to Blackjack and Craps

Sevens Wild is a popular video poker variant where all 7s are wild. How does this action-packed game compare to the "big two" of the casino: Blackjack and Craps?

### Theoretical Return Battle
- **Sevens Wild**: A full-pay machine can offer a **Theoretical Return** of over 99% (specifically 99.11% on some configurations).
- **Blackjack**: Offers a 99.5% RTP (0.5% house edge) with perfect basic strategy.
- **Craps (Pass Line)**: Offers a 98.59% RTP (1.41% house edge).

While Blackjack still offers the best base odds, Sevens Wild is significantly better than a standard game of Craps and offers more excitement with its wild card mechanics.

### Variance and Sustainability
The trade-off for the wild cards is **Variance**. Sevens Wild is more volatile than Blackjack or Craps because a significant portion of its return is tied to hitting Four Sevens or a Wild Royal Flush. For **Session Sustainability**, you need a larger bankroll for **Variance Buffering**. A "dry spell" in Sevens Wild will be deeper than one in Blackjack.

### The Max Bet Essential
"Is max bet always better for bankroll longevity?" In Blackjack and Craps, your bet size doesn't change the house edge. In Sevens Wild, the 5th coin is mandatory to get the full Royal Flush bonus and the advertised RTP. If you aren't playing max bet, the RTP drops well below that of a standard game of Craps.

### Strategy and Hit Frequency
Sevens Wild requires a very specific strategy that focuses on **Hit Frequency Optimization** for the sevens. This is more complex than Blackjack's basic strategy. For those looking for **video poker tips for beginners**, our advice is to master standard Deuces Wild before tackling the slightly different strategy of Sevens Wild.

### RNG vs. Table Randomness
While Blackjack and Craps involve physical elements, Sevens Wild relies on **Pseudo-Randomness** through a certified RNG. This ensures the game is fair and every hand is independent. To learn **how to win video poker daily**, you must trust the RNG and focus entirely on your mathematical strategy.

Sevens Wild is a fantastic alternative for those who find Blackjack too slow or Craps too random. If you can find a good pay table and have the bankroll for the swings, it is one of the most rewarding games on the floor.
