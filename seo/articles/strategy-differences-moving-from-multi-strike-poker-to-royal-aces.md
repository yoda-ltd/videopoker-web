# Strategy Differences: Moving from Multi-Strike Poker to Royal Aces

Transitioning from Multi-Strike Poker to Royal Aces is a unique step in the video poker world. Both games are niche variants that offer a "layered" or "bonus" experience. This guide provides the best video poker strategy comparison to help you understand how to play video poker like a pro during this transition.

## The Multi-Strike Poker Baseline

In Multi-Strike Poker, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is built on the fact that any pair of Jacks or better is a winning hand. This creates a very stable, low-volatility experience. When moving to Royal Aces, that "safety net" is removed. You now need at least a pair of Queens or Kings to get your money back.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from Multi-Strike Poker to Royal Aces, you'll find that **80% of your strategy remains the same**. The **20% that changes** is entirely focused on the "Pair threshold." This 20% shift causes the most **Bankroll Drain** if not corrected.

### The 20% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Pair of Jacks Devaluation:** In Multi-Strike Poker, a pair of Jacks is a winning hand. In Royal Aces, it's a "trash" hand that you should rarely hold unless you're drawing for something better (like a three-card Royal Flush).
2.  **Royal Aces Focus:** Royal Aces is named "Royal Aces" because it rewards you specifically for getting a Royal Flush or four Aces. This makes four-of-a-kind and specific straights much more valuable than in Multi-Strike Poker.
3.  **Two Pair Strategy:** While Two Pair pays 2-for-1 in both games, the higher payout for specific "Royal Aces" hands in Royal Aces means you might break a Full House to draw for a fifth card more often than you would in Multi-Strike Poker.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

Royal Aces introduces **Volatility Scaling**. Without the Jacks-or-better push, your bankroll will experience more frequent dips. **EV Erosion** occurs when a Multi-Strike-Poker player instinctively holds a pair of Jacks in Royal Aces. This is a "dead hand" and holding it is a direct loss of expected value.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Queens or Kings or Better are your new minimum "win."
*   **Modify:** Discard any pair of Jacks unless they are part of a better draw.
*   **Target:** Aggressively pursue the "Royal Aces" bonuses; they are the heart of the Royal Aces pay table.

By recognizing that the "safety net" has moved, you can successfully transition to Royal Aces without suffering from significant EV Erosion.
