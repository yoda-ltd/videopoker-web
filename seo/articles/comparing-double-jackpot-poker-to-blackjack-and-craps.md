# Comparing Double Jackpot Poker to Blackjack and Craps

Double Jackpot Poker is a variant that offers extra payouts for Four-of-a-Kind hands, especially those with high-ranking "kickers." How does this high-action game compare to the "big two" of the casino: Blackjack and Craps?

### Theoretical Return Comparison
- **Double Jackpot Poker (9/6)**: Offers a **Theoretical Return** of approximately 99.63% with perfect strategy.
- **Blackjack**: Offers a 99.5% RTP (0.5% house edge) with perfect basic strategy.
- **Craps (Pass Line)**: Offers a 98.59% RTP (1.41% house edge).

Interestingly, a full-pay Double Jackpot Poker machine can actually offer a slightly *higher* RTP than standard Blackjack, making it one of the best bets in the entire casino.

### Variance and Sustainability
The trade-off for the high RTP is **Variance**. Double Jackpot Poker is much more volatile than Blackjack or Craps because a significant portion of its return is tied to the bonus Four-of-a-Kind hands. For **Session Sustainability**, you need a much larger bankroll for **Variance Buffering**. A "dry spell" in Double Jackpot Poker will be much deeper than one in Blackjack.

### The Max Bet Essential
"Is max bet always better for bankroll longevity?" In Blackjack and Craps, your bet size doesn't change the house edge. In Double Jackpot Poker, the 5th coin is mandatory to get the full Royal Flush bonus and the advertised RTP. If you aren't playing max bet, the RTP drops below that of a standard game of Craps.
