# The History and Evolution of Hot Roll Poker

Hot Roll Poker is a "High-Energy" variant that represents the ultimate convergence of video poker strategy and the "Big Hit" psychology of slot machines. Its history is a testament to the era of "Action Gaming," where players demanded more than just a fair hand; they wanted a "Game within a Game." Today, it is a flagship title on the **best video poker machines in 2026**.

## The "Dice-Multiplier" Concept
Introduced in the early 2010s by Action Gaming and IGT, Hot Roll Poker sought to capture the "Adrenaline" of the Craps table and port it into the multi-hand video poker environment.
- **The Mechanic:** By placing a **10-coin side-bet** (doubling the standard 5-coin bet), players activate the "Hot Roll" feature. 
- **The Roll:** On random deals, a pair of flaming virtual dice rolls across the screen. The sum of the dice (2x to 12x) acts as a multiplier for any winning hand achieved on that deal.

## Evolutionary Milestones
The **history of Hot Roll Poker** is defined by three key innovations:
1.  **The "Physics-Based" Multiplier:** While games like *Super Times Pay* offered random multipliers, Hot Roll introduced "Variable Multipliers" based on the probability of a two-dice roll. This made the game feel more "Organic" and "Fair" to players—a roll of 7 (the most common) felt more natural than a computer-generated "7x" prompt.
2.  **The "Straight-Beats-Royal" Phenomenon:** Because of the 12x multiplier potential, Hot Roll Poker became one of the few games where a simple Straight or Flush could pay more than a standard Royal Flush. This significantly altered the **Side-Bet EV** calculations for professional players.
3.  **Cross-Genre Appeal:** By utilizing the iconography of the Craps table, Hot Roll successfully attracted table-game enthusiasts and slot players who found the "Static" nature of standard Jacks or Better to be too slow.

## Technical Foundations: Action Gaming Patents
The success of Hot Roll is protected by several **Action Gaming patents** that govern the timing and visualization of the multiplier.
- **Feature Hit Frequency:** Hot Roll was meticulously balanced to trigger the dice on roughly 1 in every 6 hands. This frequency is high enough to keep players engaged during losing streaks but low enough to maintain the "Special Event" status of the multiplier.
- **Variance Normalization:** Hot Roll represents "Maximum Variance." The 10-coin requirement increases the "Cost of Play," but the 12x multiplier provides the "Lifeboat" payouts needed to achieve a theoretical RTP of 99%+.

## Professional Strategy in the Hot Roll Era
Does the dice roll change the "rare video poker variants strategy"?
- **The Card Strategy:** Surprisingly, no. Because the multiplier is determined *before* or *on* the deal, the "Optimal Hold" for the underlying game remains the same. 
- **The Bankroll Strategy:** This is where the pros differ. Hot Roll requires a much larger bankroll to sustain the 10-coin bet. A professional transition to Hot Roll involves "Denomination Dropping"—moving from quarters to nickels to handle the increased volatility.
