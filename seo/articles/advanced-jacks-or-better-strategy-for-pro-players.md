## The Mechanics of Video Poker Teams: Team Variance Reduction

While JoB has low variance compared to Double Double Bonus, it still requires **pro video poker team strategy** for maximum efficiency.

### Bankroll Pooling in JoB
- **The Rationale:** Even in JoB, a "Royal Flush Drought" can last 40,000 hands (~50-80 hours of play).
- **The Solution:** A team uses **Bankroll Pooling**. By having five players grind 9/6 JoB, the team effectively "Shortens the Royal Cycle." Instead of one player waiting 80 hours for a Royal, the team (collectively) expects a Royal every 16 hours.
- **Team Variance Reduction:** The "Slow and Steady" wins from Two Pair and Three of a Kind provide the "fuel" for the team to stay in the game until the big hit.

## Scouting Logic: Finding the "Full Pay" 9/6 JoB

Professional JoB players are masters of **Information Arbitrage**. The 9/6 table (99.54%) is the *only* table a pro will play.

1. **Scouting Networks:** Pros pay scouts to find the last remaining 9/6 JoB machines. Many casinos have downgraded to 8/5 or 7/5 (97-96%), which are "Unplayable" for a pro.
2. **Scouting for Progressives:** Often, JoB is part of a "Progressive Bank." A pro team will monitor the Royal Flush jackpot. When it exceeds $1,200 (on a 25c machine), the game becomes **Positive EV** (>100%), and the team will deploy a **Session Relay**.

## Deep Dive: EV Maximization and the "Perfect Strategy"

JoB strategy is mathematically fixed. Any deviation is an EV leak.

### The "9/6" Inversion
- **Standard Play:** "Hold the High Cards."
- **JoB Pro Play:** A professional knows that a "Low Pair" is always more valuable than a single High Card, and even more valuable than two "Unsuited" High Cards.
- **EV Maximization:** The goal is the "Flush" and "Full House" (the 9 and 6). These are the hands that sustain the bankroll. A pro never "Chases the Royal" at the expense of a pat Flush or a pat Straight.
