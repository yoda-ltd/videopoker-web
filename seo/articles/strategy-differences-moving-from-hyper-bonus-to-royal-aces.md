## Multiplier Action vs. Jackpot Concentration
The core difference lies in *where* the payout value is concentrated.

- **Hyper Bonus (The Broad Broadcaster):** You bet 10 coins per line to trigger **Accumulated Multipliers** on any dealt winning hand. A pair of Jacks, a Flush, or a Full House can all be multiplied. The value is spread across the entire pay table.
- **Royal Aces (The Narrow Specialist):** You typically play a standard 5-coin or 10-coin game (depending on the cabinet) that offers massive, outsized payouts for Royal Flushes and Aces. The value is concentrated at the very top of the pay table.

## Key Strategic Adjustments

### 1. From "Hold-for-Multiplier" to "Hold-for-Aces"
- **In Hyper Bonus:** You might hold any winning combination on the deal to "lock in" a multiplier.
- **In Royal Aces:** You will often break standard winning hands (like a Low Pair or even a Straight) to hold a single Ace or a two-card Royal Flush draw. The "Royal Aces" pay table is so top-heavy that the EV of chasing the big hands outweighs the safety of the small ones.

### 2. Variance Normalization and Bankroll Management
- **Hyper Bonus Shift:** Moderate to High variance. You get frequent "Mini-Jackpots" via the multipliers.
- **Royal Aces Shift:** Extreme variance. You are in "Home Run" mode. You will experience long, brutal losing streaks where the base game feels like a "Money Pit," punctuated by massive, singular wins that "make" your entire month.
- **The Adjustment:** You need a much deeper bankroll for Royal Aces. While Hyper Bonus "Feeds" you multipliers to keep you going, Royal Aces "Starves" you until you hit the big one.

### 3. Feature Hit Frequency
- **Hyper Bonus:** High frequency (multiplier event on ~16% of hands).
- **Royal Aces:** Low frequency (hitting the specific "Aces" or "Royal" payouts happens rarely).
- **The Shift:** In Hyper Bonus, you get "Feedback" on almost every winning hand. In Royal Aces, you are playing a "High-Stakes Sniper" game. Moving to Royal Aces requires a shift from "Action" to "Discipline."
