# Common Mistakes to Avoid in Quick Quads

Quick Quads is a unique video poker variant where a 3-of-a-kind plus two other cards that sum to the same rank (e.g., three 6s plus a 2 and a 4) counts as a 4-of-a-kind. While the "extra" quads are exciting, they lead to many common player errors.

### Missing the "Quick" Quads
The most common mistake in Quick Quads is simply not seeing the potential quad. Players often discard cards that could sum to the rank of their 3-of-a-kind. This destroys your **Hit Frequency Optimization**. To learn **how to win video poker daily** on a Quick Quads machine, you must develop a "summing" mindset and see the potential in every hand.

### Over-Chasing the Quads
The opposite mistake is also common: holding a pair of low cards in the hope of getting a "sum" to match a 3-of-a-kind you don't even have yet. This lowers your **Theoretical Return**. You must follow the specific Quick Quads strategy chart, which mathematically balances the value of the extra quads with the standard poker hands.

### "Is Max Bet Always Better for Bankroll Longevity?"
In Quick Quads, you must place an extra bet (usually 6 coins instead of 5) to activate the feature. If you don't play the 6th coin, you are playing a standard game on a machine that often has a lower-than-average base pay table. For those looking for **video poker tips for beginners**, our number one tip for Quick Quads is: play 6 coins or find a different machine.

### Bankroll Mismanagement
Because the 6-coin bet is required, the game is 20% more expensive than standard video poker. This increases the **Variance** and requires more **Variance Buffering**. For **Session Sustainability**, you need a larger bankroll than you would for a 5-coin game. If you aren't prepared for the extra cost, you will bust out faster.

### Pseudo-Randomness vs. True Randomness
Some players think the machine is "hiding" the sum cards when they have a 3-of-a-kind. In reality, the RNG deals the cards using **Pseudo-Randomness** that is fair and independent. The "summing" rule is just a layer of logic on top of a standard random deal.

Quick Quads is a game of mental agility and discipline. Avoid these mistakes, master the strategy, and the "quick" quads will add a significant boost to your bankroll.
