# Aces and Faces vs. Joker Poker: House Edge and Strategy Synergy

In the landscape of "best video poker strategy comparison," **Aces and Faces vs. Joker Poker** is a battle of bonus quads vs. wild card volatility. One game rewards you for hitting specific 4-of-a-kind hands (Jacks, Queens, Kings, and Aces); the other rewards you for the creative use of a 53rd card (the Joker) to complete hands you'd never see in a standard 52-card deck. This creates a massive **Strategy Bifurcation** and alters the fundamental **Payout Weighting** of each game.

## The House Edge Analysis: Bonus Aces vs. Dynamic Joker Draws

To perform a professional **video poker house edge analysis**, we must examine the "Full Pay" versions of both.

| Game | Full Pay Version | Return (RTP) | House Edge |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Aces and Faces** | 8/5 (99.26%) | 99.26% | 0.74% |
| **Joker Poker** | Kings or Better (100.64%) | 100.64% | -0.64% (Player Edge!) |

While **Full Pay Joker Poker** is one of the rare games that can offer a positive return to the player (over 100%), it comes with a high price in terms of bankroll management and **Optimal Hold Variance**.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Natural" vs. "Wild" Royal

The most significant **Strategy Bifurcation** occurs when dealing with the Royal Flush. In Aces and Faces, every Royal Flush is "Natural." In Joker Poker, you have two distinct tiers: the **Natural Royal Flush** (usually 800-for-1) and the **Joker Royal Flush** (usually 100-for-1).

*   **Aces and Faces Strategy:** You never break a made Flush or Straight to chase a 4-card Royal Flush. You always prioritize a High Pair (Jacks or Better) over a 4-card Flush or Straight draw.
*   **Joker Poker Strategy:** If you hold the Joker and 4 to a Natural Royal, the **Optimal Hold** is always the Royal chase. However, if holding the Joker completes a "Wild" Royal, you must calculate the **Strategy Synergy**—is it better to take the guaranteed 100-for-1 or discard the non-Royal cards to hunt the 800-for-1 Natural? In most 99%+ pay tables, you take the guaranteed Wild Royal.

## Strategy Synergy: Where the Games Align

Despite the Joker, there is some **Strategy Synergy** in the "Base Game" logic when you *don't* have the Joker:

1.  **High Pair Priority:** In Aces and Faces, you always hold Jacks or Better. In Joker Poker (Kings or Better version), you always hold Kings or Better.
2.  **Aces are Key:** In both games, 4 Aces pay a bonus (80-for-1 in Aces and Faces; usually 35-for-1 or 40-for-1 in Joker Poker). You always hold a pair of Aces.
3.  **High Card Stability:** Both games pay 1-for-1 for a pair of Jacks (or Kings) or better, providing a stable floor for your bankroll.

## Variance Inversion: Bonus Quads vs. The Wild Card

The most jarring difference for players is the **Variance Inversion**.

*   **Aces and Faces:** The variance is concentrated in the "Quads." You are waiting for 4 Aces (80-for-1) or 4 face cards (40-for-1). This creates a "stair-step" volatility.
*   **Joker Poker:** The Joker smooths out the "losing streaks" by turning "nothing" hands into Pairs or Three-of-a-Kinds. However, the top-heavy nature of the Natural Royal Flush vs. the Wild Royal Flush means your long-term ROI is heavily dependent on hitting that 1-in-40,000 Natural Royal.

## Conclusion: Which to Choose?

If you prefer a mathematically "pure" game with a predictable strategy focused on holding pairs, **Aces and Faces** is an excellent choice.

However, if you enjoy the tactical complexity of managing a wild card and the frequent "saves" it provides, **Joker Poker** offers a unique challenge. For a complete **video poker house edge analysis**, always check the pay table: an 8/5 Aces and Faces machine will be more stable, but a Full Pay Joker Poker machine offers one of the best returns in the casino.
