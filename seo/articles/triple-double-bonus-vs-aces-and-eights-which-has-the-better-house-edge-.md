# Strategy Differences: Moving from Triple Double Bonus to Aces and Eights
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Triple Double Bonus** to **Aces and Eights** is a shift from a "Kicker-Based" high-volatility variant to a "Targeted Bonus" natural variant. This represents a significant **Strategic Divergence** in how you value specific card ranks and their related bonuses.

### The Comparison Matrix: Triple Double Bonus vs. Aces and Eights

| Feature | Triple Double Bonus (9/6) | Aces and Eights (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.58% | 99.78% |
| **Variance Index** | 98.0 | 21.8 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Quads with Specific Kickers | Target Specific Aces/8s/7s Bonuses |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.42% | 0.22% |

### Strategic Divergence: Kicker Jackpot vs. Bonus Tiers

In **Triple Double Bonus Poker**, the payout for Four Aces with an Ace, 2, 3, or 4 kicker is 4,000 credits—the same as a Royal Flush! This creates massive **Payout Compression** across the rest of the table. In **Aces and Eights**, the bonuses are tiered for Aces, 8s, and 7s, but without the extreme kicker multiplier. This leads to a much lower **Volatility Normalization** (21.8 vs 98.0).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 70% overlap on the core hand results. However, in Triple Double Bonus, you will break a Full House if it contains three Aces and a low kicker to draw for the 4,000-credit bonus. In Aces and Eights, you would almost always keep the Full House. This is the essence of **EV Maximization** in high-variance kicker games.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Kicker Removal:** You no longer need to manage kickers for the bonus in Aces and Eights. Your strategy is hand-by-hand.
2.  **Full House Priority:** In Aces and Eights, a Full House is a very strong winner. In Triple Double Bonus, it is often broken to chase the kicker jackpot.
3.  **Aces/8s/7s Focus:** In Aces and Eights, you are looking for specific quad tiers for the bonus.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that Triple Double Bonus is a high-action game that rewards kicker hunting, but Aces and Eights provides a much smoother ride with lower variance. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are excellent choices for the disciplined player.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Triple Double Bonus to Aces and Faces
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Triple Double Bonus** to **Aces and Faces** is a shift from a "Kicker-Based" variant to a "Faces Bonus" variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value specific card ranks and their related bonuses.

### The Comparison Matrix: Triple Double Bonus vs. Aces and Faces

| Feature | Triple Double Bonus (9/6) | Aces and Faces (8/5) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.58% | 99.26% |
| **Variance Index** | 98.0 | 22.1 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Quads with Kickers | Target Face Card Quads |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.42% | 0.74% |

### Strategic Divergence: Kicker Jackpot vs. Faces Bonus

In **Triple Double Bonus Poker**, the kicker jackpot is your primary goal. In **Aces and Faces**, the payouts for all face-card quads are increased. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through tiered quads. Aces and Faces' variance (22.1) is much lower than Triple Double Bonus (98.0), providing a smoother **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 80% overlap on the core hand results. However, in Triple Double Bonus, you will prioritize Aces and kickers above any other card. In Aces and Faces, the faces are also a major target. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the kicker jackpot in Triple Double Bonus dominates the EV landscape.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Face Card Priority:** In Aces and Faces, you prioritize Kings, Queens, and Jacks much more than in Triple Double Bonus.
2.  **Kicker Removal:** You no longer need to manage kickers for the bonus. Your strategy is hand-by-hand.
3.  **Variance Management:** Triple Double Bonus is much more "swingy" than Aces and Faces. You need a much larger bankroll to withstand the swings of kicker hunting.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games are highly rewarding for the expert player, but Aces and Faces provides a smoother ride with lower variance. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are top-tier choices for anyone looking for a player advantage.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Triple Double Bonus to All American
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Triple Double Bonus** to **All American** is a shift from a "Kicker-Based" variant to a "Straight/Flush Heavy" variant. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value connected cards versus kicker potential.

### The Comparison Matrix: Triple Double Bonus vs. All American

| Feature | Triple Double Bonus (9/6) | All American (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.58% | 100.72% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 98.0 | 60.0 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Quads with Kickers | Target Straight/Flush Payouts |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.42% | -0.72% (Player Edge) |

### Strategic Divergence: Kicker Jackpot vs. Connectivity

In **Triple Double Bonus Poker**, the kicker jackpot is your primary goal. In **All American**, the payouts for Straights and Flushes are significantly increased. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through connectivity and volume. Both games have high variance (60.0 to 98.0), requiring careful **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is minimal (less than 10%). In Triple Double Bonus, you would never discard an Ace. In All American, you will break a pair of Aces to chase a Straight or Flush draw if the math supports it. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the straight/flush payouts in All American dominate the EV landscape.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Kicker Removal:** You no longer need to manage kickers for the bonus.
2.  **Connector Priority:** In All American, four-to-a-straight and four-to-a-flush are gold. In Triple Double Bonus, they are almost entirely ignored in favor of the kicker jackpot.
3.  **Bankroll Requirement:** Both games require a large bankroll to withstand the swings of high-payout strategies.

A **video poker house edge analysis** indicates that while All American offers a significant player advantage in its full-pay version, it is emotionally taxing. The **best video poker strategy comparison** for this transition is: Forget the kickers; find the straights.
