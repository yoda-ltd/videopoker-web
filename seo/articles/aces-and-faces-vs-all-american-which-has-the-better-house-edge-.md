# Aces and Faces vs. All American: House Edge and Strategy Synergy

In the landscape of "best video poker strategy comparison," **Aces and Faces vs. All American** is a battle of bonus quads vs. draw-driven payouts. One game rewards you for hitting specific 4-of-a-kind hands (Jacks, Queens, Kings, and Aces); the other rewards you for completing long-shot draws like Straights and Flushes. This creates a massive **Strategy Bifurcation** and alters the fundamental **Payout Weighting** of each game.

## The House Edge Analysis: Bonus Aces vs. 8-for-1 Straights/Flushes

To perform a professional **video poker house edge analysis**, we must look at the "Full Pay" versions of both.

| Game | Full Pay Version | Return (RTP) | House Edge |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Aces and Faces** | 8/5 (99.26%) | 99.26% | 0.74% |
| **All American** | 8/8 (100.72%) | 100.72% | -0.72% (Player Edge!) |

While **Full Pay All American** is one of the rare games that can offer a positive return to the player (over 100%), it comes with a high price in terms of bankroll management and **Optimal Hold Variance**.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Quad" vs. "Draw" Hunt

The most significant "Strategy Bifurcation" occurs when dealing with the Straight and Flush. In Aces and Faces, they pay the standard 4-for-1 and 5-for-1. In All American, they both pay a whopping 8-for-1.

*   **Aces and Faces Strategy:** You never break a made Flush or Straight to chase a 4-card Royal Flush. You always prioritize a High Pair (Jacks or Better) over a 4-card Flush or Straight draw.
*   **All American Strategy:** Because the Straight and Flush pay 8-for-1, you will often choose to hold a 4-card Flush over a High Pair (Jacks-Aces). This is a classic example of **Payout Weighting**— the 8-for-1 payout is so valuable that it overrides the "safe" pair hold.

## Strategy Synergy: Where the Games Align

Despite the different payout structures, there is some **Strategy Synergy** in the "Base Game" logic:

1.  **Aces are Key:** In both games, 4 Aces pay a bonus (80-for-1 in Aces and Faces; usually 35-for-1 or 40-for-1 in All American). You always hold a pair of Aces.
2.  **Royal Flush Priority:** In both games, a 4-card Royal Flush is the top-tier hold.
3.  **High Pair Stability:** Both games pay 1-for-1 for a pair of Jacks or better, providing a stable floor for your bankroll.

## Variance Inversion: Bonus Quads vs. Frequent Draw Wins

The most jarring difference for players is the **Variance Inversion**.

*   **Aces and Faces:** The variance is concentrated in the "Quads." You are waiting for 4 Aces (80-for-1) or 4 face cards (40-for-1). This creates a "stair-step" volatility.
*   **All American:** The volatility is even higher (~30.0). Because you are chasing 8-for-1 Straights and Flushes, you will experience longer losing streaks between "big" wins. However, the frequent 8-for-1 payouts can quickly recover your bankroll.

## Conclusion: Which to Choose?

If you prefer a mathematically "pure" game with a predictable strategy focused on holding pairs, **Aces and Faces** is an excellent choice.

However, if you are a professional player looking for a potential edge and don't mind the high volatility of chasing 8-for-1 draws, **All American** offers one of the best returns in the casino. For a complete **video poker house edge analysis**, always check the pay table: an 8/5 Aces and Faces machine will be more stable, but a Full Pay All American machine is the holy grail for professional video poker players.
