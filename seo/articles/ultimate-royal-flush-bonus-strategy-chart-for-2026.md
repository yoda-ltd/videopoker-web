## Analysis: 3 Most Common "Pro-Level" Errors in Royal Flush Bonus

### 1. Misvaluing the Royal Flush Bonus Equity
In Royal Flush Bonus, the most important factor is the Royal Flush, which can award massive multipliers or fixed credit prizes. This changes the **Expected Return Differential** for Royal Flush draws.

**The Error:** Playing too conservatively and avoiding aggressive draws for Royal Flush.
**The Pro Reality:** Because the Royal Flush triggers a massive bonus, you should prioritize drawing for it even more heavily than in standard Jacks or Better. **Rank-Based Priority** in 2026 dictates that you should prioritize 4 to a Royal and 3 to a Royal even if it means sacrificing a small "made" hand like a straight or flush. The cumulative equity of the Royal Flush trigger makes these "long shots" more mathematically sound.

### 2. Overvaluing the "Made" Low Hand
Casual players often "lock in" small wins like a Low Pair, fearing they will lose the hand entirely.

**The Error:** Keeping a pair of 2s when you have a high-card Ace.
**The Pro Reality:** In Royal Flush Bonus, the goal is to hit the Royal Flush. The **Expected Return Differential** between a low pair and a high-card draw (which has a path to the Royal) often favors the high card. Strategy fine-tuning dictates prioritizing the high-card equity.

### 3. Ignoring the 3 to a Royal Flush Draw
The Royal Flush is the ultimate goal in RFB, and identifying 3-card Royal draws is crucial.

**The Error:** Discarding a three-card Royal Flush draw in favor of a single high card.
**The Pro Reality:** In RFB, the **Expected Return Differential** between a single high card and a three-card Royal Flush draw is significant. Pro players use **Strategy Fine-Tuning** to recognize the long-term equity of the Royal Flush draw outweighs the short-term safety of a high card.
