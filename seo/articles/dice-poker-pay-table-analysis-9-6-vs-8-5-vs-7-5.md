# Dice Poker Pay Table Analysis: 9/6 vs 8/5 vs 7/5

In Dice Poker, the pay table is the absolute blueprint of your expected return. While the game replaces traditional cards with virtual dice, the difference of a few coins in the payout for common combinations can shift the game from a fair experience to a high-house-edge trap. This analysis explores the three most common pay tables and the framework of **video poker fairness and regulation** that ensures these payouts are honest.

## The Full Pay Standard: 9/6 Dice Poker

A "Full Pay" machine is the gold standard for players who enjoy geometric patterns. The "9/6" refers to the payouts for a Full House (9-for-1) and a Straight (6-for-1). 

*   **Optimal RTP:** **98.65%**
*   **House Edge:** **1.35%**
*   **Analysis:** This table provides one of the best returns for a non-card variant. In a regulated environment, **Return to Player (RTP) Verification** ensures that the machine provides this exact return over millions of simulated rolls.

## The Common Compromise: 8/5 Dice Poker

Casinos often "Short-Pay" their machines to increase the house edge, frequently by reducing the Full House and Straight payouts by just one unit each.

*   **Optimal RTP:** **97.10%**
*   **House Edge:** **2.90%**
*   **Analysis:** While it still looks like a fair game, the 8/5 table increases the house edge by more than 100%. Over a long session, this "one-unit" difference will significantly erode your bankroll. Regulators like the **NGCB** require these tables to be clearly displayed.

## The High-House-Edge Trap: 7/5 Dice Poker

Found primarily in high-traffic or non-gaming-focused venues, the 7/5 table is a mathematical trap for the player.

*   **Optimal RTP:** **96.05%**
*   **House Edge:** **3.95%**
*   **Analysis:** At 96.0%, Dice Poker becomes less profitable than standard Jacks or Better. Professional players avoid 7/5 machines entirely, as the mathematical simplicity of the dice model no longer offers a competitive advantage.

## Technical Standards and Integrity

A professional player’s trust in these pay tables is backed by a robust regulatory framework:
- **Lab Audits:** Firms like **GLI** perform exhaustive tests to ensure the machine pays out exactly what is listed on the screen, and that the virtual dice are not "weighted."
- **RNG Certification:** The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures that each of the five dice has a perfectly random 1-in-6 chance for every face, regardless of whether you are playing on a 9/6 or a 7/5 machine.
- **Jurisdictional Mandates:** Laws that require casinos to display the payout for every combination, ensuring that "Short-Pay" machines cannot be hidden from the player.

## Summary: The Cost of a Unit

| Combination | 9/6 (Full Pay) | 8/5 (Short) | 7/5 (Avoid) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Full House | 9 | 8 | 7 |
| Straight | 6 | 5 | 5 |
| **RTP** | **98.65%** | **97.10%** | **96.05%** |

## Conclusion
The pay table is the only part of video poker that you can control before the dice are rolled. By choosing only 9/6 machines and playing on platforms with a **trusted video poker RNG certification**, you can focus on the dot patterns, knowing that the math is protected by the highest international standards. Remember: a single unit in the pay table is the difference between a fair experience and a casino trap.
