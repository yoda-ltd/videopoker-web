# The Payout Gap: Jacks or Better vs Bonus Poker Explained

In the world of **best odds video poker**, players often find themselves choosing between two of the most popular machines on the floor: Jacks or Better and Bonus Poker. While they look similar, the **EV Gap** between them depends entirely on their specific pay tables and your tolerance for **House Edge Variance**.

## The Best Odds Index: 9/6 Jacks vs 8/5 Bonus
In the **highest return video poker games** 2026 ranking, both machines offer strong professional returns when found in their "Full Pay" configurations:
*   **9/6 Jacks or Better:** 99.54% RTP.
*   **8/5 Bonus Poker:** 99.17% RTP.

At first glance, Jacks or Better appears to be the superior choice. However, Bonus Poker offers a "Bonus" for hitting Four of a Kind (specifically Four Aces, which pays 80-for-1 instead of 25-for-1). This change in the pay table structure shifts the **Theoretical Return Convergence**.

## Analysis of "Short Pay" Predation
The real "Payout Gap" occurs when casinos offer sub-optimal versions of these games. 
*   **Jacks or Better (8/5):** RTP falls to 97.30%.
*   **Bonus Poker (6/5):** RTP falls to 96.87%.

If you find a 9/6 Jacks machine next to a 6/5 Bonus machine, the 2.67% **EV Gap** is predatory. A professional player would never touch the 6/5 Bonus machine in this scenario. However, if the choice is between 8/5 Jacks (97.30%) and 8/5 Bonus (99.17%), the Bonus Poker machine is actually the much better bet.

## House Edge Variance and Strategy
Bonus Poker has higher variance than Jacks or Better. This is because more of the return is tied up in the Four of a Kind payouts. In Jacks or Better, the Two Pair payout (2-for-1) is the engine that keeps your bankroll alive. 
*   **Jacks or Better Strategy:** Focuses on bankroll preservation and hitting the Royal Flush.
*   **Bonus Poker Strategy:** Slightly adjusts to account for the higher value of Four of a Kind, particularly Aces.
