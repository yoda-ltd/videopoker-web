## Volatility Buffering: Surviving the TDB "Dry Spells"
In Triple Double Bonus, the pay table is "top-heavy." To fund the massive payouts for Four Aces with a 2, 3, or 4 Kicker (800 for 1), the game often pays nothing for Two Pair. This creates a "feast or famine" cycle.

For the professional whale, **Volatility Buffering** isn't just a concept; it's a survival tactic. When playing at a $100 denomination ($500 per hand), you can easily lose $10,000 in minutes without hitting a significant hand. A dedicated bankroll of at least $250,000 is recommended for sustained play at these levels to ensure you are still at the machine when the "Aces with Kicker" finally land.

## The $1 to $100 Denomination Leap
Moving from $1 play to the high-limit room requires a shift in how you value your hands. At a $100 **Credit Denomination**, a "mini-jackpot" of Four 5s through Kings pays $2,500. This is an **Attendant-Paid Jackpot**, requiring the machine to lock for a hand-pay.

The strategy at these stakes is "Kicker-Centric." Unlike Jacks or Better, where you would never break a Full House, in High Limit TDB, if that Full House consists of three Aces and two 2s, the professional whale *breaks* the Full House to go for the fourth Ace and the Kicker. The math at high stakes dictates that the $40,000 potential payout (800:1) outweighs the guaranteed small win.
