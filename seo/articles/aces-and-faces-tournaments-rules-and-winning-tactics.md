# Aces and Faces Tournaments: Rules and Winning Tactics
Category: Tournament Strategy

Aces and Faces is a high-octane video poker variant that is tailor-made for tournament play. Because it pays out significant premiums for Four Aces and Four of a Kind (Kings, Queens, and Jacks), it creates intense **Leaderboard Volatility**. To win an Aces and Faces tournament, you must move beyond "Standard Strategy" and embrace a high-speed **Sprint Logic** that prioritizes these high-value "Face" quads.

## HPH Optimization in Aces and Faces Sprints

In a timed 15-minute Aces and Faces tournament, your **HPH (Hands Per Hour)** is your primary competitive advantage. Because the paytable is so focused on high-ranking quads, your goal is to cycle through as many deals as possible to find those Aces and Faces.

### Volume Over Precision: The Tournament Realities
In most Aces and Faces tournaments, the difference between a podium finish and a mid-pack result is a single hit of "Four Aces."

*   **Speed-Play Index:** Aim for 850+ hands per hour. This requires an "Instant Hold" reflex for any Ace, King, Queen, or Jack.
*   **Strategic Misplays for Speed:** If you are dealt a hand with no Aces, Faces, or 3-card Royal draws, the correct "Video Poker Tournament Secret" is to discard all five cards instantly. Agonizing over a "Low Pair" (2s through 10s) is a waste of 0.7 seconds that could be spent on a fresh deal with an Ace.

## Strategy Inversion: Hunting the High-Value Quads

The most advanced **how to win video poker tournaments** tactic for Aces and Faces is "Strategy Inversion." This involves intentionally making "Strategic Misplays" to chase the premium 80-to-1 (Aces) and 40-to-1 (Faces) payouts.

### When to Break Winning Hands for Maximum Points
In an Aces and Faces tournament, a "Flush" or a "Straight" is often a "low-yield" hand. To win, you must be willing to break them:

1.  **Breaking a Flush for Triple Aces:** If you are dealt a Flush that contains three Aces (e.g., AAA-8-5 of Hearts), you **must** discard the 8-5 to chase the Four Aces (80-to-1). In a tournament, the 25 points for a Flush won't win you the prize; the 400 points for Four Aces will.
2.  **The "Face Card" Priority:** If you are dealt a "Low Pair" (e.g., Pair of 7s) and a lone King, you might choose to hold only the King. While this is a "Mathematical Error" in cash play, in a tournament, the "Vertical Mobility" of hitting Four Kings (40-to-1) is worth the loss of "Horizontal Stability" from the 7s.

## Navigating Leaderboard Volatility in Aces and Faces

Aces and Faces tournaments have a unique rhythm. The **Leaderboard Volatility** spikes whenever someone hits a "Four Aces" bonus.

*   **The "Hanging" Strategy:** If you have already hit a "Four Aces" early in the heat, do not slow down. The **Leaderboard Volatility** means another player can hit back-to-back "Four Kings" and pass you. Maintain your **HPH** at maximum until the buzzer sounds.
*   **The "Desperation" Phase:** In the final three minutes, if you are not in the top 10, you must switch to an "Ultra-Aggressive" mode. Discard everything that isn't an Ace, a Face card, or a 3-card Royal draw. You are "Hunting Quads" or nothing.

## Conclusion: The Aces and Faces Tournament Secrets

Winning an Aces and Faces tournament requires a unique blend of lightning speed and fearless aggression. By optimizing your **HPH**, utilizing **Strategy Inversion** on "low-yield" winning hands, and embracing the extreme **Leaderboard Volatility** of the premium quads, you give yourself the best chance of taking the top prize. Remember: in the high-stakes world of Aces and Faces, the player who finds the most Faces and plays them the fastest always has the edge.
