# Low-Stakes All American: Extending Your Playtime

You don't need a massive bankroll to enjoy the high-variance excitement of All American Video Poker. While the game is famous for its "big swings," it is perfectly possible to play a long, entertaining session on a modest budget—provided you know how to manage your stakes and choose the right machines.

This guide is for the "Recreational Player" who wants to maximize their "Time on Device" (TOD) while still having a shot at a 200:1 Straight Flush.

## 1. Choose the Right Denomination

The first rule of low-stakes play is to choose a denomination that fits your budget.
*   **The $0.05 (Nickel) Machine:** At 5 coins, each hand costs $0.25. A $50 bankroll gives you 200 hands—enough for a fun hour of play even on a cold streak.
*   **The $0.01 (Penny) Machine:** Rare in physical casinos but common online. At 5 coins, each hand costs only $0.05. A $20 bankroll can last an entire afternoon!

**Important:** Never play a higher denomination (like $0.25) if you can't afford to play 5 coins. Playing 1 coin at $0.25 is mathematically much worse than playing 5 coins at $0.05.

## 2. Seek Out "Short-Pay" Tables for Lower Volatility? (The Controversy)

Wait, isn't "Full Pay" always better? 
*   **The Logic:** A Full Pay All American (8/8/8) is high variance because it pays so much for rare hands. 
*   **The Reality:** While "Full Pay" is always better for your wallet in the long run, some lower-paying tables (like 8/5) actually feel less "punishing" because the casino might adjust other parts of the game (or offer them at lower denominations). 
*   **The Advice:** Always aim for the highest pay table you can find, but if you're playing for pennies, don't stress if you can't find an 8/8/8 machine. Focus on the fun!

## 3. Play at a Slower Pace

In Video Poker, the "House Edge" (or player edge) is realized over thousands of hands. The faster you play, the faster you lose (or win).
*   **The Tip:** Take your time. Enjoy a drink, look at the screen, and think about your holds. Playing 300 hands per hour instead of 800 hands per hour will double your playtime for the same amount of money.
