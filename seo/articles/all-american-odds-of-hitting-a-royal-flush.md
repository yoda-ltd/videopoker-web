# All American Video Poker: Odds of Hitting a Royal Flush

All American Video Poker is a distinctive variant that stands out due to its increased payouts for Straights, Flushes, and Straight Flushes. While these enhanced mid-tier payouts provide plenty of excitement, the Royal Flush remains the pinnacle of the game, offering the standard massive 800-to-1 payout (when playing max coins).

Understanding the mathematical probability and the odds of hitting a Royal Flush in All American is essential for any player looking to master the game's unique strategy.

## The Mathematical Probability of a Royal Flush

In a standard 52-card deck used for All American, there are exactly **2,598,960** possible five-card combinations. Out of these, only **4** combinations result in a Royal Flush (one for each suit: Hearts, Diamonds, Clubs, and Spades).

### Odds on the Initial Deal
On the initial deal, the probability of being dealt a Royal Flush is:
*   **4 / 2,598,960 = 1 in 649,740**

While catching a Royal Flush on the deal is a rare event, the majority of Royal Flushes are achieved after the draw through careful decision-making.

## Odds of Hitting a Royal Flush After the Draw

The strategy in All American is famously "aggressive" when it comes to chasing high-value hands. Your odds change significantly based on how many cards you hold:

1.  **Holding 4 Cards to a Royal Flush:**
    If you are one card away, there is 1 remaining card in the deck of 47 that completes your hand.
    *   **Odds: 1 in 47 (approx. 2.13%)**

2.  **Holding 3 Cards to a Royal Flush:**
    You need to catch 2 specific cards out of the 47 remaining.
    *   **Odds: 1 in 1,081 (approx. 0.09%)**

3.  **Holding 2 Cards to a Royal Flush:**
    You need to catch 3 specific cards out of the 47 remaining.
    *   **Odds: 1 in 16,215 (approx. 0.006%)**

## Average Frequency in a Full Game Cycle

In a standard "Full Pay" All American game (paying 8 for a Straight, 8 for a Flush, and 8 for a Full House), if you play with a mathematically perfect strategy, you can expect to hit a Royal Flush approximately once every **40,000 hands**.

Interestingly, because All American strategy often favors chasing Flushes and Straights, you may find yourself holding Royal Flush components more frequently than in a game like Jacks or Better.

| Game Phase | Odds (Approximate) |
| :--- | :--- |
| Initial Deal | 1 in 649,740 |
| Holding 4 to a Royal | 1 in 47 |
| Perfect Strategy Average | 1 in 40,000 |

## Strategy: To Chase or Not to Chase?

All American strategy differs from standard video poker because Two Pair only pays 1-to-1 (the same as Jacks or Better). This shift in the pay table forces players to prioritize Royal Flush draws even more aggressively.

*   **Never Break a Dealt Royal Flush:** Always take the maximum payout.
*   **Hold 4 to a Royal over a Made Flush or Straight:** Even though Flushes and Straights pay more in this game (8-to-1), the 4,000-coin Royal Flush jackpot still dictates that you should break a made hand to chase the Royal.
*   **Aggressive 3-to-a-Royal:** In many situations where you might hold a high pair in Jacks or Better, All American strategy might suggest holding 3 to a Royal Flush instead, due to the high value of the Straight and Flush draws you might hit along the way.

## Impact on Return to Player (RTP)

The Royal Flush accounts for approximately **2% of the total Return to Player (RTP)** in All American. On a Full Pay machine with an RTP of 100.72%, the Royal Flush is the component that puts the player "over the top" into a positive expectation scenario. Without hitting the Royal Flush, your expected return drops to around 98.72%.

## Conclusion

Hitting a Royal Flush in All American is the ultimate achievement. Because the game rewards Straights and Flushes so handsomely, the journey toward a Royal Flush is often filled with these high-paying "consolation prizes." By following an aggressive strategy and always playing max coins, you maximize your chances of seeing those five royal cards line up for a 4,000-coin win.
