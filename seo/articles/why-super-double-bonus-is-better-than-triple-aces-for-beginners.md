# Why Super Double Bonus is Better Than Triple Aces for Beginners

In the world of "Ace-Bonus" video poker, the choice between "Super Double Bonus" (SDB) and "Triple Aces" (TA) is a study in **Strategic Convergence** and the **Variance Index**. This **best video poker strategy comparison** breaks down why Super Double Bonus is the superior entry point for novices, focusing on **Payout Compression** and the "Strategy Drift" caused by the different bonus structures.

## Strategic Convergence: The "Linear" Advantage

**Super Double Bonus Poker** is a "Bonus" game. The strategy is almost identical to Jacks or Better, with adjustments for the "Heavy" payouts on 4-of-a-Kind. Because it follows the "Standard" bonus mechanic, the strategy is "Linear" and "Logical." Beginners can learn the basic "Hierarchy" of hands and apply it with high accuracy. The **video poker house edge analysis** is straightforward and predictable.

**Triple Aces** focuses "Exclusively" on Aces. This creates "Strategy Bifurcation." For every hand, you must now ask: "Is this an Ace-heavy hand?" The presence of the "Triple Ace" bonus fundamentally changes the "Value" of Aces relative to every other card in a way that is "Unique" to this game. For a beginner, this is "Cognitive Overload." You can easily make a mistake by "Chasing Aces" when the math suggests a different path. This "Strategy Drift" can cost you 1-2% in "Theoretical Return" over a session.

## Payout Compression: The "Smooth" vs. The "Ace-Specific"

In **Super Double Bonus**, the "Cost" of the massive payouts for 4-of-a-Kind is built into the Two-Pair and Full House payouts. However, the game remains "Smooth" because the frequency of 4-of-a-Kind is relatively high. Your bankroll "Churns" nicely. You are "Rewarded" for hitting common hands with a "Fair" payout.

In **Triple Aces**, the "Cost" of the "Ace" bonus is built into the *entire* payout table. To "Pay" for these rare "Triple Ace" wins, the payouts for *all* other hands are "Compressed." You often get only "Money Back" on hands that would pay more in a standard game. For a beginner, this is "Psychological Torture." You hit a "Winning" hand (like a Flush or Straight), but your bankroll doesn't grow. This "Bleed" is much more aggressive than in Super Double Bonus.

## The Variance Index: Sustainability vs. Volatility

Our **video poker house edge analysis** shows that:
*   **Super Double Bonus** has a variance of around **30**. It is a "Medium-Volatility" game. It offers "Stability" and "Longevity."
*   **Triple Aces** has a variance of around **80-100**. It is an "Extreme-Volatility" game. The "Gaps" between "Ace" hits are much longer.

For the new player who wants to enjoy a long session and learn the fundamentals with a "Linear" structure, Super Double Bonus is the clear winner. It provides the "Stability" of a standard bonus game with the "Excitement" of well-balanced payouts. Leave the "Ace-Only" hunting to the pros who can handle the "Complexity" and the "Swings."
