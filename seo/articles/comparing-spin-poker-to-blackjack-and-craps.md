# Comparing Spin Poker to Blackjack and Craps

Spin Poker is a high-velocity video poker variant that combines the draw poker experience with a multi-line layout (9 or 20 paylines). But how does this high-speed game compare to the classic table games?

### RTP and the Multi-Line Action
- **Spin Poker**: The **Theoretical Return** is identical to the base game (like Jacks or Better or Deuces Wild) that you are playing. If the base game is 99.54%, the Spin Poker variant will be too.
- **Blackjack**: Offers a 99.5% RTP (0.5% house edge) with perfect basic strategy.
- **Craps (Pass Line)**: Offers a 98.59% RTP (1.41% house edge).

While Blackjack still holds a slight edge on base RTP, Spin Poker provides a much higher level of excitement and the chance for multiple wins on any hand.

### Variance and Sustainability
The multi-line layout increases the **Variance** of the game significantly. When you play 20 lines at once, you are betting 100 coins per "spin." This dramatically increases the need for **Variance Buffering**. To achieve **Session Sustainability**, you need a much larger bankroll for Spin Poker than you would for a standard game of Blackjack. Without the extra bankroll, the cost of the 100 coins will drain your funds.

### "Is Max Bet Always Better for Bankroll Longevity?"
In Spin Poker, the answer is an absolute yes. To get the Royal Flush bonus and the best RTP, you must bet 5 coins *per line*. If you are playing 20 lines, that's a 100-coin bet. For those looking for **video poker tips for beginners**, our advice is to only play Spin Poker if you can afford the 100-coin bet. Without it, you're better off playing Craps.

### Skill and Multi-Line Strategy
Unlike Blackjack, where you must make the right decision on every hand, Spin Poker only requires you to make one set of holds for all lines. This makes it easier to play but more expensive. The **Hit Frequency Optimization** remains the same as for a single-hand game, but the results are amplified.

### RNG vs. Physical Odds
While Craps relies on physical dice and Blackjack on a physical or shuffled deck, Spin Poker uses **Pseudo-Randomness** through an RNG. This ensures that every hand is fair and independent, just like the roll of the dice in a certified game of Craps.

If you want to know **how to win video poker daily**, understanding these comparisons is vital. Spin Poker offers a great balance of risk and reward for those who enjoy high-velocity action.
