# Why Bonus Poker is Better than Aces and Faces for Beginners
Category: Comparison

For players new to the world of high-payout video poker, choosing the right starting point is a matter of **Strategic Divergence**. This guide explains why **Bonus Poker** is often the superior choice for beginners over the quad-focused **Aces and Faces**.

### The Comparison Matrix: Bonus Poker vs. Aces and Faces

This **best video poker strategy comparison** highlights why beginners should prioritize low-rank bonuses.

| Feature | Bonus Poker (8/5) | Aces and Faces (8/5) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.17% | 99.26% |
| **Variance Index** | 20.9 | 22.1 |
| **Primary Focus** | Targeted Bonuses (Aces/2s-4s) | Face Card Quad Bonuses |
| **Learning Curve** | Gentle | Gentle |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.83% | 0.74% |

### The Focus Gap: Targeted vs. Broad Bonuses

The single biggest reason **Bonus Poker** is better for beginners is its focus on low-rank quad bonuses. For a new player, achieving Four Aces or Four 2s-4s is an exciting and easily understandable goal. In **Aces and Faces**, the bonuses are extended to all face cards (Kings, Queens, Jacks), which leads to a slightly more complex focus on rank-based holds for **EV Maximization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is high strategy overlap (approximately 95%) between Jacks or Better and Bonus Poker. For a beginner, this means their existing knowledge is almost entirely transferable. In Aces and Faces, the focus on face cards requires a slight shift in hold priorities. This is the essence of **Volatility Normalization** for each game.

### Why Beginners Win with Bonus Poker
1.  **Simple Hold Strategy:** You only need to look for specific low-rank quads for the bonus.
2.  **Stable Experience:** The lower variance (20.9) ensures your money lasts much longer.
3.  **Transferable Skills:** What you learn in Bonus Poker applies directly to almost all other natural high-pair variants.

While Aces and Faces offers a slightly higher theoretical return, the intuitive and stable nature of Bonus Poker makes it the more enjoyable choice for those just starting their video poker journey.

# Why Bonus Poker is Better than Double Bonus for Beginners
Category: Comparison

Moving from a moderate-bonus game to a high-bonus game is a major **Strategic Divergence**. For beginners, **Bonus Poker** offers a much more straightforward path to mastery than the high-volatility **Double Bonus**.

### The Comparison Matrix: Bonus Poker vs. Double Bonus

| Feature | Bonus Poker (8/5) | Double Bonus (10/7) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.17% | 100.17% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 20.9 | 28.5 |
| **Primary Focus** | Moderate Quad Bonuses | High-Payout Quad Bonuses |
| **Strategy Type** | Natural/Pair | Natural/Targeted |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.83% | -0.17% (Player Edge) |

### The Stability Gap: Moderate vs. High Bonuses

The biggest hurdle for beginners in **Double Bonus** is the aggressive rank-based holds. You must often break Full Houses to chase a fourth Ace. For a new player, this feels "wrong." In **Bonus Poker**, you play a standard game where you rarely break pat winners, and the quad bonuses are a welcome supplement. This leads to a much healthier **Volatility Normalization** (20.9 vs 28.5).

**Strategy Overlap:** Bonus Poker shares nearly all its DNA with standard Jacks or Better. A beginner can pick it up in minutes. Double Bonus requires a total unlearning of standard poker logic for specific hands. This extra layer of complexity often leads to strategy errors for new players.

### Why Beginners Win with Bonus Poker
1.  **Bankroll Protection:** The low variance ensures your money lasts much longer.
2.  **Intuitive Holds:** Keeping high pairs and pat winners is the foundation of the strategy.
3.  **Low Stress:** You don't have to worry about the "all-or-nothing" nature of high-payout quad hunting.

Through a detailed **video poker house edge analysis**, it's clear that while Double Bonus offers a better theoretical return, Bonus Poker is the more stable and intuitive choice for those just starting their video poker journey.

# Why Bonus Poker is Better than Double Double Bonus for Beginners
Category: Comparison

Transitioning to a kicker-based variant like **Double Double Bonus** is an extreme leap in complexity that most beginners are not prepared for. For those new to the game, **Bonus Poker** provides a much better introduction to high-action video poker.

### The Comparison Matrix: Bonus Poker vs. Double Double Bonus

| Feature | Bonus Poker (8/5) | Double Double Bonus (9/6) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.17% | 98.98% |
| **Variance Index** | 20.9 | 42.0 |
| **Primary Focus** | Moderate Quad Bonuses | Kicker-Based Quad Jackpots |
| **Risk Level** | Low/Moderate | High/Extreme |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.83% | 1.02% |

### The Kicker Gap: Natural vs. Kicker Complexity

In **Double Double Bonus**, the payout for Aces, 2s, 3s, and 4s depends on the fifth card in the hand (the "Kicker"). For a beginner, managing the kicker potential while also tracking natural quads is a complex mental task. In **Bonus Poker**, every card is natural, and you are simply looking for ANY quad in your target range. This leads to a much lower learning curve for **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 70% strategy overlap between these games. However, Double Double Bonus's unique mechanics require a level of attention and math that beginners often find overwhelming. In Bonus Poker, your existing knowledge of natural cards is your greatest asset for **EV Maximization**.

### Why Beginners Win with Bonus Poker
1.  **Pure Poker Logic:** You don't have to worry about kicker multipliers.
2.  **Predictable Results:** Without a kicker feature, the distribution of hands feels more "Honest."
3.  **Bankroll Safety:** The low variance of 20.9 is far more manageable than the 42.0 of Double Double Bonus.

A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that while Double Double Bonus is the most popular game, most beginners will find Bonus Poker to be the smarter, more rewarding path to **Volatility Normalization**.
