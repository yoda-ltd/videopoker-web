# Good Times Pay vs. Royal Aces: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Comparing **Good Times Pay** and **Royal Aces** is a comparison of two ways to reward the player for high-value hands. While Good Times Pay uses a random multiplier to boost your wins, Royal Aces uses a specialized payout structure that places a massive premium on hitting Four Aces. To choose the right game, you must understand how **video poker fairness and regulation** ensures the integrity of these "Bonus-Heavy" models.

## The Mathematical Framework: Multipliers vs. High-Rank Payouts

The house edge in these games is determined by where the value is "Stashed."

*   **Good Times Pay:** The value is in the random multiplier (up to 10x). This feature can boost any winning hand, from a Pair of Jacks to a Royal Flush. The edge is tied to the frequency of these multiplier triggers.
*   **Royal Aces:** The value is concentrated in the Four Aces payout (often paying as much as a Royal Flush). The edge is tied to the player's ability to prioritize Aces and the RNG's ability to deliver them fairly.

## House Edge Analysis: The Accuracy of the Math

The accuracy of the advertised edge in these variants is guaranteed by **Return to Player (RTP) Verification** during independent **Lab Audits**.

| Metric | Typical Good Times Pay | Typical Royal Aces |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

**The Verdict:** **Royal Aces** generally offers a better house edge for skilled players. The concentration of value in the Aces allows for higher strategic influence than the random multipliers of Good Times Pay.

## 1. Technical Standards for Fairness

Both variants must adhere to strict **Technical Standards** enforced by regulators like the **NGCB** or **UKGC**.

*   **Good Times Pay:** The **Random Number Generator (RNG)** must provide a fair distribution of multipliers. Firms like **GLI** audit the code to ensure the 10x multiplier isn't "blocked" when a high-value hand appears.
*   **Royal Aces:** Regulators verify that every Ace has an exactly 1-in-52 chance of being dealt. The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures that the Aces aren't "weighted" to appear less frequently than other ranks.

## 2. Payout Analysis: The "Bonus" Funding

In these games, you are effectively paying a tax to access the big bonuses.
- In Good Times Pay, the base pay table for common wins (like Full Houses) is lower to fund the random multipliers.
- In Royal Aces, the payouts for mid-tier hands (like 2s through Kings) may be lower to fund the astronomical Four Aces bonus.

## 3. Regulatory Oversight and Integrity

A professional player’s trust in these bonus-heavy variants is backed by a robust regulatory framework:

## Conclusion: Which Edge is Better for You?

For the player who wants the lower house edge and enjoys the challenge of "Ace-Hunting," **Royal Aces** is the superior choice. Its 99%+ return makes it a professional-grade variant for those who can handle the extreme volatility.

However, if you enjoy the excitement of a high-multiplier surprise on any hand, **Good Times Pay** offers a fair and rewarding experience.
