# The Payout Gap: Sevens Wild vs. White Hot Aces Explained

The "Payout Gap" between Sevens Wild and White Hot Aces (WHA) represents the ultimate choice for video poker players: Wild Card volatility versus "Bonus" Quad stability. This **best video poker strategy comparison** breaks down why these two games, despite appearing vastly different, are often compared in terms of their **video poker house edge analysis** and bankroll requirements.

## Payout Compression in the Wild vs. The Bonus

Sevens Wild uses **Payout Compression** on common hands (like Three-of-a-Kind) to fund the four-of-a-kind "Sevens" jackpot. In contrast, White Hot Aces is a "Bonus" game that keeps the standard 52-card deck but significantly inflates the payouts for four Aces, Threes, Twos, and Fours.

The **EV Gap** here is found in the "Natural" vs. "Wild" frequency. In White Hot Aces, you are hunting for rare "White Hot" Quads (Aces) which pay a massive premium. In Sevens Wild, you are using the Sevens to *build* Quads. While Sevens Wild feels like you are winning more often, the actual *value* of those wins is often lower than a single big hit in White Hot Aces.

## Variance Scaling and Bankroll Risk

*   **Sevens Wild:** The variance can be surprisingly high (35-45) depending on the pay table. The game relies heavily on hitting "Four Sevens" to achieve its theoretical return.
*   **White Hot Aces:** This is a high-variance "Jacks" variant (variance often 40+). It is "White Hot" for a reason—the payout for 4 Aces is massive (often 1,200 or 1,600 coins), but the "Two Pair" payout is often reduced to 1-to-1 to pay for it.

For a beginner, the **Variance Scaling** in WHA can be demoralizing. Losing your original bet on Two Pair hands is a "slow bleed" that requires a disciplined bankroll.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Ace" Factor

The **Strategy Bifurcation** is most evident in how you handle Aces. In White Hot Aces, an Ace is a treasure; you hold it with high priority. In Sevens Wild, an Ace is just another card—unless you have a Seven to pair it with. The transition between these games requires a mental "reset" of card values. Players who try to play WHA with a "Wild" mindset (or vice versa) will quickly see their ROI vanish.

If you prefer the "grind" of building hands with wild cards, Sevens Wild is your game. If you prefer the "strike" of a massive natural payout, White Hot Aces is the superior choice.
