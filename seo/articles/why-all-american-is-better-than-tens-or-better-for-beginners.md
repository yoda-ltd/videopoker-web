# Why All-American is Better Than Tens or Better for Beginners

In the world of "Entry-Level" video poker, the choice between "All-American" (AA) and "Tens or Better" (ToB) is a study in **Strategic Convergence** and the **Variance Index**. This **best video poker strategy comparison** breaks down why All-American is the superior entry point for novices, focusing on **Payout Compression** and the "Strategy Drift" caused by the different minimum win and bonus structures.

## Strategic Convergence: The "Standard-Strategy" Advantage

**All-American Poker** is a "Standard-Draw" game with "Heavy" payouts on Flushes, Straights, and Straight Flushes. The strategy is almost identical to Jacks or Better, with adjustments for the "Hierarchy" of these specific hands. Because it follows the "Standard" draw mechanic, the strategy is "Linear" and "Logical." Beginners can learn the basic "Hierarchy" of hands and apply it with high accuracy. The **video poker house edge analysis** is straightforward and predictable.

**Tens or Better** is a "Standard-Draw" game with a lower minimum win requirement (Tens). While this seems "Easier," it creates "Strategy Bifurcation." For every hand, you must now ask: "Is the 'Tens' win worth the lower payouts on other hands?" The strategy is "Unique" and often "Counter-Intuitive" compared to Jacks or Better. For a beginner, this is "Cognitive Overload." You can easily make a mistake by holding "Tens" when the math suggests a different path. This "Strategy Drift" can cost you 1-2% in "Theoretical Return" over a session.

## Payout Compression: The "Smooth" vs. The "Low-Win"

In **All-American**, the "Cost" of the massive payouts for Flushes and Straights is built into the Two-Pair and Full House payouts. However, the game remains "Smooth" because the frequency of Flushes and Straights is relatively high. Your bankroll "Churns" nicely. You are "Rewarded" for hitting common hands with an "Enhanced" payout.

In **Tens or Better**, the "Cost" of the "Lower Minimum Win" is built into the *entire* payout table (especially the Full House and Flush). To "Pay" for these frequent "Tens" wins, the payouts for *all* other hands are "Compressed." You often get only "Money Back" on hands that would pay more in a standard game. For a beginner, this is "Psychological Torture." You hit a "Winning" hand, but your bankroll doesn't grow. This "Bleed" is much more aggressive than in All-American.

## The Variance Index: Sustainability vs. Volatility

Our **video poker house edge analysis** shows that:
*   **All-American** has a variance of around **30**. It is a "Medium-Volatility" game. It offers "Stability" and "Longevity."
*   **Tens or Better** has a variance of around **12-15**. While the variance is "Very Low," the "Low Payouts" lead to a higher "Effective Loss Rate" for beginners who make mistakes or get bored.

For the new player who wants to enjoy a long session and learn the fundamentals with an "Enhanced" reward structure, All-American is the clear winner. It provides the "Stability" of a standard game with the "Excitement" of higher payouts for common hands. Leave the "Tens" hunting to the pros who can handle the "Compression" and the "Mechanics."
