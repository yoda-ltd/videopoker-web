# Pick'em Poker vs. Quick Quads: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Comparing **Pick'em Poker** and **Quick Quads** is a study in simplified choice vs. mathematical summation. While Pick'em Poker reduces the decision to a single choice between two piles, Quick Quads adds a layer of complexity by allowing you to form Four-of-a-Kind through simple addition. To choose the right game, you must understand how **video poker fairness and regulation** ensures the integrity of these specialized "logic-based" models.

## The Mathematical Framework: Selection vs. Summation

The house edge in these games is determined by the "Path to Victory."

*   **Pick'em Poker:** You are dealt two fixed cards and choose between two hidden piles. The edge is tied to the player's ability to identify which pile offers the highest Expected Value (EV).
*   **Quick Quads:** You bet 6 coins to activate the "Summation" feature. This allows you to form Four-of-a-Kind with three of a rank and two other cards that sum to that rank. The edge is tied to the player's ability to master the "Summation Math."

Both variants must adhere to strict **Technical Standards** enforced by regulators like the **NGCB** or **UKGC**.
