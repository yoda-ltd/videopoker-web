# Strategy Differences: Moving from Triple Double Bonus to Aces and Faces

Transitioning from Triple Double Bonus (TDB) to Aces and Faces (AF) is a unique step in the video poker world. Both games are popular variants that offer specific payouts for Four of a Kind. This guide provides the best video poker strategy comparison to help you understand how to play video poker like a pro during this transition.

## The Triple Double Bonus Baseline

In Triple Double Bonus, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is built on the fact that any pair of Jacks or better is a winning hand. This creates a very stable, low-volatility experience. When moving to Aces and Faces, that "safety net" is removed. You now need at least a pair of Queens or Kings to get your money back.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from Triple Double Bonus to Aces and Faces, you'll find that **80% of your strategy remains the same**. The **20% that changes** is entirely focused on the "Pair threshold." This 20% shift causes the most **Bankroll Drain** if not corrected.

### The 20% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Pair of Jacks Devaluation:** In Triple Double Bonus, a pair of Jacks is a winning hand. In Aces and Faces, it's a "trash" hand that you should rarely hold unless you're drawing for something better (like a three-card Royal Flush).
2.  **Aces and Faces Focus:** Aces and Faces is named "Aces and Faces" because it rewards you specifically for getting four Aces or four Face cards. This makes four-of-a-kind and specific straights much more valuable than in Triple Double Bonus.
3.  **Two Pair Strategy:** While Two Pair pays 2-for-1 in both games, the higher payout for specific "Aces and Faces" hands in Aces and Faces means you might break a Full House to draw for a fifth card more often than you would in Triple Double Bonus.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

Aces and Faces introduces **Volatility Scaling**. Without the Jacks-or-better push, your bankroll will experience more frequent dips. **EV Erosion** occurs when a TDB player instinctively holds a pair of Jacks in Aces and Faces. This is a "dead hand" and holding it is a direct loss of expected value.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Queens or Kings or Better are your new minimum "win."
*   **Modify:** Discard any pair of Jacks unless they are part of a better draw.
*   **Target:** Aggressively pursue the "Aces and Faces" bonuses; they are the heart of the Aces and Faces pay table.

By recognizing that the "safety net" has moved, you can successfully transition to Aces and Faces without suffering from significant EV Erosion.
