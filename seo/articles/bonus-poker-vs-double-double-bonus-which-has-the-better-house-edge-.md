# Bonus Poker vs. Double Double Bonus: House Edge and Strategy Synergy

In the landscape of "best video poker strategy comparison," **Bonus Poker vs. Double Double Bonus** is a battle of bonus quads vs. kicker-driven volatility. Both games belong to the "Bonus" category of Jacks or Better variants, and they both prioritize 4 Aces as the top payout below a Royal Flush. However, they distribute their return-to-player (RTP) through different **Payout Weighting** systems for the remaining quads and "kickers," creating a massive **Strategy Bifurcation** and altering the fundamental **Payout Weighting** of each game.

## The House Edge Analysis: Bonus Aces vs. Multiplier Volatility

To perform a professional **video poker house edge analysis**, we must examine the "Full Pay" versions of both.

| Game | Full Pay Version | Return (RTP) | House Edge |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bonus Poker** | 8/5 (99.17%) | 99.17% | 0.83% |
| **Double Double Bonus** | 9/6 (98.98%) | 98.98% | 1.02% |

While **Full Pay Bonus Poker** technically offers a lower house edge in its purest form, the **EV Gap** between the two is often closed by how you play the quads and kickers. **Double Double Bonus** is the "pure" bonus game, paying 400-for-1 for 4 Aces (with a specific kicker), but significantly less for other quads.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Quad" vs. "Kicker" Hunt

The most significant **Strategy Bifurcation** occurs in the way you prioritize 4-of-a-kind.

*   **Bonus Poker Strategy:** Because *any* 4-of-a-kind pays 25-for-1 (or more for Aces, 2s, 3s, 4s), you will aggressively hold *any* pair. If you have a choice between a 4-card Straight and a low pair (2s-10s), the **Optimal Hold** in Bonus Poker is always the pair. The goal is to maximize your chances of hitting any 4-of-a-kind.
*   **Double Double Bonus Strategy:** In Double Double Bonus, 4 Aces with a kicker (2, 3, or 4) pay a whopping 400-for-1. This means you will prioritize holding Aces over almost any other hand, including made Straights and Flushes. You will often break a made hand to chase 4 Aces with a kicker, a move that would be mathematically incorrect in almost any other video poker variant.

## Strategy Synergy: Where the Games Align

Despite the different payout structures, there is some **Strategy Synergy** in the "Base Game" logic:

1.  **Royal Flush Priority:** In both games, a 4-card Royal Flush is the top-tier hold.
2.  **Aces are Key:** In both games, 4 Aces pay the largest bonus. You always prioritize Aces.
3.  **High Pair Stability:** Both games pay 1-for-1 for a pair of high cards (Jacks or Better), providing a stable floor for your bankroll.

## Variance Inversion: Bonus Quads vs. Extreme Volatility

The most jarring difference for players is the **Variance Inversion**.

*   **Bonus Poker:** The volatility is high (~20.0), but more "balanced." Because you have multiple tiers of bonus payouts (80-for-1, 40-for-1, 25-for-1), you will hit "significant" wins more frequently than in Double Double Bonus.
*   **Double Double Bonus:** The volatility is extreme (~42.0). Because your payouts are heavily dependent on hitting 4 Aces with a kicker or 4 2s-4s with a kicker, you will experience very long losing streaks and very massive wins. This is the highest volatility game in the video poker world, making it ideal for professional players with large bankrolls.

## Conclusion: Which to Choose?

If you enjoy the simplicity of a game where *any* 4-of-a-kind is a massive win and want to minimize risk, **Bonus Poker** is an excellent choice.

However, if you are a professional player with a large bankroll and don't mind the extreme volatility of chasing 400-for-1 Aces with a kicker, **Double Double Bonus** offers one of the best returns in the casino.
