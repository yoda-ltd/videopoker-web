# Aces and Faces vs. Aces and Eights: House Edge and Strategy Synergy

In the ultimate "best video poker strategy comparison," **Aces and Faces vs. Aces and Eights** is a battle of bonus quads. Both games belong to the "Bonus" category of Jacks or Better variants, and they both prioritize 4 Aces as the top payout below a Royal Flush. However, they distribute their return-to-player (RTP) through different **Payout Weighting** systems for the remaining quads, creating a subtle but critical **Strategy Bifurcation** for pro players.

## The House Edge Analysis: Bonus Aces vs. Balanced Quads

To perform a professional **video poker house edge analysis**, we must examine the "Full Pay" versions of both games.

| Game | Full Pay Version | Return (RTP) | House Edge |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Aces and Faces** | 8/5 (99.26%) | 99.26% | 0.74% |
| **Aces and Eights** | 8/5 (99.78%) | 99.78% | 0.22% |

While Aces and Eights technically offers a lower house edge in its purest form, the **EV Gap** between the two is often closed by the fact that Aces and Faces pays more for 4 face cards (J, Q, K), which are more common than 4 Eights or 4 Sevens.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "High Card" vs. "Mid Card" Hold

The most significant **Strategy Bifurcation** occurs in the way you handle the "Face" cards vs. the "Eights/Sevens." 

*   **Aces and Faces Strategy:** Because 4 Jacks, Queens, or Kings pay 40-for-1 (vs. the standard 25-for-1), you will more aggressively hold pairs of face cards. If you have a choice between a 4-card Straight (with no high cards) and a single face card, the **Optimal Hold** in Aces and Faces is always the face card.
*   **Aces and Eights Strategy:** In this game, 4 Eights or 4 Sevens pay 50-for-1. This creates a unique **Hand-Ranking Inversion**. You will hold a pair of Eights or Sevens over a 4-card Straight or Flush, a move that would be mathematically incorrect in almost any other video poker variant.

## Strategy Synergy: Where the Games Align

Despite the different quad bonuses, there is massive **Strategy Synergy** in the "Base Game" logic:

1.  **Aces are King:** In both games, 4 Aces pay 80-for-1. This is your primary goal. You always hold a pair of Aces, and you never break them to chase a Flush or Straight.
2.  **Royal Flush Priority:** In both games, a 4-card Royal Flush is the top-tier hold.
3.  **Jacks or Better Foundation:** Both games pay 1-for-1 for a pair of Jacks or better, providing a stable floor for your bankroll.

## Variance Inversion: Bonus Quads vs. Full House/Flush

The most jarring difference for players is the **Variance Inversion** caused by the pay table.

*   **Aces and Faces:** The variance is spread more evenly across the high cards. Because you get a bonus for any 4-of-a-kind involving Jacks, Queens, Kings, or Aces, you hit "significant" wins more frequently.
*   **Aces and Eights:** The volatility is slightly higher because the bonus is concentrated in Aces, Eights, and Sevens. You will go for longer stretches without a bonus quad, but the Eights and Sevens payout is larger (50-for-1 vs. 40-for-1).

## Conclusion: Which to Choose?

If you prefer a game with a lower house edge and don't mind the "mid-card" focus, **Aces and Eights** is the superior choice for long-term play.

However, if you enjoy the familiarity of holding face cards and want a slightly more "frequent" bonus experience, **Aces and Faces** is an excellent alternative. For a complete **video poker house edge analysis**, always check the pay table: an 8/5 Aces and Eights machine will almost always outperform a standard Aces and Faces machine in the long run.
