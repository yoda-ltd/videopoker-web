# Common Mistakes to Avoid in Five Deck Poker

Five Deck Poker is a unique and often misunderstood variant of video poker. By using five separate decks for each hand, it opens up new possibilities—and new ways for players to make costly mistakes.

### Thinking Like a Single Deck Player
The biggest mistake in Five Deck Poker is using a standard single-deck strategy. In Five Deck Poker, the probabilities change significantly. For example, the chance of hitting a specific card is higher because there are five of them in the total pool. Failing to adjust your **Hit Frequency Optimization** for this five-deck reality will lower your **Theoretical Return**.

### Bankroll Mismanagement
Five Deck Poker often has higher payouts for unique hands, which can lead to higher **Variance**. To maintain **Session Sustainability**, you need a larger bankroll than you would for a standard game. Without proper **Variance Buffering**, the unique swings of Five Deck Poker will drain your funds before you hit a big hand.

### "Is Max Bet Always Better for Bankroll Longevity?"
Yes. Like almost all video poker variants, the Royal Flush and other top-tier payouts are boosted at the 5-coin level. For those looking for **video poker tips for beginners**, our advice is the same for Five Deck Poker as it is for any other: play max bet or don't play at all.

### Misunderstanding the RNG
With five decks, players often think the machine is more likely to "clump" cards of the same rank or suit. This is a misunderstanding of **Pseudo-Randomness**. The RNG still treats every card selection as an independent event. The "clumping" is just a result of the increased number of cards, not a change in the fairness of the game.

### Strategy Over-Complexity
Players often get overwhelmed by the five decks and start making "creative" moves. To learn **how to win video poker daily** on a Five Deck machine, you must stick to a rigorous, mathematically-derived strategy. Never guess.

Five Deck Poker is a refreshing change of pace, but it requires a disciplined approach. Avoid these common mistakes, and you'll find it to be a rewarding addition to your video poker repertoire.
