# Super Aces Bonus Pay Table Analysis: 9/6 vs 8/5 vs 7/5

In Super Aces Bonus, the pay table is the absolute blueprint of your expected return. While the game logic focuses on hitting Four Aces, the difference of a few coins in the payout for common hands can shift the game from a high-stakes professional variant to a high-house-edge trap. This analysis explores the three most common pay tables and the framework of **video poker fairness and regulation** that ensures these payouts are honest.

## The Full Pay Standard: 9/6 Super Aces

A "Full Pay" machine is the gold standard for players who enjoy single-rank specialization. The "9/6" refers to the payouts for a Full House (9-for-1) and a Flush (6-for-1). 

*   **Optimal RTP:** **99.64%**
*   **House Edge:** **0.36%**
*   **Analysis:** This table provides one of the highest returns in the casino for an "action" game. In a regulated environment, **Return to Player (RTP) Verification** ensures that the machine provides this exact return over millions of simulated hands.

## The Common Compromise: 8/5 Super Aces

Casinos often "Short-Pay" their machines to increase the house edge, frequently by reducing the Full House and Flush payouts by just one unit each.

*   **Optimal RTP:** **97.55%**
*   **House Edge:** **2.45%**
*   **Analysis:** While it looks similar to the 9/6 version, the 8/5 table increases the house edge by nearly 700%. Over a long session, this "one-unit" difference will significantly erode your bankroll. Regulators like the **NGCB** require these tables to be clearly displayed, but it is the player's responsibility to recognize the lower value.

## The High-House-Edge Trap: 7/5 Super Aces

Found primarily in high-traffic or non-gaming-focused venues, the 7/5 table is a mathematical disaster for the player.

*   **Optimal RTP:** **96.40%**
*   **House Edge:** **3.60%**
*   **Analysis:** At 96.4%, Super Aces becomes less profitable than many mid-tier slot machines. Professional players avoid 7/5 machines entirely, as the mathematical "climb" to a profit is virtually impossible given the concentration of value in the 400-to-1 Ace hit.

## Technical Standards and Integrity

A professional player’s trust in these pay tables is backed by a robust regulatory framework:
- **Lab Audits:** Firms like **GLI** perform exhaustive tests to ensure the machine pays out exactly what is listed on the screen.
- **RNG Certification:** The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures that the "shuffle" is honest, regardless of whether you are playing on a 9/6 or a 7/5 machine. The house doesn't need to "rig" the deal because the lower pay table already guarantees their profit.
- **Jurisdictional Mandates:** Laws that require casinos to display the payout for every hand, ensuring that "Short-Pay" machines cannot be hidden from the player.
