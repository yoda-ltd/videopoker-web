# Strategy Differences: Moving from Triple Double Bonus to Joker Poker

Transitioning from Triple Double Bonus (TDB) to Joker Poker is a major shift in how you play video poker. TDB is a volatile variant that rewards you for getting four Aces with a kicker. Joker Poker is a wild-card variant that relies on the "Joker" to create value. This guide provides the best video poker strategy comparison to help you understand how to play video poker like a pro during this transition.

## The Triple Double Bonus Baseline

In Triple Double Bonus, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is built on the fact that any pair of Jacks or better is a winning hand. This creates a very stable, low-volatility experience. When moving to Joker Poker, that "safety net" is removed. You now need at least a pair of Kings or better to get your money back.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from Triple Double Bonus to Joker Poker, you'll find that **only about 40% of your strategy is portable**. This is why this transition causes such massive **Bankroll Drain** for TDB experts. The **60% that changes** is what separates a professional Joker Poker player from a hobbyist.

### The 60% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Pair of Jacks/Queens Devaluation:** In Triple Double Bonus, a pair of Jacks or Queens is a winning hand. In Joker Poker, it's a "trash" hand that you should rarely hold unless you're drawing for something better (like a three-card Royal Flush).
2.  **Joker Focus:** Joker Poker is named "Joker" because it rewards you specifically for getting the Joker. This makes four-of-a-kind and specific straights much more valuable than in Triple Double Bonus.
3.  **Two Pair Strategy:** While Two Pair pays 2-for-1 in both games, the higher payout for specific "Joker" hands in Joker Poker means you might break a Full House to draw for a fifth card more often than you would in Triple Double Bonus.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

Joker Poker introduces **Volatility Scaling**. Without the Jacks-or-better push, your bankroll will experience more frequent dips. **EV Erosion** occurs when a TDB player instinctively holds a pair of Jacks or Queens in Joker Poker. This is a "dead hand" and holding it is a direct loss of expected value.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Kings or Better are your new minimum "win."
*   **Modify:** Discard any habits from TDB! You now have a Joker to help you.
*   **Target:** Aggressively pursue the "Joker" bonuses; they are the heart of the Joker Poker pay table.

By recognizing that the "safety net" has moved, you can successfully transition to Joker Poker without suffering from significant EV Erosion.
