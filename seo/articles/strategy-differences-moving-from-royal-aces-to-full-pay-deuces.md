# Strategy Differences: Moving from Royal Aces to Full Pay Deuces
Category: Comparison

Moving from **Royal Aces** to **Full Pay Deuces** is a shift from a "Targeted Bonus" natural variant to a "Multi-Wild" variant. This represents a significant **Strategic Divergence** in how you value your hands versus the wild card features.

### The Comparison Matrix: Royal Aces vs. Full Pay Deuces

| Feature | Royal Aces (Full Pay) | Full Pay Deuces (100.76%) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.60% | 100.76% |
| **Variance Index** | 32.0 | 25.8 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Aces Bonus | Target Deuce-Based Wilds |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.40% | -0.76% (Player Edge) |

### Strategic Divergence: Ace Jackpot vs. Wild Deuces

In **Royal Aces Poker**, the focus is on achieving Three or Four Aces. In **Full Pay Deuces**, the four 2s are your most powerful tools, and you are hunting for the Four Deuces jackpot. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through wild card connectivity. Full Pay Deuces' variance (25.8) is lower than Royal Aces (32.0), providing a smoother **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** Overlap is minimal (less than 10%). In Royal Aces, you would never discard an Ace. In Full Pay Deuces, you break almost every natural winner to hunt for Deuces. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the 2s in Full Pay Deuces dominate the EV landscape even more than the Aces in Royal Aces.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Deuce Obsession:** Every Deuce is a keeper. Three Deuces is a massive hold, even if you have a "Natural" winner.
2.  **Ace Devaluation:** In Full Pay Deuces, Aces are only valuable for a high pair or as part of a Deuce-heavy draw. They no longer offer a special bonus.
3.  **Connectivity Strategy:** In Full Pay Deuces, you are looking for wild card connectivity to complete straights and flushes.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games are highly rewarding for the expert player, but Full Pay Deuces offers a significant player advantage in its full-pay version. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are top-tier choices for anyone looking for a player advantage.

# Strategy Differences: Moving from Royal Aces to Super Double Bonus
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Royal Aces** to **Super Double Bonus** is a shift from one "Targeted Bonus" natural variant to another. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you value specific card ranks and their related bonuses.

### The Comparison Matrix: Royal Aces vs. Super Double Bonus

| Feature | Royal Aces (Full Pay) | Super Double Bonus (10/7) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.60% | 100.17% (Full Pay) |
| **Variance Index** | 32.0 | 28.5 |
| **Primary Strategy Change** | Target Aces Bonus | Target High-Payout Natural Quads |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.40% | -0.17% (Player Edge) |

### Strategic Divergence: Aces Focus vs. Broad Bonus Quads

In **Royal Aces Poker**, the focus is specifically on achieving Three or Four Aces. In **Super Double Bonus Poker**, all four-of-a-kind hands are "Doubled" compared to standard Jacks or Better, with Aces, 2s-4s, and 5s-Ks having their own tiers. This is a game of **EV Maximization** through high-tier natural quads. Super Double Bonus Poker's variance (28.5) is slightly lower than Royal Aces (32.0), providing a smoother **Volatility Normalization**.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is high strategy overlap (approximately 80%) because both are 52-card natural games. However, in Super Double Bonus, you will prioritize Aces and other high-bonus quads more than in Royal Aces. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the high-tier hands in Super Double Bonus dominate the EV landscape.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Broad Quad Awareness:** In Super Double Bonus, you must be aware of the bonus payouts for all quad tiers (Aces, 2s-4s, 5s-Ks).
2.  **Ace Priority:** In both games, Aces are the highest-paying quads. You prioritize them over almost any other card rank.
3.  **Full House Break:** You will break a Full House containing three Aces to draw for the fourth Ace in both games. This is a common **EV Maximization** play for high-bonus variants.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games are excellent for the expert player, but Super Double Bonus Poker offers a player advantage in its full-pay version. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both are excellent choices for the disciplined player.
