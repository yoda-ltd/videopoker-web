# Pick'em Poker Pay Table Analysis: 9/6 vs 8/5 vs 7/5

In Pick'em Poker (also known as Pick a Pair), the pay table is the absolute blueprint of your expected return. While the game uses a simplified choice mechanic, the difference of a few coins in the payout for common hands can shift the game from a near-perfect return to a standard casino disadvantage. This analysis explores the three most common pay tables and the framework of **video poker fairness and regulation** that ensures these payouts are honest.

## The Full Pay Standard: 9/6 Pick'em Poker

A "Full Pay" machine is one of the best bets in any casino. The "9/6" refers to the payouts for a Full House (9-for-1) and a Flush (6-for-1). 

*   **Optimal RTP:** **99.95%**
*   **House Edge:** **0.05%**
*   **Analysis:** This table provides a near-perfect return, making it a favorite for professional gamblers. In a regulated environment, **Return to Player (RTP) Verification** ensures that the machine provides this exact return over millions of simulated picks.

## The Common Compromise: 8/5 Pick'em Poker

Casinos often "Short-Pay" their machines to increase their margin, frequently by reducing the Full House and Flush payouts by just one unit each.

*   **Optimal RTP:** **98.45%**
*   **House Edge:** **1.55%**
*   **Analysis:** While it still looks like a good game, the 8/5 table increases the house edge by more than 3,000%. For a professional player, this "one-unit" difference is the gap between a world-class game and an average one. Regulators like the **NGCB** require these tables to be clearly displayed.

## The High-House-Edge Trap: 7/5 Pick'em Poker

Found primarily in high-traffic or non-gaming-focused venues, the 7/5 table is a mathematical trap.

*   **Optimal RTP:** **97.20%**
*   **House Edge:** **2.80%**
*   **Analysis:** At 97.2%, the primary advantage of Pick'em Poker—its high RTP—is lost. Professional players avoid 7/5 machines entirely, as standard Jacks or Better often offers a better return at that point.

## Technical Standards and Integrity

A professional player’s trust in these pay tables is backed by a robust regulatory framework:
- **Lab Audits:** Firms like **GLI** perform exhaustive tests to ensure the machine pays out exactly what is listed on the screen.
- **RNG Certification:** The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures that the hidden cards in each pile are drawn from an unbiased deck, regardless of whether you are playing on a 9/6 or a 7/5 machine.
- **Jurisdictional Mandates:** Laws that require casinos to display the payout for every hand, ensuring that "Short-Pay" machines cannot be hidden from the player.
