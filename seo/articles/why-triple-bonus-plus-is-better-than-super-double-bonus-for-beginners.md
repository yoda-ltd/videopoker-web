# Why Triple Bonus Plus is Better Than Super Double Bonus for Beginners

In the world of "Bonus-Rich" video poker, the choice between "Triple Bonus Plus" (TBP) and "Super Double Bonus" (SDB) is a study in **Strategic Convergence** and the **Variance Index**. This **best video poker strategy comparison** breaks down why Triple Bonus Plus is the superior entry point for novices, focusing on **Payout Compression** and the "Strategy Drift" caused by the different bonus structures.

## Strategic Convergence: The "Standard-Strategy" Advantage

**Triple Bonus Plus Poker** is a "Standard-Draw" game with "Extreme" bonuses for 4-of-a-Kind. While the bonuses are high, the strategy is almost identical to Jacks or Better, with minor adjustments for the "Quad" hands. Because it follows the "Standard" draw mechanic, the strategy is "Linear" and "Logical." Beginners can learn the basic "Hierarchy" of hands and apply it with high accuracy. The **video poker house edge analysis** is straightforward and predictable.

**Super Double Bonus** introduces "Categorized" bonuses for *all* 4-of-a-Kind hands. This creates "Strategy Bifurcation." For every hand, you must now ask: "Is the bonus category better than the standard hold?" The strategy is "Unique" and often "Counter-Intuitive" compared to standard bonus games. For a beginner, this is "Cognitive Overload." You can easily make a mistake by holding "Bonus Cards" when the math suggests a different path. This "Strategy Drift" can cost you 1-2% in "Theoretical Return" over a session.

## Payout Compression: The "Smooth" vs. The "Categorized"

In **Triple Bonus Plus**, the "Cost" of the high 4-of-a-Kind payouts is built into the Two-Pair and Full House payouts. However, the game remains "Smooth" because the frequency of 4-of-a-Kind is relatively high. Your bankroll "Churns" nicely. You are "Rewarded" for hitting common hands with a "Fair" payout.

In **Super Double Bonus**, the "Cost" of the "Categorized" bonuses is built into the *entire* payout table. To "Pay" for these various "Bonus" wins, the payouts for common hands are "Compressed." You often get only "Money Back" on hands that would pay more in a standard game. For a beginner, this is "Psychological Torture." You hit a "Winning" hand, but your bankroll doesn't grow. This "Bleed" is much more aggressive than in Triple Bonus Plus.

## The Variance Index: Sustainability vs. Volatility

Our **video poker house edge analysis** shows that:
*   **Triple Bonus Plus** has a variance of around **35**. It is a "Medium-Volatility" game. It offers "Stability" and "Longevity."
*   **Super Double Bonus** has a variance of around **30-40**. While the variance is "Similar," the "Categorized Strategy" and "Compressed Payouts" lead to a higher "Effective Error Rate" for beginners.

For the new player who wants to enjoy a long session and learn the fundamentals with a "Standard-Strategy" structure, Triple Bonus Plus is the clear winner. It provides the "Stability" of a standard game with the "Excitement" of well-balanced payouts. Leave the "Categorized" hunting to the pros who can handle the "Complexity" and the "Swings."
