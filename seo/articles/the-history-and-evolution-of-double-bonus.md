# The History and Evolution of Double Bonus Video Poker

Double Bonus Video Poker is the game that "Defined the Professional." Introduced in the mid-1980s, it was the first variant to offer a theoretical return of over 100% on a standard machine, sparking a "Golden Age" for advantage players.

## The "Double" Evolution

As the name suggests, Double Bonus was designed to be "Better than Bonus Poker." The primary evolution was the **Doubling of the Four Aces payout**. While Bonus Poker paid 400 credits for Aces, Double Bonus pushed it to 800 credits (at a 5-coin bet).

This change made the game incredibly "Ace-Heavy." It wasn't just a bonus anymore; the Aces were now as valuable as a Royal Flush on most other machines.

## How the Game Changed the World

1.  **The 100.17% Return:** The "Full Pay" version of Double Bonus (known as "10/7" for its 10-for-1 Full House and 7-for-1 Flush payouts) was a revolutionary moment. It meant that for the first time, a skilled player could have a mathematical edge over the casino without needing a progressive jackpot.
2.  **The "Expert" Strategy:** Because the Aces were so valuable, the strategy for Double Bonus became significantly more complex than Jacks or Better. It required players to memorize hundreds of "Penalty Card" rules to maximize their return.
3.  **The "Two Pair" Sacrifice:** To fund the 100%+ return, casinos made the ultimate sacrifice: They reduced the "Two Pair" payout to 1-for-1 (even money). This changed video poker from a "steady grind" into a "High-Volatility" game.

## Essential Video Poker Terminology

When searching the complete video poker glossary, these terms are synonymous with Double Bonus:

*   **Advantage Play (AP):** Playing a game where the player has a mathematical edge over the house. Double Bonus is the "Entry Level" game for APs.
*   **Variance:** Double Bonus has "High Variance." Because Two Pair only pays even money, you will lose your bankroll much faster than in Jacks or Better if you don't hit quads.
*   **Expected Value (EV):** In 10/7 Double Bonus, the EV is >100% with perfect strategy.

## The Strategy Revolution

The evolution of Double Bonus created the "Expert Class" of video poker players. These were players who carried "Strategy Cards" and spent hours in the library (and later, on the internet) studying the math. They learned to "Break" a Full House to draw for Four Aces—a move that still shocks casual players today.

## Modern Status

Today, "Full Pay" 10/7 Double Bonus is a rare find, usually only seen in "High Limit" rooms or specific "Advantage-Friendly" casinos in Las Vegas (like the South Point or the D). However, "Short Pay" versions (like 9/7 or 9/6) remain incredibly popular because the "Thrill of the Aces" is still there.

The history of Double Bonus is the history of the "Smart Player." It remains one of the most respected games in the "Complete Video Poker Glossary" and a "must-play" for anyone serious about the game.
