# Strategy Differences: Moving from Double Bonus to Triple Double Bonus Poker

Triple Double Bonus (TDB) is the "high stakes" cousin of Double Bonus Poker. While the pay table for most hands may look similar, the 2,000-to-1 or 4,000-to-1 payouts on Four Aces with a low-card kicker (2, 3, or 4) creates a massive shift in how you must play. This is where **Strategy Drift** becomes most dangerous for a casual player.

## The Double Bonus Baseline

In Double Bonus, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** focuses on achieving four of any card. However, when transitioning to Triple Double Bonus, your strategy must include **Volatility Scaling**. You are no longer just looking for four of a kind; you are specifically hunting for "kicker hands."

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

Transitioning from Double Bonus to TDB, you'll find that **80% of your strategy remains the same** for most mid-tier hands (Pairs, Flushes, and Straights). The **20% that changes** is entirely focused on the "Kicker hunt." This 20% shift causes the most **Bankroll Drain** if not corrected.

### The 20% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Triple Aces + Kicker:** In Double Bonus, holding an Ace-Ace-Ace with a 2 kicker is a common play. In Triple Double Bonus, holding the kicker becomes **mandatory** because the payout jumps from 800 to 4,000 credits.
2.  **Full House Devaluation:** A Full House in TDB often pays less (9-for-1 or 8-for-1) than in Double Bonus (10-for-1). This makes you even more likely to break a Full House to draw for a fourth Ace.
3.  **Two Pair Discarding:** You should frequently discard one pair from a Two Pair (Aces over anything) to draw for the fourth Ace, as the Two Pair payout is only 1-for-1.

## EV Erosion and High Volatility

To **play video poker like a pro**, you must understand that TDB has some of the highest volatility in the casino. Your **EV Erosion** happens when you play "too safe." If you don't take the aggressive draws for the 4-Aces-with-a-kicker, you are literally throwing money away over the long term.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** Always hold a 2, 3, or 4 kicker when you have three Aces.
*   **Modify:** Be prepared to break more "made" hands in favor of the massive jackpot draws.
*   **Target:** Focus exclusively on the top tier of the pay table; the middle tiers are just there to keep you in the game until the big hit.

This best video poker strategy comparison shows that TDB is not for the faint of heart, but with the right strategy, it can be the most rewarding variant in the house.
