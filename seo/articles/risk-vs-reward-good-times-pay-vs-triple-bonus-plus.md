# Risk vs. Reward: Good Times Pay vs. Triple Bonus Plus

Good Times Pay and Triple Bonus Plus represent two different ways to "Amplify" your video poker wins. One uses "Multipliers" (Good Times), while the other uses "Jackpot Payouts" (Triple Bonus). This **best video poker strategy comparison** examines the **Variance Scaling** and **Payout Compression** of both "High-Reward" formats.

## The Mathematical Foundation: Multiplication vs. Addition

**Good Times Pay** is a "Multiplier" game. It typically takes a standard game (like Jacks or Better) and adds a random or feature-triggered multiplier (2x, 5x, 10x). To fund this "Feature," you must typically bet an "Extra" 5 coins. This is the ultimate form of **Payout Compression**. You are betting 10 coins but only getting "5-coin" value on hands where the multiplier doesn't trigger.

**Triple Bonus Plus** is a "Jackpot" game. It is a Jacks or Better variant that pays massive "Triple" bonuses for Four-of-a-Kind (Quads). 4 Aces might pay 800-to-1 or more. To fund these "Bonuses," the game uses **Payout Compression** on common hands like Two-Pair (often reduced to 1-to-1).

## Variance Scaling and the EV Gap

1.  **Good Times Pay:** The variance is high. You are "Pre-paying" for the chance of a multiplier. If the "Good Times" don't roll, the **EV Gap** will empty your wallet quickly. The **video poker house edge analysis** is based on the "Multiplier Frequency."
2.  **Triple Bonus Plus:** The variance is also high (around 45-50). You are "Hunting" for Quads. The **Variance Scaling** is steep because the game doesn't provide the same "Safety Net" on non-Quad hands. The **EV Gap** between a session with Quads and one without is massive.

## Strategy Bifurcation: Feature vs. Quad

The **Strategy Bifurcation** is a shift in "Target" logic:
*   In **Good Times Pay**, the strategy usually remains identical to the "Base" game (e.g., Jacks or Better). The "Multipliers" don't change *how* you play; they just change *how much* you pay for the "Rush."
*   In **Triple Bonus Plus**, the strategy changes to "Quad Hunting." You will hold small pairs and high pairs with extreme priority. You accept the "Money Back" nature of Two-Pair as a necessary evil.

If you enjoy the "Rush" of a random multiplier that can turn a small win into a huge one, Good Times Pay is your game. If you prefer the "Strategic Goal" of hitting massive Quad payouts, Triple Bonus Plus offers a more "Targeted" high-risk experience.
