# How to Play Tens or Better Video Poker: Beginner's Guide

If you're a beginner looking for the most player-friendly entry point into video poker, **Tens or Better** is the perfect choice. As the name suggests, the lowest winning hand is a pair of 10s, rather than the standard pair of Jacks. This subtle shift makes for a lower-volatility game with more frequent wins, all while maintaining the high standards of **video poker fairness and regulation**.

## What is Tens or Better?

Tens or Better is a "non-wild" variant that uses a standard 52-card virtual deck. To ensure **game integrity**, the deck's randomness is managed by a **trusted video poker RNG certification**, which ensures that every card has an equal probability of being dealt.

The primary difference between this and Jacks or Better is the threshold for a winning hand:
*   **Jacks or Better:** Smallest win is a pair of JJ, QQ, KK, or AA.
*   **Tens or Better:** Smallest win is a pair of 10-10, JJ, QQ, KK, or AA.

## Why Play Tens or Better?

For a beginner, the main advantage is **Lower Volatility**. Because it is easier to form a pair of 10s than a pair of Jacks, you will experience fewer "losing streaks." This makes it an ideal game for extending your playtime and learning the basic rhythm of video poker without risking your bankroll too quickly.

## How to Play: Step-by-Step

1.  **Place Your Bet:** Always play the **Maximum (5 coins)**. The **RNG Certification** ensures that the jackpot for a Royal Flush at 5 coins is significantly boosted, providing the best mathematical Return to Player (RTP).
2.  **The Deal:** You are dealt five cards from the virtual deck.
3.  **The Choice:** Select which cards to "Hold." If you have a pair of 10s, keep them!
4.  **The Draw:** The machine replaces your discards. The results are verified through **RTP Verification** to ensure the machine pays out exactly what the pay table promises.

## Basic Strategy for Tens or Better

To align your play with the **Technical Standards** of the game, follow these simple rules:

*   **Hold Any Pair of 10s or Higher:** This is a guaranteed win (1-for-1).
*   **Hold Three of a Kind or Better:** Never break a winning hand unless you have four cards to a Royal Flush.
*   **Hold High Cards (10, J, Q, K, A):** Because 10s are now winning cards, a single 10 is just as valuable as a single Jack for forming a pair.
*   **Four to a Flush or Straight:** Keep these draws as they lead to the higher payouts on the table.
