# Aces and Eights Video Poker: Odds of Hitting a Royal Flush

Aces and Eights is a popular video poker variant, known for its increased payouts on specific four-of-a-kind hands (Aces, Eights, and Sevens). However, like most video poker games, the Royal Flush remains the ultimate goal for players due to its massive 800-to-1 payout (when playing max coins). Understanding the mathematical probability and the odds of hitting a Royal Flush in Aces and Eights is crucial for managing expectations and refining your strategy.

## The Mathematical Probability of a Royal Flush

In a standard 52-card deck used for Aces and Eights, there are exactly **2,598,960** possible five-card combinations. Out of these, only **4** combinations result in a Royal Flush (one for each suit: Hearts, Diamonds, Clubs, and Spades).

### Odds on the Initial Deal
On the initial deal, the probability of being dealt a Royal Flush is:
*   **4 / 2,598,960 = 1 in 649,740**

While catching a Royal Flush on the deal is a rare and exhilarating event, most Royal Flushes occur after the draw.

## Odds of Hitting a Royal Flush After the Draw

The real strategy in Aces and Eights involves knowing when to chase a Royal Flush. Your odds change significantly based on how many cards you hold:

1.  **Holding 4 Cards to a Royal Flush:**
    If you are one card away, there is 1 remaining card in the deck of 47 that completes your hand.
    *   **Odds: 1 in 47 (approx. 2.13%)**

2.  **Holding 3 Cards to a Royal Flush:**
    You need to catch 2 specific cards out of the 47 remaining.
    *   **Odds: 1 in 1,081 (approx. 0.09%)**

3.  **Holding 2 Cards to a Royal Flush:**
    You need to catch 3 specific cards out of the 47 remaining.
    *   **Odds: 1 in 16,215 (approx. 0.006%)**

## Average Frequency in a Full Game Cycle

In a standard "9/6" Aces and Eights game (paying 9 for a Full House and 6 for a Flush), if you play with a mathematically perfect strategy, you can expect to hit a Royal Flush approximately once every **40,000 to 45,000 hands**.

| Game Phase | Odds (Approximate) |
| :--- | :--- |
| Initial Deal | 1 in 649,740 |
| Holding 4 to a Royal | 1 in 47 |
| Perfect Strategy Average | 1 in 40,400 |

## Strategy: To Chase or Not to Chase?

Aces and Eights strategy often dictates that you should prioritize holding a Royal Flush draw over almost everything else. However, there are nuances:

*   **Never Break a Dealt Royal Flush:** This should go without saying, but always take the jackpot.
*   **Hold 4 to a Royal over a Made Flush or Straight:** The potential payout of 4,000 coins (on a 5-coin bet) far outweighs the immediate 25 or 30 coin win.
*   **Aces and Eights Specifics:** In this variant, four-of-a-kind Aces or Eights pay significantly more than in Jacks or Better. Despite this, you should still hold 4 cards to a Royal Flush over a single Ace or a Pair of Eights.

## Impact on Return to Player (RTP)

The Royal Flush accounts for approximately **2% of the total Return to Player (RTP)** in Aces and Eights. This means if you are playing a 99.78% RTP machine but never hit a Royal Flush, your realized return will be closer to 97.78%. This highlights the importance of having a sufficient bankroll to weather the "dry spells" between Royal Flushes.

## Conclusion

Hitting a Royal Flush in Aces and Eights is a game-changing event that requires both luck and disciplined strategy. While the odds of 1 in 40,000 hands might seem daunting, following a perfect strategy ensures you are mathematically maximizing your chances of seeing that 4,000-coin payout flash across your screen. Always remember to play max coins to ensure you receive the full 800-to-1 bonus for the Royal Flush.
