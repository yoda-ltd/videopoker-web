# The Payout Gap: Sevens Wild vs. Bonus Poker Deluxe Explained
Category: Comparison

Understanding the **Strategic Divergence** between **Sevens Wild** and **Bonus Poker Deluxe** is key to mastering these high-payout variants. This comparison focuses on how the paytables differ and why those differences lead to unique **EV Maximization** paths.

| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.56% (Full Pay) | 99.64% |
| **Variance Index** | 32.0 | 30.7 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | Multi-Wild Connectivity (Sevens) | ANY Natural Quad Bonus |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.56% (Player Edge) | 0.36% |

### The Wild Gap: Natural vs. Multi-Wild connectivity

In **Sevens Wild**, the four 7s are your most powerful tools, allowing you to complete hands that would otherwise be worthless. In **Bonus Poker Deluxe**, all four-of-a-kind hands pay a uniform 400 credits (for a 5-coin bet). This creates a significant **Volatility Normalization** shift. In Sevens Wild, you are looking for multi-wild connectivity, while in Bonus Poker Deluxe, you are looking for any natural quad.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 40% overlap. In Sevens Wild, you prioritized the 7s above all else. In Bonus Poker Deluxe, you prioritize any natural pair. This is the essence of **Payout Compression**—the 7s in Sevens Wild and the quads in Bonus Poker Deluxe dominate their respective EV landscapes.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Seven Supremacy:** In Sevens Wild, never discard a 7.
2.  **Natural Quad Awareness:** In Bonus Poker Deluxe, prioritize any natural pair for its quad potential.
3.  **Return Priority:** Sevens Wild offers a superior theoretical return (100.56%), making it the better choice for the expert player seeking an edge.

The **best video poker strategy comparison** indicates that both games are elite choices for the expert player, but Sevens Wild provides a more exciting experience with multiple wild cards. A **video poker house edge analysis** confirms that both offer excellent returns in their full-pay versions.

# The Payout Gap: Sevens Wild vs. Tens or Better Explained
Category: Comparison

Transitioning from **Sevens Wild** to **Tens or Better** involves moving from a "Multi-Wild" variant to a "Natural" variant with a lower high-pair floor. This represents a major **Strategic Divergence** in how you approach **Volatility Normalization**.

### The Comparison Matrix: Sevens Wild vs. Tens or Better

| Feature | Sevens Wild (Full Pay) | Tens or Better (Full Pay) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 100.56% | 99.14% |
| **Variance Index** | 32.0 | 19.5 |
| **Primary Payout Focus** | Multi-Wild Connectivity | Natural High Pairs (Tens+) |
| **House Edge Analysis** | -0.56% (Player Edge) | 0.86% |

### The High-Pair Gap: Wild vs. Natural

In **Sevens Wild**, the reward is concentrated in Achieving wild combinations. In **Tens or Better**, the focus is on Achieving natural high pairs starting at 10s. This leads to a lower **Volatility Normalization** (19.5 vs 32.0).

**Strategy Overlap:** There is roughly 30% overlap. In Sevens Wild, you prioritize the 7s above all else. In Tens or Better, you prioritize the natural high pairs, often holding pairs as a safe baseline. This is the essence of **EV Maximization** in high-volume games.

### Strategic Key Shifts
1.  **Seven Devaluation:** In Tens or Better, 7s are just another card in the deck.
2.  **High Card Floor Shift:** In Tens or Better, the floor is 10s. This means you hold 10s, Jacks, and Queens much more frequently.
3.  **Return Priority:** Sevens Wild offers a superior theoretical return (100.56%), making it the better choice for the advantage player.

Through a detailed **video poker house edge analysis**, it's clear that while Sevens Wild offers the best return, Tens or Better is the more stable choice for those who want a simple, natural game. The **best video poker strategy comparison** for this transition is: Use the sevens for the ultimate edge, or the pairs for the stable return.
