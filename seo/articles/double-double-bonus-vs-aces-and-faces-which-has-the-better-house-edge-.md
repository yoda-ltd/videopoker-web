# Double Double Bonus vs. Aces and Faces: Strategy Overlap and Hand-Ranking Priorities

In the specialized world of video poker, the comparison between **Double Double Bonus (DDB)** and **Aces and Faces** is a fascinating study in **Strategy Convergence** and **Optimal Hold Variance**. While both games are "Bonus" variants that pay extra for specific 4-of-a-kind hands, they distribute their return-to-player (RTP) and prioritize their "face card" bonuses differently. Understanding the **Hand-Ranking Inversion** between these two is essential for anyone looking for the **best video poker strategy comparison** and learning **how to play video poker optimally**.

## The Core Conflict: Kicker Quads vs. Face Card Bonuses

The primary driver of the strategy shift between Double Double Bonus and Aces and Faces is the "Kicker" system and the Two Pair payout. Double Double Bonus (typically 9/6 or 8/5) is a "top-heavy" game that pays only 1-for-1 for Two Pair, but compensates with massive payouts for 4-of-a-kind (especially with a kicker). Aces and Faces is a "Bonus" variant that pays extra for 4 Aces (80-for-1) and any 4 Jacks, Queens, or Kings (40-for-1).

| Hand | Double Double Bonus (9/6) | Aces and Faces (8/5) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Two Pair** | 1-for-1 | 2-for-1 |
| **Flush** | 6-for-1 | 5-for-1 |
| **Full House** | 9-for-1 | 8-for-1 |
| **4 Aces w/ 2,3,4 Kicker** | 400-for-1 | 80-for-1 |
| **4 Jacks-Kings** | 35-for-1 | 40-for-1 |

This increase in the kicker-dependent 4 Aces payouts in Double Double Bonus and the 2-for-1 Two Pair payout in Aces and Faces create massive **Strategy Convergence** breakers.

## 1. Strategy Overlap: High Strategy Convergence in Foundations

If you are coming from a Jacks or Better background, you can reuse about 50% of your strategy for both Double Double Bonus and Aces and Faces. The **Strategy Convergence** is high because the basic hand rankings remain mostly traditional.

*   **High Pair Priority:** In both games, starting with a pair of Jacks or Better is a foundational hold.
*   **Three of a Kind:** In both games, Three of a Kind is a premium situation, always drawing for the 4th card.
*   **Royal Flush Draws:** The priority of a 4-card Royal Flush remains at the top of the list in both games.

## 2. Hand-Ranking Inversion: The Two Pair and Face Card Pivot

The real strategy change comes from the bonuses in each game. This causes a **Hand-Ranking Inversion** where certain cards become more valuable than others.

*   **The Two Pair Pivot:** In Aces and Faces, you ALWAYS hold Two Pair. It pays 2-for-1, which is a "win." In Double Double Bonus, Two Pair only pays 1-for-1 (even money). This means it is no longer a "win," but a "push." Because of this, the math often dictates breaking Two Pair in DDB if it contains a pair of Aces. You hold the pair of Aces and discard the other pair to maximize your chances of hitting 4 Aces with a 2, 3, or 4 kicker (400-for-1). This is the definition of **Optimal Hold Variance**.
*   **The Face Card Dominance:** In Aces and Faces, any face card (Jack, Queen, King, Ace) is a "premium" situational hold. You will often hold a lone face card over other cards to maximize your chances of hitting the 40-for-1 face card bonus.

## 3. Penalty Card Impact: The Bonus Hunt

Professional players focus on **Penalty Card Impact** when hunting for those specific quads.

*   **In Double Double Bonus:** You are looking for Aces and kicker cards (2, 3, 4). Discarding a card that could have completed a Straight but "penalizes" your kicker potential is more common.
*   **In Aces and Faces:** You are looking for face cards and Aces. Discarding a card that could have completed a Straight but "penalizes" your face card pair potential is more common.

## 4. Optimal Hold Variance: Stability vs. Bonus Potential

Double Double Bonus is significantly more volatile than Aces and Faces.

*   **Double Double Bonus Volatility:** ~42.0 (High). Your bankroll will experience long "dry spells," punctuated by massive, life-changing wins.
*   **Aces and Faces Volatility:** ~20.0 (Low-Medium). Your bankroll will experience moderate swings as you hunt for the 80-for-1 Aces and 40-for-1 face card bonus.

## Conclusion: How to Transition

When moving from Aces and Faces to Double Double Bonus, the **best video poker strategy comparison** shows that you must become "quad-centric" and "kicker-focused." 

1.  **Devalue the Two Pair:** If you have Aces and another pair, keep the Aces and throw away the other pair in DDB. The 400-for-1 potential is mathematically superior to the 1-for-1 push.
2.  **Hunt the Kicker:** In Double Double Bonus, understand that hitting 4 Aces with a 2, 3, or 4 kicker is a massive win. Don't ignore those kicker cards.
3.  **Bankroll Management:** You need a much larger bankroll for Double Double Bonus (at least 3-4x what you need for Aces and Faces) to survive the volatility.

By mastering these **Hand-Ranking Priorities**, you can navigate the shift from the 2-for-1 Two Pair world of Aces and Faces to the quad-focused world of Double Double Bonus and learn **how to play video poker optimally**.
