# The Digital Shift: From EPROMs to Server-Based Video Poker

The most dramatic chapter in the evolution of gaming technology is the shift from "Local Logic" to "Centralized Logic." This transition has fundamentally changed how **video poker fairness and regulation** are managed, moving from physical inspections to sophisticated **Lab Audits** of cloud-based systems.

## The Era of Local Logic: The EPROM Chip
For nearly three decades, every video poker cabinet was an island. 
*   **Physical Hardware:** The game's logic was stored on an **EPROM** chip plugged into a motherboard inside the machine.
*   **The Limitation:** If a casino wanted to change a machine from "Jacks or Better" to "Deuces Wild," they had to physically unlock the cabinet and replace the chip.
*   **Regulation:** To ensure **game integrity**, regulators from the **NGCB** or other bodies would apply physical tamper-evident tape to the chips after verifying the code.

## The Revolution: Server-Based Gaming (SBG)
In the modern era, the industry has moved toward **Server-Based Gaming**. In this model, the machine on the casino floor is simply a terminal. The "brain"—the **Random Number Generator (RNG)** and the game logic—resides on a secure central server.

### Advantages for Compliance and Fairness:
1.  **Instant Payout Verification:** **Return to Player (RTP) Verification** can be performed across an entire floor of machines simultaneously, ensuring that all devices meet **Technical Standards**.
2.  **Enhanced Security:** Since the code isn't stored in the machine, it's much harder for physical tampering to occur.
3.  **Audit Trails:** Every hand dealt is logged on the server, providing a perfect audit trail for agencies like **GLI (Gaming Laboratories International)** during their periodic reviews.

## Compliance and GLI-11 Standards
The move to server-based systems required a total rewrite of **Jurisdictional Mandates**. The **GLI-11 Standards** were updated to ensure that the communication between the terminal and the server is encrypted and that the **trusted video poker RNG certification** remains valid even when processed remotely. Operators in regions like **Michigan (MGCB)** or the **UK (UKGC)** now rely on digital signatures and hash verification to ensure the server is running the exact version of the software that was certified in the lab.

## The Future: HTML5 and Cloud Gaming
Today, the shift is going even further. Many modern terminals use **HTML5**, allowing providers to develop games that are cross-platform. This means that a game certified by **BMM Testlabs** for a land-based terminal can be deployed to mobile apps and desktop browsers with the same guarantee of fairness, provided the central RNG server remains the same.

## Conclusion: A More Flexible, Transparent Game
The transition from physical EPROMs to server-based systems has made video poker more transparent than ever. With real-time monitoring and centralized **Lab Audits**, players can have higher confidence in the integrity of the game, knowing that modern **Technical Standards** are being enforced by the millisecond across the network.
