# Top 5 Tips for Mastering Aces and Eights Video Poker

Aces and Eights is a fan-favorite variant based on the legendary "Dead Man's Hand." It rewards players with significantly higher payouts for Four-of-a-Kind hands involving Aces, 8s, or 7s. ## 1. Prioritize Pairs of Aces and 8s

The core of Aces and Eights strategy is chasing the 80-for-1 bonus for four Aces or four 8s. 
- **The Tip:** If you have a pair of Aces or a pair of 8s, hold them and draw for the other two. These cards are far more valuable in this game than in Jacks or Better.
- **Integrity Note:** The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures that Aces and 8s are dealt with the same statistical frequency as any other rank. The machine never "hides" the bonus cards from the player.

## 2. Don't Forget the "Lucky 7s"

While the name highlights Aces and 8s, many versions of this game also offer a 50-for-1 bonus for four 7s. 
- **The Tip:** Always check your specific machine's pay table. If the "Four 7s" bonus is active, treat a pair of 7s with higher priority than you would in other non-wild variants.
- **Fairness Note:** Independent **Lab Audits** by firms like **GLI** verify that the "Bonus" logic in the game's **Source Code** correctly identifies and pays the 50-to-1 reward for 7s.

## 3. Play Only "8/5" or Higher Pay Tables

The profitability of Aces and Eights depends on the return for Full Houses and Flushes. 
- **The Tip:** Look for "Full Pay" machines (usually 8 for Full House, 5 for Flush) which offer an RTP of **99.78%**. Avoid "Short Pay" machines like 7/5 or 6/5, as the bonus payouts alone cannot compensate for the lower base return.
- **Fairness Note:** **Jurisdictional Mandates** require casinos to display these payout values clearly. Always verify the pay table before you deposit.

## 4. Master the "Max Bet" Strategy

To achieve the top theoretical RTP, you must play with **5 coins**.

- **Integrity Note:** **RTP Verification** ensures that the machine provides a fair distribution of Royals and bonus hands, regardless of your bet history.

## 5. Trust the Mathematical Independence of the RNG

A common superstition is that if a machine hits a bonus (like 4 Aces), it won't hit it again for a long time. 
- **The Tip:** Every hand in Aces and Eights is an independent event. The RNG does not remember previous hands. If you hit 4 Aces, your chance of hitting them on the next hand is exactly the same.
- **Security Note:** Regulators like the **NGCB** or **UKGC** ensure that the RNG remains statistically neutral, providing a fair and unbiased experience for every player on every deal.
