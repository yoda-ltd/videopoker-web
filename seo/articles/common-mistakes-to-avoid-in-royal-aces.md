# Common Mistakes to Avoid in Royal Aces

Royal Aces is a thrilling video poker variant that offers a significantly higher payout for a Royal Flush compared to standard games. However, the lure of the "big hit" often leads players into several common—and costly—mistakes.

### Chasing the Royal Flush Aggressively
The most frequent mistake in Royal Aces is over-chasing the Royal Flush. Players often break apart high-paying hands (like Three-of-a-Kind or a Straight) just to keep two or three cards to a Royal. While the Royal Flush payout is higher, the **Theoretical Return** is still built on maintaining your bankroll with smaller wins. This is the essence of **Hit Frequency Optimization**.

### Ignoring the Base Pay Table
Just because a machine is labeled "Royal Aces" doesn't mean it has a good RTP. Casinos often lower the payout for the "smaller" hands (like Two Pair or a Flush) to compensate for the higher Royal Flush payout. If the base pay table is too weak, the overall RTP will be lower than a standard Jacks or Better machine. To learn **how to win video poker daily**, you must evaluate the entire pay table, not just the top line.

### Bankroll Mismanagement
Royal Aces has much higher **Variance** than standard video poker. Because a larger portion of the return is tied up in the Royal Flush, you will experience longer and deeper "dry spells." For **Session Sustainability**, you need a substantial bankroll for **Variance Buffering**. If you don't have at least 1,500 units, you're likely to bust before the big payout hits.

### "Is Max Bet Always Better for Bankroll Longevity?"
In Royal Aces, the answer is an absolute yes. The "bonus" payout for the Royal is only available at the 5-coin level. If you play for only 1 coin, you are playing a game with a massive house edge. For those looking for **video poker tips for beginners**, our number one tip for Royal Aces is: 5 coins or find a different game.

### Misunderstanding the RNG
Some players believe the Royal Flush is "locked" until a certain amount of money has been played. This is a misunderstanding of **Pseudo-Randomness**. The RNG deals the cards fairly for every hand. A Royal Flush can hit twice in a row or not appear for 100,000 hands. It is all down to the math.

Royal Aces is a game of high risk and high reward. Avoid these mistakes, play with discipline, and you just might hit the biggest payout of your life.
