# Double Down Stud vs. Super Double Bonus: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Double Down Stud and Super Double Bonus (SDB) are both variants designed for players who want something more exciting than standard Jacks or Better. While Double Down Stud focuses on a unique betting mechanic, Super Double Bonus focuses on "super-charged" payouts for specific Four of a Kind hands.

Which one offers the best mathematical edge? Let's break down the house edge for each.

## Super Double Bonus: The Bonus King

Super Double Bonus is a variant of Double Bonus Poker. It pays specialized bonuses for Four of a Kind based on the rank of the cards.

*   **Four Aces:** 160-for-1.
*   **Four Jacks, Queens, or Kings:** **120-for-1**.
*   **Four 2s, 3s, or 4s:** 80-for-1.
*   **Other Four of a Kind:** 50-for-1.
*   **Two Pair:** **1-for-1**.
*   **Full Pay RTP (9/7):** **99.69%**
*   **House Edge:** 0.31%

The highlight of SDB is the 120-for-1 payout for high face cards (J, Q, K). This is significantly higher than the 50-for-1 found in most other games.

## Double Down Stud: The High-Speed Betting Game

Double Down Stud has no draws. You see four cards and decide whether to double your bet before the fifth card is revealed.

*   **Full Pay RTP (9/6):** **98.49%**
*   **House Edge:** 1.51%

## Comparing the House Edge

### 1. Theoretical Return (RTP)
In a head-to-head comparison of "Full Pay" machines, **Super Double Bonus is the significantly better game.**
*   Super Double Bonus: **99.69% RTP**
*   Double Down Stud: **98.49% RTP**

A 1.2% difference in RTP is huge. In the long run, you will lose money five times faster on a Double Down Stud machine than on a Super Double Bonus machine, assuming perfect strategy for both.

### 2. Strategy and Difficulty
*   **Super Double Bonus strategy** is very complex. Because there are so many different bonus tiers (Aces, Face Cards, Low Cards), the rules for what to hold are counter-intuitive. For example, the way you play a pair of Jacks is different in SDB than in Jacks or Better.
*   **Double Down Stud strategy** is incredibly easy. Since you don't discard, you only have to decide whether to "Double" or "Stay" based on the four cards you see.

### 3. Volatility (The "Swing")
*   **Super Double Bonus** is a very volatile game. The 1-for-1 payout on Two Pair means your bankroll will drop quickly between bonus hits. However, the high payouts for J, Q, and K happen much more often than Four Aces, which helps "stabilize" the volatility compared to Double Double Bonus.
*   **Double Down Stud** has its own volatility. Since you are doubling your bet to 10 coins, a "cold" streak can wipe out your session budget in minutes.

## Verdict: Which Should You Play?

*   **Play Super Double Bonus if** you are a serious player who is willing to study strategy. A 99.69% return is nearly a "break-even" game and is one of the best values on the casino floor.
*   **Play Double Down Stud if** you want a simple, fast-paced game and don't care about the slightly higher house edge.

**Important Note:** As always, check the pay table! Many casinos offer "short-pay" Super Double Bonus (like 8/5), which drops the RTP to **95.9%**. If the SDB machine is 8/5 and the Double Down Stud machine is 9/6, the **Double Down Stud machine is actually the better bet!** Always check the Full House and Flush payouts before you put your money in.
