# Perfect Play in Quick Quads: Every Hand Explained

Quick Quads Poker, an Action Gaming patent, represents a significant evolution in "rare video poker variants strategy." Unlike standard Jacks or Better, Quick Quads introduces a unique mathematical layer: the ability to form a Four-of-a-Kind (Quad) using three matching cards plus two other cards whose ranks sum to the rank of the trips. For example, three 8s plus a 5 and a 3 equals an "8-8-8-8" payout. 

To achieve the maximum theoretical return, players must master **Variance Normalization** and account for the **6th coin side-bet EV**. This guide explains how to navigate these mechanics for perfect play on the **best video poker machines in 2026**.

## The 6th Coin: Side-Bet EV and RTP Impact
Playing Quick Quads requires a 6-coin wager per line (assuming a 5-coin base). This extra coin does not pay out on standard hands; instead, it activates the "Summation" feature.
- **RTP Sensitivity:** On a 9/6 Jacks or Better base (99.54%), the 6th coin actually *increases* the theoretical RTP to approximately **99.65%** because the frequency of Quad payouts (traditionally high-variance events) increases dramatically.
- **Feature Hit Frequency:** By allowing summation, the "Quad Hit Frequency" moves from roughly 1 in 423 hands to 1 in 150 hands. This shifts the game from a high-variance "Royal-chasing" model to an **Accumulated Multipliers** style of play where Quads provide the bankroll stability.

## Specific Strategy Shifts for Quick Quads
Perfect play in Quick Quads requires overriding standard hold priorities when "Sum-Kickers" are present.

### 1. The Summation Priority
In standard poker, you never hold a "kicker" with trips. In Quick Quads, you hold the kicker if it helps complete a sum. 
- **The Rule:** If you hold three cards of rank $N$ (where $2 \le N \le 10$), and you have a fourth card of rank $K$ where $K < N$, you must check if a fifth card of rank $(N-K)$ exists.
- **Strategy Shift:** Always hold the kicker if it can form the sum. Example: Holding 7-7-7-4. In standard play, you discard the 4. In Quick Quads, you hold the 4, as drawing any 3 completes the Quad.

### 2. Breaking Low Pairs for Three-Card Sums
If you are dealt a low pair and three cards that could form a sum for a higher rank, the math often favors the "Sum-Draw."
- **Scenario:** Dealt 9-9-3-2-Ace. Standard play: Hold the 9s. Quick Quads play: If the Ace-3-2 could potentially link with another 9, the EV shift is negligible, but if you already have three 9s, you *must* hold the sum-components.

### 3. Ace Limitation
Aces cannot be formed by summation (since the minimum sum of two cards is 3, i.e., 2+Ace is not allowed as Ace is usually 1 or 11, but the game treats Ace as 1 for sums). Therefore, three Aces should be played with standard strategy—never look for a sum to make Aces.

## Bankroll Management and Variance
Despite the higher hit frequency for Quads, the 6-coin requirement increases your "cost per round."
- **Variance Normalization:** Because you are betting 20% more per hand, you need a bankroll approximately 25% larger than a standard 5-coin game to survive the "dry" spells between Quad hits.
- **Expert Tip:** Look for "9/6" or "8/5" pay tables. In 2026, many machines have downgraded to "7/5" to offset the summation advantage; avoid these as the house edge doubles.
