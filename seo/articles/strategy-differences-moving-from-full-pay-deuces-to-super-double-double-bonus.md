# Strategy Differences: Moving from Full Pay Deuces to Super Double Double Bonus Poker

Transitioning from Full Pay Deuces (FPD) to Super Double Double Bonus (SDDB) is a major leap in video poker strategy. While FPD relies on the four wild cards to create value, SDDB is all about the "Four of a Kind" payouts and the specific "Kickers." This shift in **Baseline Hand Ranking** is one of the most difficult to master for a seasoned wild-card player.

## The Full Pay Deuces Baseline

In Full Pay Deuces, your **Baseline Hand Ranking** is defined by the Deuces. You are always looking for a "natural" Flush or Straight to fill your time until the wild cards arrive. When moving to Super Double Double Bonus, there are no wild cards. You must shift your focus back to "high-card" strategy and Pair valuation.

## Strategy Portability: The 80/20 Rule

When moving from FPD to SDDB, **only about 40% of your strategy is portable**. This is why this transition causes such massive **Bankroll Drain** for FPD experts. The **60% that changes** is what separates a professional SDDB player from a hobbyist.

### The 60% "Bankroll Drain" Zone:
1.  **Three-Card Straight Flush:** In Full Pay Deuces, holding a three-card straight flush is a core play. In Super Double Double Bonus, it is rarely held over a single high card or even a low pair.
2.  **Kicker Value:** SDDB pays extra for Four of a Kind with specific "Kickers" (e.g., Four Aces with a 2, 3, or 4 kicker). FPD has no kicker payouts, so your mental map for holding low cards must be completely rewritten.
3.  **Four-Card Flushes:** In FPD, holding a four-card flush is a priority. In SDDB, holding two high cards (A, K, Q, J) often has higher EV because of the potential for a Royal Flush or a higher-paying Four of a Kind.

## Volatility Scaling and EV Erosion

Super Double Double Bonus is a high-volatility game. **Volatility Scaling** means you will have long losing streaks punctuated by massive wins. **EV Erosion** occurs when a FPD player forgets that Jacks are "high" cards in SDDB. In FPD, they are just another card. In SDDB, they can be part of a winning "pair" or a Royal Flush draw.

## Summary of Modifications
*   **Hold:** High cards (J, Q, K, A) are now your primary goal.
*   **Modify:** Discard any wild-card habits! There are no Deuces to help you.
*   **Target:** Focus on the "Kickers." Learning which low cards to keep with your Four-of-a-Kind is the best video poker strategy comparison you can make.

Learning how to play video poker like a pro means recognizing that SDDB is a completely different beast than Full Pay Deuces. Master the "natural" hand values and you'll survive the transition.
