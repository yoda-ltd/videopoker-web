# Full Pay Deuces vs. Super Double Double Bonus: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Two of the most popular machines for high-stakes video poker players are **Full Pay Deuces Wild (100.76% EV)** and **Super Double Double Bonus (99.69% EV)**. While Deuces Wild relies on wild cards, Super Double Double Bonus (SDDB) relies on "Kicker" bonuses to drive its return. This article compares the house edge, math, and volatility of these two casino favorites.

## Theoretical Return (EV) and House Edge

### 1. Full Pay Deuces Wild
*   **Return (EV):** 100.76%
*   **House Edge:** -0.76% (Player Edge)
*   **Math:** The player holds an advantage due to the 200:1 Four Deuces and the 800:1 Natural Royal Flush.

### 2. Full Pay Super Double Double Bonus
*   **Return (EV):** 99.69%
*   **House Edge:** 0.31% (Casino Edge)
*   **Math:** The return is driven by massive payouts for Four-of-a-Kind with specific "Kickers" (e.g., Four Aces with a 2, 3, or 4 pays 400:1).

**The Verdict on EV:** Full Pay Deuces Wild is mathematically superior. It is one of the few games where the "house edge" is actually in the player's favor.

## Pay Table Comparison: Wild Cards vs. Kickers

The mechanics of these two games are fundamentally different.

| Hand | Deuces Wild (100.76%) | SDDB (99.69%) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Natural Royal** | 800:1 | 800:1 |
| **4 Aces w/ 2,3,4 Kicker** | (N/A) | **400:1** |
| **4 Deuces** | **200:1** | 80:1 (or 160:1 w/ Kicker) |
| **4 Aces (No Kicker)** | 5:1 (as 4-of-a-kind) | 160:1 |
| **Full House** | 3:1 | 9:1 |

In SDDB, the "Kicker" is the key. If you have three Aces and a 2, you are drawing for a **400-for-1** payout. In Deuces Wild, you are simply looking for that fourth Deuce to hit **200-for-1**.

## Volatility and Variance
*   **Deuces Wild Variance:** ~25.8
*   **Super Double Double Bonus Variance:** ~45.0

**Super Double Double Bonus is significantly more volatile.** In fact, it is one of the most "swingy" games in the casino. Because so much of the payback is concentrated in the 400:1 and 160:1 hits, you will experience long periods of losing. Deuces Wild, while still volatile, feels much smoother because the Four Deuces (200:1) hit more frequently than the 400:1 Kicker hands in SDDB.

## Strategy Complexity
**Deuces Wild** strategy is notoriously difficult because you must ignore traditional "Natural" hand rankings (like keeping a pair of Aces vs. keeping a single Deuce).

**SDDB** strategy is an advanced version of Jacks or Better. The complexity comes from the "Kicker" logic. 
*   *Example:* Do you hold an Ace and a 2? In SDDB, the answer is often yes, whereas in standard Jacks or Better, you would never hold a 2 with an Ace.

## House Edge Analysis: The Reality of the Floor
Most casinos "short-pay" these machines:
*   **Short Pay Deuces (98.9%):** Common.
*   **Short Pay SDDB (97.6%):** Found when the Full House/Flush drops to 7/5 instead of 9/6.

If you find a 99.69% SDDB machine (9/6) and only a 98.9% Deuces Wild machine, the **SDDB machine is the better mathematical play**, despite its higher volatility.

## Which Game Should You Play?

**Choose Full Pay Deuces Wild if:**
*   You want a mathematical edge over the house (-0.76% edge).
*   You want lower volatility (relative to SDDB).
*   You have mastered the wild-card strategy.

**Choose Super Double Double Bonus if:**
*   You are a "jackpot hunter" who loves the 400:1 Kicker payouts.
*   You have a massive bankroll to withstand the 45.0 variance.
*   You prefer the "Natural" game mechanics over wild cards.

## Conclusion
While **Full Pay Deuces Wild (100.76%)** is the better game for long-term profit, **Super Double Double Bonus (99.69%)** offers more excitement for players who enjoy high-risk, high-reward "Natural" poker. Just be prepared for the swings—SDDB is a roller coaster that requires a disciplined mind and a deep pocket.
