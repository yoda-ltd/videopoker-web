# Strategy Differences: Moving from All-American to Tens or Better

Moving from All-American to Tens or Better is a move from "High-Volatility" to "High-Stability." Both are "Natural" versions of video poker, but they require two completely different mindsets. This **best video poker strategy comparison** examines the **Strategy Bifurcation** and **Variance Scaling** involved in moving between these two "Jacks or Better" cousins.

## The Mathematical Foundation: Flush/Straight vs. Threshold

**All-American** is a "Premium" game. It pays massive bonuses for "Straight Flushes," "Four-of-a-Kind," "Flushes," and "Straights." To fund these "Premium" payouts, the game uses extreme **Payout Compression** on "Two-Pair" (which only pays 1-to-1) and potentially on the "Full House" payout. The **video poker house edge analysis** is dominated by the frequency of these high-ranking hands.

**Tens or Better** is a "Threshold" game. By paying you "Money Back" for a pair of Tens (instead of Jacks), the game provides the most frequent winning threshold in the casino. To fund this "Safety," the game uses **Payout Compression** on the mid-tier hands. This is the ultimate "Low-Risk" gambling experience.

## Variance Scaling and the Bankroll Buffer

1.  **All-American:** The variance is high (often 30-35). It is a "Volatile" game. Because you are only getting "Money Back" on Two-Pair, your bankroll "Bleeds" rapidly until you hit a "Premium" hand. This requires a moderate-to-large bankroll buffer.
2.  **Tens or Better:** The variance is very low (around 18-20). It is a "Stable" game. The **Variance Scaling** is shallow. Your bankroll moves slowly and predictably. You can play for hours on a small budget.

## Strategy Bifurcation: Aggressive vs. Conservative

The transition requires a shift in "Hold" intent:
*   In **All-American**, you play "Aggressively." You will break apart a "Winning" high pair to chase a "Straight Flush" or a "Straight" if the pay table is aggressive enough. You are "Premium Hunting."
*   In **Tens or Better**, you play "Conservatively." You hold high cards (Tens and up) with extreme priority to get your money back. Your goal is simply to "Stay in the Game."

If you enjoy the "Rush" of hitting a high-paying Straight Flush or Quad, All-American is your game. If you prefer the "Safety" of a low-risk game that allows for maximum playtime, Tens or Better is the mathematically superior choice. Both require a disciplined approach and an understanding of the "Cost" of the safety (or the strike).
