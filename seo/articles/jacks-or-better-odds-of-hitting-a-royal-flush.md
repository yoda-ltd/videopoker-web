## Shared-Base Variance and the "Smoothing Effect"

Jacks or Better is known for its low **Variance Index** (~19.5). In multi-hand play, this stability is enhanced by **Independent Draw Randomness**.

### The Shared Base Hand Effect
Every 100-play deal begins with a **Shared Base Hand**.

- **The Probability:** $1 - (46/47)^{100} \approx 88.5\%$. 
This high probability is the result of **Combinatorial Convergence**—the more independent trials you run, the more likely the aggregate result will match the mathematical expectation.

### The Smoothing Effect
JoB is uniquely suited for high-volume play because the frequent payouts for Two Pair (2-for-1) and Jacks-or-Better (1-for-1) provide a constant "rebate." When playing 100 hands, the **Independent Draw Randomness** ensures that even on a mediocre base hand, you will often "fill in" enough small wins to offset the cost of the bet. This is the **Smoothing Effect** in action.
