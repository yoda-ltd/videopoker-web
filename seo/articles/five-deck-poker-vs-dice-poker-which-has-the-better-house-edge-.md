# Five Deck Poker vs. Dice Poker: Which Has the Better House Edge?

Comparing **Five Deck Poker** and **Dice Poker** is a study in expanded probability. While Five Deck Poker uses five independent decks to create otherwise impossible hands, Dice Poker uses the six faces of virtual dice to simplify the poker model. To choose the right game, you must understand how **video poker fairness and regulation** ensures the integrity of these specialized "Non-Standard" models.

## The Mathematical Framework: Independent Decks vs. 6-Sided Dice

The house edge in these games is determined by the total number of possible combinations.

*   **Five Deck Poker:** Uses five separate 52-card decks for the draw stage. This allows for "Five of a Kind" and increases the frequency of duplicates. With billions of potential outcomes, the math is complex but highly favorable for specific hands.
*   **Dice Poker:** Uses five virtual dice (each with 6 faces). With only 7,776 initial outcomes, the game is much simpler, but the lack of "Suits" and the limited rank pool (1-6) completely changes the poker hierarchy.

## House Edge Analysis: The Accuracy of the Math

The accuracy of the advertised edge in these variants is guaranteed by **Return to Player (RTP) Verification** during independent **Lab Audits**.

| Metric | Typical Five Deck Poker | Typical Dice Poker |

**The Verdict:** **Five Deck Poker** generally offers a significantly better house edge for skilled players. The depth of the 5-deck model allows for higher strategic influence than the simpler dice model.

## 1. Technical Standards for Randomness

Both variants must adhere to strict **Technical Standards** enforced by regulators like the **NGCB** or **UKGC**.

*   **Five Deck Poker:** The **Random Number Generator (RNG)** must provide five truly independent shuffles simultaneously. Firms like **GLI** audit the code to ensure that a card drawn in position 1 has no influence on position 5.
*   **Dice Poker:** Regulators verify that the "dice shuffle" provides a perfect 1-in-6 distribution. The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures that the machine doesn't "near-miss" the Five-of-a-Kind dice combination to entice the player.

## 2. Payout Analysis: The "Impossible Hand" Bonus

The house edge in Five Deck Poker is often concentrated in the "Five of a Kind" payout. 
- In Five Deck Poker, hitting five identical cards (e.g., five Queens of Hearts) can pay as much as a Royal Flush. 
- In Dice Poker, the "Five of a Kind" is the standard top win, but because it's much easier to hit (1-in-1,296), the payout is lower.
