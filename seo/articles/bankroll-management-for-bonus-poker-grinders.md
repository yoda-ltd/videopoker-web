# Bankroll Management for Bonus Poker Grinders: Optimizing the Low-Variance Grind

Bonus Poker is the "goldilocks" of video poker for many professional grinders. To maximize your returns, you must apply the **best video poker bankroll management** principles to capitalize on the game's stability.

## The Mathematics of Stability: Bonus Poker Variance

In Bonus Poker, the **Variance Index** typically sits around 20.9 (for the 8/5 version). This is only slightly higher than Jacks or Better (19.5), making it one of the most stable games in the casino.

### 1. The "Two Pair" Safety Net
Like Jacks or Better, Bonus Poker pays **2-for-1 on Two Pair**. This is the single most important factor in bankroll sustainability. This payout acts as a "Volatility Buffer," providing frequent small returns that offset the cost of the "Royal Flush Cycle."

### 2. Standard Deviation and Risk of Ruin (RoR)
The standard deviation for Bonus Poker is approximately 4.57 per hand. Because the swings are smaller, your **Risk of Ruin** is significantly lower for a given bankroll size compared to Double Double Bonus. 
- A bankroll of **2,000 units** (e.g., $500 at a $0.25 machine) provides a very high level of security for a session.
- A **Life-Of-Bankroll** of 5,000 units is often sufficient for professional-level security at a 99% confidence interval.

## Kelly Criterion Application in Bonus Poker

While Bonus Poker pay tables often hover around 99.17% (8/5), the "edge" for the player usually comes from casino promotions, slot club points, and mailers. When the total return exceeds 100%, the **Kelly Criterion** can be used to determine your optimal betting unit.

- **The Conservative Approach:** Since the edge is often external (comps), professionals use a very conservative "Fractional Kelly" (e.g., 1/20th). This prioritizes bankroll longevity over aggressive growth, ensuring you stay in action long enough to collect the "back-end" value.

## Session-Based vs. Life-Of-Bankroll Strategies

For the Bonus Poker grinder, the strategy is about "time on device."

- **Session-Based Bankroll:** Since the goal is often to earn "tier points" or "comp dollars," your session bankroll should be calculated to last a specific number of hours. At 800 hands per hour, a 2,000-unit bankroll is almost "bulletproof" for an 8-hour session.
- **Life-Of-Bankroll:** This is your "insurance policy." Use a **video poker bankroll calculator** to ensure that even if you hit a rare "downward spike" (which can happen even in low-variance games), your total gambling fund remains intact.

## The Royal Flush Cycle and Bonus Payouts

The **Royal Flush Cycle** in Bonus Poker is approximately 1 in 40,000 hands. However, Bonus Poker adds "mini-cycles" for Four-of-a-Kind:
- **Aces:** 1 in 4,800 hands (80-for-1).
- **2s, 3s, 4s:** 1 in 1,600 hands (40-for-1).
- **5s thru Ks:** 1 in 500 hands (25-for-1).

These bonus payouts occur frequently enough that they act as "mini-Royals," smoothing out the variance curve and making bankroll management much more predictable.
