# How to Play Double Double Bonus Video Poker: Beginner's Guide

For players who find Jacks or Better too "quiet," **Double Double Bonus (DDB)** is the answer. DDB is one of the most popular video poker variants in the world, known for its high volatility and massive payouts for Four-of-a-Kind. This guide will teach you the rules and the unique "Kicker" system that makes DDB so exciting.

Because DDB involves higher stakes and more complex math, it is strictly monitored by **video poker fairness and regulation** to ensure every player has a fair chance at the top prizes.

## The "Double Double" Twist: Payouts for Kickers

In most video poker games, the fifth card in a Four-of-a-Kind hand (the "kicker") doesn't matter. In Double Double Bonus, the kicker can double your win.

### Key Payouts (at Max Bet):
*   **4 Aces with a 2, 3, or 4 Kicker:** 2,000 Coins
*   **4 Aces (any other kicker):** 800 Coins
*   **4 2s, 3s, or 4s with an Ace-4 Kicker:** 800 Coins
*   **4 2s, 3s, or 4s (any other kicker):** 400 Coins

These "bonus" payouts are what make DDB a high-volatility game. While you'll have more losing sessions, the "hits" are much larger. The accuracy of these payouts is guaranteed by **RTP Verification** and periodic **Lab Audits**.

## How to Play Double Double Bonus

The game mechanics are identical to other variants, backed by a **trusted video poker RNG certification**:

1.  **Deal:** You receive five cards.
2.  **Hold/Discard:** You choose which cards to keep.
3.  **Draw:** The machine replaces your discards.

The **RNG Certification** ensures that the "kicker" you need for that 2,000-coin payout is drawn from a fair, unbiased 52-card virtual deck. There is no such thing as a "near-miss" in a regulated game; every draw is an independent mathematical event.

## Foundational DDB Strategy

Because the payouts for Aces and small cards are so high, your strategy must be more aggressive than in Jacks or Better:

*   **Hold Any Ace:** A single Ace is more valuable in DDB because of the 800 and 2,000-coin potential.
*   **The Kicker Rule:** If you are dealt **Three Aces and a Kicker (2, 3, or 4)**, you hold all four cards! In most other games, you'd discard the kicker.
*   **Don't Chase Everything:** While the bonuses are great, you still need to win small hands (Pairs, Two Pair) to keep your bankroll alive.
*   **Full-Pay Standard:** Look for "9/6" DDB (9 for Full House, 6 for Flush), which offers an RTP of **98.98%**.

When playing DDB, you are protected by a suite of **Technical Standards** and **Jurisdictional Mandates**:
- **BMM Testlabs and GLI:** These organizations perform extensive **Lab Audits** on DDB software to ensure the "Kicker" logic is correctly implemented.

- **RNG Independence:** Your chance of hitting 4 Aces with a Kicker is exactly the same on every hand, regardless of how much you've won or lost recently.
