# The Payout Gap: Bonus Poker vs Triple Double Bonus Explained

In the world of **best odds video poker**, players often find themselves choosing between two of the most popular machines on the floor: Bonus Poker and Triple Double Bonus. While they look similar, the **EV Gap** between them depends entirely on their specific pay tables and your tolerance for **House Edge Variance**.

## The Best Odds Index: 8/5 Bonus vs 9/7 Triple Double Bonus
In the **highest return video poker games** 2026 ranking, both machines offer strong professional returns when found in their "Full Pay" configurations:
*   **8/5 Bonus Poker:** 99.17% RTP.
*   **9/7 Triple Double Bonus:** 99.58% RTP.

At first glance, Triple Double Bonus appears to be the superior choice. However, Triple Double Bonus offers a massive "Bonus" for hitting Four of a Kind with a kicker (specifically Four Aces with a 2, 3, or 4 kicker, which pays 800-for-1 instead of 80-for-1). This change in the pay table structure shifts the **Theoretical Return Convergence**.

## Analysis of "Short Pay" Predation
The real "Payout Gap" occurs when casinos offer sub-optimal versions of these games. 
*   **Bonus Poker (6/5):** RTP falls to 96.87%.
*   **Triple Double Bonus (8/5):** RTP falls to 95.97%.

If you find a 6/5 Bonus machine next to an 8/5 Triple Double Bonus machine, the 0.90% **EV Gap** is predatory. A professional player would never touch the 8/5 Triple Double Bonus machine in this scenario. However, if the choice is between 6/5 Bonus (96.87%) and 9/7 Triple Double Bonus (99.58%), the Triple Double Bonus machine is actually the much better bet.

## House Edge Variance and Strategy
Triple Double Bonus has significantly higher variance than Bonus Poker. This is because a massive portion of the return is tied up in the Four Aces with a kicker payout (800-for-1). If you don't hit this hand, your bankroll will drain much faster than in Bonus Poker. 
*   **Bonus Poker Strategy:** Focuses on bankroll preservation and hitting the Four of a Kind.
*   **Triple Double Bonus Strategy:** Aggressively pursues the Four Aces with a kicker.

If the gap is larger, always follow the higher RTP, provided you have the bankroll to handle the Triple Double Bonus variance.
