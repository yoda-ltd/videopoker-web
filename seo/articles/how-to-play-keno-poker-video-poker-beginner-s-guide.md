# How to Play Keno Poker Video Poker: Beginner's Guide

Keno Poker is a unique hybrid game that combines the excitement of a 20-number Keno draw with the classic rules of video poker. It's one of the most popular **hybrid video poker games** in casinos, offering a way to win with both poker hands and Keno numbers.

## The Basic Concept of Keno Poker

In Keno Poker, you are dealt a five-card poker hand. However, each card in the deck is assigned a Keno number from 1 to 80. As you draw cards, the corresponding Keno numbers are marked on a Keno board displayed on the screen.

The gameplay follows these steps:
1.  **Initial Deal**: You receive five cards and an initial set of Keno numbers are marked on the board based on those cards.
2.  **Hold and Draw**: You choose which poker cards to hold and then draw new ones to complete your poker hand.
3.  **Keno Draw**: After the poker draw is complete, a full set of 20 Keno numbers is drawn. If the numbers from your poker cards match enough of the 20 drawn numbers, you win a Keno bonus on top of any poker payout.

## How Keno Elements Affect the House Edge

The **Analysis of how Keno and dice elements affect the traditional poker house edge** is key to understanding Keno Poker. 

*   **Hybrid RTP (Return to Player)**: The RTP of Keno Poker is a combination of the base poker game and the Keno bonus. Often, the Keno portion is a "side bet" or a built-in bonus that can actually increase the **Hybrid RTP** if you hit enough matching numbers.
*   **Side-Bet EV (Expected Value)**: Most Keno Poker games require a maximum coin bet to activate the Keno bonus. Always check if the **Side-Bet EV** of the Keno bonus justifies the higher cost of playing.
*   **RNG Cross-Pollination**: The game's RNG must coordinate the poker card generation with the Keno number draw. This **RNG Cross-Pollination** ensures that the Keno outcomes are fair but also tied to your poker results.

## Professional Strategies for Keno Poker

To excel at Keno Poker, you must manage two simultaneous games:
1.  **Prioritize the Poker Hand**: In most cases, the poker payout is larger than the Keno payout. Don't throw away a "Three of a Kind" just to chase a Keno number!
2.  **Watch the Keno Board**: If you are one number away from a high-paying Keno match, it might be worth making a slightly sub-optimal poker hold to "go for the Keno." This is where calculating the **Side-Bet EV** becomes crucial.
3.  **Manage Your Bankroll**: Because of the dual payout system, Keno Poker can have higher **Payline Volatility** than standard video poker. Ensure your bankroll can handle the swings.
