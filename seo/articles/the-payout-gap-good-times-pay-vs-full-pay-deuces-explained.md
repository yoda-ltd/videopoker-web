# The Payout Gap: Good Times Pay vs. Full Pay Deuces Explained

Comparing "Good Times Pay" (a feature-heavy multiplier game) to "Full Pay Deuces Wild" (a pure mathematical classic) is like comparing a turbocharged sports car to a vintage endurance racer. This **best video poker strategy comparison** explores how **Payout Compression** and **Variance Scaling** create two completely different player experiences.

## The Mathematical Foundation

Full Pay Deuces Wild (FPDW) is famous for its 100.76% theoretical return. It is a "pure" game where the edge comes from the frequency of Deuces and the standard payouts for hands like "Four Deuces" (200-to-1). There are no "extra" multipliers; the math is baked into the 52-card deck and the pay table.

Good Times Pay, however, is a "Feature" game. It typically overlays a standard game (like 9/6 Jacks or Better) with a multiplier feature. To fund these multipliers, you must usually bet an "extra" 5 coins. This means you are betting 10 coins but only getting "5-coin" value on hands where the multiplier doesn't trigger. This is the ultimate form of **Payout Compression**.

## Variance Scaling and the EV Gap

1.  **Full Pay Deuces Wild:** The variance is moderate (around 25). It is a "grinder's" game. You win often enough to keep playing, and the Four Deuces provide the necessary "boost." The **video poker house edge analysis** is straightforward and relies on volume.
2.  **Good Times Pay:** The variance is high. You are essentially paying a "premium" for the chance to hit a 5x or 10x multiplier on a big hand. If the "Good Times" don't roll, the **EV Gap** between your total bet and your return will widen rapidly, exhausting your bankroll.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Simple" vs. The "Wild"

The **Strategy Bifurcation** here is a total shift in logic:
*   In **FPDW**, your strategy is 100% focused on the Deuces. You will hold a single Deuce over almost any other combination. 
*   In **Good Times Pay**, your strategy is identical to the base game (e.g., Jacks or Better). The "multipliers" don't usually change the *optimal* way to play a hand; they just change the *excitement* level of the outcome.

For the serious advantage player, FPDW is the Holy Grail. For the recreational player who wants the "rush" of a 10x payout on a Flush or Full House, Good Times Pay offers a fun, albeit more expensive, alternative.
