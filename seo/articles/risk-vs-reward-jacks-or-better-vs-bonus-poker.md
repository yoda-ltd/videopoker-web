# Jacks or Better vs. Bonus Poker: Strategy Overlap and Hand-Ranking Priorities

For the foundational video poker player, the choice between **Jacks or Better (JoB)** and **Bonus Poker** is the most common decision at the casino bar. Both games utilize a "Static Strategy" framework—meaning no wild cards are involved—but they differ in how they distribute their return-to-player (RTP). Understanding the **Strategy Convergence** and **Optimal Hold Variance** between these two is essential for a professional **best video poker strategy comparison** and learning **how to play video poker optimally**.

## The Core Conflict: Stability vs. The Bonus Push

In their "Full Pay" versions, these two games are closely matched, but the distribution of their payouts creates a different experience for the bankroll.

| Metric | Full Pay Jacks or Better (9/6) | Full Pay Bonus Poker (8/5) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Optimal RTP** | **99.54%** | **99.17%** |
| **Two Pair** | 2-for-1 | 2-for-1 |
| **Full House** | 9-for-1 | 8-for-1 |
| **Flush** | 6-for-1 | 5-for-1 |
| **4 Aces** | 25-for-1 | 80-for-1 |

**The Short Answer:** **9/6 Jacks or Better** is mathematically superior with a lower house edge. However, **8/5 Bonus Poker** is often a better choice when the casino has downgraded the JoB machines to 8/5 or 7/5 versions.

## 1. Strategy Overlap: High Strategy Convergence

Both JoB and Bonus Poker are built on the same strategic foundation. Because they both pay 2-for-1 for Two Pair, your primary goal is to maximize the frequency of Pairs and Two Pairs to sustain your bankroll while waiting for a Royal Flush or 4-of-a-kind.

*   **Shared Strategy:** In both games, you always hold Two Pair. It is the engine of your bankroll survival.
*   **High Pair Priority:** A pair of Jacks or Better remains the most important mid-level hand to hold in both games.

## 2. Hand-Ranking Inversion: The Bonus Quad Shift

The primary difference is where the "Bonus" comes from. In video poker, nothing is free; the casino must "take" from one part of the pay table to "give" to another. To pay for the 80-for-1 Aces in Bonus Poker, the casino reduces the Full House and Flush payouts.

*   **The Flush Draw Pivot:** In 9/6 Jacks or Better, a 4-card Flush is a stronger draw than in 8/5 Bonus Poker because of the 6-for-1 vs 5-for-1 payout difference.
*   **The Ace Value Shift:** Because 4-of-a-kind Aces pays 80-for-1 in Bonus Poker (instead of 25-for-1 in JoB), the "static" strategy changes slightly. This is a subtle **Hand-Ranking Inversion**. You become slightly more aggressive in holding lone Aces in Bonus Poker, though you still prioritize a High Pair of Jacks over a lone Ace.

## 3. Penalty Card Impact: Stability vs. Volatility

Professional players focus on **Penalty Card Impact** when evaluating these games.

*   **In Jacks or Better:** The 9/6 payout for Full House and Flush makes the game very stable. Penalty cards (discarding cards that could have helped a Straight or Flush) are less damaging because the "even" distribution of the pay table rewards consistency.
*   **In Bonus Poker:** By reducing the frequent payouts (Full House/Flush) to pay for the rare ones (Bonus Quads), Bonus Poker becomes slightly more volatile. This is a classic example of **Optimal Hold Variance**. You are trading small, frequent wins for the chance at a larger "Bonus" quad.

## 4. Identifying the Best Video Poker Strategy Comparison

The biggest hazard for players is the "Trap Table." A 9/6 Jacks or Better machine is great, but a 6/5 Jacks or Better machine has a devastating house edge.

**Comparison of Common Tables:**
1.  **9/6 JoB (99.54%):** The gold standard for low-volatility play.
2.  **8/5 Bonus Poker (99.17%):** A very respectable second choice if 9/6 JoB isn't available.
3.  **8/5 JoB (97.30%):** A trap. If you see 8/5 Jacks or Better, you are better off looking for 8/5 Bonus Poker.

## Conclusion: How to Transition

If you find a **9/6 Jacks or Better** machine, it is the superior choice for both house edge and bankroll stability. It is the "purest" form of video poker.

However, **8/5 Bonus Poker** is an excellent alternative for players who find Jacks or Better "boring" and want the excitement of a bigger payout when they hit Four Aces. Before you sit down, audit the pay table. If the JoB machine isn't 9/6, check if the Bonus Poker machine is 8/5—it might just be the best video poker strategy comparison you make all night and help you learn **how to play video poker optimally**.
