# Strategy Differences: Moving from Tens or Better to Super Aces Bonus

The transition from Tens or Better to Super Aces Bonus (SAB) is the most radical move a player can make in the Jacks or Better family. You are moving from the "Safest" game to the "Strikest" game. This **best video poker strategy comparison** examines the **Strategy Bifurcation** and **Variance Scaling** involved in this high-risk transition.

## The Mathematical Foundation: Survival vs. Jackpot

**Tens or Better** is the ultimate "Survival" game. By paying you for a pair of Tens, the game provides a massive "Safety Net." The **video poker house edge analysis** is based on "Minimizing Losses" while waiting for a natural big hand.

**Super Aces Bonus** is the ultimate "Jackpot" game. It pays a massive premium for 4 Aces (often 160-to-1 or 800 coins). To fund this, the game uses heavy **Payout Compression** on "Flush" and "Full House" hands and potentially on "Two-Pair" (depending on the pay table). The **EV Gap** in SAB is massive; you are "Bleeding" bankroll on every hand that doesn't include an Ace.

## Variance Scaling and the Bankroll Buffer

1.  **Tens or Better:** The variance is low (around 18-20). The **Variance Scaling** is shallow. Your bankroll moves in small, manageable increments. You need a very small "Buffer" to keep playing.
2.  **Super Aces Bonus:** The variance is high (often 45-55). The **Variance Scaling** is steep and "Aggressive." Your bankroll will "Liquidate" rapidly if you don't hit the Aces early. You need a bankroll buffer at least 5x larger than what you'd need for Tens or Better.

## Strategy Bifurcation: The "Hold" Reset

The move requires a total "Hold" adjustment:
*   In **Tens or Better**, you are a "Conservative Generalist." You hold high cards (Tens and up) with extreme priority. You never "Chase" anything that puts your "Money Back" threshold at risk.
*   In **Super Aces Bonus**, you are an "Ace Specialist." You will hold a single Ace with extreme priority. You will break apart "Winning" high pairs to keep a lone Ace. You will break apart "Winning" Two-Pair to chase the Ace. This is a level of "Risk" that Tens or Better players often find "Reckless."

If you are ready to move from "Playing for Time" to "Playing for the Win," Super Aces Bonus is the ultimate destination. It offers the biggest "Rush" in the Jacks or Better family, but it requires the "Nerves" and the "Bankroll" of a seasoned advantage player.
