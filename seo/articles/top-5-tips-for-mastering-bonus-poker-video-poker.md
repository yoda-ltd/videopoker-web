# Top 5 Tips for Mastering Bonus Poker Video Poker

Bonus Poker is a favorite for players who enjoy the stability of Jacks or Better but crave bigger rewards for hitting Four-of-a-Kind. While the "Bonus" in the name refers to higher payouts for Aces, 2s, 3s, and 4s, mastering this game requires a refined approach to strategy and a deep trust in the **video poker fairness and regulation** framework.

Here are the top five professional tips for mastering Bonus Poker, supported by the integrity of modern **Technical Standards**.

## 1. Hunt the "Bonus" Payouts Aggressively

The defining feature of Bonus Poker is the tiered payout for Quads. Four Aces typically pays 80-for-1, while 2s, 3s, and 4s pay 40-for-1. 
- **The Tip:** If you have a pair of Aces, 2s, 3s, or 4s, hold them and draw for the bonus. These cards are significantly more valuable in this variant than in standard Jacks or Better.
- **Integrity Note:** The **trusted video poker RNG certification** ensures that Aces and small cards are not "weighted" to appear less frequently than other cards.

## 2. Verify the Full House and Flush Payouts

Bonus Poker is often found in various versions, with the "8/5" version (8 for Full House, 5 for Flush) being the standard Full Pay. 
- **The Tip:** Always check the pay table. Avoid "6/5" or "7/5" machines, as the lower return on common hands will quickly erode your bankroll.
- **Fairness Note:** Regulated casinos must adhere to **Jurisdictional Mandates** that require them to clearly and accurately display these payout tables for every hand.

## 3. Don't Discard the "Kicker" Too Soon

While you should almost always discard a kicker with a pair, Bonus Poker strategy shifts slightly when you have a Three-of-a-Kind. 
- **The Tip:** If you have three Aces, hold only the Aces and draw two new cards. However, ensure you are playing the correct strategy for the specific "Bonus" version (like Double Bonus), as kickers matter more there.
- **Security Note:** Independent **Lab Audits** by firms like **GLI** verify that the machine’s **Source Code** correctly identifies and pays the "Bonus" combinations without error.

## 4. Trust the Randomness of the Draw

A common myth is that if you hit a "Bonus" payout, the machine will go "cold." 
- **The Tip:** Every hand in Bonus Poker is an independent event. The RNG does not care how much you just won.
- **Integrity Note:** **RTP Verification** ensures that the machine provides a fair, statistical distribution over the long term. A big win does not change your mathematical odds on the next hand.

## 5. Master the Small Denominations First

Because Bonus Poker has slightly higher variance than Jacks or Better, it's wise for beginners to start small. 
- **The Tip:** Play at a denomination that allows you to weather a "Quad Drought." This ensures you have enough bankroll to hit the big 80-for-1 or 40-for-1 payouts.
- **Integrity Note:** Only play on platforms with a **trusted video poker RNG certification**. This ensures that even at low stakes, the game integrity and fairness are maintained to professional standards.

## Conclusion
Mastering Bonus Poker is about balancing the search for high-value Quads with the discipline of standard poker strategy. By targeting the bonus cards and playing on regulated, fair platforms, you can enjoy a game that offers both stability and high-payout excitement. Remember: the bonus is in the Aces—play them right!
