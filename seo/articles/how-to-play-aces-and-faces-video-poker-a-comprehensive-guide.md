# How to Play Aces and Faces Video Poker: A Comprehensive Guide

Aces and Faces is a popular "Bonus" style video poker game that adds a layer of excitement to the classic Jacks or Better framework. By offering specialized payouts for Four of a Kind hands involving—you guessed it—Aces and Face cards, this variant provides a unique tactical challenge. To master the game, players must understand **Pay Table Verification** and the nuances of the **EV Differential** between different machines.

## A Brief History of Aces and Faces

Aces and Faces gained popularity in European and Australian casinos before making a significant splash in the online gaming world. It was designed to bridge the gap between the low-volatility Jacks or Better and the extremely volatile Double Double Bonus. By rewarding "High Quad" hands (Aces, Kings, Queens, and Jacks), it gives players a reason to cheer for specific four-of-a-kind combinations without the extreme bankroll swings of "Double" variants.

## Pay Table Verification: 8/5 vs. 7/5 Aces and Faces

When looking for the **best odds for Aces and Faces**, you must look at the Full House and Flush payouts. Unlike Jacks or Better, the "Full Pay" version of Aces and Faces is typically an 8/5 table.

*   **8/5 Aces and Faces:** Pays 8 for a Full House and 5 for a Flush. This version typically offers an RTP of approximately 99.26%.
*   **7/5 Aces and Faces:** This common "short-pay" version drops the Full House to 7, significantly increasing the house edge.

The **EV Differential** here is roughly 1.1%, which might seem small but is massive over the course of a 1,000-hand session. Always check the pay table before committing your bankroll.

## Aces and Faces vs. Jacks or Better: Comparative Analysis

The core gameplay is identical, but the "Quad" payouts change everything:
1.  **Aces Quad:** Usually pays 80-for-1.
2.  **Face Card Quads (J, Q, K):** Usually pay 40-for-1.
3.  **Other Quads (2-10):** Usually pay 25-for-1.

Because of these increased payouts, the **Volatility Index** of Aces and Faces is slightly higher than Jacks or Better. You are essentially "trading" a bit of the payout from smaller hands (like the Full House) to fund the "bonus" payouts for high quads.
