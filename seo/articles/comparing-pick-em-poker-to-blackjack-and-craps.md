# Comparing Pick 'em Poker to Blackjack and Craps
Category: Comparison

When choosing between **Pick 'em Poker** video poker, **Blackjack**, and **Craps**, players are essentially deciding between a game of "Fixed Outcome Choice," "Skill-Based Odds," and "Pure Probability." Each game offers a unique path to **EV Maximization**, but the mechanics and house edges differ significantly.

### The Comparison Matrix: Pick 'em Poker vs. Blackjack vs. Craps

This **best video poker strategy comparison** highlights how the different house edges and skill requirements impact your long-term return.

| Feature | Pick 'em Poker (Full Pay) | Blackjack (Standard Rules) | Craps (Pass Line + Odds) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTP (Theoretical)** | 99.95% | ~99.50% | ~99.40% |
| **Variance Index** | 15.0 | Low (~1.2) | Medium (~2.0) |
| **Skill Requirement** | High (Exact Choice) | High (Basic Strategy) | Low (Simple Bets) |
| **House Edge Analysis** | 0.05% | 0.50% | 0.60% |

### Strategic Divergence: Simple Choice vs. Card Totals

In **Pick 'em Poker**, you are shown two fixed cards and must choose between two "stacks" of three cards. This is a game of **Volatility Normalization** through simple mathematical choice. In **Blackjack**, your return is skill-dependent, focusing on the total value of your cards versus the dealer's. In **Craps**, your return is primarily determined by the specific bets you place (like Pass Line with Odds), with minimal skill required once the bet is made.

**Strategy Overlap:** There is zero (0%) strategy overlap between these games. While they all aim for **EV Maximization**, the methods are entirely different. In Pick 'em Poker, you are choosing between two stacks; in Blackjack, you are hitting or standing for 21; in Craps, you are placing chips for dice results.

### Key Strategy Adjustments
1.  **Pick 'em Poker Skill:** You must learn the exact math of choosing the better stack.
2.  **Blackjack Discipline:** You must follow Basic Strategy perfectly to achieve the 0.50% house edge.
3.  **Craps Simplicity:** To achieve the best odds in Craps, you simply place the Pass Line bet and back it with the maximum allowed "Odds" bet. This is a form of **Payout Compression**—the "Odds" bet has zero house edge.

Through a detailed **video poker house edge analysis**, it's clear that Pick 'em Poker offers a superior return compared to standard Blackjack and Craps, provided you are willing to learn the exact choice strategy. However, Blackjack offers a much lower variance, making it a smoother ride for the risk-averse player.
