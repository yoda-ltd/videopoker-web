How to Play Aces and Faces Video Poker — Royal Card Bonus Strategy

Aces and Faces is a sophisticated video poker variant that rewards players for hitting Four of a Kind with premium cards: Aces (80×) and face cards — Jacks, Queens, and Kings (40×). Based on Jacks or Better rules but with a special focus on high-value Aces and Face cards, this game is popular in European casinos and online video poker sites. With optimal strategy, it returns approximately 99.26% RTP.

Basic Rules — How to Play Aces and Faces

1. Standard 52-card deck, no wild cards. Minimum winning hand: a pair of Jacks or better.

2. Bonus payouts activate on Four of a Kind depending on rank: Aces (80×), Face cards J/Q/K (40×), other ranks (25×).

3. Bet max (BET 5): Royal Flush pays 4,000 credits at max bet.

4. Key strategy: Treat Aces and face cards (J, Q, K) as premium cards worth holding and drawing to aggressively.

Basic Strategy — Beginner Tips

Ace and Face card priority: Hold Aces and face cards (J, Q, K) with extra care — they're your ticket to bonus payouts.

Three Aces: Always hold three Aces and draw 2 cards — Four Aces pays 80× and you have approximately a 1-in-46 chance.

Three face cards: Similarly, hold three Jacks, Queens, or Kings and draw to Four of a Kind (40×).

Pairs of Aces: A pair of Aces is more valuable than any other pair — hold it preferentially and draw 3 cards.

Don't break straights/flushes: Unlike Bonus Poker, Aces and Faces doesn't justify breaking a flush for an Ace draw unless you have 3+ Aces already.

Advanced Strategy — Expert Play

Full House exception: With three Aces and a pair (Full House), breaking the full house to draw for Four Aces (80×) is mathematically correct. The EV of drawing for quads exceeds the guaranteed full house payout.

Kicker strategy: When holding a pair of Aces, consider holding a face card kicker (J, Q, or K) — this increases the chance of hitting Four Aces with Kicker (same payout) while maintaining a draw to a Full House.

Royal Flush priority: Four cards to a suited Royal Flush (A-K-Q-J-10 same suit) always takes priority over any made hand except a made flush or better.

Suited Ace-Face combinations: A♠ K♠ or A♥ Q♥ suited combinations are excellent holds — they draw to both Royal Flush and pairs of premium cards.

Bankroll consideration: The bonus payouts create moderate variance. Maintain a bankroll of 100-150× your max bet for comfortable play.
