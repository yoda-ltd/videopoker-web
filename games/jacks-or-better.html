Jacks or Better is the most popular video poker game in Las Vegas casinos and online casinos worldwide. Also known as "Draw Poker," it forms the foundation of all video poker variants. Whether you're a beginner learning video poker strategy or an experienced player chasing the 4,000-credit Royal Flush jackpot, Jacks or Better offers the best combination of simplicity and skill.

Basic Rules — How to Play

1. Place your bet: Choose BET 1 through BET 5. Always play BET 5 (max bet) — it unlocks the 4,000-credit Royal Flush bonus instead of the standard 1,250.

2. Receive 5 cards: You're dealt 5 cards from a standard 52-card deck.

3. Select cards to HOLD: Click the cards you want to keep. The goal is to build the strongest possible poker hand.

4. Press DRAW: Discarded cards are replaced from the remaining deck. Your final hand is evaluated against the payout table.

5. Minimum winning hand: A pair of Jacks, Queens, Kings, or Aces pays 1× your bet.

Basic Strategy — Beginner Tips

Always hold: Any made hand (pair of Jacks+, two pair, three of a kind, straight, flush, full house, four of a kind). Never break a made hand chasing a Royal Flush — except when holding 4 cards to a Royal Flush.

Drawing priority (highest to lowest):

1. Four of a kind, straight flush, royal flush

2. Four cards to a royal flush (discard even a pair)

3. Three of a kind, straight, flush, full house

4. Four cards to a straight flush

5. Two pair

6. High pair (Jacks, Queens, Kings, Aces)

7. Three cards to a royal flush

8. Four cards to a flush

9. Low pair (2s–10s)

10. Four cards to an outside straight

Advanced Strategy — Expert Play

The 4,000 Royal Flush jackpot: With BET 5, a Royal Flush pays 4,000 credits vs. 1,250 at BET 1. This bonus increases the RTP by ~2%, making max bet essential for serious players.

Never hold a kicker: When holding a pair, always discard the 5th card — holding an "ace kicker" with a pair reduces your expected value.

Inside vs. outside straights: Prefer open-ended straight draws (e.g., 5-6-7-8) over gutshot straights (e.g., 5-6-8-9) — outside straights have twice the outs.

Royal Flush suited cards: Three suited high cards (e.g., A♠ K♠ Q♠) are worth holding over an unsuited high pair — the Royal Flush premium is that valuable.

Expected Value (EV) of common decisions: Holding a low pair (EV: 0.82) beats holding two unsuited high cards (EV: 0.49). When in doubt, the pair wins.
